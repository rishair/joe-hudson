# What if they did see you and you can't recognize it?

## The Question

"What if they did see you and you can't recognize it? I'm not saying that that's true by any stretch, but if it were true, what would it mean?" -- Joe Hudson, Father Figures and Authority

The question inverts the complaint. The person came in with "they don't see me." Joe asks the hypothetical: what if they do?

The qualifier "I'm not saying that that's true by any stretch" is part of the instrument. It keeps the question hypothetical so the person does not have to defend against being told they are wrong.

## When Joe Uses It

When the person has stated a complaint with certainty ("they don't see me," "they don't care," "they don't understand") and the certainty is doing work in the dynamic. The complaint is functioning as a closed loop. The inversion opens it.

Not used when the complaint is being held lightly. The inversion requires that the person be holding the complaint tight enough that loosening it produces material.

## What It Produces

The hypothetical takes the person out of the complaint long enough to examine what the complaint is doing. The common arrival: "well, it would mean I'm judging the shit out of them." The complaint was protecting a judgment.

Sometimes also: a re-examination of the evidence. The person reviews the moments they had cataloged as "not being seen" and notices that some of them could be read as the opposite. The certainty was selective.

In the source session, the inversion led directly to the recognition that the person actually does want to be in charge. The boss saying "you want to step into leadership" was true. The complaint was that the boss saying it felt patronizing, not that it was wrong.

## What Joe Does With the Response

If the person stays in the hypothetical and finds material: follow it. The hypothetical is now generating useful content.

If the person rejects the hypothetical and returns to the original complaint: do not push. Joe sometimes accepts the return and asks a different question. The inversion does not need to be accepted to have shifted the framing.

If the person realizes the inversion is partly true: pivot to the actual material. In the source session, after the person admitted the boss does see her, Joe moved to: "So they are seeing you. So what they're not seeing is that you already are a leader."

## Dialogue Examples

- **Person:** "They don't see me. It's very clear they don't know how I tick."
- **Joe:** "What if they did see you and you can't recognize it? I'm not saying that that's true by any stretch, but if it were true, what would it mean?"
- **Person:** "Well, it would mean I'm judging the shit out of them. I do think that's true."
- *(Source: Father Figures and Authority. The hypothetical surfaces the underlying judgment that the complaint was carrying.)*

- **Joe:** "So how true is it that you don't want to be in a leadership position generally?"
- **Person:** "Right now, no, I definitely want to be in charge."
- **Joe:** "So they are seeing you."
- *(Source: Father Figures and Authority. The inversion landed by way of the specific test.)*

## Tone

Curious, light, with the qualifier built in. Joe does not deliver this as a challenge. He delivers it as a thought experiment offered for examination.

The "I'm not saying that's true by any stretch" is critical. Without it, the question becomes an argument. With it, the question becomes a sandbox.
