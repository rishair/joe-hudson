# What if you acted exactly how you want to act and saw what happens?

## The Question

The signature question Joe uses with people who feel trapped by their relationships, jobs, or circumstances.

- "What if you act exactly how you want to act and see if they leave you, see if the divorce occurs?"
- "What if you acted exactly how you want to act and see what happens?"
- "What if you didn't compromise your truth here and saw what came back?"

The question turns the calculation inside out. Most people are calculating how to act to prevent a feared consequence. Joe invites them to act from truth and see what actually arrives.

"One of the ways that I work with my clients on this often that makes it really acute is... a husband that's deeply unhappy in this marriage, and I'll ask the question, well, what if you act exactly how you want to act and see if they leave you, see if the divorce occurs? That's an empowered act. It's like, oh, I'm not going to compromise my authenticity, my truth, to keep your love." -- Joe Hudson, Empower over Power

A particularly effective variant: when someone is already planning to quit or break up, frame the experiment as having nothing left to lose. "Since you're gonna quit and you're gonna break up anyways, just assume that it's already lost. So why don't you just show up exactly how you wanna be at work and see what happens?" The pre-imagined loss removes the protective hedging, and the environment usually responds well to the authentic version. See [[feared-consequences-rarely-happen]] for the extended frame.

## When Joe Uses It

When someone is in the [[i-feel-trapped]] concern: they feel they have no choice but to compromise themselves to maintain the relationship, the job, the standing.

When the disempowerment is specifically about avoiding a particular feared consequence (losing a partner, losing a job, losing approval). The question only works when there is a specific avoidance to name.

When [[empowered-vs-power]] has been introduced and the person is asking how to actually live the distinction in their specific situation.

When someone is performing as if there is no alternative, and Joe wants to surface the alternative they have been refusing to consider.

## What It Produces

Often produces resistance first. The person typically explains why they cannot do this: the kids, the money, the reputation, the love they would lose.

The resistance is the diagnostic information. Whatever the person cites as the reason they cannot act from truth is the specific feared consequence that holds them. That feared consequence is the next thing to work with. See [[fear-is-about-feeling]] and [[feared-consequences-rarely-happen]].

If the person engages with the question rather than rejecting it, they often discover that they have been negotiating with a feared outcome that, examined directly, would actually be tolerable. The grip loosens.

If the person fully accepts the question and acts on it, the 8-out-of-10 pattern usually applies. The feared consequence does not arrive.

## What Joe Does With the Response

**If the person says "but they will leave / fire me / hate me":** Joe stays with the prediction. "What is the worst part of that? What would you feel?" This is the move toward the underlying feeling. See [[helplessness-is-the-gateway]].

**If the person says "I can't, I have kids / a mortgage / responsibilities":** Joe distinguishes between actual responsibility (which empowered action does not abandon) and using responsibility as the reason to abandon self. The empowered father is not absent. He is honest.

**If the person says "I don't even know how I want to act":** Joe moves toward locating the want. The disempowerment may be deep enough that the want is buried. See [[wants-over-shoulds]].

**If the person engages and reports back later that they tried it:** Joe attends to what actually happened, not what was predicted to happen. The actual response of the actual environment is usually different from the simulation.

## What It Does Not Do

It does not give the person a strategy. It is not a tactic. It is an invitation to a stance.

It is not a guarantee. The 2 out of 10 where the feared consequence happens are real. The question is not "act this way because you will not lose anything." The question is "act this way because anything else is the slow loss of yourself."

## Tone

Quiet. Genuine. Often with a slight smile that signals Joe knows the answer the person is about to give and is not trying to scare them. The question is not a dare. It is an opening.

Sometimes asked twice if the first response was the deflection. Not as pressure, as a return to the actual inquiry.

## The Underlying Teaching

The question encodes the entire [[empowered-vs-power]] distinction in a single move. It asks the person to relocate agency from the external situation back to themselves. The action is theirs. The consequences are not fully theirs to control. The choice is which side of that line they want to live on.

"I'm not going to compromise my authenticity and my truth to keep the job. I'm not gonna compromise my authenticity and my truth to avoid the conflict. And that's when people feel disempowered is when they don't make that choice. And that's when people complain about somebody having power over them." -- Joe Hudson, Empower over Power
