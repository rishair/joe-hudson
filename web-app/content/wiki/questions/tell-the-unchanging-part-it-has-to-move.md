# Connect with the part of you that has never moved. Now tell it it has to move.

## The Question

A two-part move. First a contact instruction, then a provocation.

**Part 1 (contact):**
- "How do you get in touch with the part of you right now that has never been moved, that has never been touched, that has never been affected?"
- "The part of you that's always been there the same way since the beginning till now."
- "I don't need an answer as much as can you connect with that part?"

**Part 2 (provocation):**
- "Now tell it that it has to move, and I want to hear what its response is. You have to move to be valuable. You have to get somewhere to be valuable."
- "Say it out loud if you would be so kind."

The instruction is to address the unchanging interior directly with the demand that has organized the person's life. The response is what the unchanging part says back when ordered to perform.

## When Joe Uses It

After the person has surfaced a [[false dichotomy]] between being-and-going-somewhere. Specifically: the person has located something internal that functions as a compass ([[whats-the-scorecard-that-cant-be-taken-away]]), and then the mind has thrown up the objection "but isn't being-with-the-compass insufficient if it doesn't move me somewhere?"

The trigger is the embedded assumption that value requires movement. The move surfaces what the unchanging part actually has to say about that assumption.

It is also useful when:
- The person is in [[scorecard-keeping-as-identity-structure]] and the scorecard is doing the work of "telling them they are getting somewhere."
- The person has a strong achievement drive and an inability to feel okay without producing forward motion.
- The person has just glimpsed an internal substrate and immediately worries it will lead to stagnation.

The question requires the person to already have some contact with the unchanging part. Asked too early, it produces a confused "I don't know what you mean." Asked after the person has been in a spacious state or has located an internal compass, it produces a real answer.

## What It Produces

The unchanging part usually does not respond the way the speaker expects. The expected response would be calm equanimity, the part saying "I don't need to move." The actual response is often more revealing.

K's response when ordered to move:

- **K:** "It's saying it's basically kind of scared of the authority figure, the person giving the order, saying like, where do you want me to go? Just tell me. It almost like it has no agency, that when it's waiting to be told. And scared. I mean, fear comes up often."
- *(Source: What's Your Scorecard? — the unchanging part is revealed as scared of authority, agency-less, waiting for instructions.)*

What surfaces: the unchanging part, when addressed by the demand to perform, reports being scared of the authority issuing the demand. The part is not a calm sovereign witness. It is a child waiting to be told what to do.

This is the diagnostic. The fact that the unchanging part responds with fear-of-authority reveals where the demand-to-perform has been living. It has been an internal authority issuing orders to a part that has organized around obeying. The scorecard, in this frame, is the part's attempt to find out what the authority wants so it can comply.

Common responses:
- "Scared. Waiting to be told. No agency."
- "It doesn't know what you want from it."
- "It's frozen. It doesn't know how to answer."
- "It wants to know what the right answer is."
- Sometimes (less commonly): "It doesn't want to move." A clear refusal. This is the response of a part that has already established its sovereignty.

The response that didn't come for K but might come for others: peaceful refusal. "It's saying it doesn't need to. It's been here the whole time. It doesn't need to go anywhere." When this response comes, the work is largely done. When the fear-of-authority response comes instead, the work moves to the childhood-imprint material: where the authority that the part is scared of originated.

## What Joe Does With the Response

**If the unchanging part responds with fear-of-authority / waiting-to-be-told:** Joe usually pivots to childhood. "When you were a kid, how often did the rules of the game change?" The fear of authority is rooted in the original authority where the part learned to wait for instructions. The work then moves to surfacing the family-of-origin imprint that installed the waiting-for-instructions stance. See [[childhood-imprinting]] and [[how-you-related-to-parents-is-how-you-relate-to-god-and-money]].

**If the unchanging part refuses to move (with peace):** Joe stays with the felt sense of that refusal. The peaceful refusal is what the rest of the work has been trying to surface. The person can now feel directly that the unchanging part does not require movement to be valuable. The session has arrived at its destination.

**If the person can't get to the unchanging part:** Joe waits or rephrases. "The part of you that has always been the same. Not the part that's stressed, not the part that's worrying. The part underneath that's just been here." If the person still can't access it, that itself is the data: the identity has been organized so completely around movement that the unchanging substrate has not yet been located. Other moves are needed first — usually returning to [[whats-the-scorecard-that-cant-be-taken-away]] or [[strip-away-externals-thought-experiment]].

**If the person tries to answer from the intellect ("intellectually it knows it doesn't have to move"):** Joe redirects to the body. "Not what you think. What does it actually say? Say it out loud." The intellect will always produce the correct-sounding answer. The body has to be the one to speak.

**If the unchanging part responds with anger ("don't tell me what to do"):** This is also a productive response. The anger is the part recovering its sovereignty in real time. Joe stays with the anger.

## Dialogue Example

The setup, from K's session:

- **K:** "So the dichotomy is that being and just being with that internal compass is insufficient if it doesn't actually move you to some other place."
- **Joe:** "Ah, yeah. What's the part of you that's not — what's the part of you that's never been — I don't need an answer as much as how do you get in touch with the part of you right now that has never been moved, that has never been touched, that has never been affected? Or the part of you that's always been there the same way since the beginning till now."
- **K:** "Do you mean to kind of connect with that part?"
- **Joe:** "Yeah. And now tell it that it has to move, and I want to hear what its response is. You have to move to be valuable. You have to get somewhere to be valuable."
- **K:** "It—"
- **Joe:** "Say it out loud if you would be so kind."
- **K:** "Yeah, it's saying it's basically kind of scared of the authority figure, the person giving the order, saying like, where do you want me to go? Just tell me. It almost like it has no agency, that when it's waiting to be told. And scared. I mean, fear comes up often."
- *(Source: What's Your Scorecard? — the unchanging part reveals itself as a child waiting for instruction.)*

The opening for the childhood material comes from this response. The next move is "When you were a kid, how often did the rules of the game change?"

## Tone

Slow. Spacious. There is room around the words. Joe is not rushing the contact. The pause between "connect with that part" and "now tell it it has to move" is deliberate. The person needs time to actually locate the part before the provocation lands.

The provocation itself ("you have to move to be valuable") is delivered flat, almost as if reading a stage direction. Joe is not performing the authority; he is asking the person to receive the message they have been carrying internally for decades. The flatness keeps the focus on the part's response rather than on the delivery.

When the unchanging part speaks, Joe receives whatever comes without correcting it. If it speaks fear, the fear is welcomed. If it speaks defiance, the defiance is welcomed. The part is allowed to be exactly what it is.

## What This Move Is Not

It is not a guided meditation. The intent is not relaxation or spaciousness as a state. The intent is to make audible what the person's interior actually says when the demand-to-perform is named.

It is not parts work in the IFS sense. The "part" Joe is asking for is not one of several parts; it is the substrate, the unchanging interior. The provocation isn't aimed at a part to be negotiated with. It is aimed at the place where the demand has been being absorbed.

It is not a test. There is no correct answer for the unchanging part to give. Whatever it says is the data. Joe does not redirect if the answer is "uncomfortable" or "wrong."
