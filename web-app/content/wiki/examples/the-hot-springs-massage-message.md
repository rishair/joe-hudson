# The Hot Springs Massage Message

## The Story

Joe was at a hot springs, getting a massage from "a very woo woman." Mid-massage, she stopped, said "they have a message for you," and delivered something to the effect of: "We can't wait any longer. You know what you're supposed to do."

The woman knew nothing about Joe. She had no context for whatever he was working through. The message arrived without preparation. The response was immediate: "Oh, shit, she's right."

"I was at this hot springs and I'm getting a massage from this woman, definitely a very woo woman, right? And she's giving me this massage and she stops and she said, 'They have a message for you.' And I was like, 'Uh?' And the message was something to the effect of, 'We can't wait any longer. You know what you're supposed to do.' This woman knew nothing about me, didn't know if I was in any question of any of it, but she just—" -- Joe Hudson, How To Find Your Purpose

Brett caught the structure immediately: "What an evocative prompt anyway." Joe confirmed: "Totally, exactly. Exactly."

"And when it hit me, I, I was like, oh, shit, she's right. It required that prompt. For whatever reason it came, for whatever weirdness that happened, serendipity that happened in my life, I knew at that moment, oh, there's something that I'm supposed to be sharing. Me being of service and helping people see themselves more clearly is what I'm supposed to be doing." -- Joe Hudson, How To Find Your Purpose

## What This Example Illustrates

The example illustrates [[purpose-is-recognized-not-found]]. The woman did not give Joe his purpose. She gave him the prompt to acknowledge what he already knew. The information was already there. The acknowledgement was what had been deferred.

Three structural points:

**The recognition is already in the person.** The message landed not because it delivered new information but because it delivered permission to acknowledge information that was already known. The "Oh, shit, she's right" response is the response of recognition, not of learning. New information produces processing time; recognition is immediate.

**The prompt does not have to be sophisticated.** A stranger at a hot springs, with no context, with what could be dismissed as "woo woo" framing, was sufficient. The prompt's authority does not come from the speaker's credentials. It comes from the listener being ready to hear it. The same words from a more conventional source might not have landed as well; the unexpectedness was part of what bypassed the defenses.

**The deferral had been internal.** Joe was the one waiting. The "we can't wait any longer" framing made the deferral visible as something he had been doing, not as a structural absence of his purpose. The acknowledgement was overdue, not undiscovered.

## The Specific Content of the Recognition

The recognition was specific: "There's something that I'm supposed to be sharing. Me being of service and helping people see themselves more clearly is what I'm supposed to be doing."

This is not a generic purpose-statement. It is a specific shape — service, helping people see themselves more clearly. Joe's career since then has been an unfolding of this exact shape, in different forms: songs and comics in one era, business in another, the current human-development work. The shape was stable from the moment of acknowledgement. The forms have varied.

The recognition did not specify the form. It specified the through-line. The through-line is the purpose. The forms are the vehicles. See [[purpose-is-how-not-what]].

## Why the Message Could Land

Several conditions made the moment possible:
- Joe had been in some quiet inquiry about purpose, even if not consciously framed as such.
- He was in a relaxed state (the hot springs, the massage), not in a defensive or busy state.
- The deliverer had no agenda. She was not trying to sell Joe on his purpose. She was just channeling whatever she was channeling.
- The framing of the message ("we can't wait any longer") implied that someone or something else was waiting — which sidestepped Joe's ego-resistance and addressed something deeper.

These conditions are not strict requirements, but they help explain why this particular prompt worked. The general principle: the recognition arrives when the conditions for recognition exist, and those conditions usually involve relaxation, openness, and a deliverer with no stake in the outcome.

## When to Use This Story

The story is useful when:

- A coachee is reporting that they have been "looking for their purpose" for a long time. The story reframes the search as a deferred acknowledgement.
- A coachee dismisses the idea of intuitive recognition as "woo." The story owns that framing directly — Joe calls the woman "definitely a very woo woman" — and lands the recognition anyway. The recognition does not care about the listener's metaphysics.
- A coachee asks how the moment of recognition is supposed to arrive. The story illustrates that it can arrive through any prompt, often unexpectedly, often through someone with no qualifications to be the messenger.
- A coachee is in resistance to acknowledging something they already know. The "we can't wait any longer" framing names the deferral as a choice that has been costing.

## When Not to Use This Story

The story can be misheard as "wait for a stranger at a hot springs to tell you your purpose." That is not the teaching. The teaching is that the recognition is already available; the prompt can come from anywhere; the work is to be receptive enough to let it land when it arrives.

The story can also be misheard as a romanticization of unsolicited spiritual messages. Joe's tone is bemused — he names the "woo" framing, acknowledges the serendipity, does not insist on a particular interpretation of how the message arrived. The story is offered as data, not as a model to be replicated.

## What the Story Does Not Promise

The story does not promise the recognition will arrive comfortably. Joe describes the response as "Oh, shit." Not "Oh, wonderful." The acknowledgement carries weight. Whatever the recognition is, acting on it requires moving toward something that was being avoided. The avoidance had a reason.

The story also does not promise that one moment of recognition resolves the work. Joe's purpose has continued to unfold over decades. The recognition opened the door; the living of the purpose has been the ongoing work. See [[purpose-is-an-unfolding-not-a-destination]].

## The Pattern: Recognition Through Another Person

This story is one instance of a recurring pattern: the recognition of one's own truth arrives through another person seeing it first. The pattern shows up elsewhere in Joe's work:

- A woman at one of his early retreats saw his choicelessness about his work before he had fully acknowledged it himself: "You don't have any choice, do you?" See [[the-retreat-woman-no-choice]].
- Joe's coaching method often involves naming what the client already knows but has not let themselves admit. The client's "Oh, shit, you're right" is the same response as Joe had to the hot springs message.

The pattern is structural: when someone has been deferring an acknowledgement, hearing it spoken aloud by someone with no stake in the outcome makes the deferral suddenly unsustainable. The defense had been "I don't know." Once someone says it, "I don't know" stops working. The recognition has to be admitted.

For the AI coach: this is part of why simply asking the right question, or naming the obvious thing the person has been pretending not to see, can be transformative. The coach is not delivering new information. The coach is creating the conditions for an acknowledgement the person has been deferring.
