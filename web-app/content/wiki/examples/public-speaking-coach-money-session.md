# The Public Speaking Coach Money Session

## The Point It Illustrates

A very short, rapid-fire session showing how a childhood imprint about "X was more important than you" gets traced (in two questions) to the parent's avoided emotion, then applied (in one question) to the adult presenting problem of not being able to market what you love.

This session is the canonical rapid-fire example of [[i-make-money-only-when-i-hate-what-im-doing]] — the love-money inverse pattern — being resolved through (1) drilling past the surface object the parent prioritized to the parent's avoided emotion, (2) applying that emotion to the present avoidance, (3) testing whether the shame is in the room, (4) naming the skill-transfer, and (5) refusing the shame frame when it arrives at the close.

The whole session is under 25 turns. Joe does not develop the work, give business advice, or offer techniques. He just keeps applying the template surfaced in the first two minutes to whatever the client says next.

## The Setup

The client (a public speaking coach) opens with the question "Why do I resist money so much?" He immediately self-discloses that he recently had a realization about the connection between money resistance and his relationship with his father — triggered by a Joe Hudson podcast. The realization: "the money's more important than I am."

The childhood scene he reports: his father drank heavily. A deal had been made — Dad would not drink on weekends, would pay $200 each time he did. The kid had accumulated $1,200. At age 7, the kid bragged about it in front of the family. The dad "went nuts." The kid took from this that "money's more important than I am."

The session takes that childhood scene and works it forward to the present cash-flow problem.

## The Arc

### Step 1: Drill past the surface object

The kid's initial story is "money was more important than me" (because dad raged at the bragging about the $1,200). Joe drills:

- **Joe:** "What was it really, if it wasn't money that was more important than you were?"
- **Guest:** "Booze."
- **Joe:** "And what did the booze represent?"
- **Guest:** "Avoiding, avoiding his feelings, avoiding his helplessness."
- **Joe:** "So avoiding his helplessness was more important than you?"

The surface object was booze. The deeper object was the dad's avoidance of his own helplessness. This is the [[what-was-really-more-important-than-you]] move in compressed form.

### Step 2: Drill the second scene

Joe then drills the $1,200 bragging moment separately:

- **Joe:** "And, and so that moment when he was like fucking pissed because you were bragging about that $1,200, what was more important than you at that moment?"
- **Guest:** "He was ashamed."
- **Guest:** "The shame was more important. Not feeling the shame, avoiding the shame by getting angry. Yeah, raging."

The dad's avoided shame is now in the room. The dad's anger was the management strategy. The kid received: "shame-avoidance is what I get traded for."

### Step 3: Pivot to the present

- **Joe:** "Yeah. And, and so what's your issue with money?"
- **Guest:** "When I love what I'm doing, I don't make any money. And when I hate what I'm doing, I've made lots and lots of money. So I love what I'm doing now. Yeah, it's a bit of a struggle."

The presenting problem emerges as the inverse pattern. See [[i-make-money-only-when-i-hate-what-im-doing]].

### Step 4: Establish the practical context

- **Joe:** "Do you need money?"
- **Guest:** "Yeah."
- **Joe:** "And what are you doing right now?"
- **Guest:** "I'm a coach. Public speaking."
- **Joe:** "What is it that, like, what's hard about making money here in coaching public speaking?"
- **Guest:** "Just cash flow, clients. Getting enough clients."

The client gives the market frame. Joe does not engage it.

### Step 5: Apply the childhood pattern to the present problem

- **Joe:** "How much are you — how much are you making shame more important than —"
- **Guest:** "Yeah, much more."
- **Joe:** "And how much are you making avoidance more important than you, slash money."

The childhood pattern (avoiding shame was more important than you) is applied directly to the adult (you are making avoidance — of marketing, of asking — more important than yourself and your income). See [[apply-the-childhood-pattern-to-the-present-problem]].

### Step 6: The binary that forces the avoidance into view

- **Joe:** "Either you're really a crappy coach, or you are not marketing yourself. I'm going to assume it's the second. And so that would be avoidance due to shame."

The binary collapses the external frame. See [[binary-that-forces-the-avoidance-into-view]].

### Step 7: Test the shame in the room

- **Joe:** "Oh, Yeah, even now the shame is there. If you look at me, do you, do you see that I'm ashamed of you?"
- **Guest:** "No."
- **Joe:** "If you look at all the people, do you see that they're ashamed of you for [this]?"

The shame is located internally, not relationally. See [[is-the-shame-in-the-room-or-in-you]].

### Step 8: Surface the want, then name the skill-transfer

- **Joe:** "How do you want to market yourself? Give me like 3 adjectives that would make you fucking excited to let people know what you do."
- **Guest:** "Authentic, honest. Yeah, blunt."
- **Joe:** "Yeah, I bet you teach those things in your public speaking."
- **Guest:** "Yeah."
- **Joe:** "Yeah, so you know it all. You know how to market yourself. If you know how to do public speaking, you know how to market yourself. It's the same principles."

The client teaches what he is blocked from doing. The principles transfer. See [[skill-transfer-redirect]].

### Step 9: Refuse the shame frame at the close

- **Guest:** "Yeah, that's embarrassing."
- **Joe:** "It's human."

When the client collapses into "that's embarrassing," Joe refuses the moral frame. See [[whats-wrong-with-the-thing-theyre-judging]].

## What the Session Demonstrates About Joe's Method

**Speed.** The whole session is under 25 turns. The childhood is surfaced and traced in three questions. The application to the present is one question. The diagnostic binary is one sentence. The shame test is one sentence. The skill-transfer is one sentence. The closing normalization is two words.

**Refusal to engage the surface frame.** The client repeatedly offers external explanations (cash flow, clients, nobody public speaks anymore). Joe never engages any of them. He stays on the internal pattern.

**The template, then everywhere.** Once the parent's avoided emotion is named (shame), Joe applies it everywhere. To the marketing problem ("making shame more important than money"). To the felt sense in the room ("even now the shame is there"). To the response to the recognition ("that's embarrassing"). The same emotion runs through every layer of the session. Surfacing it once gives Joe the lens for all subsequent moves.

**No business advice.** Joe does not give a single piece of marketing strategy, sales advice, or business coaching. The presenting problem is not a business problem. The session does not become a business conversation.

**Use of the client's own language.** When asking for marketing adjectives, Joe asks for what would make the client "fucking excited" — using the client's own register. The client responds with "authentic, honest, blunt" — words that match the skill domain (public speaking) Joe then uses for the transfer.

**Closure without integration ritual.** The session ends with "It's human." There is no homework, no exercise, no formal closure. The recognition is the work. The client will or will not act on it; Joe does not engineer the next step.

## When Joe Reaches For This Example

- When teaching the [[apply-the-childhood-pattern-to-the-present-problem]] move and a compressed instance is needed
- When a coach-in-training needs to see how short a money session can actually be
- When the example needs to show that "what was the surface thing really for?" is often more useful than asking about the surface thing itself
- When demonstrating that the [[i-make-money-only-when-i-hate-what-im-doing]] concern resolves through the same childhood-imprint work as other money concerns
- When showing how the [[skill-transfer-redirect]] move closes a session on visibility/marketing/self-promotion problems

## Comparison to Related Sessions

- **[[am-i-a-fake-coaching-session]]:** Same rapid-fire structural shape, also under 25 turns, also traces an adult complaint to a parent's avoided emotion (shame at not being able to provide). The fake-love session works at a deeper layer (whether love itself is corrupt); this session works at a more practical layer (whether the work can be visible enough to be paid for). The mechanism is the same.
- **[[joes-pleasing-dad-money-story]]:** Joe's own version of a childhood-to-money trace, but going the other direction — from a should about making money back to pleasing the father. This session goes the other direction — from a should about not asking for money back to avoiding the father's pattern of shame-management. Same surrogate, opposite stance.
- **The differences:** Joe's hammock story is slow and somatic (an extended cry in a public pool). The public speaking coach session is fast and dialogic. Both end with the same essential recognition: the money pattern is not about money.
