# The "Am I a Fake?" Session

## What Happened

A woman (Guest 1) opens a coaching session with a self-accusation: she has hidden agendas around love, she wants something from it, therefore she must be fake. Her authority figures — mother, ex-husband, father, sisters — had told her over the years that her motivation was corrupt. She had learned to believe them. Now, whenever she feels love, she is mesmerized by the question of whether it is real.

Joe works the session in under twenty turns. The arc:

1. **Refuse the moral collapse.** Joe asks "what's wrong with that?" four times in quick succession, refusing to join her self-judgment. The supposed corruption is not accepted as the problem. See [[whats-wrong-with-the-thing-theyre-judging]].
2. **Surface the totalizing frame.** Joe asks "How fake? 1%? 2%?" She cannot give a percentage. The unanswerability is the data — the belief is operating at the body level. See [[any-amount-answer-as-total-installation]] and [[calibrate-the-threshold]].
3. **Ground the body and bait the welcoming.** Joe has her plant both feet, drop attention into the body, eyes open, and say aloud: "I want all of me loved." See [[have-them-say-i-want-all-of-me-loved]].
4. **Scan and watch.** Joe asks her to scan her body for the part that cannot be loved. Within seconds the body retreats. See [[scan-body-for-what-cant-be-loved]].
5. **Name the retreat in real time.** "Oh, look what just happened. Where'd you go? You just pulled back." See [[name-the-body-defense-in-real-time]] and [[the-pulled-back-from-part-is-the-unloveable-part]].
6. **Find the hidden part.** She names it: "I can be very cunning, and I did not love that part."
7. **Reframe the part as universal.** "Can you imagine a 5-year-old girl who is cunning? I don't know any. I love my girls' cunningness."
8. **Trace to the original installation.** Joe takes her to the moment her mother could not give her what she wanted. He has her feel into her mother's emotion in that moment.
9. **Name the parent's shame.** Mother felt "shut" — Joe names it as guilt or shame at not being able to provide. See [[wanting-got-wired-to-unlovability]].
10. **Name the wiring.** "She got off the love. And you heard that as: when I go for what I want, I'm not lovable."
11. **Notice the body coming back online.** Joe sees her come whole into the room: "There you are, whole body you." See [[let-your-body-remember-this-moment]].

## Why This Session Matters

This is one of the cleanest demonstrations of a full childhood-imprint untying in a short coaching window. The transcript is under 130 lines. The work is locatable, repeatable, and observable end-to-end.

Several teachings are visible in compressed form:

- The refusal of the moral collapse around the self-accusation is what opens the work. If Joe had agreed (or even disputed gently), the session would have stayed in the head. See [[whats-wrong-with-the-thing-theyre-judging]].
- The body produces the diagnostic faster than the mind. The scan-and-watch sequence locates the hidden part in seconds. The mind would have needed paragraphs.
- The hidden part ("cunning") is reframed as universal among children, not bypassed and not validated. This is the [[loving-all-parts]] move in compressed form.
- The trace to the parent's specific emotion (shame at not providing) is what locates the actual mechanism. Not "your mom didn't love you well" — too vague. The specific naming ("she felt shame she couldn't provide") makes the wiring legible.
- The naming of the wiring out loud completes the circuit. "She got off the love. And you heard that as: when I go for what I want, I'm not lovable." Once heard, the wiring can begin to dissolve. See [[wanting-got-wired-to-unlovability]].
- The body squaring up at the end is the integration signal. Joe names it. See [[let-your-body-remember-this-moment]].

## What Joe Did Not Do

- He did not ask for the backstory before going into the body. The mother story comes only after the body has surfaced the hidden part. The story serves the work, not the other way around.
- He did not reassure her that her love is real. Reassurance would have validated the very frame Joe was refusing.
- He did not dispute the cunningness. He honored it.
- He did not pathologize the mother. He named her emotion specifically (shame at not being able to provide) without making the mother wrong.
- He did not extract an insight statement. He let the body integrate. The "thank you, thank you" at the end is the receipt of the work, not a recap.
