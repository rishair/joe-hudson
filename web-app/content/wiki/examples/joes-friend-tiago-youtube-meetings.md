# Joe's Friend Tiago: From "There's No Way to Enjoy YouTube Meetings" to a Channel That Jumped

## The Story

In a mastermind conversation among content creators, Joe's friend Tiago said something that landed as universal truth: "There's just no way to enjoy YouTube meetings." The other people in the room, all content creators themselves, laughed -- it sounded like a fact of the work.

Joe and the others pushed back. Of course you can enjoy YouTube meetings; in fact, we do. They went through the specific things Tiago found unenjoyable about the meetings. Each item became a candidate for redesign. As the conversation went on, Tiago started to light up. You could see him start to see that there was a different way to do YouTube -- a way that could be enjoyable.

The next year, Tiago's YouTube channel jumped substantially. The numbers went way up.

The diagnosis Joe offered: he found a way to enjoy doing YouTube. The enjoyment produced the doing. The doing produced the result.

"My friend Tiago is talking to us one day about how he hates YouTube meetings, and there was like 3 of us there. We were in this mastermind. We were all chatting about being content creators and everything. And he said, 'There's just no way to enjoy YouTube meetings.' And we were all laughing. Of course you can, we do. And we went through all the different things that were completely unenjoyable about the meeting. And you could see him start to light up. You could see him start to see that there was maybe a different way to do YouTube, something that was enjoyable. Because if I enjoy it, I'm gonna do it. And doing it is more important than anything else. Next thing you know, His YouTube channel has completely jumped over the last year. You can just see the numbers has gone way up because he found a way to enjoy doing YouTube." -- Joe Hudson, Your To-Do List is Just Your Inner Critic on Paper

## The Point Joe Uses It For

The story is the worked example for [[enjoyment-attracts]] applied to content creation. The general principle: enjoyment is the variable that determines whether the work happens and whether the work works. The Tiago story shows it in a high-stakes, observable form -- a content creator's metrics, before and after a shift in how the work was being approached.

The story also operates as a counter-argument to a common belief: that some kinds of work are intrinsically unenjoyable and must be endured. Tiago believed this about YouTube meetings -- "there's just no way to enjoy" them. The belief was wrong. The work had an unenjoyable version (the one Tiago had been doing) and an enjoyable version (the one he had not been imagining). The "no way to enjoy" framing was hiding the existence of the enjoyable version.

This is the same dynamic at play in [[lack-of-enjoyment-as-diagnostic]]. The lack of enjoyment Tiago felt was data about how he was approaching the work, not data about the work itself. Once the data was read correctly, the path to a different approach opened up.

## The Mechanism: How the Shift Worked

The shift was not "force yourself to enjoy what you hate." That move does not work; it falls into the [[enjoyment-as-path]] anti-pattern of trying-to-enjoy as resistance. The mastermind conversation did something different. It worked through the specifics:

- What exactly is unenjoyable about the meetings? (Surface the actual mechanism.)
- What if we did them differently? (Open the configuration to redesign.)
- What way could you do this that you would actually enjoy? (Move from the default-how to a chosen-how.)

This is the operational form of [[plan-how-not-just-what]]. The what was fixed -- there were going to be YouTube meetings, the work of producing the channel was going to happen. The how was the variable. Once Tiago could see that the how was variable, he could see configurations that would let him enjoy the work. Once he saw the enjoyable configurations, the doing became possible at a different rate.

The "lighting up" moment Joe describes is the somatic signature of the shift. Tiago's body responded to seeing the possibility before he had implemented any of it. The body knew the new configuration would work. The implementation followed the body's recognition.

## Why the Numbers Jumped

Joe's explanation is structural: "if I enjoy it, I'm gonna do it. And doing it is more important than anything else." The content creator who enjoys the work produces more work. The content creator who does not enjoy the work avoids the work and procrastinates on the work, and the work that does get done is the lesser version of itself. The variable that distinguishes the high-output creator from the low-output creator is often not skill, not strategy, not opportunity -- it is whether they have found a way to enjoy doing the work.

This is also [[enjoyment-attracts]] in action: the enjoyment is somatically detectable in the work itself. When Tiago started doing YouTube from a state of enjoyment, the audience could feel it. The work being made from enjoyment attracts the people who want to be in enjoyment. The work being made from endurance attracts the people who are also enduring something, and the engagement reflects that. See [[consciousness-is-the-product]] for the structural reason this matters.

The "numbers jumping" is the downstream observable. The upstream cause is the state-shift in the person making the work.

## What the Story Does Not Say

The story is not saying that every content creator's numbers will jump if they enjoy the work more. There are skill variables, market variables, timing variables, all of which matter. The story is saying that, given a certain baseline of competence and opportunity, the enjoyment-vs-endurance variable is the one that often distinguishes the breakthrough from the plateau. It is the variable creators most underweight in their own self-diagnosis.

The story is also not saying that the YouTube meetings should be eliminated or that Tiago should have quit YouTube. The work was the work. The shift was in how the work was being done. Same work; different how; different result.

## How This Example Connects to the Larger Teaching

The story is positioned in the to-do list teaching as evidence for the broader principle that the how-of-doing determines whether the work happens and whether the work produces the result. The to-do list with the right items but the wrong how will not produce the life the person wants. The same items configured for enjoyment produce a different outcome.

See [[plan-how-not-just-what]] for the principle. See [[to-do-list-as-inner-critic-on-paper]] for the related diagnostic concept. The three pieces together form the case that the relationship to the work matters more than the work itself, and that finding the enjoyable how is the operational entry point.
