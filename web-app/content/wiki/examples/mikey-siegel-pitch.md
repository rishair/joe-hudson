# Mikey Siegel Pitch Meeting

## The Story

Joe was meeting with Mikey Siegel (a public figure who has told this story himself). They were having a genuine connection. Then Siegel shifted into pitch mode. After about one sentence, Joe said: "I noticed my entire body is getting tense right now."

Joe had no idea how Siegel would react. No agenda for the outcome. He simply named what was happening in his own body.

The result: a moment of recognition for Siegel, who realized how visibly he had shifted into trying-to-get-something mode. The honesty created a level of connection that lasted years.

"It just created such a deep level of connection between us and our friendship has been years of close relationship." -- Joe Hudson, Impartiality -- Connection Course Series #3

## What It Illustrates

1. **Naming physical experience is a form of [[vulnerability]].** Joe did not say "you're pitching me" (judgment). He said "my body is getting tense" (his own truth).
2. **[[impartiality]] in real time.** Joe did not try to manage the moment. He did not know what would happen next and did not need to.
3. **The benefits of dropping partiality exceed whatever the agenda was chasing.** The relationship that emerged was more valuable than any pitch outcome.
4. **Owning the moment instead of enduring it.** Most people in Joe's position would have sat through the pitch politely, feeling the tension but not naming it. The naming is the move.

## When To Reach For This

When illustrating how vulnerability and impartiality work together in business or professional settings. When someone fears that being honest about their experience will damage a professional relationship. When explaining that connection produces better outcomes than persuasion.
