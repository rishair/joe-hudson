# Doug and the Hug-the-Demon Session (How Do I Feel Financially Safe?)

## The Situation

A rapid-fire coaching session, roughly six minutes of dialogue. Doug, in his fifties, in Salt Lake City. He has more than enough money and has had for a decade. He enjoys his work, it is easy, it pays incredibly well, and he is considering retiring. He cannot.

The block is not financial. It is loyalty. His childhood was tough; he started working at 8. The 13-year-old version of him who busted his butt for 10 hours a day in the summer for 100 days to make what he now makes in a day will not give him permission to walk away. The surface question Doug brings: "How do I say thank you to that 13-year-old boy?"

The session moves through five distinct sub-arcs in six minutes: the role-swap with the 13-year-old, the surfacing of the protection-from-assholes surrogate, the pivot to the internal asshole, the demon-hug move, and the closing on what would make life 10% more enjoyable.

## The Sequence

### 1. The Role Swap

Joe redirects Doug from third-person reporting to first-person speaking as the 13-year-old. See [[swap-roles-with-the-younger-self]].

- **Joe:** "If you were to ask the 13-year-old boy, what do I need to get your permission to quit? What would that be?"
- **Doug:** "Because that 13-year-old boy is still inside of me. I think of myself like a book."
- **Joe:** "I'm asking you to ask the 13-year-old boy inside of you and tell me his answer."

After Doug begins answering as the kid, Joe formalizes the role swap: "You're gonna be the 13-year-old boy, I'm gonna be you. For what? What do we need it for?"

The kid's answer comes immediately: "Security, protection."

### 2. The Protection-From-Assholes Surrogate Surfaces

Joe asks what the protection is for. The childhood material emerges:

- **Doug:** "Uh, I, I had a childhood that was tough, so I needed to be independent. I needed to be able to survive on my own."

Joe names the surrogate: "Security from assholes." Doug confirms.

### 3. The Pivot to the Internal Asshole

Doug then describes his external "no asshole rule" of 30 years -- three strikes and out, no exceptions. Joe matches: "Me too. I have that same rule."

Then the move that pivots the entire session inward. See [[extend-the-no-asshole-rule-inward]].

- **Joe:** "Yeah. How about your internal asshole?"
- **Doug:** "What, what, we all -- I, I have one, so what about --"
- **Joe:** "Yeah, that's what I mean. Yeah, yeah, exactly. Where, where's the protection from that?"

Doug's response surfaces that he has been at war with the internal asshole for a long time. He uses domination language: "He's, he's largely, uh, dominated now. That was a long, long struggle. That asshole came out of my childhood... I had this demon inside of me, but the demon's been slayed."

Joe notes the framing of "dominated" and "slayed" -- the diagnostic that the wrong relationship has been in place. The demon was met with violence and the violence won, but the demon still has Doug.

### 4. The Demon-Hug Move

Joe instructs Doug to turn the 13-year-old toward the demon and ask what the kid needs. The kid says he needs a lot of protection, that he is not strong enough, that he is a thread away from being devoured.

Joe makes the diagnostic statement: "I just want to comment that money is not going to help you here." The kid is being told that the protection it has been seeking through money cannot reach the threat the kid is actually scared of.

Joe then notices the emotion arriving and gives the chin-down instruction. See [[chin-up-as-emotion-block]]:

- **Joe:** "I see the emotions coming and I see you kind of evading the emotion. So chin down, bring your chin down, chin down, chin down. Yeah, there we go."

Then the demon-hug instruction. See [[chase-it-around-the-room]]:

- **Joe:** "Dougie, I want to tell you what I told my kids when they were 5 years old, that the demon is just looking for hugs. I don't want you to just face the demon. I want you to literally chase the demon around trying to hug it."
- **Doug:** "It went, it went from being an ugly dragon to like a slow, cuddly teddy bear."

The shape of the internal figure literally re-perceives itself in the body within a few sentences. Decades of demon-shape resolves into a teddy bear once the relationship inverts from fight to hug.

Joe instructs Doug to stay arm-in-arm with the teddy bear dragon. Then he asks the kid from that contact what he needs around money:

- **Doug:** "Nothing. If the demon's not here, then money's not as important."

The release comes through the kid's voice. The 13-year-old, no longer scared, releases the adult.

### 5. The 10% More Enjoyable Close

Joe pivots to the present-self with his standard closing question:

- **Joe:** "And now I just want to ask Old Doug a question, like, how do you make your life 10% more enjoyable?"
- **Doug:** "Help others, help my family, help my kids. And then I like teaching, so teach others, share with others kind of what I've learned through my journey."

The answers that arrive in this position are characteristic of post-achievement coaching. See [[whats-next-after-i-reached-my-big-goal]]. The pattern that runs through them is [[connection]] -- help, share, teach. The same pattern Ali Abdaal arrived at in his session.

Doug closes by naming the shift: "Yeah, hug the demon, don't, don't fight. It's a total -- that framework is -- I was not expecting that at all. That's -- yeah, because I, I've spent my life fighting that demon."

Joe's final reflection captures the diagnostic for when the chase-and-hug move is needed: "I know. Yeah, you kicked its ass, but it still always kind of had you."

## What This Session Demonstrates

This is a near-textbook integration of several distinct Joe moves into a single short arc. The session works because the moves stack in the right order:

1. The role swap makes the kid audible.
2. The protection question surfaces what the kid was actually seeking.
3. The external-internal pivot relocates the threat from "out there" to "in here."
4. The chin-down instruction keeps the emotion-channel open as the demon is approached.
5. The demon-hug move replaces decades of war with a different relationship.
6. The kid is asked again, from after-the-contact, what they need. The release is given in their voice.
7. The present-self is asked what they want now that the kid no longer requires the protection function.

The order matters. The release at step 6 is not available without the demon contact at step 5. The demon contact is not available without the chin-down at step 4. The chin-down would have been premature without the threat-relocation at step 3. Each step opens the next.

The session is also a demonstration that what looks like a money question can be resolved without the money being the working surface. Doug came in asking how to get permission from a 13-year-old to walk away from a high-paying job. The work happened inside, in a demon that money had been being asked to protect against. Money never got addressed directly. The protection function dissolved at the source, and the money question dissolved with it.

## The Anti-Cramming Frame

This session is the canonical demonstration of multiple distinct moves: [[swap-roles-with-the-younger-self]], [[extend-the-no-asshole-rule-inward]], the demon-hug variant of [[chase-it-around-the-room]], the in-the-moment chin-down variant of [[chin-up-as-emotion-block]], and the protection-from-assholes variant of [[money-as-surrogate]]. Each move is documented in its own article; this example shows how they integrate in real time when the right sequence is available.

## When To Reach For This Example

- When teaching the chase-the-demon move and showing that it works on internal figures, not just emotions
- When coaching someone who has built strong external boundaries and is still operating as if threatened
- When coaching the inability to retire or step back despite having met the external goal
- When demonstrating that money-as-surrogate can resolve at the level of the underlying surrogate without needing to address money directly
- When teaching that the kid's release is the operative release in post-achievement work, and that the adult cannot grant it to themselves
