# The Political Signs Steakhouse

## The Story

Joe's family had evacuated California for Arizona during the fires. After a day at a lake, they drove past a desert steakhouse displaying political signs that strongly conflicted with his daughters' beliefs. One daughter was scared. The other refused: "I don't want to deal with these people."

Joe asked, in effect: how is this different from racism? If you think they are ignorant, no one chooses their ignorance any more than they choose their race. What makes it acceptable to refuse to even be near them?

That question started the wonder.

They went in. The neighboring patrons were loud and rowdy, which heightened the kids' anxiety. Then the new puppy got into the mix, the waiter was warm, the room shifted. Joe describes it as a moment of being humans together again.

On the drive home, both daughters spontaneously named some of the wisdom on the other side of the political argument. Not agreement. Not even movement on the policy positions. Just: "Oh, I see how they would view things this way, given this circumstance, given this thing."

"Their heart opened up because they were in wonder about this whole situation. It was an incredibly beautiful thing to watch." -- Joe Hudson, Wonder

## The Point It Illustrates

Wonder dissolves us-vs-them without requiring agreement. The mechanism is not persuasion. It is the shift in posture from "I know these people are wrong" to "I do not know what produced their view." That shift makes the heart available again.

The daughters did not change their positions. They lost the closure around the other side. The opposing position became something they could see, not just dismiss.

The trigger that locks the heart is the certainty that the other side is contemptible. Wonder works on the certainty, not the disagreement.

## When Joe Reaches For This

When someone is in righteous anger at a group they oppose. When a parent is teaching children to hate the other side. When a couple, a team, or a family has split into camps and the camps no longer engage. When [[us-vs-them]] dynamics are calcifying and the person cannot see how to relate without abandoning their position.

## What It Leads To

The deeper teaching: we cling to knowing because we want to avoid the feeling of uncertainty. Wonder turns out to handle uncertainty without difficulty. The thing we were defending against is not actually a threat once we are in wonder.

"We come across our knowing and we don't want it to be messed with because we don't want that feeling of uncertainty. And it's amazing what wonder does is like uncertainty doesn't matter in wonder." -- Joe Hudson, Wonder
