# Wants Help, Resists Help, In Real Time

## What Joe Notices

The client opens by asking for help with something they cannot manage themselves. The asking is sincere. Within minutes, the same client is resisting the help the coach is offering — through confusion, through "I'm trying to understand," through pivoting to a new problem, through reformulating the question, through saying "I don't get it" to what was offered. The asking and the resisting are happening simultaneously.

The defining signals:

- The client describes wanting help (often having paid for help, often having tried multiple times to get help) and within the same conversation is resisting what the coach is offering
- The client says "I'm trying to understand" or "I'm trying to grasp" when the coach has just said something clear
- The client says "I don't get the question" when the question was answerable
- The client reformulates the coach's offer back as a question the coach now has to answer differently
- The client says they want the help and then says "I'm too tired" or finds another reason to stop the help that is arriving
- The body posture is open and seeking and the verbal/conceptual response is refusing
- The pattern produces no movement. Every exchange ends in the same place the prior one started.

The decisive marker: when the coach mirrors the pattern back ("I'm doing this because I'm showing you how you do it"), the client recognizes their own move. The recognition is the data. The pattern was operating without consciousness; the mirror makes it conscious.

## What This Implies

The client is running a configuration developed in childhood for relating to a primary helper-figure (usually a parent, often the father). The original configuration was: ask for what you need, refuse what is offered. The configuration was the only available way for the child to remain in relationship with the parent while preserving an autonomous self. The child needed the parent. The child also could not receive what the parent offered, either because the offer came with terms the child could not accept, or because the offer required submission to an authority the child needed to resist to stay intact, or because the offer was in a register that did not match what the child actually wanted.

In adulthood, the configuration projects the parent onto every available helper. The marketers, the previous coaches, the current coach, the friends, the partner, the spiritual figure — all of them become the parent in the client's body. The asking-and-resisting fires automatically in any relationship where help is on offer. The client experiences the failure of each help-attempt as the helper's deficiency. From the outside, the failures are a structural feature of the configuration operating across helpers.

The configuration's persistence is what produces the chronic stuckness. The client has been asking-and-resisting for years across many help-attempts and is now in the felt position of "nothing works, no one can help me." The position is structurally produced by the configuration. The configuration is structurally produced by the unfelt original want underneath, which is usually something like "to be loved and seen" — what the parent could not deliver and what the asking-and-resisting has been substituting for.

The configuration appearing in the coaching room is not an obstacle to the work; it is the work. The session is the first opportunity in years where the configuration can be made visible while it is firing, in the presence of a coach who is not the parent and is not going to be deflected by the resistance. The live demonstration is the leverage.

This is [[trauma-is-present-experienced-as-past]] operating relationally. The client is not actually relating to the coach; the client is relating to the original parent, projected onto the coach. The same resistance the client performed toward the parent at age 5 is being performed toward the coach in the session. The body cannot tell the difference; the configuration is firing as if the original scene were still in progress.

## What Joe Does

Name the live pattern. "You want help and you don't want help. That's all we have to focus on. What's going on there?" The naming does not analyze the pattern abstractly; it points at what is happening in the room right now. The client cannot dispute it because the evidence is the exchange that just occurred.

If the client deflects into confusion ("I don't get the question"), Joe stays with the live event rather than re-explaining. "This thing that you're dealing with in this moment is the thing to be addressed." The naming refuses to follow the deflection into more content. The configuration uses content-generation as a defense; Joe does not feed the configuration.

Perform the pattern back at the client. "I'm mimicking you right now, so you know what's creating that." The behavioral mirror makes the pattern visible from the outside. The client can now see what they have been doing because the coach has done it at them. See [[do-the-deflection-back-to-show-them-how-they-do-it]] for the broader form of this move.

Refuse to answer the inevitable "so how do I stop?" question. "I can't tell you because whatever I say you're going to resist. Anything I say, you're going to resist." The refusal is not a withholding; it is a structural recognition that the question is the configuration firing in question-form. See [[refuse-the-answer-because-it-will-be-resisted]].

Locate the parent in the projection. "Do you have parents who constantly were trying to help you get to where they wanted you to go and you constantly resisted them?" The question is offered as hypothesis, not as accusation. When the client confirms ("yeah, I resisted my dad all my life"), Joe makes the projection explicit. "So your marketers are your dad. I'm your dad right now. And you're resisting our help."

The naming of the projection is the inflection point. Once the client sees that they are not actually relating to the coach but to the projected father, the resistance loses its grip. The current coach is not the father. The current marketers are not the father. The configuration that was firing automatically can now be seen as historical material being applied to a present situation that does not match it.

Find the original want underneath. "What's the game you wanted to play with your dad? Not the game that was given to you, but the game that you wanted to play in your heart." The question asks the client to feel what the resistance has been substituting for. The answer is usually something like "to be loved and seen." The want is the structural material the rest of the work will route through.

## Dialogue Examples

From the source session, the early naming of the live pattern:

- **Client:** "I'm stuck and I don't know how to unstuck myself."
- **Joe:** "Stuck in what?"
- **Client:** "I can't move forward. I don't see anything forward. I'm feeling hopeless, suicidal."
- **Joe:** "Um, did you say hopeless and suicidal? Okay, because failed at a business?"
- **Client:** "No, because I've been trying for a very long time, and life hasn't quite moved forward for me. I feel like I'm in the same position that I've been in last 10 years."
- *(later, after client describes wanting to help others and needing help himself)*
- **Joe:** "So it's an interesting thing that I'm seeing in your situation, which is that like on one level you say, I can't, so I need to be helped, and on another level you want to be able to help everybody. So what's happening there?"
- **Client:** "I, I didn't get the question."
- **Joe:** "How is that not the thing that's stopping you? One part of you is like, I want to be helped, and it's going in one direction, and one part of you is like, I want to help, and it's going in a different direction, and it's creating stagnation in your life."

The exchange shows the read in action. The client asks for help with stuckness, immediately deflects the coach's first naming ("I don't get the question"), and the coach stays with the live event rather than re-explaining the content.

The behavioral mirror:

- **Client:** "Okay. I don't see how though. That's where—"
- **Joe:** "So, you want help and you don't want help. That's all we have to focus on."
- *(after client deflects again into describing the marketing problem)*
- **Joe:** "This thing that you're dealing with in this moment is the thing to be addressed. It's not, how do I move my life forward? It's like, what's creating this? I'm mimicking you right now, so you know what's creating that."

The mirror lands when the client begins to confirm rather than to deflect.

The projection-naming:

- **Joe:** "Do you have parents who constantly were trying to help you get to where they wanted you to go and you constantly resisted them?"
- **Client:** "Yeah, I resisted. Yeah, I resisted my dad all my life."
- **Joe:** "Yeah, great. So your marketers are your dad. I'm your dad right now. And you're resisting our help. You see it?"
- **Client:** "Yeah. Okay."
- **Joe:** "You constantly resisted your dad and that relationship didn't develop. Now you've switched dad to life and you're constantly resisting that and it's not developing."

*(Source: She's Been Stuck for 10 Years)*

The "you switched dad to life" sentence is the structural compression: the original parent-relationship was the template, and every subsequent helper-relationship has been the parent re-projected. The 10 years of stuckness are the cost of running the projection unconsciously.

## Tone

The naming is direct, not soft. The client has been getting soft treatment from helpers for years and has been resisting all of it. A softer naming would feed the same configuration that has been operating. Joe's tone in the source session is firm and a little fast — the speed itself is part of the move, refusing to let the configuration use the slow back-and-forth to install another delay loop.

The directness is also warm. Not warm as in "I'm being gentle"; warm as in "I am present with you and I am not going anywhere." The body of the coach has to communicate that the resistance is not going to drive the coach away. The configuration was developed against a parent who responded to resistance by giving up or by escalating; the coach has to be the figure who does neither.

When the client begins to see the pattern, the tone softens. The naming of "to be loved and seen" is delivered with care. The receiving of God's love in the room is met with appreciation: "I'm watching you actually accept his love right now. You're actually playing the game you wanted to play as a kid." The shift in tone marks the shift in what is happening in the room.

## Distinction from Related Reads

- **[[the-trying-is-the-pattern-firing-now]]** — the trying-to-grasp variant of this read. Trying-is-the-pattern is the broader principle; wants-help-resists-help-in-real-time is the specific configuration where the trying is operating in service of resistance to the help being offered. The trying-to-understand is one of the forms the resistance takes in this configuration.

- **[[perpetrator-projection-onto-the-coach]]** — the related but distinct read where the coach becomes the trauma-figure so the held rage has a target. The perpetrator-projection is about giving the rage a place to land. The wants-help-resists-help configuration is about preventing the help from landing. Different mechanisms, different responses. Both involve projection of an original figure onto the coach; the work after the projection is named is different.

- **[[double-checking-help]]** — the pattern article for how self-reliant people sabotage help that arrives. The double-checking variant operates after the help has been delivered; the wants-help-resists-help configuration operates during the offer itself. The wants-help-resists-help client may also double-check, but the primary failure point is upstream — the help cannot land in the first place because the offering is being resisted as it is being made.

- **[[the-answer-is-already-known]]** — the read where the client has the answer they're searching for. This is sometimes a downstream phase of the wants-help-resists-help configuration — once the resistance has loosened, the client often turns out to have known the answer all along. The deferred recognition is part of what the resistance has been preventing.

## What to Look For That Confirms the Read Is Landing

- The client stops formulating new questions and begins to track the live exchange
- The client says some version of "I see it" or "yeah" without immediately pivoting to a new content question
- The body shifts — softening, breathing, dropping a held posture
- The client names the original parent without prompting
- The client begins to feel what the resistance has been substituting for (often longing, often the original want)
- The client's energy shifts from refusing-the-offer to receiving-something

If none of these arrive after the naming and the mirror have been deployed, the configuration is heavier than this single session can address. The work then becomes about honoring the configuration's intelligence (it kept the client alive in the original scene) rather than trying to dissolve it in one pass. The next session can pick up the work; what matters in this session is that the configuration has been seen.
