# Heat or Tension in the Body as Resistance Data

## What Joe Notices

The client is talking about a topic — wanting to do X, trying to ask for Y, hesitating to commit to Z — and their body is responding. The response is usually:

- Heat in the chest, face, body. "I feel a lot of heat right now in my body."
- Tension. Bracing. Tightening of the jaw, shoulders, gut.
- A shift the client may not name themselves: looking away, closing the eyes, going quiet, eyes filling.
- A reportable felt-sense that the client surfaces when asked directly: "what's happening to you right now?"

The body is responding to the topic with activation that is structurally identical to the activation it produces when actually doing the thing. The talking about it is producing the same body-state as the doing of it. The body is treating the conversation as the threat the conversation is about.

## What This Implies

The body's activation is the resistance speaking in its own language. The body knows what the mind is hedging about. The mind can produce a fluent narrative about why the ask is hard, why the resistance is there, what the historical roots are. The body is producing the present-tense felt fact of the resistance, right now, in this conversation.

The structural implication: the work cannot proceed through the mind alone, because the mind has been organizing around the resistance for years. The body is the access point. The activation the body is producing is exactly what the work needs to engage.

A second implication: the body's activation in the conversation IS the activation that prevents the asking. The same heat, the same tension, the same bracing. The client is currently in the resistance, not just talking about it. The session can use the present-tense version directly rather than working with a remembered or imagined version.

This is also a cross-domain read. If the client reports the resistance shows up in sports, in childhood, in dating, in business — and the body is bracing right now in the coaching conversation — then the resistance is structural, not situational. The same body-pattern is firing across contexts. The work is on the pattern, not on the specific business situation the client presented.

## What Joe Does

- **Name what's happening.** "What's happening to you right now? It seems like I'm asking you questions but it seems like — what's going on?" The naming makes the body's activation a participant in the conversation. The client has to acknowledge what the body is doing.

- **Don't move past it.** The activation is the data. Don't redirect to the narrative. Don't ask for more story. Stay with what the body is doing.

- **Use the body location as the entry to the part work.** "I'm going to talk to the resistance" — and the resistance has an address: the heat, the tension, the place in the body. The body location anchors the parts work that follows. See [[answer-as-the-resistance]] for the move that uses the body activation as the access point.

- **Trace the body pattern historically (briefly).** "When you were playing tennis, did your body do this same thing? Yeah? That's the same kind of resistance that's happening right now." The historical trace confirms the body-pattern is cross-domain. Don't spend long here; the point is the recognition.

- **Treat the body activation as the conversation's center of gravity.** The verbal content is secondary. The body is leading the work. The coach matches the body's pace — slower when the body is in deep activation, more spacious when the body is opening.

- **Use the body's response as the diagnostic for whether the work is landing.** When the body shifts (softens, opens, releases), the work has done something. When the body stays braced, more work is needed. The body is the primary signal, not the verbal report.

## Dialogue Examples

The source session shows the read in action:

- **Client:** "I feel a lot of heat right now in my body."
- **Joe:** "Okay."
- *(later)*
- **Joe:** "When you were playing tennis, did you have a hard time going after what you wanted? Like, you're on the court. Did you have a hard time going after what you wanted?"
- **Client:** "My body would tense up, but, um, I, I gave it my all."
- **Joe:** "So even there, like if you were being fully aggressive in your wants on the tennis court, you would have some resistance. The same kind of resistance that's happening right now."
- *(Source: Why You Can't Ask for What You Want)*

The "same kind of resistance that's happening right now" is the load-bearing observation. The historical body-pattern and the present-tense body-pattern are the same. The current activation is the access point to working the pattern.

## Tone

Direct but quiet. The read is delivered without drama. "What's happening to you right now?" is asked with curiosity, not concern. The coach is not alarmed by the body's activation — the activation is the data, and the coach is glad to have access to it.

When the client reports the heat or tension, the coach receives it without fanfare. "Okay." The minimal acknowledgment lets the body know it has been heard without making a production of it. Making a production would activate the body further; the minimal acknowledgment lets the body stay present.

The historical trace is delivered with the same quiet directness. "Did your body do this in tennis?" The question is matter-of-fact. The coach is not investigating; the coach is confirming a pattern.

## Distinction From Other Body Reads

This read is specifically about the body's activation during a conversation about a topic the client is resistant to. It is different from:

- [[body-language-of-someone-sharing-trauma]] — which is about the body's protective posture during trauma-sharing, where the body is recovering from past activation
- The post-trigger body read — where the body is settling after a fight or rupture
- The arousal-as-want body read — where the body's activation is signaling desire, not resistance

The diagnostic for this read: is the body bracing or activating in a way that matches what the body does when the actual action (asking, committing, going for it) is attempted? If yes, this is the resistance read. The body is doing the resistance now, in the conversation, the same way it does the resistance in the feared action.
