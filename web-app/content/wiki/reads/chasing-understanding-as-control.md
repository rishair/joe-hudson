# Chasing Understanding as Control

## What Joe Notices

The person is being misunderstood and is responding to the misunderstanding by adding more words, more careful framings, more elaborate explanations, more emotional emphasis. The mechanics look like care and effort. The receiver experiences it as pressure.

- The person uses phrases like "I'm trying to communicate with someone," "I'm making every effort," "I'm using the best communication hygiene I possibly can."
- The person frames their effort as proof of good faith: "if I'm falling all over myself, I'm at least still trying."
- The person admits, often within the first few exchanges, that the chasing is "the illusion of control." The word *illusion* arrives because the person already knows.
- The pace and density of the explanation increases when the person senses they are not being received. The body tightens. The voice presses.
- The person can describe doing this across multiple domains — work, partner, family — without yet seeing themselves as the constant.

The somatic signature is forward-leaning. The body of the speaker is reaching across the gap to install the meaning in the listener's head. The listener's body reads this and pulls back. The speaker reads the pull-back as further misunderstanding and pushes harder. The loop closes.

## What This Implies

The reach for understanding is a control move dressed as clarity. Joe is direct about the mechanism: the more you chase being understood, the more controlled the listener feels, and the less they listen. The intent is to be heard; the effect is to install a meaning in someone else's nervous system. The listener registers the install attempt and resists.

It is also a self-abandonment move. To monitor whether your speech is landing correctly in someone else, you have to be in their nervous system, not yours. The speaker leaves their own body. The speech that arrives at the listener is delivered from a meta-position — not from the speaker themselves but from the speaker's anxious model of how the speaker is being received. That speech feels different to the listener even when the words are good. It feels like someone reaching across the table to control rather than someone speaking from where they are.

The control-for-understanding pattern is a special case of [[wanting-others-to-listen-is-the-block]]. The fix is the same: stop chasing, start listening to yourself.

## What Joe Does

The path is a live demonstration, not an explanation. Joe sets up a scenario in the session where the person can feel the dynamic directly.

- **Ask the cost question first.** "What would happen if you didn't fall all over yourself to try to get them to understand you?" The person discovers the anxiety underneath. They name it ("a panic room of concrete blocks of anxiety"). The naming is part of the work.
- **Surface the illusion-of-control.** Reflect back what the person has just said. "If you fall all over yourself, what does that get you?" The person almost always says some version of "the illusion of control." Once the word is in the room, the move can be addressed.
- **Invert the responsibility.** "What makes it your job to be understood? What makes it not my job to try to understand you?" The reframe lands fast. The person was carrying a job that is not actually theirs.
- **Run the [[say-the-same-sentence-five-ways]] move.** Have them say the same sentence from different states — armor, ego-protected, vulnerable, and so on. The person hears how the same words land differently depending on the state. The chasing becomes audible.
- **Bring it back to self-listening.** "Listen to yourself 10% more while you say it." The chasing dissolves. The person becomes audible to themselves and, downstream, to the listener.

What Joe does NOT do: give communication tips, suggest better phrasings, or validate the framing that the listener is the problem. The chasing is the problem. The fix is to stop the chasing, which requires bringing attention back inside the speaker.

## Dialogue Examples

A compressed exchange from the session that demonstrates the read and the move.

- **Savannah:** "If I'm falling all over myself, I'm at least still trying. So that still gives me the illusion of control or a sense of being able to impact the situation."
- **Joe:** "What makes it your job to be understood? What makes it not my job to try to understand you?"
- **Savannah:** "Well, I feel like it's my job to make sure that I'm expressing myself in a way that's clear."
- **Joe:** "Clear to who? ... I might communicate something in a way that's clear to 20% of the people, unclear to 80%. ... How much control do you actually have over people's capacity to understand you?"
- *(Source: 2024-04-12 How Do I Stop Being Misunderstood?)*

The intervention is not an instruction. It is a reframe that lets the person see how much of their effort is being spent on a target they cannot control.

A later exchange shows what Joe is reading when the chasing happens in the room:

- **Joe:** "Yeah, intellectually, that's where you are. The way it's being received is someone's trying to control me."
- **Savannah:** "Yeah."
- **Joe:** "And when someone controls you, how do you respond?"
- **Savannah:** "I hate that. ... I listen differently when someone's trying to control me. I listen in ways to figure out what they're actually trying to get rather than what is actually happening."
- *(Source: 2024-04-12 How Do I Stop Being Misunderstood?)*

This is the loop made visible. The person discovers, in her own words, what the listener does when controlled. The understanding does not arrive as Joe's claim. It arrives as her own report.

## Tone

Direct. Slightly playful. Joe matches the energy of the chase without absorbing it. He is willing to push on the question — "you clearly don't [have control over their understanding], though, right?" — and he does it with warmth, not with judgment. The bluntness is the medicine; the warmth is what makes the bluntness land instead of activating defense.

There is also a willingness to be the test case. "Okay, let's just assume I had no idea what you just said. I don't fucking understand at all the thing that you just said. I want you to make it my responsibility to understand you. How would you do that?" The willingness to be the difficult listener in the room makes the demonstration possible.
