# Abandoning Self While Speaking

## What Joe Notices

The person is talking, but they are not in themselves while talking. Their attention is at a meta-position outside their body, monitoring how the speech is landing in the listener. The body of the speaker has been vacated. The speech still comes out, but it is coming from the monitor, not from the speaker.

Specific signals:

- The pace and density of speech are higher than the content requires. The speaker is producing speech faster than they are processing what they are saying. They are not the first audience for their own words.
- The eyes track the listener for confirmation. Each phrase ends with a slight question — *did that land?*
- The body is forward-leaning, slightly clenched, and the breath is shallow. The somatic anchor is in the listener's nervous system, not the speaker's.
- The person admits, when asked, "I have a tendency to fully abandon myself when I'm interacting with someone else." The person knows. The knowing is preverbal.
- When the speaker is asked to listen to themselves 10% more while speaking, the speech immediately becomes more present, more grounded, and more audible. The shift is dramatic and fast. The person did not need a technique; they needed permission and a percentage.
- The speaker may "go into their head" for an answer when asked a feeling question, then catch themselves and produce the body answer when prompted.

This read is distinct from [[in-their-story]]. In their story, the person is in a narrative; the attention is on what happened. In abandoning-self-while-speaking, the person is not in a narrative — they may be answering a present-tense question — but they are not located in themselves while answering. The signature is the absence, not the content.

## What This Implies

A person who is not in themselves while speaking cannot be heard. Not because the listener will not hear them, but because there is no *them* arriving for the listener to receive. The words come from a place adjacent to the speaker. The listener registers this absence and either drifts (no one is here to listen to) or feels managed (someone is trying to install something).

The speaker's complaint of not being heard or not being understood is often the felt experience of not being in themselves while being heard. The other person cannot supply the contact that the speaker is failing to make with themselves. Even when the listener does receive, the speaker cannot register the receiving — because the speaker is not located where the receiving would land.

This read is the bridge between the surface concern ("they don't understand me") and the deeper teaching ("listen to yourself"). The path is not to convince the speaker to communicate better. The path is to bring the speaker back into themselves, then watch what happens to the conversation.

The deepest implication: a person who abandons themselves while speaking has usually been trained to do so. The training comes from childhood environments where the speaker's own state was less important than managing the state of an unpredictable other. The pattern is competent — it accomplished what it needed to accomplish at age 6 — and now is producing the very disconnection the adult is reporting. See [[how-you-related-to-parents-is-how-you-relate-to-god-and-money]] for the general principle of childhood templates persisting; the specific imprint here is "my happiness depends on managing your reaction." See also [[childhood-imprinting]].

## What Joe Does

The primary move is to bring the speaker back into themselves, in real time, with the smallest possible intervention.

- **Ask them to listen to themselves 10% more.** Not 100%. Not "be in your body." Just 10%. The small number is load-bearing. Most people can find 10% even when they cannot find more. The shift in their speech is immediate and visible. See [[listen-to-yourself-ten-percent-more]].
- **Hand them the inner-ear hack.** "Put your attention in your inner ear." A simple, almost mechanical instruction that produces the somatic shift on demand. See [[put-attention-in-inner-ear]].
- **Have them speak from a body answer, not a head answer.** When the person goes into their head for an answer, name it and redirect. "Instead of going to your head, go into your heart. Whole body for the answer." See [[what-does-your-heart-say]].
- **Catch and call out the disappearance.** "Wait, hold on, still listen to yourself. Like, I'm happy to have the conversation with you, but I'm gonna call you out when you run out and try to manage the world and you're not in yourself." The naming is gentle but firm. Each time the person leaves, name it and invite them back. The repetition is the practice.
- **State the principle.** "By the way, you can't be seen or heard when you're not in yourself. How could I ever see you? You're not you. You're running away from yourself to manage me." See [[you-cant-be-seen-when-youre-not-in-yourself]].

What Joe does NOT do: try to get the person to relax through breath work, give them a meditation practice, or address the childhood imprint up front. The body-level fix has to be available now, in the conversation, before any deeper work can be useful. The 10% shift is the door.

## Dialogue Examples

The move where Joe brings the speaker back in is small and fast.

- **Savannah:** "So I have a tendency to fully abandon myself when I'm interacting with someone else because I want them to—"
- **Joe:** "Great. That's me again. Listen to yourself just 10% more. Same thing. Just, but listen to yourself 10% more as you're saying it."
- **Savannah:** "Which thing? The thing I just said about having tendency? Yeah. Okay."
- **Joe:** "Actually, I don't give a shit what you say. You can just say it better. Just yourself 10% more."
- **Savannah:** "I have a tendency to fully abandon myself when I'm trying to interact or make sure that I'm understood in communication."
- *(Source: 2024-04-12 How Do I Stop Being Misunderstood?)*

The first version was a performance of self-awareness. The second version, after the 10% redirect, was the same content delivered from inside herself. The difference was audible.

A later moment showed Joe holding the line when the person tried to leave again:

- **Joe:** "Wait, hold on, still listen to yourself. ... Whatever you want to say from the place of being in yourself, because now I'm with you. You can't— by the way, you can't fucking be seen or heard when you're not in yourself. How could I ever see you? You're not you. You're running away from yourself to manage me."
- *(Source: 2024-04-12 How Do I Stop Being Misunderstood?)*

The metaphor is exact. The speaker is "running away from yourself to manage me." Once the metaphor lands, the speaker can feel the absurdity of being upset that no one sees the person who is not there.

## Tone

Warm but uncompromising. Joe does not let the person slip back out without naming it. The naming itself carries care — the underlying message is "I want you here, not over there" — but the firmness is required. Without the firmness, the person's habit of leaving would keep winning.

When the person makes the shift, Joe acknowledges it immediately and specifically. "How hard was that?" "Did you see how it just happened naturally?" The acknowledgment is part of the wiring — the body is learning that being-in-itself is welcome and noticed.
