# The Part the Person Pulls Back From Is the Part They Fear Can't Be Loved

## What Joe Notices

When a person is invited to scan their body for the part of themselves that cannot be loved (or to say something like "I want all of me loved"), the body almost always produces a specific retreat at a specific moment. The eyes shift. The breath shortens. The body draws back. Something in them turns away from a specific image.

The retreat happens fast. It is easy to miss. Joe watches for it during scans, during welcoming statements, during any move that asks the person to extend love to all of themselves. The retreat is the data.

The retreat is not generic resistance. It is targeted. The body is turning away from a specific part of the self. That specific part — the one the body refuses to look at — is the part the person is scared can't be loved. It is the part they hide.

Observable signals during the retreat:
- A subtle backward shift in the torso or head
- Eyes drop or look away
- A small wince
- A held breath or quickened breath
- A pause where the body was previously moving
- The voice gets smaller or stops

Joe will sometimes say something like "Oh, look what just happened. Where'd you go?" The naming is the move. Once named, the person can usually feel the retreat themselves and locate what they were retreating from.

## What This Implies

The retreat is the body running a defense that was installed long ago. The defense says: do not let love near this part, because last time love met this part, love withdrew. The part is usually something the person was made to feel ashamed of as a child — wanting too much, being cunning, being angry, being needy, being independent, being scared. The exact content varies. The mechanism is the same.

The retreat also means the work is now available. The defense is firing in real time, in the room, where it can be met. Before the retreat, the part was hidden behind a story ("I'm fake" or "I'm corrupt" or "I'm too much"). After the retreat is named, the part is locatable. The story was a screen. The retreat lifted the screen.

The implication for the AI coach: when the person names a self-judgment ("I'm fake," "I'm too much," "I'm cold," "I'm cunning"), the work is not to dispute the judgment. The work is to invite a body scan or a self-welcoming statement and watch for what the body refuses to extend love to. That refused thing is the actual material.

See [[childhood-imprinting]] for the developmental mechanism. See [[loving-all-parts]] for the broader frame.

## What Joe Does

- Names the retreat in real time. "Look what just happened. Where did you retreat into? Where'd you go? You just pulled back." See [[name-the-body-defense-in-real-time]].
- Sends the person back to the part they retreated from. "Go to the thing you pulled away from. That's the part that you're scared can't be loved. That's the part you hide."
- Once the person can name the part (cunning, wanting, etc.), Joe reframes it. The reframe is specific — a 5-year-old version of the part, the universal-among-children version of the part. "Can you imagine a 5-year-old girl who is cunning? I don't know any."
- Traces the wiring to the childhood imprint. "Of course, because your parents — every time you wanted something, your parents felt guilty they couldn't give it to you. So they didn't want to feel it." See [[wanting-got-wired-to-unlovability]].
- Watches for the body coming back online once the part has been met. The signal: the person stops dissociating, the body squares up, the breath drops. Joe names that too: "There you are, whole body you."

## Dialogue Examples

The clearest example is from the "Am I A Fake?" session:

- **Joe:** "Will you do me a favor and sit squarely with both feet on the ground. Bring your attention all the way down. Eyes open. There it is. I'd love to hear you just say: I want all of me loved."
- **Guest:** "I want all of me loved."
- **Joe:** "I want all of you loved too, by the way."
- **Guest:** "Thank you."
- **Joe:** "Take a second to scan your body and see what part of you can't be loved in your mind, in your heart. Any part of you? Oh, look what just happened. That was amazing. Where did you retreat into? Where'd you go? You just pulled back."
- **Guest:** "It was a doubt. Yes."
- **Joe:** "Yeah, so when you pulled back, the thing you pulled away from — go to the thing you pulled away from. That's the part that you're scared can't be loved. That's the part you hide. Oh yeah, there you are. There you went."
- **Guest:** "I can be very cunning, and I did not love that part. Yeah, it was like a contradiction for me."
- **Joe:** "Can you imagine a 5-year-old girl who is cunning? I don't know any. I love my girls' cunningness."
- *(Source: Am I A Fake?)*

The session moves from "I'm fake" (a story) to "I can be very cunning" (the actual hidden part) in three turns, entirely through watching the body retreat and naming it.

## Tone

Quiet, present, fast. Joe does not announce that he is watching the body. He simply notices and names, often with a small surprise in his voice ("Oh, look what just happened. That was amazing"). The tone communicates that the retreat is interesting, not bad — something to be curious about, not something to be cured.

When the part is finally named, Joe's tone shifts to warm and matter-of-fact. The reframe of the "shameful" part (cunning, wanting) is delivered as obvious, as if everyone already knows children are cunning, as if there is no big deal here.
