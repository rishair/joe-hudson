# Every Question They Ask Becomes 'What's Wrong With Me?'

## What Joe Notices

The person asks questions about an outer thing (money, work, the relationship, the body, the project). On the surface the questions are about the outer thing. But each question, when listened to closely, has the same shape underneath: "what is wrong with me that this is happening?"

The signature is invariance under topic change. The topic of the question moves — money, then work, then the relationship — but the underlying structure of the question stays identical. Each question is the deficiency-investigation in different costume. The person is not asking different questions; they are asking the same question about themselves over and over, using whatever surface topic the moment makes available.

"Every question that you ask is, what's wrong with me? Yeah, you will turn any question into some version of, what's wrong with me." -- Joe Hudson, You Don't Have A Money Problem

Specific signals to listen for:

- The questions all have the same emotional weight regardless of how different the surface topics are
- The person rejects available evidence that does not confirm the deficiency (the room contains the computer, the rent, the trip — and the person responds with "but I'm always in debt")
- The person produces additional grievances to maintain the deficiency-frame when the original is dissolved (when "I have no money" is dissolved, "I tried to build my business and it doesn't get traction" arrives to take its place)
- Any positive content the person produces is immediately followed by a qualification that converts it back to deficiency ("I moved to Switzerland with no money and got a job" → "Oh, there's a lot of stress involved")
- The person uses "but" and "still" frequently to negate evidence against the deficiency-frame

## What This Implies

The questions are not actually questions. They are the inner critic running in interrogative form. The lens is so total that the person cannot see the lens — they think they are inquiring about their life. What is happening is the lens producing one piece of evidence after another that confirms the lens itself.

The interrogative form is what makes the pattern hard to see. If the person were saying "I'm not good enough" the criticism would be audible as criticism. The interrogative ("why am I not getting what I want?") sounds like genuine curiosity. It is the same criticism, dressed in inquiry.

This means the surface questions cannot be answered helpfully. Answering them as if they were genuine questions feeds the deficiency-frame, because each answer becomes additional content the lens converts into "see, even the answer confirms there's something wrong with me." The person who is "asking the same question in multiple ways" is not seeking an answer. They are seeking confirmation of the lens. Helpful answers fail to satisfy because they were never being asked for.

The work, therefore, is not to answer the questions. The work is to name the lens. Once the lens is visible to the person, the questions stop being able to do their old work. The person can begin to notice when they are running the lens and when they are not.

## What Joe Does

The intervention is structural, not content-based. Joe does not engage the surface questions on their merits. He names the lens directly.

**First, he answers the surface question quickly and structurally rather than investigatively.** When the client asked "why is the money flow blocked?", Joe surfaced that the money flow was not blocked (the client had bought a phone, was paying rent, had gotten to Switzerland). The surface question dissolved in one move. The dissolution was not the work — it was the demonstration that the surface question was not the actual subject.

**Second, he names that the surface question was the wrong question.** "There's no blockage. So you've been asking the wrong question." This is the pivot from content to structure.

**Third, when the person produces additional questions to refill the deficiency-frame, he names the pattern.** "So you're asking me the same question in multiple ways." The repetition under topic change becomes the data. The person has been asking the same question — about themselves — for years.

**Fourth, he names the lens directly.** "Every question that you ask is, what's wrong with me? You will turn any question into some version of, what's wrong with me."

**Fifth, he uses the live data of the session to confirm the lens.** When the client produced a positive statement ("I moved to Switzerland with no money and got a job") and immediately qualified it ("Oh, there's a lot of stress involved"), Joe caught the qualification in real time: "Completely antithetical to every question that you've asked me so far... Wait, see, even that — hold on a second — even that, there's a lot of stress involved. Everything — you're seeing everything through this lens."

The naming uses the person's own utterances as the evidence. The lens is demonstrated in the moment, not described abstractly. The client confirms: "Everything through the lens that I'm not good enough."

## What This Reveals About the Underneath

When the person sees the lens, the question shifts to what the lens has been doing. It has been generating questions that cannot be answered, because the answer to "what's wrong with me?" is "nothing" — and the lens does not accept that answer. The lens needs the answer to be deficiency, so it keeps producing new questions until it finds one whose answer feels like deficiency.

The engine underneath: the person cannot confirm themselves. Without self-confirmation, the question of okayness has to be asked of an external arbiter — the money, the audience, the partner, the body. When the external arbiter does not deliver, the lens converts the non-delivery into evidence of deficiency. The loop is closed: the absence of internal confirmation produces the external-confirmation-seeking, which produces the deficiency-lens, which produces the questions, which produce the absence of answers, which fuels the lens.

See [[youll-never-get-the-confirmation-from-others]] for the structural impossibility that locks the loop. See [[chase-your-own-approval]] for the inversion that breaks it.

## Dialogue Example

- **Client:** "Why is the money flow blocked? Why is the money flowing to me blocked? I don't know."
- **Joe:** "Am I doing it? It clearly isn't... Is that a computer you're using to talk to me? Did you buy it? How did you get the computer?"
- **Client:** [accounts for the computer, the rent, the trip] "It feels like it's just enough, like I'm always in debt. I can never get over zero."
- **Joe:** "Hold on, no, no, the question was how is the money blocked? The answer is it's not."
- **Client:** "Okay. So why am I still in debt after—"
- **Joe:** "There's a good question."
- **Client:** "Like when I get a lot at once, I spend it."
- **Joe:** "Well, that's why you're in debt. Cool, now you've answered that question. What's the next question?"
- **Client:** "Why is it when I tried to get clients, I tried to build my business, it just doesn't get traction."
- **Joe:** "So you're asking me the same question in multiple ways. Yeah, because you've had a client. Yeah, you just haven't had enough. You have money, but when you have enough, you spend it. I don't have blockage. I do have blockage. What's the question? You've been asking this question for years. You have all these clues. You've been spending money on these clues."

[Later, after the actual question has surfaced and a positive thing is named]

- **Joe:** "So can you find one thing to confirm about yourself? One thing that you can say is pretty fucking awesome about you?"
- **Client:** "I moved to Switzerland with no money and got a job."
- **Joe:** "Completely antithetical to every question that you've asked me so far."
- **Client:** "Oh, there's a lot of stress involved."
- **Joe:** "Yeah, stress is involved. That's what happens in life... Wait, see, even that — hold on a second — even that, there's a lot of stress involved... Everything — you're seeing everything through this lens."
- **Client:** "Everything through the lens that I'm not good enough."

## Tone

Direct, increasingly firm as the pattern repeats. The first surface question is met with a quick structural answer that dissolves the question. When the second question appears in the same form, Joe names the repetition: "you're asking me the same question in multiple ways." When the third appears, the directness escalates: "you've been asking this question for years."

The escalation is not impatience. It is the firmness required to make the pattern audible to a person whose lens has been running for so long that the pattern is invisible. A softer delivery would let the pattern continue. The directness closes the escape routes.

The tone shifts to warmth once the lens is named and the client recognizes it. The work that follows — finding one thing to confirm about themselves, giving compassion to the part being criticized — is delivered gently. The directness was to surface the lens. The warmth is for the part of self that has been living under the lens.
