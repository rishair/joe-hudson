# Fixing as Avoidance of Presence

## What Joe Notices

The person is in the room with Joe and is doing everything except being there. They are going into their head to find the right answer. They are reaching to fix Joe (offering to support him, asking how to help). They are reaching to fix themselves (asking what's wrong with them, asking how to do this better). They are reframing the question. They are checking whether they are doing it right. Each move is a doing. Each doing has a target. The target is anywhere but the present mutual moment.

Observable signals:

- The person breaks eye contact when an actual moment of meeting starts to occur.
- The person asks "how do I do this?" instead of doing it.
- The person reformulates the work in their own words instead of staying in the work.
- The person reaches to support or care for the coach when the coach has not requested support.
- The person turns the experience into a learning ("so what I'm getting from this is..." mid-experience).
- The person catches themselves and says "wait, I'm doing it again" — and the noticing is itself another fix-move, not a return to presence.
- The person becomes briefly anxious when the fix-options are exhausted and presence is the only available move.

The read often surfaces in the middle of a session after a moment of actual contact has occurred. The contact happens; the person registers it; the person does something to break it. The breaking is the fix-pattern operating on the contact itself.

"What I'm noticing is everything you're doing is trying to avoid just actually being with me. Like, you go into your head to stop being with me, you go to fix me, you try to go to fix yourself. And then when you're with me, like right here, there's like some anxiety that occurs." -- Joe Hudson, Why Am I Always Trying To Fix Myself & Others?

## What This Implies

The fixing is not a separate behavior from the avoidance of presence. The fixing IS the avoidance. Every direction the fix can take — toward the self, toward the other, toward the world, toward the conversation, toward the question — is a direction away from the present mutual moment. The catalog of fix-targets is, structurally, the catalog of available escapes from being-here.

The person is rarely aware of this. From inside the pattern, the fixing feels productive, virtuous, even caring. The fact that each instance is also a small escape from contact is invisible. The body has been doing this for so long that the equivalence (fix = escape) has become automatic.

The read is informative because it reveals the function of the fixing in a way the moral framing of the fixing hides. The person who is "trying to help" or "trying to make this better" is, in the structural reality, fleeing the moment in which they would have to just be here. The flight is the operative fact. The help is the cover story.

A specific corollary: this read explains why the fixing produces the chronic sense of failure that drives more fixing. See [[everything-i-touch-fails-drives-fixing]]. The fix never reaches the thing it claims to address because the fix is structurally about avoiding the contact in which the actual address would happen. The activity loops.

## What Joe Does

He names the catalog of fix-moves as a catalog of avoidance-moves. The naming has to be specific and current — pointing at the moves the person is making right now, not at a general tendency.

"Everything you're doing is trying to avoid just actually being with me. You go into your head to stop being with me. You go to fix me. You try to go to fix yourself."

The naming is a single sentence with three concrete examples drawn from what the person has just done in the last 90 seconds. The specificity is what makes the naming land. A general "I notice you're avoiding presence" would not produce recognition; the specific three-part catalog (head, fix-me, fix-yourself) is undeniable because the person can recall each move.

Once the naming has landed, Joe immediately re-extends the invitation to the alternative. "And then when you're with me, like right here, there's like some anxiety that occurs." The re-invitation does not require the person to stop fixing first; it just points at the moment when fixing is paused (which is happening right now in the noticing) and names what arises in that moment (the anxiety).

The anxiety is then the next material. See [[everything-i-touch-fails-drives-fixing]] and [[make-the-session-itself-the-demonstration]] for what comes next.

## Why the Catalog Form Matters

The read could be delivered as a single naming: "you're avoiding being with me." The single naming is true but less useful. The catalog form ("you do it three different ways: head, fix-me, fix-yourself") gives the person multiple specific instances to recognize. Each instance is a foothold. Together they form the picture.

The catalog also forecloses the most common defense, which is "but I do this one specific thing for these specific reasons." The person who could defend going-to-the-head as needed cognitive processing cannot also defend going-to-fix-the-coach as caring, also defend going-to-fix-the-self as growth. The three defenses individually might survive; collectively they reveal the pattern as the operative thing, with the moral frames as the variable surface.

The catalog should be drawn from what just happened in the room. The catalog form is alive when the items are recent; it is dead when the items are abstract or hypothetical. The coach has to be watching closely enough to assemble the three items in real time.

## Dialogue Example

The full sequence from the source session, after Joe has invited the woman to be with him with eye contact and no fixing:

- **Guest:** "My first thought is to go to, like, to be here to support each other, which I don't know how that's much different. So you see that sense of—"
- **Joe:** "What I'm noticing is everything you're doing is trying to avoid just actually being with me. Like, you go into your head to stop being with me, you go to fix me, you try to go to fix yourself. And then when you're with me, like right here, there's like some anxiety that occurs."
- **Guest:** "Yeah, I want it— I want it to be right, and I don't know how to get there, and so I feel like I'm failing at it over and over again."
- *(Source: Why Am I Always Trying To Fix Myself & Others?)*

The read produced immediate movement. The woman's response is not defensive; it is a recognition. She moves from the fix-mode into naming what is actually here for her (the want-it-to-be-right and the failure-feeling). The naming is the work. The fix-as-avoidance read has delivered her into the material that the fixing had been holding off.

## What Joe Does NOT Do

He does not list the three items neutrally and stop. He immediately points at what happens when the fixing is briefly absent (the anxiety). The naming is paired with what's-available-once-the-naming-lands. Without the pairing, the naming is just a diagnosis. With the pairing, the naming opens the door to the next move.

He does not turn the read into a lesson about avoidance. There is no "this is a pattern many people have." The read stays specific to this person, in this room, in this minute. The generalization would convert the read into content the person can process intellectually; the specificity keeps it operational.

He does not ask the person to stop fixing. The asking-to-stop would be its own fix-instruction, which would launch another fix-cycle. The work is to name what is happening and then go into what's underneath. The fixing will drop on its own when the underneath is met.

## Tone

Direct, warm, slightly amused. The catalog is delivered like a friend pointing out something obvious that the person has not been able to see. "You go to your head, you go to fix me, you go to fix yourself" has a small lift in it — the lift of seeing something clearly for the first time.

The warmth is what makes the directness landable. Without the warmth, the naming would feel like a critique. With the warmth, it feels like an observation made by someone who is on the person's side. The person can receive the observation without defending.

The tempo is quick. The naming happens in one breath, followed immediately by the pivot to the underneath material. A slow, weighted delivery would convert the read into a teaching moment, which is exactly the kind of intellectualizing the read is naming.
