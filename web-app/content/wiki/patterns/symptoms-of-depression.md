# The Symptoms of Depression

## The Pattern

A specific cluster of experiences shows up in depression. Each is a symptom -- a downstream effect of one or more of the four causes (negative self-talk, repressed emotion, depleted nervous system, isolation). Each is something the depressed person notices and often tries to fix directly. Direct treatment of the symptoms produces partial relief at best. See [[symptoms-vs-root-causes-of-depression]] for why.

The diagnostic value of the symptom list is recognition, not intervention. When several of these appear together, depression is likely operating, and the work moves to addressing the four-layer causes (see [[depression-four-levels]]).

## The Symptoms

### 1. Negative Self-Talk That Cannot Be Trusted on Its Own Terms

A [[voice-in-the-head]] running constantly with self-attack. Three signatures distinguish depressive self-talk from ordinary self-criticism:

- **Automatic.** It runs without being prompted. It does not arise from a specific situation; it arrives at all situations.
- **Not based on data.** When examined, the underlying claims are weak. See [[untruth-in-every-thought]].
- **Contradictory.** The voice says you talked too much. The voice says you didn't speak enough. Same situation. Both delivered with confidence.

"They're automatic, that they're not based on data, and they contradict each other... and they're constantly attacking you instead of actually trying to adjust. Like, the voice in the head isn't trying to adjust itself, it's trying to adjust you, which is abusive." -- Joe Hudson, Exploring Depression in the Head, Heart, and Gut

### 2. Pessimism

A pervasive expectation that things will not work out. Not a considered evaluation of specific situations. A baseline view of the future that colors all assessments.

### 3. Forever Thinking

The conviction that the current state will never end.

- "It's never going to change."
- "It's always going to be like this."
- "How am I ever going to get out of it?"

The forever-thinking is itself a form of the voice running on selective data. See [[symptoms-vs-root-causes-of-depression]] on selective data.

### 4. The Feeling of Being Stuck

A felt sense that movement is not available. Not a specific obstacle that could be addressed. A global immobilization. Often this is repressed anger producing the feeling. See [[trappedness-feeling]] and [[three-forms-of-anger-repression]] Form 1.

### 5. Soul Dysmorphia

Inability to see yourself clearly. Friends report you are great; you cannot believe it. See [[soul-dysmorphia]] for full treatment.

"You have a soul dysmorphia, you can't see yourself clearly. You have friends telling you how awesome you are and you can't believe it." -- Joe Hudson, Exploring Depression in the Head, Heart, and Gut

### 6. Short Attention Span

Difficulty focusing. Thoughts pull away from what is in front of you. This is partly the voice consuming bandwidth, partly the nervous system in scanning mode, partly fatigue.

### 7. Adrenal Fatigue

Low energy that cannot be addressed with more rest or with stimulants. The body has been running on stress for a long time and is in a recovery state that gets interrupted by the shoulds the person uses to try to push through. See [[nervous-system-recovery-after-stress]].

Symptoms include: "I don't have the energy to do something." "I don't want to do this." "I'm tired all the time but can't sleep." See [[exhaustion-is-unexpressed-anger]] for the related mechanism where exhaustion is also repressed anger.

### 8. Treating Yourself Differently Than You Treat Others

The depressed person would never speak to a friend the way they speak to themselves. The double standard is one of the cleanest diagnostics.

"You're treating yourself the way you wouldn't treat somebody else typically." -- Joe Hudson, Exploring Depression in the Head, Heart, and Gut

## What These Symptoms Are Not

These symptoms are not personality. They are not character flaws. They are not laziness. They are the visible surface of a multi-layered pattern with mechanical causes.

They are also not the depression itself. Treating any one of them directly (forcing positive thinking, demanding focus, pushing through fatigue) reinforces the underlying pattern by adding more should-pressure on top of what is already depleted. See [[symptoms-vs-root-causes-of-depression]].

## The Diagnostic Use

When the symptoms cluster, depression is likely present. The work is not to address the symptoms; the work is to identify which of the four causes is most accessible right now and enter through that door. See [[depression-four-levels]].
