# Us-vs-Them as a Universal Manipulation Pattern

## The Pattern

The structural recipe of a cult is also the structural recipe of a startup pitch deck, a political campaign, a beauty advertisement, a religious tradition gone wonky, an abusive partner's tactics, and the voice in your own head. The same architecture appears across all these domains:

1. **A good guy.** Us. The ones who see, who know, who have the answer.
2. **A bad guy.** Them. The billionaires, the unawakened, the wrong political tribe, the other companies, the toxic people, the close-minded.
3. **A crisis or urgency.** Something terrible will happen if action is not taken. The world is at stake, the body will fail, the company will lose the market, the relationship will end, society will collapse.
4. **A solution only the good guy possesses.** Only this product, this practice, this candidate, this teaching, this leader can address the crisis.
5. **A bid to take your authority.** The structure exists to recruit you into ceding your discernment to the good guy. Buy the deodorant. Vote the way. Believe the teaching. Trust the partner.

Recognizing the structure once makes it recognizable everywhere. The same pattern operates whether the messenger is a guru, a CEO, a politician, a marketer, a partner, or your own internal critic.

## In Joe's Words

"This is like every political rant ever. There's a good guy, that's us. There's a bad guy, that is pick A, billionaire, B, radical left, C, fascist right, like progressives. There's always a bad guy, there's always a good guy, it's always us against them. There's always some fear lurking around the corner, then the world needs us or the whole thing is going to fall apart. And there's somebody who — and there's not transparency, and there's like, we know and they don't know, and there's some — and you're ceding your authority over to some religious or some political figure instead of thinking for yourself, or some news channel instead of thinking for yourself." -- Joe Hudson, Who Owns Your Authority?

The structural identity across contexts is what makes the pattern useful as a diagnostic. When you hear the structure, you can pause and ask: what am I being recruited into? Who benefits from my buying this?

## The Pitch Deck Application

The common wisdom about writing a fundraising pitch deck is a recipe for the structure: here is a problem, this big problem, it needs to be solved, inevitable consequences to it not being solved, we can solve it, only we can solve it, this is the team that can solve it. Underneath that: use FOMO to get investors to pile in before they miss out, see the herd dynamics and exploit them.

The pitch deck is doing the same thing a cult recruitment does. It is creating the urgency, the us-vs-them (we are special, our team is the right team), the implicit threat (you will miss out, the problem will go unsolved). It works because the structure works. It works on investors for the same reason it works on cult members.

"Look at the common wisdom about how to write a pitch deck for fundraising. It's like, right, here's a problem. There's this big problem. Yes, it needs to be solved. There's inevitable consequences to it not being solved. We can solve it. Only we can solve it though. This is the team. This is the team that can solve it. And then, you know, underneath that, there's like the seeing the herd dynamics in investors and using some FOMO to get everybody to like dive in and like pile in on this investment before they miss out. And that's just how things are done." -- Brett Kistler (Joe affirms), Who Owns Your Authority?

This is not a moral judgment. It is a structural observation. The pitch deck works on humans because humans have the same response to the structure regardless of whether the source is a guru or a startup. The same is true for marketing.

## The Marketing Application

A beauty advertisement is the same pattern: here is a way you are special if you use this, here is the inevitable crisis if you do not (you will smell horrible, you will not be loved, you will be left behind), here is the in-group you join by buying. The deodorant ad and the cult recruitment are running the same software.

"Here's a way that we are special. It's like the whole thing pitches the way in which we are special, and you can be a part of this special thing. And we're special because there's others who aren't like us. There's an us against them. There's, we're the only ones. There's the inevitability of crisis if we don't handle this. You know, like, oh my God, everybody's gonna smell you and you're gonna smell horrible if you don't use our deodorant. It is literally the same thing." -- Joe Hudson, Who Owns Your Authority?

## The Political Application

Political cults — once just religious — are now a recognized phenomenon. The structure of political belief has acquired the architecture of cults: good guys (our party), bad guys (their party), urgent crisis (democracy is dying, the country will collapse), only-we-can-fix-it (vote, donate, do not engage with the other side), in-group/out-group, ceding of authority to the leader or to the party.

The same person who would notice the structure in a religious cult can be invisible to it in their own political tribe. The fish does not see the water. The mainstream is invisible as a mainstream to the mainstream.

## The Relationship Application

In intimate relationships, the structure can show up subtly or starkly.

The stark version is the controlling partner: "you don't need those friends anymore," "everyone else is out there trying to get us," "it's you and me against the world." The us-vs-them is explicit. The isolation is the goal. See [[walking-on-eggshells]] for the relational version of the pattern.

The subtle version is more common and harder to see. The agreement that one partner is the smart one and the other is the emotional one. The pattern of "I can't do that because I need them to be happy." The ceding of authority around money decisions, parenting decisions, social decisions. It looks like compromise. It is the same structure operating below the level of awareness.

"90% of the relationships I have seen, there's an agreement that this person's the smart one and this person's not so smart, or this one's the intelligent one, this one's the emotional one. There's the ceding of authority all the time. How many times, like, oh, I can't do that because I need her to be happy, or I need him to be happy. So all the time, somebody's need for control around a certain subject inside of a marriage creates this same kind of level dysfunction using the same tools." -- Joe Hudson, Who Owns Your Authority?

This is the same recipe inside a marriage. The crisis is small (the partner's mood, the dinner choice, the weekend plan). The good guy and bad guy are softer (you and your partner are not enemies, but the implicit framing is "I need you to do this or something will go wrong"). The result is the same: authority transferred from one party to the other, away from the conscious choice of both.

## The Voice-in-the-Head Application

The structure operates internally too. The voice in the head is doing the same thing the cult leader does. "You have to do this or you're going to be in pain." "Only you can do it." "Why haven't you gotten off the couch?" The voice creates the good guy (the future you who has done the thing) and the bad guy (the current you who hasn't), the urgency, the only-this-action-will-solve-it framing, and the demand that you cede your authority to its instructions.

"All this is happening inside of us. We have a voice in the head. For most of us have a very strong voice in the head, and we believe that voice in the head when it says, 'You have to do this or you're going to be in pain,' or, 'It's only you that can do it,' and, 'Why haven't you gotten off the couch?' All those exact same things, the us and thems, the have-to-do-it, the urgency — that's all happening from the voice in our head talking to ourselves." -- Joe Hudson, Who Owns Your Authority?

The internal cult is the original. The external cults, the marketing, the politics, the controlling relationships — all of these are projections or amplifications of the structure that is already running inside.

See [[voice-in-the-head]] and [[cult-of-one]] for the internal version. See [[binary-thinking-signals-fear]] for the related cognitive structure.

## What Makes the Pattern Work

The pattern works because humans are wired for belonging, for safety inside an in-group, for relief from uncertainty, for the simplification of complex choices into us-vs-them. The wiring is real. The wiring is not the problem. The exploitation of the wiring is the problem.

The pattern's effectiveness is not evidence that the cause it is selling is wrong or right. The structure is content-neutral. It can sell a political program that does in fact have the country's best interests in mind, and it can sell a doomsday cult, and the mechanism is the same. The recognition of the structure is therefore not a way to decide what to believe. It is a way to notice when you are being recruited into believing through means that bypass your discernment.

## The Subconscious Versus Conscious Version

A complication worth naming: in most cases, the people running the pattern do not see themselves as manipulating. They actually believe the urgency. The politician giving the rant actually thinks the country is in crisis and they alone can address it. The marketer actually thinks the product solves a problem. The controlling partner actually thinks the world is dangerous and the partner needs protection. The guru actually thinks the world needs their teaching.

"Very few of these people are consciously going, 'Okay, I know how to manipulate a whole bunch of people. I'm gonna use pathos and ethos and logos, and I'm gonna do an us against them.' Some of it's happening, but most of the time it's like they act — the politician speaking it actually thinks it. They actually think that there's somebody who's gonna come and get them." -- Joe Hudson, Who Owns Your Authority?

This is important for two reasons. First, the response is not primarily about defending against bad actors with conscious intent. It is about recognizing the structure regardless of whether the source has bad intent. Second, the person inside the pattern often cannot see the pattern in themselves. The corrective requires external feedback or sustained internal inquiry.

## The Diagnostic Use

Once the pattern is recognized, it becomes a diagnostic that can be applied across domains. When you hear:

- "Only we can fix this" — flag.
- "The world needs us" — flag.
- "They are dangerous" — flag.
- "Time is running out" — flag.
- "You are special because you are with us" — flag.
- "Don't talk to them about this" — flag.
- "Trust me, I know what I'm doing" — flag.

None of these on their own is necessarily a cult or a manipulation. Each of them combined with the others, used to bypass your own discernment, is the pattern. The work is the recognition, and then the asking: do I want to consent to this? What does my own discernment say?

## Connection to Empowerment

The corrective is not to reject everything that uses the pattern. The pattern shows up everywhere because it is built into how humans communicate urgency and belonging. The corrective is to develop your own authority — to make decisions from your own discernment rather than from the recruitment.

See [[love-requires-empowerment]] and the larger framework of authority ownership Joe articulates: you cannot be in a healthy relationship with any external source until you are in a healthy relationship with your own authority. The structure of us-vs-them is what fills the gap when authority is not internally located.

## Tone

Joe describes this pattern with the calm of someone who has watched it operate in many contexts and lost the impulse to be outraged about any particular instance. The pattern is structural. Outrage at any one instance misses the structural point. The recognition is what is useful. The recognition does not require anger at the sources of the pattern; it requires only that the recipient stay in their own authority.
