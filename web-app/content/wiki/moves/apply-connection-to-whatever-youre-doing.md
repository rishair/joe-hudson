# Apply Connection to Whatever You're Doing

## The Move

When a person describes a task or category of work -- especially a mundane, rote, or non-exciting one -- Joe redirects the question from "how do I make this go away" or "how do I do this efficiently" to "how do you do this in the most connected way possible, while being connected with yourself?"

The move has two simultaneous instructions baked into it:

1. The task gets done in a way that maximizes connection (with self, with team, with the person on the other end, with the larger system).
2. The person doing the task stays connected with themselves throughout.

Both instructions matter. The first alone produces theater. The second alone produces a person who is in their body but still doing the task in the old draining way. Together they produce a different version of the same activity.

The verbal forms vary:

- "How do you do this in the most connected way possible?"
- "How do you do this while being connected with yourself?"
- "How does this become -- how do I do this in a more connected way?"
- "How do I maximize the connection here?"

The move is structurally similar to [[apply-enjoy-to-whats-here]] -- redirecting an absolute (enjoyment / connection) to whatever activity is in front of the person. The difference is the target value. Enjoyment is the personal felt state. Connection is the relational orientation. They overlap heavily but emphasize different aspects.

## When Joe Uses It

- When the person has just named a task they find boring, draining, or beneath them.
- When the person is asking what to do next in their career and the answer keeps pointing toward connection without them noticing.
- When the person reports loving a part of their work but disliking another part. The disliked part is the candidate for the move.
- When the person says "but I have to do X" about a task that is structurally part of the work. The move accepts the X has to happen and changes how it happens.
- When the person is approaching burnout from the operational parts of a creative or relational business.
- After establishing that connection is what the person actually wants (see [[connection-as-life-bottom-line]]) and the question becomes how to live by that principle in daily life.
- When the person is searching for a new big move and is missing that the next move could be applied to the existing work instead.

## What It Produces

Almost immediately: a small explosion of creative options the person did not see before. The cognitive frame "do this efficiently" produces one set of options. The frame "do this in a way that maximizes connection while staying connected to myself" produces a completely different set.

The mechanism: most operational tasks are bottlenecked by the operating frame, not by the task itself. Joe describes asking Ali how he could film sponsorship segments more connectedly. Within seconds Ali had four options: reach out to sponsors he actually used and loved, work only with companies he vibed with, turn the sponsored segment into a song or poem he would enjoy making, and stop chasing the inbound stream of misaligned offers. None of these required different skills. They required the different frame.

The deeper effect: the relationship between the person and the work changes. Tasks that were treated as the cost of doing the loved work start to become candidates for becoming part of the loved work. The artificial split between the meaningful parts and the mundane parts begins to dissolve.

Operationally, in a business: the move propagates. The CEO who applies it to one task starts asking the question across the organization. Sub-domains that were assumed to be necessarily energy-draining start to be redesigned. See [[how-could-you-do-this-and-gain-energy]] for the energy-focused parallel of this same intervention.

## What Joe Does Next

- Stay with the ideas as they arrive. Do not evaluate. Let the person notice that the ideas came easily once the frame changed.
- Generalize. "That same lens could be put at everything you do. How do I buy camera equipment? How do I -- like, everything you do, there can be this question of, like, how do I maximize the connection?" The move is not for this one task. It is for the whole life.
- Install the daily-practice version. The frame is too valuable to use only when prompted. The person needs a way to remember to apply it. See [[ten-percent-more-connected-experiment]] for the practice form.
- Address what the connection will do for the practical concerns. If the person has a money fear underneath their work, name what increased connection typically does for the business. Most public figures, most service businesses, most teams operate better when the person at the center is more connected with themselves and the work. The connection is not separate from the practical outcomes -- it is the lever underneath them. See [[three-effects-of-connection]] and [[connection-as-life-bottom-line]].

## Sustained Use in a Single Session

This move can structure an entire session. In the Ali Abdaal session it was applied at least four times to successively more mundane parts of the work:

- How to spend time with the team more connectedly (in-person reset weeks vs. continuous remote work).
- How to teach more connectedly (in-person seminars instead of just YouTube).
- How to make videos more connectedly (live coaching content vs. algorithm-driven content).
- How to film sponsorship segments more connectedly (which sponsors, what format, what energy).

Each application produced specific, generative options. By the end of the session Ali said: "I think even just running it through that filter of like, what does 10% more connected look like -- I've got the answers."

The repetition matters. The repetition teaches the person that the frame works on everything. The person finishes the session not just with answers to today's questions but with a portable filter they can apply to any future task.

## What This Move Is Not

- Not productivity reframing. The point is not to make tasks more efficient. The point is to do them in a way that includes connection.
- Not affirmation. "Be present while you do it" is not the same instruction. The move is more specific: structure the task so connection is the operating principle, not an internal state laid over the top.
- Not bypass of the practical question. If the task should not be done at all, the move does not address that. The move assumes the task is happening. It changes how.
- Not a substitute for the conditions that allow connection. If the person is exhausted or in collapse, the move does not work yet. The conditions for being able to apply the frame -- some baseline of rest, presence, capacity -- have to exist first. See [[nervous-system-recovery-after-stress]] and [[dont-go-after-every-emotion-at-once]].

## Tone

Curious, generative, slightly playful. The frame loses its medicine when delivered as a mandate. The energy is "let's see what comes up if we ask the question this way." Not "you should be doing this connectedly."

When the person produces an idea, Joe matches the energy. Often a small "yeah, great" or "what a fun way to do that." The endorsement is not of the specific idea -- it is of the fact that the frame produced an idea at all. The endorsement reinforces the value of the frame.

## Dialogue Example

After Ali had been describing his various career options, Joe set up the move on the most mundane part of the work.

- **Joe:** "Give me just the thing about the business, like, for some businesses, it would be billing... but either way, is something that your business does. It's this kind of rote, mundane, non-exciting thing."
- **Ali:** "Filming sponsorship segments for the videos and for the podcast."
- **Joe:** "Awesome. I want you to do two things. One is I want you to think about how to do that in the most connected way possible, not -- meaning as a whole system, not just you being there doing the sponsorship, but the whole organization being deeply connected in that process. And I want you to do it while being connected with yourself."
- **Ali:** "Yeah, I can think of some ways to do that." [generates four specific creative options]
- **Joe:** "Yeah. Great. So you could take even the thing that is most mundane that you dislike the most, put it through this goal of, oh, how does this become -- how do I do this in a more connected way? While being connected, thinking about it, and immediately it sparks this another level of creativity. So that same lens could be put at everything you do."

*(Source: Ali Abdaal coaching session)*

The structure is worth noting. Joe asked for the most mundane task on purpose. The frame works most visibly when applied to the least promising candidate. If the frame produces creative options on the mundane task, it will produce them on anything.
