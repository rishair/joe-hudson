# Show Me the Bracing, and Show Me the Opposite of Bracing

## The Move

When a person reports that bracing is what makes them productive (or focused, or safe, or held-together) and predicts chaos if they stopped, do not argue. Have them demonstrate. The body will resolve in seconds what the mind has been arguing about for years.

The form:

1. "Show me the bracing."
2. "Now show me the opposite of bracing."
3. Reflect what just happened. "When you took the bracing out, you got more present. That's not bouncing off walls. That's more available, not less."

If the person produces something other than the opposite of bracing (a manic version, a story about chaos), name the difference. "That bouncing isn't the opposite of bracing. That's still bracing, just bigger. Try again — really take the bracing out."

The move can be repeated until the body actually drops the brace. The repetitions are not failures. The mind keeps trying to substitute its prediction (chaos) for the actual answer (presence). Each loop gives the body another chance to overrule the mind.

## When Joe Uses This

- When a person reports the felt-sense version of [[im-afraid-i-wont-get-anything-done]]: bracing is the engine, dropping it produces chaos.
- When a person can describe the bracing intellectually but says they cannot stop doing it.
- When the conversation has gotten heady and the body has dropped out. The demonstration drags the body back into the room.
- When the person's story about what would happen if they stopped is so vivid and so confident that argument will not penetrate it. The body's actual answer to the same question is the only thing that will.
- When the person is asking "how do I stop?" -- the question itself is a brace. The demonstration shows them they have already stopped, repeatedly, in front of the coach, just by being asked to.
- After [[are-you-bracing-or-embracing]] has confirmed bracing is present. The diagnostic question gets the answer; this move makes the answer dance.

## Why It Works

The mind has a story about what dropping the brace would produce. The story is wrong. It was constructed by the brace to protect the brace. Asking the mind to imagine dropping the brace activates the story. Asking the body to demonstrate dropping the brace bypasses the story.

When the body shows the opposite of bracing, what shows up is presence. The person can see it on themselves -- breath deepens, jaw softens, shoulders drop, eyes settle. The person can also see that this is not the chaos they predicted. The prediction has been falsified in real time, by their own body, in front of them.

The contradiction between the predicted-failure-mode and the actually-shown-state is the leverage. The mind cannot maintain "if I stop bracing I'll be unproductive" while the body is sitting in increased presence. The contradiction does its own work.

The move is also self-applicable. Once the person has done it once with a coach, they can do it themselves. They have a felt reference for what the opposite of bracing actually is, separate from the mind's story about it.

## Dialogue Example

The person came in saying: bracing is what makes me work, and if I stop, I'll bounce off the walls.

- **Joe:** "Show me the bracing."
- **Person:** [demonstrates the brace]
- **Joe:** "Yeah, and then show me the opposite of bracing. So it's this place you're not going to get anything done from."
- **Person:** [shifts posture]
- **Joe:** "But the opposite of bracing that just happened wasn't bouncing."
- **Person:** "No, I'm a bit settled right now, I suppose."
- **Joe:** "Uh, yeah. So brace for me. And now do the opposite of bracing. I mean, the presence that you're showing now definitely says to me you'll get more done here than bracing — more energy."

The person then introduces the "bouncing" idea verbally. Joe redirects to the body again:

- **Joe:** "Put yourself in that imagined place of being completely fucking recklessly enthusiastic and unfocused and just running around. Yeah, just imagine it. And now take all the bracing out of it. Did that increase your presence or decrease your presence? When you take all the bracing out, that's bracing. I just watched you do it. You were not bracing. You got more in your body. And then your jaw shut down."

The double pass matters. The first pass gets the body's first answer. The second pass takes the person's own predicted failure mode (reckless enthusiasm) and applies the same removal of bracing to it. Even the imagined chaos becomes more present, not less, when the bracing is removed from it.

## What Joe Does With the Response

- **If the body actually drops the brace:** stay with it. Reflect what happened. "That presence is more capacity, not less." Do not move on too fast. The body needs a moment to register that the predicted chaos did not occur.
- **If the person introduces a new prediction ("but I'd bounce off the walls"):** apply the move to the prediction. Imagine the bouncing. Now take the bracing out of the bouncing. The bouncing was itself braced.
- **If the person says "but it keeps happening":** the move has done its acute work; the chronic work is the question [[what-am-i-bracing-for]] asked many times a day. The move shows that the release is available; the question keeps the release available.
- **If the identity layer surfaces ("I have to hold it together; who else is gonna do it?"):** the demonstration uncovered the structural fear. Move to [[bracing-hardens-identity-makes-you-needed]].
- **If the person reflexively reinstates the brace after dropping it:** name the moment. "You were not bracing. Then your jaw shut down." Watching the reinstating live is itself the teaching. The brace becomes visible as a choice that can be made differently.

## Why This Is Distinct From the Diagnostic Question

[[are-you-bracing-or-embracing]] is an internal diagnostic. The person checks their own felt sense. It is private, can be done alone, and produces a verbal answer ("I'm bracing" / "I'm embracing" / "unclear").

This move is the external demonstration. The person performs the bracing and its opposite. The coach watches. The contradiction between the predicted failure mode and the actually-shown opposite of bracing is observable to both, and the body's answer is unarguable.

The diagnostic is for ongoing self-practice. The move is for live coaching in a specific moment of stuck story.

## Tone

Light, slightly playful, slightly experimental. Not solemn. Not "let's get serious about your body now." Closer to "okay, do that thing. Now do the other thing. Look at that." The lightness keeps the demonstration from becoming a new performance of seriousness, which would itself be a brace.

The "completely fucking recklessly enthusiastic" framing is deliberate. The exaggeration gives the person permission to actually try the predicted failure mode, which lets them see that even the failure mode is more present than the bracing.

Joe is direct in his observations. "I just watched you do it. You were not bracing. You got more in your body. And then your jaw shut down." The directness is not harsh; it is precise. The body did a specific thing; Joe names the specific thing. The naming gives the body something to recognize.
