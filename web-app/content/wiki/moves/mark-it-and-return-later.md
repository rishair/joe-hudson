# Mark It and Return Later

## The Move

The person produces a clear body-level signal mid-conversation -- a body shift, a chin lift, a closed eye, an unusual pause, a felt-sense report that uses an unexpected word. The coach notices the signal and would normally pursue it. But the main thread of the session is doing useful work and the signal can wait. The move is to mark the signal explicitly, tag it for later, and continue with the main thread.

The verbal form is short: "I want to mark that. We'll come back to it." Or: "I just want to mark it then go back to where we were." Or: "I'll stop going meta for a second and just deal with the thing itself."

The mark serves two functions:

1. It tells the person: I saw that. I am not going to pretend it didn't happen. It will be available to use later.
2. It tells the coach: do not lose track of this. The signal is now an explicit element of the session, not just a mental note.

The deferral is the choice not to chase. The signal does not have to be processed immediately. Pursuing every signal in real time fragments the session. Marking and deferring keeps the session coherent while keeping the signal in play.

## When Joe Uses It

- When the person produces an interesting body signal during a thread that is doing other useful work.
- When the body-level material is too rich to pursue in passing but the main conversational arc is also too valuable to interrupt.
- When the coach wants to do a quick teaching about a somatic mechanism (chin position, jaw clamp, breath pattern) but the teaching would distract from the topic the person came in to address.
- When the deferral itself sends a signal of trust: I see this, it matters, we will get to it, and we are also okay continuing with what we were doing.
- When the coach has already started a meta-observation thread (about the body, about the process) and wants to step out of meta to deal with the content for a while.

## What It Produces

The session becomes more spacious. The person knows that things are being noticed even when they are not being pursued. The implicit message: this coach is tracking more than is being acted on at any given moment.

The marked signal often resurfaces on its own later in the session. The person, knowing the signal was noted, brings it back when it becomes relevant. The coach does not have to "remember to come back to it" because the signal had a job and that job often gets done by the person from inside the conversation.

When the coach does explicitly return to the marked signal, the return is integrative. The signal is now placed in context. The teaching about the body or the process lands more cleanly because there is now an arc of evidence to attach it to, not just one moment.

## What Joe Does With the Mark

The mark itself is a small move. The choice of what to do with the marked material later is the larger move. Several options:

- **Use the mark as evidence later.** When a related signal appears, Joe will reference the earlier one. "Earlier when you closed your eyes -- that was the same thing happening." The pattern becomes visible because two instances are now connected.

- **Return to teach the mechanism.** "I want to come back to what I marked earlier. When your chin went up just now and then a few minutes ago, here's what's happening." The marked signal becomes the example for the teaching.

- **Let it dissolve.** Some marked signals don't need to be returned to. They were useful as data points and have served their purpose. The "we'll come back to it" is honored loosely. Joe is not held to actually returning if the return would be forced.

- **Use the mark to ground the closing.** At the end of the session, refer back to one or two early marks to show the person that the session followed a coherent arc. The marks function as anchors that make the arc visible.

## Dialogue Example

Early in the Ali Abdaal session, Joe noticed Ali's body had taken a different form when he was about to "give a real answer" versus when he was in podcast presenter mode. Joe wanted to mark this but the conversation was in the middle of working on the money-fear topic.

- **Joe:** "Yeah. So just to say what I noticed you say, you said, I wanted to make sure it feels like something real. Literally using the word feel. And to do that, you actually put yourself in -- your body went into a very particular position that was unique from this other position. I just want to mark that. We'll get to it later, but I just want to mark it and then go back."

Joe then returned to the money-fear thread. Later in the session he referenced the chin position again when he taught the chin-lift mechanism explicitly. The mark made the teaching land as the recognition of a pattern that had already appeared, not as a new abstract piece of information.

Later in the same session Joe used a closely related framing: "I'll stop going meta for a second. We'll just actually deal with the thing itself." This is the same move in a slightly different form -- the coach pulls out of an active meta-observation thread to handle content for a while, with the implicit promise that the meta is still available.

## Tone

Light, fast, almost parenthetical. The mark is not a heavy intervention. It is a brief audible bookmark, often delivered in the middle of a sentence. The lightness is what makes the mark non-disruptive. If the mark were heavy, it would itself become the new topic. The lightness lets the mark coexist with the main thread.

The tone also conveys that the coach is genuinely tracking the signal but is not anxious about it. The signal is interesting but not urgent. The session has time. This calm tracking is itself part of what the person learns from being coached.

## What This Move Is Not

- Not [[follow-wonder-flag-the-deflection]]. That move pivots away from the question and follows new wonder. This move stays on the current thread and bookmarks something else for later.
- Not avoidance. The mark is precisely the opposite of avoidance: the signal is explicitly noted. The deferral is a choice about timing, not about whether to engage.
- Not a promise. The "we'll come back to it" is held loosely. The coach is not bound to return. The bookmark may turn out not to need the return.
- Not a substitute for actually noticing the signal. If the coach uses "mark it and return later" reflexively to avoid pursuing things in the moment, the move becomes a dodge. The move only works when the deferral is a live choice between two valuable threads, not a way to avoid the harder one.

## Reads That Trigger This Move

- Any body signal that is rich enough to teach from but would interrupt the current useful thread.
- A pattern that has only appeared once so far -- marking creates the bookmark so the second instance can be recognized.
- A meta-observation about the conversational dynamic itself that would derail the current content work.
- A moment of dissociation, manufacturing, or repression that is mild enough to defer and important enough to track.
