# Enter the Inquiry With a Soft Sense of Self (Not a Decided One)

## The Move

Before beginning [[emotional-inquiry]], soften the sense of self. Treat your identity, your decisions, your expectations of what you will do next, as also being in wonder. If you go in with everything about who you are and what you will choose already locked in, the practice cannot do its work. The emotions will be inquired into; the body will be felt; nothing will change. The practice fails by completing successfully.

The move is small and rarely named but determines whether the practice can actually move anything.

## Why This Matters

The practice operates on what the body is holding. What the body is holding is partly the emotional material itself and partly the identity-scaffold that has been organized around suppressing it. If the identity-scaffold is held rigid entering the practice -- "I am someone who does X, who decides Y, who will continue to behave Z" -- then the body has nothing to release into. The emotional material may surface; the scaffold has no give; the surfaced material has nowhere to go.

Brett's articulation of the discovery, which lands the move:

"If I go into emotional inquiry with the notion of who I am being concrete and solid, and the kind of decisions that I make being decided, and then I'm like, okay, I'm gonna do the emotional inquiry, then I'm gonna go do exactly the thing that I was gonna do anyway. It doesn't tend to work. It's like I have to go into it with the childlike perspective of being intimately in wonder with the frog, as well as with myself, my sense of self, my sense of what kind of decision I might expect myself to make, having actually integrated and experienced and savored any of the emotions that come up." -- Brett Kistler, Emotional Inquiry

The wonder has to be aimed at the self too, not just at the emotion. The same childlike openness that meets the emotion has to meet the question "and who am I, after this?"

## What This Looks Like Concretely

Before the practice, three small recognitions:

1. **I may decide something different after this than I would have decided before it.**
2. **I may behave differently after this than I would have behaved before it.**
3. **The person who finishes this inquiry may not be the person who started it.**

None of these are commitments. None require believing change is likely. They are just leaving the door open. The door being open is what allows the practice to do its work.

The opposite is the closed posture: "I'm going to do the practice, then I'm going to go ahead and do what I was already planning to do." This treats the practice as an interlude that happens between two moments of identical-self continuing its identical course. The body reads the closure and the practice produces nothing -- or worse, produces something that confirms the existing plan.

## Joe's Affirmation

Joe's response to Brett's articulation is full agreement and naming this as central:

"Yes, that's so well said. And what a great reminder." -- Joe Hudson, Emotional Inquiry

Joe then connects it to the broader observation he had noticed that morning -- people returning to the Decisions course not because they failed the first time, but because they had become different people in the year since and the course would land differently in the new person.

The connection matters. The same softness toward the sense of self that lets emotional inquiry work is what lets repeated exposure to teachings keep landing freshly. The teaching does not change. The person who encounters it does. And the person who encounters it can keep changing only if they enter each encounter willing to be other than who they were entering the last one.

## When to Use This Move

The move is upstream of the practice itself. It is set up before any inquiry begins. In coaching, this looks like:

- Before guiding someone through an inquiry session, asking what they are expecting to come out of it. If the answer is highly specific ("I want to feel less anxious about the meeting"), naming the trap and suggesting they release the specific expectation.
- After someone has been doing the practice for a while and reporting that nothing is shifting -- asking whether they have been entering each session with their decisions already made.
- When someone is using inquiry to validate a choice they have already made. Naming that this is not what the practice does; the practice can dissolve the choice as easily as confirm it, and going in willing for either is the condition for the practice to do anything.

## What This Move Differs From

It is not the same as having no agenda. Most people have an agenda entering inquiry: an emotion they want to meet, a pattern they want to understand. The agenda is fine. What needs to be soft is the assumption about who will be on the other side of meeting it.

It is not the same as expecting transformation. Expecting transformation is its own form of decided-ness. The softness is to neither expect nor refuse change. Whatever happens, happens.

It is not the same as being uncertain about decisions. A person can be quite certain of a decision and still enter the practice willing to discover the decision differently afterward. The certainty about the decision is not the rigidity. The pre-commitment to not let the practice touch the decision is the rigidity.

## The Mechanism Visible in Reverse

The clearest evidence that this move matters is what happens when it is absent. A person does the practice "correctly," reports the right phenomenology, and then makes the same choice they would have made anyway. They report later that the practice "did not work." It worked exactly the way they configured it to work. They asked it to be an interlude inside an unchanged self. It obliged.

The corrective is not technical. It is upstream. The sense of self has to be entered into the practice along with the emotion. Both have to be in wonder. Both have to be available to be touched.

## Tone

Direct, almost casual. The move is small enough that it should not be over-weighted. Heavier framing would itself install a "now I must be soft about my identity" rigidity that defeats the move. The right delivery is something like: "by the way, before we start, can you hold your decisions about what you're going to do next a little less tightly?"

## Upstream (What Leads Here)

- When someone reports doing [[emotional-inquiry]] and nothing shifting.
- When someone is about to do the practice and has stated explicit expectations of what will or won't change.
- When a person has been doing the work for a long time and feels stuck despite consistent practice.
- When [[epiphany-becomes-rut]] is operating and the rut is in the entering posture, not in the practice itself.
- When wonder needs to be extended to the self, not just to the emotion.
