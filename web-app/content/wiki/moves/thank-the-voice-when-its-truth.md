# Thank the Voice (When It's Been Telling You the Truth)

## The Move

Once a critical voice has been reframed from attack to intuition (see [[assume-the-voice-is-right-but-not-bad]]), the next move is to thank the voice directly. Not silently translate its meaning. Not appreciate it abstractly. Speak the thanks to the voice itself, the way you would thank a person who has been telling you something true that you have been refusing to hear.

"If the thing is a good thing, if it's like your intuition speaking, if it is you seeing through the veil, what makes you resist it? Like right now, what if you were just like, thank you?" -- Joe Hudson, Am I Being Authentic Or Fake?

The move is short. The question — "what is it just like to thank the voice that's like, this is all fake?" — invites the person to drop the resistance and offer thanks. The person typically reports a body sensation in response. "It feels really fucking good."

## What This Is Not

This is distinct from the translation move in [[voice-as-care]], which silently translates the voice's content into the care underneath ("I see that you really care about my health"). That move is for the misguided-but-caring voice. This move is for the voice that has been correctly perceiving something the person has been refusing to see.

It is also distinct from the more general [[apply-view-to-the-voice]] practice, which is a relational stance toward the voice over time. This move is a one-beat gesture inside a coaching session: drop the resistance, say thank you, notice what happens.

## When Joe Uses It

The move applies specifically after:

- The voice has been reframed as accurate perception, not distortion ([[assume-the-voice-is-right-but-not-bad]])
- The person has acknowledged some part of what the voice has been saying is intuition
- The person has been resisting the voice and the resistance has been giving the voice energy (see [[resistance]])
- The person is in a state where they can actually speak the thanks rather than perform it

The move does NOT apply when:

- The voice's content is genuinely distorted self-attack. Thanking distortion confirms the distortion.
- The person has not yet reframed the voice as intuition. Thanking it from the old frame is just compliance with the attack.
- The thanks would be performative rather than felt. The move requires the actual gesture, not the words.

## What It Produces

The most common immediate response is a body sensation that the person finds pleasant. Energy moving. A feeling of opening. Sometimes literally "it feels really fucking good." The body knows when an actual gesture of receiving has happened, and the body knows when a resistance has been dropped. The pleasant sensation is the evidence of the receive.

Often the next thing that surfaces is a worry: "but if I stop resisting this voice, will I still be able to function with other people?" The worry is the next layer of resistance, this time at the social level. That worry is addressed separately. See [[fear-of-disconnection-marks-real-growth]].

## The Mechanism

The move works because the voice has been fueled by resistance. The person has been in a war with the voice for years. The war has kept the voice loud and the person organized around defending against it. The thanks is the gesture that ends the war.

The end of the war is not the silencing of the voice. The voice may still arrive. The relationship has changed. What used to land as an attack now lands as a piece of intelligence the person can receive. The voice no longer requires defense, because there is no longer anything for it to attack — the person has already received what it has been trying to say.

Joe names the doing-undoing structure of this:

"That would be like just receive. It's a doing that's an undoing, so it's a little — it gets a little bit weird. But all you have to do is just not resist it." -- Joe Hudson, Am I Being Authentic Or Fake?

The thanks is the doing that is the undoing. There is an action (saying thank you) but the action is itself the cessation of the resistance. The person is not doing something new; they are stopping doing the resistance and the natural state underneath is the receive. See [[opposite-of-trying]] for the broader principle.

## What Joe Does After

After the person thanks the voice and reports the pleasant body sensation, Joe stays with the sensation. He does not interpret it, direct it, or try to amplify it. "Great. Enjoy them." The body is doing the integration; the person's job is to be in the integration.

The next move that often follows: noticing the worry about social consequences. The person who has just thanked the voice and felt the opening will often immediately wonder if they will still be able to relate to people from this new state. Joe addresses that as a separate layer. See [[fear-of-disconnection-marks-real-growth]].

## Tone

Light, slightly invitational. The question — "what is it just like to thank the voice?" — is offered the way you would suggest trying a flavor of something. The person is invited to try the gesture and report what happens. The lightness is what makes the move possible. If the move were delivered with weight ("you must thank your inner voice"), the person would brace against it.

The move also carries some of the quality of a discovery. Joe is not teaching the person that the voice should be thanked. He is pointing out that they could try it and see what happens. The discovery happens in the trying, not in the teaching.

## Anti-Patterns

- **Thanking the voice as a discipline or rule.** The move is a one-time live gesture, not a practice. Making it a should turns it into the new performance.
- **Thanking the voice as a strategy to make it stop.** The move is genuine receiving, not manipulation. If the thanks is offered in order to silence the voice, the voice will register the manipulation and continue.
- **Thanking the voice when the voice is actually delivering distortion.** The diagnostic is whether the person privately recognizes truth in what the voice has been saying. If yes, the move applies. If the content is purely shaming distortion, this move confirms the distortion. See [[lawyer-the-voice]] instead.
- **Treating the move as a one-time fix.** The voice will continue to operate. The relationship may be different. The work continues. See [[dont-forget-the-tool-when-things-feel-good]].
