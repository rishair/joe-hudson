# Excavating the Inner Critic in Group

## Definition

A group exercise used in week-long courses. Participants identify the recurring, abusive voice in their head and write it on the wall where everyone can see it. The exercise is not about analyzing the voice or getting rid of it. It is about making it visible and being witnessed in having it.

## What It Produces

"When people excavate all this stuff and then they look around the room and they see 11 other people loving them despite of this, that that's no excuse not to love yourself. It does something for the human." -- Joe Hudson, A Discussion with Dr. Bessel van der Kolk & Joe Hudson

The mechanism: shame depends on hiding. When the voice is externalized and the group responds with love rather than rejection, the logic of shame breaks. The person cannot maintain "I am unlovable because of this" when 11 people are demonstrating the opposite.

## How Joe Describes It

"The whole week-long course is about looking at the voice in our head and particularly the abusive, recurring voice in our head and laying it out in a way and then approaching it and how -- figuring out how to have different relationships with it. Not getting rid of it, but learning to love it." -- Joe Hudson, A Discussion with Dr. Bessel van der Kolk & Joe Hudson

Key framing: the goal is not elimination of the voice but a different relationship with it. This aligns with [[loving-all-parts]].

## When To Use

- In group settings where trust has been established
- When shame is the primary obstacle
- When the person needs experiential (not intellectual) evidence that they are lovable
