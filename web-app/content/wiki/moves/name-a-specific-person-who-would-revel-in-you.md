# Name a Specific Person Who Would Revel in Your Full Self

## The Move

When the person delivers some version of "the world can't meet me with my full self" or "no one can handle this," do not argue with the generalization in the abstract. Name a specific person — by name, ideally one the audience also knows — who would in fact revel in the full self the person is describing.

The specificity is the move. A general response ("there are people who would meet you") leaves the person's generalization intact. A specific named person collapses it. The person cannot maintain "no one would meet me" while a coach is naming a real human being who would.

"The world can't meet you with — some part of the world can. If there's a woman named Christy Tripp right here, right now, I would literally bring her on and you all would just like have a conversation together without any words and love each other by the end of like 2 minutes. And she's like one of the like pillars of our community." -- Joe Hudson, I'm Not Responsible For How You Feel

The structure is three beats:

1. **Refuse the generalization briefly.** "The world can't meet you — some part of the world can." Not a long argument; just the correction.
2. **Name the specific person.** Use their actual name. Place them in the present tense. "If there's a woman named Christy Tripp right here, right now."
3. **Describe what would happen.** Make the scenario concrete. "I would literally bring her on and you would have a conversation without any words and love each other by the end of 2 minutes." The specificity of the imagined scenario makes it harder to dismiss than an abstract claim.

The move is then completed with a small reframe: "She's like one of the pillars of our community." The person being named is established as real, known, and respected.

## When Joe Uses It

- When the person delivers the "the world couldn't meet me" story, often phrased as a question or hypothetical ("what if I learned that the world couldn't meet me with my emotions?"). The story is the apparatus generalizing its own historical evidence into a universal claim.

- When the person has just located the original wound (the parent who couldn't meet them, the environment that treated them as too much). The wound is real and historical. The story that the wound generalizes to all of humanity is the apparatus's mistake. The move corrects the mistake.

- When the person is using the unmet-ability as the justification for chronic containment. "Since no one can meet me, I have to hold this in." The justification depends on the universality. Disconfirming the universality dissolves the justification.

- When the coach can in fact name a specific person who would do the meeting. The move requires the coach to actually know such a person — a real human, named, known, ideally also known to the audience. Without a real specific person, the move becomes the abstract claim it was meant to replace.

- Often after [[the-cry-laugh-at-the-edge]] has been named and the live containment has been surfaced. The move is part of the offering of an alternative future to the contained present.

The move does NOT apply:

- When the coach does not actually know a specific person who would meet the client. The move requires real specificity. Made-up examples have the wrong texture.

- When the person's "world can't meet me" story is genuinely accurate to their current life context (no community, no available people, isolated from connection). In that case, the work is building the context, not disconfirming the story.

- When the person is in active dissociation and not receiving anything. The move requires the person to be present enough to receive the specificity.

## What It Produces

**The generalization breaks.** The person cannot simultaneously believe "no one can meet me" and "Christy Tripp would meet me." The contradiction is forced. Something has to give. Usually what gives is the generalization, because it was built on the historical evidence the wound provided and was never tested against present evidence.

**An imagined future becomes available.** The person has been operating with a future in which their full self is met by no one. The named-specific-person introduces a future in which it is met by at least one real human, which by extension may be met by others. The frame shifts from "I have to contain myself forever" to "I can find people who can meet me."

**A small lightness, often a smile.** The specific named person is a real human with their own quirks. The scenario of being met by them is light, not heavy. The lightness is the antidote to the seriousness of the apparatus's claim that this is impossible.

**The frame becomes percentage-based.** Joe usually pairs this move with the percentage reframe: "The whole world's not going to be able to meet you, but a lot of the world is going to be able to meet you, actually revel in you." The all-or-nothing frame is replaced with a sliding scale. The work becomes finding the percentage, not waiting for everyone.

## What Joe Does With the Response

- **If the person softens visibly:** Joe stays with the softening. He may name what is happening. He may move to the next question: "What would your life look like if you were actually your full emotional self and you just saw that there was a percentage of people who just adored that?"

- **If the person verbally agrees but the body does not soften:** Joe may add more specificity. He can solicit confirmation from the audience: "She'll probably afterwards like put in the chat — I'll ask them to just tell you how much everybody adores Christy Tripp." The crowd-sourced confirmation is the corroborating evidence the verbal agreement was not delivering by itself.

- **If the person tries to maintain the generalization ("but most people couldn't"):** Joe accepts the partial truth and reframes: "The whole world's not going to be able to meet you, but a lot of the world is going to be able to meet you." The percentage frame holds both truths — most can't, some can, the some can is what the work is built on.

- **If the person tries to make the named person an exception ("she's special"):** Joe lets it go and moves to the next move. The point has landed even if the person is verbally maintaining the exception. The body has registered the existence of one real person who would. That is enough to start the shift.

- **Person:** "Mm-hmm. Lighter. Yeah. She was like — because she takes it for him to hold it."
- **Joe:** "Yeah, yeah. I was looking at all the faces. I saw it in you immediately without like no words. You were like bursting against your own rigidity."

*(Source: I'm Not Responsible For How You Feel)*

The structural arc visible in the exchange: the woman delivers the world-can't-meet-me hypothetical. Joe refuses the generalization with the specific named person. He extends with the concrete scenario (Christy Tripp would have a wordless conversation with you and love you by minute two). He establishes her as real and respected ("a pillar of our community"). He installs the percentage frame. He invites the person to feel into the alternative life. The person's body softens ("lighter"). Joe names what he saw in her body the moment the alternative landed.

## Why It Works

The move works because the apparatus's claim is built on generalization from a small dataset. The dataset is the original family environment in which the full self was not met. The generalization to "the world" was a child's necessary extrapolation; the child had no way to know that the family was not representative. The adult version of the generalization runs on the same extrapolation, untested against the much larger and more varied present-tense data.

A single named real person who would meet the client is a refutation of the generalization at its weakest point. The generalization claims universality. Universality is broken by one counterexample. The move is the counterexample.

The move also works because the specific named person carries the texture of reality. An abstract claim ("there are people who would meet you") is a thought; a specific named person is a body. The body-of-the-named-person enters the person's imagination and starts doing the work of being met. The imagination begins to populate.

The move also works because it is often deliverable with an audience. In a group call, the named person may actually be known to others. The audience confirmation ("yes, Christy is amazing, everyone adores her") adds layer of evidence that the coach alone cannot provide.

## Tone

Warm, slightly playful, and certain. The certainty is the key tonal element. The coach is not floating a possibility ("maybe there's someone"); the coach is naming a fact ("there's a woman named Christy Tripp"). The certainty signals that the person being described is real.

The playfulness comes from the specificity of the scenario. "You all would just like have a conversation together without any words and love each other by the end of like 2 minutes" is a concrete, slightly funny image. The humor is the antidote to the apparatus's seriousness.

The voice usually warms when the named person is mentioned. The warmth is the coach's actual feeling for the person being named. The person being coached can hear the warmth and registers that the named person is real because the warmth is real.

## What This Move Is Not

- It is not a generic "there are people who would meet you" reassurance. The specificity is the move. A generic reassurance does not collapse the generalization; the specific named person does.

- It is not a recommendation to seek out the named person. The named person is a counterexample to the universality, not a prescription. The person being coached does not have to meet Christy Tripp to receive the benefit of the move.

- It is not flattery of the named person. The named person is being used as evidence, not praised. The warmth is real but the move is functional.

- It is not [[live-group-consent-as-reality-check]]. That move uses the live audience to vote on "is this too much?" This move uses a specific known person (who may or may not be in the audience) as a single named counterexample.

- It is not [[find-your-people]] (if exists). That broader frame is about building community. This move is the in-session counter to the generalization that no community exists.
