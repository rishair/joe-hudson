# Lean Into the Connection Mode You Avoid (And Deepen the One You Tend Toward)

## The Move

For the lonely person, the prescription has two parts that go together:

1. **Lean into the connection mode you tend to avoid.** If you are always with people but lonely, lean into time alone with yourself. If you are always alone but lonely, lean into being with people.

2. **Deepen connection in the mode you tend toward.** In the mode you already inhabit, find a deeper quality of connection than you have been getting. If you are with people a lot, share more of yourself in those interactions. If you are alone a lot, find a deeper connection to yourself in that solitude.

The move is two-handed because each side does what the other cannot. The avoided mode breaks the structural pattern. The deepened familiar mode addresses the quality problem inside the existing configuration.

"Try the thing that you're avoiding. And lean into the connection to self or other available there. And also in the one that you tend towards, how do you lean into the connection available in that version?" -- Brett Kistler, Dear Lonely Person

"Do the one you don't have the propensity to do, lean in there, and then figure out how to be more connected in the one that you tend to do. That's exactly — that's beautifully said." -- Joe Hudson, Dear Lonely Person

## Why Both Halves

Either half alone is insufficient.

**Just leaning into the avoided mode** without deepening the familiar one risks running the same surface pattern in a new configuration. The person who was alone in a crowd starts being alone-in-themselves (which is a different problem). The person who was alone in solitude starts being alone-in-a-crowd (which is the other type of loneliness).

**Just deepening the familiar mode** without leaning into the avoided one risks the shame structure remaining unchallenged. The person who is alone in a crowd may get better at vulnerable sharing in the social field but never develop the capacity to be alone with themselves, which leaves them dependent on social contact. The person who is alone in solitude may develop deeper self-connection but never test whether they can be seen by others, which leaves the original shame intact.

The two-handed move ensures both axes shift. The structure changes (avoided mode is no longer avoided) AND the quality changes (familiar mode is no longer surface).

## Why This Is Hard

Each lonely person tends to think the answer is more of what they already do. The alone-in-crowd person thinks they need more or better social contact. The alone-in-isolation person thinks they need more or better solitude. Both are wrong about the same thing: the missing piece is in the mode they have been avoiding.

The avoidance is structural, not preferential. The alone-in-crowd person avoids solitude because being alone with themselves activates the shame — the company has been a way of not having to meet themselves. The alone-in-isolation person avoids contact because being with people activates the shame — the solitude has been a way of not having to be seen.

The move asks each to do precisely the thing the shame has been organizing to prevent. Hence: titrate. See [[loneliness-titrates-like-exercise-pain]]. Hence: experiments. See [[16-experiments-for-connection]]. The move is not "go do the scary thing." It is "design a small experiment in the avoided mode and run it."

## How To Apply It in Coaching

1. **Identify which type the person is.** See [[two-types-of-lonely-person]].

2. **Name both halves of the move.** "There are two things to try. One is to lean into the mode you don't usually do — and that's going to feel uncomfortable because you've been avoiding it. The other is to deepen connection in the mode you do do — and that's going to feel uncomfortable because you've been keeping it surface."

3. **Get specific about what each looks like for them.** For the alone-in-crowd person: "What would 30 minutes of intentional time with yourself look like this week?" For the alone-in-isolation person: "What is one small, brief, deep connection you could have this week?" The specificity matters because both halves are otherwise abstract.

4. **Use the experiment form.** See [[16-experiments-for-connection]]. The list should include items in both halves of the move — both avoided-mode and familiar-mode-deepened.

5. **Anticipate the resistance.** The avoided half will produce more resistance than the familiar half. Name this. The body wanting to skip the avoided experiments is the diagnostic that the avoided experiments are exactly what is needed.

## Example Configurations

**Person who is always at work, always in meetings, always with people, deeply lonely:**

- Avoided mode (solitude): start with 20 minutes alone in the morning before phone or screens. Build to a full Saturday with no plans. Add: meditation, walk in nature, journal practice.
- Familiar mode deepened (people): in the next conversation that allows it, share one thing one notch more real than you would have. The next, one notch more. The goal is depth, not volume.

**Person who lives alone, works from home, sees almost no one, deeply lonely:**

- Avoided mode (contact): one brief, intentional contact this week. Coffee with one person. A call with a friend. A 12-step meeting. The goal is one experience of being met, not full social re-engagement.
- Familiar mode deepened (solitude): notice how the solitude is being used. Is it nourishing or numbing? Add quality to the solitude — actual practice, actual presence, actual attention to what is good — rather than just being alone with the phone.

**Person who oscillates: heavy social weeks then total withdrawal:**

- The oscillation pattern itself suggests both modes are being avoided in their nourishing form. The social weeks are about not being alone with self; the withdrawal weeks are about not being seen. Both halves of the move apply with extra weight: find self-contact during the social weeks; find any real contact during the withdrawal weeks.

## What This Is Not

This is not "go socialize more" advice for the lonely person. It is structurally cross-directional: the prescription depends on which way the person has been avoiding. Telling the alone-in-isolation person to "deepen their solitude" is wrong (they have too much already); telling the alone-in-crowd person to "find their tribe" is wrong (they have a tribe and are lonely inside it).

This is also not the same as [[ten-percent-more-connected-experiment]], though they pair well. The 10% experiment is about adding connection-quality to whatever is already happening. The lean-into-the-avoided-mode move is about adding a structurally different mode of connection entirely. The 10% is in the texture of the day; the lean-in is in the architecture of the week.

## Tone

Concrete, practical, slightly playful. The two-handed structure is offered as obvious once seen, even though most people have been doing only one or the other. The "do both" framing is what makes the move different from generic loneliness advice.
