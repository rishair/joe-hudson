# It's Okay to Run, I Don't Have Any Judgment

## The Move

When the person is running from contact with something — usually love, presence, or an emotion they have been trying to access — explicitly accept the running. "It's okay to run, by the way. I don't have any judgment."

The move is offered while the running is happening. Not before, not after. Joe is naming what the person is doing in real time and removing the moral charge from it in the same moment. The running is allowed. The fact of the running is data, not failure.

The move is essential because the alternative — fighting the running, pushing the person to stay with the contact — is the same gear as the running itself. Pushing-through-the-running and running-from-contact are both forms of management. The acceptance breaks the management.

## Why This Works

The running is being held in place by the system's belief that the running is wrong. The wrongness produces shame about the running. The shame is itself another form of running (see [[feel-bad-as-the-running]]). The whole structure depends on the moral framing.

When the coach removes the moral framing — "it's okay, I don't have any judgment" — the structure loses one of its supports. The running no longer has to defend itself. The running can be what it is: a body move the system makes when contact is too much. A body move that's understandable, not a sign of failure.

Once the running is allowed, something paradoxical happens: the running becomes less necessary. The system was running partly to escape the shame of running. With the shame removed, the running has less to do. Some of the contact starts to land. Not all of it. Just enough that the body registers ("tingling," "goosebumps," "different but goosebumps").

This is the same structural mechanism as [[welcome-the-trying]] and [[welcome-the-pattern-by-naming-its-brilliance]]. The pattern dissolves when welcomed, not when fought. The welcoming has to be specific: it's okay to run. Not generic ("everything is okay"), not abstract ("we welcome all parts"). Specific to what's happening right now.

## What This Move Produces

The visible signature: the running often continues for another beat or two and then softens. The person reports something landing — often body sensation (tingling, goosebumps, warmth, breath dropping). The contact the running was avoiding becomes partly available.

The verbal report is often something like "I feel a little bit of it" or "something's coming through" or "I feel bad for running" (which opens the next move, [[feel-bad-as-the-running]]). The "I feel bad for running" response is itself a sign that the meta-acceptance has not yet fully landed; the system is still operating from the moral framing. Joe re-issues the acceptance and stays.

The deeper landing produces a shift in the loop itself. The person recognizes "I get into this loop a lot." That recognition is the loop becoming visible as a loop, which is the first step toward its dissolution. Joe accepts the loop too: "yeah, you do. That's okay." The acceptance scales up to the meta-level.

## What Not to Do

- **Do not say "it's okay" while pushing the person to stop running.** The contradiction destroys the move. The acceptance has to be actual, not strategic.
- **Do not explain why running is natural.** The move depends on its directness. Adding explanation moves the person into the head, where the running can continue dressed as analysis.
- **Do not deploy this move without staying through the running.** The acceptance only works if the coach is present for the running itself. Issuing the acceptance and then redirecting to "okay, now let's try again" defeats the move.
- **Do not use this move when the running needs to be confronted.** Some patterns require direct confrontation (see [[the-trying-is-the-pattern-firing-now]]). This move is for the running that is already in the open and being shamed by the person; it is not for running that needs to be made visible first.
- **Do not deliver the move with heaviness.** "It's okay to run" has to sound light. Heavy tone here installs the moral framing it's trying to remove.

## Dialogue Example

The full sequence from the source session:

- **Joe:** "That's what you're doing. Okay, running, running, running. There you go. You let — you're letting some in. See what it's like to let, let a little more in. Running, running, running, running. Running, running. It's okay to run, by the way. I don't have any judgment."
- **Client:** "I feel bad for running."
- **Joe:** "It's natural. That's how you run. One of the ways you run is to feel bad."
- **Client:** "Feel like I get into this loop a lot. I get into you and then I get into this."
- **Joe:** "Yeah, you do. That's okay."
- **Client:** "Feeling tingliness, oh, through my body and shoulders. It's like, it's like different but goosebumps."
- **Joe:** "Yeah, yeah, it's like you're, you're letting some of it in."
- *(Source: Why You Can't Stop Trying So Hard)*

The full arc shows the move as part of a larger meta-acceptance pattern. Each iteration of the running is named and accepted. The loop itself is named and accepted. The contact begins to land somatically (tingling, goosebumps). The work is the staying, not the breakthrough.

## Tone

Light, matter-of-fact, slightly playful. The "by the way" is doing work — it suggests the acceptance is casual, not a special accommodation. The acceptance is the default stance, not a deliberate intervention.

The voice is warm but not therapeutic. Joe is not soothing. He is reporting the moral situation: the running is allowed. The lightness is what keeps the acceptance from becoming yet another instruction the person has to comply with.

## When to Use This Move

- When the person is running from contact and the running is visible in the room
- When the person has already started judging themselves for the running and the self-judgment is operating as continued running
- After [[why-dont-you-just-love-yourself-as-you-are-right-now]] when the love directed at the self triggers the run
- When the person reports "I want to run" or "I want to go away" or "I can't stay with this"
- When a pattern is happening live and the person is fighting the pattern and the fighting is making the pattern stronger
- When the coaching field needs the moral charge removed so the body can register the contact
