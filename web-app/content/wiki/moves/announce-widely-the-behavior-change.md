# Announce the Behavior Change Widely (To Shift the Identity-Held-By-Others)

## The Move

After a recognition that you have been running an interpersonal pattern you don't want to run, announce the change to as many people in your life as possible. Wider is better. Ask for explicit acknowledgment that they received the announcement.

This is the first step of the [[four-as-of-behavior-change|4 A's]]. It is distinct from [[announce-the-behavior-change-as-meta-context]], which is the move that asks the receivers how they want the new behavior delivered. The wide-announcement move is about *exposure* and *identity restructuring*. Its function is to make the old behavior structurally harder to return to.

## The Two Mechanisms

**1. Identity is held partly by other people's expectations.**

Identity is not just an internal phenomenon. A significant portion of it is held in place by how the people around you see and respond to you. The old pattern persisted partly because the people you operate with expected you to run it. Announcing the change to all of them disrupts that expectation in one move.

"Part of my identity is held in place by who I think I am, but part of my identity is held in place by who you think I am. And so if I'm used to having an interaction with you where you're like, oh, you're Joe, you're gonna take care of me instead of hold me accountable, then that's how you're going to treat me. But if I've announced to you, I'm not doing that anymore, the next time it happens, you're going to be like, okay, well, what's going to go down here? So you're not holding me into an identity because I've announced it widely." -- Joe, How to Change Your Interpersonal Patterns

The mechanism is that other people who know the announcement will now read your behavior through a different lens. They have new information. The behavior they see will be filtered by "oh, they said they were working on this." This filter is what removes the implicit social pressure that was helping the old pattern persist.

**2. Wide announcement forces the avoided feelings up front.**

The fears the wide announcement creates — reputational loss, public failure, being seen as inconsistent — are usually the exact fears the pattern was protecting you from. By feeling them as the announcement happens, you stop needing the pattern to avoid them.

"This is often gonna bring up like a feeling of reputational damage, like, oh, people are gonna think less of me, or, oh, I'm making sure that I am doing something. If I fail, everybody's gonna see that I fail. If I announce to everybody I'm changing and then I don't change... And you have to have all those significant emotions that are happening." -- Joe, How to Change Your Interpersonal Patterns

The narrower the announcement, the less the feelings get surfaced and the weaker the change. The wider the announcement, the more the avoided feelings have to be felt now, and the more effective the change.

"The wider you can announce it, the more effective it is that you're going to change, right? Because you have more people who know about what's happened for you. And it also shifts your identity." -- Joe, How to Change Your Interpersonal Patterns

## The Crucible Framing

A second way Joe frames the wide-announcement: triangulating yourself in a crucible. The breadth of the audience is what creates the heat. You are pinned in by your own public statement and by the matrix of people now expecting you to follow through. The crucible image is positive — heat as the condition for transformation, not heat as punishment.

"The trick with announcing is announcing widely, widely. There's a way that you're really putting yourself, triangulating yourself in the crucible here." -- Joe Hudson, You See The Pattern. Your Life Hasn't Changed. Now What?

The implication for clients: if the announcement feels safe and easy, it isn't wide enough yet. The crucible heat is the diagnostic that the announcement is doing the work the move is for. The discomfort of triangulating yourself is the structural condition for the identity grip of the old pattern to actually break.

## The Acknowledgment Requirement

The execution detail that distinguishes real wide announcement from cover: ask for explicit acknowledgment from every recipient that they received the announcement.

"I asked every single person in the company to acknowledge that I'd written it. Just send me a happy face or a thumbs up or whatever. I, like, very much wanted to make sure everybody saw it." -- Joe, How to Change Your Interpersonal Patterns

Without acknowledgment, you can tell yourself you announced when really you sent a message you suspect was skimmed. The acknowledgment closes that loophole. It also makes the announcement a real social event rather than a private speech-act, which is what the identity-restructuring requires.

## The Feelings That Have To Get Felt

To make the wide announcement *cleanly*, certain feelings have to be moved through:

- Fear of reputational loss.
- Fear that you'll mess up and people will see it.
- Fear of being seen as bad or inconsistent.
- Grief that you've been running the pattern for as long as you have.

"To announce it widely and cleanly, I have to feel through the grief of maybe that I'm gonna mess it up, maybe that I'm gonna be seen as bad, maybe I'm not gonna be consistent. I have to feel through all of that stuff, which usually you don't do until you've lived through it. But if you feel through it first, then often you're not gonna be living through it." -- Joe, How to Change Your Interpersonal Patterns

The structural insight: usually we feel these feelings *as a result of* the pattern (the rejection, the failure, the inconsistency). The announcement reverses the order: feel the feelings before the failure, and the pattern often stops generating the failure to begin with. The feelings were always going to be felt; the question was whether they got felt up front (in service of changing) or down the line (as the consequence of not changing).

## What This Is Not

- Not a brave public commitment intended to impress people. The announcement is not about being seen as someone who is changing. It is about restructuring the social field.
- Not a meta-context invitation. That is a different move: see [[announce-the-behavior-change-as-meta-context]]. The wide announcement names what you've been doing and what you're going to stop doing. The meta-context move asks how the new behavior should be received.
- Not a contingent statement. "I'm going to try to do this if you help me" hedges the announcement. "I have been doing X. This isn't how I want to be. I'm going to stop." The shift is owned by the announcer.
- Not a request for permission. The change is not up for vote. The announcement is informational.

## What Joe Does With Resistance to the Wideness

When a person hears "announce to as many people as possible" and immediately starts narrowing the audience ("I could just tell my partner"), the narrowing is the pattern reasserting itself. The narrower scope keeps the avoided feelings small enough to not require change. The widening is precisely what produces the change.

The move from the coach is to name what the narrowing is doing. "You're trying to keep this small enough to not have to feel it. The wideness is what makes it work." Then return to the recommendation: as wide as possible. The discomfort with the wideness is the diagnostic that the wideness is correct.

## Tone

The announcement itself is delivered direct, owned, and upright. Not heavy, not apologetic in the announcement-line itself (the apology comes in step 2). The tone of the announcement signals what kind of change it is. A heavy announcement signals shame and is downstream of [[shame-drives-behavior]]. An upright announcement signals that the change is empowered and the speaker is in their own agency.

## When To Recommend This Move

- When a client has just had a recognition about an interpersonal pattern (team, family, partner, kids) and is ready to act on it.
- When the recognition is fresh enough that the felt sense of "this isn't how I want to be" is still alive.
- When the client starts to narrow the audience instinctively — that's the moment to point at the wideness as the leverage.
- As the first concrete action after a coaching breakthrough where the pattern was clearly named.

## What This Move Produces

Often produces immediate emotional movement: the announcement is itself a feeling event. Grief and relief frequently arrive together. The recipients respond, often more supportively than expected. The presumption-of-judgment that was holding the pattern in place gets disconfirmed by the actual responses.

The downstream effect is that the next time the situation arises where you might run the old pattern, the social context is different. People know what to expect now. The pattern has lost its hiding place.
