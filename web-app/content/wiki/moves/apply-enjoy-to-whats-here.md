# Apply Enjoy-Of to Whatever Is Here

## The Move

When a person describes seeking enjoyment elsewhere -- in the future, after some condition is met, in a different activity than the current one -- Joe redirects the target of enjoyment to whatever is actually happening right now. The current rushing. The current shame. The current stress. The current chronic pain. The current inability to come up with an answer.

The move is small but decisive. The person's framing assumes enjoyment is located somewhere other than where they are. Joe relocates it. Enjoyment becomes a relationship to whatever is here, not a property of a different experience.

The verbal form of this move is the question [[how-would-you-enjoy-x]]. The move is the underlying coaching choice: instead of helping the person get to a better experience, help them apply enjoyment to this one.

## When Joe Uses It

- Whenever the person reaches for [[i-keep-postponing-enjoyment]] in any form ("when my pain ends... when this project ends... when I have time...").
- When a difficult feeling is present in the room and the person is performing their way past it.
- When the person is stressed and is trying to escape the stress.
- When the person is in shame and is trying to outrun it.
- When the person says "I'd enjoy this if X were not happening." X is the candidate for enjoyment.
- When the person is reporting glimpses of enjoyment and pulling out — Joe applies the move to the act of pulling out: "how do you enjoy oscillating in and out of it?"
- When the person is struggling with a specific kind of social or performance situation (fast-paced group conversations, public speaking, navigating complex interactions). The move redirects from "how do I get the result right" to "how do I enjoy this thing I am already in." A lot of the difficulty drops out naturally when the target shifts from performance to enjoyment.

## The Group Conversation Application

A specific application of the move that shows its operating principle. When someone reports difficulty staying in VIEW or being themselves during fast-paced group conversations, the route is not more practice on the conversational mechanics. The route is shifting the target from getting it right to enjoying the interaction.

"Just focus on enjoying yourself, not on the results of the group interaction. How do I enjoy this group interaction, this fast-paced group interaction? How do you enjoy that? If you can focus there, a lot of this stuff goes away naturally." -- Joe Hudson

The mechanism: the difficulty in being present and responsive in group conversations almost always traces to partiality (wanting to be liked, not wanting to be hated, wanting to look smart). The partiality produces self-judgment about what is about to be said. The self-judgment is what blocks the expression. See [[self-judgment-protects-from-emotion]].

Shifting the target from "perform well in this conversation" to "enjoy this conversation" routes around the partiality without having to directly dismantle it. The enjoyment target is incompatible with the rightness target. Whichever one is primary, the other recedes.

## What It Produces

A brief category disturbance. The person was operating on a model where enjoyment is selective (you enjoy enjoyable things). The move asserts a different model: enjoyment is non-selective (you can enjoy whatever you are doing). The disturbance is the gap between the two models. The gap is where the work happens.

After the disturbance, several things often happen:
- The person's body finds a small allowing toward the thing they had been pushing against.
- The thing they were pushing against transforms in quality. The shame, met, softens into something usable. The pain, met, opens into spaciousness. The stress, met, becomes presence.
- The person discovers that the enjoyment they were seeking in the imagined future was, by structure, never going to arrive there. It can only arrive here.

## What Joe Does Next

- Stay with whatever just happened. If the person reports a body movement or a small softening, name it without amplifying it. "Notice that."
- If the person finds a small enjoyment of the difficult thing, ask the inverse: "and how would you NOT enjoy it?" The pairing makes the brake visible. See [[whats-stopping-you-from-enjoying-x]].
- If a deeper feeling arrives (anguish, grief, rage), make room. Apply the move again to whatever just arrived. The grief is now the candidate for enjoyment. Each layer is the same work.
- If the person bypasses the difficult feeling with a story about enjoyment ("I'm just so grateful for everything"), bring them back to what is actually present. See [[manufacturing-the-feeling]].

## Sustained Use in a Single Session

This move can structure an entire session. Joe applied it at least eight times in the chronic-pain coaching session it comes from, each time to a different thing the person was running from:

- Enjoy yourself right now (when the person was describing future enjoyment).
- Enjoy oscillating in and out of enjoyment (when the person was reporting glimpses).
- Enjoy rushing through your day (when the person named the rushing as the problem).
- Enjoy the sadness (when sadness arrived in the room).
- Enjoy the shame (when shame about being a victim was named).
- Enjoy not being able to come up with an answer (when the person was stressed about not having a response).
- Enjoy the pain (when the chronic pain was the field).
- Enjoy being a victim for a moment (when the resistance to victim-feeling was the brake).

The repetition is not random. It is the same move applied to each successive layer that emerges. The person's experience teaches them by the end of the session that enjoyment is available regardless of what is happening, and that the previous structure (postpone until X is solved) was itself the obstacle.

## What This Move Is Not

- Not bypass. Applying enjoyment to shame is not the same as deciding the shame is not there. The shame is fully felt while being enjoyed. See [[dont-name-emotion-to-avoid-feeling]].
- Not positive reframing. The thing is not being relabeled as good. It is being met with allowance rather than resistance.
- Not enforced cheerfulness. The tone is curious, not bright. The person who finds a small allowance of their pain is not pretending the pain is pleasant. They are stopping the second-order suffering of resisting the pain. See [[suffering-vs-pain]].
- Not a substitute for action. The person can apply this move and then take the next obvious step. The two are compatible. The move addresses the relationship to what is, not the actions taken from there. See [[running-toward-yourself]].

## Tone

Light, curious, playful when possible. The move loses its medicine when delivered heavily. The question "how would you enjoy your shame?" works partly because it sounds slightly absurd at first. The absurdity opens a small crack in the person's certainty that the shame cannot be enjoyed. Through the crack, the small allowance can arrive.
