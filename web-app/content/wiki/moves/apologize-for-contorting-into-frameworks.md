# Apologize to Yourself for Trying to Fit Into Someone Else's Framework

## The Move

Once the person has named — usually with Joe's help — that they have been trying to contort themselves into someone else's framework for years, offer them an apology to themselves. Not a coach-led apology. An offered apology: "you want to apologize to yourself for doing that?" The person speaks the apology aloud, in their own words, to the part of themselves that has been forced into shapes that were not the self.

"You want to apologize to yourself for doing that?" -- Joe Hudson, What Do I Offer The World?

The apology completes the recognition. The naming of the pattern by itself is intellectual. The apology brings the recognition into the body and into the relationship-with-self that was being damaged by the contortion.

## When Joe Uses This

The move comes out after the structural seeing is complete. Specifically:

- The person has named [[contorting-into-someone-elses-framework]] as something they have done (and confirmed it has been "many" times)
- The phrase "awkward contortionist experience" or similar has been spoken
- The person is in the recognition that the framework-search is foreign to them
- The naming alone is producing a small relief, but the body has not yet released the years of self-imposed contortion
- The person is ready to address the part of themselves that was being contorted, rather than just talking about the pattern

The move is not for every coaching moment. It is specifically for moments when self-recognition has happened and what is needed is a felt-act of reconciliation with the self that was being betrayed.

## What the Apology Sounds Like

The person crafts the words themselves. The guest in this transcript said:

"I'm so sorry for trying to force you into somebody else's rigid framework when really that has nothing to do with me. And I love you. Thank you." -- Guest, What Do I Offer The World?

The structure visible in this apology:
- An acknowledgement of the specific harm ("trying to force you into somebody else's rigid framework")
- A naming of why the harm was wrong ("when really that has nothing to do with me")
- An expression of love ("I love you")
- A thank you (often to the part of the self that survived the contortion, or to the apology itself for being received)

The person does not need to follow this exact structure. The structure emerges from the felt-quality of the upright apology. See [[upright-apology]] for the body and tone the apology should have.

## How to Do It

1. **Ensure the recognition has landed.** The apology is not a substitute for the seeing. The person has to actually see that they have been contorting before the apology has anything real to address.

2. **Offer, do not impose.** "You want to apologize to yourself for doing that?" The wanting is given to the person. They can take it or not. The offering tone matters; an imposed apology has the same self-betrayal quality as the framework-fitting.

3. **Let them find the words.** Do not script the apology. The words have to come from the person. Whatever they say is what the part of them being apologized to needs to hear.

4. **Watch the body.** A real upright apology produces a body shift. The person softens. Sometimes there is grief. Sometimes there is laughter. Sometimes both. Watch for the shift; it confirms the apology landed.

5. **Receive the apology with them.** "You're welcome. What a pleasure." Joe's response in the source transcript validates that the apology has been received — both by the part of the person being apologized to, and by the larger field that is holding the recognition.

## What Tends to Happen

The apology often produces:

- **A body softening.** The years of contortion held tension. The apology lets the tension release. The person often visibly relaxes.
- **Brief grief.** Sometimes tears arrive. The grief is for the years of effort spent on the wrong project. See [[grief-at-the-disproportion]].
- **Quiet relief.** Often the dominant response is relief. "Thank you" emerges naturally because the part being apologized to has been waiting for the recognition.
- **A reorientation.** After the apology, the person's energy is different. The framework-search has stopped. The attention is now on the actual self, not on the framework that was being approached.

The apology does not need to "fix" anything. The fixing happened in the seeing. The apology marks the seeing in the body.

## Tone

Warm, soft, slightly celebratory. The apology is being offered as a gift, not as a corrective. "You want to apologize to yourself for doing that?" carries the energy of "here is something nice you could do for yourself right now." Not "here is what you should do."

The receiving response ("you're welcome, what a pleasure") matches. The apology being received by the field around the person is part of the work. The pleasure is real. Watching someone reconcile with themselves is a pleasure.

## Anti-Patterns

- Do not script the apology. The person's words have to come from them. A scripted apology is itself a contortion into someone else's framework.
- Do not require the apology to produce a specific emotion. Some apologies produce grief. Others produce relief. Others produce stillness. Let what comes come.
- Do not extract the apology. If the person does not want to apologize, do not push. The offer is not a demand. See [[dont-extract-apologies]].
- Do not let the apology become self-flagellation. The upright apology has no shame in it. If shame arrives, the apology has slipped into the old contortion-pattern in a new costume.
- Do not skip the recognition step. An apology without the underlying seeing is just words. The seeing has to come first.

## When This Move Generalizes

The same move applies to many other patterns of self-betrayal, not just framework-fitting. Wherever the person has been doing something to themselves for years that hurt them, the apology to self can complete the recognition:

- Apologize to self for years of self-criticism (see [[apologize-as-daily-practice-for-bad-habits]])
- Apologize to self for years of putting others first to the point of self-erasure
- Apologize to self for years of believing the inner critic
- Apologize to self for years of staying in a relationship that was harming
- Apologize to self for years of overriding the body's signals

The structure is the same: recognition → apology offering → person speaks → body releases → reorientation. The content varies; the move is constant.

## The Companion Practice

The single in-session apology often opens the door to a [[apologize-as-daily-practice-for-bad-habits]] practice. If the contortion-pattern is long-standing and likely to recur, a daily upright apology can keep the recognition active and prevent the old pattern from quietly reasserting.
