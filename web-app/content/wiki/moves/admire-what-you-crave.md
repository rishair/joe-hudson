# Admire What You Crave in Others

## The Move

When the person describes resenting another person for something that person has, is, or does, redirect them to admire the same thing. The admiration produces what the resentment was trying to produce, without any of the cost.

"Take something that you resent somebody for having, and then just see the admiration, experience the admiration -- because if you resent it, you admire it. And then just feel the admiration and see what it does to your resentment. It just makes it fade away." -- Joe Hudson, How To Stop Being Resentful

The move is operationally simple: name the thing being resented, then ask the person to feel the admiration that is also there. The admiration is already present; it is just being covered by the resentment. Surfacing the admiration is the entire intervention.

## Why It Works

The structural insight: resentment of what another person has is always also envy, and envy is always also a disowned want. The resentment is doing three things at once:

- Pointing at a quality the resenter has not given themselves permission to want
- Pushing the quality away by refusing to recognize it as desirable
- Producing the chronic sour energy that suppressed envy carries

Admiration cuts all three at the root:

- It openly acknowledges the quality as desirable
- It treats the quality as available rather than off-limits
- It releases the held envy by letting it be what it actually is

"When you admire something in somebody, you're giving yourself permission to have it, but you're also satisfying that itch in you. It's not a direct satisfaction, but there's this great satisfaction where you just go, 'Oh.'" -- Joe Hudson, How To Stop Being Resentful

The "oh" is the body recognizing that the wanting has been allowed. The wanting was the source of the resentment. Allowing the wanting dissolves the source.

## Resentment Is Rejection, Admiration Is Invitation

The move also changes what the resenter is energetically transmitting to the other person and to the universe.

"It also makes it much more likely that you get to have the thing that you admire. Because when you admire something, it's an invitation, whereas if you resent it, it is a rejection of the thing itself. Emotionally, you are rejecting the thing." -- Joe Hudson, How To Stop Being Resentful

Resentment of a quality says: "I do not want this near me." It pushes the quality away from the resenter's life. Admiration of the same quality says: "I welcome this." It invites the quality closer.

The same person who has been resenting their partner's freedom and therefore having no freedom of their own can switch to admiring their partner's freedom and start finding freedom in their own life. The shift is not just emotional. It is structural. The energy of rejection produces less of the rejected thing; the energy of invitation produces more of it.

This is connected to [[craving-is-the-pushing-away]] in reverse: where craving pushes away the thing being grasped at, admiration draws closer the thing being recognized.

## The Marriage Application

The most common live use of this move is in marriages where resentment runs in both directions. Each partner has something the other does not, and each resents the other for having it.

"This kind of resentment often happens in marriages. So maybe one person is like, 'Hey, I really resent the fact that you get to work all the time and feel powerful.' And the other person says, 'Oh, I resent the fact that you get to be home all the time and really show care in your work, and you don't have to be all hard strong.' And that just breeds this resentment back and forth in a marriage." -- Joe Hudson, How To Stop Being Resentful

The reframe both directions:

"Instead, an easy fix is to say, 'Hey, I really admire how you go out into the world every day and make things happen for us.' And the other person saying, 'Hey, I really admire how much you get to be home and just nurture and take care of everything for the family.'" -- Joe Hudson, How To Stop Being Resentful

The structural insight for couples: each spouse is usually resenting the other for the very quality they have not been letting themselves develop. The resentment is the disowned part naming itself. The admiration both releases the resentment and opens the door for each spouse to develop the disowned quality in themselves.

See [[we-have-built-up-resentment]] for the coaching of built-up resentment in long marriages. The admiration move is one of the cleanest first interventions.

## How to Run the Move

1. Locate the specific thing being resented. Not "I resent her" but "I resent that she gets to be carefree" or "I resent that he doesn't have to worry about money."

2. Ask the person to say the admiration version out loud, in its specific form. "I admire how carefree she is." "I admire that he carries the financial weight."

3. Watch the body. The first time the admiration is said, there is often resistance: the body tightens, the voice flattens, the person hedges. That is the disowned quality refusing to be owned. Stay with it. Have the person say it again.

4. When the admiration lands cleanly, the body softens. There is often a small "oh" or exhale. That is the work happening.

5. Notice what becomes available. The person can usually now name what they want for themselves. The wanting was waiting under the resentment. Once the resentment relaxes, the wanting can be said directly.

The move is fast. The whole sequence often takes under two minutes. The transformation is in the body, not in the intellectual understanding.

## When the Move Does Not Land

If the admiration produces no shift, two diagnostics:

- The resentment is not actually about what it claims to be about. Acute injustice (genuine betrayal, abuse, broken agreement) produces resentment that is not envy-disguised and does not respond to admiration. Run the diagnostic by asking: "is what they have actually something you would want, or is the issue that they did something to you?" If the latter, the work is not the admiration move; it is direct anger and boundary work.

- The disowned quality is too far in the shadow to be admired yet. The person cannot even let the admiration form on their lips. In this case, the upstream work is [[pointers-to-unseen-parts]] and [[love-the-aspect-through-someone-else]] to bring the quality slowly out of the shadow before the direct admiration move is available.

## The Self-Generosity Application

The same move applies to the resentment people carry against themselves for being stingy with their own admiration of others. Being stingy with admiration tends to track with being stingy with self-admiration. Becoming generous with admiration toward others tends to free up admiration toward self.

The canonical example: [[jesse-and-generous-admiration]] -- a conference organizer whose generosity with admiration produced both his own business growth and a room full of people generously giving each other their gifts. Joe's recognition in the moment: "I realized to myself, like, 'Wow, I'm a little stingy with my admiration. What does it feel like to actually be really generous with my admiration?'" The shift from stingy to generous produced concrete returns: other people started sharing how they had built the things Joe admired, which let Joe learn from them.

## The Difference From Forced Positivity

The move is not "find something nice to say about the person who annoys you." It is not the reframe-as-pretending-to-be-grateful move. It is locating the admiration that is already present and being held down by the resentment.

The test: if the person has to manufacture the admiration, the move is not landing. If the person discovers the admiration is already there underneath the resentment, the move is doing its work. The discovery has a specific felt sense (the "oh"); the manufacture has the flat, dutiful quality of forced positivity.

## Tone

Joe delivers the move with warmth and a small smile. It is presented as a "kind of strange one" and "a head-turner" -- the strangeness is part of how it works, because it cuts directly across the cultural training that says envy is bad and should be pushed away. The lightness of the delivery is part of what makes the move usable; a heavy delivery would invite resistance.
