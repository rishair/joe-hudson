# Reframe the Question From the Behavior to the Blocker Emotion

## The Move

When the person presents a binary decision about a behavior they cannot stop (do I drink or not, do I keep eating sugar or not, do I stay in this job or quit), and they have just named what the behavior is giving them (escape from shame, relief from guilt, avoidance of fear), restate the question. Replace the behavior in the question with the underlying emotion the behavior is managing.

The structural form: "So what if the question was, should I give up [the blocker emotion] or not?"

The canonical example:

- **Person:** "I'm struggling to decide about sobriety... I'm feeling into the shame and guilt piece that I'm trying to escape."
- **Joe:** "It gives you a relief from shame and guilt."
- **Person:** "Yeah. Yeah."
- **Joe:** "Yeah. So what if the question was, um, should I give up shame and guilt or not?"
- **Person:** "Juicy. Gets to the heart of it for sure."

*(Source: Do I Stop Drinking?)*

The move takes the form of the original question seriously while changing its object. The reframe is not "the question is wrong." It is "the question makes sense — and there is a more upstream version of the same question that, if asked, would actually move."

## When Joe Uses This

- When a person presents a habit-change question (drinking, eating, smoking, scrolling) as a fork
- After Joe has asked "what does the behavior give you?" and gotten an answer that names a blocker emotion (shame, guilt, judgment of self)
- When the person has been in the decision loop for a long time without resolution — the looping is the signature of [[treating-it-as-a-decision-when-its-an-emotion]]
- When the surface question is about whether to continue the symptom and the underlying question is about whether to keep avoiding the feeling

The move requires the underlying material to have surfaced first. The reframe works because the person has already named the emotion. Joe just restates the question in that emotion's terms. If Joe reframes before the underlying material has surfaced, the move becomes an assertion of his interpretation rather than a reflection of what the person already knows.

## What It Produces

The reframe usually lands with a recognition that is felt in the body, not in the intellect. The person hears the reframed question and something in them goes "yes, that is actually the question." Joe's client said "juicy" — a word that registers somatic recognition. Another person might say "oh," or "fuck," or simply pause.

The reframe also collapses the rebellion-or-submission loop around the behavior. The original question ("should I stop drinking?") has had shoulds and counter-shoulds running on it for years. The new question ("should I give up shame and guilt?") has not. It arrives clean. The rebellion-or-submission machinery is not yet attached to it.

What also happens: the absurdity of the new question becomes visible. Who would say no to giving up shame and guilt? The asking of the question is almost the answering of it. The work then becomes how, not whether. And the how is the emotional work, not the behavioral work.

## What Joe Does With the Response

The response is rarely a clean "yes, that's the question." More commonly the person makes a sound of recognition, then either drops into the feeling of the reframed question or starts intellectualizing about it. Joe's next move depends on which.

- **If the person drops into feeling:** Joe stays with the feeling. He does not move to action. The work is in the contact with what the question opens. "How does that feel? Not what do you think of it? What's the emotional?"
- **If the person intellectualizes:** Joe redirects to the body. "Out of your head for a minute. Feel it." The reframe was meant to open the feeling-channel; if the intellect closes it again, the move has to be repeated at the body level.
- **If the person resists the reframe:** Joe does not insist. The reframe is offered as a question, not an assertion. If the person does not recognize the underlying material as the actual question, the surface work continues until the underlying material surfaces in a form they recognize.

## Why It Works

The mechanism is structural. Behaviors managed by blocker emotions cannot be changed by behavioral force, because the force re-summons the blocker emotion that the behavior is managing. See [[shame-drives-behavior]]. The cycle is locked.

Reframing the question to the blocker emotion exits the cycle at the structural level. The work moves from "manage the symptom" to "address the cause." The behavioral question dissolves into the emotional question. And the emotional question is one that responds to actual coaching moves (welcoming, feeling, undoing the avoidance), where the behavioral question only responds to willpower (which does not work for this class of problem).

The reframe also performs the [[the-shame-avoidance-loop]] visibly. When the original question is "should I stop drinking?" and the drinking is for relief from shame, the person is using the avoidance of one emotion (shame) to make a decision about a behavior that is managing the same emotion. The loop is invisible from inside the original question. The reframed question makes the loop visible by naming the emotion as the actual subject.
