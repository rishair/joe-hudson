# Skeptical Inquiry vs. Self-Inquiry

## The Distinction

Two activities that look almost identical from the outside, with opposite mechanics and opposite effects.

**Skeptical inquiry** is questioning oriented toward winning. The questioner has a position. The questioning is designed to make the other position lose. The act of questioning is a form of skepticism, defense, or rebellion. The questioner is moving toward being right.

**Self-inquiry** is questioning oriented toward seeing. The questioner does not have a position to defend. The questioning is designed to reveal what is and is not true about every position, including the questioner's own. The act of questioning is a form of opening. The questioner is moving toward being free.

Both activities use the same surface moves — questioning assumptions, looking for holes, surfacing what is not being examined. The difference is what is driving the activity and what it produces.

## Why This Matters

The distinction matters because [[questioning-assumptions]] is the practice both modes use. A person can be doing the skeptical version while thinking they are doing the self-inquiry version. The activity looks the same to them. The effects diverge sharply: skeptical inquiry produces a more rigid person, hardened in a contrarian identity. Self-inquiry produces a softer person, free of any single position.

The misidentification is common because skeptical inquiry feels productive. Each successful debate is a small win. The system rewards it. The wins accumulate into an identity ("I am the one who sees through things"). The identity is invisible to the person holding it because it is reinforced by the same activity it is built on.

Joe's own history with the distinction:

"I kind of avoided questioning the assumption for a bit in a weird way because I saw a lot of people who were naturally questioning the assumption, and it was just them trying to prove themselves right. It was lawyering. It was debate to be right." -- Joe Hudson, Question the Assumption

He also names the second form of the same misidentification — questioning as rebellion:

"Then I questioned, as you said, like a questioning assumption because I'm not this, I'm not that. But that was really just me rebelling against the authority figure that was my dad. You see that a lot with even people who say they're contrarian investors. It's like, really? So tell me about your rebellion against your father who was an authority. It's almost always there." -- Joe Hudson, Question the Assumption

## Signature of Skeptical Inquiry

The recognizable features:

- **Aggressive Socratic energy.** The questioning has bite. The questioner is impatient with the other position.
- **The other position has to be wrong.** The success criterion is the other person seeing they were wrong.
- **The questioner's own position is not on the table.** The skeptic is not equally interested in surfacing the assumptions inside their own view.
- **Identity attached to being the questioner.** "I am the contrarian. I am the skeptic. I am the one who doesn't take things at face value."
- **Energy after the inquiry is the energy of winning or losing.** Not the energy of opening.
- **Defensiveness when attacked.** The skeptic, despite being constantly on the offensive, gets defensive when their own position is questioned. This is the diagnostic.
- **Rebellion-flavor.** The questioning often has the felt quality of pushing against something — an authority, a convention, a parent figure. Even when no one is present, the rebellion is the energy.

## Signature of Self-Inquiry

The recognizable features:

- **The questioner's own positions get questioned with the same rigor.** Whatever the questioner is doing to others, they are doing to themselves more.
- **No single position is being protected.** Each question, including ones the questioner started with, is on the table.
- **Discomfort is present.** Self-inquiry runs into the load-bearing assumptions of the inquirer's own life. It is not a pleasant activity in the way debating others is. See [[disorienting-middle-of-self-inquiry]].
- **No defensiveness when the inquirer is challenged.** The challenge is welcome. It is more material to inquire into.
- **The energy after is opening, not winning.** Even if the inquiry produces a clearer understanding, the felt quality is "more space" rather than "I beat the other side."
- **No contrarian identity.** The self-inquirer does not need to be the one who doesn't believe X. They are willing to find truth in X and not-X both.

## The Rebellion-Driven Subvariant

A specific form of skeptical inquiry worth naming: questioning that is fueled by rebellion against an authority figure, often a parent. This form has its own signature:

- The questioner is "against" some position, where the position is structurally aligned with the original authority figure.
- The questioner's freedom from the position is felt as freedom from the authority.
- The questioner cannot rest until the position has been refuted.
- Other people's belief in the position is felt as a threat or an offense.

The structural problem: as long as the questioning is fueled by rebellion, the rebellion is still controlled by what it is rebelling against. The supposed independence is dependence dressed up. The contrarian investor is still being controlled by the orthodoxy they oppose; they need it as the thing to be opposite of.

Joe is explicit that this was his own first form of the practice:

"I discovered the way that I was using it at that time was very much still being controlled by the system because I was rebelling against something. So I couldn't see the truth in the thing itself. It was just to find the other way that was more right. It wasn't actually seeing the truth in both sides. And the lack of truth in both sides." -- Joe Hudson, Question the Assumption

The rebellion-driven form is a real form of inquiry but a partial one. The next stage is being willing to find truth on the rebellion-target's side. This is the move that the rebellion structurally prevents.

## How the Distinction Reveals Itself

The cleanest test: notice what happens when someone attacks the questioner's own position.

**The skeptic gets defensive.** The position the skeptic was protecting (often a contrarian one) reveals itself when challenged. The defensiveness is the data.

**The self-inquirer welcomes the attack as material.** It is more grist for the same mill. There is no position to defend.

A subtler test: notice the energy of the activity. Skeptical inquiry has a charge — the small pleasure of taking another position apart. Self-inquiry has a steadiness — the slow loosening of held positions, including one's own.

A third test: notice what is being avoided. Skeptical inquiry has favorites. Some positions get questioned ruthlessly, others get a pass. The pattern of which positions get the questioning and which get the pass reveals the inquirer's actual loyalties.

## How a Coach Uses This Distinction

Several coaching applications:

**When a person describes their inquiry practice with pride:** worth checking the signature. The pride itself can be a clue. Self-inquiry tends to produce a humbler relationship to the practice (because the inquirer keeps finding themselves wrong about things they were sure of). Skeptical inquiry tends to produce an identity attached to being the one who sees through.

**When a person is in active rebellion:** the rebellion itself can be the inquiry-target. "What is the rebellion against? What would happen if you were willing to see truth on the rebellion-target's side?" The point is not to make them stop being against what they're against. It is to see whether the rebellion is the structure or whether it is just the current content.

**When a person reports defensiveness:** worth treating the defensiveness as data about which positions are still operating as load-bearing. The skeptic and the self-inquirer both encounter defensiveness; the difference is whether they treat it as a problem to dismiss or as a sign of where the inquiry still has work to do.

**When a person is "above" the inquiry process:** the signal of skeptical-inquiry-as-identity. The self-inquirer is in the inquiry, not above it. The "above" posture is the identity to inquire into.

## The Distinction Is Developmental, Not Categorical

Many people start in skeptical inquiry and move toward self-inquiry over time. Joe describes his own trajectory: avoidance of the practice because he saw it being lawyered → his own rebellion-driven version → eventually the deep self-inquiry that produced the destabilizing week and a half → the integrated practice.

The distinction is not a moral verdict on the skeptical-inquirer. It is a developmental map. The question for the coach is "where on this map is the person?" not "which side are they on?"

The transition between modes is often gradual. Brief windows of self-inquiry open inside what is mostly skeptical inquiry, and over time those windows widen.

## Why the Skeptical Form Persists

Skeptical inquiry is sustained by the rewards it produces:

- The pleasure of being right.
- The pleasure of seeing through what others believe.
- The identity of being a critical thinker.
- The social bonding of shared contrarianism.
- The (false) sense of freedom from the system being questioned.

Self-inquiry does not produce these rewards in the same way. It produces a more subtle reward (the felt loosening of identification) that is not visible to people who have not experienced it. From the outside, the self-inquirer can look less impressive than the skeptic. From the inside, the freedoms are different orders of magnitude.

This is part of why the transition is gradual. The skeptical rewards are immediate; the self-inquiry rewards are delayed. The system that is reinforced by the immediate rewards has to be willing to forgo them to access the delayed ones.

## Tone

The distinction is held without contempt for the skeptical form. The skeptical form is a real form of inquiry, often the first form anyone encounters. Joe does not write off the people who are doing it; he describes the limitation it has and points at what becomes available next.

## Upstream (What Leads Here)

- When a person describes themselves as a critical thinker, contrarian, or skeptic with identity attached.
- When a person's inquiry practice consistently lands them on the same conclusions ("the institutions are wrong," "the mainstream is wrong").
- When a person reports defensiveness when their inquiry-conclusions are challenged.
- When the rebellion against an original authority figure is visible in the person's framing of inquiry.
- When [[questioning-assumptions]] has been introduced and the practitioner is doing the wrong form.
