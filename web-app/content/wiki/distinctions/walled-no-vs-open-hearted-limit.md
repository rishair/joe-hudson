# Walled No vs. Open-Hearted Limit

## The Distinction

A partner says they want something you cannot give -- non-monogamy, a particular kind of life, a specific arrangement that violates a real limit of yours. The same word "no" can be delivered two ways. The two ways look surface-similar and feel completely different to both people.

**Walled no:** "I will not do that." A closed sentence. Defended. Final. The partner's want has been refused without being heard.

**Open-hearted limit:** "I really hear that you're [unsatisfied / wanting this thing]. I want you to get your needs met. AND, this is not okay with me. And this scares the fuck out of me because I think we might be about to get a divorce because you have a need that I'm not going to be able to fulfill."

The limit is the same. The container is opposite.

## Joe's Naming

"It is so much different than saying, oh, I really hear that you're sexually unsatisfied in the marriage, and I want you to be sexually satisfied in the marriage, and I'm not okay with us having relationships outside, or I'm not okay with us not having relationships outside of the marriage as long as we're not connected, or whatever the boundary is. It's so much different than 'I won't do that.' 'I won't do that' is this armored defensive than saying, oh, I can really acknowledge and see your needs and I really want you to get your needs met. And oh, by the way, this scares the fuck out of me because I think we're about to get a divorce because you have a need that I'm not going to be able to fulfill." -- Joe Hudson, How Relationships Reveal Us
