# See Yourself and Acknowledge Others (The Daily Bidirectional Seeing Practice)

## The Practice

A daily practice with two parts that work together. Neither part alone produces the result.

**Part one (internal):** practice the internal gaze of seeing self. Name out loud or in your head what you see about you. Specific things you noticed yourself doing or being today.

- "I see how hard I worked to finish that project."
- "I see how much time I gave to my kids today."
- "I see how much joy I had in the garden this afternoon."
- "I see that I showed up to that hard conversation."
- "I see how scared I was and how I did it anyway."

**Part two (external):** practice the same seeing with the people in your life. Acknowledge out loud what you actually see in your partner, your direct reports, your kids, your community.

- "I see how hard you worked on the dishes."
- "I see how you hit all those goals beyond their due dates -- that's awesome."
- "I see how much you've been holding for the family this week."
- "I see how careful you were with that team member."

The two parts are not interchangeable. The internal seeing without the external becomes self-focus. The external seeing without the internal becomes the chronic giver pattern -- the person who sees everyone else and never gets received. The bidirectionality is the practice.

## What This Produces

The practice works by directly meeting the chronic hunger of [[the-desire-to-be-seen]] from the only direction the person has full control over: doing the seeing themselves. The external acknowledgment also functions as a muscle that, when worked, makes the person more able to receive seeing when it arrives from others.

"Oftentimes we wanna be seen and we can't see all the ways we are being seen. You know, it might be that our partner is telling us things all the time, but we're like blocking it or literally not hearing it or not translating it as being seen. So the more we do it, the more we work that muscle and make ourselves available to actually being seen as well." -- Tara Howley, The Desire To Be Seen

Two things converge:

1. **The hungry-ghost ache stops being chronic** because the basic need is being met by self-acknowledgment, which is the one form that does not require another person to behave in a specific way.

2. **The capacity to receive being-seen-by-others opens** because the muscle of recognizing the seeing has been built. When a partner says something acknowledging, the practice has trained the system to recognize it as the seeing it is, rather than blocking it or failing to translate it.

## When to Use It

- Daily, ideally before sleep. "Oh, I see how I did X today."
- After any moment when you notice yourself wanting to be seen by someone else and not getting it
- When you find yourself bragging or self-conscious about sharing a story (one of the [[pointers-to-unseen-parts]]), redirect to seeing that part of yourself directly
- When a compliment lands and the deflection arrives, replace the deflection with internal acknowledgment
- When a partner or team member does something worth acknowledging, name it specifically instead of letting the moment pass

## How This Differs From Gratitude

The practice has a family resemblance to gratitude practice but the orientation is different. Gratitude tends to be oriented at things received from outside. Seeing is oriented at what is actually present, in self or other, that can be noticed and acknowledged.

The connection to [[daily-gratitude-for-your-own-transformation]] is close: both practices acknowledge the actual person's actual movement. Where they differ: the gratitude framing emphasizes appreciation for the change. The seeing framing emphasizes recognition that the change happened, that the work was done, that the person was there for it. The two practices stack rather than substitute.

This is also related to the phrase "be the change you want to see" -- if you want more love in your life, share more love; if you want to be seen, see. The mirror effect is structural.

"There's a reason that phrase is so widely used... if we want more love in our lives -- I want more love in my life -- the more love we share with our community, it's that mirror effect. It comes back to us and we're practicing the muscle. So when it does come back to us, we can receive it." -- Tara Howley, The Desire To Be Seen

See [[from-looking-for-love-to-being-love]] for the broader form of this principle.

## The Felt-Sense Requirement

The internal half of the practice does not work if it is performed as a checklist. The seeing has to actually land in the body for the practice to meet the need. The instruction is to let each acknowledgment hit you in the heart and the gut and travel through you, not just be said and moved past.

"Letting it hit the body, hit the heart and the gut, ricochet around like a pinball machine." -- Tara Howley, The Desire To Be Seen

The felt landing is the practice. The words are the trigger.

## What to Watch For

- **The mechanical-list form:** rattling off "I see this, I see this, I see this" without letting any of it land. Slow down. One acknowledgment that lands is worth ten that don't.
- **The achievement-only form:** acknowledging only things you accomplished, missing things you felt, things you let yourself be moved by, things you allowed. The seeing is broader than the achievement list.
- **The other-only form:** acknowledging everyone else and not yourself. This is the chronic-giver default and will reproduce the same hunger the practice is meant to address.
- **The self-only form:** doing only the internal half and skipping the external. The muscle for receiving will not build without the external practice.

## Pairing With Other Practices

- After completing the internal seeing each day, add a layer of [[daily-gratitude-for-your-own-transformation]] for the growth that is visible
- When the external seeing reveals what your partner has been carrying, let the gratitude land in your body before naming it (see the felt-sense requirement)
- When the practice surfaces something you can see in yourself that you have been deflecting from others, pair with [[let-it-move-through-and-clean-the-channel]] the next time a compliment about that quality lands
- The [[ten-percent-more-connected-experiment]] is a natural companion: be 10% more connected by seeing 10% more

## Tone

The instruction has a gentle, low-overhead quality. This is not a heavy practice. It does not require setup, props, or special time. The instruction is mostly to do something that takes thirty seconds, do it often, and let it land each time. The weight of the practice is in the cumulative repetition, not in any single instance.
