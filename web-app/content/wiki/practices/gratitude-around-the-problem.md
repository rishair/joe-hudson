# Gratitude Around the Problem

## The Practice

When the team or the individual is stuck on a specific problem, the gratitude is deliberately oriented at the problem itself, not away from it. Instead of being grateful for things unrelated to the problem (which would be a form of dissociation), the gratitude is directed inside the difficulty: what is the difficulty giving you? what is it teaching? what resources is it surfacing? what data is it generating?

This is the most counterintuitive form of gratitude practice and the most generative.

"If you're working on a problem, it's really important to see what you're grateful for around what you think is lack or what you think is the problem... that expedites the process." -- Joe Hudson, How Does Gratitude Create Better Teams?

## Why It Is Different From General Gratitude

General gratitude is a maintenance practice. It builds the baseline capacity to see what is right. Gratitude around the problem is a targeted intervention. It is what you do when there is a specific stuck thing and you need new solutions.

The mechanism is the same as [[see-whats-right-to-build-on-it]], but applied with surgical focus to the specific area of stuckness. Instead of widening the scan, you narrow it -- and within the narrowed field, you flip the polarity of attention from what is wrong to what is right.

## The Concrete Examples Joe Gives

**The non-selling product:**

"If the biggest issue right now is that your product isn't selling, then it would be great to spend gratitude around -- this is difficult for people -- around your product not selling, because then you can start seeing all the opportunities in it. Like, oh wow, I'm really grateful that our customers are telling us what they don't like about the product. I'm really grateful that the salespeople are telling us their concerns about what makes them not be able to sell the product. I'm really grateful for the learnings of that before we create a product next time, we should make sure it sells before we create it." -- Joe Hudson, How Does Gratitude Create Better Teams?

The structure: the problem is held constant. The orientation toward the problem changes. The same fact (the product is not selling) reveals different content depending on whether the scan is looking for deficits or assets.

Once the assets are visible -- customer feedback, salesperson honesty, pre-launch learning for the next product -- the next steps are different. The non-selling product becomes the cheapest market-research instrument the company could have built. The work shifts from "force this product to sell" to "extract everything this product is teaching us."

**The scarcity of money:**

"If you feel like you're always poor and don't have enough money, to be grateful for the resources you do have is a great way to figure out how more resources come to you quickly." -- Joe Hudson, How Does Gratitude Create Better Teams?

The orientation: gratitude for what is present in the area of the lack. The result Joe asserts: more resources come more quickly. The mechanism is partly perceptual (you see options you did not see when you were scanning for absence) and partly behavioral (you act differently when you are oriented toward what is here rather than what is missing).

See [[resources-vs-resourcefulness]]. The scarcity scan looks for missing resources. The gratitude scan looks for available resourcefulness.

## Why This Is Hard

Joe acknowledges directly: "this is difficult for people." The difficulty has two roots.

First, the negativity bias resists. The brain is built to scan the problem for danger. Being asked to scan the problem for gifts is fighting hardware.

Second, gratitude around a problem can feel like denial. The person scanning for gratitude inside a failing product can feel like they are betraying the urgency of the problem, lying about reality, or performing optimism. The practice has to be done with the understanding that the negative scan is also still happening. Gratitude does not replace the urgency; it widens the data set the urgency has to work with.

The practice is not "be grateful instead of being concerned." The practice is "be grateful in addition to being concerned, so that the concern has more material to work with."

## How to Run It in a Team

A modified version of the [[gratitude-rounds-before-meetings]] practice. The team is in a meeting about a specific stuck problem. Before diving into the problem-analysis, run three rounds of gratitude oriented at the problem.

Round one: what about this problem are you grateful for? (Each person names three things.)

Round two: same orientation. Force the scan to keep generating.

Round three: same orientation. By this point, the room often has surfaced concrete data that was invisible at the start.

The felt-quality requirement still applies. See [[gratitude-rounds-before-meetings]]. Performed gratitude produces nothing. The body has to actually drop into the felt sense.

## How to Run It Alone

Same structure. The person sits with the problem and writes (or speaks aloud) what they are grateful for about the problem. The scan must produce items that are actually felt. If the scan only produces generic items -- "I'm grateful for the learning experience" -- the scan is performing and not generating. Slow down. Find one item that is specifically true about this specific problem and that you can actually feel.

The scan often surfaces things the person already knew but had not been letting register. The data was always available; the orientation made it accessible.

## What It Tends to Reveal

Three categories of revealed material:

**Feedback.** The problem is producing information about the system. The information was not previously named as a gift.

**Constraints that are clarifying.** The problem is forcing decisions that would have been avoided otherwise. The forced decision is often the right one.

**Capacity that is being built.** The problem is producing skills, relationships, or systems that did not exist before. These will outlast the problem.

The scan asks: what is here that I had been treating as cost but is actually asset?

## When This Is Used

- A team is stuck in a problem-loop and cannot generate solutions
- A leader is exhausted from fighting a difficulty and needs a different orientation to it
- A person is in scarcity-thinking around money or resources
- A coaching session has surfaced a stuck problem and the standard problem-analysis is not moving anything
- An individual is in a difficult emotional pattern that has been treated as pure deficit for a long time

## Anti-Pattern

This practice can be used to dissociate from the problem instead of going deeper into it. The marker: the gratitude scan produces generic items rather than specific ones, the body does not actually feel grateful, and the person uses the practice to avoid the difficult emotion that the problem is also producing. See [[dont-step-on-the-signal]]. The practice is not a way to skip the fear or the sadness or the anger that the problem is producing. It is a way to add information to what is otherwise an incomplete picture.
