# Is This Mine?

## Definition

A simple internal question for distinguishing your own emotions from emotions absorbed from another person. When a strong feeling arises during or after an interaction, ask: "Is this mine?"

## Instructions

1. Notice a strong emotion arising while you are with another person or shortly after.
2. Ask yourself: "Is this mine?"
3. Let the answer come from the body, not from analysis.
4. If the answer is no, the emotion often drops immediately. The system recognizes it as borrowed and releases it.

## When To Use It

- When highly empathetic people lose track of the boundary between their emotional state and another's.
- When anxiety, sadness, or agitation appears suddenly during or after a conversation and its origin is unclear.
- When working with someone in an intense emotional state and you start to feel swept into it.

Joe describes working with a client who had intense COVID anxiety. He asked: "Is this yours?" The client immediately dropped the anxiety and said no, it was not theirs. The emotion belonged to the environment, not to the individual.

"Oftentimes highly empathetic people go beyond empathy and they're not able to tell what's their emotional state and what's another person's emotional state." -- Joe Hudson, Empathy

## Why It Works

Mirror neurons allow humans to sense and absorb the emotional states of others. This is biologically useful and unavoidable. The problem is not the sensing. The problem is forgetting that the sensation is a mirror, not an origin. The question "Is this mine?" breaks the spell. It reintroduces the boundary between self and other without shutting down the empathic channel.

## What It Produces

- Immediate clarity about emotional boundaries.
- The ability to remain empathic without being overwhelmed.
- A quick way to shed emotional residue from intense interactions.
- Pairs with [[staying-in-your-body]] as part of the practice of empathy without merging.
