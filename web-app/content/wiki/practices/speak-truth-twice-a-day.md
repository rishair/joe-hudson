# Speak Your Truth Twice a Day

## The Practice

Two times a day, say something true that you would normally not say. Make it slightly uncomfortable. Not a grenade. Not a discharge. Just the actual thing you were going to leave unsaid, said out loud, from an open heart, in a grounded body. Start small. Keep going. Make the next one a little bigger.

The instruction is deliberately modest. The point is not to dump your accumulated unspoken material. The point is to install a daily reps of the truth-speaking move so that the underlying mechanism — speaking as self-approval — gets activated repeatedly until it becomes the default. See [[speaking-truth-cures-approval-seeking]] for the principle this practice operationalizes.

"The simple thing is to have the practice of speaking your truth, even if it's uncomfortable, and make it just a little bit uncomfortable, 2 times a day." -- Joe Hudson, How To Stop Seeking Approval

"You can speak your truth 2 times a day. Make it simple stuff. Just keep on going bigger and bigger and bigger." -- Joe Hudson, How To Stop Seeking Approval

## How to Do It

1. **Find two opportunities a day.** They are everywhere. The thing you almost said in the meeting and let slide. The preference you suppressed at dinner. The disagreement with the friend that you bridged over. The opinion you swallowed so that the conversation could move on. Two of those, per day, deliberately not-swallowed.

2. **Make it slightly uncomfortable.** Not the easiest one. Not the hardest one. The one that has just enough edge that saying it requires a little courage. The slight uncomfortable-ness is part of the practice — that is where the [[pucker-before-truth]] arrives, and walking through the pucker is the muscle being trained.

3. **Speak from an open heart in a grounded body.** Not after closing the heart in preparation. Not as a defensive bracing-and-release. The speaking is the practice; the inner state is the prerequisite. If you cannot find the open-hearted grounded body, the practice does not work — see "Why It Fails Without the Inner Posture" below.

4. **Stay in the conversation after.** Do not drop the truth and run. The body says no anyway: "You can't do that if your heart is open and you're grounded." If you have to run, you closed your heart. The staying is the proof that the inner state was real.

5. **Notice what happens.** Both what happens in the other person and what happens in you. The other person's response is information, not the result. The shift in you — the felt sense of self-approval that arrives when the truth has been spoken — is the actual product.

6. **Make tomorrow's a little bigger.** The practice compounds. Today's two were the warm-up for next week's two. Over time, the things that count as "slightly uncomfortable" expand to include things that would have been unthinkable to say last month.

## What Counts as Truth

Small truths count. "I'd rather not eat there tonight." "I didn't like that show." "That comment in the meeting bothered me." "I'm tired and I want to leave now." "I noticed I held my breath when you said that." "I don't actually agree with what I just said yes to."

Larger truths count more. "I think you're avoiding the thing." "I'm not happy in this job and I haven't said it." "Our friendship has felt different lately and I want to ask why." "I love you and I am scared to say it." "I want a different role on this team."

What does not count: editorialized opinion that is not actually your felt experience. Outrage that is borrowed from a column you read. Performance of contrarianism. The "truth" has to be your truth — a perception of what is actually happening in you or in the room as you see it. See [[channeling-quality-of-truth]] for the felt quality this points at.

## You Cannot Drop the Grenade

A specific anti-pattern: speaking truth as a hit-and-run. Say the hard thing, then leave the room before the response lands. The practice does not work that way, structurally:

- **Joe (when Brett asks: do I jump in, drop the grenade, then run?):** "You can't do that if your heart is open and you're grounded. Ah. Say the thing that's scary. It would not feel good to run. You'd have to shut that all down to run under most circumstances. You can't intellectually get there. There's a somatic component that is required." -- Joe Hudson, How To Stop Seeking Approval

The body confirms whether the inner state was real. If you can run, you closed the heart to enable the running. The closure means the speaking was not the practice — it was a defensive discharge. The discharge does not activate the self-approval mechanism. The discharge produces the same shame loop that the practice is supposed to dissolve.

The grounded staying is what makes the practice land. The staying says: "I said this, and I am here for what happens next." That second move is the integral that closes the loop.

## Why It Fails Without the Inner Posture

The instruction includes "open heart and grounded body" as load-bearing. Without these, the practice produces the opposite of what it intends.

A closed-heart truth-bomb hits the listener as attack. The listener defends. The conversation that follows is conflict, not contact. The truth-teller may feel briefly powerful (the discharge) and then guilty (the impact) and then doubtful (the doubt loop). None of these are the self-approval the practice is supposed to install.

The grounded body is the prerequisite for the open heart in the moment of speaking. A grounded body can stay open under the pressure of the saying. An ungrounded body either braces (closing the heart) or flees (closing the heart). Both routes prevent the practice from doing its work.

See [[staying-in-your-body]] and the related instruction to put 10% of your attention in your body. The body-attention practice is the prerequisite training for the body-state the truth-speaking requires.

## What If You Cannot Feel Your Body?

If a person reports they cannot feel their body, the truth-speaking practice is not the right first move. The first job is body access. Joe's specific instruction: spend 2-3 weeks keeping 10% of your attention in your body as much as possible. If you cannot feel your body, smack yourself in the face and feel your cheek. Tap your fingers together. The minimum claim is: you can feel your body unless you are blind to all sensory input, and the operational definition is just to put attention on a place.

"You can feel your body. Everybody can. That's like saying, how do I see? Unless you're blind, you can see. So spend time keeping 10% of your attention in your body as much as possible for 2, 3 weeks. By the way, that can be an incredibly intense trip for people, so be gentle with yourself if you do that." -- Joe Hudson, How To Stop Seeking Approval

See [[staying-in-your-body]] and [[attention-is-feeling]]. Once the body is reliably available, the truth-speaking practice has a foundation to operate on.

## Tracking the Discrimination

A second practice, paired with this one: every time you make an immediate decision, notice how your body feels. Over weeks, you will start to discriminate between intuition (heart opens) and conditioning (heart closes). See [[intuition-vs-conditioning]].

The pairing matters because the truth that gets spoken in this practice is increasingly informed by the intuition signal as the discrimination develops. Early in the practice, the truths are basic preferences and unspoken thoughts. Later, the truths become the things the body is actually saying that the mind has been overriding. The discrimination practice opens the channel for those deeper truths to be available for speaking.

## What This Produces

- Self-approval as a daily occurrence, not a sometime-felt thing
- A growing felt sense of what is actually true for you (the channel becomes more available with use)
- A reduction in the felt need for others' approval — see [[speaking-truth-cures-approval-seeking]]
- A gradual change in the people around you. The eggshell-people leave or change shape. The people who can hold the truth deepen with you.
- A growing capacity to take a stand without needing the stand to be validated
- The arrival of [[channeling-quality-of-truth]] as the practice becomes default
- An emergent groundedness that other people experience as leadership

## Anti-Patterns

- **The grenade-and-run.** Already addressed above. Truth without the grounded staying does not work.
- **The performance of edgy truth.** "I'm just being honest" delivered with smugness or contempt. This is not the practice. The practice is unhedged speech from an open heart. The smugness is the closed-heart variant.
- **The everything-all-at-once dump.** Three weeks of suppressed material delivered as one truth-bomb. The practice is two-a-day, not one-a-month-in-an-explosion.
- **The over-prepared truth.** Rehearsing the exact wording until the saying is calculated. The practice is closer to channeling than to scripting. See [[channeling-quality-of-truth]].
- **The using-it-as-weaponization.** "I am just speaking my truth" used to justify cruelty. See [[spiritual-narcissism]] for the misuse pattern.
- **Truth that is borrowed.** Reciting the opinion you read in an article and calling it your truth. The practice is for your actual felt perception, not for borrowed positions.

## Tone

Joe gives this with the lightness of someone giving a manageable homework assignment. "Two times a day, simple stuff, keep going bigger." The lightness is part of the prescription — the practice does not need to be heavy, and the heaviness is itself a signal that the wrong version is being attempted.
