# Approach the Pink Elephant First

## The Principle

In any project, relationship, or organization, there is something everyone is avoiding. The thing nobody wants to look at, talk about, or work on. The pink elephant in the room. Effectiveness depends on going there first.

The principle inverts the natural pull. The natural pull is to do what you already know how to do, talk about what is comfortable, work on the part of the problem you understand. The principle says: do the opposite. Do the part you do not know how to do. Talk about the thing nobody wants to bring up. Work on the part of the problem you understand the least.

"You look at the part of the problem that you understand the least about, and you start your work there." -- Joe Hudson, Embracing Intensity (citing an MIT principle)

## Why This Is an Application of Embracing Intensity

The pink elephant is the place with the most intensity. Everyone is avoiding it because something about it is uncomfortable. The avoidance is the bracing. Approaching it is the embracing. See [[embrace-intensity]] and [[embrace-vs-brace]].

A person who is willing to embrace intensity is naturally drawn to where the intensity is. They go to the avoided thing first because they have less to lose by going there. The person who is bracing against intensity will spend the day doing easy work, comfortable conversations, familiar tasks, and the pink elephant will still be there at the end of the day, larger.

"If you have a marriage and there's that pink elephant in the room, to approach that, and to talk about that, and to feel into that immediately, as quickly as you can, will make you more effective. Have a better marriage, be happier, be better to each other, be more loving." -- Joe Hudson, Embracing Intensity

## In Practice

The pattern is visible across domains.

**New project.** Team members work on the parts they already know how to do. The parts they do not know how to do sit untouched until the deadline panic. By then it is too late to do them well. The MIT principle: start with what you understand the least, because that is where the time needs to go.

**CEO of a company.** There is one thing the CEO is not looking at. The conversation with the underperforming executive. The strategic problem with the core product. The financial reality that does not fit the story. The thing not being looked at is usually the thing that determines whether the company succeeds.

**Marriage.** Couples build elaborate routines around the topic nobody is bringing up. The longer the topic is avoided, the larger it grows, and the more elaborate the avoidance routines become. Bringing it up is the work. The relief and intimacy that follow are direct consequences.

## The Felt Sense of the Pink Elephant

The pink elephant has a specific somatic signature. There is a heaviness in the room when it is present. A subtle steering of conversations away from it. A noticing-then-not-noticing on the part of everyone involved. A sense of "we are pretending."

The person doing the embracing-intensity work can feel the elephant. It is the thing nobody is willing to feel. The willingness to feel makes it visible.

## Why Effectiveness Follows

Effectiveness, in Joe's framing, is the capacity to do what is uncomfortable. The pink elephant is concentrated discomfort. Going there directly is a use of capacity that produces outsize results.

"We're more effective when we can do the things that are uncomfortable for us." -- Joe Hudson, Embracing Intensity

The corollary: a person whose effectiveness is increasing is a person whose tolerance for discomfort is increasing. The increasing tolerance is what makes more of the problem space available to them.

## The Habit Reversal

The natural human pattern is to clear the easy tasks first and approach the hard ones after warming up. The principle reverses this. The hard ones get done first, when energy and clarity are highest. The easy ones get done after, or get done less well, or do not get done at all because they did not actually need to be done.

The reversal feels wrong. The mind argues for the warm-up. The body wants the small wins. The principle holds anyway.

## When the Pink Elephant Is Internal

The principle also applies inside one person. There is something the person is not letting themselves feel. There is a conversation they are not letting themselves have with themselves. There is a desire they are not letting themselves own.

The internal pink elephant is approached the same way: directly, first, before the day fills with comfortable activity. See [[feeling-the-unfelt-emotion]] for the internal version of the move.

## Tone

Matter-of-fact, almost obvious. Joe presents this as a basic feature of how effectiveness works, not as a contrarian insight. The unusual thing is not knowing this; the unusual thing is doing it.
