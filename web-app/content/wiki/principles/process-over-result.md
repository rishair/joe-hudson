# Process Over Result

## Definition

Shooting for a specific result produces worse outcomes than staying faithful to a process. This applies to conversations, business, coaching, creative work, and directing actors. Hold a clear intention. Then trust the process to deliver results you could not have designed.

This is not passivity. It is the recognition that the path to the best outcome cannot be planned in advance. The process handles what planning cannot.

## In Practice

Outcomes require time to stop. There is no end state. Show someone a snapshot of their future where they are happy and wealthy; two days later they could be broke and miserable. Which moment is "the outcome"? Attachment to outcomes is attachment to a frozen frame in a process that never stops.

"I don't believe in outcomes. The only way you can believe in an outcome is if you believe time stops." -- Joe Hudson, Impartiality -- Connection Course Series #3

Joe connects this principle to design theory. A TED Talk on design process made the point explicit: teams that stay focused on the process consistently produce better output than teams that fixate on a particular result. The process will go sideways. It will not look like progress. But the output, measured over time, is better.

"If you're shooting for a result, you won't get it. The thing you have to do is stay focused on the process and it's gonna go sideways. It's not gonna go well at times, but the output is consistently better than if you're going for a particular result." -- Joe Hudson, Introduction to VIEW (Connection Course)

The consultant Case, whose conversational ability inspired [[view]], pointed to this principle constantly. Before conversations, he would say: "I have no idea what the hell is going to happen." That was his advantage, not his limitation. Knowing what should happen would have collapsed the space for what could happen.

Living by [[view]] is a form of principled living. You commit to a set of principles (vulnerability, impartiality, empathy, wonder) because you know that consistently living by them produces results aligned with your truth. You do not need to know the result in advance. The principles carry you.

"It's very much like living principled. You're saying, here are the principles I'm living by because I know consistently if I live by them, it works out in the way that works for me and works for my deepest truth." -- Joe Hudson, Introduction to VIEW (Connection Course)

This is also the personal application of [[principles-free-you]]. A set of principles is itself the process. The internal drama dissolves because the principle has already decided what would otherwise need to be re-decided. Empowered action becomes available because the principle removes the moment-by-moment negotiation with fear of consequence.

When directing actors, giving a specific desired performance produces stiff results. Giving the actor a clear objective and trusting them to find their way produces excellence. The parallel to coaching and managing teams is direct: hold the objective, release the method.

## Enjoyment as the Engine

The distinction between [[enjoy-over-manage]] is the lived version of process-over-result. Management is outcome-fixation made habitual. Enjoyment is process-trust made embodied. A person who is enjoying the process is, by definition, not gripping the result.

The practical form: hold general intentions (a great company, a product that wins) while releasing specific methods. Determination and intention remain. What drops is the management of *how*. The person listening for [[listening-to-impulse]] rather than planning the next move is in process. The person scripting the conversation is in outcome.

"You have to keep all that determination, you have to keep all that fortitude. And there is also a reading the river and letting the river flow and paying attention to that river and following it to get there." -- Joe Hudson, Enjoy over Manage

## In Integration

This principle applies directly to [[integration]] after transformation. The impulse to "figure out" what happened, to put words on the experience, to force understanding, works against the integration process. The mind always figures it out eventually. Patience with not-knowing produces deeper integration than premature articulation.

"It's not about trying to stay in the not knowing, it's more about not trying to get into the knowing." -- Joe Hudson, Integrating a Transformative Experience

The [[three-stages-of-integration]] framework shows this concretely: cutting the first stage (surprise/not-knowing) short prevents the full depth of the transformation from landing.

## No Rush in Transformation

This principle extends to personal transformation itself. The urgency to fix all the problems, embrace all the intensity, resolve all the resistance is itself an attachment to outcome. The problems wait. "Whatever it is that you want to transform about yourself, it's gonna stay there. The problem doesn't go away until it's addressed. So you can be patient." -- Joe Hudson, If You Can't Love the Feeling, Love the Resistance

Patience with the process is itself a release of [[resistance]]. The rush to transform is another form of fighting what is.

## The Stockdale Distinction

The POW survivors did not predict release by a specific date. They held the vision of freedom without gripping the timetable. The optimists, who attached to "out by Christmas," died when Christmas passed. See [[pow-optimist]]. This is process-over-result in extreme conditions: trust the unfolding, do not grip the form.

## Upstream (What Leads Here)

- When someone is gripping a specific outcome and creating rigidity.
- When [[impartiality]] is hard to practice because the stakes feel too high to release control.
- When a conversation feels forced or mechanical despite good technique.
- When [[view-as-technique]] has been identified: the person is focused on getting a result through VIEW rather than being in the VIEW state.
- When urgency to transform is itself creating [[resistance]] and [[layered-resistance]].
- When someone is performing success rather than embodying the empowered stance. See [[empowered-vs-power]].

## Downstream (Where This Leads)

- Reinforces [[impartiality]]: the practice of not attaching to how the goal is reached.
- Makes [[wonder]] natural: if you do not need a specific result, you can genuinely wonder what will happen.
- Builds trust in VIEW as a reliable system rather than a gamble.
