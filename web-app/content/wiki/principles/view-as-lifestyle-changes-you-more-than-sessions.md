# VIEW Practiced as a Lifestyle Is a Bigger Change Agent Than Any Coaching Session

## The Principle

The largest source of transformation in a person's life from this methodology is not the coaching sessions they receive. It is the practice of [[view]] as a lifestyle -- being more vulnerable, more impartial, more empathetic, more in wonder, across every domain of daily life. Meditation, conversations, work, parenting, sitting with yourself. The state of mind is the change agent. The coaching session is a place where that state is sometimes catalyzed; the lifestyle practice is the place where it accumulates.

This inverts a common assumption that coaching is the lever and life is what happens between sessions. In this methodology, the daily life is the lever. The session is occasional support for the daily practice.

"What I started to realize was that was a bigger change agent than any of the coaching sessions that I had been in. That was actually the thing that started to make a significant difference in my life." -- Joe Hudson, How I Have Life-Changing Conversations

## How Joe Discovered It

After identifying the four characteristics that the master coaches had in common, Joe ran an experiment on himself for many months. The experiment was not "use VIEW in coaching sessions." The experiment was: how do I deepen into vulnerability, impartiality, empathy, and wonder, in every part of my life?

"I was going to do it just as a lifestyle. And so that was my experiment for many, many, many months. I was just like, how do I deepen into vulnerability, impartiality, empathy, and wonder?" -- Joe Hudson, How I Have Life-Changing Conversations

The change was larger than what coaching itself had produced.

## What VIEW-as-Lifestyle Looks Like Across Domains

The same four components apply across every activity. The application looks different in each, but the underlying practice is identical.

**Meditation.** Before VIEW, meditation was about getting somewhere -- achieving a state, fixing the mind, reducing thoughts. After: meditate with impartiality (no agenda for the meditation), with wonder (what is actually happening here?), with empathy (be with whatever arises), with vulnerability (let what is hidden be felt). The enjoyment of meditation increased. See [[view-as-meditation-pointer]] for the full treatment.

"Because before that, when I was meditating, it was trying to get somewhere. But then impartiality— what, like, well, if I'm not trying to get anywhere, what happens when I'm meditating? And the enjoyment increased. Or if I'm in wonder when I'm meditating, it's a very different experience than if I'm trying to get somewhere, or if I'm like not being empathetic with myself in meditation." -- Joe Hudson, How I Have Life-Changing Conversations

**Conversation.** Any conversation -- not just coaching -- becomes the testing ground. Can I be in wonder here? Can I be empathetic with this person while staying in myself? Can I be impartial about the outcome of this conversation? Can I say the slightly more vulnerable thing?

**Coaching.** Yes, VIEW works in coaching. But the lifestyle practice is what makes the coaching version possible. A coach who only does VIEW in sessions is performing VIEW. A coach who does VIEW all day every day is in VIEW when the session starts.

**Sitting with yourself.** The same four components can be brought to the internal experience. VIEW with the inner critic, with fear, with shame. See [[emotional-inquiry]] and [[view-around-self-as-identity-diagnostic]].

## The "All of a Sudden" Cascade

The lifestyle practice produces a cascade of life changes that, from the outside, look like coincidence and feel like luck. The mechanism is structural: people respond differently to a person in VIEW.

"All of a sudden people wanna be hanging out with me. All of a sudden people wanna do business with me. All of a sudden my relationships are better. All of a sudden I can like figure out with a customer what the product they really want is easier. All of a sudden my management conversations with people who work for me just get nicer. All of a sudden, sitting with myself is just easier." -- Joe Hudson, How I Have Life-Changing Conversations

The "all of a sudden" framing is meaningful. The shifts arrive faster than the person doing the practice expects. The person was working on themselves; what shifted was the entire field of relationships around them. The mechanism is the one named in [[presence-over-technique-in-coaching]]: how the person shows up determines how the world shows up around them. When the person is in VIEW, the world reconfigures.

The list spans every relational domain a person operates in -- social, business, intimate, customer, management, internal. That breadth is itself diagnostic. A practice that improves one domain is a domain-specific tool. A practice that improves all domains at once is operating at the state level, which is the level at which all relational outcomes are determined.

## Why the State-of-Mind Comes From Practicing the State-of-Mind

A subtle but load-bearing point: VIEW is not learned by understanding it. It is developed by being in it. The understanding is intellectual; the state is somatic. The intellectual layer does not deliver the state. The repeated practice of being in the state delivers the state.

Joe specifically notes that the master coaches he studied did not just have the state in their work. They had the state because they had been in the state, across years of their own lives, deeply enough that it had become available.

"This state of mind came from the practicing of the state of mind, but it also came because there was a consciousness that had been developed in these people that they realized that this way of being, this vulnerability, impartiality, empathy, and wonder was just really comfortable. It felt really good. It was heart-opening, and it was really effective as far as getting, you know, a life that you wanted." -- Joe Hudson, How I Have Life-Changing Conversations

This is the recursion that makes the practice self-reinforcing. The practitioner discovers that the state itself is pleasurable. The pleasure becomes the motivation for continuing the practice. The practice deepens the state. The deeper state becomes available across more situations. The result is a person who is operating from VIEW most of the time, not just when they remember to apply the technique.

## The Implication for the AI Coach

This principle disrupts the implicit model of coaching as the thing that produces change. If the lifestyle practice is what produces the change, then the coach's job is not primarily to deliver insight in the session. The coach's job includes pointing the person back to the daily practice and helping them see the state as something they can cultivate outside the session.

The operational form for the AI coach: when a person reports being stuck or wanting change, the question is not always "what do we work on in this conversation?" Sometimes it is "what would it look like to be in VIEW with yourself for the next week, and what would you notice?" The conversation primes the practice. The practice does the work. The next conversation builds on what the practice produced.

This also dissolves a common dependency pattern. A person who waits for the next coaching session before working on themselves is operating from the model that coaching produces change. The corrective is to give them the practice and trust the practice. The session becomes one of many catalysts, not the sole source of movement.

## Why the Pleasure Matters

The pleasure of being in VIEW is not incidental to the principle. It is structural. A practice that is not pleasurable does not get sustained. A practice that the person is forcing themselves into runs on the same engine as everything else they force themselves into -- and eventually the engine fails.

VIEW as a lifestyle works because being in VIEW is, in itself, more comfortable and more effective than being out of it. The practitioner is not running on discipline. They are running on the discovered fact that this way of being feels better and produces better outcomes than the alternatives. Discipline is not the engine; recognition of what works is the engine.

This connects to [[enjoyment-as-path]] and [[enjoyment-attracts]] as the broader principle of how sustainable change actually happens. VIEW is not an exception. It is a specific application of the same dynamic: do the thing that produces pleasure and effectiveness, notice that it does, and do it more.

## The Test Joe Offers

The same instruction Joe gives for the larger methodology applies here: do not take this on belief. Run the experiment.

The form of the experiment: for some period -- a week, a month, longer -- try to deepen into vulnerability, impartiality, empathy, and wonder, in every part of daily life. Watch what changes. The change will be visible in the relational domains that matter to the practitioner. If nothing changes, the experiment falsifies the principle for that person. If the cascade arrives, the practice has confirmed itself by what it produces. See [[dont-take-my-word-for-it]] for the broader instruction about how to relate to teachings.
