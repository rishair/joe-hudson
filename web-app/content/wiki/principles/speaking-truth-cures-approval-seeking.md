# Speaking Your Truth Is the Cure for Seeking Approval

## The Principle

The conventional framing is backwards. Most people think: first I have to stop needing approval, then I can speak my truth. The actual mechanism runs the other direction. Speaking your truth is what stops the approval-seeking. The truth-speaking is the cure, not the consequence.

The mechanism is structural. When you speak your truth, you are approving of your own perception by the act of voicing it. The speaking is the self-approval. Saying it is treating it as valuable enough to leave your mouth. Once you have approved of your own perception in this way, you do not need someone else to do that work. The external approver is no longer required because the internal one has been activated.

"If you speak your truth, then you're approving of yourself and you don't have to go looking outside for it. If you speak your truth, you say, this thing that I'm saying, this that I'm feeling, this perception of the world is valuable. I approve of it. I approve of it so much, I'm going to speak it. I'm going to validate it. So I don't need to get validation outside. I don't need to get approval outside." -- Joe Hudson, How To Stop Seeking Approval

## Why the Conventional Order Fails

The conventional order is: get over needing approval, then speak freely. The order fails because there is no separate operation called "getting over needing approval" that runs independently of speaking. The need for approval is the structural opposite of speaking truth. While the approval-engine is running, the speaking is filtered, hedged, or suppressed. The speaking itself is the engine that runs the opposite direction.

Telling someone "stop needing approval and then speak your truth" is asking them to perform the destination without the path. The path is the speaking. The speaking is what produces the stop.

The same structural error appears in many self-help framings: "love yourself first, then you can love others"; "be confident, then act confidently"; "trust yourself, then make decisions." In each case, the state is produced by the action, not by an internal switch flipped in advance. The action is the practice that produces the state.

## The Observable Pattern

The signature appears in other people too. The people who are not hedging what they say are also not looking for your approval. The people who are looking for your approval are also hedging what they say. The two qualities co-occur because they are the same orientation expressed two ways. Self-possession produces speech. Speech produces self-possession.

"If you look around the world, you'll see that the people who aren't really looking for your approval are the people who are also not hedging what they say. They're being self-possessed, they're looking for the truth." -- Joe Hudson, How To Stop Seeking Approval

The observation runs both ways. If you want to know who is self-possessed, listen to whether they hedge. If you want to know who is approval-seeking, listen to whether they speak their actual perception or a managed version of it. The hedge is the diagnostic. The unhedged speech is the cure.

## The Practical Move

The practice that produces the structural shift: [[speak-truth-twice-a-day]]. Two times a day, say something true that is slightly uncomfortable to say. Not a discharge. Not a grenade. Just the actual thing you would normally not say. The repetition installs the mechanism. The act of saying the thing functions as self-approval each time. Over time, the approval-engine that had been running outward starts running inward.

The practice has prerequisites. The truth has to be said from an open heart and a grounded body. Said from a closed heart, it becomes a weapon and the self-approval mechanism does not engage. See [[doubt-requires-closed-heart]] for the reverse: when the heart is closed, the speaking-truth move is unavailable because doubt is operating instead.

## The Daughter Example

Joe's own teenage daughter discovered this directly. She had been doing the work of approval-management — making sure people liked her, doing the right thing, being a certain way — and the work was painful. She realized: if she had to present a certain way to have friends, she did not have friends. She had surrogate friends, friends with the version of her she was performing.

Her decision: just speak her truth. Say what was true to her, let the chips fall. A few weeks later she reported back: the approval-seeking had stopped. Not because she had worked on stopping it. Because she had started speaking. The speaking was the cure.

## Why It Works

The approval-engine runs on a specific delegation: my perception is not validated until someone else validates it. The structure requires the perception to remain unspoken or hedged, because once it is spoken cleanly, the only person who can have validated it is the one who said it. The unhedged speech breaks the delegation by completing the validation inside the speaker.

The same mechanism explains why approval received does not satisfy the approval-seeker. The compliment lands on the surface but cannot reach the place that needed validating, because the place is structurally configured to receive validation only from inside. The compliment lands as data about the other person. It cannot become the missing self-approval. Only the self can do that, and the speaking is how.

## What This Is Not

This is not a license to be verbose, oversharing, or socially clumsy. The principle is about speaking what is actually true for you, not about speaking constantly. A self-possessed person often speaks less than an approval-seeker, because the approval-seeker is filling the air with managed speech. The truth-speaker says the thing and stops.

This is not a justification for cruelty. Speech delivered from a closed heart does not engage the self-approval mechanism, regardless of how "true" the content is. See [[speak-the-elephant-from-open-heart-not-fear]] and [[compassion-as-truth-from-open-heart]] for the structural form of speech that does engage it.

This is not a one-time fix. The mechanism works through repetition. Each instance of unhedged speech does a small amount of installation. The compounded effect over weeks and months is the shift in operating mode.

## The Channeling Quality

A late-stage feature: when this practice becomes a default, speaking your truth starts to feel less like "expressing myself" and more like channeling. There is a felt sense of surrender. You are following a call rather than producing content. See [[channeling-quality-of-truth]] for the full account. The arrival of this quality is one of the markers that the practice has become structural rather than effortful.

## Tone

Joe delivers this with the recognition of an inversion that surprises people. "The reality is oftentimes it's speaking your truth that is the cure for seeking approval" — the "oftentimes" softens the claim while still landing it. The tone is plain, almost matter-of-fact. The point is not to argue the inversion; it is to name it so the listener can run the experiment.
