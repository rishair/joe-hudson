# What the Mind Fears Feelings Will Do vs. What Feelings Actually Do

## The Principle

The mind has a catalog of predictions about what each feeling will do if it is let to run. The predictions are reliably catastrophic. The actual outcomes of the feelings, when let to run, are reliably the opposite of the predictions. The mind is not a reliable predictor of what feelings do. The body, which actually runs the feelings, knows.

The principle delivers a specific contrast between the predicted catastrophe and the actual gift. The mind's catalog is consistent across people; the actual gifts are also consistent. The contrast is teachable because both halves are reliable.

"That's what the mind does. The mind says the sadness is never ending, the anger will destroy everything I love. The fear will make me incapable. But we have a good cry and we feel better. We get angry and we get clarity and, and we care about what we love more deeply. We get scared and we become more capable." -- Joe Hudson, Her Need to "Find" Her Purpose Dissolved In 10 Minutes
