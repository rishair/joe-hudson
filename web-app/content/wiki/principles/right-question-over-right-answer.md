# The Right Question Over the Right Answer

## Definition

Answers are abundant. Questions are scarce. The quality of someone's life, business, or relationships is determined by the questions they are living in, not the answers they have accumulated.

"In the age of Google, anybody can find an answer. It's really the right question. Are you asking the right question? And being in wonder helps you ask the right questions." -- Joe Hudson, Wonder

## In Practice

Two people with equal effort and intelligence build vastly different outcomes based on the question they are pursuing. One asks "What's the phone that everybody will use?" and another asks "What's the phone that's easy to use?" Two different phones, two different futures. The difference is not effort or knowledge but the question being lived in.

In coaching, the person sitting across from you already knows more about what they need than anyone else could. They know their full history, their full context. The coach's job is not to provide answers but to help the person find the right question. The right question, asked in [[wonder]], unlocks what the person already knows but cannot yet see.

## Upstream (What Leads Here)

- When someone is collecting information but not making progress, they may be answering the wrong question.
- When [[wonder]] is present, better questions arise naturally.
- When someone is stuck in [[knowing|knowing-as-opposite-of-wonder]], they are defending old answers rather than finding new questions.

## Downstream (Where This Leads)

- Shifts focus from expertise to exploration.
- Opens the door to [[impartiality]]: if the value is in the question, attachment to a specific answer loosens.
- Supports the coaching stance of not needing to know the answer.
