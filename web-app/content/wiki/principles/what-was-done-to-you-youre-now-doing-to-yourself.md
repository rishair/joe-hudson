# What Was Done to You, You're Now Doing to Yourself (The Introject Lives in Your Voice)

## The Principle

The instructions, commands, judgments, and demands that were applied to you as a child by other people are now being applied to you, constantly, by you. The original source — parent, teacher, sibling, culture — has been replaced. The instruction continues. You are the one issuing it now.

This is not a metaphor. The voice that says "compose yourself," "don't cry," "be quiet," "behave," "stop being so much" is now literally the client's own voice. The original speaker is, at this point, structurally unnecessary. The instruction has been fully absorbed and continues to fire automatically whenever the conditions that originally triggered it reappear.

"Yeah, well, it's you, so it is all people, meaning like you're saying it to yourself, so of course." -- Joe Hudson, I'm Tired Of Taking Responsibility

The line lands the way it lands because the client has just said "fuck those people" about the original sources of the instruction. The "those people" is a defensive externalization. The principle dissolves it: those people are now you. You have become the one. The hatred you have for them you would, if you traced the voice honestly, have for yourself.

## Why It Matters

The principle reorganizes the work in a specific way. As long as the client believes the problem is the original people (the yelling parent, the critical teacher, the demanding culture), the work stays in the past and stays external. The client can rail at the source, can grieve the source, can do a great deal of historical processing — and the introject continues unaltered because the source was not where the introject was living. The introject was living in their voice.

Once the principle is felt — not just understood — the work relocates. The client can now address the instruction at the place it is actually issuing from: themselves, right now, in this moment, every time the instruction fires. The historical work was not wrong; it was incomplete. The introject completes when the client realizes they are the one doing it.

This also reorganizes the relationship between blame and responsibility. The client may have been done wrong; that remains true. But the current operator of the harm is the client themselves. Blame stays with the original; responsibility for stopping the current iteration belongs to the present self. See [[fault-vs-responsibility]] for the broader frame.

## The Mechanism

A child cannot survive without the family they are born into. When the family applies pressure to behave a certain way ("compose yourself," "stop crying," "be good"), the child has two options: either the family is wrong (which threatens the bond the child depends on for survival) or the child is wrong (which preserves the bond at the cost of the self). Children consistently choose the second option because survival outranks self-coherence.

The chosen position — "I am wrong, the instruction is right, I must apply it to myself" — becomes a permanent feature of the inner landscape. The child internalizes the voice of the family and begins to apply the family's instructions to themselves preemptively, so the family won't have to. By adulthood, the original family is not needed; the introjected voice is doing the work continuously and without conscious effort.

This is the developmental architecture of the inner critic, the perfectionist, the self-policer, the people-pleaser. All of them are introjects — formerly external voices now operating as internal ones. The voice never had to be invented by the person; it was inherited and adopted.

## The Naming Move

When the client says some version of "fuck them" about the original sources, the principle is named immediately:

- **Person:** "I got yelled at all the time for not being composed. And so I learned, yeah, fuck them, fuck those people. I'm afraid that's all people."
- **Joe:** "Yeah, well, it's you, so it is all people, meaning like you're saying it to yourself, so of course."

The naming is fast. There is no long therapeutic build-up. The client has already done the work of identifying the historical source; the move is to point at the present operator. The "fuck them" externalizes; the response re-internalizes — not as shame, but as clarity about who is actually doing this now.

The fear of "all people" that the client reports is also explained by the principle. Once the original family's voice has become the self's voice, every encounter with another person reactivates the same voice. The other person doesn't have to do anything; the introjected voice will fire from inside, attribute itself to the other person, and produce the felt experience of being judged. The other person looks like "all people" because they appear to be issuing the same instruction. They're not. The instruction is coming from inside.

## What This Enables

Once the principle is felt, several moves become available that were not available before:

- [[tell-me-to-compose-myself]]: have the client direct the introjected command outward at the coach, externalizing the voice so it can be seen.
- [[visualize-the-kid-composing-themselves]]: use developmental absurdity to make the self-application of the introject feel as wrong as it actually is.
- [[apologize-to-the-other-for-what-you-do-to-yourself]]: name the pattern as something the client is now doing to others as well as to themselves, and apologize for the projection.

All three moves depend on the principle being live. Without the felt knowing that "I am the one doing this now," the moves become exercises. With it, they become unmaskings.

## Boundary Conditions

The principle does not erase the wrongness of the original imposition. The parent who yelled at the child was wrong; the principle does not exonerate them. What it does is locate the work of stopping the pattern in the only place where stopping is possible: the present self.

The principle also does not apply uniformly. Not every painful childhood experience produces an active introject. The marker of an active introject is that the same instruction continues to fire in the present, automatically, in the client's voice. If the historical event is grievable but does not appear to be currently operative, the work is different — it lives in [[honor-the-patterns-intent]] and grief work, not in introject-externalization.

The principle also has to be delivered without becoming itself an instruction. "You're doing this to yourself" can land as "stop blaming others, take responsibility, fix yourself" — which is, ironically, exactly the kind of introjected instruction that produced the problem. The delivery has to make the principle a relief, not a new should. The relief is: I have been confused about where the voice is coming from; now I know; now I can address it where it actually lives. Not: I'm a bad person for not having seen this before.

## Connected Principles and Moves

- [[fault-vs-responsibility]]: fault belongs with the original; responsibility for stopping it belongs with the present self.
- [[shame-drives-behavior]]: the introject's reliable engine is shame. When the introject fires, shame is the consequence. The shame then produces the behavior the instruction was demanding, completing the loop.
- [[seeing-through-essential-wrongness]]: a parallel principle. The introject installs the belief that there is something essentially wrong with the self; the principle deconstructs that belief from the inside.
- [[wanting-got-wired-to-unlovability]]: a specific instance of this principle. The parent's shame at not being able to provide became the child's wiring that wanting itself was unlovable.

## What Joe Does NOT Do

Joe does not validate the externalization for longer than necessary. The client's "fuck those people" is real; the original people were wrong; and the validation is not where the work lives. Lingering in the externalization keeps the introject undisturbed.

Joe also does not soften the principle. He does not say "well, in some sense you might also be doing this to yourself." The principle is delivered cleanly: "it's you." The cleanness is what gives it the force to land. Hedged delivery produces hedged uptake.

He does not, however, deliver the principle as accusation. The voice is warm and matter-of-fact. The client is not being blamed for the introject; the introject is being relocated so it can be addressed.
