# You Can Hire Someone to Speak a Language for You, But You Can't Hire Someone to Be Yourself

## The Principle

Any capacity that can be hired or outsourced is, structurally, not the most important capacity. The most important capacities are the ones that are necessarily yours -- the ones nobody else can do for you no matter how skilled or expensive.

The phrase from Joe's friend in the kid-raising story is the compressed form:

"You can hire someone to speak a language for you, but you can't hire someone to be yourself." -- Joe Hudson's friend, quoted in How to Succeed in the Age of AI

This is the operating principle for what to invest your developmental time in. It is also the operating principle for what to develop in your kids. It is also the operating principle for what to develop in a team. The thing worth developing is whatever cannot be hired away.

## What the Principle Implies

What can be hired:
- Speaking languages
- Writing emails
- Doing the accounting
- Coding the software
- Researching the topic
- Drafting the strategy
- Summarizing the situation
- Producing the deck
- Most of what schools used to teach
- Most of what white-collar jobs used to be

What cannot be hired:
- Making the decision about who you are
- Making the decision about what relationship to have with your mother
- Making the decision about whether to scroll all day
- Making the decision about whether to do the hard thing
- Feeling the difficult feeling
- Walking toward what you have been avoiding
- Being present with your family
- Being seen by other humans
- Being yourself

Each item in the second list is what wisdom is in practice (see [[wisdom-as-being-human]]). The list does not get shorter with better technology. It might get longer as more of the hirable work gets handed off, because the un-hirable work becomes more visible.

## The Parenting Application

The story is about Joe's daughters. By age six his nieces were speaking three languages because of their education. His daughters were not yet reading because the family had chosen a different developmental investment: teach the kids to be in themselves, to have their will, to get things done, to be with themselves. The intellect, the assumption was, will come along for the ride.

The story has the worried-parent texture. Three languages versus one, reading versus not reading, on paper a clear loss. The friend reframed it: the language can be hired later, the self cannot. The reframe held. The kids grew up smart and also themselves. The bet on the un-hireable capacity paid out.

The principle is for any parent making the same kind of bet. The educational system optimizes for measurable intelligence because measurable intelligence is what was scarce and useful. The bet on developing the unmeasurable parts -- the will, the capacity to be with oneself, the comfort in one's own decisions -- is the bet that pays in the long run, especially as the measurable parts get outsourced.

## The Leadership Application

A small team of three hundred can now do what ten thousand people used to do. The three hundred are the most valuable people in the company. What is the differentiator among them?

Not their intelligence -- the AI now matches or exceeds it on most metrics. Not their domain knowledge -- the AI can summarize any domain in seconds. What is left is what cannot be hired: the quality of decisions they make, the quality of the relationships they hold, the capacity to have hard conversations, the willingness to walk into what others are avoiding, the willingness to be themselves at work rather than performing the role.

The principle, applied to hiring: hire for what cannot be hired again later. The hireable parts can be hired; the un-hireable parts are the only thing worth selecting on.

## The Personal Development Application

The same principle, applied to your own time. If you have an hour to develop yourself, the question is what cannot be hired. Reading more on the topic can be hired. Researching the field can be hired. Drafting the document can be hired. Knowing what to choose -- not hireable. Feeling what is here -- not hireable. Having the hard conversation -- not hireable. Being with what is uncomfortable -- not hireable.

This is also why the inner work is operational, not just personal. The inner work is the only kind of work whose output cannot be produced by anyone else. The leader who invests in it is investing in the only differentiator that compounds.

## Why The Principle Is More Valuable Now

The principle has always been true. A person who could be hired to do everything could not be hired to be themselves. Most of the time this fact was hidden because most of the things people did for a living were the hireable things, and there was no urgent reason to notice the un-hireable ones.

The arrival of AI makes the distinction load-bearing. When everything hireable becomes very cheap, the un-hireable things become the entire game. The person who has invested in the un-hireable capacities was already ahead; they now appear far ahead, because the gap between them and the person who has only the hireable skills is no longer hidden.

## What This Principle Does Not Say

It does not say the hireable skills are worthless. Speaking French is useful. Writing emails is useful. The principle is about prioritization when developmental time is limited. The skills you can hire later should not crowd out the skills you cannot.

It does not say there is one un-hireable self that you have to discover. The un-hireable thing is the active exercise of being yourself, which is something you do continuously, not something you find once. Each decision, each conversation, each move is an instance of the un-hireable work.

It does not say the un-hireable skills cannot be developed. They can. The development is just slower than the development of the hireable ones, and it does not happen in the same way. The hireable skills get developed by repetition and study. The un-hireable skills get developed by feeling what is here, making decisions under uncertainty, having hard conversations, being with what is uncomfortable -- which is most of what the compendium describes.

## Joe's Words

"You can hire someone to speak a language for you, but you can't hire someone to be yourself. And that is the basis of wisdom." -- Joe Hudson's friend, quoted in How to Succeed in the Age of AI

"Now you can hire something for free to write your emails for you. Or speak a language for you. And speak a language for you. But it can't make the decision on whether you're going to scroll all day, and it can't make the decision on whether you're going to get addicted to AI, and it can't make the decision on what relationship you're going to have with your mom." -- Joe Hudson, How to Succeed in the Age of AI
