# Power Erodes Empathy

## Definition

As a person's power, influence, or audience grows, their capacity to be moved by the specific people they affect tends to decay. The decay is not a moral failing of the person. It is a structural consequence of the gap between the scale of impact and the bandwidth of personal contact. Decisions get made about people who have become statistics. Praise and criticism arrive in volumes that cannot be felt individually. The leader is no longer being touched by the people the leadership touches.

This is the empirical reality behind several well-known dynamics — the seemingly-cold executive, the messianic-feeling political figure, the spiritual teacher who has stopped being affected by students. None of these requires the person to be a bad actor. The structural condition produces the empathy loss whether the person intends it or not.

The studies are real. Joe references them in passing:

"There's this whole thing about power that like, as you have more power, you lose empathy. There's like a whole bunch of studies on it." -- Joe Hudson, How I Learned To Stop Fighting Myself

## The Mechanism

The mechanism is the abstraction of impact. When a person affects a small number of people, the affected people are concrete: each one has a face, a story, a relationship with the affecting person. The empathy is operational because the contact is operational.

As the number of affected people grows, the contact becomes selective. The affecting person cannot maintain individual relationship with every affected person. Some get abstracted into "the audience," "the team," "the customers," "the followers," "the students." The abstraction is necessary for scale. The abstraction is also what makes empathy decay possible.

Once a person has been abstracted into a category, they are no longer present in the affecting person's body as an individual. Decisions made about the category are made without the felt weight of the specific people inside it. The empathy was generated by contact. The contact has been replaced by category. The empathy decays.

This happens whether or not the leader notices. It typically happens faster than the leader notices, because the abstraction is happening in the background. By the time the leader notices that "I don't really feel the impact of my decisions on individuals anymore," the empathy has already been decaying for a while.

## Why This Matters For Teachers and Coaches

The teaching and coaching professions are especially exposed to this dynamic because the work specifically requires the practitioner to be moved by the people they work with. A teacher whose empathy has decayed is technically still teaching but has lost the contact that made the teaching land. A coach who is no longer affected by their clients is performing coaching without the substrate that makes coaching work.

The risk is structural. As a coach's reputation grows, more people seek them out. As more people seek them out, the coach has less time per person. As they have less time per person, the people become more abstract. As the people become more abstract, the empathy decays. The same growth that creates the appearance of success creates the conditions for the work to hollow out.

Joe is naming this specifically as the next problem in his own work. The platform is growing. The DMs and handwritten notes of gratitude are arriving daily, sometimes twice a day. The volume of impact is becoming the kind of volume that abstraction can handle. The question is what to do.

His answer is permeability. See [[let-it-break-your-heart-on-a-consistent-basis]] for the operational practice and [[dissolution-of-self-vs-permeability-of-self]] for the structural distinction.

## The Hot Springs Example That Made It Concrete For Joe

Joe was at a hot springs. He encountered a stranger who told a single story: at a Walmart, he had helped a woman interpret a dream, and the moment had made a difference in her life. As he told the story, he started weeping.

Joe's recognition: "You can't do that if it's not personal."

The man at Walmart had not abstracted the woman into "a stranger I helped." She was a specific person whose life he had touched, and the contact was still alive in him years later. The story could only be told the way he told it because the personal-ness was preserved.

The same applies on the other side. The teacher who cannot weep about the people their work has changed has stopped being in personal contact with those people. The work is still happening; the contact is not.

The hot springs encounter named for Joe what the corrective looks like. The man at Walmart was a model of how someone holds the impact of helping another person without losing the personal-ness of it. Whatever scale Joe's work reaches, the maintenance of that quality of contact — at least with the people he does encounter — is the structural practice.

## What This Concept Does Not Say

This concept is not a critique of power or influence per se. Some people are going to be in positions of influence. The question is not how to avoid the position but how to hold it without the decay. The concept names the structural risk so that the practitioner can install the counter-practice.

The concept also does not say that everyone in power loses empathy at the same rate. Some people install practices that preserve contact. Some people are temperamentally more resistant to abstraction. The studies describe a tendency, not a determinism. The point is that the tendency is real and the counter-practice is structural, not optional, for anyone whose work depends on empathy.

## The Specific Failure Mode

The failure mode is the leader who is "good with people" in the abstract sense — gives moving speeches, expresses concern for the population they are responsible for, performs care — while having no body-level felt contact with any specific individual inside that population.

The performance can be sustained for a long time. The person delivering the speeches may even sincerely believe the care. But the actual mechanism that would make the care operational — being affected by the specific people affected — has decayed. The decisions get made on the basis of the category. The category-level decisions can be catastrophic for individuals inside the category in ways the leader would have caught if the empathy had still been operational.

This is the structural origin of many leadership disasters that get described in moral terms after the fact. The leader was not a monster. The leader was a person whose empathy had been hollowed by scale, who continued making decisions as if the empathy were still operational, and whose decisions were therefore systematically miscalibrated.

## What Counteracts It

The counter-practice has to match the cadence of the empathy-decay-producing input. If the affected-people-as-abstractions are arriving daily, the contact-with-specific-people has to be available daily. The cadence of the practice has to match the cadence of the abstraction.

The operational form Joe is naming:

- When a message from someone affected by the work arrives, pause and let it land before moving on
- When an in-person encounter happens with someone whose life shifted, let yourself be moved
- Pick a specific person each day or week and stay with what their experience has been; refuse the move to abstract them
- Notice when "the work is touching a lot of people" is becoming the cognitive substitute for being touched by specific people
- If you cannot remember the faces or stories of the people who recently reported impact, the abstraction is winning
- When tears arise in contact with someone changed by the work, let them arise

See [[let-it-break-your-heart-on-a-consistent-basis]] for the developed practice.

## The Permeability Frame

The deeper structural response is the move from "dissolution of self" to "permeability of self." Dissolution alone — "it's not me, it's coming through me" — can become the cover for the empathy decay. The frame is true and it can also become the mechanism by which the practitioner avoids feeling the impact of the work.

Permeability is the move where the self gets larger and more porous rather than smaller. The personal is not abandoned in favor of being a clean channel; the personal is held alongside the channel-frame. The both-and is what makes the work sustainable at scale without the empathy decay.

See [[dissolution-of-self-vs-permeability-of-self]] for the distinction this concept points toward as its structural corrective, and [[the-hollowing-out-process]] for the developmental arc that puts both moves in sequence.
