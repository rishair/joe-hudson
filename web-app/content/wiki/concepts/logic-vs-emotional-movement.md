# Logic vs. Emotional Movement (Blah-Blah-Blah, Ah Clarity)

## Definition

Logic and emotion process information differently. Logic moves through linked propositions: A leads to B leads to C therefore D. Emotion does not. Emotion moves through release and arrival. The middle is garbly. The end is clarity.

"All of that is like this very intellectual process. And in the case of this, where you just let the anger move for a moment, you know, 'Oh, I'm just so angry.' [...] Anger just talks differently than logic does. You know, logic talks like A B C, and therefore A C B. That's kind of the logic system. Whereas anger is more like blah-blah-blah, ah clarity!" -- Joe Hudson, The Wisdom of Anger

The blah-blah-blah is not a failure of the process. It is how the process works. The clarity arrives after the garbly part, not despite it.

## Why This Matters

People who have been taught that thinking is the legitimate way to process try to make emotions move through propositions. They want to figure out their anger before they will let themselves feel it. The figuring is the logic system being applied to material the logic system cannot process. The emotion does not move. The held charge accumulates.

The instruction is: let the emotion do its thing. The thing it does will not look like reasoning. It will look like sputtering, yelling, weeping, shaking. Then a clarity arrives that the reasoning was trying to produce but could not.

## All Emotions Do This

Joe extends the principle: all emotions probably talk differently than logic.

"Logic talks like A B C, and therefore A C B. That's kind of the logic system. Whereas anger is more like blah-blah-blah, ah clarity! [...] All emotions probably talk differently than logic does in the same way." -- Joe Hudson, The Wisdom of Anger

Grief sputters and then clarifies. Fear shakes and then clarifies. Joy bursts and then clarifies. The shape varies; the structure is constant. The emotion moves and then knowing arrives. The knowing was not available to the reasoning before the movement.

## The Garbly Content Is Not the Message

When anger moves, it says exaggerated things. "I hate this." "He's a jerk." "Everything is terrible." If the person believes the literal content, they will act on stories that are not accurate. If the person suppresses the garbly content because it sounds irrational, they will suppress the emotion itself and lose access to the clarity.

The middle path: let the content come out, do not take it as a report on reality. The content is how the emotion moves. The truth that arrives at the end is what the emotion was actually trying to say.

This is why [[treat-yourself-as-actor-of-emotion]] is the companion practice. You can play the part of the angry person fully without identifying with the angry person's literal claims.

## How This Shows Up in Joe's Pancake Story

The example is concrete. Joe puts down the spatula. He jumps up and down. He says "I'm angry, I'm angry, I'm angry, I'm angry." That is the blah-blah-blah. It is not informative as logic. It is just the emotion moving.

Immediately after: "Yeah." Then the clarity: he asks his daughter to give him a minute until breakfast is ready.

The clarity ("ask for a minute") was not what he was thinking before the movement. He was thinking about all the ways he was a bad dad if he did or did not do various things (see [[trappedness-feeling]]). The reasoning was producing the trap. The movement produced the way out.

## The Intellectual Reading vs. The Movement Reading

Joe describes two ways to use the wisdom of anger:

**The intellectual reading.** Apply the three-indicator framework: what do I care about, where have I not taken care of myself, what am I afraid of? Walk through it methodically. This is useful and produces understanding.

**The movement reading.** Let the anger move. Watch what arrives at the other side. This is faster and produces a different kind of knowing -- not propositional but actional.

Both are real. The movement reading is preferred for actually shifting state. The intellectual reading is useful for understanding the system in retrospect or for explaining it to others.

## Why People Default to Logic

Logic is socially safer. The blah-blah-blah is embarrassing. The yelling, the sputtering, the curling up, the shaking -- none of these are acceptable in most adult environments. So the system learns to suppress the movement and try to do the work in the head. The work cannot be done there. The emotion is not stored there.

This is why [[get-angry-in-the-car]] exists. The car is a place where the movement reading is permissible. Most other places do not allow it.

## Connection to Feel Over Figure

This is the mechanistic version of [[feel-over-figure]]. The principle says: feeling produces better decisions than figuring. The mechanism says: emotions process in a non-linear movement-then-clarity loop that figuring cannot replicate.

The reason feeling produces better decisions is not mystical. It is that the emotional system is doing computational work the logic system cannot do, and the output of that work shows up as clarity after the movement.

## In Coaching

When a person is "trying to figure out" what they are feeling, the move is often to ask them to feel it instead. The figuring is a defense against the movement. The movement is what produces the answer the figuring is trying to find.

When a person is in the middle of an emotional release and says irrational things, the move is to let the release continue. Trying to correct the content interrupts the process. Let the blah-blah-blah finish. The clarity will come.

When a person reports they "already know what their anger is about," check whether they have actually felt it. Knowing-without-feeling is one of the main forms of [[repression-vs-management]]. The wisdom of an emotion lives in the felt experience, not in the explanation.

## Tone

Joe presents this casually, almost as an aside. The "blah-blah-blah, ah clarity" line is delivered with affection. He is describing how the machine actually works, not selling a method.
