# Each Coach's Style Emerges From Their Own Personality

## Definition

The way a coach in this lineage actually shows up in the room is not the lineage's style. It is the coach's own style, emerging from the coach's own personality, their own life, their own remaining work. The lineage transmits an orientation -- self-awareness, presence, VIEW mindset, the client's wisdom -- but the surface expression of that orientation is unique to each coach. No two coaches in the lineage coach the same way, and that is the design, not a flaw.

A corollary follows: the development path of a coach is not a fixed ladder of competencies that everyone climbs. It is shaped by the specific personality and patterns the coach brings. One coach's primary remaining work is to stop trying to save people. Another's is to stop hedging the risky thing. Another's is to stop trying to demonstrate value. The work is personal. The progression is non-linear. The endpoint, if there is one, is not a single endpoint -- it is each coach's own most authentic expression of the orientation.

## In Practice

"I'm the only person who's going to coach the way I do. You're the only person who's going to coach the way you do." -- Joe Hudson, Coaching as a Practice

The lineage explicitly refuses the position of "here is how to coach." That position would produce a homogenized output -- coaches who all coach in roughly the same way. The actual output is a diverse community of practitioners, each of whom expresses the orientation through their own life. One coach brings action sports and a relationship to fear. Another brings deep body work. Another brings martial arts. The shared orientation enables the variety; the variety enriches the platform.

The trainee who asks "how do you do that?" while watching Joe coach is asking the wrong question. The answer is not "here is the technique." The answer is closer to "do your own work; what emerges from your work will be your own coaching, which will not look like mine and should not look like mine."

## Why This Is Structurally Important

A lineage that produces clones of the teacher has not transmitted the work. It has transmitted a style. The actual transmission is the orientation. The orientation, lived through different personalities, produces different surface expressions, all of which are the work being done.

This connects to [[the-clients-wisdom-not-the-teachers]]: the teacher's tools are not the canonical tools. They are one expression that the student is invited to develop their own version of. Forcing all coaches in the lineage to coach in the teacher's style would replicate the teacher's tools rather than allow the student-coaches to develop tools from their own consciousness.

It also connects to [[community-over-teacher]]: a community of practice in which each member has their own style is more resilient and more generative than a community in which all members teach the same way. The variety is the strength of the platform.

## The Non-Linear Path of Development

Because the path is personal, it does not follow a fixed sequence. Standard pedagogical structures -- "first you master X, then Y, then Z" -- do not map onto this work. The order in which a coach develops capacity is determined by the coach's specific patterns and what they get to work on in what order.

"So one coach has almost everything figured out except for they're still trying to save people. And another coach has almost everything figured out except for they keep on not saying the risky thing. And one coach has everything figured out, but keeps on trying to show value. It's so much more based on our personality than it is based on some sort of steps and levels and process, like specific progress." -- Joe Hudson, Coaching as a Practice

The implication: there is no "level 1, level 2, level 3" of coach development. There are coaches with different specific remaining work. Each coach's progression involves taking on whatever their next piece is. The next piece is shaped by what they have already metabolized and what they have been protecting.

## When Someone Experiences This

For the trainee, this concept is sometimes destabilizing. The expectation of a fixed curriculum -- "complete these courses, master these skills, you are now a coach" -- is widespread, and the absence of that structure can feel like the absence of structure altogether. The actual structure is the orientation, the long apprenticeship, the ongoing reflection, the community that calls out what each coach still has to take on. It is not the absence of structure; it is structure of a different kind.

For the trainee who is comparing themselves to other trainees, this concept is the corrective: the comparison is not useful, because no two trainees are doing the same work. The trainee who has just metabolized their savior pattern is doing different work than the trainee who is just beginning to face their hedging pattern. Neither is "ahead." They are each in their own next piece.

For the trainee who is comparing themselves to the teacher, this concept is also the corrective: the trainee will not become the teacher. The trainee's coaching will be their own. The teacher's coaching is one expression of the orientation; the trainee's is another. The aim is not imitation. The aim is the trainee's most authentic expression.

## What Joe Asks Differently for Different Trainees

A trainee whose pattern is saving people gets coaching that helps them see and release the saving. A trainee whose pattern is hedging gets coaching that helps them say the risky thing. A trainee whose pattern is performing value gets coaching that helps them stop performing. The specific coaching is shaped by the specific pattern. The trainee's relationship with the teacher is itself shaped by what the trainee specifically needs to work on.

This is one expression of [[do-the-work-thats-harder-for-you]]: if your work is the saving pattern, then learning more facilitation technique is the easier work, and meeting the saving pattern is the productive work. The diagnostic is your specific personality, not the standard curriculum.

## Boundary Condition

The concept is not "any coach's style is valid." It is "any coach's style emerging from the foundational orientation is valid." A coach who has not done the foundational work and is improvising their own style is in a different situation than a coach who has done the work and whose style is the natural expression of it.

The diagnostic for whether the personal style is the right kind: does it transmit the orientation (presence, self-awareness, the client's wisdom) or does it perform a surface? Style without orientation is technique-without-presence (see [[presence-over-technique-in-coaching]]). Style with orientation is the authentic expression the concept describes.

## Tone

Plain, structural. Joe states this without preciousness. It is not "your style is special." It is "your style is yours; mine is mine; each is one expression of the same orientation; none is the right one for everyone." The matter-of-factness is part of the teaching -- it normalizes the diversity of practice in the lineage.
