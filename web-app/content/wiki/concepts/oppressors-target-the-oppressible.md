# Oppressors Target the Oppressible

## Definition

The signature of feeling chronically stuck or trapped -- the freeze pattern, the held breath, the bracing, the inability to push back -- is detectable. People who look for victims to prey on can spot it with high precision. The chronic stuck-stance is not just an internal experience; it is a broadcast that attracts the exact people who will confirm the stuck-narrative.

"Usually people who oppress, people who are looking for victims for their behavior, can actually spot that exact quality with laser precision." -- Joe Hudson, How To Get Unstuck

The implication: the stuck person is not just suffering the trap they're in; the trap is being recreated by the stance they're holding. The stance generates the dynamics.

## The Crosswalk Study

The source surfaces a specific piece of research that makes the mechanism concrete:

"There's this study that was done of some aggressive people who -- I don't think they were rapists, but there were people who, like, stole purses or something, and they just had them watch people who were crossing the crosswalk, and they could just say, that person, that person. They all agreed on who was the victim." -- Joe Hudson, How To Get Unstuck

The study has been replicated in various forms. Predators given footage of people walking can identify, with high inter-rater agreement, who would be a good target. They are reading something specific in the gait, the posture, the eye contact, the energetic field of the person. They are not guessing. They are reading a signal that is actually being broadcast.

The signal correlates with what Joe calls "very oppressible." The walking pattern of a person who is chronically braced, who flinches in advance, who has the felt-shape of not-being-able-to-protect-themselves -- that pattern is visible to people whose work depends on reading it accurately.

## The Mechanism

The mechanism has two parts:

**Part one: the stuck-pattern produces a behavioral signature.** A person who has been freezing for years has a distinctive way of moving through the world. Held breath. Hunched shoulders. Eyes that don't fully meet others. A small flinching quality in response to interaction. An apologetic energy when taking up space. The body's chronic preparation for attack is visible to anyone trained to see it.

**Part two: people who oppress have learned to read the signature.** Whether predators on the street or controlling bosses in offices or abusive partners in relationships, the people who exploit others have developed (often without explicit awareness) the skill of identifying who will not push back. The skill is functionally important to them; without it, they would pick targets who fight back, which would make their exploitation costly.

The two parts combine to produce the dynamic: the stuck person broadcasts the signal, the predator reads it, the predator approaches, the stuck person freezes as predicted, the exploitation proceeds, the stuck-pattern is reinforced. The trap is being completed by exactly the people the trap is supposed to protect against.

## The Confirmation in Adult Relationships

"There's a weird way in which you're inviting it. If you actually feel stuck, you're inviting an oppression." -- Joe Hudson, How To Get Unstuck

"You're inviting oppressors, or at least you're putting off something that people will recognize and they can manipulate. The aggressive, abusive boss will tend to find the person who's going to acquiesce to all of their demands until eventually the whole thing falls apart and it doesn't work for anybody." -- Brett Kistler, How To Get Unstuck

The adult version of the dynamic plays out across domains:

- **At work:** the aggressive boss finds the employee who freezes when criticized. The employee acquiesces to expanding demands. The boss escalates because there is no pushback. Eventually the situation collapses.

- **In romantic relationships:** the controlling partner finds the partner who will not draw boundaries. The control expands. The freezing partner becomes more frozen. The relationship becomes increasingly defined by the dynamic.

- **In friendships:** the dominant friend finds the friend who never says no. The friendship operates increasingly on one person's terms. The freezing friend resents but cannot speak.

- **In families:** the family member who runs the family finds the member who will absorb the family's projections. The freezing member takes on the role of the one-who-doesn't-cause-trouble. The role becomes permanent.

In each case, the person did not consciously choose to be in the dynamic. The dynamic was generated by the meeting of two complementary patterns: the freezing pattern and the looking-for-someone-to-freeze pattern.

## The Compounding Mechanism: Freeze Triggers Frustration

A subtler version of the mechanism, surfaced by Joe in the source:

"Like, oh wait, every time I am a little frustrated, you freeze. So now I feel like I can't depend on you. That really fucking frustrates me. I'm going to even be more frustrated, right? There's all these ways in which that pattern invites the oppression." -- Joe Hudson, How To Get Unstuck

The freeze, in addition to attracting the predator, also produces frustration in people who are not predators. A spouse who is upset and looking for connection finds their partner has frozen. The spouse feels unmet. The unmet feeling produces frustration. The frustration produces more pressure on the frozen partner. The frozen partner freezes harder. The cycle compounds.

The frustrated partner is not a predator. They are someone with a real need for response who is not getting it. But the freeze creates the conditions under which their frustration grows, and the growing frustration creates the conditions under which the frozen partner feels even more attacked. Both partners can be acting in good faith, and the dynamic still produces the same trap shape.

This means the principle applies even to non-predator dynamics. The freeze invites pressure even from people who would not have applied pressure to a non-freezing person.

## The Connection to Victim Stance

This concept is the operational mechanism behind [[victim-stance-invites-abuse]]. Victim stance is not just a psychological position; it is a behavioral signature. The signature attracts the people who will confirm the victim role.

The principle [[what-we-fear-we-invite]] names the broader pattern. This concept names the specific perceptual mechanism by which the inviting happens. The fear is not metaphysical; it operates through detectable behavioral patterns that other people read.

## The Liberating Implication

The concept does not blame the victim. It does name a structural feature that is part of the work of getting unstuck. The person who has been chronically stuck has been broadcasting a signal that has been doing some of the work of recreating the situations they are stuck in.

Changing the signal is part of the work. The signal changes when the underlying pattern changes -- when the freeze releases (see [[double-the-freeze]]), when the anger and fear move (see [[anger-first-then-fear-out-of-stuck]]), when the self-oppression is recognized and discontinued (see [[feeling-stuck-is-self-oppression]]), when the person starts being themselves despite consequences (see [[be-yourself-despite-consequences-undoes-stuckness]]).

The work produces a different behavioral signature. The new signature attracts different people. The new dynamics confirm the new pattern instead of the old one. Over time, the person finds themselves surrounded by people who treat them well, not because the world has changed but because they are emitting a different signal.

## Why This Concept Is Useful

For the coaching work, the concept is useful in several ways:

- **It removes the externalization of blame.** A person who keeps ending up in oppressive dynamics has been blaming "bad bosses" or "bad partners" or "bad luck." The concept names a mechanism that the person can act on. The frame is empowering, not blame-inducing.

- **It explains the repetition.** People who keep ending up in the same dynamic often cannot understand why. The concept names why: the signal is operating, the matching person is finding them.

- **It motivates the felt work.** A person who sees that their stuck-pattern is recreating the situations they're stuck in has additional reason to do the unsticking work. The work is not just for their internal experience; it is for the dynamics they live in.

- **It predicts what will change.** As the work progresses, the predator-attraction will decrease. This gives the person a measurable check on whether the work is taking effect.

## Used Carefully

This concept must be deployed with care. It can be misused:

- **Do not weaponize it against the person.** "You attracted this abuser" can become "this abuse is your fault." The concept is about the mechanism that recreates situations, not about moral responsibility for what others have done.

- **Do not deploy it during acute trauma.** A person currently in an abusive situation needs safety and support, not analysis of what they did to invite the situation. The concept is for the work of patterns over time, not for acute crisis.

- **Do not collapse it into "just stop being stuck."** The signal does not change by trying to project a different signal. The signal changes when the underlying pattern changes, which requires the actual felt work.

- **Recognize that some predators target everyone.** Not all victimization is signal-driven. Some predators are opportunistic and would target anyone they could get away with targeting. The concept describes a real mechanism without claiming it explains all victimization.
