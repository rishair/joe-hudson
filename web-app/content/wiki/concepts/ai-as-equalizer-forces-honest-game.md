# AI as Equalizer (Information Symmetry Forces the Honest Game)

## Definition

When AI gives every party in an interaction access to the same expertise, the asymmetric-knowledge advantage that used to drive outcomes collapses. The interaction shifts from a contest of who-knows-more to a contest of who-can-see-more -- who can read the patterns in themselves and others, who can stay clean under pressure, who can be themselves in the room. The bluff, the technical-jargon intimidation, and the credential-based browbeating stop working when both sides have the same AI counsel in their pocket.

The result is not a fairer game in the sense of equal outcomes. The result is a more honest game. The variance shifts from information access to wisdom -- the un-hireable, un-outsourceable capacities that determine what happens when two well-informed parties meet.

"Now we both have access to Gemini and ChatGPT law. And so there's actually a way that it, like, makes the interaction more honest because it's as though you had a bunch of poker players at a table... And one of them's really good at reading cards, one of them's really good at reading people, another one's just really statistically masterful. But if everybody had access to all three of those things, you'd be much more inclined to play the straight game." -- Joe Hudson (Brett Kistler), AI and The Return to Being Human

## The Mechanism

The standard structure of high-stakes interaction has been built on information asymmetry. One party has the lawyer; the other does not. One party has the medical expertise; the other does not. One party has read the field; the other has not. The party with more information can deploy the gap as leverage -- intimidating, browbeating, jargon-flooding, or simply outrunning the less-informed party's capacity to process what is happening.

When AI is universally available, every party can call on roughly the same expertise in real time. The lawyer-with-AI is still better than the layperson-with-AI in many ways, but the gap is much smaller than the lawyer-with-AI versus the layperson-without. The layperson can now ask, "what does this clause actually mean?" and get a reasonable answer in seconds. The intimidation moves stop working because they were operating on a gap that no longer exists.

What remains is the part of the interaction that does not depend on information access: the patterns of how each party shows up, the integrity of their stance, the quality of their decisions under pressure, the capacity to read what the other party is doing. This is the wisdom layer. See [[intelligence-vs-wisdom]].

## Why This Matters Now

The collapse of information asymmetry is not theoretical; it is happening across domains.

- **Legal.** Both parties in a dispute can now have AI-assisted legal counsel. The party with the cheaper lawyer is no longer structurally disadvantaged on knowing the law.
- **Medical.** Patients walking in with AI-summarized research are now standard. The doctor's information monopoly is eroding.
- **Negotiation.** Buyers can run market-comparable analysis instantly. The seller's information advantage on pricing has compressed.
- **Hiring.** Candidates can rehearse interview questions with AI before showing up. The interviewer's structural advantage has shrunk.
- **Education.** Students can verify a teacher's claims in real time. The expert-as-authority shape is becoming harder to sustain.

In each case, the part of the interaction that ran on information asymmetry is shrinking. The part of the interaction that runs on wisdom -- pattern recognition, presence, integrity, decision-making -- is becoming the differentiator.

## The Coaching Implication

A person heading into a high-stakes interaction in an AI-equalized environment cannot rely on out-knowing the other party. The work shifts to:

- Reading what the other party is doing in real time, including the relational moves -- intimidation, performance, manipulation, sincerity. See [[reads]] for the general capacity and [[wisdom-as-being-human]] for the framing.
- Staying clean in oneself so that the other party's moves do not pull a reaction. This is the inner-game work. See [[the-three-games-outer-inner-team]].
- Being able to name what is actually happening between the two parties when it would serve. This is the team-game work translated to a two-person interaction.

The leverage is the same leverage that has always existed under information asymmetry; it is now just the entire game rather than one element of it.

## Joe's Specific Example: The Intimidation in the Legal Negotiation

Joe references a concrete instance from his own life. In a legal negotiation, the counterparty was using body language and presence to intimidate -- doing what may have been an unconscious move to make Joe feel small, in the hope he would bend.

"You sat with this person that you have a lawsuit with, and they tried to intimidate you... There was a guy, one of the people there, was trying to intimidate you. That was their internal game towards you. And your internal game was, 'Oh, I recognize that you're doing this thing of trying to make me feel small so that I'll bend.'" -- Brett Kistler describing Joe's account, AI and The Return to Being Human

"I'm not even sure he was recognizing it. I just noticed the body language thing happening." -- Joe Hudson, AI and The Return to Being Human

The relevant capacity was not legal knowledge -- both sides had access to that. The relevant capacity was Joe noticing the move, recognizing what was being attempted, having compassion for the fact that the person was probably not even aware they were doing it, and not reacting in the way the move was designed to produce. That capacity is wisdom. It is the un-hireable, un-outsourceable, un-replicable advantage that determines what happens in the room when both parties have the same information.

"You were like, 'Yeah, I see that thing,' which gave you the competitive advantage in that negotiation because you understood yourself and you understood them. You understand them enough to even have compassion. Even to understand that they might even not recognize what they're doing. But you're like, 'Oh, I get that. I get -- I see what you're doing, whether you know it or not. And I know how most people react to that, and I'm not going to react that way.' And that is the competitive advantage of wisdom when that great equalizer happens." -- Brett Kistler, AI and The Return to Being Human

## When Someone Experiences This

The person who has experienced the AI-equalizer in their own field reports:
- A felt shift in what kind of preparation matters -- less time spent on memorizing facts, more time spent on getting clean in themselves before the conversation
- Surprise at how often the supposedly expert other party is leaning on tactics that no longer work
- A new sense of available stance -- they do not need to be the expert in the room to hold their ground
- A re-prioritization of what to develop -- the inner-game work and the reading-of-others work become more obviously valuable

The person who has not yet experienced it reports:
- Continued investment in information accumulation as the path to competence
- Anxiety about not knowing enough in high-stakes situations
- Reliance on credentialed others to handle anything technical
- A felt sense that the playing field is still tilted by information asymmetry, when in many domains it no longer is

## What the Concept Does Not Say

It does not say expertise becomes worthless. Deep expertise still matters -- the AI does not replace it, and the person with both deep expertise and AI access outperforms the person with only AI access. The point is that the asymmetry between expert and layperson has compressed, and the part of the interaction that depended on the gap is shrinking.

It does not say all interactions become symmetric. Power, capital, leverage, and structural position still differ between parties. The equalizer compresses the information dimension; the other dimensions remain.

It does not say AI is universally available right now. There is still a gap between people who use AI fluently and people who do not. The concept describes a direction, not a current state. The direction is toward symmetry; the rate varies by domain and demographic.
