# Sense of Time Expansion (And Eventual Implosion Into the Present)

## Definition

The temporal axis of self-development. As the person develops, the time horizon they can feel into expands — from days, to weeks, to years, to generations. Then at some point the entire structure implodes and what is left is the present. The expansion-then-implosion is the head-level marker of transformation alongside the parallel marker [[expanding-sense-of-self]].

"You can look at your sense of time and you can look at your sense of self and how they develop over time. And the more universal that they become, the further along on the path you are." -- Joe Hudson, How To Measure Your Progress In Personal Development & Transformation

## The Stages

### The Child's Time Horizon

A two-year-old, told their birthday is in 8 months, cannot get excited. The time scale is too large for the felt-sense. Excitement only attaches to things a week or two out.

"Little kid, 2 years old, and you say, 'Hey, your birthday's in 8 months.' They don't get excited." -- Joe Hudson, How To Measure Your Progress In Personal Development & Transformation

The child is not failing to understand "8 months" as a quantity. The child cannot make the future eight months from now feel real enough to produce a present-tense emotional response. The capacity to feel into time is not yet developed.

### The Adult Mid-Range

The same person at 50, told retirement is 8 months away, can feel that as excitement. The time horizon has expanded. Eight months feels close. The future is real enough to register in the body.

"When you're like 50 years old and you're like, retirement's in 8 months, you're like, oh, you can feel that excitement." -- Joe Hudson, How To Measure Your Progress In Personal Development & Transformation

The expanded mid-range adult can get excited about and plan for things two to four years out. The future is now a substantial part of the experienced field of possibility.

### Generational Thinking

The next expansion. The person starts doing things today for people who will be alive after them.

"I'm doing something today for my grandkids." -- Joe Hudson, How To Measure Your Progress In Personal Development & Transformation

The marker here: action that is anchored in a time horizon longer than the actor's own lifespan. The motivation is not about the actor experiencing the outcome. The outcome lives on a generational time scale.

### The Implosion

At some point the entire expanding structure collapses on itself and what is left is the present.

"At some point, the thing implodes on itself or seemingly implodes on itself and there's just the present." -- Joe Hudson, How To Measure Your Progress In Personal Development & Transformation

The implosion is not loss of time-awareness. The person can still plan, still remember, still anticipate. The implosion is the recognition that the past-future axis was always a construction the mind was using. The actual living was always happening here. The construction can still be used (planning, remembering) but it is no longer the locus of the experienced self.

This parallels the implosion described in [[expanding-sense-of-self]] — at some point the expanding self collapses into "everything is you, and you are everything and/or nothing." The two implosions are the same implosion read on two different axes. Time and identity were the two main constructions; both dissolve when development reaches its limit.

## Why This Is a Head-Level Marker

The sense of time lives primarily in the prefrontal cortex. The capacity to anticipate, to plan, to project, to feel-into-the-future is an intellectual capacity. The expansion of time horizon is therefore a head-level marker — visible to a coach as the person's planning frame and motivational time scale shifts.

A person whose time horizon has not expanded past the next month is operating at one level of development. A person organizing their life around what their grandkids will inherit is operating at another. A person who can speak about both but is actually living in the present is operating at another.

These levels are not better or worse. They are different. See [[there-is-no-better-person-on-the-other-side]] for why the framing matters. The measurement is a map. The measurement does not say the present-living adult is superior to the generational planner. The measurement says where the development has gone.

## The Parallel With Sense of Self

Time and self expand together. They develop on parallel tracks and implode together.
