# The Energetic System

## Definition

A fourth layer that some traditions name alongside the intellectual, emotional, and nervous-system layers — what Ayurvedic medicine, Traditional Chinese Medicine, Tibetan medicine, and some Western somatic traditions call the energy body. The energetic system is the least-mapped of the four layers, with the thinnest research base and the most variable language. It is also, in some traditions, treated as the substrate from which the others emerge.

What it points at is real. The phenomena it describes — sensation in the body, the felt experience of breath shifting blood chemistry, the way one person's state propagates to another in a room, the experience of presence — are observable. The language used to describe these phenomena varies enormously across traditions, and modern science does not have a compelling unified definition.

## The Research Asymmetry

A useful frame for where the work sits:

"If I look at just even the research on it, for instance, the intellectual cognitive development has so much work, so much research behind it. And then the emotional stuff is far thinner. It's starting to get popular now, but the emotional stuff is far thinner. The nervous system is pretty thin. The very worst is the energetic system." -- Joe Hudson, 'WTF Is A Nervous System?' With Jonny Miller

The hierarchy of research depth from most-studied to least:
1. Intellectual / cognitive development (centuries of work)
2. Emotional intelligence (decades, recently mainstream)
3. Nervous system / polyvagal (relatively recent, modest research base)
4. Energetic system (almost no Western scientific basis)

This is also the order in which the layers are typically accessible to people. Most people have language for thoughts. Many have language for feelings. Some have language for the body's autonomic states. Few have language for the energetic layer. Without language, the experience is hard to name and easy to dismiss.

## Why Energy Language Creates Defense

Joe is explicit about why this language often triggers resistance:

"It is the only thing that I can do in a session that would turn more people off than 'let's talk about your chakras' is to be like, 'let's talk about God and how Jesus's wisdom...' It's one of those things that creates so much defense in the system. And I think for good reason, frankly. I think there's been a lot of attack. A lot of people have been attacked through the language of religion, and a lot of people have been attacked through the language of energy. It's like 'you just have bad energy, so I can't be around you.' And it's like, what the fuck do I do with that?" -- Joe Hudson, 'WTF Is A Nervous System?' With Jonny Miller

The defensive response is appropriate. Vague energetic language has historically been used to condemn, to dismiss, to gatekeep, to manipulate. "You have bad energy" is an undefined accusation with no defense available. The body knows this and braces against the language.

The coaching implication: use the language only with people who already have a relationship to it. With most people, the same phenomena are better described in nervous-system or emotional language. The point is not to make people believe in energy. The point is to help them have access to their full experience.

## Multiple Languages, One Phenomenon

The structural insight: every tradition that maps the human (Western science, Ayurvedic medicine, Traditional Chinese Medicine, polyvagal theory, the chakra system, the Reichian body types) is pointing at the same underlying reality with different language. Each is partial. Each catches something the others miss.

"They have a language that makes sense to them, but it doesn't really make sense to potentially other people who are speaking other languages. And so you can look at something like the energy body... there's not a really great definition, but you can see scientific research about how Ayurvedic medicine, traditional Tibetan medicine, or traditional Chinese medicine has really good results in things like ulcers. So there's a system that's completely based on the energetic body that has really good results, but we still don't particularly have a definition or know how to work with energy." -- Joe Hudson, 'WTF Is A Nervous System?' With Jonny Miller

The concrete example: a person does certain breathwork, the nitrogen level in their blood rises, and their body tingles. One language calls this an energetic state. Another language calls it elevated blood nitrogen producing peripheral nerve sensation. The phenomenon is the same. The languages are describing different facets of one event.

"On one level, the energetic system or the people trying to describe the energetic system, they are describing something that's happening, potentially happening scientifically. But when science defines it, they're not going to define it as energetic. They're going to define it as elevated nitrogen. And so this is the thing that I think happens in all of the areas: because we're one system, this language is learning to describe the whole system. The intellect describes a part of the system a lot better than the energetic system. The energetic system will describe something a lot better than the intellectual system. But over time, you can see that they're pointing often to very similar things with different language." -- Joe Hudson, 'WTF Is A Nervous System?' With Jonny Miller

## Where Science Is Catching Up

Two areas where adjacent measurable phenomena point at what the energy frame has long described:

- **HeartMath research on heart rate variability and electromagnetic resonance.** HRV in dog owners and dogs entrains. HRV in people singing together entrains. The interpersonal effect is measurable.
- **Brainwave and blood-chemistry shifts during certain breath practices and altered states.** The same practices that produce "energetic experiences" reliably produce measurable changes in nitric oxide, oxygen saturation, alpha/theta brainwave activity.

These are not direct measurements of "energy" in the traditional sense. They are measurements of correlates. The energetic traditions have been describing something for thousands of years that Western instrumentation can now partly capture. Decades from now there will likely be more direct measurements. The map is being filled in slowly.

## Coaching Use

For most people, the energetic layer is best accessed through other languages:
- A person in their head responds to intellectual framings of the same phenomenon
- A person in their body responds to somatic framings
- A person already steeped in spiritual traditions can use energetic language directly

The coach finds the language the person has access to. If they are a yoga teacher, energy language might be the direct route. If they are a CEO, intellect or body is usually faster. If they are religious, theological language for the same phenomena may land best.

"The art of the work that we do is kind of finding the languaging that resonates with the person. If they're doing a yoga teacher training, the energy stuff might resonate. And if they're leading a company, it's probably going to be more on the intellect or the somatic realm." -- Jonny Miller, 'WTF Is A Nervous System?' With Jonny Miller

This is the same principle that runs through all of Joe's teaching: meet people where they are, in the language they have access to, and walk into the territory from the door they can open. ## The Epistemological Point

A broader principle is at stake. No single intelligence — head, heart, gut, or energetic — has the irrevocable truth. Each is partial. The body lies just as much as the mind ([[body-lies-as-much-as-mind]]). The mind lies. The heart lies. The energetic intuition lies. None is a direct line to reality.

What is reliable is the combination. When multiple intelligences are giving the same report, the report is more trustworthy. When they disagree, that disagreement is itself information. The point of the multi-layered frame is not to find the one true layer; it is to triangulate using the layers that are available.

The energetic language has been treated as a substitute for science in some traditions and dismissed wholesale in others. Both moves are mistakes. It is one more partial map. Use it when it serves. Hold it loosely.

## When Someone Experiences This

- They feel a person's presence shift the room without anything being said
- They do a specific breath and their body tingles in a way they cannot explain mechanically
- They have a felt experience that does not fit emotional or somatic categories
- They are in a tradition (yoga, meditation, healing arts) where energy language is native and the language is opening doors for them
- They are in a tradition that has dismissed energy language and they cannot relate to it at all
- They are stuck between two frames — the scientific one that says it's not real and the experiential one that says it clearly is

## Tone

Cautious and respectful. Joe does not dismiss the energetic frame, and he does not insist on it. He acknowledges the real defense responses people have to vague energetic language, and he honors the long traditions that have produced real results using it. The tone is "this is a thing that points at something real, the science is thin, here is why people get defensive, here is how to use it carefully." Not evangelical. Not dismissive. Curious.

## Boundary Conditions

The energetic frame is most useful when:
- The person already has a relationship to it
- The other frames (intellectual, emotional, somatic) are not reaching what is happening
- The coaching is happening inside a tradition that treats this layer seriously

It is least useful when:
- The person has been hurt or manipulated through energy language
- The conversation needs scientific precision
- Other languages are doing the job better

A coach who insists on the energetic frame with someone who doesn't have access to it is not coaching; they are evangelizing. A coach who refuses to use the frame with someone who lives in it is also failing the person. The skill is meeting the language to the person.
