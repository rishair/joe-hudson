# How is that not here right now?

## The Question

When the person describes what some imagined future state would provide them (alignment, joy, accomplishment, connection, peace), ask whether that thing is in fact already here in the present interaction.

Variations:

- "How is that not here right now?"
- "What about that is not already in the room?"
- "Is it possible that's already happening?"

The question collapses the distance between what the person is seeking and what is. It treats the seeking itself as the obstacle to noticing.

## When Joe Uses It

- When [[desire-for-resolution]] has been named and the person has identified what the resolution would supply.
- When the person describes wanting a future state (peace, connection, clarity) that the present conversation is, in fact, already providing.
- When [[improvement-vs-authenticity]] is active and the person is striving toward an outcome they are already standing in.
- After "what's the resolution going to get you?" has produced an answer that names what the person is already experiencing.

The question is not a Socratic trap. It is a genuine inquiry. Sometimes the answer is "you're right, it is here." Sometimes the person notices what is blocking them from receiving what is here. Both are useful.

## What It Produces

The most common response: a small surprise. The person was operating on the assumption that they were missing something. Realizing that the thing they wanted is in fact present produces a brief disorientation followed by relaxation.

Sometimes the person resists. "Stories in my head prevent me from seeing it" is a common acknowledgment. Joe lets that stand without arguing. The story is the thing in the way, and naming it is the work.

Sometimes the person tries to negate the question: "well, but I wanted a podcast, not a coaching session." Joe gently puts pressure on the negation: "would it have been better than this? I don't know." The person almost always concludes that the present moment is at least as valuable as the imagined alternative, often more so.

## What Joe Does With the Response

- **If the person agrees the thing is here:** Joe does not move on. He stays. Lets the recognition land. Sometimes follows with "so what is it that you want the resolution for?" This question opens the next layer — usually the finish-line story. See [[what-if-there-is-no-finish-line]].

- **If the person resists ("yeah, but..."):** Joe listens to the "but" and meets it. Often the "but" reveals what the person is using to maintain the seeking. See [[no-finish-line]].

- **If the person looks surprised but cannot say more:** Joe stays in the silence. The recognition is doing its work. Words can come later.

## Dialogue Example

- **Joe:** "What's the resolution going to get you?"
- **Brett:** "Alignment, joy, accomplishment, connection."
- **Joe:** "And how is that not here right now?"
- **Brett:** "Stories in my head prevent me from seeing it."
- **Joe:** "I feel connected to you."
- **Brett:** "I mean, I feel—"
- **Joe:** "I'm enjoying the conversation."
- *(Source: Allowing Yourself to Change)*

Joe immediately follows the question with a personal report of what he is experiencing ("I feel connected to you"). This is not a separate move. It is the same move continued. The question pointed at the present; the report gives the present specific evidence to receive.

## Tone

Quiet, matter-of-fact. Not triumphant. The question is gentle, almost off-handed. The energy is "I'm just noticing" rather than "I have caught you." A bit of warmth, no urgency.

## Variations for Different Contexts

- For someone seeking love: "How are you not loved right now?"
- For someone seeking peace: "What about this moment is not peaceful?"
- For someone seeking clarity: "What is unclear right now, exactly?"
- For someone seeking accomplishment: "What have you not already accomplished by being here?"
- For someone seeking enjoyment: "How do you enjoy yourself right now?" / "How about right now?"
- For someone seeking trust in themselves: "How are you not trusting yourself right now?" / "Your preparation didn't happen, and here you are, prepared and trusting yourself."

The variant should be a precise reflection of what the person just named they were seeking. The precision is what lets the question land rather than glance off.

## The Enjoyment Variant: "How About Right Now?"

A particularly direct application: someone says "I want to stop postponing enjoyment to the future." Joe responds immediately: "How about right now? How do you enjoy yourself right now?" The question collapses the postponement structure in the first exchange of the session.

What happens next reveals the person's relationship to the question. The client in the chronic-pain session reported "I am enjoying it" — but his face was confused, his head not following his words. Joe named the gap: "you're telling me you want to not have enjoyment as an abstract thing in the future, and you're telling me you're enjoying yourself right now, and I see this big smile on your face, and I see your head is confused. I see your thoughts are like not quite jiving. So what's happening?"

The question worked. The contradiction it surfaced was the operative material. The person's lived experience already contradicts the structure they came in with, and the question makes that contradiction visible without having to argue against the structure directly.

The same logic applies to "trust in myself" presented as something to develop in the future. Joe asks: "How are you not doing that now?" The person had prepared for this conversation, the preparation didn't happen, and here they are without their script, doing fine. The trust they wanted to develop is operating in the room. The question makes them notice.

> "Whatever it is you prepared for in our conversation is not what's going on right now. You even said it. You're like, wait, hold on. Like, we're not supposed to jump into it this quickly... So your preparation didn't happen, and here you are prepared and trusting yourself. So tell me, what, what am I missing? What do you want that isn't happening?" -- Joe Hudson, How Do I Stop Postponing My Enjoyment?

The question, used this way, is essentially an invitation to notice that the present already contains the future-state the person was waiting for. The waiting was the only thing preventing them from seeing they were already there.
