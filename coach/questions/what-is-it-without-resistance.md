# What is it without resistance in it?

## The Question

Variations:
- "What is procrastination without any resistance in it?"
- "Feel what [X] becomes if there's no resistance in the [X], what happens?"
- "What is that experience if there's no resistance in it?"

The question asks the person to hold the feeling (not the story about the feeling) and strip away the layer of fighting it. What remains is the raw impulse or emotion underneath.

## When Joe Uses It

After the person has been brought into present-moment experience (often via [[not-sometimes-right-now]]). The person can feel the thing but is still conflating the feeling with their resistance to it. This question separates the two.

Joe uses it specifically with procrastination in this transcript, but the structure is universal. Any pattern that involves avoidance can be held this way: what is this pattern if you are not fighting it?

## What It Produces

The distinction between the experience and the fight about the experience. In the transcript, Brett discovers that procrastination without resistance becomes anxiety, which becomes energy, which becomes the impulse to do the thing. The procrastination was not the problem. The resistance to doing the thing was the entire substance of the procrastination.

"What is procrastination without resistance in it?" -- Joe Hudson, If You Can't Love the Feeling, Love the Resistance

Brett's answer: "It feels like the impulse to do something."

Joe's frame: you cannot procrastinate something you do not care about. The feeling of procrastination requires the impulse to act. Without the resistance, only the impulse remains.

"I'm not launching a thermonuclear missile at this moment, and I don't feel like it's procrastination." -- Joe Hudson, If You Can't Love the Feeling, Love the Resistance

## What Joe Does With the Response

Follows the thread. The person has now contacted the raw feeling underneath the pattern. Joe stays with that feeling. The next move depends on what emerges. If the person contacts energy or impulse, Joe may ask about the wisdom in the resistance (what was the resistance protecting?). If the person contacts sadness or fear, this leads toward [[feeling-the-unfelt-emotion]].

## Tone

Quiet, spacious, curious. The question is asked gently because the person needs space to feel rather than think.
