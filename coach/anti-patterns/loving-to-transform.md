# Don't love to transform

## The Anti-Pattern

A person discovers that love changes things. Loving a part of themselves unfreezes it. Loving another person creates space for the other to grow. Loving a situation allows it to evolve.

Then they start loving in order to change things.

The instant love becomes a strategy for change, it stops working. The same act, with the same words, performed for the new reason, produces no change. The person is confused: I am doing what I was doing before, and it is not working.

"What happens for a lot of people is they start to feel the power of love and they start to feel how loving unconditionally starts transforming the world, they start wanting more of it. And so then they start loving to transform the world, and then it stops working." -- Joe Hudson, Love over Defense

## Why It Fails

The transformation that love produces depends on the welcoming being real. See [[love-is-the-best-change-agent]]. Welcoming-in-order-to-change is not welcoming. It is management wearing a costume.

The receiver detects the difference. Whether the receiver is another person or a part of yourself, the agenda is sensed. Sensed agenda activates defense. Defense disables the unfreezing that the welcoming was going to provide.

"If you're trying to love to transform the world, you're not loving anymore." -- Joe Hudson, Love over Defense

The change the original love produced was not delivered by the love-as-technique. It was delivered by the welcoming as state. When the welcoming becomes technique, the state is gone, and the change is gone with it.

## How to Catch It

The signal: love that used to produce shift, opening, or relaxation now produces nothing. Or worse, produces resistance.

Specific tells:
- You are watching for the other person (or the part of yourself) to change after you love them.
- You are disappointed when the love does not produce the result.
- You are loving them more vigorously to get the change to happen.
- You can articulate the change you want the love to produce.
- The love feels like work.
- The love has become a routine you maintain to keep things stable.

The clean version of love has no future-tense in it. The love is the welcoming of what is here now. The transformation is incidental. If you cannot do the love without the eye on the transformation, the love has already turned.

## The Larger Pattern

This anti-pattern is a specific case of [[tools-to-change-not-love]]. The broader pattern: any transformation tool turned into an improvement project disables itself. Meditation done to become calm becomes ineffective. Self-inquiry done to arrive at an answer stops revealing anything. Love done to change something stops changing things.

The structural reason is the same in every case. The tool works through acceptance, recognition, welcoming. Using the tool to change something is by definition not acceptance. The using-to-change cancels the tool's mechanism.

## What Joe Says Instead

The corrective: love because it is what is here, not because of what it will produce.

"To love everything means that you're really happy to feel everything, that you're happy to express everything, that you're happy to be wrong about everything. That you're happy to be empowered and you're happy to feel helpless." -- Joe Hudson, Love over Defense

The love that works has no exit strategy. You welcome the situation including the possibility that it never changes. You welcome the person including the possibility that they never grow. You welcome the part of yourself including the possibility that it remains forever. The unconditional in "unconditional love" means: without conditions, including the condition of producing change.

The paradox: love without the condition of producing change is precisely the love that produces change. Love with the condition does not.

## The Pre-Catch Question

Before loving, the catch can be checked.

- If this person/part/situation never changed, could you still love it?
- Is the love you're about to deliver contingent on a particular outcome?
- Could you welcome this and let it stay exactly as it is?

If any answer is no, the love has already been tainted. The work is to feel what is underneath the conditional. Usually fear. Sometimes need. The path is to feel the fear or need fully before attempting to love.

## What Looks Like This But Is Not

Loving a person and also having a boundary with them is not loving-to-transform. The boundary is not an attempt to change them. See [[boundary-with-vs-boundary-against]].

Loving a part of yourself and also taking action that the part does not want is not loving-to-transform. You can welcome the part and still act from a different part. The welcoming is not a vote on the part's preferred direction.

Loving someone you want to change, while being honest with yourself that the wanting is present, is more honest than performing love that pretends the wanting is absent. The work is to feel the wanting without acting on it through fake love.

## When Someone Experiences This

When someone reports that love practices that used to work no longer do.

When someone is "loving" a partner who is not changing and getting more frustrated about the lack of change.

When the loving-of-a-part-of-self has become another improvement project.

When a person is asking "why isn't my love working anymore?" -- the answer is often that the love has shifted into strategy.

## Upstream (What Leads Here)

- When someone has been doing the welcoming work for a while and recently started using it to produce specific changes.
- When a person reports that loving used to feel good and now feels effortful.
- When someone has just learned that "love changes things" and is reaching for love as a method.
- When a meditator is loving everything to become enlightened.
- When a partner is loving the other to make them grow.
