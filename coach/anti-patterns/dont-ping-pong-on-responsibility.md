# Don't Ping-Pong Between "I'm Responsible" and "Fuck Responsibility"

## The Anti-Pattern

When a person carries a felt responsibility for someone else's emotional experience -- often a partner's potential grief, a parent's potential disappointment, a child's potential distress -- they often oscillate between two positions:

- "I'm responsible. I have to be safe / stable / dependable / predictable for them."
- "Fuck responsibility. I don't owe them anything. They have to deal with their own emotions."

The oscillation can happen across years, weeks, or within a single conversation. Both positions feel like the truth in the moment. Neither produces a settled state.

The anti-pattern is treating this oscillation as a real binary to be solved by picking a side. It is not. The oscillation is a symptom of an unfelt feeling underneath the responsibility question. Until the feeling moves, the oscillation continues, because both positions are defenses against the same thing.

## Why It Fails

Each position is generated by the other. "I'm responsible" creates the felt confinement that produces the rebellion of "fuck responsibility." "Fuck responsibility" creates the felt isolation and abandonment of the other that produces the swing back to "I'm responsible." The oscillation is structural, not a sign of indecision.

Picking a side does not stop the oscillation. The person who lands on "I'm responsible" continues to feel the cage and eventually swings to rebellion. The person who lands on "fuck responsibility" continues to feel the guilt and eventually swings back. Either landing is unstable because the underlying feeling has not been addressed.

From the transcript, after the partner had finally said the underlying apology ("I'm sorry for any way that I've tried to create safety in any way that suppresses myself or us"), the coach named the trap:

"When we haven't gone down to the bottom of it with that apology, 'I'm sorry,' that you just said to her so beautifully, then we can only ping-pong between, 'Oh, I'm responsible,' and 'Fuck responsibility,' right? We can only kind of ping-pong between the two opposed. Right? We just kind of go back and forth reacting." -- Tara Howley, Marriage, Love, and Death

The structural insight: the ping-pong is what happens in the absence of the bottom-of-it work. The two positions are not real options. They are the two coping postures that arise when the actual feeling has not been let in.

## What's at the Bottom

What is at the bottom varies. In the transcript, the bottom was an apology: "I'm sorry for any way that I've tried to create safety in any way that suppresses myself or us." See [[apologize-for-trying-to-control]] and [[creating-safety-suppresses-self]].

In other configurations, the bottom may be:
- The grief of the loss that the responsibility is trying to prevent.
- The fear of being defined by the partner's emotional experience.
- The anger about having been made responsible for someone else's state in the first place (often in childhood).
- The shame about wanting freedom from the responsibility.
- The love that has been suppressed because both the responsibility-stance and the rebellion-stance keep the love at arm's length.

The coach's job is not to predict what is at the bottom. The coach's job is to notice the ping-pong and redirect from the surface oscillation to whatever the person is avoiding underneath it.

## What to Do Instead

**Notice the oscillation as a marker.** Whenever a person swings between "I'm responsible" and "fuck responsibility," the swing itself is the data. It says: there is an unfelt feeling underneath the responsibility question. Both positions are defenses.

**Stop debating the surface question.** The conversation about whether the person is or is not responsible cannot resolve, because the responsibility frame is the wrong frame. The question is not "what is the correct level of responsibility." The question is "what feeling is the responsibility-debate preventing."

**Apply [[can-you-hang-out-with-it]] to the felt sense underneath.** Often the person can point to a body sensation that comes up when the responsibility question arises. Hang out with that. Let it sequence. The oscillation often resolves not by picking a side but by the felt thing underneath moving through.

**Watch for an apology emerging.** Often the bottom of the ping-pong produces an apology -- not about being responsible, but about whatever the responsibility-stance has been hiding. "I'm sorry I tried to make myself safe for you in a way that suppressed me." "I'm sorry I made you carry that." See [[upright-apology]].

**Once the bottom has been felt, the surface question often dissolves.** It is no longer "am I responsible." It is "I see what I was doing. I see what I want to do differently. I can hold both my care for them and my truth about myself."

## Joe's Words / Tara's Words

"When we haven't gone down to the bottom of it with that apology, 'I'm sorry,' that you just said to her so beautifully, then we can only ping-pong between, 'Oh, I'm responsible,' and 'Fuck responsibility,' right? We can only kind of ping-pong between the two opposed. Right? We just kind of go back and forth reacting." -- Tara Howley, Marriage, Love, and Death

The phrase "go back and forth reacting" is the key diagnostic. Reaction is not response. The ping-pong is reactivity at the surface of an unfelt thing. The bottom is non-reactive because the thing underneath has been let in.

## When This Anti-Pattern Shows Up

- A person describing a partnership conflict about whose responsibility something is.
- A person who has decided "I am taking responsibility for everything" and is exhausted.
- A person who has decided "I am no longer responsible for anyone's emotions" and is brittle.
- A person who is currently in the middle of swinging from one to the other.
- A person who reports the swing as the problem ("I just go back and forth, I don't know what to do").
- A parent oscillating between "I have to protect my kid from this" and "they have to learn to deal with it."
- A leader oscillating between "I owe the team certainty" and "the team has to handle uncertainty."

The anti-pattern is the same in each domain. The work is the same: find what the ping-pong is preventing, hang out with it, watch the oscillation lose its energy.
