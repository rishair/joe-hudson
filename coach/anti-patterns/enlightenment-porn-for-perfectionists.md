# Don't Use Enlightenment as Porn for Perfectionists

## The Anti-Pattern

A specific failure mode in people who relate to themselves through perfectionism: enlightenment becomes the ultimate self-improvement project. The same internal mechanism that previously drove the chase for the perfect body, the perfect career, the perfect performance, the perfect emotional regulation now gets pointed at awakening. Awakening is the new top of the perfecting ladder, the final achievement that will at last make the person finished, complete, acceptable to themselves.

Joe's coined phrase for this: "enlightenment, porn for perfectionists."

"I was just reminded suddenly of the time that you said, 'Enlightenment, porn for perfectionists.'" -- Joe Hudson, What Happens When You Wake Up

"Yeah, I mean, God knows that I was in that state for a long time of like trying to perfect myself. And then this, once I reached this enlightenment, I didn't know what it was." -- Joe Hudson, What Happens When You Wake Up

The "porn" framing is precise. Pornography is not the actual thing it depicts; it is an idealized stimulus that activates the desire without delivering the real object. Enlightenment-as-pursued-by-the-perfectionist is structurally identical: an idealized stimulus that activates the perfectionism without delivering the actual recognition. The perfectionist gets to keep performing the chase, feeling the chase, organizing identity around the chase, with the promise that the eventual arrival will at last be the perfect state — even though, as [[if-mind-can-think-it-it-isnt-it]] makes clear, the imagined arrival cannot be the actual recognition.

## Why It Fails

The perfectionism that drives the chase is precisely what the recognition would dissolve. The chase therefore reinforces the structure that the recognition is supposed to dismantle. Each iteration of "I am working hard toward enlightenment" strengthens the bounded perfectionist self by giving it a new project to perfect itself through.

This is one specific form of [[state-seeking]] — the perfectionist version. State seeking in general is contractive, chases an imagined state, and reinforces the seeker. The perfectionist subspecies adds: the chase is also a way to extend the perfecting machinery into the spiritual domain so that no domain of life is exempt from the perfecting impulse.

The structural problem: the perfectionist's relationship to themselves is what produces the suffering the awakening is supposed to address. Pointing the perfectionism at awakening therefore preserves the suffering by preserving the relationship. The person stays exactly where they started, only now they have spiritual vocabulary for it.

Joe spent years in this configuration. The chase was sincere and effortful. The chase was also exactly the wrong move. The recognition arrived only after the chase had quieted — see [[joes-bathroom-realization]] and [[state-seeking]]. The chase did not produce the recognition. The chase prevented the recognition from being seen even when it was available.

## The Specific Diagnostic Markers

How to detect this pattern in someone (or in yourself):

- The language around the spiritual practice has the same flavor as the language around any other achievement project. "I'm working on X." "I'm trying to get better at Y." "I need to be more Z."
- The practice has metrics. Hours sat, retreats completed, books read, teachers studied with. The numbers matter to the person.
- The internal narration is evaluative. "I'm not meditating well enough." "I'm not having the experiences I should be." "Someone else is further along than I am."
- The body during practice is tense, oriented toward producing something, not relaxed into what is.
- There is shame around perceived stagnation. Periods without spiritual progress are felt as failure, not as part of the territory.
- The person resists being told the recognition is not produced by effort. The resistance is structural: their entire relationship to themselves runs on the assumption that effort produces results.
- The person seeks teachers who confirm the chase rather than teachers who would gently dismantle it.

## Why This Pattern Is Especially Sticky

Most chases reveal themselves over time as not delivering what they promised. The perfectionist chases the body and eventually notices the body never becomes good enough. The perfectionist chases the career and eventually notices the career never becomes finished. The disillusionment is what eventually softens the chase.

The enlightenment chase resists disillusionment for longer because the destination is, by design, perfect. The chase never has to encounter the disappointment of arriving at an imperfect destination because the destination is the most idealized version of completeness available in the culture. The perfectionist can chase enlightenment for decades and never run out of imagined further-along to chase toward.

This is also why the warning matters. Other chases self-correct through disappointment. This one does not. The perfectionist who turns toward enlightenment can lose a lifetime to the chase without ever being forced to see what the chase is doing.

## What Joe Says Instead

The corrective is not to stop the spiritual practice. It is to interrupt the perfectionism that is using the practice. The practice can continue. The perfecting impulse pointed at the practice has to be seen and dropped.

The internal move: notice when the practice has become another arena for the perfectionism, and meet the perfectionism with the same welcoming the practice is supposed to be training. The perfectionist who can welcome their own perfectionism without trying to perfect their relationship to it has begun to do the actual work. The perfectionist who tries to "do their perfectionism perfectly" is back in the loop.

A more general corrective: see [[awakening-as-natural-development]]. If awakening is what happens when development continues, then no special chase is necessary. Whatever the perfectionist is using to drive the chase is doing nothing to accelerate the awakening; it is only producing suffering en route. Letting the perfectionism quiet does not delay the awakening. It may be what allows the awakening to be seen when it lands.

See also [[state-seeking]] for the broader anti-pattern this is a specific subspecies of, and [[i-need-to-improve-myself]] for the underlying concern that the perfectionism is built on.

## The Trap Within the Corrective

A specific trap to flag: the perfectionist who hears this teaching can immediately try to perfect their non-perfectionism. "I need to stop being a perfectionist." "I am going to work on letting go of the chase." The chase reinstalls itself on the meta level.

The corrective to the corrective: notice the meta-chase too. Welcome it. The perfectionism is allowed to be present and allowed to be welcomed. It does not need to be defeated. It needs to be met with the same not-perfecting that the practice itself is pointing at.

Over time, the perfectionism softens by being met without being fought. The fight is what feeds it. The welcoming is what dissolves it.
