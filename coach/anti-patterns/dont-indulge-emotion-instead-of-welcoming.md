# Don't Indulge the Emotion Instead of Welcoming It

## The Anti-Pattern

A failure mode of emotional work that looks correct from the outside and is happening on the wrong layer underneath: the person stops resisting the emotion, sits in it, lets it be present -- and then nothing moves. They are not avoiding the emotion. They are also not welcoming it. They are in the space in between, which Joe names "indulging" the emotion.

The classic shape: a person reports they have been "really feeling their shame" for weeks, months, sometimes years, and it has not shifted. They are feeling it. The kick in the stomach arrives on cue. They are not running. But there is no curiosity, no warmth, no love directed at the shame itself. The shame is just present, and they are present with it, and the system is stuck.

"There's a space in between those two. A lot of people, and I would call it like indulging uncomfortable emotions." -- Joe Hudson, The Golden Algorithm

The space in between non-avoidance and welcoming is where the emotion gets indulged. The form looks like the welcoming work. The internal stance is the missing piece.

## What Welcoming Actually Requires

Welcoming an emotion has a specific internal stance that is different from just feeling it. Joe names the components directly:

"It's really about having the mental attitude of, I want to understand this, I want to explore it, I want to listen to it, I want to hear the information it has, I want to respect it, I want to give it the love that it never got, I want to welcome it, and I'm not here to try to change it. I'm here to just fully be with, appreciate this emotional experience." -- Joe Hudson, The Golden Algorithm

The list is the diagnostic of whether welcoming is actually happening. Curiosity about the emotion. Respect for what it carries. Active attention as an offering, not as endurance. The willingness to give the emotion the love it never got. None of these are present in indulgence. Indulgence is presence without the relational qualities.

This is the same internal stance described in [[emotional-inquiry]] -- VIEW directed at the emotion itself. Vulnerability lets the emotion in. Impartiality drops the agenda for it to be different. Empathy stays with the felt experience without flinching. Wonder looks at it like a child finding a turtle for the first time. Indulgence has dropped resistance but not engaged any of the four VIEW qualities. The container is missing.

## Brett's Worked Example: Shame That Stayed Because It Was Being Indulged

The clearest example of the anti-pattern comes from Brett's own period of depression. He was feeling shame, repeatedly, sustainedly. He was not avoiding it. And it was not moving.

"It was, for me, it was shame. And I didn't realize that I was actually kind of indulging in the shame and that being in the shame felt safe. It felt like somebody was -- I had an excuse to not be empowered and that somebody might come rescue me or at least be with me. And so I wasn't actually allowing the shame to move through. It wasn't like I wasn't willing to feel the shame dissolve. I was willing to feel the shame and wondering why it wasn't going away. I was, but not to actually shift and change and then become whatever it was going to become." -- Brett Kistler, The Golden Algorithm

Joe's correction names the missing piece:

"You were feeling the shame, but you weren't welcoming the shame... because it's the welcoming that lets it move all the way through." -- Joe Hudson, The Golden Algorithm

The shame stayed because the relationship to it was incomplete. The shame was being held, but it was not being met. Two structural features of indulgence appear in Brett's report and matter generally:

- **The emotion provides a secondary gain.** Being in the shame felt safe. It gave him an excuse not to be empowered. It opened the possibility that someone might rescue or be present with him. The emotion was doing a job, and indulging it kept the job in place. Welcoming would have allowed the emotion to complete its movement, which would have ended the secondary gain.
- **The intention is not for the emotion to change.** "I wasn't willing to feel the shame dissolve." The person is open to feeling the emotion, but not open to it being transformed by the feeling. The lack of openness to transformation is what keeps the emotion in place. Welcoming is not just about allowing the emotion in; it is about allowing what happens next.

## Why Indulgence Fails

Three mechanisms keep indulgence stuck:

**The relational quality is missing.** The emotion is not just looking for permission to be present. It is looking for the love it never got. An emotion that arrives and is simply tolerated is in a different situation from an emotion that arrives and is welcomed, met with curiosity, given attention as an offering. The first situation produces stagnation; the second produces movement.

**The secondary gain anchors the emotion in place.** Indulgent emotion often serves a function -- it justifies the position the person has taken about their life, it produces a particular kind of attention from others, it confirms a story about who they are. The function is the anchor. The emotion does not move because its job is not done. Welcoming would release the function and let the emotion go.

**The "I'm feeling it" identity becomes its own protective layer.** Once a person identifies as someone who is "doing the work of feeling," they have an investment in the emotion continuing. The emotion is the evidence of the work. Letting the emotion shift threatens the identity built around feeling it. This is a sophisticated form of avoidance that uses the form of the practice to prevent the function of the practice from operating. See [[trying-to-get-rid-of-the-emotion]] for the related failure mode where the welcoming has been corrupted in the opposite direction.

## What Welcoming Looks Like From the Inside

The diagnostic for whether the welcoming is real, not indulgent:

- Is there curiosity about what the emotion is showing? Wonder is the most reliable signal that welcoming is happening. Where there is no wonder, the welcoming has not started. See [[wonder]].
- Is there warmth toward the emotion as a thing being welcomed home? The emotion is a part of the self that has not been met. The welcoming includes the felt sense of meeting that part with care.
- Are you open to the emotion changing? The welcoming includes openness to whatever happens after the meeting. If you are not open to the emotion shifting, you are still controlling it.
- Are you here to fully be with the emotion, or are you here for the emotion to do something? The "here to fully be with" stance is welcoming. The "here for the emotion to do something" stance is either management (see [[trying-to-get-rid-of-the-emotion]]) or indulgence, depending on what the something is.

The internal test: would you look forward to feeling this emotion again? If yes, welcoming is happening. If you are tolerating it but not looking forward to its return, the practice has slipped into indulgence or management. See [[can-you-look-forward-to-feeling-it]].

## What Joe Recommends Instead

The corrective is to add the missing relational qualities. The instruction is not to feel the emotion more; it is to bring curiosity, respect, and love to the emotion that is already being felt.

The diagnostic question to ask in the moment of indulgence: "What would it be like to give this emotion the love it never got?" The answer often involves a felt softening, a shift in posture, a release of the grip that was being held even in the indulgence. The emotion that was stuck starts moving once it has been met with the missing quality.

See [[emotional-inquiry]] for the operational form of the welcoming. See [[love-the-anxiety-not-just-with-it]] for the related distinction between being-with an emotion and loving an emotion, which is the same distinction in different language.

## Joe's Words

"There actually has to be the not avoidance of it, but there also has to be the learning to welcome it. And there's a space in between those two. A lot of people, and I would call it like indulging uncomfortable emotions." -- Joe Hudson, The Golden Algorithm

"You were feeling the shame, but you weren't welcoming the shame... because it's the welcoming that lets it move all the way through." -- Joe Hudson, The Golden Algorithm

## Tone

Joe describes this pitfall without judgment. Indulgence is a recognizable stage of the work that many people pass through, especially around shame. The fix is not to push through harder. The fix is to bring the missing relational quality. The tone is corrective without being scolding.
