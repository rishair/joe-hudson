# Don't Use Inquiry to Win an Argument

## The Anti-Pattern

Treating [[questioning-assumptions]] as an instrument for demonstrating that the other person (or the other position) is wrong. The person doing this looks like they are doing inquiry — they are asking pointed questions, probing assumptions, surfacing what is not being examined. What they are actually doing is lawyering. The orientation is winning. The questioning is the costume.

"There was a lot of really, why is that? Like it was lawyering. It was debate to be right." -- Joe Hudson, Question the Assumption

The activity superficially resembles self-inquiry. The mechanics diverge entirely. Self-inquiry softens the person doing it. Lawyering hardens them.

## Why It Fails

The mechanics of why the lawyering form does not produce what self-inquiry produces:

**It only questions the other side.** The questioner's own assumptions are protected throughout. The questioning is asymmetric. The asymmetry guarantees that the questioner's view will survive every inquiry — not because it is true but because it was never put on the table.

**It strengthens the questioner's position.** Every successful "win" reinforces the questioner's identity as the one who sees through things. The position the questioner started with becomes more entrenched, not less. The opposite of what real inquiry does.

**It produces a contrarian identity.** Over time, the lawyering inquirer becomes "the skeptic," "the contrarian," "the one who doesn't believe X." The identity is itself an assumption that is not being questioned — and is in fact a kind of structural mirror of whatever it is contrarian against. The contrarian investor is still being controlled by the orthodoxy they oppose; they need the orthodoxy to be the thing they are opposite to.

**It generates defensiveness in others.** The lawyering form, used relationally, creates the experience that the other person is being attacked. The other person closes. The questioning produces less truth, not more.

**It cannot access the disorienting middle.** Real self-inquiry destabilizes the questioner's own ground. Lawyering inquiry never touches the questioner's ground. The result is a person who has done a lot of inquiry-looking activity but has not encountered the [[disorienting-middle-of-self-inquiry]] that real inquiry produces.

**It tightens rather than loosens.** The body-felt signature of lawyering inquiry is tight, sharp, focused. The body-felt signature of real inquiry is loose, open, spacious. The body knows the difference even if the mind does not.

## What Joe Says Instead

The corrective is to apply the same questioning to one's own assumptions at least as hard as to the other person's. The marker is whether the questioner's own position is on the table.

"It was just to find the other way that was more right. It wasn't actually seeing the truth in both sides. And the lack of truth in both sides." -- Joe Hudson, Question the Assumption

The shift: from "your position has a hole in it" to "every position, including mine, has truth and has untruth." When the questioner is genuinely interested in finding the untruth in their own view, the activity has become inquiry. Until then, it is lawyering wearing the costume of inquiry.

A second corrective: notice the felt energy. Lawyering has charge — the small pleasure of taking another position apart. Real inquiry has steadiness, even spaciousness. The energy is the diagnostic.

A third corrective: notice defensiveness. The lawyer gets defensive when their own position is challenged. The self-inquirer welcomes the challenge as more material. The defensiveness test reveals which form is operating.

## How to Recognize When You Have Slipped Into This

Live-time signals:

- You are taking pleasure in the other person seeing they were wrong.
- Your questions have an edge.
- You are not actually curious what the answers are — you know what answers will land your point.
- You are not asking the same questions of yourself.
- The other person has closed and you are continuing.
- After the conversation, you feel "won" rather than "opened."
- You have a position you are protecting throughout.

If any of these are true, the activity has slipped from inquiry into lawyering. The fix is not to stop questioning. The fix is to turn the questioning on yourself with the same vigor.

## When Lawyering Is the Right Tool

A caveat: there are contexts where lawyering is appropriate. Adversarial legal proceedings, debate competitions, certain forms of policy analysis. In these contexts, the asymmetric questioning is the activity. The anti-pattern is not lawyering itself. The anti-pattern is using lawyering and calling it self-inquiry, or expecting lawyering to produce what self-inquiry produces.

The mismatch produces the harm. A person using lawyering and calling it inquiry will not get the freedom the inquiry was supposed to produce, will harden their existing positions, and will damage their relationships. None of these failures are visible to them because they are doing inquiry-shaped activity.

## The Relationship to Rebellion

Lawyering inquiry is often fueled by rebellion against an authority figure — typically a parent or institution. The questioning is the rebellion in conversational form. The signal: the lawyering inquirer is consistently questioning positions that align with the original authority and consistently protecting positions that are contrary.

See [[skeptical-inquiry-vs-self-inquiry]] for the full distinction including the rebellion-driven variant.

The rebellion form is the hardest to see because it feels like independence. The questioner thinks they have escaped the authority. Structurally they are still controlled by it — they need it as the thing to be opposite of. The escape is genuine when the authority's position becomes equally questionable, alongside the questioner's contrarian one. Until then, the rebellion has not finished.

## Upstream (What Leads Here)

- When a person describes their inquiry practice with an edge of pride or contempt for those who don't do it.
- When a person reports that they are constantly questioning others' beliefs but does not report questioning their own.
- When the questioning is consistently producing the conclusion "I was right and they were wrong."
- When [[questioning-assumptions]] has been introduced and the practitioner is doing the wrong form.
- When a person's relationships are deteriorating in parallel with their increased "inquiry" practice.
