# Don't Seek States

## The Anti-Pattern

Seeking a particular emotional or spiritual state: "I want to feel that expansion again." "I want to be awakened." "I want to get back to how I felt after that retreat." This is future-living. The person is not in their current experience but reaching for a remembered or imagined one.

The opposite version is also future-living: "This is going to be so hard. I have to feel my emotions again. This sucks." Both are ways of leaving the present moment and both kill enjoyment.

## Why It Fails

The moment is far less enjoyable when thinking about how you are going to end up. Most moments of deep enjoyment have no striving in them. Often the best moments happen right after achieving something, in the gap before the next goal forms. Then a new goal appears and enjoyment disappears.

"If you think about the moments that you've enjoyed yourself most, you probably weren't thinking about your conclusion. Some of the times we enjoy ourselves most is when the proposed conclusion just happened. And so there's just nothing to strive for in that moment." -- Joe Hudson, Embarking on the Journey

State seeking makes [[pendulation]] unbearable. The person clings to the peak and fights the trough. The trough is where learning happens.

## What to Do Instead

Stay with the current experience. If the bliss is gone, how do you enjoy the not-knowing? If the discomfort is here, how do you enjoy the discomfort? See [[enjoyment-as-path]].

Comparative mind ("Am I further along? Am I behind?") is a form of state seeking that requires past and future living to operate. Drop the comparison. Return to this moment.

## State Seeking Is Often Craving Dressed as Wanting

The line between [[wanting-vs-craving]] is relevant here. State seeking is almost always craving: the grip on a specific internal experience, the calculation of how to get back to it, the disappointment when it does not arrive on cue. Clean wanting holds the direction without the grip. If a state arises, it arises. If it does not, no problem.

The diagnostic: in the body, is the want expansive or contractive? State seeking is consistently contractive. The chest tightens, the breath shortens, the mind narrows. Clean wanting is consistently expansive.

This is also why the spiritual versions of state seeking ("I want to be enlightened, I want to be in oneness, I want to recover that retreat experience") collapse the spiritual tradition's own language. The Sufis and the tantric traditions celebrate desire because the desire they mean is wanting -- expansive, present, close to love. State seeking is craving wearing the costume of that desire.
