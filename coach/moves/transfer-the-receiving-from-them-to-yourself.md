# Receive the [Emotion] the Same Way You Wanted [Them] to Receive the [Compliment]

## The Move

When the person has very recently demonstrated, named, or wished for a specific quality of receiving — usually directed at someone else, often through wanting another person to fully take in something they were given — borrow that quality and direct it inward toward the current emotional material.

"So let's see what it's like to feel that all the way, like the way that you wanted that woman to receive the compliment. I want you to receive the anxiety the same way."

The move uses a recent moment of clear felt-sense as the template for the current move. The person already knows how to want full reception. The challenge is applying that knowing to themselves, in this moment, with what is actually here.

## When Joe Uses It

This move requires a specific setup. The person needs to have just demonstrated or articulated the quality of receiving in some other context. Common setups:

- The person has just talked about wanting someone in their life to truly take in something
- The person has just witnessed or described a moment of full receiving and named what they saw
- The person has just expressed frustration that someone didn't receive something the way they hoped
- The person has just described what they wish a parent or partner could do with their love

The proximity matters. The felt template is most useful when it's recent enough that the body still has the sense of it. If the example is from a year ago, the template has gone cold. If it's from five minutes ago, the template is warm and available.

## What This Move Does

The person already has the capacity to imagine full reception. They have it as a wish for others, as a critique of how others receive, as a description of what they wanted to happen. The capacity is intact. The system has just been applying it outward.

The move redirects the application. The same felt sense the person has been wishing onto someone else gets aimed at the self, at the current material. The novelty isn't a new capacity; it's the same capacity used differently.

This is structurally the same as the [[love-as-deep-welcoming]] reversal-of-the-flow move ("find the feeling you have toward a dog, give it to yourself"). Both use a known outward-directed felt sense as the template for the inward-directed move. The advantage of this version: the template was generated in the session itself, so the source is fresh and the person can't intellectualize it away.

## What This Move Produces

The person often pauses. They were holding the receiving as something they wanted someone else to do. Now they are being asked to do it themselves, with the same quality. The category shift is small but disorienting.

The first attempt often misses. The person tries to receive but does it with effort, which is not what they wanted from the other person. Joe usually stays with this and re-issues: "no, not like that — like you wanted her to do it." The mismatch between the effortful attempt and the felt template the person already has becomes visible. The person can self-correct based on the template.

When the move lands, the person reports a felt change. Often softer. Often more in the body. Sometimes tears. The receiving has become available because the template has been borrowed.

## What Not to Do

- **Do not use this move without a fresh template.** If the person has not recently demonstrated or articulated the quality of receiving, the move has nothing to anchor to.
- **Do not let the person abstract the template.** The template has to stay specific. "Like you wanted *that woman* to receive *that compliment*" — the specific other person, the specific moment, the specific quality. Generalizing it ("receive things openly") dissolves the move.
- **Do not stop at the first attempt.** The first attempt usually misses. The mismatch between the effortful first attempt and the actual felt template is part of the work. Stay through the iterations.
- **Do not move on too quickly after it lands.** The felt landing is fragile. Let the person stay in the receiving for a beat. The body needs the time.

## Dialogue Example

The compressed sequence from the source session:

- **Joe:** "Great. What are you going to have to feel? If you weren't afraid, what would you be feeling?"
- **Client:** "No anxiety right now."
- **Joe:** "Yeah, so let's see what it's like to feel that all the way, like the way that you wanted that woman to receive the compliment. I want you to receive the anxiety the same way."
- *(Source: Why You Can't Stop Trying So Hard)*

The move arrives in compressed form here — the broader context is that the client had earlier described or wished for full reception, and Joe uses that as the template for receiving the anxiety. The move is a single sentence that does the redirect.

## What This Reveals About Joe's Coaching Stance

The move embodies a specific principle: the capacities the person needs are usually already present in their system, just being applied to the wrong target. The coaching work is often not building new capacity but redirecting existing capacity inward.

This is structurally how [[capacity-to-love-other-is-capacity-to-love-self]] operates. The person can love others (often very well). The capacity for self-love is the same capacity, used differently. The work is the redirect, not the build.

The advantage of using a moment generated in the session itself: the template is alive, the felt sense is present, the person can't dismiss it as something they couldn't do. They just did it. The instruction is to do it again, aimed at themselves.

## Tone

Direct, clean, slightly inviting. The "let's see what it's like" is exploratory rather than commanding. Joe is offering a move, not demanding it. The voice is matter-of-fact about the transfer — as if it should be obvious that the template would apply.

The move is short. Joe does not lecture about the parallel. He names the template, asks for its application, and waits. The person does the work.

## When to Use This Move

- When the person has very recently articulated, wished for, or critiqued a specific quality of receiving in another person
- When the current work requires the person to receive something (an emotion, a compliment, love) and the direct instruction isn't landing
- When the person can describe full reception clearly but can't seem to do it themselves
- When [[love-as-deep-welcoming]] needs a more specific operational form and a fresh template is available
- When the felt template generated in the session can be borrowed before it cools
