# Assume the Voice Is Right, But It's Not Bad

## The Move

When a person is in a long-running fight with a critical voice — especially a voice that has been telling them something they secretly suspect is true — Joe offers a specific reframe: assume the voice is right, but it's not bad.

"So what if we assume it's right? What if we assume it's right, but it's not bad?" -- Joe Hudson, Am I Being Authentic Or Fake?

The move is structurally a decoupling. The voice's content has two layers fused together: the perception (which is often accurate) and the moral verdict on what is perceived (which is what makes it unbearable). The move accepts the perception and removes the verdict. What is left is intuition.

## What This Is Not

This is not [[voice-as-care]], which reframes the voice's intention (love with bad strategies). It is not [[voice-as-guide-to-unhealed-places]], which uses the voice as a map of what needs healing. It is not [[untruth-in-every-thought]] or [[lawyer-the-voice]], which challenge the accuracy of the voice's content.

The move here is the inverse of challenging accuracy. It grants accuracy. It removes the verdict.

A useful comparison: [[if-i-were-inherently-good]] runs a similar decoupling on a *want* the person is ashamed of (yes, you want X, and if you were inherently good, what would that want look like). This move runs the same decoupling on a *perception* the voice keeps delivering (yes, the voice is perceiving accurately, and if the perceived thing is not bad, what is the voice actually doing).

## When Joe Uses It

The move works specifically when:

- The voice has been recurring for a long time on the same content
- The person partially believes the voice is right but has been treating that belief as evidence of their own brokenness
- The person has been trying to silence, fight, or argue down the voice and the war has not ended
- The content of the voice is something where the perception itself can be reframed as neutral or good (the voice says you are fake — what if seeing the fakeness is the veil dropping; the voice says you want too much — what if the wanting is a clean signal; the voice says you are different from people around you — what if the difference is the becoming-yourself)

The move does NOT work when:

- The voice's content is actually inaccurate, self-attacking distortion (e.g., "you are worthless," "no one will ever love you"). The move presumes the perception has some accuracy to grant. For voices like that, the lawyering move applies. See [[lawyer-the-voice]].
- The person is collapsed into the moral verdict so deeply that the decoupling cannot be heard. In that case, work on the moral collapse first. See [[whats-wrong-with-the-thing-theyre-judging]].
- The voice is trauma-installed at a clinical level. See the boundary conditions in [[voice-as-care]].

## How the Move Operates

The full move is two questions delivered in close succession:

1. "What if we assume it's right?"
2. "What if we assume it's right, but it's not bad?"

The first question alone is not enough. The first question grants the perception. That can land as confirmation of the very thing the voice has been weaponizing. The second question is the one that does the work: it grants the perception AND removes the moral frame in the same beat. The simultaneity matters.

If only the first question is asked, the person can spiral further into shame: "yes, I knew I was fake, the voice was right, I am bad." The second question prevents that spiral by reframing the perception as not-bad in the same gesture that grants it as true.

## What It Produces

The most common response is somatic: a body sensation that the person finds hard to describe. Energy moving. Something going up. A weird feeling. The person often says "this feels like the same way it feels when my own intuition is speaking to me." That is the diagnostic that the move has landed. The voice has been reframed from attack to intuition, and the body knows the difference between hearing an attack and hearing intuition.

"Oh man, that felt like the same way it feels like when my own intuition is speaking to me." -- guest in Am I Being Authentic Or Fake?

The follow-up is often confusion or scrambling. The person has spent years organizing themselves around the voice as enemy. The new frame disorients. The work after the move lands is to stay with the body sensation and not try to manage the disorientation. See [[slowness-after-the-drop]].

## The Buddhist Veil Pairing

Joe often pairs this move with the question about the Buddhist concept of the veil dropping:

"What do you think they're talking about dropping if it's not that?" -- Joe Hudson, Am I Being Authentic Or Fake?

The pairing is operationally important. The person hearing "assume it's right" needs a frame for what the right-but-not-bad perception actually is. The veil-dropping frame supplies that. The voice that has been calling you fake has been pointing at the veil. The work the voice has been doing is the work of seeing through the mask. What you have been calling an attack on yourself is what the contemplative traditions call awakening.

The frame transposition is from "I am being attacked by an inner critic" to "I am the one perceiving the veil dropping, and the perception has been arriving in the form of a voice I have been treating as an enemy."

## What Joe Does After the Move Lands

1. Stay with the body sensation. "Great. Enjoy them."
2. Decline to interpret. The person may try to explain what is happening; Joe does not engage the interpretation.
3. Move to the thanking. Once the voice has been reframed as intuition, the natural next move is to thank it. See [[thank-the-voice-when-its-truth]].
4. Catch the worry about social consequences. The person often worries they cannot operate from this new place around other people. That is addressed separately. See [[fear-of-disconnection-marks-real-growth]].

## Tone

Light, slightly playful. The move is offered as an experiment, not as a teaching. The two questions are asked the way you would ask someone to try on a different pair of glasses. The person is invited to look through the new frame and report what they see. The lightness is part of why the move can land — if it were delivered with weight, the person would brace against it.

## Anti-Patterns

- **Delivering only the first question and not the second.** Granting the voice's perception without removing the verdict can deepen shame. The two questions are one move.
- **Using the move to bypass actual work.** If the voice is pointing at a real piece of mask the person has been operating with, the work is not over once the perception is granted. The work has just begun. The grant is what allows the work to happen without the war. See [[voice-as-guide-to-unhealed-places]] for what comes next.
- **Applying the move to genuinely distorted content.** If the voice is saying "you are worthless" or "you are unloveable," the perception is not accurate and granting it deepens the harm. The diagnostic for whether to use this move: is there a piece of the content that the person privately believes is true? If yes, the move applies. If the content is purely shaming distortion, use [[lawyer-the-voice]] instead.
