# What Happens Right Before You Go Into the Pattern?

## The Move

When a seeker is in a recurring pattern (questioning, doubting, going-into-the-head, dissociating, withdrawing) and the pattern is currently firing, ask what happens right before the pattern fires. The seeker usually identifies the pattern itself. The move is to insist on going one more beat back — what was the state immediately before the pattern engaged?

The structural shape:

1. **Name the pattern.** "I notice you keep going into your mind."
2. **Have the seeker confirm.** "Yeah, I go into my mind."
3. **Back up the timeline.** "Right before that, what — right before you go into your mind, what happens?"
4. **Receive the substrate-report.** The seeker, asked to back up, often discovers a brief moment of presence, of I AM, of substrate, that was there before the fleeing began.

"What happens before you question? ... right before that, what — right before you go into your mind, what happens? ... Before that, I'm just— I am." -- Joe Hudson and seeker, Her Need to "Find" Her Purpose Dissolved In 10 Minutes

The seeker's answer ("I am") is the move's payoff. By asking for the beat before the pattern, Joe surfaced the substrate the pattern had been fleeing from. The substrate was always there; the pattern's job was to make it disappear so fast that the seeker did not register it. The move slows the timeline down enough for the substrate to be seen.

## When Joe Uses It

The move applies when:

- A pattern is firing in real time and the seeker is currently in it
- The seeker has confirmed the pattern is theirs
- The pattern has the quality of being immediate, automatic, not-chosen
- The seeker has trouble locating what the pattern is doing (because the pattern is the only thing they see)
- The work needs to access whatever the pattern is fleeing from

The move is especially useful when the pattern is a flight-pattern — when its function is to make something else disappear. Doubt is a flight-pattern from the substrate. Going-into-the-head is a flight-pattern from the body. Dissociation is a flight-pattern from the feeling. In every flight-pattern there is a state that was being fled. Backing up one beat finds the fled state.

The move does NOT apply when:

- The pattern is not currently firing (the seeker is talking about it abstractly, not in it)
- The seeker is in distress that needs immediate stabilization
- The pattern is not a flight-pattern but a content-pattern (a story being told, a topic being raised)
- The seeker has not yet recognized the pattern as a pattern

## What It Produces

The most common response is a small surprise. The seeker has been treating the pattern as the beginning of the relevant material. The move surfaces that there was something before the pattern — and the something is often the very thing the seeker has been trying to get to.

Three typical outcomes:

1. **The substrate appears.** The seeker, asked to back up, finds themselves in presence, in I AM, in the felt experience the pattern was fleeing. This is the most direct outcome and the one most useful for the I AM → doubt work. See [[i-am-and-the-immediate-flight-to-doubt]].

2. **A feeling appears.** The seeker, asked to back up, finds a felt-experience the pattern was protecting against — sadness, fear, anger, longing. The move has surfaced the avoided feeling. The work moves to being with the feeling. See [[doubt-is-protection-from-a-feeling]] for the structural account.

3. **A different pattern appears.** The seeker finds that before the visible pattern, there was another pattern. This is data too — the seeker has stacked patterns, and the visible one is the outer layer. The move can be applied again to back up further.

In all three cases the move reveals that the pattern was not the beginning. It was the response to something the seeker had not been tracking. The tracking is what the move makes possible.

## What Joe Does With the Response

The follow-up depends on what was surfaced:

- **If the substrate appears:** name what was found. "Yeah, yeah, yeah. So that's the thing you're not trusting. Your mind is questioning that." The naming makes the structure visible: the substrate was the thing, the pattern was the flight from it. The seeker can now see the pattern as the protective response rather than as the primary material.

- **If a feeling appears:** move into being with the feeling. Use [[can-you-hang-out-with-it]] or [[ask-the-body-the-question]]. The feeling was being protected; now the protection is suspended; the work is to meet the feeling with presence.

- **If another pattern appears:** apply the move again. "And what was right before that?" The stacking is the architecture; backing up through it surfaces what is underneath all of it. Each layer is one more beat of fleeing.

In the source session, Joe's follow-up after the seeker said "I am" was: "Yeah, yeah, yeah. So that's the thing you're not trusting. Your mind is questioning that. That's what I keep seeing. It's like, boom, you, you show up and then you're like, question, question, question. So I, I ask the question, you show up, and then question, question, question." The follow-up did several things at once: confirmed the substrate as the relevant material, named the pattern as the flight from it, and surfaced the recurrence of the sequence as a sequence rather than as isolated events.

## Why This Move Works

The move works because patterns are usually treated as the starting point of attention. The seeker has been watching their pattern for years, perhaps. They know the pattern intimately. What they have not been watching is the one beat before the pattern. The beat before is invisible because the pattern's job is to occupy attention immediately upon firing — there is no available attention left to track what was happening the moment before.

The move forces attention to slow down to the beat before. The slowing-down is the intervention. The seeker, asked to attend to a moment they had not previously been able to see, has to actually look. The looking reveals what was always there.

The move also works because the language of "right before" is precise enough to direct attention to a specific instant rather than to a general period. "Before you go into your mind" could mean ten minutes earlier, ten seconds earlier, ten milliseconds earlier. The "right before" specifies the immediately-preceding instant. The specificity is what makes the substrate accessible — the substrate was the immediately-preceding state, not a state from earlier in the day.

The move's effectiveness also depends on the pattern being currently firing. The seeker has to be in the pattern, or just having been in it, for the question to access the live material. Asked about the pattern abstractly, the seeker will produce a generic account that bypasses the substrate. Asked about it in the moment, the seeker has access to the actual recent timeline.

## The Pairing With Other Moves

The move is rarely used alone. It typically appears as part of a sequence with other moves:

- **After [[ask-the-question-as-you-live-your-purpose]]:** the seeker tries the stance-shift, the doubt fires, and this move is used to find what was there before the doubt fired. The sequence is: stance-shift instruction → doubt fires → back-up-the-timeline → substrate surfaces.

- **Before [[can-you-hang-out-with-it]]:** the move surfaces the feeling or substrate; the hang-out move is used to stay with it. The sequence is: back-up → surface the avoided material → hang out with what was surfaced.

- **With [[ask-the-body-the-question]]:** the body is often the organ that knows what came before the pattern, because the body holds the felt-record of the moment. Asking the body what was just before is one variant of the move.

- **With [[i-am-and-the-immediate-flight-to-doubt]]:** the move is one of the central tools for making the I AM → doubt sequence visible in real time. The naming of the pattern requires the substrate-side to be findable; this move is what makes it findable.

## Variations

The move has several variants for different patterns:

- **For doubt:** "What happens right before you go into doubt?"
- **For the head:** "What happens right before you go into your mind?"
- **For dissociation:** "What happens right before you check out?"
- **For caretaking:** "What happens right before you reach for the other person's experience?"
- **For self-criticism:** "What happens right before the critic fires?"
- **For closing the heart:** "What happens right before you close down?"
- **For numbness:** "What happens right before you go numb?"

The structural form is the same: ask for the beat before the pattern. The state-content of the answer will vary, but the structural payoff (something was there before the pattern) is reliable.

## What This Move Is Not

It is not a history question. "What happened in your childhood that caused this pattern?" is a different move and a different timescale. This move is about the seconds immediately before the pattern fires, in this session, in this moment. The micro-timeline is the relevant unit.

It is not a content question. "What were you thinking right before you went into your mind?" is closer to what someone might ask, but it can land in the wrong register. The seeker will produce a thought. The move is asking for the state — felt, somatic, prior to thought — not for the content of the next thought. The wording "what happens" rather than "what are you thinking" is intentional; "happens" implies an event, "thinking" implies a content.

It is not a Socratic move designed to lead the seeker to a predetermined answer. The seeker may find substrate, a feeling, a different pattern, or nothing. All of those are useful. The move is genuinely inquiring, not setting up a punchline.

It is not the [[downregulate-to-interrupt-the-trauma-loop]] move. That move is about exiting a pattern that is firing by using the senses to land in the present. This move is about staying inside the pattern's timeline and looking at what was there before. The two can pair (downregulate first, then back up the timeline once the system is stable enough) but they are different operations.

## Dialogue Example

The exchange from the source session:

- **Seeker:** "It's really hard for me to do. Like, I, I just come back to this trust to the mind."
- **Joe:** "It's really hard for you to do. What happens before you question?"
- **Seeker:** "I go into my mind. I go into—"
- **Joe:** "Right before that, what — right before you go into your mind, what happens?"
- **Seeker:** "Before that, I'm just— I am."
- **Joe:** "Yeah, yeah, yeah. So that's the thing you're not trusting. Your mind is questioning that. That's what I keep seeing. It's like, boom, you, you show up and then you're like, question, question, question."

The seeker's first answer ("I go into my mind") was a near-miss. They identified the pattern but did not yet see the beat before. Joe's repetition of "right before that" is the operative repetition — it insists on one more beat back. The seeker, given the explicit instruction to go further back, finds "I am." The two-beat backing up was the move's full execution.

## Tone

Curious, slow, gentle. The move requires the seeker to slow down their attention to a finer time-resolution than they usually use. The tone has to model the slowing. A fast or pressured tone produces a fast answer, which usually misses the substrate. A slow, curious tone gives the seeker permission to actually look.

The repetition ("right before that") should also be delivered gently. The seeker has just produced an answer; the repetition might be heard as "your answer was wrong." Joe avoids this by treating the first answer as part of the process — "yeah, that's the pattern, and right before that…" The first answer is not corrected; it is built on.

After the substrate or feeling surfaces, the tone shifts. Now it is recognition. "Yeah, yeah, yeah, so that's the thing." The recognition tone marks the discovery and gives the seeker permission to receive it as significant.

## Anti-Patterns

- **Asking the question abstractly when the pattern is not currently firing.** The seeker will produce a theoretical answer that bypasses the felt material. The move requires the pattern to be live.
- **Accepting the first answer when the seeker has identified the pattern itself rather than what came before.** "I go into my mind" is the pattern, not what was before it. The repetition is required.
- **Asking many times in a row when the seeker cannot find the beat before.** If the seeker genuinely cannot locate the beat before, the move has done what it can. Force-pushing will produce fabrication. Pivot to a different access point.
- **Adding interpretation before the seeker reports.** The move requires the seeker's own discovery. The coach providing the answer ("it sounds like there was a moment of presence") removes the felt-payoff of the discovery.
