# Go Down One Level to Find the Projection

## The Move

When the projection is not obvious on the surface — when the person honestly cannot find how the trait they are triggered by is also true of them — go down one level. Find the analogous trait, the deeper version, the same dynamic in a different form. The projection is rarely a literal match. It is almost always a structural match one layer deeper than the surface accusation.

"The problem that people get into when they can't see their triggers, that they just don't quite go deep enough. So for instance, you're triggered by somebody who has lied and you're like, 'Well, I've been honest. I've actually not told a lie.' And well, have you been transparent? Have you actually shared the important things or have you not shared the important things? And how is that not a lie?" -- Joe Hudson, Triggered By Others? Watch This

The literal answer ("I have not lied") closes the inquiry too soon. The deeper version ("I have withheld important things, which is a form of lying") keeps it open.

## When Joe Uses It

- When a person identifies a trigger but defends against the projection on the surface ("I would never do that")
- When the [[mirror-the-judgment]] move bounces off because the literal version is genuinely not present
- When a person is working the [[five-triggers-projection-experiment]] and one of the five does not seem to fit
- When [[triggers-reveal-your-own-beliefs]] has been named but the belief is not landing
- When a person says they cannot find what they would have to believe for the trigger to land

The move is for the situation where the person is not defending out of shame but actually does not see it. The defense ("I do not do that") is honest. The work is to widen what counts.

## What It Produces

Recognition that arrives more slowly than a direct mirror, but tends to land deeper because it required the person's own seeing rather than acceptance of the coach's frame.

The deeper match often surfaces something the person genuinely had not seen — a form of the trait so socialized or so habitual that it did not register as the same trait. The "I haven't lied but I haven't been transparent" recognition is often more useful than the literal-lying recognition would have been, because the transparency issue is alive in the person's present life and the lying issue may have been historical.

Occasionally produces continued defense ("transparency isn't lying"). When this happens, the move is not to argue. Sit with the defense. Sometimes the deeper recognition arrives later, after the conversation has ended.

## What Joe Does With the Response

If the person sees the deeper version: follow it. The projection has been found one level down, and the work proceeds from there toward the apology and the self-loving. See [[five-triggers-projection-experiment]] for the full sequence.

If the person does not see it: do not force. Suggest the possibility ("how is that not a form of lying?") and let it sit. The seeing may need to happen on its own time. The move is the offering, not the extraction.

If the person collapses into "oh god I am also a liar" — redirect. The collapse is the shame form of recognition and undoes the work. Reframe: the recognition is not "you are bad at this" but "you also do a version of this, which means the trigger has a source you can work with."

## Examples of One-Level-Down Mappings

The literal trait does not match. The structural trait does. Examples:

- **Triggered by liar; have not lied:** withheld important things, omitted the truth, presented a curated version, hedged when a clear answer was due
- **Triggered by selfish person; "I'm not selfish":** prioritize your own comfort in small ways, take care of yourself at the expense of others' minor needs, hold back generosity in ways you have not named
- **Triggered by controlling person; "I don't control people":** manage outcomes through hints and indirect pressure, withhold approval to shape behavior, set conditions on your warmth
- **Triggered by arrogant person; "I'm humble":** dismiss someone else's expertise in your head, hold private contempt for what others believe, position yourself as the one who really sees
- **Triggered by lazy person; "I work hard":** avoid the hard internal work, work hard on what is easy and skip what is uncomfortable, use busyness to avoid the actual thing
- **Triggered by ignorant person; "I do my research":** dismiss material that challenges your worldview without engaging it, refuse to learn in the specific domain that would change you
- **Triggered by manipulator; "I'm direct":** manipulate through honesty (using directness as a power move), use vulnerability to control, share strategically

The pattern: every literal disavowal has a structural analog. The structural analog is usually the projection.

## Why The Surface Version Misses

Most projections are not the most literal version of the trait. They are the version of the trait that the person has socialized themselves out of being able to recognize. The literal-version check is a self-defense the trigger has built around itself. The "I don't do that" answer is too clean because the person has organized their identity around not doing the literal thing.

The actual trait lives one level deeper, where the person has not yet built the identity defense. The transparency issue has not been worked the way the lying issue has been worked. The structural form survives because the literal form has been disowned.

## The Risk: Going Too Deep

The move can be overdone. If every trigger requires three levels of abstraction to find the projection, the work has become an exercise in finding-something-to-feel-guilty-about. The point is recognition, not generation. If the deeper version does not actually land in the body, it is probably not the right level.

The diagnostic: does the deeper version produce the small "oh" of recognition? If yes, that is the level. If no, keep looking — and also be open to the possibility that this particular trigger is genuinely about something else (a values violation, an actual harm), not a projection at all. Not every reaction is a trigger and not every trigger is a projection. See [[trigger]] for the diagnostic.

## Dialogue Examples

- **Person:** "I am so triggered by my coworker — they are constantly lying."
- **Joe:** "And how is that not true about you? Have you been lying?"
- **Person:** "No, I really haven't. I make a point of telling the truth."
- **Joe:** "Have you been transparent? Have you shared the important things, or have you not shared the important things? And how is that not a lie?"
- **Person:** "...oh."
- *(Reconstructed from Triggered By Others? Watch This, applying the move structure to the lying example Joe gives.)*

## Tone

The move is delivered with gentle persistence. Not "you must find it" — more "let's look a layer deeper together." The example phrasing ("how is that not a form of lying?") is Joe's preferred form: not telling the person what they are doing, asking them to consider whether the structural analog fits.

The move fails when delivered as if the coach has already decided the projection is there and is hunting for evidence. It works when delivered as collaborative searching.
