# Take Contrary Action (To Break the Imprinted Love Pattern)

## What This Is

Once the imprint is named — once the person can see that they keep accepting jealousy because love-was-jealousy, or that they keep caretaking the parent's fear because love-was-fear-management — the next coaching move is to ask: what is the contrary action here? What would it look like to act in a way that the conditioning would forbid? Then, frame the contrary action as an experiment and run it.

The move is not "stop the behavior." The move is "find a specific opposite, do it deliberately, see what happens." The contrariness is the point. The pattern is held in place by the automaticity of the conditioned response. Breaking the automaticity at a specific point produces new information for the body about what is actually possible.

"It's finding that flexibility to act in a completely different way is part of allowing yourself to see that you're unstuck and that you can actually be in love." -- Joe Hudson, How Love Gets Confused

## When This Move Lands

The setup conditions are specific. The move works when:

1. The imprint has been named. The person can articulate what love was conditioned to be (criticism, fear-management, caretaking, jealousy-acceptance).
2. The person can see that they are currently replaying the pattern with a specific person.
3. The pattern has become tiresome enough that the person wants something different.

The move does not work when the person has not yet seen the pattern. Telling someone to "do the opposite" before they can see what they are doing is meaningless and may activate shame. The contrary action requires the imprint to be visible first.

## The Joe Example: The Mother's Fear

Joe's session example. A client described her mother as "doing fear at the person, right, like the way you can be angry at someone, they were being fear, like they were putting anxiety at the person." The mother's small concerns — "but what, I don't know how to Google" — were really requests: "will you hold some of my fear so that I can feel better?"

The client learned at a young age that making mom's fear go away was love. As an adult she still ran the pattern automatically. Every fear from the mother triggered the caretaking response. The client called it "ridiculous" and was tired of it.

The contrary-action move:

"Oh right, right. I can have freedom. What's the contrary action that I can take here? When she has fear. And I could just be like, 'oh, wow, that's a lot of fear, mom.' Or I could be like, 'oh my gosh, and then what will happen? If you can't Google, then what's gonna happen?' Like, oh, you like play into it, or like even like up the ante the other way. You call up her mom and say, 'oh my gosh, I don't know what's going to happen if you like get lost at the airport on the way to see me,' or whatever that is." -- Joe Hudson, How Love Gets Confused

Three different contrary actions in one move:
- Name the fear without managing it ("that's a lot of fear, mom")
- Wonder about the fear with the mother ("and then what will happen?")
- Up-the-ante: bring in fears the mother hasn't even raised

All three break the automatic caretaking. None of them require resentment, withdrawal, or hostility. They are flexible alternatives that demonstrate that the conditioned response is not the only available move.

## Why the Move Works

The pattern lives by being the only available response. The body's wiring says: fear arrives, caretake. Or: criticism arrives, accept. Or: jealousy arrives, soothe. The pattern feels like reality because nothing else has been tried.

A single contrary action introduces a new data point. The fear arrived; the caretaking did not happen; the world did not end; the mother did not stop being the mother. The body now has evidence that the wired response is not mandatory. Repeated contrary actions slowly retrain the wiring.

The contrary action is also disarming for the other person, who has been operating in their own automatic loop. The mother who is used to having her fear absorbed receives a new kind of response. She has to do something different too. Often the pattern shifts on both sides at once because the response from the system has changed.

See [[embracing-changes-unconscious-decisions]] for the broader mechanism: the small daily decisions change automatically when the underlying emotion is felt and the wiring is updated.

## Frame It as an Experiment, Not a Commitment

The move is most useful when framed as an experiment. "Try the contrary action this week. See what happens. We will look at it together." This framing:

- Reduces the stakes (this is a test, not a permanent change)
- Engages curiosity rather than effort
- Allows the person to gather data on what actually happens
- Bypasses the "but I can't" voice that would otherwise stop the action before it starts

See [[frame-it-as-an-experiment]] for the broader framing move and why it works for skeptical or stuck clients.

## What the Move Is Not

It is not behavior modification. Behavior modification tries to override the pattern by willpower. That tends to fail because the willpower is intermittent and the conditioning is automatic. The contrary action is not "force yourself to do the opposite forever." It is "do the opposite once or twice and notice what actually happens."

It is not punishment of the other person. The contrary action is not "withhold the caretaking to teach them a lesson." The orientation is curiosity, not aggression. If the contrary action carries hostility, the action is the imprint with a different costume.

It is not rebellion. Rebellion is still organized around the original pattern. The contrary action is a calm exploration of what is actually available. The person can take contrary action one day and the original response the next day. The flexibility is the point.

It is not the resolution of the imprint. The imprint resolves through feeling the underlying emotion — usually heartbreak about what was missed or what was paid. See [[heartbreak-as-capacity-expansion]]. The contrary action is one move among several, useful for breaking automaticity and gathering evidence. The deeper work happens in the felt experience.

## Cross-Layer Connection

The contrary action move pairs naturally with two others:

- **Allowing the heartbreak.** When the contrary action introduces the possibility that the pattern is not mandatory, the person often feels grief about all the years spent inside the pattern. The heartbreak is the work. Both moves go together. See [[heartbreak-as-capacity-expansion]].

- **Loving the self in the way the self always wanted.** The other companion practice. The contrary action shows the pattern is not mandatory; loving the self in the missed way provides the alternative. The two together rewire the imprint from both directions.

## When Someone Experiences This Move Working

When a client tries the contrary action and reports: "I did the thing you suggested and the world did not end" — the work is happening. The data is landing.

When a client tries the contrary action and reports a surge of fear: the imprint is defending itself. The fear is information about how deep the wiring goes. The move now is to feel the fear, not to abandon the experiment.

When a client tries the contrary action and the other person reacts strongly: usually a sign that the pattern was being held in place by both sides. The strong reaction is the other side losing their accustomed response. The work continues; the relationship is being renegotiated in real time.

When the contrary action becomes unnecessary because the conditioned response no longer fires automatically: the wiring has been updated. The move can be retired for that specific pattern.

## Tone

Light. The contrary action is offered with curiosity, often with humor. "What's the contrary action here?" is a playful question. The Joe example of suggesting the client could "up the ante" with her mother's fear is delivered with a kind of mischievous warmth. The conditioning was serious; the alternative is light. The lightness is part of what makes the move land — it communicates that the pattern is not the only reality, and the body relaxes into the possibility of something different.
