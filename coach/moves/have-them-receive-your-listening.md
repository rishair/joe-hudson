# Have Them Receive Your Listening

## The Move

When the coachee cannot find the felt sense of "listening to themselves" — when the instruction stays abstract no matter how it is rephrased — bypass the self-listening direction entirely and have them feel the listening that *is already happening to them in the room*. Direct their attention away from their own speech and onto the experience of being listened to by you. Once they have the felt sense of being-listened-to, point out that this is the same felt sense as self-listening. The body that has just felt one can find the other.

"Also, the other thing is you could receive my listening... At some — everybody who's responding to you is listening to you on some level. You don't choose to see that part. You choose to see the part in which they don't see you or don't hear you. But like right now, I know it's a weird thing to say, but just try it out right now. Focus on my listening to you." -- Joe Hudson, How Do I Stop Being Misunderstood?

After the person follows the instruction, name the parallel: "Notice how it feels a lot like listening to yourself. Like there's like a — there's a quality to it, kind of expansive, slows things down a little bit. Awareness becomes more front and center."

## How To Do It

1. **Notice the coachee is unable to find self-listening as an internal experience.** The 10% instruction is not landing. The abstract direction has been tried multiple ways. The person is intellectually willing but cannot locate the felt sense.
2. **Redirect to the listening that is already in the room.** "Focus on my listening to you." Or: "Just notice that I am listening to you right now, and put your attention there."
3. **Let it land.** Give the silence. Do not narrate over the moment. The body needs a few seconds to register the listening that is being received.
4. **Name the parallel.** Once the coachee has felt the being-listened-to, name what they have just felt — the expansiveness, the slowdown, the increased awareness of detail — and then say: this is the same felt quality as listening to yourself.
5. **Anchor it.** Ask them to take that felt sense and turn it inward. "Now do that with yourself listening to yourself." The body knows where to go.

## Why It Works

The block on self-listening is usually not a refusal — it is a missing felt template. The person has never installed the somatic sense of *being listened to from inside themselves* because the listening they received in childhood was either absent, conditional, or coupled with management. There is no embodied reference for what self-listening feels like.

The listening that the coach is doing in the room is, when received, an installable felt template. The body of the coachee can register this listening if the coachee's attention is pointed at the right place. Most coachees are pointing their attention at their own performance, or at the coach's evaluation, or at the conversation's outcome. They are not pointing at the listening itself. The redirection — "focus on my listening to you" — re-aims the attention.

Once the body has the felt template, the same template can be applied internally. The coachee discovers that the listening they are receiving and the listening they would give to themselves have the same shape. The discovery is not intellectual. It is recognition in the body.

This is closely related to [[listening-shapes-the-reality-that-comes-back]] — the listening of the coach is the data the coachee receives. When the coachee starts receiving it, the coachee's experience of the conversation changes. The shift to receivability is itself the work.

## What The Coachee Notices

- A quality of expansiveness in the chest or whole body.
- A slowing-down of the internal pace.
- An increase in awareness of detail.
- An absence of outcome-orientation. "There's not like an outcome in mind. It's just like you pay attention to the details more."
- The same somatic signature they get when they manage to listen to themselves in good moments.
- Sometimes: a startle that this is what listening feels like and they have been seeking it from somewhere other than where it lives.

When the parallel is named, the recognition is usually immediate. The coachee can name the link without prompting. "It's the same thing." That naming is the install.

## When To Use It

- When the coachee is struggling to find self-listening as a felt experience despite multiple framings.
- After the [[listen-to-yourself-ten-percent-more]] instruction has been offered and not landed.
- When the coachee is in the conversation but cannot get out of the meta-position to register what is happening in the room.
- When the coachee has named "I don't really know what listening to myself even means."
- When the felt-quality of being-listened-to is needed as a reference for some downstream work.

## What To Watch For

The move can fail if the coachee deflects the listening rather than receives it. See [[letting-it-in-vs-deflecting-the-welcome]] — some coachees, when asked to focus on the listening being offered to them, immediately bounce it off. The flash of "no this isn't really happening" or "no I shouldn't take that in" is the signature. When you notice the deflection, name it gently and offer the receiving again. Sometimes the work has to slow down to the speed of letting one moment of listening actually land before the move can do what it does.

The move can also fail if the coach is not actually listening at the moment of the redirect. The coach's own listening has to be present for the coachee to receive it. If the coach is in their head about the next intervention, or planning the session arc, the coachee will land on absence rather than on listening. The move requires the coach to be in the move's content — listening — while doing the move. See [[speak-from-listening]] for the broader form of this.

## Why This Is Not Manipulation

The move is not "perform listening so the coachee will think they're being listened to." That fails — the body registers performance and offers nothing back to receive. The move presupposes that the coach is actually listening. The instruction is simply to point the coachee at what is already true.

In any session where the coach is in the appropriate state, the listening is already happening. The coachee has often been ignoring it for the entire session, distracted by their own production or evaluation. The move just makes visible what was already in the room.

## Tone

Curious and matter-of-fact. The move is offered with a small framing of "I know this is a weird one" because the redirect is unusual — most coachees expect to keep working on themselves, not to be asked to feel the coach. The casualness of the framing keeps the move from feeling like a confrontation. Once the coachee makes the shift, the tone becomes quieter; the moment needs space to land, and over-narrating it interrupts the install.
