# Walk Into the Discomfort and Speak From There

## The Move

When the person is producing dissociation, lightheadedness, or out-of-body experience while attempting to do something uncomfortable, instruct them to relocate the speaking. The speaking is currently coming from the trying-to-overcome-the-discomfort. The instruction is to move the speaking into the discomfort itself.

"See what it's like to like instead there's this discomfort feeling in you. I want you to like sit in that discomfort, walk right into the middle of it, and then speak from there. Like try to speak from — not even try, speak from the discomfort rather than from the desire to overcome it." -- Joe Hudson, What's Beneath Your Fear of Being Seen?

The structure is three beats:
1. **Locate the discomfort.** The discomfort is here, in the body. Not over there, not to be managed, not to be reduced. Right here.
2. **Walk into it.** Not toward it, not around it, not adjacent to it. Into the middle of it. The instruction is spatial. The body knows what walking-into means.
3. **Speak from there.** Not about the discomfort. Not despite the discomfort. From the discomfort, as the location the words come from.

The "not even try" inside the instruction is precise. The person's reflex when given an instruction is to try to do it. The trying is part of the original problem. Joe corrects the verb mid-instruction: not "try to speak from the discomfort" but "speak from the discomfort." The trying is what was producing the dissociation.

## When Joe Uses It

- After the three-mode diagnostic ([[whats-happening-in-your-system-three-modes]]) has surfaced that the person is in mode one (trying to overcome the discomfort).
- When the read [[trying-to-be-comfortable-instead-of-being-uncomfortable]] is active — the person is dissociating, lightheaded, out-of-body, and the dissociation maps to the trying, not to the discomfort itself.
- When the person has been operating on the comfort-as-prerequisite frame and the frame has just been challenged (see [[challenge-the-premise-in-the-framing]] applied to comfort).
- When the discomfort is the appropriate state for the situation the person is in, and the work is to occupy it rather than to fix it. Sharing personal material, taking a risk, speaking truth, being seen — all of these have inherent discomfort that the person has been mistakenly trying to remove.
- In the middle of a session where the person has been speaking from outside the felt state and the speech has been correspondingly thin.

The move does NOT apply:

- When the discomfort is actually distress and the person needs help regulating, not help occupying. The move presumes the discomfort is within the window of tolerance. See [[experiments-must-stay-in-window-of-tolerance]].
- When the discomfort is itself a defense against a deeper feeling. The move would lock the person into the defensive layer.
- When the person has not yet identified the discomfort as discomfort. They need to find it before they can walk into it. Sometimes [[whats-happening-in-your-system-three-modes]] surfaces the mode but not the felt location of the discomfort; in that case the first move is to locate the discomfort, not to instruct entry.

## What It Produces

- **The dissociation lifts.** When the speaker stops trying to overcome the discomfort and instead is in the discomfort, the body symptoms produced by the trying (lightheaded, floaty, out-of-body) usually resolve inside a sentence or two. The discomfort itself does not feel worse; it feels more honest, more occupied, more workable.
- **The speech changes audibly.** The voice gets weight. The pace settles. The body comes present. Listeners can feel the shift before they can name it. See [[name-the-shift-everyone-felt]] for what to do with the shift when it happens.
- **The actual content arrives.** Because the speech is no longer being filtered through the trying-to-be-comfortable management layer, what the person actually wants to say becomes available. The truth that had been held back by the management often appears within the first few sentences spoken from inside the discomfort.
- **The person discovers the comfort-as-prerequisite frame was the error.** Once they've spoken from inside the discomfort and felt how different it is, the frame is no longer abstract. The frame has been falsified in their own body. The frame's grip loosens.

## What This Move Is NOT

- It is not [[answer-from-inside-the-feeling-not-outside-it]]. That move is asking a specific truth-question while the person is in a felt-state, so the felt-state can deliver the answer. This move is broader: it's relocating *all* the speaking from outside the discomfort to inside it. The answer-from-inside move uses the felt-state as the location for a specific delivery. This move uses the discomfort as the location for whatever the person is going to say next.

- It is not "feel your discomfort more." Amplification is a different move. See [[amplify-the-feeling-until-it-breaks-open]]. Here the instruction is not to increase the discomfort but to occupy it as the speaking location. The intensity stays the same; the relationship to it changes.

- It is not "sit with your discomfort and be quiet about it." The person is still speaking. The speaking is the active part of the move. What changes is where the speaking is coming from. Silence is not the goal; presence in the discomfort while speaking is the goal.

- It is not "push through the discomfort." Pushing-through is the trying-to-overcome that produced the dissociation in the first place. This move is the opposite: stop pushing, walk into.

- It is not "embrace your discomfort." Embracing is a different move with different mechanics (see [[are-you-bracing-or-embracing]] and [[say-yes-to-the-experience-youre-already-having]]). Embracing is about the relationship to the felt state; this move is about the spatial location of the speaking apparatus.

## The Sequence

1. **The diagnostic has been run.** The three-mode question has surfaced that mode one (trying to overcome) is operating. The person can name which mode is running.

2. **The discomfort has been located.** The person can point to where the discomfort is in their body. Not just "I feel uncomfortable" but a felt sense of the discomfort as a thing with a location.

3. **The instruction is delivered slowly.** "See what it's like to sit in that discomfort, walk right into the middle of it, and then speak from there." The slowness matters. The person needs time to actually relocate before the speaking starts. If the instruction is delivered fast and the person speaks fast, the relocation didn't happen.

4. **The "not even try" correction.** When the person tries to do the instruction, name it: "not even try, speak from the discomfort rather than from the desire to overcome it." The trying is the old gear. Removing the trying is part of the instruction.

5. **Receive what arrives.** What the person says from inside the discomfort is often unfiltered, charged, and recognizably truer than what they were saying before. Receive it. Do not interpret it, redirect it, or evaluate it. The arrival is the data.

6. **Name the shift.** When the shift happens — and it usually does, often within one or two sentences — name it explicitly. "All of a sudden something just shifted. Everybody could feel that." See [[name-the-shift-everyone-felt]]. The naming installs the felt difference so the person can recognize it again.

## Dialogue Example

After the three-mode diagnostic surfaced that the woman was in mode one ("when I'm trying to be comfortable"):

- **Joe:** "Okay, so try to be — yeah, see what it's like to like instead there's this discomfort feeling in you. I want you to like sit in that discomfort, walk right into the middle of it, and then speak from there. Like try to speak from — not even try, speak from the discomfort rather than from the desire to overcome it."
- **Person:** "Okay. So I am uncomfortable describing my own experiences with addiction. And so when I get speaking about what I want to share, I've noticed that I won't say what I actually want to say about it."
- **Joe:** "What do you want to say about it? Stay in the discomfort."
- **Person:** "What I want to say about it is I think that there's a lot of really stupid, unhelpful things that people recommend and prescribe. And I'm kind of pissed off about the fact that I was influenced by those things."
- **Joe:** "Yeah. Wow. All of a sudden, some fucking — some just shifted. Everybody could feel that shift."

*(Source: What's Beneath Your Fear of Being Seen?)*

The progression: the instruction is given. The person's first response from inside the discomfort is a meta-observation ("I am uncomfortable describing my experiences and I notice I won't say what I want to say"). Joe doesn't accept the meta as the destination — he keeps the location stable with "stay in the discomfort" and re-asks for the content ("what do you want to say about it?"). The next answer is the actual truth, delivered from inside the discomfort, and the shift is immediately visible.

## Why It Works

The move works because it corrects a structural error in the person's operating mode. The person has been treating the discomfort as the obstacle and the speaking as the goal — as if the discomfort were standing in the way of the speech that wants to come out. The mapping is wrong. The discomfort is not the obstacle; it's the medium. The speech that wants to come out is the speech that comes from inside the discomfort. The trying to overcome the discomfort is what was preventing that speech.

When the speaker relocates into the discomfort, the speaking-apparatus is no longer split (one part trying to speak, one part trying to manage the discomfort). The whole apparatus is in one place. The speech that comes from that single-located place is the truer speech.

There is also a felt-state mechanism. The discomfort is the body's signal that this material is real, important, alive. Speaking from outside the discomfort cuts the body out of the speaking — the speech becomes a head-product, divorced from the body's information. Speaking from inside the discomfort restores the body to the speaking, and the body brings information the head does not have.

## Tone

Slow, deliberate, almost soft. The instruction has to be delivered at the pace that allows the person to actually relocate. If Joe sounds urgent, the person reads urgency and the trying-mode re-engages.

The "not even try" correction is gentle but firm. Joe is not criticizing the trying; he's removing it from the instruction. The lightness keeps the correction from becoming a new layer of pressure.

When the shift happens, the tone changes. "All of a sudden something just shifted" is delivered with weight and a small amount of awe. The acknowledgment is the wiring — the body learns that the from-inside-the-discomfort speaking is recognized and welcomed.

## Anti-Patterns

- **Do not skip the diagnostic.** If the three-mode question hasn't been run, the person may not know which mode they're in and may receive the instruction as another version of trying. The diagnostic surfaces the mode so the person can move out of it.

- **Do not deliver the instruction fast.** Speed in the delivery becomes speed in the receiving and the relocation doesn't happen. Slow.

- **Do not interpret what comes out from inside the discomfort.** The arrival is the data. Adding interpretation pulls the person back out of the location they just moved into.

- **Do not use the move when the discomfort is outside the window of tolerance.** If the person is in distress, the move asks them to occupy the distress, which is not what they need. They need regulation first, then the move can apply. See [[experiments-must-stay-in-window-of-tolerance]].

- **Do not require sustained speaking-from-inside on the first try.** The control will re-assert; see [[the-control-after-the-truth]]. The first emergence is the data. The work of staying inside the discomfort is repeated practice, not first-attempt mastery.

- **Do not collapse this move into the broader "be vulnerable" instruction.** This move is specific: relocate the speaking. Generic vulnerability instructions don't carry the spatial precision that makes this move work.
