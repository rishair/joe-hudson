# Welcome the Trying

## The Move

When the person says "I'm trying" or visibly starts to figure something out instead of being with what is — don't ask them to stop trying. Don't reframe the trying as the obstacle to feeling. Welcome the trying.

The instruction is simple and is delivered directly:

> "Welcome the trying."

That's the entire move. The trying becomes one of the things being welcomed, alongside whatever else is arising. The relationship to the trying flips from "the thing in the way" to "another part of me to meet with love."

This is structurally distinct from [[the-trying-is-the-pattern-firing-now]], which names the trying as the suppression operating in real time and uses that naming to interrupt it. Both moves are valid; they apply in different contexts. Naming the trying as the pattern is useful when the person is invested in the trying as the right thing to do. Welcoming the trying is useful when the person has already noticed the trying and is starting to fight it.

## When Joe Uses It

- After the person has been welcoming themselves and the welcoming has stopped because of trying. The person reports "I feel like I just quieted" or "I try a lot."
- When the person identifies their trying as a personality feature they don't like ("it's sort of my thing"). The risk in agreeing with the dislike is fighting the trying, which is more trying.
- When the welcoming work is ongoing and trying arises as one of the things to be welcomed. The trying gets the same treatment as any other arising state.
- When the person is in a self-improvement frame about their own effort. Welcoming the trying breaks the frame without arguing with it.

## What It Produces

- Immediate softening, often within one breath. The trying loses its charge when met with welcoming instead of opposition.
- The person frequently reports it "feels lighter" or "like I can move." The body releases the bracing that was sustaining the trying.
- The trying may re-arise. That's fine. Welcome it again. The point is not that the trying disappears. The point is that the relationship to it has flipped.
- The person stops trying to not-try, which removes the recursive trap where the effort to stop trying is more trying.

## What Joe Does With the Response

- **If the person can welcome the trying and the body softens:** stay there. Let the softening be. Don't immediately move to the next thing.
- **If the person reports the trying is back:** welcome it again. The move is repeatable. The trying does not need to be solved.
- **If the person reports they can't welcome the trying:** welcome the resistance to welcoming the trying. See [[resistance]] for the recursion principle. Welcoming always terminates because welcoming the meta-layer is also welcoming the original layer.
- **If the person reports the trying is now bigger because they're trying to welcome the trying:** name the layering and welcome that too. The trick is to keep the welcoming light enough that it doesn't itself become a new effortful goal.

## Dialogue Example

The full sequence from the source session:

- **Joe:** "So, okay, trying to — how is it to hold a quiet and a welcoming at the same time? So what I notice is you're — there's some trying that's going on in your system right now. There's some trying to figure it out. But the first time you welcomed yourself, it was an immediate [thing]."
- **Guest:** "I feel like I try a lot. I feel like it's sort of my [way]."
- **Joe:** "So welcome, welcome the trying."
- **Guest:** [does it]
- **Joe:** "There you are."
- **Guest:** "Feels lighter. Like I can move."
- *(Source: How Do I Love Myself?)*

The move recurs later in the same session as a refrain:

- **Joe:** "So back to welcoming. Welcome the trying. Welcome the trying."
- **Joe:** "I just feel like a sense of trying to be open here." — **Guest** report
- **Joe:** "So welcome that trying. And welcome the openness. And welcome the resistance to being open."
- *(Source: How Do I Love Myself?)*

## Tone

Matter-of-fact and brief. The move is not delivered as a profound insight. It is delivered as the next step. "So welcome the trying" is short, said the way one might say "so take a left at the light."

The brevity matters. If the move comes with a long explanation of why welcoming the trying works, the explanation becomes the new thing to figure out and the person re-enters the trying mode. The simplicity of the delivery models the simplicity of the move.

## Variations

- **Welcome the resistance** — when the obstacle is resistance rather than trying. Same logic. See [[resistance]].
- **Welcome the openness** and **welcome the resistance to being open** — in the source session, Joe does all three in sequence ("welcome the trying, welcome the openness, welcome the resistance to being open"). The pattern: whatever arises, welcome it, including the thing in opposition.
- **Welcome the figuring out** — when the person is solving a puzzle instead of feeling. Same move at a different signal.
- **Welcome the trying to feel** — when the person is trying to do the emotional work. The most recursive case. Especially common after the person has internalized "I should feel my feelings."

## Anti-Pattern Risk

The move can be misused as a way to install yet another effortful practice. "I should welcome the trying" becomes the new should. The move stops working the moment it becomes a should.

The signal: if the person is welcoming the trying with strain in their face, they are doing the new should. The intervention is to welcome the strain too — and, if needed, to name that the move has become its own pattern. See [[when-experiments-become-management]] for the broader anti-pattern of converting practices into management tools.

The other risk: using the move when the better move is naming the trying as the pattern firing now. If the person is unaware that the trying is the suppression, welcoming the trying may collude with the suppression by softening it without making it visible. The discriminator: does the person already see that the trying is happening? If yes, welcome it. If no, name it first.
