# Love the Worst-Feeling Part the Way You Wanted Love From Your Parents

## The Move

A four-step sequence that operationalizes [[loving-all-parts]] for a person who does not know how to love themselves but does know how they wanted to be loved as a child. The move uses the wanted-parental-love as the felt template for the self-love instruction.

The sequence:

1. **Locate the worst-feeling part of the body.** Not the whole body. The specific part. Bypass the mindfulness-style scan-the-whole-body instruction and ask for the part that feels the worst right now.
2. **Hang out with that part.** Put attention on it without doing anything for a moment. Let the person make contact before the loving instruction arrives.
3. **Ask what they wanted from their mom and dad.** Not in general — specifically. What did they want from their parents in this kind of moment? The answer is usually one word: love. Sometimes: patience, presence, to be held, to be told it would be okay.
4. **Direct that love at the part.** "I want you to love that thing. Go back to it, pay attention to it, and I want you to love it exactly the way that you want love from your mom and dad. All the patience. Not needing it to change."

The crucial qualifier is "not needing it to change." The parental love the person wanted was love without an agenda. The self-love being directed at the part has to carry the same quality. If the loving has the intent of dissolving the part, it is not loving; it is technique. See [[loving-without-trying-to-change]].

## When Joe Uses It

- When a person is overwhelmed by bodily sensations and the existing tools (mindfulness, awareness, breathwork) have failed to relieve them. The mindfulness was awareness without loving. The move supplies the missing loving.
- When a person presents [[im-depressed|depression]] and reports trying to "feel everything" without relief. The feeling-everything was un-loved feeling. The move directs love at the worst-feeling part specifically.
- When a person says "I don't know how to love myself" but has reference points for how they wanted to be loved as a child.
- When a person reports years of unsuccessfully trying to fix or improve themselves. The fixing was the absence of loving. The move installs the loving.
- After [[have-them-say-i-want-all-of-me-loved]] has surfaced what the body retreats from — the part the body refused becomes the worst-feeling part to direct the loving at.

## Why It Works

Three mechanisms layered:

1. **Specific location beats generalized scan.** Telling someone overwhelmed by sensations to "feel everything" or "scan your body" is asking for more of what is already overwhelming. Asking for the single worst part makes the focus tractable. The body knows where the worst is and can point to it within seconds.

2. **The wanted-parental-love is a felt template the body already has.** Every person carries a felt picture of how they wanted to be loved as a child. The picture contains all the qualities a self-love instruction is trying to gesture at — patience, presence, not needing the child to change, staying when the child is in pain. Pointing the body at "love it the way you wanted love from your parents" supplies the precision the abstract "love it" was missing. The body has somewhere to go.

3. **Loving the body part bypasses the conceptual self.** The instruction is not "love yourself" (which the conceptual self can resist as ridiculous or impossible). The instruction is "love this part of your body that feels bad." The conceptual self can step aside while the loving happens at a more local level. The local loving then aggregates upward into the larger felt-state shift.

## Dialogue Example

The canonical demonstration of the full sequence:

- **Person (18 years old, overwhelmed by bodily sensations, depressed):** "I keep getting overwhelmed by bodily sensations. They're extremely uncomfortable and they make it very difficult to rest. Every single time I try to feel my feelings the same way right now, I'm trying to feel everything. I'm trying to feel my hands. I'm trying to feel this whole experience, but it feels so distressing and overwhelming. I've been using mindfulness."
- **Joe:** "So I don't want you to feel your whole body. I just want you to look into your body to the part of yourself that feels the worst. Not the story, but the visceral sensation that feels the worst in your body right now."
- **Person:** "I don't know how to do that. I've been listening to all your podcasts and I can't find out how to do that."
- **Joe:** "Well, you just told me you don't feel good, so there must be some way that you notice that."
- **Person:** "I can just feel it. It's just there. It's like this area." *(gestures)*
- **Joe:** "Great. So here's what I want you to do. I want you to go and hang out with that part. Just spend time with it for a minute."
- *(Person sits with it; reports shaking.)*
- **Joe:** "What do you want from, say, your mom and your dad right now? What would you want? Not just generally, what do you want from your mom and dad?"
- **Person:** "I would say love."
- **Joe:** "Great. Okay, so I want you to love that thing. Go back to it, pay attention to it, and I want you to love it exactly the way that you want love from your mom and dad. All the patience. Not needing it to change. Don't forget to breathe."
- *(Source: This is How To Love Yourself)*

The whole sequence took under two minutes. The person reports an almost immediate shift: a moment later, they say "this is what I've been looking for. So good to love myself unconditionally. It really feels what I've been looking for my whole life."

## What Joe Does With the Response

- **If the person reports they don't know how to do it:** treat the not-knowing as the next thing to refuse. Use [[how-question-as-defense-against-having]]. The body already knows; the not-knowing is the head reinstalling the search. "Well, you just told me you don't feel good, so there must be some way you notice that."
- **If the person locates the part but reports resistance to loving it:** name the resistance without making it a problem. "You don't need to resist it. It's not going to destroy you." The resistance is information — the body is showing the protection that has been around the part. Let the resistance be there alongside the loving.
- **If the person is suppressing the expression of the part (covering mouth, holding back tears):** name the suppression. Joe in the canonical session: "You're like covering your mouth. You're trying to like not have the full expression of the sadness." The release of suppression is part of the loving — the part is being told it is allowed to express itself.
- **If the person says "I don't know what love is" or "I don't know what love feels like":** redirect to the parental reference. "What did you want from your mom and dad?" The wanted-from-parents register is almost always more available than the abstract-love register.
- **If the body softens visibly and the person reports feeling good:** anchor the moment. Name what just happened. "What just happened is you hung out, you loved this aspect of yourself. Before, you weren't happy. Now you're happy." See [[name-the-gap-between-felt-story-and-lived-evidence]] for the broader version of this anchoring.
- **If after one round the person asks "how do I do this?":** refuse the how-question. "I'm not gonna buy that you don't know how to do it. I saw you do it." See [[how-question-as-defense-against-having]].

## What This Move Is Not

- It is not a one-time technique. The move is the entry into a sustained practice. The first round demonstrates the felt-shape; the practice is the repeated application over days and weeks.
- It is not a way to make a feeling go away. The loving is for its own sake. If the loving is being used to dissolve the worst-feeling part, the engine of [[loving-to-transform]] is running and the loving will not land.
- It is not the same as a body scan. A body scan asks for awareness across the whole body. This move asks for love at a specific location. The specificity is part of what makes it work.
- It is not "love yourself." That instruction is too abstract for many people. This is the operational form: love this part, the way you wanted love from your parents.
- It does not require having had loving parents. People who did not get the love they wanted know precisely what they wanted. The felt-picture is often clearer for them. The move uses the wanted-love, not the received-love.

## Tone

Direct, specific, warm. The instructions are concrete ("look into your body," "spend time with it for a minute," "what do you want from your mom and dad"). Joe does not soften them with qualifications. The directness is part of the medicine — the person has been asking for help and the move is the help, delivered without preamble.

The "don't forget to breathe" carried at the end of the loving instruction is functional. The person concentrating on loving a body part will often hold their breath. The reminder to breathe lets the body stay open while the loving happens.

The tone shifts to lighter when the loving lands and the felt-shift arrives. "Fuck your neighbors" (in the canonical session, when the person says they are holding back the expression because the neighbors might hear) is delivered playfully — see [[permission-the-loud-expression-fuck-the-constraint]] for the full move. The lightness signals that the breakthrough is allowed and not an emergency. The body can stay open and the loving can continue.

## What Joe's Commentary Adds About Why It Lands

In the breakdown of the canonical session, Joe explicitly identifies what allowed the move to land so quickly for the 18-year-old. The key factor: the client did not bypass.

"He's not doing the thing that a lot of people do when they're meditating, which is to bypass. So he actually said, oh, to do the thing that Eckhart Tolle said, and then he actually sits with it, and there's a little bit of a bypass, and then I'm like, oh, what is it to love it? And all of a sudden, whatever that bypass is, is gone, which means that it's not like a deeply set-in pattern in his system. And it tells you how emotionally and energetically sensitive he is." — Joe Hudson, *"This Is How You Love Yourself" Coaching Session Breakdown*

The implication for using this move: the move lands fast when the person's system has not hardened into a meditation-as-bypass pattern. If the person has been doing years of awareness practice without loving, the awareness has become its own avoidance, and the move has to first interrupt the bypass before the loving can land. The first sign that the move is working: the person briefly bypasses, the coach redirects ("what is it to love it?"), and the bypass dissolves rather than reasserting itself.

Joe also notes the kid was "still holding something back" even after the initial round of loving. The commentary surfaces the move's natural follow-up: when the loving has begun but the person is still partially holding, the next move is to name the resistance and permission its release. In this session that took the form of "I notice you're resisting this thing. You don't need to resist it. It's not going to destroy you" followed by the [[permission-the-loud-expression-fuck-the-constraint|fuck-your-neighbors]] permissioning when the holding-back was traced to a small external constraint.

The structural point: the loving-the-part move is rarely a single delivery that completes. It is usually a delivery, an initial felt-shift, a noticing of what is still held back, a permissioning of the held-back element, and then a fuller landing. Each step can be the move's natural extension.
