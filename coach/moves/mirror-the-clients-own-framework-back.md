# Mirror the Client's Own Framework Back at Their Self-Rejection

## The Move

The client has a framework -- religious, philosophical, ethical, professional -- that they consciously hold and that contains a principle. The principle, when applied to other people, says one thing (everyone deserves love; everyone is whole; everyone is doing their best). When applied to the client themselves, the same principle either is not applied or is applied in the opposite direction.

The move is to notice the contradiction and mirror it back. Not to argue with the framework. Not to introduce a different framework. Just to point at the place where the client's own framework excludes the client.

The diagnostic line in the source session, after the client said she believed in a God who loves people as they are:

"Everybody but you." -- Joe Hudson, I've Done The Work. Why Am I Still Single

Three words. The whole move.

The full exchange:

- **Client:** My theological worldview... is that I don't have to fix people. I don't have to make people whole. I believe in a God who steps in—
- **Joe:** Everybody but you.
- **Client:** To do that. What do you mean everybody but me?
- **Joe:** Well, you're not good enough to be loved. You need to be more.

## Why This Works

The client's framework is something they already trust. They have organized their life around it. They have done years of work inside it. The framework is not a fragile new idea that can be defended against. The framework is the most stable structure in the client's interior.

When the coach introduces a different framework ("you are enough as you are"), the client's existing framework treats the introduction as foreign and either rejects it or domesticates it. The work bounces off.

When the coach mirrors the client's own framework back, the client cannot reject the framework. The framework is theirs. The contradiction the coach is naming is a contradiction within what the client already believes. The work has nowhere to bounce.

The client's body recognizes the contradiction before the conscious mind does. The recognition is the workable moment.

## How to Use the Move

The move requires the coach to actually understand the client's framework well enough to mirror it accurately. A coach who has not listened carefully to what the client believes cannot do this move. The coach has to know what the framework says about other people in order to notice when the same framework is not being applied to the client.

The minimum the coach needs:

- What the framework says about how love works
- What the framework says about who is worthy of love
- What the framework says about what is required to be loved
- What the framework says about the relationship between effort and worth

Once the coach has this, the move is the simple act of holding the framework up to the client like a mirror and pointing at the place where the client is not in the mirror.

## Variations

**The everybody-but-you variant.** Used in the source. The client describes a universal principle ("God loves everyone as they are") and the coach notes the implicit exclusion ("everybody but you"). The form is a short verbal pivot.

**The how-does-that-work variant.** When the contradiction is between two principles in the same framework: "So God's able to love you, but the person you want isn't able to love you. How does that work?" -- Joe Hudson, I've Done The Work. Why Am I Still Single

The form is a direct question that holds the two principles side by side and asks the client to reconcile them. The reconciliation is impossible without revealing the gap.

**The God's-not-as-smart-as variant.** When the client's framework implies an authority that knows better than the client about love, and the client is overriding that authority with their own judgment: "So God is not as smart as the menu date?" -- Joe Hudson, I've Done The Work. Why Am I Still Single

The form is provocative and slightly humorous. It exposes the absurdity of the client trusting their own gating criterion over the framework's authority on the same question.

**The you're-trying-to-replicate-it variant.** When the client is doing in their relationships what they believe their framework already does, while denying that they need to do it: "How much are you trying to replicate God's love to love the [person]?" -- Joe Hudson, I've Done The Work. Why Am I Still Single

The form names that the client is unconsciously taking on the framework's role themselves -- attempting to be the source of unconditional love for another person, which their framework says is not their job. The contradiction is between the framework's stated theology and the client's lived behavior.

## What This Move Is Not

It is not an attack on the framework. The framework is honored. The framework is what makes the move work. A coach who dismisses the framework cannot do this move.

It is not the coach claiming to know the framework better than the client. The coach is reflecting what the client has already said. The framework's content comes from the client. The coach is only noticing where the client has not applied it consistently.

It is not a debate. The coach does not argue that the client should believe what their framework says. The coach simply notices that the framework, as the client describes it, does not exclude the client -- and the client has been treating themselves as if it did.

It is not a generic "be kind to yourself" reframe. The generic reframe is something the coach is adding. The mirror move uses what the client has already provided. The specificity of the move comes from the specificity of the framework the client brought in.

## When This Move Misfires

The move misfires when the coach mirrors a framework the client does not actually hold. If the client has named the framework as a belief they are inspecting but not yet trusting, the mirror does not have purchase. The framework has to be something the client actively organizes their life around.

The move misfires when the coach uses it to score points. The "ah-ha, you're contradicting yourself" tone collapses the work. The tone has to be genuine curiosity about the gap, not triumph at having found it.

The move misfires when the contradiction the coach is naming is not actually present in the framework. If the client's framework legitimately does say "I am exempt from the universal principle," the move has no traction. The coach has to be reading the framework accurately.

## Joe's Words

"Everybody but you." -- Joe Hudson, I've Done The Work. Why Am I Still Single

"So God's able to love you, but the person you want isn't able to love you. How does that work?" -- Joe Hudson, I've Done The Work. Why Am I Still Single

"Ah, but wait, I'm confused because that's how you're trying to love these people to make them whole." -- Joe Hudson, I've Done The Work. Why Am I Still Single

"In the -- back to God for a second -- that idea is that God loves you as you are doesn't mean that God puts up with it." -- Joe Hudson, I've Done The Work. Why Am I Still Single

The "I'm confused" is a recurring frame Joe uses to deliver the mirror. The mock-confusion presumes the contradiction is the client's contradiction to resolve, not an error the coach is correcting. The framing shifts the responsibility for noticing the gap to the client.

## Tone

Curious, slightly puzzled. Joe sometimes uses "I'm confused" as a verbal frame to deliver the contradiction. The mock-confusion is not condescension. It is the genuine puzzlement of someone trying to hold two pieces of what the client has said and finding that they do not fit together.

The directness lands as care, not as attack, because the framework is honored throughout. The client experiences the move as "Joe is taking what I actually believe more seriously than I have been," not as "Joe is trying to make me look foolish."

## Upstream (What Leads Here)

- When the client has articulated a worldview, framework, or set of principles that contains universal claims about love, worth, wholeness, or capacity
- When the client is presenting self-rejection that violates the universal claim
- When [[whatever-you-think-you-need-to-be-lovable-is-self-rejection]] has been named and the next move is to surface the specific way the self-rejection contradicts what the client believes about others
- When the client has invoked their framework as authority and the framework's actual content has not been applied to them
- When the client is trying to think their way out of the self-rejection and a structural move is needed that the thinking cannot bypass
