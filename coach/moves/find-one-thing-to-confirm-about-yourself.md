# Find One Thing to Confirm About Yourself

## The Move

After the [[every-question-becomes-whats-wrong-with-me|deficiency lens]] has been named, the immediate next move: ask the person to produce one thing about themselves that they can confirm. The smallest possible request — not a list, not a self-esteem inventory, just one. The smallness is part of the move.

"So can you find one thing to confirm about yourself? One thing that you can say is pretty fucking awesome about you?" -- Joe Hudson, You Don't Have A Money Problem

The "pretty fucking awesome" framing matters. It is asking for confirmation, not for an honest assessment. The deficiency-lens will produce balanced assessments forever; what it has not been producing is unqualified positive verdicts. The request specifies that the answer must be unqualified positive, which is the exact thing the lens has been blocking.

## When Joe Uses This

- Immediately after the [[every-question-becomes-whats-wrong-with-me|not-good-enough lens]] has been named and recognized
- When the person has just said "I can't confirm myself" or its equivalent
- After [[youll-never-get-the-confirmation-from-others]] has been delivered and the work has shifted to internal confirmation
- When the client is in the [[how-do-i-stop-seeking-approval|approval-seeking concern]] and the immediate need is to demonstrate the existence of an internal source

The move is positioned right after the diagnostic and right before any deeper work. It is the first proof-of-concept that the internal source exists. The person who has been outsourcing the okayness-verdict for years often does not believe an internal source is even available. Producing one piece of self-confirmation, out loud, is the live evidence that it is.

## What It Produces

The first response is often a long pause. The person scans for something to say and the lens immediately disqualifies each candidate. The pause itself is the data — it makes visible how thoroughly the lens has been blocking self-confirmation. The pause is also the territory where the work happens. The coach holds the silence and waits.

When the person does produce something, it usually arrives with a specific shape: the positive statement, followed by an immediate qualification that converts it back to deficiency. The client in the canonical session said "I moved to Switzerland with no money and got a job" and then, before Joe could respond, said "Oh, there's a lot of stress involved." The positive content was generated; the lens immediately attached the deficiency-rider.

The move sets up the next move: [[catch-the-tiny-self-doubt-inside-the-answer]]. The coach names the live appearance of the lens in the qualification. The person sees that they could not let a single piece of self-confirmation stand on its own. The lens is visible in real time, in their own mouth, in the seconds after the move was requested.

The combination — request the confirmation, catch the qualification — is the live demonstration that allows the lens to become visible as a current operation rather than as an abstract pattern.

## What Joe Does With the Response

The response is rarely a clean self-confirmation. The move surfaces the structure underneath, which is what it is for.

- **If the person produces a confirmation with a qualification:** Joe catches the qualification (see [[catch-the-tiny-self-doubt-inside-the-answer]]). The catch is the next move.
- **If the person cannot produce anything:** the pause itself becomes the work. "Notice that you cannot produce one thing." The inability is the diagnostic. Joe stays with the person in the inability rather than supplying the thing for them. Supplying it from outside is the configuration the person has been operating in for years; what is needed is for the source to be inside.
- **If the person produces a clean confirmation that stays clean:** rare, but when it happens, Joe lets the person feel it land. The body is being given a permission it has not had. The landing is the work.
- **If the person produces an obviously deflected confirmation ("I guess I'm a decent person"):** Joe asks for it again with more specificity. "Can you give me one that's actually fucking awesome?" The deflection is itself the lens; specificity refuses it.

## Why "One" Matters

The move asks for one thing, not a list. The size of the request is operational. A list invites the lens to balance — to produce three positives and then three negatives, which feels like an accurate assessment and changes nothing. One thing cannot be balanced; it has to stand on its own.

One is also the size that exposes the lens's totality. If the person genuinely cannot produce one — out of all the things they have done in their life — the totality of the lens becomes undeniable. The lens has been disqualifying everything. The undeniability is the doorway to seeing that the lens itself is the problem, not the available content.

## Why "Pretty Fucking Awesome" Matters

The colloquial registration is doing work. "Awesome" is a stronger word than "good" or "okay." It explicitly requires a positive verdict that the lens has been refusing for years. The "fucking" intensifies the request and makes the move slightly playful — the playfulness is part of how the move bypasses the lens's standard defenses.

A polite request ("can you name something positive about yourself?") gets a polite answer ("I'm pretty conscientious"). The polite answer leaves the lens intact. The strong request ("pretty fucking awesome") either gets answered with something strong (which is the work) or gets refused (which is also the work, because the refusal is visible as a refusal).

The strong request also models for the person what self-confirmation actually sounds like. They have been delivering confirmation to others in this register — they tell their friends their friends are "amazing" without hedging. The move asks them to direct that same register at themselves. The asymmetry between how they speak about others and how they speak about themselves becomes visible.

## Why This Is Not Affirmation Practice

The move looks superficially like affirmation work but operates differently. Affirmation practices ask the person to repeat positive statements about themselves until the body believes them. The mechanism is repetition.

This move is single-shot. It asks for one thing, once, with the coach present. The mechanism is not repetition. The mechanism is the live surfacing of the lens that has been preventing self-confirmation from being possible. The work is not in the confirmation itself; it is in seeing what the lens did with the request to make even one piece of confirmation a struggle.

The downstream work — the practice of building the internal source over time — does involve repetition. But this move is the entry point, not the practice. It is the diagnostic that proves the internal source exists and shows what has been preventing it from being used.

See [[say-ouch-to-the-inner-critic]] for the related diagnostic move that surfaces the criticism the lens has been producing. The two moves work the same territory from opposite angles: the ouch move surfaces what the lens has been saying; the find-one-thing move surfaces what the lens has been blocking.

## Tone

Direct, slightly insistent, warm. The "pretty fucking awesome" framing carries playfulness — Joe is not delivering this as a clinical instruction. The warmth is in the implicit message that the person does have such things to confirm, even if they cannot currently see them. The directness is in refusing the disqualifications the lens will produce.

If the person cannot produce anything and the silence stretches, the tone stays warm. The inability is not a failure to be solved by pressure. It is the visible signature of the lens, which Joe is content to let the person see. The coach stays present, not pushing, not rescuing.

When the qualification arrives ("Oh, there's a lot of stress involved"), the tone sharpens. Joe interrupts: "Wait, see, even that — hold on a second — even that." The sharpening is required to name the lens in the act of running. A softer interruption would let the qualification stand.

## Anti-Patterns

- Do not produce the thing for the person. "Well, you're clearly intelligent, you're here doing the work, you have a successful business" — these are external confirmations supplied by the coach. The configuration the person has been running for years is "I need the confirmation from someone else." Supplying it perpetuates the configuration. The move requires the source to be internal.
- Do not soften the request. "Can you find anything good about yourself?" lets the person give a balanced assessment. The strong-positive form is what surfaces the structural block.
- Do not accept the deflection as the answer. "I guess I'm a decent person" is the lens producing a hedged answer that satisfies neither side. Ask again, with more specificity.
- Do not move on once a confirmation has been produced with a qualification. The qualification is the work. Catching it (see [[catch-the-tiny-self-doubt-inside-the-answer]]) is the next move. Letting the qualification stand teaches the body that confirmation always comes with deficiency-riders.
- Do not turn this into a homework assignment ("write down 10 things you confirm about yourself every morning"). The move is single-shot, live, in-session. The homework version is affirmation practice, which operates differently and does different work. This move is the diagnostic; affirmation practice is one of many follow-on practices.
