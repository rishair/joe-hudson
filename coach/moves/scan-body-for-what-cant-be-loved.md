# Scan the Body for the Part That Can't Be Loved

## The Move

Invite the person, while their body is grounded and they have just said or heard something welcoming (often "I want all of me loved"), to scan their body for the part of them that cannot be loved.

The framing matters. The instruction is not "scan for what's wrong with you." It is not "find the bad part." It is specifically "scan for what part of you can't be loved." The framing puts the question in the body, in the present tense, and locates the obstacle as the body's own refusal rather than as an objective flaw.

Specific phrasings:
- "Scan your body and see what part of you can't be loved in your mind, in your heart"
- "What is there? Any part of you?"
- "Where in your body is the part you're scared can't be loved?"

The scan typically takes 5–15 seconds. The body produces an answer fast, often before the person has time to construct a verbal response. The answer is usually a retreat, a wince, an image, or a sudden thought ("It was a doubt").

## When Joe Uses It

- After [[have-them-say-i-want-all-of-me-loved]] — the scan gives the welcoming statement somewhere to land or refuse
- When the person has named a self-judgment in words but the actual hidden part has not yet surfaced
- When the work is about welcoming all parts and the person cannot yet welcome a specific one
- When the body needs to become the source of the answer, not the mind
- When the person has been intellectualizing the self-judgment and the work needs to drop down

## What It Produces

- A retreat. The body draws back from a specific image. See [[the-pulled-back-from-part-is-the-unloveable-part]].
- An image or naming. The person may say "I see [X]" or "I feel [Y]" — a specific part of themselves they can name as the unloveable one.
- A sudden thought arising ("It was a doubt"). The thought is often the part itself wearing a story.
- A pause or freeze. The body sometimes goes still rather than retreating. The stillness is itself the data.
- A specific body location for the refusal — chest, throat, gut. Useful information for where the wiring lives.

## What Joe Does With the Response

- If a retreat happens: name it in real time and send the person back to what they retreated from. See [[name-the-body-defense-in-real-time]] and [[the-pulled-back-from-part-is-the-unloveable-part]].
- If the person names the part directly ("cunning," "wanting," "needy," "cold"): Joe reframes it without bypass. The reframe is usually that the part is universal among children, or universally human, or beautiful in its own right.
- If nothing arises: the person may not be grounded enough, or the welcoming statement was performed without contact. Joe re-grounds and re-states.
- If the person tries to explain why the part is unloveable: Joe interrupts and returns to the body. Explanation is the next layer of defense.

## Dialogue Examples

From "Am I A Fake?":

- **Joe:** "Take a second for — scan your body and see what part of you can't be loved in your, in your mind, in your heart. Like, what is there? Any part of you? Oh, look what just happened. That was amazing. Where did you retreat into? Where'd you go? It's like — hold on a second. You just pulled back."
- **Guest:** "It was a doubt. Yes."
- **Joe:** "Yeah, so when you pulled back, the thing you pulled away from — go to the thing you pulled away from. That's the part that you're scared can't be loved. That's the part you hide."
- *(Source: Am I A Fake?)*

The scan produces the retreat within seconds. The retreat is the data. Joe names it, and the work goes immediately to the hidden part.

## Tone

Quiet, slow, attentive. Joe is watching the body the entire time the person is scanning. He does not rush them. The seconds of the scan are working time — the body is sorting and producing the answer.

When the retreat is detected, Joe's tone shifts slightly to surprised pleasure: "Oh, look what just happened. That was amazing." The surprise is genuine; the pleasure is at the body producing such clear data so quickly.

## Anti-Pattern Risk

If used as a diagnostic checklist ("find the bad part so we can fix it"), the move backfires. The point is not to identify a defect. The point is to surface what the body has been protecting from love so that it can be met with love. If the AI coach treats the surfaced part as a problem to solve rather than a part to welcome, the move becomes [[fixing-people]] in a body-scan wrapper.

The other risk: doing this when the person is dissociated or freshly traumatized. The scan presumes a body that can produce signal. If the person cannot feel their body at all, the prerequisite work is body re-entry, not scanning for parts.
