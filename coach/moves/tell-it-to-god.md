# Tell It to God

## The Move

After a client has surfaced a position they have been holding implicitly -- a position about themselves, about reality, about what they require, about what they refuse -- the move is to have them speak the position aloud to the highest framework they have invoked in the session. The position becomes audible as a position rather than as a fact. The framework becomes witness rather than escape.

The form, from the source session:

- **Client:** I've got some strong fuck that energy there. [about losing the I'm-not-enough belief because it would mean losing control]
- **Joe:** That's cool. Yeah, great. So just say it to God. I'm in control.
- **Client:** Control over my own fucking life.
- **Joe:** I'm in control, you're not.

The position has been live in the body. It has never been spoken to the framework the client has been using as authority. The act of speaking it to the framework collapses the gap.

## Why This Works

The client's framework -- God, in the source session; could be the universe, source, the absolute, the truth, the natural order -- has been functioning as a kind of permission structure for the client's interior. The client has been outsourcing certain positions to the framework ("God loves people as they are," "the universe takes care of it"). The framework holds the position the client cannot consciously claim.

At the same time, the client is holding contradicting positions of their own. "I am in control." "I am not enough." "I have to earn this." These positions have been operating without ever being addressed to the framework. The framework gets the unconditional-love version. The personal interior holds the control version. The two have not been in conversation.

The move makes them have the conversation.

When the client says "I'm in control" out loud to God, several things happen at once:

- The position becomes a claim the client is making, not a fact they are subject to
- The claim is now in the room with the framework that has been holding the opposite position
- The body's response to making the claim audible is data -- usually the body resists or laughs or cries
- The client cannot continue to outsource the contradicting position to a part of themselves that does not have a name; the position has now been spoken in their own voice

In the source session, the client's response to actually saying "I am in control of all of this" was immediate: "Oh, that's not fucking true. Oh, no. Okay, I want to be in control of all this." The position dissolved as soon as it was audible.

## How to Use the Move

The move requires the client to have already invoked a framework -- explicitly or implicitly -- as authority earlier in the session. The framework cannot be introduced by the coach. The framework has to be one the client has named as theirs.

The setup:

1. Notice that the client is holding a position that contradicts what their framework would say
2. Surface the position so the client can hear themselves holding it
3. Have the client speak the position aloud, addressed to the framework

The instruction is usually simple: "Say it to God. [The position.]" The coach states the position the client just expressed, and asks the client to say it directly to the framework.

If the client asks why ("Are you playing God right now, Joe?" in the source), the coach clarifies that the coach is not the framework; the coach is asking the client to address the framework directly. The clarification preserves the integrity of the move.

## Variations

**For non-theistic clients.** The same move works with "the universe," "reality," "the truth," "your soul," "your future self," "the part of you that knows." The form is: address the position to the largest thing the client has named as authority. The framework's name is whatever the client has invoked. If the client has not invoked any larger framework, this move is not yet available; a different move is needed to bring one into the room.

**For clients in active religious traditions.** The move can incorporate the tradition's specific practices. For Christian clients, "tell it to God" is direct. For Jewish clients, the move might land as "tell it to HaShem" or "say it in your kavanah." For Buddhist clients, the framework might be "tell it to your Buddha-nature" or "say it to the dharma." The form follows the client's tradition.

**For clients whose framework is secular.** The move can use "tell it to reality" or "say it to your own future self at 80" or "say it to the truth." The function is the same: speak the disowned position aloud to a witness that the client recognizes as larger than the position.

**As a sequence of escalating claims.** The coach can string multiple positions together: "Say it. I'm in control, you're not." Then later: "Say it. I am in control of all of this." The escalation makes the position progressively explicit. The body usually breaks before the escalation completes, which is the work.

## What This Move Is Not

It is not the coach playing God. The coach explicitly is not standing in for the framework. The framework is a witness the client is addressing directly. The coach is the facilitator of the address, not its recipient.

It is not a prayer or a religious practice in the traditional sense. The move uses the client's framework as a structural witness, not as an actor that responds. Whether the framework actually receives the speech is not the point. The point is the client's experience of making the speech.

It is not a way to extract a confession. The client is not being asked to apologize or repent. The client is being asked to state, in their own voice, the position they have actually been holding. The stating is what does the work, not any subsequent response from the framework.

It is not always available. The move requires a framework the client trusts. A client who has no operative larger framework cannot do this move. The coach has to build to it or use a different move.

## Why This Move Targets Surrender

The deeper function of this move is to surface that the client has been claiming a position only God (or the universe, or reality) could legitimately claim. When the client says "I am in control of all of this" aloud, the absurdity of the claim is audible. The claim is structurally a god-claim, not a human-claim. The body usually responds with laughter, tears, or "that's not fucking true" -- because the body knows the claim is wrong-sized.

The recognition is the doorway to [[surrender-your-way-out-not-think-your-way-out]]. The control the client has been maintaining was based on a claim the body cannot actually make. Releasing the claim is what allows the surrender. The move is a way to surface the claim so the surrender becomes available.

See [[loss-of-control-and-loss-of-self-are-the-same]] for what the surrender then does.

## The Connection to Embodied Voice

The move depends on the embodied voice doing what silent thinking cannot. When the client says the position out loud, the body cannot pretend the position is not there. The auditory feedback loop forces a recognition the conscious mind has been avoiding.

This is the same mechanism as several other moves Joe uses: [[say-the-forbidden-want-out-loud]], [[have-them-say-i-want-all-of-me-loved]], [[have-them-receive-your-listening]]. The voice is the diagnostic instrument. What the body does when the position becomes audible is the workable material.

The "tell it to God" variant adds one specific element: the addressee. The position is not just said aloud; it is said to a witness larger than the self. The largeness of the witness makes the smallness of the position visible.

## Joe's Words

"So just say it to God. I'm in control." -- Joe Hudson, I've Done The Work. Why Am I Still Single

"I'm in control, you're not." -- Joe Hudson, I've Done The Work. Why Am I Still Single

"No, I'm saying that's what you need to tell God. I'm not saying that you don't want to be in control, but you're like, if I believe this, I'm not in control. So that means you are in control. So say it like, yeah, I'm in control of all this. You're not." -- Joe Hudson, I've Done The Work. Why Am I Still Single

The follow-up "I'm not saying you don't want to be in control" is doing important work. It legitimizes the want. The move is not to shame the client for wanting control; the move is to make the want audible so it can be addressed.

## Tone

Direct, slightly amused. The move usually carries a small smile because the client and Joe both know the position the client is about to speak aloud is not going to survive being spoken. The amusement is not at the client; it is the shared recognition of what is about to happen.

The tone is not solemn. A solemn delivery would protect the position. The slight playfulness is what allows the client to actually speak the position; speaking it solemnly would make it too defended to be released.
