# Fully Feel the Forbidden Arousal (Without Fantasizing)

## The Move

A specific instruction for the body when an arousal, desire, or attraction arises that the person believes they cannot act on. The instruction has two parts and the second part matters as much as the first.

1. **Fully feel the arousal.** Do not cut it. Do not stop it. Do not push it away with "I am not allowed to feel this." Let the sensation be in the body completely.

2. **Do not fantasize.** Do not let the mind run forward into what would happen next. The fantasizing is a different operation that produces a different result. The instruction is to feel what is here, not to construct what is not.

When this is done, the arousal arises, runs its course, and dissipates. The person notices they are no longer fixated on it. The desire was not satisfied by being acted on, and it was not repressed by being cut. It was felt all the way through, and what it required — being felt — was given.

"You're just going to fully feel that sexual arousal in the moment. And then like, you'll notice you're not fixated on it anymore. It just like comes and it goes." -- Joe Hudson, Pleasure

## Why This Is Counterintuitive

The cultural training is the opposite. The training is: if you feel attracted to someone you cannot have, push the attraction away as quickly as possible. The push-away is treated as the responsible move. The push-away is also the move that generates the fixation. The forbidden-charge is created by the cutting.

"It's literally when we feel that arousal and we cut it that the fixation starts happening, like, oh no, I'm not allowed to. And then that's where we actually drive towards the thing that is destructive for us." -- Joe Hudson, Pleasure

The push-away is the avoidance that invites the thing it was supposed to prevent. See [[what-we-fear-we-invite]] for the broader principle. This move is the domain-specific application of that principle to arousal. The way to prevent the destructive behavior is not to suppress the arousal. It is to feel the arousal fully so that it does not need to be acted out.

## Why the Fantasy Distinction Matters

The move requires the precise distinction between feeling-the-sensation and fantasizing-about-the-outcome. They are different operations:

**Feeling the sensation:** Present-tense. In the body. The warmth, the activation, the pull, whatever the actual felt experience is. Has a beginning, middle, and end on its own.

**Fantasizing:** Constructed. In the mind. The imagined scenario, the imagined satisfaction, the imagined future. Does not have a natural endpoint because the mind can extend it indefinitely.

The fantasizing is itself a form of acting on the desire, just in the imagination rather than in the world. It feeds the chase. It keeps the fixation active. The feeling-the-sensation move does not feed the chase, because it is not directed at any outcome. It just lets the body have its experience.

"It's not a fantasize, so it's not you're fantasizing over what's going to happen next — that's a different thing. But you're just going to fully feel that sexual arousal in the moment." -- Joe Hudson, Pleasure

The discipline of staying with the sensation and not letting the mind take over with the scenario is what makes the move work. Without that discipline, the move turns into its opposite — a fantasy session disguised as a feeling practice.

## What This Move Addresses

The move addresses a specific fear that many people have about pleasure-practice: that if they allow themselves to fully feel pleasure, they will be driven to take destructive action on every desire that arises. Joe names this fear directly:

"I'm scared to allow myself to feel pleasure because then I am going to like cheat on my husband, then I am going to like buy the car that I want that it would be financially irresponsible for me to buy. I'm going to be this evil thing if I actually allow myself to feel pleasure." -- Joe Hudson, Pleasure

The move is the structural answer to this fear. Feeling-the-arousal is not the same as acting-on-the-arousal. The destructive behavior is generated by the suppression-then-rebellion cycle, not by the feeling itself. The person who fully feels the arousal in the moment is less likely to act destructively, not more, because the arousal has been honored at the level it was actually asking for.

"We do those things because we disallow them, because we're like, because they become kinky, because they become shameful, and we're addicted to that cycle." -- Joe Hudson, Pleasure

The kinky-because-disallowed pattern is the cycle the move interrupts. Feel it, don't act, watch it dissolve. The arousal stops needing to be acted on because it stops being suppressed.

## How to Use This

The framing in coaching:

1. **Name the configuration.** The person is reporting they cannot stop being fixated on a forbidden desire (an attraction, an item, a behavior they have committed not to do). The pattern is that the more they try not to want it, the more they fixate. Name this as the cutting-creates-the-fixation mechanism.

2. **Give the instruction in two parts.** First: when the desire arises, feel it fully in the body. Do not suppress, do not cut, do not push away. Second: do not let the mind run forward into the scenario. Stay with the sensation as sensation.

3. **Predict what will happen.** The arousal will come up, the body will feel it, and within a relatively short time it will dissipate. The person will notice they are no longer fixated. This prediction is part of the move because the person needs to know what to look for so they can verify the result.

4. **Address the predicted fear.** Most people will worry that fully feeling the desire will make them act on it. The reverse is true. The cutting-cycle is what produces the action. The feeling-cycle dissipates the action. Name this directly.

5. **Frame as repeatable.** This is not a one-time fix. The desire will arise again. Each time, the same move. Over time, the system learns that the desire does not have to be acted on, the fixation softens, and the underlying restriction (which was generating the chase) starts to loosen.

## What This Move Is Not

- **Not a license to act on every desire.** The move is about feeling the sensation, not following the impulse. The action is still chosen separately.
- **Not a suppression technique disguised as a feeling technique.** If the feeling is being done in order to make the desire go away faster, the feeling is itself the suppression. The configuration matters. The feeling has to be honest noticing, not strategic dissipation.
- **Not a single-use intervention.** The first time will produce a partial release. Repeated use is what produces the structural shift in the relationship to the desire.
- **Not appropriate for compulsive or addictive material that the person is actively destabilized by.** For someone in active addiction or active crisis around a behavior, the move requires additional scaffolding and is not the first intervention. See the appropriate context-of-use for [[feeling-the-unfelt-emotion]].

## Tone

Matter-of-fact. The move is taught as information about how the body works, not as a moral teaching. The example Joe uses is the everyday one: feeling sexual arousal for someone walking down the street who is not your partner. The mundane example makes the structural point without making the person feel they are being asked to undertake something exotic.

## Dialogue Example

Joe walks through the configuration in the transcript with Brett:

- **Joe:** "So you feel sexually aroused towards somebody who's not your partner and they're walking down the street, you feel sexually aroused for them. Most of us will just stop that sexual arousal. Oh no, I'm not allowed to feel that. And they'll stop it."
- **Joe (giving the instruction):** "You can just feel it. Yeah, look at that, I'm totally feeling it. And you'll just notice, like, everybody can try this. Fully feel it. Don't — it's not a fantasize, so it's not you're fantasizing over what's going to happen next, but you're just going to fully feel that sexual arousal in the moment. And then like, you'll notice you're not fixated on it anymore. It just like comes and it goes."
- **Brett (confirming):** "Yeah, we push that desire or that impulse down and then of course it's gonna rebel. And it's gonna try to squeak out another way."
- **Joe (closing):** "Exactly. Well, if I couldn't have my pleasure there, then I'm gonna do it later at night when I'm going to bed, and I'm tired and my dopamine is a little low, then I'll be attracted to porn or something."

The structure of the exchange is the structure of the move: configuration named, instruction given, prediction made, common alternative (rebellion into a different domain) acknowledged as what happens when the move is not used.
