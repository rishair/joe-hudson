# Apply VIEW to the Voice

## The Move

Direct the [[view]] state of mind at the [[voice-in-the-head]] itself. Treat your inner critic as a relationship to be developed, not a problem to solve. Bring vulnerability, impartiality, empathy, and wonder to the voice.

"You can bring view, you know, from the first podcast and from the course, you can bring that same methodology and point it towards the voice in your head, right?" -- Joe Hudson, Who is the Voice in Your Head?

The move converts VIEW from an outward-facing communication methodology into an inward-facing relational practice. Most of the time VIEW is described in the context of conversations with other people. This is the same VIEW applied to the most persistent conversation partner most people have: the voice in their head.

## The Four Components Applied Inward

**Vulnerability with the voice.** Tell the voice the truth about its impact. Not in argument, in honest report. "Ow, it really hurts when you tell me I should lose weight." The vulnerability is real. It is not a tactic. The voice has been hurting you. Saying so is vulnerable.

"Being vulnerable with it, like, 'Ow, it really hurts when you tell me I should lose weight.'" -- Joe Hudson, Who is the Voice in Your Head?

**Impartiality with the voice.** Drop the agenda of getting rid of it. Stop trying to make it different than it is. Approach it without trying to control the outcome. This is the antithesis of [[dont-fight-the-voice]].

"Being impartial with it, like, 'I'm not gonna try to get rid of you. Like, what's going on? What do you really have to say?'" -- Joe Hudson, Who is the Voice in Your Head?

**Empathy with the voice.** Feel into what it is feeling. The voice that sounds harsh is usually scared. The voice that controls is usually terrified. Be with that fear, not against the voice it produces.

"Being empathetic with it, like, 'How scared is the voice in the head to be like shouting at you like this? Like, what's it so afraid of?' and to feel that." -- Joe Hudson, Who is the Voice in Your Head?

**Wonder about the voice.** Bring genuine curiosity. What is the voice actually saying underneath the bossy phrasing? Where did this come from? What does it want? See [[wonder]].

The full sequence is a relational repair, the kind of repair anyone might do with a difficult person in their life — except this one lives inside.

## Why VIEW Works on the Voice

The voice was installed in childhood as a defense against external criticism. It has been attacked by you for years (or has been ignored, which the voice reads as the same thing). VIEW is the state of mind the voice has never been met with.

The voice softens in response to being heard. It hardens in response to attack or dismissal. This is the same pattern as any defended part of a person. The mechanism is not different just because it is inside.

See [[voice-as-care]] for the underlying reframe that makes VIEW available. If the voice is enemy, VIEW does not apply. If the voice is a poorly-trained relative who actually loves you, VIEW is the right approach.

## When To Make This Move

- After [[voice-in-the-head]] has been named and the person is asking "what do I do with it?"
- When the person has been fighting the voice for a while and is exhausted. See [[dont-fight-the-voice]].
- When [[voice-as-care]] has landed and the person is ready to operationalize it.
- When [[view]] is already a familiar framework and can be redirected inward.

## What This Produces

When done with real VIEW (not as technique — see [[view-as-technique]]), the voice often softens within days or weeks. It does not disappear. It becomes less harsh, less constant, less authoritative. A relationship develops where the voice can be heard without being obeyed, and where the voice can express care without being abusive.

People often report that the voice's content also shifts. It stops being only criticism and starts including warmth, encouragement, even humor. The voice was always able to do these things. It just never had the relationship that allowed them.

The shift is gradual. The voice has been operating its current way for decades. Expect the change to take time.

## How To Fail At This

The standard failure modes:

**VIEW as technique.** Performing vulnerability, performing wonder, performing empathy. The voice immediately detects the falseness because the voice IS you. There is no audience to fool. The performance lands as more attack. See [[view-as-technique]].

**Loving the voice to transform it.** Using VIEW with a hidden agenda of "if I love it correctly it will leave." This is the same fight in nicer clothes. See [[loving-to-transform]].

**Trying to be perfect at VIEW with the voice.** This is the voice running the new project. See [[trying-to-be-right-is-the-voice]].

The check: do you have an outcome you are trying to produce by VIEWing the voice? If yes, you are not yet in VIEW. Real VIEW arrives without an agenda. The voice softens as a side effect, not as the goal.
