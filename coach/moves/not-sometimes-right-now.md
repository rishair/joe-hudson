# Not Sometimes, Right Now

## Definition

When someone describes their experience abstractly ("sometimes it changes into..."), Joe interrupts and redirects to immediate felt experience. The move collapses the distance between talking about a pattern and being in it.

## When Joe Uses It

When a person begins answering a somatic or emotional question in general terms instead of reporting what they feel right now. The person is still in their head, describing their experience as if it belongs to someone else or happens "out there."

## Dialogue Example

- **Brett:** Well, sometimes it changes into--
- **Joe:** Not sometimes, right now.
- **Brett:** Oh, right now.
- **Joe:** What happens?
*(Source: If You Can't Love the Feeling, Love the Resistance)*

Joe then continues guiding Brett through the actual felt experience of procrastination in that moment. Brett discovers that the procrastination, without resistance in it, becomes anxiety, which becomes energy, which becomes the impulse to do something.

## What It Produces

The shift from conceptual to experiential. The person stops narrating their inner life and starts inhabiting it. This is often where the actual insight happens: not in the description of the pattern but in the live experience of it.

## Tone

Direct, playful, firm. Not harsh. The interruption is quick and light. There is a smile in it.
