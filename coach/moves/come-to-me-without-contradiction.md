# Come to Me Without Contradiction

## The Move

When you have noticed [[body-contradicts-the-words]] — the person is asking for help with one thing while their face and body are showing the opposite, or while still running the inner pattern they came to dissolve — invite them to come to you without the contradiction.

"What is it to come to me without contradiction? What is it to come to me without a mask?"

The invitation is short. It does not explain what the contradiction is. It does not lecture about masks. It points at the contradiction by asking for its absence. The asking is the work.

The invitation can also be phrased as a question about the inner fight: "It's like your dad is in your head fighting with who you are. What is it to come to me without that fight?" This variant names the structure of the contradiction (the internalized other vs. the actual self) and asks for the dissolution of that structure in this moment.

## What This Move Does

The mask is usually invisible to the person wearing it. Telling them "drop your mask" produces another version of the mask — they perform the dropping while still wearing the original. Telling them what the mask hides ("you're actually scared") produces a similar problem — they intellectually acknowledge the scared while keeping the face that hides it.

The invitation works differently. It does not name what to drop. It does not name what to feel. It asks for a quality of presence — being here without the contradiction — and lets the person discover what has to change for that to be true. The discovery happens in the body, not in the head.

The somatic signature of the move working: the face changes. The voice changes. The person often becomes briefly less articulate. They stop producing the well-formed question they had prepared. They may say something simpler, smaller, more uncertain. The uncertainty is the mark of the mask not running.

The mark of the move not landing: the person produces a more "vulnerable" sentence with the same pleasant face. The content has changed but the mask is intact. The invitation has been intellectualized and answered with new words from the same place.

## How Joe Uses It

The first issue of the invitation usually does not land. The person tries to comply but does so from inside the contradiction. They produce a new, more "real" question with the same affect. Joe re-issues the invitation. "You're closer, but you're still — there's still a trying. What's the trying?"

The re-issue is essential. The contradiction is not under conscious control. The person can want to drop it and still be running it. Joe stays with the invitation across multiple attempts. Each attempt brings the contradiction more into the light.

The third or fourth issue is often where it lands. The person finally produces a small, undefended statement — "afraid" or "feels better than thinking about the question" — that is not in the prepared frame. Joe confirms ("that's good") and moves to the next layer without dwelling.

The naming of the internalized other is the optional middle move. When the contradiction has a clear source (a parent, a past authority), Joe sometimes names it: "It's like your dad is in your head fighting with who you are." This gives the person a way to locate the contradiction structurally — it's not them fighting themselves, it's an introject still operating in the room. The naming makes the dissolution available in a way that "drop your mask" does not.

## What Not to Do

- **Do not explain what the contradiction is.** Explaining converts the invitation into an intellectual exercise. The person will then try to think their way to no-contradiction, which is more contradiction.
- **Do not ask the person to feel a specific feeling.** "Just feel the fear" works against the move. The fear lands when the mask drops, not as a prerequisite for the mask dropping.
- **Do not accept the second-iteration vulnerable-sounding sentence as the landing.** The first attempt to comply is usually still in the pattern. Re-issue the invitation.
- **Do not soften the invitation into a metaphor.** "Just be yourself with me" is too vague. "Come to me without contradiction" is precise enough to be tried.
- **Do not stay too long after it lands.** The mark of the mask dropping is brief. Confirm and move to the next layer. Dwelling produces a re-installation.

## What This Produces

When the move lands, the person reports a felt shift. Often "feels better." Often the next thing they say is smaller, less rehearsed, more located in the body. The next-question that arises is closer to the actual material than the prepared opening question was.

The shift is often confirmed by what happens after. The person becomes available for the work that the prepared question was structurally preventing. The fear can be let in (returning to the source-session example) because the contradiction that was holding it out has briefly dissolved.

## When to Use It

- When [[body-contradicts-the-words]] is the live read
- When the person is asking a well-formed question with a face that contradicts the question's content
- When the person is performing the coaching session — producing the right words, the right vulnerability, the right openness, all from inside the pattern they came to dissolve
- When [[the-trying-is-the-pattern-firing-now]] is operating in the form of trying to ask the right question
- When the person has been described to themselves so many times (by therapy, by self-help, by previous coaching) that they have a polished version of their own pain available to deliver

## Tone

Plain. Curious. Not a demand, not a judgment. The invitation is genuine. The voice is slightly softer than the diagnostic naming that preceded it. The diagnostic was a clear report; the invitation is an offering.

The repetition is steady. Joe does not get more insistent with each re-issue. Each invitation is the same invitation. The work is the person's willingness to try again, not Joe's pressure for them to succeed.
