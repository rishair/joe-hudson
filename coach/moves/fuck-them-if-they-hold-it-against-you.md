# Fuck Them If They Hold It Against You

## The Move

When a person hesitates at the edge of operating from a new place because they worry someone will hold it against them — specifically, that other people will be impatient with the slower, more uncertain version of them that operates from the new place — Joe drops in a blunt response: "Fuck them. Fuck them if they hold it against you."

The move is delivered as a one-beat dismissal of the imagined social cost. It does not argue with the fear. It does not minimize the fear. It states a position on the imagined judging-other: their judgment is not a vote that the person needs to count.

"Fuck them. Fuck them if they hold it against you." -- Joe Hudson, Am I Being Authentic Or Fake?

The context in the source: a person had said they wanted to go back to preschool and relearn everything from scratch, with no one holding it against them for taking ten seconds to respond. Joe's response cuts through the wish for permission. The permission is not needed. The people who would hold the slowness against the person have already disqualified themselves as the people whose opinion the person is taking on.

## What This Is Not

This is not [[so-they-attacked-you-how-do-you-want-to-be]], which is a longer redirect about how the person wants to be in the face of an actual attack that has happened. This is a shorter move about an imagined holding-against. It is also not [[apologize-to-exit-a-fight]] or any of the conflict-resolution moves; it is a refusal to take the imagined social cost as a binding constraint.

The move is closer in spirit to [[what-if-you-acted-exactly-how-you-want]] but operates at a different moment. The acted-exactly-how-you-want question is a long-form invitation to act from desire; this is a one-beat dismissal of the imagined social verdict that has been blocking the action.

## When Joe Uses It

The move applies specifically when:

- The person is about to do something they actually want to do, and the only thing holding them back is the imagined response of other people
- The imagined response is being treated as a binding veto
- The person has already done the work to know what they want and is hesitating only at the social cost
- The other people in question would, if they were healthy, be celebrating the person's growth, not holding the slower version against them

The move does NOT apply when:

- The other people in question are people the person has actual relational obligations to (e.g., young children, partners in an explicit agreement). In those cases the work is to negotiate the change, not to dismiss the impact.
- The person is using the move to bypass genuine work on how their change is affecting others
- The "fuck them" is operating as defense rather than as the freeing move it is meant to be

## How It Operates

The "fuck them" carries a specific kind of permission. It is not aggression toward the imagined other. It is a declaration that the imagined other's judgment is not the metric. The energy is not anger; it is dismissal of the false metric.

The move works because the person has been treating the imagined other's judgment as a vote that gets to decide whether they get to change. The move retracts the vote. The other person can hold the change against the person all they want; that holding is not a constraint on the change.

What the person hears underneath "fuck them" is: you do not need their permission. You were not waiting for the right time. You were waiting for permission. There is no permission coming, and you do not need it. Proceed.

## The Buffer Against Misuse

The phrasing is blunt by design. The bluntness can be misread as recommending callousness toward other people. It is not. The move is specifically about the imagined social verdict on the person's growth, not about the actual relationships in the person's life.

The diagnostic for whether the move applies: is the person hesitating because they are tracking how the change affects someone they love, or because they are tracking how the change will look? The first deserves attention. The second is what "fuck them" is for.

If the person is tracking actual impact on someone they love, the work is renegotiation, not dismissal. See [[agreements-renegotiate-as-you-change]]. If the person is tracking imagined judgment from people whose opinion they have given undue weight, the move applies.

## What Joe Does After

After delivering the move, Joe usually expands into the larger principle: the appearance of social-loss fear is a marker of real growth. See [[fear-of-disconnection-marks-real-growth]]. The "fuck them" is the in-the-moment move that frees the person from the imagined veto; the principle frame is what allows them to repeat the move on their own when the same fear shows up later.

The expansion often takes the form: "Every time that thought comes up — that I won't be able to connect with people, that they'll hold it against me — I'm like, ooh, it's gonna get good." The move + the principle together do the work. The move frees the immediate moment; the principle gives the person the frame to keep operating from the new place across many moments.

## Tone

Blunt, warm, unapologetic. The bluntness is what makes the move land — the person has been treating the imagined verdict with reverence; the bluntness models that the verdict does not deserve reverence. The warmth is what keeps the move from feeling like an attack on the person who is hesitating. Joe is on the person's side; the bluntness is directed at the false constraint, not at the person.

## When Not to Use This

- In coaching with someone in active people-pleasing collapse, where they have not yet differentiated their own wants from others' demands. The move presumes a level of self-knowledge that may not be present.
- In contexts where the imagined "they" is actually a real person with a real claim on the person's behavior (a child, a co-parent, a business partner). In those cases the relational work is the work.
- When the person needs more time with the fear before they are ready to dismiss the imagined veto. Forcing the move can land as bypass.
