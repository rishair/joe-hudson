# State the Want, Not the Action

## The Move

When something needs to happen, name the want and stop. Do not specify the action. Do not assign the owner. Say only: "I want and need X."

The contrast is between two forms a leader's contribution can take in a meeting:

- **Old form:** "There is an action here. The action is Y. The owner is Z. The deadline is W."
- **New form:** "I want and need X."

The new form leaves the action, the owner, and the implementation unsaid. The room fills in the rest.

"I used to say, okay, there's an action here, I want to give an action, and here's the action, and here's who has to do it. Now I just say, I want and need X." -- Carla Piñeyro Sublett, Finding Compassion in Self-Indulgence

## What It Produces

The first time this move is used in a team, often nothing happens. The team is trained to wait for the action assignment. They hear the want, recognize there is no assignment, and wait for the second sentence that does not come.

Once the team learns the pattern, two responses emerge that the action-assignment form could not have produced:

**Cross-functional volunteering.** People take on the want regardless of whether it fits their role. The person who wants to engage with the want raises their hand. The person who has bandwidth raises their hand. The action lands with someone who actually wants to do it, which is not always who would have been assigned.

"Folks who actually want to take on the challenge regardless of their function will jump in and be like, 'Oh, that's what you want me to -- I think I can help. I got you.'" -- Carla Piñeyro Sublett, Finding Compassion in Self-Indulgence

**Self-organized collaboration.** Multiple people decide together how to address the want, often combining in ways the leader would not have predicted.

"Sometimes I'll have multiple people say, 'You know what, Carla, we're going to go work on that together and we'll come back to you,' which has been super cool to see too." -- Carla Piñeyro Sublett, Finding Compassion in Self-Indulgence

The leader's coordination work decreases. The team's self-organization increases. The match between the work and the worker improves.

## Why It Works

When the leader assigns the action, the team's job is to execute. The execution either matches the assignment or fails to. The variable is performance against a specified target.

When the leader names only the want, the team's job is to figure out what the want requires. The variable is now the team's intelligence applied to the want. The team can route the work to the right person, combine in unexpected ways, and bring more of its capacity to the question.

This is the workplace operation of [[community-over-teacher]] applied to leadership specifically. The leader who pre-specifies the answer collapses the room's intelligence to the leader's own. The leader who states the want and stops invites the room's intelligence to operate.

It is also the operation of [[wants-vs-partiality]] in practice. The want is owned ("I want and need X") without being weaponized into a directive ("you must do X this way"). The ownership is what makes the want credible. The absence of the directive is what frees the team to engage.

## What the Move Is Not

This is not the abdication of leadership. The leader is still naming what they want. The want is clear. The accountability for the want lives with the leader who stated it.

This is not vague aspirational language. "I want and need X" is specific. The X is concrete: a deliverable, an outcome, a state of the team or the customer. The vagueness people fear is not produced by this move. The vagueness is produced by leaders who hide their wants. See [[sublimated-wants-become-politics]].

This is not "you figure it out." The leader is not punting. The leader is naming the destination and leaving the route to the people who will travel it.

## When to Use This Move

- In meetings where the team has been waiting for the leader to specify everything.
- When the leader has been over-coordinating and the team has been under-engaging.
- When the leader notices that the action assignment they are about to make is based on assumption rather than knowledge.
- When the team is mature enough to navigate ambiguity productively. See "When Not to Use" below.
- When the leader has been practicing [[wants-vs-partiality]] and is ready to operationalize it.

## When Not to Use This Move

- In acute crises where speed of execution matters more than fit. State the action.
- With teams that lack the structure to absorb ambiguity. The want without structure produces drama. See [[structure-and-empowerment]].
- When the want is actually a preference about how something is done, not what gets done. If the want is implementation-specific, say it. Hiding the implementation preference as if it were a clean want creates the dynamic [[sublimated-wants-become-politics]] describes.

## The Action-and-Trigger Pair

The move pairs with [[name-your-trigger-as-vulnerability]]. Together they form the new leader's communication mode:

- When triggered: name the trigger as vulnerability.
- When wanting: state the want, not the action.

Both moves operate by replacing instruction with information about the leader's internal state. The team is no longer parsing directives. The team is responding to a person.

## Related Moves and Patterns

- [[no-doctrine-leadership]] is the related practice for disagreements: lead with the "no" rather than the platitudes around it.

## Tone

Plain. The "I want and need X" is delivered as a statement of fact, not a request, not a question, not a softened ask. The plainness is the work. Any softening reintroduces the hedging that the move is replacing.

## Upstream (What Leads Here)

- A leader has been over-functioning and the team has been under-engaging.
- A leader has just done [[tell-them-like-an-asshole]] and discovered how much they were hiding.
- A leader is asking how to "let go" without losing leadership.
- A leader has been reading their meetings as inefficient and is looking for a structural change.
- A coaching conversation about [[wants-vs-partiality]] has landed and the leader is asking what to do with it Monday morning.

## Downstream (Where This Leads)

- Increased team initiative and self-organization.
- Better match between work and worker.
- Toward [[community-over-teacher]] applied at the team level.
- Toward [[structure-and-empowerment]] as the structural support that allows this move to be sustainable.
- Toward the leader recognizing they no longer need to be the coordinator they thought they were.
