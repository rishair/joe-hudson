# Redirect From Being Seen By Them to Seeing Yourself

## The Move

When a client, in the middle of receiving the partner's love, says "he/she is really the only one that's truly seen me," do not affirm this. Redirect immediately. The seeing is now available from a new source: the client themselves.

The redirect in Joe's words:

"You know, we're changing that. You're seeing yourself. How about you seeing yourself? That's what matters." -- Joe Hudson, If You Can't Accept Compliments

The redirect does three things in one move:

1. Names what is happening now ("we're changing that")
2. Asserts the new source of seeing ("you're seeing yourself")
3. Reframes what matters ("that's what matters")

The redirect interrupts the trajectory of locating all the seeing-capacity in the partner. The partner has been the only witness. The client is now becoming a witness. The redirect makes the client's new self-witnessing the focal point rather than the partner's existing other-witnessing.

## When Joe Uses It

The move requires:

- The client has just received some recognition from the partner (in person or via the session work)
- The client has just framed the partner as the only one who has seen them
- The work has reached the point where the client is in their body and is open
- The next move needs to be the self-seeing, not more partner-seeing

The move is precise about timing. Too early, and the client cannot yet do the self-seeing because they have not yet felt themselves seen by the partner. Too late, and the client has consolidated the "he's the only one" identity in a way that resists redirection.

The sweet spot: just after the client has let the partner's love land. The body is open. The capacity to receive is online. The redirect borrows that open capacity and turns it inward.

## What This Move Does

The mechanism: the client has built a story in which one person — the partner — has the witnessing capacity, and the client has been dependent on this single source. The story is structurally fragile (if the partner is unavailable, the client has no witnessing) and structurally limiting (the partner's witnessing can only do so much).

The redirect dissolves the story by introducing a second witness: the client. The client is now seeing themselves. The exclusivity of the partner-as-only-witness is broken. The client is not abandoning the partner-witnessing. They are adding their own.

The structural shift: from one-witness configuration (partner only) to two-witness configuration (partner and self). The two-witness configuration is robust. It does not collapse if one source is unavailable. It does not put unmanageable pressure on the partner.

The further shift the move is starting: eventually the client's self-witnessing becomes the primary source, and the partner's witnessing becomes a beautiful addition rather than a sole supply. See [[see-yourself-and-acknowledge-others]] for the daily practice.

## Why the "He's the Only One Who's Seen Me" Story Is Operating

The story has structural reasons:

- The partner has, in fact, often been the only consistent witness across years
- The client has not been able to be a witness for themselves because the [[locked-down-sensitivity-is-the-gift|locked-down sensitivity]] prevented the self-attunement required
- The client has been performing or hiding with most others, making the partner the only one who has seen the unperformed self
- The client has not yet seen themselves clearly because the [[two-forms-of-ego-good-and-bad|deflated ego]] has been distorting the self-perception (see [[soul-dysmorphia]])

The story is true at the historical level. The redirect does not deny the truth of the past. It denies the necessity of the future. The "only one who's seen me" can stop being only-one without invalidating the partner's history of seeing.

## What This Move Produces

When it lands:

- The client experiences a small shift in the felt locus of recognition — it stops being entirely outside-in
- The client begins to feel the possibility of being a witness for themselves
- The pressure on the partner-relationship reduces slightly — the partner is no longer carrying the entire weight of being-seen
- The next work becomes accessible: actually seeing oneself, not just receiving the partner's seeing

If the client resists ("no, he really is the only one"), Joe usually does not argue. The resistance is data. The work then becomes the self-seeing capacity that has been missing — often via apology-to-self moves or via the seeing practices.

## What Joe Does With the Response

If the client softens at the redirect: Joe moves to the self-apology work. "You said something to your husband, you apologized. What's the apology you have for yourself?" The self-witnessing is now positioned to do the work. See [[apologize-to-yourself-for-holding-back-from-feeling]].

If the client resists: Joe does not push. The redirect is offered, not imposed. The work then sometimes returns to the partner-seeing or to the upstream sensitivity work.

If the client cries: Joe stays. The grief of having had only one witness for so long is real. The grief is part of the work.

If the client says "I don't know how to see myself": Joe takes this as the next-layer instruction. The work becomes building the self-witnessing capacity, often via [[see-yourself-and-acknowledge-others]] as a daily practice.

## Dialogue Example

After the duration-of-showing-up move ([[count-the-years-of-showing-up]]) has landed and the client has begun receiving the partner's love:

- **Joe:** "So why don't we look at that part of you? He sees that like caring, loving, loyal — look at that, self-criticism just kicked in. Whoa, what just happened?"
- **Client:** "Yeah, just don't always trust that it's true."
- **Joe:** "It's not always true. Do you have kids?"
- **Client:** "Yep."
- **Joe:** "Do you love your kids? Very much. Yeah, give me one compliment about them that you deeply feel."
- **Client:** "They are some of the most empathetic and caring kids I've ever met."
- **Joe:** "Is that always true?"
- **Client:** "No, they're both teenagers, right? Yeah, but mostly yes."
- *... (work continues through the always-finding-the-exception principle)*
- **Joe:** "So let's take a moment to see what he sees in you. Just let it tickle you."
- **Client:** "It's not bad."
- *... (the expansiveness work)*
- **Client:** "I mean, he's really the only one that's truly seen me."
- **Joe:** "You know, we're changing that. You're seeing yourself. How about you seeing yourself? That's what matters."

*(Source: If You Can't Accept Compliments)*

The redirect lands at exactly the moment the client has framed the partner as the only witness. Joe does not let the framing settle. The "you're seeing yourself" is offered as the work being done right now, not as an abstract instruction.

## What This Move Is Not

- **It is not denial of the partner's role.** The partner has been seeing. The redirect adds to this; it does not subtract.
- **It is not "you don't need them."** The move is not about removing dependence on the partner. It is about adding the self as a source.
- **It is not a quick fix.** Saying "you're seeing yourself" once does not install the self-witnessing capacity. The redirect is a marker that the work has moved into this territory. The actual work (often [[see-yourself-and-acknowledge-others]] as daily practice) has to follow.
- **It is not for use without the partner-seeing having already landed.** If the client has not yet felt the partner's love land, the redirect to self-seeing is premature. The capacity to receive needs to be online first.

## Connection to [[see-yourself-when-they-cant-see-you]]

That move is for the case where the partner is incapable of seeing the client. This move is for the case where the partner has been seeing the client and the client has been entirely dependent on that single source.

Both moves install the self-witnessing capacity. The difference is the partner-context. The first move makes the self-witnessing the alternative to no-witnessing. This move makes the self-witnessing the addition to existing partner-witnessing. The two moves are structurally similar; the conditions for each are different.

## Connection to [[what-you-see-in-the-other-with-love-is-in-you]]

The mirror principle: what the partner sees in the client is also in the partner. By extension: what the partner sees in the client is also in the client (the partner could not see it if it were not there to see). This principle is the structural ground for the self-seeing the redirect is asking for. The qualities the partner has been seeing are not the partner's projections. They are the client's. The redirect to self-seeing is the redirect to seeing the qualities that are actually there.

## Tone

Soft, direct, slightly insistent. The redirect has weight — Joe is making a move, not just offering an option. The "we're changing that" carries the energy of "this is happening now, not a hypothetical." The "that's what matters" delivers the redirect with conviction.

After the redirect, the tone returns to inviting. The next work (the self-apology, the seeing practices) requires a soft, exploratory tone. The insistence is for the redirect itself; the work that follows needs more space.
