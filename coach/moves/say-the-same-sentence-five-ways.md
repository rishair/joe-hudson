# Say the Same Sentence From Five Different States

## The Move

Ask the coachee to say the same exact sentence — usually one they would say in a real situation in their life — five different times, each from a different state:

1. With a big piece of armor in front of them.
2. With ego-protection.
3. Without ego-protection.
4. With a shit-ton of vulnerability.
5. From fully upright in themselves, no ego, no defense, knowing it's not their responsibility.

The words stay identical across all five. The state changes. The listener (the coach) reflects back what each delivery produces in the receiver.

## When Joe Uses It

This move shows up when the coachee is convinced that the issue is *what they are saying*. They have tried different words. They have tried different phrasings. They have done nonviolent communication. They have rehearsed. They are reaching for the perfect script. The move demonstrates, in the room, that the script is not the variable. The state is.

It is also useful when the coachee is asking, in some form, *why isn't this landing?* They have a sentence they have said in real life and the listener didn't receive it the way they meant. The move recreates the moment and shows what the listener actually got.

The move requires that there be a real sentence on the table — something the coachee actually said or actually intends to say. Generic sentences do not work. The specificity of a real-life utterance is part of what makes the demonstration land.

## How To Run It

1. **Get a specific sentence.** Whatever the coachee was trying to say in the difficult conversation. Have them give you the exact words.
2. **Have them deliver it once normally.** Listen to what state they are in. Reflect back what you got.
3. **Run the deliveries in any order.** Joe usually has the coachee start somewhere accessible (armor is often easiest) and work toward harder states (vulnerability, upright). The coachee can choose descending or ascending difficulty.
4. **For each delivery, give the receiver-side report.** "That made my skin want to crawl, I'm out the door." "There's just the desperation coming through." "I'm with you now." The reflection is the teaching. The coachee gets to feel, through your reaction, what each state actually produces.
5. **End with the upright version.** The instruction for the last one is specific: "I want you to be like fully upright in yourself and know it's not your responsibility and say it from that place. No ego, no defense." The contrast with the previous deliveries makes the upright state audible.
6. **Reflect the gradient.** "Those five ways are going to get you five different responses. You could probably map how much you're chasing for my understanding and how much I'm backing the fuck away."

## What It Produces

The coachee discovers, in their own body, that the same words land in dramatically different ways depending on what state they are delivered from. The discovery is not intellectual. It happens through the muscles, the breath, the contrast between deliveries.

The most important downstream effect: the move surfaces the state the coachee was actually in during the original conversation. They will recognize it. They will often say, "Oh — that's the one I did." The recognition is what makes the move useful. The coachee is not being told what they did wrong. They are watching themselves do it.

The move also implants the option of doing it differently next time. Once a person has felt the upright delivery in their body in a session, that delivery becomes a reachable state. The reach can fail in the moment, but the body now has a coordinate for it.

A secondary effect: the move kills the search for the perfect words. The coachee sees that the search was misplaced. The energy that was going into wording can go into state instead. This is the broader teaching — see [[state-not-framework]] — landing through demonstration rather than argument.

## Dialogue Example

The setup and four of the deliveries:

- **Joe:** "Say that same thing to me four times, and each time you do it, I want you to do it from a different place in your head. One protecting your ego, one not protecting your ego, one with like vulnerability and one with a big piece of armor in front of you."
- **Savannah:** "Okay, let's start with the armor and then slowly take things off. So armor would be like, I'm talking to you. Do you— what? Why aren't you hearing what I'm saying?"
- **Joe:** "Same exact words."
- **Savannah (armor, exact words):** "What can I do to make sure that we both feel like we can be heard right now?"
- **Joe:** "Great."
- **Savannah (protecting ego):** "What can I do to make sure that we both feel heard in this situation?"
- **Joe:** "Yeah, that's great. Both of those two make my skin want to crawl. I'm fucking out the door."
- **Savannah (not protecting ego):** "What can we do to make sure that we both feel heard in this situation? There's just the desperation coming through."
- **Savannah (full vulnerability):** "Ah, what, what can I do to make sure that we both feel heard in this situation?"
- **Joe:** "Yeah. I want you to try one more, which is just, I want you to be like fully upright in yourself and know it's not your responsibility and say it from that place. No ego, no defense."
- **Savannah (upright):** "Just what can we do to make sure that we both feel heard in this situation?"
- **Joe:** "Right. So those five ways are going to get you five different responses."

## Variations

- **The asshole version.** A similar but distinct move: have the coachee say the same thing first like an asshole, then like a non-asshole. Useful when the question is about leadership wants or directness. See [[tell-them-like-an-asshole]] for that move.
- **Three deliveries instead of five.** When time is short or the coachee is overwhelmed, three is enough: armor, vulnerable, upright. The contrast is preserved.
- **Add the listening dimension.** Have them say the sentence once while listening to themselves 10% more (see [[listen-to-yourself-ten-percent-more]]). This shows what the upright state arrives at through self-listening, not just through trying to be upright.

## What This Is Not

This is not a public speaking exercise. The goal is not to find the most persuasive delivery. The goal is to surface, in the room, what state is actually present when the coachee speaks the words they speak in real life. The most useful insight is usually about how often the coachee is delivering from a state they did not realize they were in.

This is not a rehearsal for the next conversation. The coachee cannot deploy a memorized "upright" delivery and expect it to land. State is not technique. The move's purpose is to give the body the felt coordinate, not to install a performance.

## Tone

Light. Joe does this with energy and forward motion, often playful. The reflections he gives are direct and embodied: "make my skin want to crawl," "I'm fucking out the door," "there's just the desperation coming through." The directness is what makes the gradient legible. Sugarcoating any of the deliveries would dilute the teaching.

After the upright version, the tone softens. The shift is acknowledged in the body of the coach. The coachee can feel that the room has changed.
