# Bring the Presenting Problem Into the Room Now

## The Move

When the person presents a relational or social problem ("I get anxious in relationships," "I freeze around authority," "I can't say what I want around people"), and that problem is visibly happening right now in the coaching conversation — pivot. Stop discussing the abstract pattern. Use the live instance as the example. "Let's just talk about how it happened right now."

The move converts the presenting complaint from a thing-that-happens-elsewhere into a thing-that-is-happening-here. The person can no longer discuss the problem from outside it. They are in it. The work has the same content but is now happening in real time, with the coach as a witness to the actual phenomenon rather than a recipient of a report about it.

The structural form: when a person describes a relational pattern AND that pattern is firing in the room, treat the live firing as the example. Do not let the person abstract their way into discussing it as if it weren't currently happening.

## When Joe Uses It

- When the person presents a social or relational anxiety ("I get anxious in relationships," "I get nervous around people") and they are visibly anxious right now.
- When the person is describing their tendency to hide and they are hiding right now ("I don't like being seen" — and they're avoiding eye contact while saying it).
- When the person describes a pattern of freezing in front of authority and they have just frozen because Joe addressed them.
- When the person describes themselves as someone who can't speak up, and they are struggling to speak up right now.
- When the person says "I have a lot of questions but they're all the same question" — the multiplying-and-not-committing is itself the pattern.
- When a performer asks how to stop being self-conscious on stage and they are self-conscious right now in the coaching call. The on-stage version is being demonstrated live.

The qualifying condition: the pattern has to actually be firing in the room. If the person is describing social anxiety but is currently relaxed, this move does not apply. The move is only available when the live instance is empirically present.

Often the person will telegraph the live firing without naming it. They will swear when called on ("Oh fuck. I didn't expect to get called."). They will laugh nervously. They will preface with self-deprecating disclaimers. They will say their questions are all the same. They will hedge. All of these are the pattern showing up in the now. The move is to point at the live evidence.

## What It Produces

**The pattern becomes concrete.** A person describing relationship anxiety in the abstract can keep the anxiety at arm's length. A person whose anxiety is being named in the room cannot. The abstraction was the protection. Removing the abstraction puts the work right where the pattern is.

**The work shrinks to its actual size.** What looked like a complex relational issue becomes a specific moment in this room with this coach. The complexity collapses. The person and the coach are looking at one instance, not a category. The instance can be worked.

**The person stops performing the description and starts being the data.** When the pattern is in the abstract, the person is the narrator. When the pattern is in the room, the person is the example. Narration is a cognitive activity; being the example is a felt activity. The move shifts the channel.

**The whole session becomes the live experiment.** Once the move has been made, every subsequent moment in the conversation is part of the demonstration. The person noticing they're still anxious is data. The person noticing they're less anxious is also data. The room is the laboratory.

In the source session, the move took less than 30 seconds to install the frame. The man said "When I'm in a relationship, I get super, super anxious very quickly." Joe replied: "Yeah, just like right now." The man hedged: "Yeah, well, in— yeah, in a way." Joe held the frame: "So let's just talk about how it happened right now. So what's happening right now is you're being seen. How is that working for you?" The session was no longer about relationship anxiety in general; it was about being seen by Joe and the audience in this moment. The whole rest of the work happened inside the live instance.

## What Joe Does With the Response

If the person accepts the redirect ("yeah, I'm anxious right now"): Joe stays with the live phenomenon. The next move is usually to ask what specifically is happening — what they're avoiding, what they're hiding, what they're scared they'll be seen as. See [[ask-whats-wrong-with-you-into-the-shame]].

If the person hedges or resists the redirect ("well, kind of, in a way, but not really"): Joe holds it lightly but does not let go. He may name the hedge ("yeah, just like right now"), then ask the question anyway. The hedge is itself part of the pattern; naming it is part of the work, but does not need to be belabored.

If the person tries to redirect to the abstract pattern: Joe gently brings them back. "How is that happening right now?" or "What's happening in your body right now?" The right-now is the anchor. Each time the person leaves it, the move is to bring them back.

If the person uses the live instance as the example and goes deeper: Joe follows. The work continues to use the live instance as its referent. Every subsequent observation, question, or instruction points at what is happening here, not what happened elsewhere.

The downstream moves are usually some combination of:
- [[ask-whats-wrong-with-you-into-the-shame]]: the question into the shame that's operating live
- [[interrupting-your-own-descent]]: when the person starts going down into the felt place and then surfaces, Joe names it
- [[is-the-shame-in-the-room-or-in-you]]: when the person is projecting shame onto the audience that is in the room
- [[name-the-body-defense-in-real-time]]: when the body is visibly defending
- [[surface-the-spectrum-of-presence-in-real-time]]: when a shift has happened and needs to be named

## Dialogue Example

- **Guest:** "When I'm in a relationship, I get super, super anxious very quickly."
- **Joe:** "Yeah, just like right now."
- **Guest:** "Yeah, well, in— yeah, in a way. And I don't know why that happens or what to do about it."
- **Joe:** "So let's just talk about how it happened right now. So what's happening right now is you're being seen. How is that working for you?"
- **Guest:** "Uh, I, yeah, I definitely, I don't like being seen. I like to hide myself. I, yeah, I do this in public all the time. Like all of my— I'm either putting on a mask or I'm just running away from people."
- **Joe:** "Yeah. Great. So that's the reason. So what's the question?"

*(Source: Social Anxiety Breakthrough)*

The dialogue shows the move's full arc in four turns. Joe makes the pivot ("just like right now"). The man partially accepts ("in a way"). Joe holds the frame ("let's just talk about how it happened right now") and asks the in-the-room version of the question ("how is that working for you?"). The man, now answering about the live moment, gives the actual answer — he doesn't like being seen, he hides himself, he wears a mask or runs. The answer is more useful than any narrative about past relationships could have been because it is about what is actively happening.

A second example, from a stand-up comic asking how to be authentic on stage. After Joe has clarified the real question (see [[whats-the-real-question]]) is about self-consciousness, the move is the immediate pivot:

- **Guest:** "Yeah, I think it would be how to not be self-conscious, because what I've gotten feedback from a lot of coaches are—"
- **Joe:** "Is, um, like, I don't, I don't want to hear that. Okay, I don't care. Is it so right now you're self-conscious? Yeah."
- **Guest:** "Yeah."

*(Source: How Do I Overcome Self-Consciousness?)*

Note the structural difference from the first example. Here, Joe first refuses the abstract content (the feedback the person was about to recount) and then makes the live pivot. The refusal is part of the move — the abstract content is exactly what would prevent the live work from happening. With the abstract content cleared, the question "right now you're self-conscious?" is the entry to the work. The on-stage self-consciousness the person is asking about is the same self-consciousness operating in the Zoom call. The Zoom call becomes the laboratory for the on-stage work. From there, the session moved to scaling the self-consciousness (see [[scale-the-feeling-1-to-100-with-anchors]]) and asking for a one-percent shift (see [[ask-one-percent-less-then-how-did-you-do-that]]).

## Why It Works

The move works because the pattern is harder to hide from when it is firing live. A description of a pattern is an artifact the person can edit. The pattern itself, happening in the moment, cannot be edited at the source. The body is doing what it does. The defense is operating. The avoidance is in motion. The coach can see all of it. The person cannot pretend it isn't happening because the coach is naming what they see.

The move also works because it eliminates the gap between the work and the problem. Most coaching has a structural gap: the client describes a problem that happened elsewhere; the coach offers something to apply elsewhere; the client takes it away and tries it. This move closes the gap. The problem is here. The work is here. The application is also here. There is no elsewhere to go.

The move also accelerates everything. The shame that drives the social anxiety is operating right now; testing it in the room ([[is-the-shame-in-the-room-or-in-you]]) takes seconds rather than the weeks it would take to test it in actual relationships. The descent into the felt territory ([[interrupting-your-own-descent]]) can happen now; there is no need to wait for the right moment in a real relationship. The recognition can land in this conversation, in this body, in this minute.

## Tone

Direct, light, slightly cheerful. The move is not a confrontation. It is a noticing. "Yeah, just like right now" is delivered as an aside, not as a gotcha. The lightness is what makes the person able to accept the redirect — if the move were heavy, the person would defend.

There is also a quality of welcoming. The coach is making the live anxiety a welcome guest, not an obstacle. "So let's just talk about how it happened right now" treats the live instance as a gift, not as a problem. The treating-it-as-a-gift is part of what makes the work possible. The person can stay with their anxiety because the anxiety is being treated as the interesting thing, not the embarrassing thing.

## What This Move Is Not

- This is not "let's role-play your difficult relationship." Role-play introduces an artificial element; the move uses the actually-happening element.
- This is not "tell me about a specific instance." That move uses memory; this move uses the present moment.
- This is not [[surface-the-spectrum-of-presence-in-real-time]]. That move names a shift that has happened during the session. This move uses the live presenting-problem as the entry point at the beginning of the work.
- This is not [[trigger-the-shame-live]]. That move deliberately evokes the shame by role-playing the offended other. This move uses the shame that is already firing without needing to evoke it.

## The Work-Question-Becomes-Relational-Question Variant

A specific form of this move that arises when the presenting concern is framed as a work or domain-specific issue ("how do I bring my full self to my work?", "how do I show up authentically with my team?", "how do I enjoy my creative process?") and the same containment is firing in the coaching conversation itself. The move converts the work-question into a relational question by leaving the work-frame explicitly.

The structural form: when a person asks how to bring their full self to a context X, and the same containment they describe in context X is operating right now in the coaching, the work-question is the wrapper. The pattern is relational, not domain-specific. The move is to leave the wrapper.

The pivot has three beats:

1. **Confirm the live instance.** "How much of that's happening right now?" When the person says "a lot," the live instance is confirmed.
2. **Leave the work frame explicitly.** "Let's leave your work aside for just a second, because if it's happening now, it's not just about work." The frame change is named, not just done.
3. **Ask the relational question.** "What's the thing that you feel like you have to contain to connect with me?" See [[whats-the-part-of-yourself-you-disconnect-to-connect-with-me]]. The question is the new entry point. The whole rest of the work happens in the relational frame.

The work-frame would have allowed the conversation to drift back to specifics of the work (what kind of art, what kind of client, what kind of presentation). The relational frame holds the work where the work actually lives — in how the person shows up in real time with other people, including the coach. The work-context becomes one instance of a broader pattern, not the pattern itself.

In the source session (2025-11-27), the woman opened with "how can I bring my whole expression to my work without feeling so pressured by outcome and money?" Joe pivoted with "how much of that's happening right now?" Once the live instance was confirmed, he made the frame-change explicit: "let's leave your work aside for just a second, because if it's happening now, it's not just about work." The relational question followed and the rest of the session unfolded in the relational frame. The original work-question was never returned to, because the work-question was the wrapper and the work happens at the level the wrapper was hiding.

## The Fix-Pattern Variant: Note-Taking as the Live Instance

When the presenting concern is a fix-everything or controlling pattern, the live instance often shows up at the very beginning of the session as preparation. The person has been taking notes throughout the call to formulate the perfect question. The note-taking is the fix-pattern firing in the room before the formal start of the session.

The move in this variant runs through a specific sub-move: [[set-aside-the-notes-be-here-with-me]]. The coach asks the person to set the notes aside, frames it as a favor, and follows immediately with a compression request that strips the prepared formulation to its bone.

The variant differs from the canonical use only in that the live instance is the opening of the session rather than something that surfaces mid-session. The same structural feature is operating: the presenting concern is happening right now in how the person is presenting it, and the move uses the live happening as the entry.

In the source session, the woman who presented with "I want to fix everything, I'm always trying to fix things" had been preparing the question throughout the call. The preparation was the fix-pattern. Joe asked her to set the notes aside ("can you do me a favor and not take any notes and just be fully here with me?") and compressed her formulation into one sentence. The session was already in the live work in the first 60 seconds. See [[reading-from-notes-or-script]] for the read this opening serves.

## Anti-Patterns

- Do not deploy when the pattern is not actually firing in the room. The move requires the live instance. Without it, the move is a fishing expedition that the person will rightly resist.
- Do not deploy with too much weight. "Just like right now" is light; "I notice you are experiencing the exact pattern you are describing in this very moment" is too much. The lightness is part of what makes the move land.
- Do not turn the move into a recursive pattern. Once the live instance is being used, work with it. Do not keep pointing out that the pattern is in the room. The pointing-out is once; the work is from there.
- Do not use the move to score a point. The move is not "see, I caught you doing it." The move is "since the thing is here, let's use it." If the person feels caught, they will defend.
