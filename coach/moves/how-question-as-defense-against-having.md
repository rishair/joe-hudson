# Catch the 'How Do I?' Question as a Wall

## The Move

When a person has just demonstrated a receive-state (felt a shift, dropped into presence, received what was being offered) and then immediately asks "how do I do this?" — the question is the next wall. It is the search frame re-installing itself, this time around the receive-state. The person is converting what they just did into a thing-to-figure-out, which is the search-state again, which is the cut-off again.

The move: refuse the how-question and point at what just happened.

The form:
1. "How did you just receive? How'd you do it?"
2. "You just did it. You just smiled. You had that same thing, the tears. It just happened. And then you told me you don't know how. You've now done it twice."
3. "This is another wall. This is you saying, oh no, I don't want to actually have that level of fulfillment."

The move refuses to teach a how-to. It treats the question as data about what is happening right now — the system reaching for a strategy to avoid actually having what was just received.

## When Joe Uses This

- Immediately after the person has demonstrated a receive-state and the body has registered the shift.
- When the person has just had an emotional response to a new felt-state and then asks "how do I do this?"
- After [[feel-the-searching-then-feel-the-opposite]] has produced the receive-state and the person is now asking for the technique.
- After [[hands-together-exercise]] when the person asks how to apply the opposite-of-trying to a specific situation.
- When any receiving move has just landed and the person is converting it back into a search.
- When the person says "I don't know how" about a thing they have visibly just done.

The move is specifically for the moment after the recognition, when the system is about to reinstall the structure that the recognition just dissolved. It is a catch, and the catch has to be made quickly. Within seconds. Otherwise the how-question will re-establish the search frame and the work will be undone.

## Why It Works

The receive-state cannot be acquired through technique. Technique is the search frame applied to the receive-state. The person who is asking "how do I receive?" has stepped back out of the receive-state into the trying-state in order to ask the question. The question itself is the leaving.

The move makes the leaving visible. "You just did it. Then you told me you don't know how. You've now done it twice." The person is being shown the gap between what they actually did and what their head is reporting they did. The body did the receiving. The head is producing the search-question as if the receiving had not happened.

The naming of the question as a wall ("this is another wall, this is you saying, oh no, I don't want to actually have that level of fulfillment") is structural, not accusatory. The person is not being blamed for asking the question. The question is being identified as a function — the function of refusing to have just received. The function is universal, not personal. The receive-state is hard to inhabit because it requires giving up the search-state, and the search-state has been doing structural protective work. The how-question is the system protecting itself one more time.

## The Pattern Generalizes

The same catch applies to any receiving teaching. After [[opposite-of-trying]] has been demonstrated, the person asks "how do I do the opposite of trying?" — and the question is the trying. After [[listening]] has been explained, the person asks "how do I listen better?" — and the question is the not-listening. See [[dont-make-listening-a-morality]] for the parallel.

The structure is consistent across domains. Whenever a receiving teaching lands, the how-question is the reflex. The catch is structurally the same: refuse the how-question, point at the receiving that already happened, treat the question as the defense against having received.

The catch is also self-applicable. Once the person has had the catch made for them once, they can recognize the pattern in themselves. "I am asking how to do the thing I just did. That is the wall." The recognition becomes available because the structure has been seen.

## Dialogue Example

After Joe had run the somatic A/B and the person had felt the receive-state, intense emotion arrived. The person then asked the how-question.

- **Person:** "How do I, how do I receive?"
- **Joe:** "How did you just receive? How'd you do it? This is another wall. This is you saying, oh no, I don't want to actually have that level of fulfillment."
- **Person:** "Right now I just felt I had no other choice but to receive."
- **Joe:** "Yeah, so great. That's how you do it."
- **Person:** "Okay, I feel like I don't know how. I just—"
- **Joe:** "You just did it. You just— it just hit. You just smiled. You had that same thing, the tears. It just happened. And then you told me you don't know how. You've now done it twice."
- *(Source: How Do I Find My Purpose?)*

The catch is made twice in this exchange. First, when the original how-question arrives. Second, when the person produces a "I feel like I don't know how" even after the first catch. The second catch is more direct: the person has done it twice in the same minute. The "I don't know how" is treated as data about the system's resistance, not as a truthful report.

The same structure applies after the loving-instruction has just been completed by the body:

- **Joe:** "Let's go back again to feeling the part of you that is most in pain. Your job is to love it the way you wanted to be loved by your parents."
- **Person:** "I always — I mean, I tried to do it earlier, but I've always had confusion when you said love, how to do that. I don't know how to do it though."
- **Joe:** "You did it."
- **Person:** "I did it. So literally just put my attention there?"
- **Joe:** "No. And then you loved it. You didn't ask me how to do it the first time, so I'm not gonna buy that you don't know how to do it. I saw you do it. You didn't ask."
- *(Source: This is How To Love Yourself)*

The structural form is identical to the receiving variant but applied to the act of loving. The person has just demonstrably loved the part (the body softened, the felt-state shifted) and is now asking "how do I do that?" — converting the live competence into a thing-to-figure-out. Joe refuses the conversion: "I'm not gonna buy that you don't know how to do it. I saw you do it." The refusal is the same catch. The competence being protected is loving rather than receiving, but the move is the same.

The phrasing "I'm not gonna buy that" is operationally important. It is not "you're wrong" or "you're avoiding." It is a friendly refusal of a transaction the person is offering. The transaction is "let me reset to not-knowing so you can teach me again." The refusal preserves the recognition that the person has just done the thing without making them feel caught or attacked.

## What Joe Does With the Response

- **If the person stays in the recognition:** the work is done. The receive-state has been demonstrated and the next wall has been caught. Anchor the felt state in the body. See [[let-your-body-remember-this-moment]].
- **If the person continues to insist they don't know how:** stay light, point at it again, do not lecture. "You just did it." Two or three repetitions of the catch usually suffice; the body knows what it did even when the head is protesting otherwise.
- **If the person says "it feels too easy that that would be the answer":** agree, and name the grief at the disproportion. The "too easy" is the system protecting against the proportion (years spent on a thing that was simple). See [[the-natural-integration-arc-after-breakthrough]].
- **If the person produces a new strategy ("so should I just sit quietly every day and try to receive?"):** the strategy is the search frame again. Refuse it. "You just did it without sitting quietly every day. You did it by feeling the opposite. That is the move." Do not let the practice be converted into a daily discipline of trying-to-receive.
- **If the person reproduces the how-question across multiple sessions:** the pattern is the work. The how-question is one of the most reliable structures for refusing what was offered. Each instance is an opportunity to make the catch again until the pattern softens.

## What This Move Is Not

This move is not "shame the person for asking a question." The question is treated as data, not as wrong. The person is not asked to feel bad about the question; they are shown what the question is doing.

It is not "answer the question differently." The temptation is to say "well, you receive by relaxing into the present moment and letting the universe..." — and the moment that explanation begins, the search frame has won. The whole work was the somatic recognition. Explanation undoes it.

It is not a one-time move. The how-question will return. Each return is another opportunity to make the catch. Over time, the person learns to make the catch in themselves before the question fully forms.

## Tone

Quick, light, slightly amused. The catch is made the way someone might point at a pickpocket: "look, there it is, doing the thing." Not heavy, not solemn. The lightness keeps the catch from becoming a lecture about why the person is doing the wrong thing.

When the person resists the catch ("I feel like I don't know how"), Joe's tone stays the same. He does not escalate. He repeats with more specificity: "You just did it. You just smiled. You had that same thing, the tears." The repetition with specifics is more effective than emphasis.

The naming "this is another wall, this is you saying, oh no, I don't want to actually have that level of fulfillment" is direct and structural. Not therapeutic, not interpretive. Just the structure. The structure is what is true, and naming the structure is what gives the person the chance to see it.
