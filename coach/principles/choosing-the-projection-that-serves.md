# Choose the Projection That Serves You

## The Principle

Once you see that every perception is a projection, the question is not "how do I have no projections?" — that is not on offer. The question becomes which projections create the life you want, and which create the life you do not want. You learn to choose.

"You start choosing the projection that serves you best, the projection that allows for more freedom, that allows for more love, that allows for more joy, and you start choosing it. But you can't stop seeing through it." -- Joe Hudson, Your World is a Projection

The seeing-through and the choosing happen together. The seeing-through prevents the projection from running you. The choosing lets you still use a frame to act.

## Useful vs. Not Useful, Not Good vs. Bad

The frame shifts from moral evaluation to functional evaluation. Projections are not good or bad. They are more useful or less useful for the life you actually want.

"What are the projections that are useful, that create peace and joy, productivity, love in our lives, purpose, whatever it is that one thinks that they're after? And what are the projections that create the things that we're not after?" -- Joe Hudson, Your World is a Projection

A useful example Joe gives: "I project onto snakes that they're deadly. And now obviously all snakes aren't deadly, and I might find a snake and think it's deadly and jump away, but it's not deadly." The projection might be inaccurate in any given case but still serves survival. The criterion is not accuracy but functional contribution to what you actually want.

This is related to [[judgment-vs-discernment]]. Discernment selects between projections without moralizing them. Judgment piles preloaded condemnation onto them. The shift is from "this projection is wrong" to "this projection does not get me where I want to go."

## The Confrontation: "How Will I Act If I Don't Know What's Right?"

The fear that arises when "everything is a projection" lands is: I will be paralyzed. I will not be able to act if I do not know what is true.

"The fear for me when I was entering into that way of looking at the world was like, 'Oh, shit, I'll never be able to act.' How will I act if I don't know what's right and what's wrong? How will I be able to act if I don't know, like, if I know that everything is and isn't true?" -- Joe Hudson, Your World is a Projection

The answer is empirical: action continues. Animals act without certainty about truth. Dragonflies act. The body still moves. Decisions still get made. The fear was hypothetical. The actual experience is the opposite of paralysis: there is more freedom to choose, because no projection has the absolute claim that demands obedience.

"You act the same way that you would if you were an animal or a dragonfly. Like, action still exists, and you're still processing information, and you're still having emotions." -- Joe Hudson, Your World is a Projection

## The Liberating Recognition

The freedom is not "I have transcended projection." The freedom is "I get to choose."

"You just realize at some point, like, if all of it's true and not true, then I actually just get to be who I am, who I want to be." -- Joe Hudson, Your World is a Projection

This is connected to [[inherent-goodness]]: when no projection is forced, what wants to live through you is allowed to live through you. The choice of useful projections is downstream of the deeper recognition that you are not the projection — you are the one choosing.

## The Counterweight: Consequences Become Less Important

A related shift: once the choosing is internal rather than imposed, external consequences lose some of their grip. The criterion becomes the quality of your inner life, not the avoidance of bad outcomes.

"If I was to choose, 'Hey, do I want to go to prison and love myself, or do I want to stay out in the free world and hate myself?' You know, it's like consequences become less important than the actual freedom to see yourself in the world in a way that is enlivening, that is joyful." -- Joe Hudson, Your World is a Projection

The point is not that consequences do not matter. The point is that the projection you choose to live in matters more than the external situation, because the projection is what determines your experience of any situation.

## When Someone Experiences This

- Newly aware that every perception is loaded; wondering how to act
- Stuck between "this is true" and "but I see how this is just a frame"
- Worried that seeing through illusions will paralyze decision-making
- Looking for permission to pick the more generative frame even knowing it is a frame

## Upstream (What Leads Here)

- When [[projections]] has been understood and the person is hitting the paralysis question
- When [[falling-in-love-with-projections]] has landed and the person is asking what to do next
- When the recognition that "everything is a projection" is producing fear rather than freedom
- When someone is between equally true competing stories and looking for a way to choose

## Downstream (Where Joe Goes From Here)

- Toward the actual choosing — not just intellectual permission but live selection of frames in concrete situations
- Toward [[wonder]] as the quality of attention that makes frame-selection possible without rigidity
- Toward [[self-realization]] when the person is ready to ask what is doing the choosing

## Tone

Joe delivers this matter-of-factly, often paired with the lightness of "you act the same way as an animal or a dragonfly." The reassurance is that the consequences feared from "everything is a projection" do not materialize. The instruction is practical: choose, with full awareness that the chosen frame is also not ultimately true.

## Boundary Conditions

This principle assumes the seeing-through has already happened. Applied before the person has actually loosened their grip on a particular projection, it becomes "I'll just pick a better story" — which is itself in the same trap, just with a new flavor.

The principle also assumes the underlying emotional work has been done or is being done. Choosing a more generative projection while the body is still trying to recreate an unfelt emotion will not work. The body wins. See [[feeling-the-unfelt-emotion]] for the prerequisite.
