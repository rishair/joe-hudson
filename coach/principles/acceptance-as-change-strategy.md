# Acceptance Is the Most Effective Path to Change

## Definition

Asking something or someone to be different without first accepting how they are is one of the least efficient ways to produce change. It generates resistance, exhausts the asker, and tends to produce the opposite of what is being asked for. The more effective sequence is to accept how the thing is in this moment, then work to change it for the next moment. Acceptance and asking-for-change are not in opposition. They are sequential.

"It's very healthy to accept the way that the system works, even if you want to change it. Asking somebody to be different, asking something to be different instead of accepting them is a very inefficient path to having people transform, or money transform, or the system to transform." -- Joe Hudson, Money Can't Save You

## What Acceptance Is Not

Acceptance is not resignation. It is not agreement. It is not the abandonment of [[boundary-with-vs-boundary-against]] or the surrender of preferences. It is not pretending the thing is fine.

"Acceptance somehow is conflated as acceptance means that I am not gonna draw boundaries or I'm not going to ask for things to change. Acceptance is, this is how it is at this moment. And I'm not going to fight how it is in this moment. I'm going to work to change it for the next moment. It's 100% possible with the acceptance." -- Joe Hudson, Money Can't Save You

## The Mechanism

Two effects produce the power of acceptance:

- **It takes less out of the asker.** Fighting how something is consumes the energy that change requires. Acceptance frees that energy for actual work.
- **It produces a different response in the thing being asked to change.** A person being told "you shouldn't be the way you are, change" recoils. A person being told "I love you and accept you, and this makes me uncomfortable, and I would prefer that we interact this way" tends to consider it. The same is true of systems and self.

"If it's more like, I love you and I accept you and this makes me uncomfortable and I would prefer that we interact in this way, that's a far more effective way to do it. And it takes a lot less out of me." -- Joe Hudson, Money Can't Save You

This is a corollary of [[love-is-the-best-change-agent]] applied to system change and interpersonal change. Welcoming the thing as it is allows the thing to move. Fighting it freezes it in place.

## In Practice: Personal

Self-change works the same way. The voice that says "I shouldn't be this way, I need to change" produces the same resistance internally as it does externally. Accepting how you are at this moment is the precondition for actually changing. The acceptance is not the change. It is what makes the change possible. See [[enjoy-over-manage]] and [[loving-all-parts]].

## In Practice: Systems

When discussing whether to accept the financial system, Joe is explicit that acceptance does not mean leaving systemic injustice in place. It means working within reality as the platform for changing it, rather than refusing to acknowledge what is.

"By no means am I saying we should all just accept the way that the system works and not move to change it. What I'm saying more precisely is that it's very healthy to accept the way that the system works, even if you want to change it." -- Joe Hudson, Money Can't Save You

The activist who rages at the system from outside it exhausts themselves and produces little. The activist who accepts the system as it is and then organizes from within reality has more leverage. Acceptance is not endorsement. It is the practical baseline from which change becomes possible.

## In Practice: Relationships

A partner who is told they should not be the way they are usually doubles down on being that way. A partner who is told they are loved as they are, and that a specific pattern is hard, has room to move. The acceptance is what creates the room.

The same applies to self-relationship, to children, to employees. Anywhere a person or system is being asked to change, doing so on a base of acceptance is more efficient than doing so on a base of rejection.

## When Joe Reaches For This

- When someone is fighting reality so hard that they have no energy left for changing it
- When a person describes wanting to change something about themselves with disgust and self-rejection
- When activism, frustration with a system, or interpersonal grievance has hardened into ineffective rage
- When the change strategy already in place has been tried and is not working
