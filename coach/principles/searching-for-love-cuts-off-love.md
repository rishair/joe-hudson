# Any Strategy to Get Love Cuts You Off From Love

## The Principle

Any strategy used to get love structurally requires the assumption that love is not currently present. The trying itself is a cut-off from love. The looking is the absence. The earning is the prerequisite-installation that ensures the love being earned will not feel like love when it arrives.

This is the generalization of [[obligation-cuts-off-love]]. Obligation is one strategy; trying to be powerful is another; performing competence is another; managing other people's perception is another. Each strategy carries the same structure: do X to get Y. The doing of X presupposes the absence of Y. The presupposition is what cuts off Y.

"Any strategy you use to get love in a way cuts yourself off from love. So I'm going to try to be powerful to get love. I'm trying to, you know, trying to be — trying, trying in itself is a way to kind of cut off from love." -- Joe Hudson, Love and Obligation

## Joe's Framing of the Mechanism

"The search for light requires darkness. The search for love requires lack of love. Right. You actually have to cut yourself off from love instead of see the love that you have in this moment to recognize the love." -- Joe Hudson, Love and Obligation

The strategy depends on the lack. If love were already here, no strategy would be needed. So the strategy must operate on the assumption of lack. That assumption, held continuously, becomes the closure. The closure makes the lack real. The lack motivates more strategy. The loop runs indefinitely.

## The Resolution Path

The resolution is not "stop strategizing." Trying to stop strategizing is itself a strategy. The resolution is a recognition: love is already here, available, in this moment. The work is to see it, not to earn it.

"At some point, there's this just recognition that nope, love's just available. It's like that. It doesn't require anything besides not cutting it off. Yeah, but if you're looking for it, then you cut it off." -- Joe Hudson, Love and Obligation

This is what Joe calls "the journey of unconditional love." It is described not as the achievement of love but as the dissolution of the conditions on love that the person was running.

## The Subtle Forms

The principle has to be applied carefully because it can be misread as anti-self-development. Joe distinguishes:

- "I have to find myself" → strategy to get self-love. Cuts off the self-love available now.
- "I have to love myself more" → strategy to get self-love. Cuts off the self-love available now.
- "I have to be awake" → strategy to get the experience that requires no strategy. Cuts off the experience.
- "I have to find my purpose" → strategy to get a sense of meaning. Cuts off the meaning available now. See [[searching-for-purpose-pushes-it-away]].

Brett added: "Even I have to find myself, or I have to love myself more."

Joe agreed: "Yeah, exactly. I have to be awake, or whatever the fuck it is."

The implication: the work itself can become a strategy that cuts off what the work is pointing at. See [[state-seeking]] and [[no-finish-line]] for related forms of the trap.

## The Parallel Across Domains

The same structural principle operates wherever a person tries to acquire something that lives in the receive-state:

- **Love:** any strategy to get love cuts you off from love. (this article)
- **Purpose:** searching for your thing pushes it away. See [[searching-for-purpose-pushes-it-away]].
- **Gratitude:** searching for gratitude pushes it away. See [[always-something-spectacular-happening]].
- **Listening:** trying to listen better is the anti-listen. See [[dont-make-listening-a-morality]].
- **Spiritual states:** chasing states pushes the state away. See [[state-seeking]].
- **A partner:** love arrives when you stop being scared of losing yourself. See [[love-arrives-when-youre-not-scared-of-losing-yourself]].

The unifying principle: anything that lives in the receive-state cannot be acquired through the search-state. The search-state is what cuts off the thing being searched for. The exit is not a better search; it is dropping into the receive-state and noticing what is already available. The somatic move that demonstrates this in seconds is [[feel-the-searching-then-feel-the-opposite]].

## What This Is Not

This is not "don't do the work." Doing the work and running a strategy to get love are different. The difference is the orientation of the doing:

- Strategy: do this to earn that.
- Practice: do this because it is the truthful thing to do right now, with no claim on the outcome.

The somatic test: strategy clenches; practice relaxes. If the work has clenched, it has become a strategy. See [[trying-vs-doing]].

## The Difference Between Looking and Assuming It's There

Joe distinguishes the looking-as-strategy from the noticing-of-presence. The noticing is not a strategy.

"There's a difference between looking and assuming it's there. Like, I, I'm not saying like, oh, see, see the love that's there is not a looking. It's like, it's when it becomes in time, when it becomes I have to do X, Y, and Z for — I have to blank for love, rather than, oh, I, I can just see the love that's available. Available." -- Joe Hudson, Love and Obligation

The noticing is in the present tense. It does not require any action to be performed first. It is not contingent. The strategy is in the future tense. It says: after I do this, then love will arrive. The future-tense framing is the cut-off.

## The Grief That Often Accompanies the Shift

When a person sees that they have been strategizing for love their whole life, and that the love was available the entire time without the strategy, the recognition often comes with grief. Brett named this:

"The looking is the cutting ourselves off from love. And I imagine there's to get back to that love, there's the grief, grieving that that's been the case all along." -- Brett Kistler, Love and Obligation

Joe confirmed. The grief is part of the path. Not a sign that something is going wrong. The grief is the recognition completing itself. See [[grief-as-gateway-to-self-love]].

## Application: Why "Self-Improvement" Often Fails

The reason most self-improvement projects do not deliver what they promise is that they are organized as strategies for self-love. "When I am thinner, smarter, richer, more enlightened, more healed, then I will love myself." Each goal is the lack-of-self-love speaking. Achieving the goal does not change the speaker. The internal voice that demanded the achievement simply moves the goalpost. See [[no-finish-line]] and [[improvement-vs-authenticity]].

The exit is not the next achievement. The exit is seeing that the next achievement was never going to be the answer because the structure that demanded it was the problem.

## Application: Why "Looking for the Right Partner" Often Fails

The same mechanism shows up in dating. The person looking for love is running the strategy. The looking confirms the absence. The absence is what is communicated to potential partners, who feel the lack and either reach into it (the rescue dynamic, which dies fast) or move away.

See [[from-looking-for-love-to-being-love]] for the intention-shift practice. See [[the-partner-checklist-doesnt-survive-the-actual-relationship]] for why checklist-based searches confirm the strategy mode.

## When Someone Experiences This

- The exhaustion of constantly trying to be loved and never feeling it
- The flatness after an achievement that was supposed to deliver the feeling and did not
- The recognition that all the things you were going to do for love would not actually have delivered love
- The grief that opens when you see how long you have been running the strategy
- The strange relief when the strategy quiets and the available love becomes visible
- The disorientation of having no project, no scorecard, no thing-to-do for love anymore
