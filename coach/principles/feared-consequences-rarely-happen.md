# Feared Consequences Rarely Happen (8 out of 10)

## Definition

When people act in an empowered way, the feared consequence does not materialize about 8 out of 10 times. The moment before the action, the consequence feels inevitable. Afterward, it rarely arrives.

"When people act empowered 8 times out of 10, maybe 7 times out of 10, the consequence that they're scared of doesn't come to pass. Even though the moment before they take that action, they're pretty sure it's like inevitable." -- Joe Hudson, Empower over Power

## Examples

- "If I'm saying I'm going to be true to my wife even though I might lose her, 8 times out of 10, I'm not going to lose her."
- "If I'm saying I'm going to be true to myself, even if I might get fired, 8 out of 10 times I don't get fired."

The fear creates a vivid, near-certain simulation of the bad outcome. The simulation is almost always wrong.

## And When It Does Happen

The remaining 2 out of 10 still resolve toward the truth, but on a longer timeline. The wife who leaves eventually becomes the relationship that works. The job that ends becomes the path to better work.

"Even when you act empowered and things don't go the way you want them to go, they end up going the way you want them to go eventually. Meaning, yes, maybe your wife leaves you, but eventually you get in a relationship that works for you. Meaning that as you act empowered, as you act in your truth, the world that can handle your truth surrounds you, and that becomes your reality bubble." -- Joe Hudson, Empower over Power

This connects to [[acting-as-if-already-true]] and the echo chamber effect: the world that can hold your truth gathers around you over time.

## Why This Matters for Coaching

The disempowered stance treats the feared consequence as a near-certainty. The empowered stance recognizes the prediction is usually wrong, and that even when it is right, the long arc bends toward truth.

When someone is gripping a relationship, a job, an identity out of fear of losing it, naming the 8-out-of-10 pattern can loosen the grip. The grip itself is what makes the loss more likely, not less. See [[what-we-fear-we-invite]].

## The Mechanism

Predicted constraints are not the same as real constraints. The fearful mind simulates constraints that the actual environment does not impose. Empowered action operates against real constraints; disempowered action operates against simulated ones.

Most of the simulated constraints come from childhood. The boss is not the parent who would withdraw love. The partner is not the parent who could not handle truth. But the system reacts as if. Empowered action tests the actual response of the actual environment, and the actual environment almost always responds better than the simulation predicted.

## What This Is Not

This is not optimism about outcomes. It is not a guarantee. The number is not 10 out of 10. The 2 out of 10 where the feared consequence happens are real, and they are real losses.

This is also not a reason to act recklessly. Empowered action is acting from truth, not testing fate. The 8-out-of-10 pattern emerges naturally from truth-action; it cannot be reverse-engineered by simulating the action without the truth.

- When fear of a specific outcome is preventing aligned action
