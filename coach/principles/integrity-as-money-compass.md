# Integrity as the Compass for Money Decisions

## The Principle

Once money is impersonal, the question becomes how to act with it. Joe rejects the game frame ("money is a game I play") and the means-to-an-end frame ("money is what I need to do my real work") as not enough. What he holds instead: integrity as the moment-to-moment compass for money decisions. Listening to deep truth about what alignment requires in each interaction.

"How do you handle it? The answer that I found that creates the most freedom in me is with integrity. When I listen to my deep truth, when I am spending money or when I am receiving money, what does that require of me? What do I have to do to feel most aligned around my behavior with money? That's the question that I ask." -- Joe Hudson, Money Can't Oppress You

## What This Replaces

This principle replaces several common frames that do not hold up under examination:

- **Money as game.** Useful for depersonalizing money. Dangerous when the game becomes the only frame and the win condition becomes wealth itself. The example Brett raises in the episode: bring a company public, dump it on the public, walk away with the bags. That is the game played without integrity. The game frame alone does not prevent this. See [[money-is-impersonal]].
- **Money as means to an end.** Useful when the end is clear and aligned. Dangerous when the end is hollow or when "the end justifies the means" allows damaging behavior on the way to it.
- **Money as moral statement.** Saving people with money, refusing to participate in the money system, accumulating wealth to prove worth. All are personal stances dressed up as principle.

Integrity as compass does not pre-commit to any frame. It asks, in each transaction, what alignment requires.

## The Koan Underneath

Brett names a koan in the conversation: money is both entirely projection (such that the projection can be let go) and entirely real (such that environmental factors, privilege, luck, family relationship all shape what is possible with money). How does a person hold both?

Joe's response: this is the question of life. How does a person recognize the unity that they are within the self-interest that moves every one of their actions. The answer is not a position. The answer is integrity, moment by moment.

"This is the question of life. It's like, how do we recognize the unity that we are in the self-interest that moves every one of our actions. The answer that I found that creates the most freedom in me is with integrity." -- Joe Hudson, Money Can't Oppress You

## What Joe Reports From This Practice

Joe describes what does and does not feel aligned for him:

- It does not feel aligned to have no money
- It does not feel aligned to accumulate wealth for the sake of accumulating wealth
- It does not feel aligned to try to use money to save people
- It feels important to use money to work with people for mutual freedom and mutual flourishing

The specifics are his. The method is generalizable: the alignment is felt, not calculated. The body knows. The work is to listen.

## The Connection to Impulse

This principle is the money-domain expression of [[listening-to-impulse]]. The same internal sensing that guides what to do next in any moment also guides what to do with money. The compass is the alignment of the body, not the rule book.

When the compass is operating, money decisions become moment-to-moment learning rather than commitments to one ideology about money. Each transaction is asked: how does this feel? What does this require? What is mutually beneficial here? The answers refine over time.

"It's this really interesting thing where it's like this moment-to-moment learning for me around money. How does it feel right now? What does this transaction feel like? How does having a transaction feel as compared to being in connection and letting the money sort itself?" -- Joe Hudson, Money Can't Oppress You

## When This Principle Becomes Available

Integrity as compass requires that money first be impersonal. See [[money-is-impersonal]]. When money is still loaded with personal projection, what feels aligned will be the projection talking, not the deeper truth. The compass will point at the surrogate.

The sequence: depersonalize money first (see [[money-as-surrogate]] for the somatic trace). Then integrity becomes a usable instrument. Then the question "what does alignment require here?" produces signal rather than noise.

## In Coaching

When a coachee asks "what should I do about this money question?", the work is not to give them an answer. The work is to develop their capacity to feel the answer themselves. Joe's pattern of questions:

- What feels aligned to you right now?
- What does this transaction feel like in your body?
- What would you do if you trusted what you felt?
- How does the way you are holding the money affect the relationship with the other person?

These are versions of the same compass question. The coachee learns to ask them of themselves.
