# Holding Back Power Is Holding Back Joy

## The Principle

The channel through which power moves is the same channel through which joy moves. When a person has been holding back what they identify as anger, aggression, or power, they have also been holding back joy. The body cannot suppress one without suppressing the other. The suppression is broad-spectrum, even when the person experiences it as focused on a single emotion or capacity.

The principle is observable in real time. When a person claims their disowned power aloud (see [[claim-the-disowned-identity-aloud-ten-times]]), a smile arrives. The smile is not random. The smile is the joy that has been held in the same channel as the power, coming online as the channel opens.

"The more you said it, the more your smile came up, like on average, like if we were to correlate, the more your smile would show up. So how much of your power that you're holding back is actually also your joy?" -- Joe Hudson, What Happens When the 'Calm One' stops Holding Back

The answer, almost always: 100%. The held power and the held joy are the same held material.

## Why This Happens

The mechanism is structural. The body has channels for aliveness. The channels are not separated by emotion type. When the body suppresses, it suppresses through a channel-level operation: this stream of aliveness is not to come through. The stream contains everything that flows through that channel — anger, power, exuberance, sexuality, joy, love — and the suppression hits all of it.

The person consciously identifies the suppression as being about a specific content. "I'm holding back my anger." "I'm being careful not to be too aggressive." The conscious labeling is a partial read of what is happening. The body is not holding back anger; the body is holding back aliveness, which includes anger but is not only anger.

This is why the person who has been "the calm one" for years often reports not just suppressed anger but also chronic flatness, low joy, difficulty being silly, trouble feeling fun. The flatness is not a separate condition. It is the same suppression operating on the joy stream that runs in the same channel.

It is also why the path back to joy often runs through claiming power. The two come back together. The person who starts letting their power move discovers that joy starts moving too, not as a consequence but as a co-arising. They were never two separate things being managed; they were one channel being closed.

## What the Smile Tells You

In live coaching, the smile arriving during a power-claim is the diagnostic that the principle is operating in this person. The smile is involuntary. The body is recognizing that the channel is opening and joy is part of what is now coming through.

The naming move is precise: name the smile, then name what the smile means. "The more you said it, the more your smile came up. How much of your power that you're holding back is actually also your joy?"

The person almost always says some version of "all of it." Or "100%." The recognition lands because the body has just demonstrated it. The smile was the proof.

If no smile arrives, the principle may not be operating, or the channel-opening has not started yet. The smile is the read, not just a side effect. Its absence is data too.

## The Daily-Life Form

The person who is suppressing power and joy together usually reports:

- "When I drop the battle, I can be kind of funny. I make little jokes, fun to be around, and it feels good. But it's hard for me to get there." -- the source-session description
- A general flatness with brief flashes of aliveness that get re-absorbed quickly
- People in their life saying "you're so calm" or "you're so together" while the person feels isolated
- Difficulty enjoying things at the level the person knows is available
- Periodic outbursts (the "popping" pattern) where the channel opens briefly and a lot comes through at once

The outbursts are the channel doing the work it was supposed to be doing continuously. The cost of the chronic suppression is that the channel-opening becomes a rare event rather than a daily flow.

## What This Reframes

The principle reframes "I need to stop being so angry" or "I need to control my power" as targeting the wrong thing. The work is not to fine-tune the suppression. The work is to let the channel run. The joy will come through with the power. The power will come through with the joy. The integration is the byproduct of the channel being open, not a separate skill.

It also reframes "I want to be more joyful" as not separately learnable. Joy will not arrive while the channel is closed. People who try to install joy through gratitude practice, meditation, or positive thinking, while keeping the channel closed for power, often find the practices producing surface results without depth. The depth is on the other side of the channel-opening, not on the side of more management.

See [[joy-as-matriarch]] for the related principle that joy will not come without the family of emotions that travel with her. This principle is the live-room version: the family includes power, and the smile during the power-claim is the family arriving.

## What Joe Does With This

In coaching, the application is to use power-work to surface joy and joy-work to surface power, recognizing that they are the same channel.

- When a person is asking how to be more alive, joyful, fun, the move is often to surface what power is being held back. The held power is in the way of the joy.
- When a person is asking how to express their anger or power, the move is often to notice the joy that comes online when the power is claimed. The joy is the evidence that the work is landing in the right place.
- When the smile arrives during a power-claim, name it and pivot. The pivot from power to joy makes both available at once.
- When the person experiences the channel-opening as broad-spectrum, validate the breadth. "Yes, that's all the same material. You weren't just suppressing anger. You were suppressing the whole channel."

## When Someone Experiences This

If you find yourself:
- Suppressing your anger or power and also chronically flat in your joy
- Watching a smile come up unexpectedly when you let yourself say something direct
- Being the "calm one" or the "controlled one" and also being the person who has to work to feel happy
- Discovering that letting yourself be angry once briefly produces a wash of joy you did not expect
- Being told you are "so contained" while feeling that you are also not very alive

... then your held power and your held joy are the same material being held in the same channel. The work of releasing one is the work of releasing the other.

## Tone

Direct, with a note of revelation. The principle is offered with the energy of something surprising-but-obvious — like watching a magic trick whose mechanism becomes visible once it is named. The person almost always has the "oh, of course" response. The body has known this all along.

## Boundary Conditions

- The principle applies to suppression-pattern joy, not to all joy. Joy that is missing for other reasons (grief that has not been felt, depression of organic origin, trauma) is not addressed by this principle. See [[joy-as-natural-state]] for the broader framing of joy.
- The principle is not "express anger to feel joy." That instruction can produce performance without the channel actually opening. The principle is structural: the channel that runs anger also runs joy. The opening of the channel produces both.
- The principle should not be used to instruct a person whose anger is currently harming people in their life. In that case, the anger needs to find its appropriate destination (not the loved ones, see [[dont-do-anger-at-your-partner]]) before the channel-and-joy work is useful.
