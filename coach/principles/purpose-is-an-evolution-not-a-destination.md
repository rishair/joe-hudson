# Purpose Is an Evolution, Not a Destination

## The Principle

The standard frame treats purpose as a destination: there is a thing to be found, you find it, then you have it. The reframe: purpose is an evolving process. The idea of purpose at year two is different from the idea at year twenty. The activities change. The understanding of what purpose is changes. The relationship to purpose matures.

Trying to skip this evolution is like a child trying to be older. The process is the process. It cannot be jumped.

"Living your purpose is a process. It's an evolution, it grows, and part of the task time of living your purpose is discovery and fighting against it. And part of it is acknowledging it and doing the scary thing. And part of it is a feeling of choicelessness. And you don't get to shortcut that stuff." -- Joe Hudson, The Myth Of Finding Your Purpose

The shortcut-fantasy is itself the obstacle. The person who keeps trying to skip ahead to the having-found-it stage never enters the evolution. The person who accepts that the evolution is the form lives the purpose as it develops.

## The Stages Are Not Optional

Joe names specific stages that the evolution typically includes:

1. **Discovery.** Trying things, seeing what calls and what does not.
2. **Fighting against it.** Resisting the call when it shows up, not wanting to do the scary thing.
3. **Acknowledging it.** Naming what is being asked.
4. **Doing the scary thing.** Acting despite the fear.
5. **Choicelessness.** The deliberation drops; the call becomes settled. See [[choicelessness-as-purpose-marker]].

These stages are not optional. The person cannot leap to choicelessness without having fought against the call first. The fighting is part of the process. The fighting builds the trust that, when the choicelessness arrives, the person will follow it.

This is why young people typically do not have the choicelessness — they have not yet run the loop. The loop has to be run, multiple times, before the trust accumulates. Telling a young person "just follow your purpose" without honoring the discovery-and-fighting stages is asking them to skip something that cannot be skipped.

## The Idea of Purpose Itself Changes

A specific feature of the evolution: the idea of what purpose is changes as the person lives more of it.

"Two years in the journey, you look back and the idea of purpose has totally changed. 20 years, you look back and the idea of purpose has totally changed." -- Joe Hudson, The Myth Of Finding Your Purpose

At year two, the person may understand purpose as a specific career. At year twenty, the person may understand purpose as a way of being that runs underneath any career. The activity-level description was not wrong; it was the year-two understanding. The how-of-being description was not available at year two; it became available at year twenty.

This means the person at year two should not be expected to have the year-twenty understanding. The understanding has to develop through the living. Demanding mature understanding from immature experience produces shame and self-attack. The right pace is the pace at which the understanding actually unfolds.

For the AI coach: do not push someone to a stage of purpose-understanding they have not yet earned through experience. Meet them at their current stage. The development happens through the living, not through being told the next stage's framing.

## The Forms Change; The Through-Line Stays

Joe describes his own evolution in form:

"You know, at first I wrote songs and did comic strips. I did art form, and that was the good thing. That was my purpose. But business wasn't. And there is this understanding that that's going to change for my daughter. That changed for me into another thing, into another thing. And then looking back, oh, there's this thread that no matter what I was doing, business or art, there's a way in which I was constantly interested in who am I, what am I, how do humans work, how do we see ourselves more clearly. It was always there." -- Joe Hudson, The Myth Of Finding Your Purpose

The forms changed completely: art, business, coaching. The through-line — interest in human nature, helping people see themselves more clearly — was constant. The constancy was invisible during the evolution. It became visible only in retrospect.

This means the person living the evolution should not be expected to see their through-line yet. The through-line emerges from the accumulated experience. Looking for it prematurely produces false through-lines that get attached to and defended. Letting it emerge produces the actual through-line, which is more textured and more reliable.

See [[purpose-is-how-not-what]] for the principle that the through-line lives in the how, not in any specific what.

## The Daughter at Seventeen

Joe's daughter, at seventeen, felt purpose in studying nature with the Smithsonian and did not feel it touring Europe drinking and sightseeing.

"She just felt that sense of purpose in doing it. And I have no doubt that in her life she'll do many other things that will give her a sense of purpose and many other things that will not give her a sense of purpose." -- Joe Hudson, The Myth Of Finding Your Purpose

What is striking is that she did not say "I found my purpose"; she said the activity felt purpose-ful and the other one did not. This is the right age-appropriate read. Purpose at seventeen is local, episodic, comparative — this felt purposeful, this did not. It is not yet a stable through-line. The stable through-line is decades away.

The coaching implication: when someone reports an episodic purpose-experience like this, do not try to upgrade the framing into "this is your life's purpose." Let the episode be what it is. The accumulation of episodes will eventually reveal the through-line, but it has to accumulate first.

## The Trying-to-Jump-Ahead Pattern

A common pattern: someone reads about purpose, decides they want to have-found-it now, and tries to skip directly to the choicelessness or to the stable through-line. The result is performance, not purpose. The person describes a purpose they have not actually lived into, defends it, and stays stuck.

"That trying to jump it is, you know, it reminds me of my daughters when they were younger wanting to be older. You know, it's like, what? This is the process." -- Joe Hudson, The Myth Of Finding Your Purpose

The child wanting to be older is not wrong about wanting; the child is wrong about the possibility. The process cannot be jumped. The wanting is appropriate; the trying-to-skip is not.

For the AI coach: when someone is trying to skip stages, name the pattern gently. The wanting is fine. The skipping is what produces the suffering. The stages have to be lived through, in their own time, even when the person can see what comes later.

## The Retrospective Recognition

A specific feature of the evolution: the past chapters become visible as having been leading to the present chapter, even when at the time they did not look like they were leading anywhere.

"Every time in my life that I have this moment of, oh, here I am, I look back and I realize everything I did, the video that I did is now helping me in this YouTube video. The venture capital that I did is helping me understand how to build this business. Everything that I did in my past was leading to this moment." -- Joe Hudson, Finding Your Purpose is Hard Until You Understand This

The video work Joe did earlier in his life is now operational equipment for the YouTube videos he makes now. The venture capital experience is now operational equipment for building his coaching business. None of this was visibly leading to where he stands. All of it was.

The retrospective recognition is recurring, not one-time. Joe says "every time in my life that I have this moment" — implying the recognition happens repeatedly across the unfolding. At each new arrival, the past becomes visible as having been the route to here. The route was not visible at the time of walking; it becomes visible at each arrival, and the visibility itself is part of the evolution's reward.

Joe also names the forward-looking corollary:

"I know that this is not the end. And I'll have this moment of going, oh wow, here I am, I'm living my purpose. I know that I feel more fulfilled than I ever have in the rest of my life in this moment. But I know that if I keep on following it, in another 10 years I'm going to have another moment like that, maybe even a whole life like that of holy crap, it just keeps on getting better." -- Joe Hudson, Finding Your Purpose is Hard Until You Understand This

The "it just keeps on getting better" is qualified specifically. It is not "I keep getting what I want." It is "I keep discovering possibilities I did not know existed." The expected destination at each stage is never as good as the actual unfolding produces.

"The idea that I had in my head for what I wanted is never as good as what I get." -- Joe Hudson, Finding Your Purpose is Hard Until You Understand This

This collapses the destination-frame at the level of imagination. The destination-frame assumes the imagined arrival is the best possible arrival. The evolution-frame produces arrivals that are better than what the imagination could construct. The imagination is therefore a poor advisor for what to chase. The unfolding is a better deliverer than the chasing was a designer.

For the AI coach: when someone is gripping a specific imagined destination, the retrospective-recognition framing is the antidote. The destinations that have already been reached were better than what was imagined; this destination will likely follow the same pattern. The frame is not "give up your goals" but "do not believe your imagination is the upper limit of what is possible."

See [[creative-career-story]] for the full arc that demonstrates this — Joe's investment-banking-to-art-to-venture-capital-to-coaching path, with each chapter only retrospectively visible as preparation for the next.

## The Refinement After Each Stage

At each stage of the evolution, the question shifts. The early question is "what should I do?" The later question is "how am I doing what I'm doing?" The much later question is "where can I surrender more deeply into the purpose that is already living me?"

"There's a way you can be more attuned to your purpose, more aligned with your purpose. There's a way that you're not surrendered to it. There's a way you're not fully acknowledging your purpose. The more it's living you, the more it's living you. And so I would just keep on going into that place of surrender, and then whatever reveals itself will reveal itself." -- Joe Hudson, The Myth Of Finding Your Purpose

Even at the late stages, there is more to refine. The evolution does not stop. The depth of inhabitation can always deepen. The person who thinks they have arrived at the final answer is at the stage of "thinking they have arrived," which is itself a stage to be evolved through.

This is consistent with [[every-moment-is-an-exit-point]]: every moment offers the possibility of refining what the person is and how they are inhabiting their purpose. The refinement is the form. The destination is not a destination.

## What This Looks Like in Coaching

For someone in the early-stage purpose-question:
- Honor the discovery phase. Do not push toward premature clarity.
- Let them try things and see what calls.
- When they fight the call, do not collapse the fight. Let the fight do its work.

For someone in the middle-stage purpose-question:
- Help them feel the difference between activities they have done in alignment and out of alignment. Help them start to recognize the through-line.
- Honor the choicelessness when it arrives without demanding it before it is ready.

For someone in the late-stage purpose-question:
- Help them notice where more surrender is available. The through-line is alive; the inhabitation can deepen.
- Trust that whatever reveals itself in the deepening will be appropriate.

The AI coach has to meet the person at their stage rather than projecting any single stage's framing onto everyone.

- Toward [[choicelessness-as-purpose-marker]]: the choicelessness arrives at the appropriate stage; earlier than that, it is forced
- Toward [[purpose-cracks-you-open]]: each stage involves its own cracking; the cracking is not finished after the first one
- Toward [[searching-for-purpose-pushes-it-away]]: the search-state often arises from trying to skip the evolution; relaxing into the process collapses the search

## Tone

Patient, warm, slightly amused at the trying-to-jump-ahead. The principle is offered as relief to people who feel they should already have the answer they have not yet earned the right to have. The tone communicates that the evolution is the form, not a failure. Joe's analogy to a child wanting to be older is offered with affection, not condescension.

When working with someone in despair about not being at a later stage, the tone is quiet. The despair is the felt-cost of comparing one's stage to someone else's. Let the despair be felt; do not rush to reassure. The recognition that purpose has to evolve usually lands more reliably when the despair has been honored.
