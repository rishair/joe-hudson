# You Can't Fix Anything If You Can't Be With It

## The Principle

Fixing requires presence as its precondition. The person who fixes things without being able to be with them is doing surface work on top of what they have not seen. The work fails because the fix is addressing the shape of the problem, not the thing the problem is made of.

This is not a moral statement about how one should approach problems. It is a structural statement about why fix-from-absence reliably produces the chronic sense of failure that drives more fixing. The fixer who cannot be with the thing being fixed is unable to perceive what the thing actually requires.

"How can you fix anything if you're not here like this with me? You're trying to fix something without being able to be with it." -- Joe Hudson, Why Am I Always Trying To Fix Myself & Others?

The corollary, in the person's own words from the source session: "Because it's easier to fix things that are surface." The fix-from-absence is easier because surface is what one can perceive without being-with. The depth requires the presence.

## Why Fix-From-Absence Fails

Three structural reasons.

**The problem is not where it appears to be.** Most of what presents as a problem is the visible part of a deeper structure. The visible part is the surface; the deeper structure is what is actually generating the visible part. A fix applied to the surface changes the surface and leaves the structure intact. The structure then re-generates the surface, often in a slightly different shape. The fixer experiences this as the problem recurring.

**Being-with reveals what the problem is made of.** Presence is the perceptual mode that registers depth. The fixer who is not present is registering the surface and only the surface. The information needed to actually address the problem — what feeling is underneath, what need is being signaled, what dynamic is being maintained — is not available to surface-perception. It is only available to presence.

**The very act of fixing-without-being-with is part of what generated the problem.** This is true at both the interpersonal and intrapersonal level. The parent who tries to fix the child without being with the child reliably produces a child who has a hard time being with themselves. The person who tries to fix themselves without being with themselves reliably produces a self who cannot trust the fixer. The fixing reinforces the dynamic it was meant to address.

## The Easy-Surface Trap

The fixer often finds the surface fixing easier and the being-with harder. This is structurally correct from inside the fixing-pattern: the surface is mapped, the being-with is unmapped. The surface has techniques; being-with has none. The surface produces immediate visible movement; being-with produces no visible movement at all.

But the easier work is the work that does not address what is happening. The harder work — the being-with — is the work that does. The fixer who consistently chooses the easier work is consistently choosing the work that will not produce the result they want.

This is also why the fix-from-absence pattern is self-perpetuating. The fixer, doing the easier work, gets the same chronic failure-feeling. The failure-feeling drives more fixing. The more fixing is more of the easier work. The harder work — being-with — never gets done because it is structurally the path the fixer is avoiding. See [[everything-i-touch-fails-drives-fixing]].

"That's why you always have that weird sense of failure. And your mind just went to trying to fix it." -- Joe Hudson, Why Am I Always Trying To Fix Myself & Others?

The weird-sense-of-failure is the diagnostic. It is the body reporting that the work being done is not the work that needs doing. The body's report is accurate. The mind's response — to try harder to fix — is the wrong response, because the failure-signal is pointing at the fixing-mode itself, not at the inadequacy of any particular fix.

## What Being-With Actually Is

Being-with is a perceptual posture, not a doing. It involves:

- Letting what is be what it is, without immediately moving to change it.
- Feeling what is happening in the body in response to what is being attended to.
- Allowing the thing being attended to to take up the space it needs without compressing it.
- Treating the thing as something to be perceived rather than as something to be operated on.
- Tolerating the discomfort of being present to something that may not be solvable.

The discomfort is the load-bearing element. Most fixing happens because the fixer cannot tolerate the discomfort of being-with. The being-with is the harder skill because it requires the capacity to hold the discomfort without converting it into action. The fixing-as-action is the conversion-mechanism.

Building the capacity for being-with is the work. The capacity is what the fixing has been substituting for. Once the capacity is present, fixing becomes one option among many rather than the default. When fixing is chosen, it is informed by what the being-with has revealed, and it actually addresses what needs addressing.

## The Domains This Applies To

The principle is general. It runs the same way across domains:

- **Self.** You cannot fix what you cannot be with in yourself. The pattern you keep failing to change is a pattern you have never sat with. The work is not the next behavioral intervention; the work is sitting with what the pattern is made of.
- **Partner.** You cannot fix your partner's problem if you cannot be with them in it. The being-with is what your partner has been needing all along; the fixing is what you have been substituting for it. See [[dont-try-to-change-your-partner]].
- **Child.** You cannot fix what your child is going through if you cannot be with them in it. The fix-without-with reads as not-being-met, which compounds whatever the child was struggling with.
- **Team.** You cannot fix the team dysfunction if you cannot be with the team in it. The fix-from-absence is registered as management, and management without presence is what produces team disconnection. See [[disconnection-precedes-decline]].
- **World.** You cannot fix the world's injustice from outside the experience of the injustice. The activism that comes from fix-without-with produces the rejection the activist experiences from the very people they are trying to help.

The principle is operationally the same in every domain: the perceptual posture of being-with is the precondition for any fixing that will actually land. The fixing that skips the being-with is the easier work that does not work.

## Why People Reject Being Fixed

A specific corollary: the people the fixer tries to fix tend to reject the fixing. This rejection is not arbitrary. It is the rejected person's body registering that they are being operated on rather than met.

"That's why everybody rejects you trying to fix it." -- Joe Hudson, Why Am I Always Trying To Fix Myself & Others?

The body knows the difference between being-with and being-fixed-on. The being-fixed-on signal triggers protective responses: defensiveness, withdrawal, escalation, complaint. The fixer often experiences these responses as evidence that the recipient is not ready for help or is being difficult. From the recipient's side, the response is accurate: the fixer is not actually with them, and the not-being-with is what is being rejected.

This is the relational form of the principle. The fixer who has not built the capacity for being-with is reliably going to be rejected by the people they try to fix. The rejection is information. The information is that fixing is the wrong move; being-with is the move that has not yet been tried.

## The Coaching Move That Demonstrates the Principle

The principle is most reliably taught by demonstration rather than explanation. The coach drops the fixing-stance in the room, asks the person to do the same, and lets the person discover what becomes available when both people are with each other instead of fixing each other. See [[make-the-session-itself-the-demonstration]].

The discovery in the room is the principle in its operational form. The person realizes that the thing they have been chasing through all the fixing — the feeling of contact, the recognition, the meeting — is here, in the room, available the moment the fixing is dropped. The principle becomes self-evident because it has been experienced.

In the source session, the woman registered this directly. After the conceptual conversation about fixing, Joe invited her into being-with. The aliveness arrived in seconds. She named it: "Definitely a lot of sensation. Definitely in my, my— I can feel the end of my skin, like, everywhere that's exposed." The thing the fixing had been chasing was here the moment the fixing was set down.

## The Surface-vs-Depth Diagnostic

For coaches and for self-practice, the principle generates a diagnostic question: "Am I trying to fix the surface, or am I being with what's underneath?" The question can be asked in any situation where a fix is being attempted.

If the answer is "I'm trying to fix the surface," the question becomes: "What would it take for me to be with what's underneath?" The answer is usually short and concrete — stop, breathe, feel, look, listen. The instruction sounds like nothing. The instruction is the work.

The diagnostic also works retrospectively. A fix that has failed can be re-examined with the question: "Was I trying to fix this without being with it?" If yes, the failure is informative. The fix did not fail because the wrong fix was chosen; it failed because no fix-from-absence would have worked. The next move is to go back to the situation and be with it first.

## What This Principle Is Not

- It is not a recommendation against action. Action is still appropriate. The principle says that action must be preceded by being-with, not that action must be avoided.
- It is not a meditation prescription. Being-with is not the same as meditating. Being-with happens in any situation; meditation is one practice that develops the capacity for it.
- It is not a way to avoid responsibility. The principle does not say "don't try to make things better." It says that making things better requires being with them first, which is itself work.
- It is not about being right. The principle is about perceptual access. The fixer who cannot be with the thing they are trying to fix does not have access to what the thing is. Without access, the fix is uninformed.

## Tone

Plain, direct, slightly insistent. The principle is stated as a structural fact, not as a wisdom-teaching. "You can't fix anything if you can't be with it." There is no qualifier, no maybe, no in-most-cases.

The insistence is because the principle is counterintuitive from inside the fixing-pattern. The fixer hears "you can't fix" and wants to argue with the can't. The simplicity of the statement resists the argument. The fixer has been fixing for years; the principle is what would have to be true to explain why the fixing has not produced what the fixer wanted.
