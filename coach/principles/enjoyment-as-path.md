# Enjoyment as Path

## Definition

The most important thing to know about transformative work: it is a process to be enjoyed. Not "enjoyable because that's nice" but because enjoyment makes the process more effective.

The reframe: the question is not "how do I do the things I enjoy?" It is "how do I enjoy the things I'm doing?"

"If it means that you are listening to this digital recording, even if you notice that you're criticizing yourself, how do you enjoy criticizing yourself? That's the question." -- Joe Hudson, Embarking on the Journey

This is the golden mean. Everything else revolves around it. Conditions, principles, what to expect on the journey, impediments: all of it is organized around the central question of enjoyment.

## In Practice

Same experience, opposite effects. Two people on a roller coaster going down. One screams with excitement, one screams with terror. The excited one releases anti-carcinogenic chemicals. The scared one releases carcinogenic chemicals. Same ride. The only variable is the relationship to the experience.

"The exact same experience can be used to destroy somebody or to heal somebody. You're going to be on the roller coaster ride. How do you enjoy it?" -- Joe Hudson, Embarking on the Journey

Practical applications:
- If a feeling you valued has disappeared, how do you enjoy the not-knowing? See [[pendulation]].
- If you notice you are criticizing yourself, how do you enjoy the criticism?
- If you are yelling at your spouse, how do you enjoy the yelling? (Nobody really enjoys yelling. If you try, the behavior shifts or stops.)
- If you are in [[resistance]], the question becomes: "How do I enjoy the resistance?"

The virtuous loop: enjoyment makes approach easier. Easier approach produces more learning. More learning produces more enjoyment. This is why forcing transformation through shoulds fails. See [[wants-over-shoulds]].

## Upstream (What Leads Here)

- When someone is grinding through transformation with effort and willpower
- When the work has become joyless or obligatory
- When someone asks "how long will this take?" (the question reveals that the process is not being enjoyed)
- When [[resistance]] to the process itself has become the main obstacle

## Enjoyment Is the Measure of Efficiency

The Art of Accomplishment episode adds a structural reason for the primacy of enjoyment: it is the actual measure of human efficiency. For cars, efficiency is fuel-per-distance. For humans, efficiency is enjoyment. When enjoyment is high, friction is low, the parts of you are aligned, and the work is being produced with minimum fuel. See [[efficiency-as-enjoyment]].

This makes enjoyment not a luxury but a measure of how well the system is functioning. The exhausted, frenetic, joyless worker is not more productive than the rested, enjoying worker. The illusion otherwise is documented in the [[sleep-deprived-soldier]] study.

## Downstream (Where This Leads)

- Toward [[process-over-result]]: if you enjoy the process, the result becomes irrelevant
- Toward [[embrace-intensity]]: the "embrace" part is what makes intensity enjoyable
- Toward [[efficiency-as-enjoyment]]: the structural reason enjoyment is not optional
- Toward [[art-of-accomplishment]]: enjoyment is what makes the work art
- Toward [[consciousness-is-the-product]]: the enjoyment in the making becomes part of what is delivered
- Toward [[enjoyment-as-surrender]]: enjoyment functions not only as path but as the instrument that gauges alignment with the deeper call
- Away from state-seeking and future-living: enjoyment is present-tense
