# Anything of Significance Will Be Used to Oppress

## The Principle

Any tool, teaching, or idea of significance will eventually be used to oppress people. The words of Christ, the words of Buddha, the concept of love, the technology of the internet, the practice of meditation -- each has been used by some to oppress others. This is not a failure of any particular teaching. It is what happens to ideas that get large enough to be picked up by people with unexamined shadow.

There is no design that prevents the weaponization. There is also no avoidance that prevents the original good. The pattern is structural: good emerges, gets corrupted, the corruption produces new good as people respond to it, and the new good will itself eventually be corrupted.

"Anything, anything of significance gets used to oppress people eventually. Even love gets used to oppress people." -- Joe Hudson, The Business Behind our Courses

## The Mechanism

People with unexamined shadow encounter a powerful tool and pick it up. Without examining their own shadow first, they project the shadow into the use of the tool. The tool becomes the vehicle for whatever the person was already carrying. A person carrying control turns the tool of communication into a tool of control. A person carrying superiority turns the tool of compassion into a tool of superiority.

"It's just, it's oftentimes someone's like, oh, I can use this tool to help a whole bunch of people. And they haven't looked at their own shadow. And so their shadow just kind of, you know, just completely takes over the work." -- Joe Hudson, The Business Behind our Courses

The damage is not from the tool. It is from the shadow that picked up the tool. The same tool in a different person produces different effects.

## The Specific Form This Takes

For [[view]] specifically, the predicted weaponization has a shape. The tools become a new morality. Someone tells their partner, "You're not asking how-what questions" or "You're not in wonder enough." The partner experiences the tools as an instrument of judgment. The partner becomes oppressed by the morality the tools have been converted into. The partner rejects the tools. The rejection becomes its own pattern.

"Someone's gonna bring the VIEW and they're going to say something like, you're not asking how-what questions to somebody, to their wife. And their wife's going to be like, fuck the VIEW. And then the VIEW is going to be seen [as oppressive]." -- Joe Hudson, The Business Behind our Courses

This is consistent with how every significant tool has been used. It will happen with this work. It will happen with any work that becomes widespread enough to be encountered by people who have not done their own inner work.

## The Generative Side of the Cycle

The principle is not pessimistic. The cycle is generative, not just destructive. The atrocity prevents other atrocities. The other atrocities, when prevented, would have produced their own atrocities. The system is alive, not static.

"There's an atrocity that occurs, and it is through that atrocity that 10 other atrocities are prevented. And it's through the prevention of those atrocities that the next atrocity occurs. I don't see a way around good being corrupted into bad and bad creating the next good. It seems to be just the way of things." -- Joe Hudson, The Business Behind our Courses

This is risky framing because it can be heard as justifying atrocity. Joe is not. He is naming the structural pattern that history shows. The pattern does not justify the harm. It frames the harm as part of a longer movement that, in aggregate, produces development.

## What This Implies for Anyone Doing Significant Work

The implication is not to avoid scaling the work. The implication is to accept that the work will be corrupted and to design against the corruption while accepting that no design fully prevents it.

The available defenses:
- Require permission for use of specific tools (Joe's current practice)
- Encourage people to develop tools from their own consciousness rather than carrying yours (see [[community-over-teacher]])
- Design the work to become unnecessary (see [[become-unnecessary]])
- Name the predictable weaponization patterns explicitly so people can recognize them (see [[view-as-technique]], [[wonder-as-strategy]], [[vulnerability-as-control]], [[loving-to-transform]], [[tools-to-change-not-love]], [[dont-evangelize-the-work]], [[spiritual-narcissism]])

None of these eliminate the corruption. They reduce the rate. The acceptance that the corruption will happen anyway is what allows the leader to keep working without being paralyzed by the inevitability.

"It breaks my heart and that I know to some degree that this stuff has already been weaponized. I know that on a small scale." -- Joe Hudson, The Business Behind our Courses

## The "No Way Out" Recognition

A specific version of this principle landed when Joe was researching cult dynamics after watching The Vow. The pattern of high-control group formation showed up everywhere he looked: in companies, political groups, religious traditions, even relationships. The conclusion was bleak before it became generative.

"No matter what you do, humans will make it a cult. Like no matter, and by humans, I mean me. I'm not excluding me from that. So it was just like, well, no matter what you do, this is what it devolves into because, or evolves into, but because people like to have clear understanding of what's going on. They like to have rules, they like to have hierarchy." -- Joe Hudson, Group Cohesion vs. Cult Dynamics

The "no way out" version of the principle is the cult-specific application of the broader rule that anything of significance gets corrupted. The structural pull toward hierarchy, rules, and clarity is what produces the cult dynamic at scale. The pull is not pathological. It is how human groups self-organize. The work of preventing the cult formation is not eliminating the pull; it is designing structurally against the pull while accepting that the design can only reduce the rate, not eliminate the outcome.

What got Joe back to engaging with the work after this recognition was finding the specific markers of high-control groups (the "25 things that let you know you're in a high control group") and seeing that the absence of those specific features made the work he was doing structurally different even when surface practices looked similar. The recognition was: there is a real distinction, even if the gravitational pull toward cult formation is universal. See [[community-over-teacher]] for the operational design and [[subjugation-prevents-healing]] for what defines the difference at the participant level.

The personal corollary at the individual scale is [[cult-of-one]]: even the leader doing the work to prevent the cult dynamic is themselves operating with internal cult-like structure. The acceptance of that fact is what allows the work to continue without the leader needing to occupy a pure-of-cult position they cannot occupy.

## The Personal Posture

The grief is real. The leader who builds significant work and watches it get corrupted has to be able to feel the heartbreak without letting it stop the work. The heartbreak is appropriate. The withdrawal that protects against the heartbreak prevents the work from reaching the people it would otherwise serve.

"It breaks my heart. And it's been happening for, I mean, look what people have done with the words of Christ or the words of Buddha. And they oppress themselves and others with them all the time. It's going to happen here." -- Joe Hudson, The Business Behind our Courses

The posture: hold the work as offered, accept that the corruption is coming, design against it where possible, grieve when it happens, keep working.

## What This Means for the Coachee

When a coachee is wrestling with the question of whether to scale their work because of fear of the corruption, this principle is directly relevant. The fear is reasonable. The corruption will happen. But the fear is not sufficient reason to withhold the work, because withholding does not prevent harm; it just prevents the help that would otherwise reach more people than the eventual harm reaches.

When a coachee is encountering the weaponized version of a teaching they care about (someone using meditation against them, someone using VIEW against them, someone using therapy against them), this principle is also directly relevant. The corruption is real. The corruption is not the teaching. The work is to recognize what has happened and to release the corruption without releasing the underlying truth.

## Upstream (What Leads Here)

- When a coachee is afraid to scale their transformative work because of how it might be misused
- When a coachee has been hurt by a weaponized version of a teaching they otherwise believe in
- When a coachee is asking whether to keep using a tool that has been corrupted by others
- When the conversation is about intellectual property, gatekeeping, or who can use what
- When a coachee is grieving the corruption of something they offered in good faith

## Downstream (Where This Leads)

- Toward grief as the appropriate response to the heartbreak
- Toward design choices that reduce the rate of corruption (see [[become-unnecessary]], [[community-over-teacher]])
- Toward naming the specific weaponization patterns to make them recognizable (see [[view-as-technique]] and related anti-patterns)
- Toward acceptance of the cycle as structural rather than personal failure
- Toward [[epiphany-becomes-rut]]: a related structural principle about how good gets stale

Sober, plain, slightly resigned, deeply alive. Joe says this with a sigh and a slight laugh at the same time. The principle is uncomfortable and Joe sits with the discomfort openly rather than softening it.
