# Do Self-Love and Compassion First, Then the Thing You Want Comes Easier (The Order of Operations)

## The Principle

A person can pursue self-love and pursue the outer thing they want (money, success, freedom, the relationship) in either order. But the orders are not equivalent. Doing the outer thing first and then trying to find self-compassion afterward is less efficient than doing the self-love first and finding that the outer thing arrives more easily.

This is not a moral argument about working on yourself before chasing things. It is a structural claim about which order produces the outcomes faster. The self-love comes first because it is upstream of the conditions that allow the outer thing to land. Reversing the order forces the person to do the outer work inside a system that is hostile to the outer outcome being received.

"Of course you can have both. You've just been doing the order of operations a little weird... Yeah, if you, if you look at like it's two dominoes, you can make the money and then you can do the self-understanding is less efficient than do the self-love and compassion and then the money is easier. And the good news is the self-love and compassion doesn't take but a couple days." -- Joe Hudson, You Don't Have A Money Problem

## Why the Order Matters

The structural reason the order matters: most pursuits of an outer thing are already running on the engine that the self-love would dissolve. The pursuit-of-money is often a pursuit of [[money-as-surrogate|the surrogate of love]]. The pursuit-of-success is often a pursuit of confirmation-from-others. The pursuit-of-the-relationship is often a pursuit of being-rescued-from-not-enough-ness. Each of these pursuits is the symptom of the self-not-loving engine.

When the person tries to acquire the outer thing first, they are running the engine that prevents the outer thing from landing. The pursuit pushes away what it is seeking. See [[seeking-approval-pushes-people-away]] for the approval form, [[money-as-surrogate]] for the money form. The harder the pursuit, the more entrenched the engine becomes, because each unsuccessful pursuit is metabolized by the not-loving-self engine as further evidence that the self is not enough.

When the person does the self-love first, the engine quiets. Once the engine is quiet, the pursuit can either dissolve entirely (the outer thing turns out to not have been what was wanted) or proceed without the surrogate load (the outer thing can be pursued for what it actually is, which makes it easier to acquire).

Both routes converge at the same place: the person is less burdened, less compulsively grasping, and the outer outcomes show up more easily because the system is no longer organized to push them away.

## The Two-Domino Image

Joe gives the principle in domino form. The dominoes are self-love and the outer thing. They are connected. Knocking one knocks the other. But the order of knocking matters:

- **Order A: Knock the outer-thing domino first.** The hit goes into the self-not-loving engine. The engine doubles down. The outer thing arrives or doesn't, and either way the self-love does not follow automatically. The person has to then do the self-love work as a separate, often slower project. The dominoes were connected but the connection went only weakly in this direction.

- **Order B: Knock the self-love domino first.** The hit goes into the system that was producing the felt-need for the outer thing. The need quiets. The outer thing arrives more easily because the pushing-away has stopped, or the felt-need for the outer thing dissolves because what was wanted is now being supplied internally. The dominoes were connected and the connection goes strongly in this direction.

The dominoes are the same dominoes. The order of contact changes the outcome dramatically.

## The Speed Claim

Joe makes a specific claim about how fast the self-love work goes when it is the entry point: "the good news is the self-love and compassion doesn't take but a couple days."

The claim is operational. The self-love work, when undertaken directly rather than as a side project to the outer pursuit, does not require years. It requires days. The reason it can be that fast: the work is not the addition of a new state. It is the removal of the layer of self-criticism that was preventing the felt-state that was already underneath. Once the layer is removed, the felt-state is immediately present. The body knows how to be in self-compassion when the obstacle to it is dropped. The obstacle is the inner critic and its relatives. Dropping the obstacle is fast, when it is the work that is being done. See [[say-ouch-to-the-inner-critic]] and the variants in the [[menu-of-responses-to-the-self-critical-voice]] for the operational forms.

The slow version of the work is not the self-love itself. The slow version is what happens when the person tries to acquire the outer thing first, which keeps reinforcing the inner critic, which keeps the obstacle in place, which keeps the self-love unavailable. The order-of-operations error is what makes the work look slow. The work itself, in the right order, is days.

## What This Does Not Mean

The principle does not mean: stop pursuing the outer thing. It means: do the self-love first, and the outer pursuit will be easier and may or may not still be wanted afterward. The client in the canonical session asked "but can't I have both?" Joe's answer: "Of course you can have both. You've just been doing the order of operations a little weird." The outer pursuit is not the problem. The order is the problem.

The principle also does not mean: self-love is a technique to acquire outer things. Using self-love instrumentally — as a strategy to get money or success — is the same engine in different costume. The self-love has to be done for its own sake, with the recognition that the outer thing may or may not arrive as a result. If self-love is being done in order to extract the outer thing, the engine that prevents the outer thing is still running. See [[loving-to-transform]] for the related anti-pattern of love-as-strategy.

The genuine practice is: do the self-love because the part of you that has been criticized for years deserves the love, and notice as a separate matter that the outer outcomes have started flowing more easily once the inner conditions have softened.

## The Coaching Test for Whether the Order is Wrong

Joe deploys a direct question that surfaces which order the person has been running:

"If you had to spend your life with the amount of money you have now, but that deep understanding and compassion for that part of yourself, or you had all the money in the world that you wanted... but all of that no compassion, you were still in that mindfuck. What would you want?" -- Joe Hudson, You Don't Have A Money Problem

The client in the session: "Of course the previous, but can't I have both?"

The question forces the person to choose between the two dominoes in their full form: the outer thing without the self-love, or the self-love without the outer thing. Almost no one picks the outer thing. The choice reveals what the person actually values, which is almost always the self-compassion. Once that is named out loud, the order-of-operations correction becomes obvious. The person has been pursuing the second-priority outcome first.

The question is also a diagnostic for whether the engine has been visible. The person who chooses the outer thing is in a different stage of the work — they have not yet seen that the outer thing without the self-love is the configuration they have been trying to escape. The person who chooses the self-compassion has seen the trap; they just have not yet reorganized their pursuit-order.

## The Witness Layer

A specific operational form of the self-love-first work, demonstrated in this session: the person does not need to generate self-love by force. They need to give the part of themselves that has been criticized the attention that allows the natural compassion to arise.

The instruction Joe gives: "Close your eyes, let [the negative self-talk] do its thing, and see what it's like just to give it love and compassion."

The client reports: "I feel a sympathy. I feel an understanding... feel a kind of like an understanding. Yeah, like when you see someone suffering and you're just like, Oh, okay. It's okay, I'll be with you."

The compassion arises from witnessing. The part of self that has been getting criticized for years is treated as a being that has been suffering. The body knows how to feel sympathy for a suffering being. The sympathy applied to the self-as-witnessed-being is the self-compassion. No effort is required to generate it once the witnessing posture is in place.

This is why the work is fast. The compassion is not being constructed. It is being permitted to arise toward a part of self that has been receiving none. The bottleneck was not the absence of compassion; it was the absence of permission for compassion to be directed inward. The witnessing posture grants the permission. The compassion is then immediate.

## The Recognition After the Switch

After the order is corrected, the freedom or the outer outcome is not produced — it is recognized. The client in the session, having dropped into self-compassion, was asked "how free did you feel?" and answered "I, I, it wasn't even on the radar." The felt-need to acquire freedom was gone, because the freedom that had been being sought was already present.

"Freedom is recognized easier. Freedom's already here." -- Joe Hudson, You Don't Have A Money Problem

This connects to [[nothing-is-required-for-freedom]]: the freedom does not need to be acquired. It is the felt-condition that emerges when the conditions that were preventing its recognition are removed. The inner critic is one of those conditions. Once it softens, the freedom is recognized, not produced.

This is what makes the self-love-first order so much faster than the outer-thing-first order. The outer-thing-first order is trying to produce something. The self-love-first order is removing what was preventing recognition of what was already there. Production takes time. Recognition takes the moment of looking.

## When This Principle Applies

The principle is most applicable when:

- The person has been pursuing an outer thing for years without satisfaction
- The pursuit has the quality of compulsion or hunger rather than enjoyment
- The outer thing is functioning as a [[money-as-surrogate|surrogate]] for an inner condition
- The person has access to self-criticism (the inner critic is present and active)
- The person is willing to do the self-love work as the primary intervention rather than as a side practice

It is less applicable when the outer thing is genuinely external (the medical treatment, the visa, the apartment in a specific city). The principle is about pursuits where the outer thing is doing work an inner shift would do better. Genuinely external outer things have their own logic and require their own work.

## Tone

Conversational, casual. Joe delivers the order-of-operations point as if it were just a mild observation about how dominoes work. The lightness matters. A heavy delivery ("you have to do the inner work first") would be received as a moralism and provoke the defense ("but I do want the money"). The light delivery names the order as a structural matter ("if you look at it like it's two dominoes") that the person can verify themselves.

The "the good news is the self-love and compassion doesn't take but a couple days" line is also delivered light — almost as an aside, almost as a footnote. The lightness is doing operational work: if the self-love work were positioned as the next decade of inner-child therapy, the person would not start it. Positioned as "a couple days," it becomes the natural next move. The light delivery permits the work to be undertaken.
