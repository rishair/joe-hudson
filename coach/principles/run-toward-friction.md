# Run Toward the Friction

## The Principle

When a team or company feels friction -- between people, between functions, between the product and the customer, between the strategy and reality -- the move that produces alignment, trust, and speed is to run at the friction immediately rather than to manage around it. The friction is where the information is. The friction is where the next solution lives. The friction is where the next layer of trust gets built. Running at it produces the company that can navigate massive change without rupturing. Running away from it produces the company that looks stable until it isn't.

The principle is the organizational form of [[embrace-intensity]]. The embrace-intensity principle operates at the individual emotional level. Run-toward-friction operates at the team and company level. The mechanism is the same. The avoided tension compounds. The approached tension dissolves into a better solution.

"We developed in a way a deep resilience in our culture and are embracing change in a way where we don't look at it from a perspective of overwhelm, but more from a perspective of, okay, hey, there is tension, let's run towards that tension." -- Johannes Langgraf, A Company Culture That Moves At The Pace of AI

## What Friction Looks Like in Practice

Friction is not abstract. It shows up in specific forms across the company. Each form is an invitation to run at it.

- **Interpersonal friction in leadership.** Product disagrees with engineering. Sales disagrees with operations. The CEO is unsure about the CTO's decision. The friction-as-information move: the disagreement is data; the work is to surface it, not to suppress it.
- **Product architecture friction.** The product has been built one way and the customer experience or development velocity is being limited by it. The friction is the system telling the team what wants to change.
- **Customer relationship friction.** A customer is unhappy or a key account is at risk. The friction signals that something in the company's offering or relationship pattern needs to evolve.
- **Strategic friction.** The plan that was made six months ago no longer fits the market. The friction is the gap between the old plan and the new reality.
- **Cultural friction.** Two parts of the company hold different values or norms. The friction shows where the alignment work needs to happen.

In each case, the body of the company is signaling where the next move is. The move is to go to the signal, not to dampen it.

"Whenever we feel that friction, we kind of immediately run towards that." -- Johannes Langgraf, A Company Culture That Moves At The Pace of AI

## The Three-Layer Argument for Why This Works

Joe gives the structural argument in three layers.

**1. The biggest problems are where the most value is.** A Spotify founder quote Johannes invokes: the value of a company is the sum of the complexity of all problems solved. The biggest, meatiest problems produce the most value when solved. Those problems are usually located where the friction is greatest. Running at the friction is running at the value.

**2. Tension stands in the way of efficiency.** When two functions cannot talk to each other -- when sales has stopped sharing with product, or engineering has stopped sharing with customer service -- the organization loses the data exchange that would have produced the optimal decision. Avoidance limits the data pool. The decision quality drops because the inputs to the decision have been pre-filtered by the avoidance.

**3. Avoided friction compounds.** The unaddressed tension does not stay the same size. It grows. The conversation that was hard last week is harder next month. By the time the conversation cannot be avoided any longer, the discharge required is much larger than what would have addressed the friction in the first place. See [[conflict-as-evolutionary-tension]] for the compounding mechanism in full.

"If you're avoiding tension, if you're avoiding your fears, if you're avoiding conflict in your organization, you're limiting the amount of data points that can help you make a better decision as an organization." -- Johannes Langgraf, A Company Culture That Moves At The Pace of AI

## How Running Toward Friction Builds Resilience

The companies that can absorb massive change without rupturing are the companies that have practiced running at small frictions before they had to run at big ones. Each small friction run-at builds the muscle. By the time the big change arrives (the AI pivot, the layoff, the company rename), the team has rehearsed the move enough that the big version feels like a familiar pattern, not a first-time event.

This is the structural source of what Johannes calls "deep resilience in our culture." The resilience is not a personality trait of the team. It is the accumulated trust that comes from many friction-run-ats together. The team has data, from prior experiences, that running at friction works. The body of the team trusts the move because the body has done it before.

The contrast: a team that has been managing around friction for years has no rehearsal for the moment when friction can no longer be managed. When the big change arrives, the team experiences it as catastrophic because they have not built the capacity to meet smaller versions of it. The team that has been embracing intensity all along experiences the same change as significant but workable.

## What This Move Requires

Running toward friction is not the same as starting fights, escalating tension, or weaponizing transparency. The move requires specific conditions to operate as principle rather than as theater.

**A trust foundation.** See [[trust-requires-conflict]]. The team that runs at friction without trust produces destruction, not alignment. The trust is the substrate that lets the friction-running produce a better solution rather than blow up the relationships. Trust gets built by running at friction successfully, but the first runs require enough baseline trust that the team can survive them.

**An orientation to seeing each other.** See [[conflict-as-evolutionary-tension]] -- the orientation toward conflict that makes it generative is "this conversation is about seeing them and seeing if they can see me," not "this conversation is about winning." Running toward friction with the wrong orientation produces battles. With the right orientation, it produces alignment.

**A leader who can stay present.** The leader who runs toward friction but cannot stay present when the team responds produces a culture of staged confrontations the leader cannot actually hold. The team learns that friction-running is performance, not real. The leader has to be able to be present with whatever the friction surfaces, including criticism of themselves, including their own uncertainty, including the things they have been avoiding. See [[leaders-calm-becomes-the-teams-calm]].

**Operational structure for the run-at.** Spontaneous friction-running can produce chaos. Companies that do this well usually have structures: regular forums where friction can surface (the RFC process, mini-councils, post-mortems), shared vocabulary that gives people permission to invoke the move (see [[open-the-drawer-as-shared-verb]]), and a cadence for running at things before they compound.

## Specific Forms the Move Takes at Ona

Several structural forms of running-toward-friction in operation at Ona.

**The Council program.** Monthly sessions where the leadership team uses a structured process to surface and work through friction. The structure makes it safe to surface what would otherwise be avoided. The shared vocabulary and the trust built over months makes the surfacing productive rather than destabilizing.

**Mini councils.** Smaller, ad-hoc versions of the council process. When friction arises between two functions, a mini council can be set up to address it directly. The structure prevents the friction from compounding because it has a designated place to land.

**RFCs as friction-surfacing mechanism.** Request-for-Comments documents that name the problem, the success criteria, and the proposed solution. Anyone can add data points. The structure surfaces the disagreements that would have stayed hidden in a verbal-only process.

**Sharing board packs with the whole company.** The transparency removes one of the most common sources of cultural friction: the team feeling that there is a parallel reality leadership is operating in. When the board pack goes to the whole company, the friction between the visible operating layer and the hidden strategic layer dissolves.

**Quarterly touch-bases.** Manager-and-employee conversations explicitly framed as "opening the drawer" with each other. The structure makes the friction-running cadence regular and personal.

These structures are not the principle. They are how the principle gets installed in a real organization. Other structures can serve the same purpose. The principle is the move; the structures are the scaffolding that makes the move repeatable.

## The Inversion This Demands of Most Leaders

Most leaders, when friction appears, reach for management tools to reduce the friction. Restructure the team. Reassign the responsibility. Move the offending person. Layer in a process. Each of these moves the friction out of the leader's immediate field of view without addressing what the friction was signaling.

The principle inverts the move. When friction appears, the question is not "how do I reduce this friction?" The question is "what is this friction telling me, and how do I run at the underlying thing?" The friction is a teacher. The avoidance moves are silencing the teacher.

The diagnostic for the inversion having happened: the leader's calendar starts to fill with the hard conversations rather than emptying of them. The leader who has installed this principle spends more time in friction than less. The friction is not gone; it has been moved into the open where it can be worked.

## What This Rules Out

This principle rules out the standard story that a "harmonious" team is a high-performing team. A team without visible friction is usually a team with extensive hidden friction. The harmony is the visible cost of the hidden friction. The high-performing team has visible friction that is being worked productively; the friction is in the open, where it can be addressed.

It also rules out the manager-as-firefighter pattern, where the leader's job is to suppress brushfires of friction as they appear. The firefighting consumes the leader's attention without addressing the structural conditions producing the brushfires. The friction-running orientation looks for the underlying source and addresses it, so the brushfires stop appearing.

## What This Move Produces

Several downstream effects compound when this principle is operating at scale.

- **Better decisions.** More data on the table produces decisions that integrate more of what the company knows.
- **Faster execution.** Decisions made with the friction worked-through do not get re-litigated underneath. The team can act because the disagreement has already been processed.
- **Deeper trust.** Each friction-running that ends with the relationship intact and the team aligned adds to the trust pool. The pool funds the next friction-running.
- **Cultural resilience.** The capacity to absorb hard things grows. By the time the genuinely hard thing arrives, the team has rehearsed the move enough that it feels normal.
- **Talent retention.** The strongest performers want to work where the truth gets surfaced. The friction-running culture keeps them. The toxic-niceness culture loses them.
- **Customer responsiveness.** The same orientation that runs at internal friction also runs at customer friction. Problems get surfaced, addressed, and resolved faster than competitors who are managing around them.

See [[disconnection-precedes-decline]] for the inverse principle: the team that stops running at friction is the team whose connection erodes and whose decline starts before the metrics show it.

## Tone

Joe delivers this principle as practical, almost engineering-language. It is not about being brave or confrontational. It is about where the value is located in an organization and what the disciplined move toward that value looks like. The framing is structural: the friction is information, the running-at is the move, the alignment is the product. The team that has internalized this no longer experiences friction as a threat. They experience it as the system telling them where to look next.
