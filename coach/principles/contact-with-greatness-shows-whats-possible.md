# Contact With Greatness Shows You What's Possible

## The Principle

Direct contact with someone who is exceptional at something opens a possibility in the witness that argument and instruction cannot open. The witness sees that the thing exists. The witness sees how it is being approached. The witness's own capacity in that domain shifts -- not because they were taught, but because they observed someone doing it.

Joe's framing comes from golf: a person who has played their whole life with people who are not very good has a particular shape of game. They play one round with someone who is genuinely great at golf, hear a few of their words, watch how they approach the shot, and their own golf game improves. Not because they were given technique, but because they saw what was possible.

"If I've been playing golf my whole life with people who suck at golf, and then one day I meet somebody who's amazingly great at golf and I play a game with them, I know that there's something possible out there. I see how they approach it. My golf game is gonna improve just by playing one game with them and hearing a couple of their words. But I also like, oh, I know how, like, I can see how they got there so I can get there too." -- Joe Hudson, The Handbook for Accidental Awakening

The principle generalizes well beyond golf. Joe applies it specifically to awakening, but it operates everywhere development happens. Watch someone be present and your presence opens. Watch someone be in their grief and your grief opens. Watch someone be in love with their life and your love opens.

## Why Contact Works

Three mechanisms operate at the same time:

**Possibility:** The witness sees that the thing exists. Before the contact, the thing was theoretical. After the contact, it is real -- it can be done, by someone, in something close to their own body and situation. The shift from theoretical to real is a structural change in the field of what the witness can move toward.

**Map:** The witness gets a partial map of how the great person got there. Not the technique, exactly -- often the technique is invisible. But the orientation, the relationship to the thing, the way the great person carries themselves. The witness picks this up below the level of cognitive instruction.

**Permission:** The witness's own attempt at the thing is no longer attempted from inside a peer group where it is unfamiliar. The example has now appeared. The attempt is supported by the fact that the thing is being done -- somewhere, by someone who can be remembered. Permission is structural, not given.

## The Awakening Application

Joe makes the application to awakening explicit. The reason being in community around someone in an awakening process matters is partly the witness effect.

"You can see it at Groundbreakers often. You can see like one person pops and then it's like a couple other people see it and they're like, oh, I can go there." -- Joe Hudson, The Handbook for Accidental Awakening

The "ricochet" pattern: one person's recognition lands in the group. Other people in the group see it land. The seeing is contagious. Their own systems pick up the possibility and the orientation, and the next several recognitions arrive faster.

This is one of the structural reasons Joe's work runs through community rather than one-on-one teaching. The community is the contact-with-greatness mechanism running continuously. People are constantly watching each other do things they did not know were possible.

"I think that also really just points to the value of doing the work in community. Because you get to see a number of people going through it from various distances. And at various stages in various ways, ways of noticing it, ways of it showing up in their lives, ways of responding to it." -- Joe Hudson, The Handbook for Accidental Awakening

## Why This Matters Beyond Awakening

The principle is not specific to awakening. It is a general teaching about how development happens.

"There's something really important, not just around awakening, but having contact with greatness. Somebody who's really exceptional at anything is a privilege that allows you to know that something's possible and then be able to shoot for it." -- Joe Hudson, The Handbook for Accidental Awakening

The implication: seek the contact. If you want to develop in a domain, find someone who is genuinely exceptional in that domain and spend time with them. Not to be taught directly. Just to watch. The watching is the transmission.

This connects to Joe's broader teaching about [[transmission]]: the substance of teaching is not the content but the way the teacher carries themselves. A teacher who has genuinely integrated the material transmits it through their presence. The student picks it up not by listening but by being around it.

## The Caveat: Watching Without Trying to Be Them

The misuse of this principle: watching the great person and trying to imitate them. This produces a copy, not a development. The witness who tries to imitate ends up performing what they observed without having the underlying capacity. The transmission requires watching without attempting to install.

The right relationship is more like absorption. The witness lets the experience of being around the great person sink in. What develops in the witness is the witness's own version of the capacity, in their own shape, on their own timetable. The great person is not the model; the great person is the proof that the territory is real.

This also disarms the comparison trap. If the witness watches someone genuinely great and tries to be them, they end up smaller. If the witness lets the contact open something in themselves, they end up larger -- in their own form, not the form of the person they watched.

## The Counter-Principle: Greatness Is Not a Caste

The principle does not say great people are a separate category. It says contact with greatness in a domain reveals a possibility. The great person was not always great. They started as a person who saw greatness in someone else and let that contact open something. The chain of contact-with-greatness goes all the way back.

This is one of the reasons Joe is careful not to install the awakened-person-as-special framing (see [[dont-identify-as-awakened]] and [[awakening-as-natural-development]]). The point of contact with someone who has had the recognition is not to elevate them. It is to use the contact to open the possibility in oneself.

## Coaching Implications

When someone is stuck in a developmental domain (emotional fluidity, presence, awakening, courage to act on what they want), the question "who can you be around who is doing this thing?" is operative. If the answer is no one, the person's options are limited. If the answer is someone, the contact itself is the work.

When someone is in a community that has people doing the work at different stages, the work has accelerated structurally. The ricochet is happening continuously. The community is the curriculum.

When someone is trying to develop in isolation, that is also possible, but slower. The map-and-possibility have to be assembled from texts and recordings rather than from direct presence. The development happens, but the timeline lengthens.

## What This Doesn't Say

It does not say teachers are unnecessary. The teacher is one form of contact with greatness. The contact is the operative thing; the formal teaching role is one container for it.

It does not say content doesn't matter. The great person is great in part because they have content to transmit. The principle is that the transmission happens through contact, not that there is no content.

It does not say you have to find one specific person. The great-person can be plural. Different domains call for different contacts. The witness builds a constellation over time.

It does not say the witness becomes a copy of the great person. The opposite: the witness becomes more themselves through the contact. The contact opens what was already there.

## Tone

Joe delivers this principle with the same casualness he uses for most of his teaching. The golf metaphor is deliberately ordinary. The principle is not framed as mystical or rare. The implication is practical: find your version of contact with greatness and the rest follows.

The tone also carries the implication that community is sufficient and necessary for this work. Not because community is required by ideology, but because the contact-with-greatness mechanism is one of the primary engines of development, and community is where it runs.
