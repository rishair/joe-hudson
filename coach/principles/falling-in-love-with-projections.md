# Fall in Love with Your Projections

## The Principle

Fighting your projections only stabilizes them. The way through projections is the same as the way through any internal pattern: welcome them, love them, even enjoy finding them.

"Fighting against your projections is only a way that increases their stability." -- Joe Hudson, Your World is a Projection

The framing as something to love comes from Brett in the source episode, and Joe immediately embraces it: "I've never thought about it as falling in love with projections, but I think that it's beautifully said."

## The Birdwatching Metaphor

The clearest image: if you go birdwatching but you do not like birds, you will not find many. If you love finding projections, you will spot them easily, including in yourself, without the dread that would otherwise make you look away.

"If you love finding a projection, it's like, oh wow, I'm projecting right now, cool. That might be useful. Also, it might be useful to do it a little bit differently or to experiment with it a little bit." -- Brett Kistler, Your World is a Projection

The shift from "projection = bad thing I have to root out" to "projection = interesting thing I have noticed" is the entire move. The hostile stance prevents seeing. The affectionate stance reveals.

Joe extends the same image: the love of finding projections also becomes proof. The more you welcome spotting them, the more clearly you see how they were operating. "It's great because that's when you say, how many birds will you find? It's like proof." -- Joe Hudson, Your World Is A Projection (2022). The affectionate stance does not just allow you to see one projection; it shows you the structure of how the projection has been organizing your life. The friendly hunt becomes its own diagnostic.

## Why Fighting Fails

A projection is a perceptual lens built on top of an unfelt emotion. Fighting the projection means resisting the perception. Resisting the perception keeps the underlying emotion unfelt. The unfelt emotion keeps generating the conditions that the projection notices. The fight is the fuel.

This is the same mechanism as [[resistance]]: what you resist, you reinforce. Specifically applied to projections: every time you catch yourself in a projection and respond with self-criticism ("I shouldn't be projecting again"), you have just generated more of the avoided emotion (shame, in this case) and made the projection more entrenched.

The trap a self-aware person hits: "Oh, I've identified a projection. That's bad. Projections are bad — which is just another way of disowning yourself." Disowning the projection is itself one more projection.

## Why Love Works

Love welcomes. Welcoming allows the underlying emotion to move. When the emotion moves and completes, the body no longer needs to generate the conditions to feel it. The projection loses its job.

This is [[love-is-the-best-change-agent]] applied specifically to projections. And [[acceptance-as-change-strategy]]: accept the projection as it is to change how it operates. Resignation says "I'm just a projecting mess, oh well." Acceptance says "I see this is here, and I welcome it, and now I can choose."

## The Practice Form

Three operations in sequence:
1. **Notice the projection.** "Oh wow, I'm projecting right now."
2. **Be glad you noticed.** Not glad you projected — glad you saw it. Cool, useful, interesting.
3. **Experiment.** Try doing it a little differently. Try a contrary action. Try just sitting with it. The projection becomes material to play with rather than evidence of failure.

## How This Lands in Different Layers

For psychological projection (triggered by another person): when you catch yourself accusing or idolizing, the welcoming version is "interesting, what part of me is showing up in that judgment?" — not "I shouldn't judge, I'm a bad coach/partner/person."

For projection onto the world: when you catch yourself recreating a pattern, the welcoming version is "of course, this is the shape my system knows" — not "I can't believe I'm doing this again."

For projection of self: when you catch yourself seeing your interior in the exterior, the welcoming version is curiosity about what is being revealed about your relationship with yourself.

For projection of I: when you sit with the question "what am I?" and feel the identity flicker, the welcoming version is staying with the flicker rather than grabbing back at a story of self. See [[what-am-i]].

## When Someone Experiences This

- Catching themselves criticizing themselves for having projections
- Building a self-improvement project around "seeing through projections"
- Feeling frustrated that they keep falling into the same perception they have already identified
- Treating projection-awareness as the next thing to be good at

The principle is for the person who has gotten the conceptual frame and turned it into a new battlefield.

## Tone

The tone Brett models and Joe affirms is playful, almost cheerful. "Oh wow, I'm projecting right now, cool." Not solemn. Not grave. The lightness is part of the medicine. A heavy approach to projections recreates the heaviness that fuels them.
