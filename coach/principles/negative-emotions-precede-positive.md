# Negative Emotions Move First, Then the Positive Ones

## The Principle

When a person begins to allow their emotions, the so-called negative emotions almost always need to move first: anger, sadness, fear. The positive emotions -- joy, bliss, peace, deep love -- arrive after, not before. This is not a moral or theoretical ordering. It is what consistently shows up in practice.

"Usually the negative emotions, almost always I'm not even sure if I've seen a case where it hasn't been the case. The negative emotions need to move first and then the positive emotions." -- Joe Hudson, Stages of Emotional Development

The specific common order: anger and sadness, then fear, then the positive ones begin to flow.

## Why This Ordering

The system has been guarding against the difficult emotions, and the muscular and intellectual structures of that guarding also block the positive emotions. See [[joy-as-matriarch]] and [[kinked-emotions]]. Releasing the guarding releases both directions, but the held-back negative material has been accumulating longer and has more pressure to move. The water that was held back flows out first.

The positive emotions have also been suppressed, often without the person knowing. Most people assume joy will appear when conditions allow, not realizing that the channel has been narrowed. The clearing of the channel happens through the difficult emotions first; only then does the channel widen enough for the positive ones to come through.

## "Positive" and "Negative" Are Provisional Labels

The labels are how society categorizes what is acceptable and what is not. They are not statements about the emotions themselves.

"What society would say, sadness, fear, and anger were the negative ones, and bliss and deep love, joy, that kind of stuff, that's called the positive ones." -- Joe Hudson, Stages of Emotional Development

Later in the developmental arc the distinctions blur. Sadness wrapped in love stops being negative in any useful sense. Excitement and fear become indistinguishable. The labels are useful early as descriptions of the resistance the person carries; they stop being useful when the emotions are flowing.

## The Often-Surprising Discovery

When people start opening to their negative emotions and the positive ones begin returning, they often discover that the positive emotions carry as much or more resistance.

"Oftentimes people don't understand that they're repressing the positive emotions... the positive ones often have as much, if not more, resistance." -- Joe Hudson, Stages of Emotional Development

The person who has been working anger for months and then feels deep joy for the first time may be shocked at how unfamiliar and even uncomfortable it feels. The body has limited capacity for intensity, and intensity is intensity regardless of valence. See [[pleasure-capacity]].

This is why the work continues after the difficult emotions have moved. The positive emotions need their own welcoming.

## Coaching Implications

When someone reports a loss of joy, do not chase the joy. Look for what is being held back among the difficult emotions. The route to joy goes through the family. See [[joy-as-matriarch]].

When someone has been doing emotional work and is now reporting unfamiliar joy or pleasure, treat this as a development to be supported, not just a relief. The positive emotions will meet resistance in their own way. Welcome them in.

When the ordering does not hold -- when positive emotions seem to be moving before the negative ones -- look closely. Often what looks like joy is excitement-flavored anxiety, or relief, or the high that precedes a crash. The principle is consistent enough that an apparent exception usually rewards closer inspection.

## Why the Order Is Stable

Why almost always negative first? The accumulated pressure is greater in the held-back direction. The body's protective patterns developed primarily against the emotions that were punished or shamed -- almost always the difficult ones in most upbringings. Releasing the protective patterns releases what was being protected against first.

The positive emotions were not punished as directly, but they got shut down as a side effect. They are not at the front of the line because they were not the original target.

## Tone

Matter-of-fact, descriptive. Joe presents this as a consistent observation rather than a rule. The ordering is given as something a person can rely on when they are early in the work.
