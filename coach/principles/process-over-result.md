# Process Over Result

## Definition

Shooting for a specific result produces worse outcomes than staying faithful to a process. This applies to conversations, business, coaching, creative work, and directing actors. Hold a clear intention. Then trust the process to deliver results you could not have designed.

This is not passivity. It is the recognition that the path to the best outcome cannot be planned in advance. The process handles what planning cannot.

## In Practice

Outcomes require time to stop. There is no end state. Show someone a snapshot of their future where they are happy and wealthy; two days later they could be broke and miserable. Which moment is "the outcome"? Attachment to outcomes is attachment to a frozen frame in a process that never stops.

"I don't believe in outcomes. The only way you can believe in an outcome is if you believe time stops." -- Joe Hudson, Impartiality -- Connection Course Series #3

Joe connects this principle to design theory. A TED Talk on design process made the point explicit: teams that stay focused on the process consistently produce better output than teams that fixate on a particular result. The process will go sideways. It will not look like progress. But the output, measured over time, is better.

"If you're shooting for a result, you won't get it. The thing you have to do is stay focused on the process and it's gonna go sideways. It's not gonna go well at times, but the output is consistently better than if you're going for a particular result." -- Joe Hudson, Introduction to VIEW (Connection Course)

The consultant Case, whose conversational ability inspired [[view]], pointed to this principle constantly. Before conversations, he would say: "I have no idea what the hell is going to happen." That was his advantage, not his limitation. Knowing what should happen would have collapsed the space for what could happen.

Living by [[view]] is a form of principled living. You commit to a set of principles (vulnerability, impartiality, empathy, wonder) because you know that consistently living by them produces results aligned with your truth. You do not need to know the result in advance. The principles carry you.

"It's very much like living principled. You're saying, here are the principles I'm living by because I know consistently if I live by them, it works out in the way that works for me and works for my deepest truth." -- Joe Hudson, Introduction to VIEW (Connection Course)

When directing actors, giving a specific desired performance produces stiff results. Giving the actor a clear objective and trusting them to find their way produces excellence. The parallel to coaching and managing teams is direct: hold the objective, release the method.

- When [[impartiality]] is hard to practice because the stakes feel too high to release control.
- When a conversation feels forced or mechanical despite good technique.

## Downstream (Where This Leads)

- Reinforces [[impartiality]]: the practice of not attaching to how the goal is reached.
- Makes [[wonder]] natural: if you do not need a specific result, you can genuinely wonder what will happen.
- Builds trust in VIEW as a reliable system rather than a gamble.
