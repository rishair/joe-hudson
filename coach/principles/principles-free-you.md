# Living By Principles Frees You

## Definition

A set of clear, durable principles is the internal structure that produces sustained empowerment. Principles remove the burden of recalculating each decision based on fear of consequence. The principles handle the calculation in advance.

"I've given myself a structure that it doesn't change very readily, you know, it takes some time to change that set of principles, but I'm going to operate in that way no matter what. And that helps me feel deeply empowered, which is strange, right? It's like a set of criteria that I live by that actually makes me feel empowered." -- Joe Hudson, Empower over Power

This is structurally identical to [[process-over-result]]: commit to a process, trust the outcomes that follow. Here the process is the set of principles for action.

## Why Principles Empower Rather Than Constrain

A principle removes the decision-by-decision negotiation with fear. If your principle is "I do not work with assholes," you do not need to weigh the billion-dollar offer from an asshole. The principle has already weighed it.

"If I have a principle that basically says I am not going to work with assholes, and somebody says, here's a billion dollars to work with an asshole, I'm going to say no. It's a set of principles. I'm not going to operate any differently than that." -- Joe Hudson, Empower over Power

The drama inside the mind drops. There is no internal political negotiation. The principle is the answer.

## Principles Are What Visionaries Have In Common

The people who change the world without resources, who keep their empowerment under pressure, share one feature: they live by a set of internal and external principles.

"If you look around at the people who you just are like, holy crap, they had—they didn't have resources, but they were empowered and they changed the world. It's something else they all have in common. They were living by a set of principles internally and externally. Not perfectly, obviously. Humans were not made perfect, but it's, you know, generally how one lives their life." -- Joe Hudson, Empower over Power

They are also the people who do not blame and do not feel subjugated. The principles handle what fear would otherwise handle.

## The Inquiry Into Values

The entire process of values inquiry serves this principle. Through inquiry, the values consolidate into an elegant structure for living. Once consolidated, the complicated what-if calculations drop away. Life becomes: "this is simply how I live, and I will accept the consequences."

"This entire process of inquiry into values is to create a more and more consolidated, elegant structure by which we live our lives so that we don't have to think about the complicated consequences and how the consequences are gonna play out of what if I say this to my boss or speak my truth here or leave this job, it's just, This is simply how I want to live, and that's—I'll accept the consequences if that's what it takes." -- Brett Kistler synthesizing Joe, Empower over Power

## Principles Apply Inside Organizations Too

Empowered organizations rest on principles, not just rules. The Pentagon war game where a small unit beat the US Army worked because principles allowed decentralized decision-making. Soldiers could be cut off from command and still know what to do, because they were operating from shared principles.

The same applies inside a single person. Empowered action under pressure depends on having principles internalized clearly enough to act without consulting the mind. See [[structure-and-empowerment]].

## What This Is Not

This is not rigidity. The principles are not rules to enforce. They are commitments to operate from. They evolve, but slowly.

This is not principle-shopping. The principles are not imported from a book and laid over the personality. They emerge from sustained inquiry into what matters and from honesty about what already does.

## Internal Drama Disappears

When you act from principle, the internal political theater drops. There is no negotiation between the part that wants to please and the part that wants to be honest. The principle holds.

"That's when all the drama in me starts disappearing... I've given myself a structure that it doesn't change very readily." -- Joe Hudson, Empower over Power

The [[fear-triangle]] runs on the absence of clear principles. Where there are no principles, the victim, savior, and bully strategies fill the void. Where principles operate, the triangle has nothing to grip.
