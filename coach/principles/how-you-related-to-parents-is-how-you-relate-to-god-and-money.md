# How You Related to Your Parents Is How You Relate to God and to Money

## The Principle

The way a person related to their parents in childhood becomes the template for how they relate to whatever inherits the "equivalent power" slot in their adult life. The most universal slots that inherit the template: God (or the spiritual / cosmic / authoritative-beyond-personal), money, the boss, the institution, the partner. The relationship with parents is not a separate domain. It is the substrate from which the relationships with all of these other forces are extrapolated.

"Oftentimes the way that we relate to our parents is the way that we relate to God, is the way that we relate to money. So we're constantly trying to get our parents' love. We're often trying to get money. Or if we feel like our authority figure was never going to save us, and usually we don't believe that God is ever going to save us." -- Joe Hudson, Joe Hudson's Story (Part I)

The principle is the direct extension of [[childhood-imprinting]] applied beyond money. Money is one popular landing place for the projection. God is another. The principle is more general than either: the original relationship with the most powerful figures in childhood is replayed onto whatever holds the equivalent power slot later.

## The Mechanism

A child's body learns its relational stance with the most powerful figures in its life. The stance is encoded at the level the imprint was installed at (the [[childhood-imprinting]] theta-state encoding). When the child grows up and leaves the original parents, the stance does not dissolve. It transfers. It needs an equivalent power to attach to.

For most adults, the equivalent powers are:

- **Money:** Has immediate practical power, is treated as scarce, can be held or owned. See [[money-as-surrogate]].
- **God / the divine / the cosmic:** Has ultimate power, is the figure of last resort, is the structure of the universe.
- **Authority figures:** The boss, the institution, the government, the spiritual teacher. See [[reframe-boss-as-customer]].
- **Partners:** The chosen intimate. Often inherits the projection of the opposite-sex parent or the same-sex parent depending on what was unmet.

Whatever stance was installed in childhood gets played out across all of these. If the parents were unreliable, money feels unreliable, God feels unreliable, the boss feels unreliable, the partner feels unreliable. If the parents were withholding, money is withheld, God is withholding, the boss withholds approval, the partner withholds intimacy. The substrate is one. The surface appearances are many.

## Joe's Specific Case: No Salvation

Joe's discovery of his own version came through his exploration of Catholicism. He took solace in the parables of Jesus as a child. The transformation idea spoke to him. The salvation idea did not.

He could not let salvation in because salvation was not in his template. His parents had not been the saving force. He had learned to be self-reliant because no one was coming to rescue him. The same posture installed in childhood made the Catholic concept of being saved structurally unavailable to him as an adult.

"Salvation wasn't in there because I was self-reliant. There's no fucking way salvation was gonna happen. There was a whole bunch of stuff that I had to go over, get over — like I'm not going to hell, that there's no heaven and hell and that kind of stuff. The idea of being saved was like, that's not happening. That couldn't even be a part of my reality." -- Joe Hudson, Joe Hudson's Story (Part I)

Salvation, as a concept, presupposes that there is a more powerful figure who will intervene. Joe's body had learned, at the level of imprint, that more powerful figures do not intervene. He could read about salvation, he could understand it intellectually, but he could not believe it experientially. The same wall that prevented him from depending on a partner prevented him from depending on God.

The transformation concept, by contrast, made sense to him because transformation does not require a savior. Transformation is something the person does — or, more accurately, something the person allows to happen by getting out of the way. Self-reliance translates cleanly onto transformation. It does not translate onto salvation.

## What This Looks Like in Other People

The pattern produces predictable parallels across domains:

- **Parent's love was conditional → God's love is conditional, money is conditional, the boss's approval is conditional, the partner's love is conditional.** The person works tirelessly for love that is always being earned and never quite arriving. See [[joes-pleasing-dad-money-story]] for one specific surface.
- **Parent was unpredictable → God is unpredictable, money behaves erratically, the boss is volatile, the partner blows hot and cold.** The person stays on guard, never settling.
- **Parent was absent → God is absent, money won't show up, the boss doesn't see them, the partner is emotionally unavailable.** The person concludes that their needs are not going to be met by any larger structure.
- **Parent was rescuing / over-involved → God is rescuing, money is the rescuer, the boss takes care of them, the partner saves them.** The person organizes their life around being rescued and resents the rescuer.
- **Parent was punitive → God is punitive, money is punishing, the boss is judgmental, the partner is critical.** The person carries internalized punishment as a constant.
- **Parent was warm and attentive → the substrate of basic safety carries through.** The person has more bandwidth to engage God, money, work, partnership without the older imprint distorting them.

The point of the principle is not that the parent's specific behavior determines adult life. The point is that the relational stance the child developed in response to the parent becomes the relational stance the adult brings to the equivalent-power figures. The behavior is not transmitted directly. The response to the behavior is.

## Why This Matters Operationally

If a person is trying to change their relationship with money, or with God, or with their boss, and they are working only at the surface — earning more, praying more, communicating more — they are working downstream of the substrate. The substrate is the original parent-child stance. Until the substrate updates, the surface adjustments will be absorbed back into the original template.

This is the operational meaning of "you can't heal the money relationship at the level of money" from [[money-as-surrogate]]. The same logic applies to God: you cannot heal the God relationship at the level of God. The relationship with the divine is being replayed from the original imprint. The work happens at the imprint, not at the relationship with the divine.

The corollary: working with the substrate often produces simultaneous shifts in all the downstream relationships. A person who heals the original parental imprint often finds that their relationship to money relaxes, their relationship to authority loosens, their relationship to partners becomes more available, and their relationship to whatever spiritual frame they hold starts to feel more reciprocal. This is not coincidence. It is the substrate updating beneath all of the downstream surfaces simultaneously.

## Boundary Conditions

- The principle is not deterministic. Adults can have qualitatively different relationships with God, money, and partnership than they had with their parents. The principle describes the default trajectory, not the locked outcome.
- The slots are culturally specific. A person who does not have a relationship with money in the modern sense (a child in a non-monetary economy) will not have the money slot. They will still have an equivalent-power slot; the content fills with whatever is the powerful presence in that culture.
- The principle holds even for non-religious people. The "God slot" fills with whatever the person treats as ultimately authoritative — science, the universe, fate, the market, history. The relational stance to whatever sits there will mirror the original parental template.
- The principle is not a recipe. Knowing that your relationship with money mirrors your relationship with your mother does not, by itself, change either relationship. The work is to feel the original imprint, not to analyze it.
