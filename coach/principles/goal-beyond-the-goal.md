# Focus Through the Goal

## Definition

Having a goal beyond the goal makes the original goal more likely to be hit. The mind that is aimed at a target alone has the target as its ceiling. The mind that is aimed past the target has the target as a waypoint, which makes the target easier to reach.

"Having a goal that's beyond the goal that you have makes the goal more likely to be gotten." -- Joe Hudson, The Art of Accomplishment

## The Tennis Quarter Demonstration

The clearest demonstration of this principle is the [[tennis-quarter]] story. Joe's college girlfriend Kate was a good tennis player. She would serve and try to hit a basket sitting on the court, and would land the ball in the basket 2 or 3 times out of 5. One day her coach removed the basket and put a quarter where the basket had been sitting. He told her to serve and hit the quarter. She did not hit the quarter once, but she would have hit the basket every single time, because every ball landed within the area the basket had covered.

Aiming past the basket (at the quarter) produced more precision than aiming at the basket itself. The original goal was achieved as a byproduct of aiming past it.

## How It Applies in Business

The same dynamic operates in business and life. Focusing only on the goal of earning a million dollars makes you less likely to earn the million dollars. Having a goal beyond that (being the best in your category, building something of beauty, serving customers in a particular way) makes the million dollars more likely.

"Whether it be being the best company in the world, or whether it means beating the competition, or whether it means trying to change the world for the better through environmental solutions, or whether it means great customer service, whatever it is, having a goal that's beyond the goal that you have makes the goal more likely to be gotten." -- Joe Hudson, The Art of Accomplishment

The mechanism: the larger goal organizes attention, choices, and effort around something that is not just hitting a number. The work becomes oriented to a bigger reason. The number gets hit because everything else got coherent.

## The Connection to Consciousness

The goal beyond the goal also addresses the consciousness question. If the only target is the number, the consciousness of getting there is mercenary. If the target is something larger (quality, service, beauty, impact), the consciousness of getting there is aligned with that larger thing. The consciousness gets transmitted into the work. See [[consciousness-is-the-product]].

## What Someone Experiences

The signal that you are aimed only at the goal: every action is justified by "does this get me to X." Choices feel transactional. The work has the texture of grinding.

The signal that you are aimed past the goal: actions are evaluated by whether they serve the larger orientation. Choices feel coherent. The work has the texture of building.

The reorientation: ask what is the goal beyond the goal. Why does the goal matter? What does the goal serve? Aim attention there. Notice the goal itself becomes easier.
