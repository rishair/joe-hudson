# Enjoyment Gauges Surrender to the Call

## The Principle

Enjoyment is not the goal. Enjoyment is the instrument that tells you how deeply you are surrendering to the call. The "call" is the internal pull toward something the deeper self is moving toward, whether or not the surface mind can articulate it. The more aligned the surface action is with that pull, the more enjoyment arises. The less aligned, the more friction, even when the surface action is something the mind says should be enjoyable.

This is distinct from hedonism. Hedonism is the pursuit of pleasure as the end. The principle here is the use of enjoyment as the signal that you are following something larger than the pursuit of pleasure. The enjoyment is the byproduct of alignment, not the target.

"My enjoyment is the way that I get to gauge how deeply I'm surrendering." -- Joe Hudson, The Business Behind our Courses

## The Mechanism

There is a pull. Joe names it variously as the deeper call, the gravity of internal exploration, the gravity of divinity or oneness, the call of God. The naming is less important than the recognition: there is a directional impulse in the system that is not the surface mind's preferences.

When the person acts in alignment with that pull, enjoyment arises. The body relaxes, the work flows, the day moves with momentum. When the person acts against the pull, friction arises. The body braces, the work drags, the day feels effortful in a way that has nothing to do with the actual difficulty of the task.

The instrument is the enjoyment itself. Track the enjoyment, and you are tracking the alignment.

"My enjoyment is a way for me to directly get in touch with that call, that thing that's pulling me, the gravity of my internal exploration, the gravity of divinity or oneness, whatever you want to call that thing, God." -- Joe Hudson, The Business Behind our Courses

## The Baby Metaphor

The image Joe uses: a baby surrenders to a cry. The cry is not the baby's choice. The baby does not deliberate about whether to cry. The cry arises and the baby surrenders to it. The cry comes through the baby because the system is moving toward something.

The adult version is similar but obscured. The pull is there. The system is moving toward something. The mind interrupts, deliberates, evaluates, asks whether the pull is reasonable, asks what others will think. The deliberation produces friction. The friction is felt as the absence of enjoyment.

"It is an act of surrender. So it's like I'm surrendering to the call, just like a baby surrenders to a cry. Like there's a movement that's happening." -- Joe Hudson, The Business Behind our Courses

## Why This Is Not Hedonism

Hedonism would say: do what feels good. The principle here is more demanding. It says: track what produces enjoyment in the felt body sense, and let that be the data about where the deeper alignment is. This is harder than hedonism because the felt enjoyment can be subtle, can be obscured by surface preferences, and can require honesty about what is actually arising.

The hedonistic version chooses dessert. The aligned version notices that the third cookie is no longer enjoyable, that the body has shifted to a different signal, and stops. The hedonistic version chases the high. The aligned version follows the deeper call, which may produce hours of intense work that the surface mind would not have chosen.

"It sounds like that's a little bit hedonistic almost, but it's not quite that way in the fact that what I've learned over time is that when I'm following the deeper call, there's more enjoyment in my life." -- Joe Hudson, The Business Behind our Courses

The test is durability. Hedonistic pursuits produce a spike and then a crash. Alignment with the call produces a sustained quality of enjoyment, including through the difficult parts of the path.

## The Business Application

Joe describes his own business decisions through this lens. The motivation is not "I want to change the world." That framing was alive in him two or three years before and is no longer. What is alive is the felt enjoyment of doing the work, of seeing the breakthroughs, of building something beyond himself.

"Maybe two or three years ago, I would have said, I really want to bring this out to people because it creates happiness, and I want to change the world. And that's really not alive in me anymore. It's far more alive in me. It's just this is something I really enjoy. I get off on." -- Joe Hudson, The Business Behind our Courses

The "change the world" framing was a should-shaped wrapper around the deeper pull. The deeper pull is the enjoyment itself. When the wrapper is dropped, the work becomes lighter and the decisions become clearer.

## What This Reframes

This reframes several common questions:

**"What is my purpose?"** Becomes: what produces durable enjoyment when I follow it? Track that. The purpose reveals itself in the pattern, not in advance.

**"Should I do this?"** Becomes: when I move toward this, does the body relax or brace? The body knows before the mind.

**"Am I just being selfish?"** Becomes: am I following the deep enjoyment or the surface preference? The deep enjoyment is selfish in the sense that it serves the self, but the self being served is the larger self that includes others. See [[selfishness-is-the-engine-of-unity]].

**"What am I trying to accomplish?"** Becomes potentially: nothing, in the sense of an end-state. The accomplishment is the surrender. Goals exist but they are not the point.

"I'm really good with things not working because I'm not attached to, tied to, my value isn't created from the business outcomes." -- Joe Hudson, The Business Behind our Courses

## Distinguishing Real Enjoyment From Surface Pleasure

The risk: surface pleasure can be mistaken for the deeper enjoyment. The mind says "this feels good" about cocaine, about social media, about being right in an argument. These produce immediate sensation but not durable alignment.

The diagnostic is the aftermath. The deeper enjoyment leaves the system more open, more available, more rested. The surface pleasure leaves the system depleted, contracted, requiring more.

This is one of the places where this principle works in concert with [[wants-deepen-as-you-follow-them]]. The surface enjoyment is the first layer. Following it into the body, listening for what it actually wants, often reveals a deeper want underneath. The deeper want produces durable enjoyment when followed. The surface want produces a momentary spike.

## In Coaching

When a coachee asks "what should I do with my life," the standard moves include:

- What are you enjoying right now in your life that you have not allowed yourself to count as data?
- What activities leave you more open versus more depleted?
- Where in your current life is there friction that feels like it has nothing to do with the actual task?
- What is your body's first response when you imagine doing this versus that?

The questions are not about figuring out the right answer. They are about restoring access to the instrument that already knows.

## Tone

Joe describes this in soft, slow language. The principle is held with reverence, not assertion. He is naming something he has discovered through following it, not arguing it as a position. The tone invites the listener to test it for themselves rather than to accept it.
