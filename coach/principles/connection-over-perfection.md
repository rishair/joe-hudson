# Connection Over Perfection

## Definition

Connection produces better results than perfection. This applies to products, conversations, businesses, and internal self-relationship. Neurochemicals propel humans to connect, not to be perfect. Working with this design rather than against it produces both better output and deeper satisfaction.

Connection is the acceptance of people and things as they are in the moment. It is the surface area of awareness. Every calculation, every comparison, every "how could this be better" reduces that surface area and therefore reduces connection.

"Connection is basically, it's like the surface area of our awareness. And we take away surface area when we start looking for things that can be better or things that are different." -- Joe Hudson, Connection over Perfection

## In Practice

The [[spaghetti-tower-experiment]] demonstrates this concretely: kindergartners beat CEOs at building structures because they iterate (connect with the materials and each other) rather than plan toward a perfect design. Adding a single connector (administrative assistant) to the CEO group reverses the result.

In product development: sell before you build. Connection with the customer reveals what they will buy. Planning the perfect product in isolation reveals what you imagine they will buy.

In sales: the best results come from asking questions and connecting with what the person actually needs, not from perfecting a pitch. The 30-rejections exercise (tell an artist to collect 30 rejections; after 10 calls he had 3 gallery representations) works because it shifts the mindset from perfection to connection.

In self-relationship: the ability to connect with others mirrors the ability to love and accept oneself. "The more I can love every aspect of myself, the more I can love every person I come across as they are." -- Joe Hudson, Connection over Perfection

## Iteration Is Connection

Iteration is a connected process. It is what life does. Evolution does not evolve toward a perfect end. Trying to reach a perfect end pulls you out of the flow of life and away from the natural energy that drives productivity.

"If I'm just iterating and I'm learning and growing, that is a connected experience. That's what life does. It evolves. Like, it doesn't evolve to a perfect end." -- Joe Hudson, Connection over Perfection

## Connection as Bottom Line

When people adopt connection as their bottom line (measuring their daily level of felt connection rather than a performance metric), the effect is substantial. The practice is to feel more connected each day, not to achieve a target. The trick is not to try to get there, because trying to get there is a form of disconnection.

Connection is asymptotic. You get closer and closer but never arrive. There is no perfect connection, just as there is no perfect anything. This is the feature, not the limitation.

## The Fear Underneath

Perfection is fear-based. Connection is allowing-based. Perfection creates a finite game (achieve the standard or fail). Connection is an infinite game (keep playing, keep iterating). Fear of imperfection, fear of judgment, fear of being wrong all push toward perfection and away from connection.

"The idea of perfection is really a fear-based idea. The idea that you have to be perfect, that you have a right answer, that there's the right way to do it, that's all fear-based, and fear does not make great decisions." -- Joe Hudson, Connection over Perfection

## What Perfection Actually Points To

The things humans perceive as most perfect (a flower, a landscape, an ecosystem, a great product) are all expressions of connection, not of manufacturing precision. What looks like perfection from the outside is connection from the inside.

"If you close your eyes and you think of the things that you feel are most perfect in the world, those are also things that are deeply connected." -- Joe Hudson, Connection over Perfection
