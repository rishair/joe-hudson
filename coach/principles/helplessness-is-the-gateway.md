# Helplessness Is the Gateway to Empowerment

## Definition

Feeling helplessness does not make you more helpless. Feeling helplessness makes you more capable. The avoidance of the feeling of helplessness is what produces disempowerment. Moving through the helplessness is what produces empowerment.

"Feeling helpless doesn't make you more helpless. Feeling helpless makes you more capable. And it's so counterintuitive, but if you do it, you know it." -- Joe Hudson, Empower over Power

## The Mechanism

Most decisions are made to avoid an emotional state. Helplessness is one of the most-avoided states. The strategies for avoiding it (the [[fear-triangle]] roles, the pursuit of power over others) all begin where the willingness to feel helplessness ends.

"So much of our decision-making process is based on trying to avoid an emotional state. The emotional state of helplessness is one of the ones underlying most of our avoidance." -- Joe Hudson, Empower over Power

When the helplessness is felt fully, the avoidance becomes unnecessary. What looked like a trap becomes the doorway. Capacity returns.

## What the Helplessness Is Made Of

The helplessness is the blame and shame felt all the way through. When you stop telling yourself the story of who is doing this to you (blame) or what is wrong with you (shame), what remains is the raw feeling of being unable to change the situation. That feeling is the helplessness.

"It's that helplessness is the feeling of that blame and shame felt all the way through that we don't want to feel." -- Joe Hudson, Empower over Power

The fear underneath the [[fear-triangle]] is the fear of feeling that helplessness. The triangle exists to keep the helplessness out of awareness.

## Why It Works

This is an instance of the broader pattern [[fear-is-about-feeling]]: fear of an outcome is never about the event itself, it is about what will have to be felt if the event happens. Once the feeling has been felt, the fear loses its grip.

Helplessness is the substrate emotion under many other fears. Fear of losing a job, a partner, a reputation, a future is usually fear of the helplessness that would follow. Feel the helplessness in advance and the constraint dissolves.

## In Practice

When someone is gripping control, the coaching path is not to give them more strategies for control. It is to invite them into the helplessness they are working so hard to avoid.

When someone is in the [[fear-triangle]] (victim, savior, bully), the coaching path is not to choose a better role. It is to feel the helplessness underneath any of them.

When someone has had a breakthrough and now grips it ("how do I keep it?"), the helplessness about losing it is what needs to be felt. See [[quit-hope-lose-cycle]].

The mistake is to try to think your way to empowerment, or to perform empowerment as a posture. Empowerment arrives on the other side of felt helplessness, not as a replacement for it.

## The Counterintuitive Direction

People reach for empowerment by trying to feel less. The actual direction is to feel more. The feeling that the protective strategies are organized around is precisely the feeling that needs to come through.

This is why power-seeking does not produce empowerment. Power is the strategy to avoid feeling helpless. Empowerment is the result of feeling it.
