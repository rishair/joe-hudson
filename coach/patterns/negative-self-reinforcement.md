# Negative Self-Reinforcement

## Definition

Every time you think about the thing you are avoiding, you punish yourself. This associates the thing with pain, which makes you avoid it more, which creates more self-punishment. The loop tightens. Unlike a hot frying pan that you drop immediately, this is an AC current that makes you grip harder.

"Every time you think about doing the project, you're giving yourself a negative reinforcement. So why the fuck would you wanna do it, right? Think about it on a mammalian level. A dog comes and every time it sees you, you kick it in the face. It doesn't wanna see you anymore." -- Joe Hudson, If You Can't Love the Feeling, Love the Resistance

## When Someone Experiences This

Procrastination is the most common expression. The person wants to do the thing. Every time they think about doing it, they criticize themselves for not having done it. The criticism makes the task feel worse. The worse it feels, the harder it is to approach.

The same mechanism appears in habits, addictions, and grief. Joe describes a woman who lost her father as a child. Every time she thought about her father, it hurt. So she stopped thinking about him. This produced denial, unfelt emotions, and rigidity. The pain of remembering was unconsciously reinforced until the entire topic became unapproachable.

In Joe's own experience with a pot habit in his twenties, the critical inner voice grew louder with each use. The more self-abuse, the more desire to escape the self, the more pot. "The more that I was abusing myself about it, the more I wanted to escape myself, the more I smoked the pot." -- Joe Hudson, If You Can't Love the Feeling, Love the Resistance

## The Fix

Reverse the reinforcement. Instead of punishing yourself every time you think about the avoided thing, envision yourself enjoying it. Positively reward the approach rather than negatively reinforcing it.

"Procrastination can be really easily solved if every time you think about the problem, you then envision yourself enjoying the shit outta doing it. Instead of, why haven't I done that yet?" -- Joe Hudson, If You Can't Love the Feeling, Love the Resistance

This is not visualization as a trick. It is interrupting the automatic association between the task and self-punishment. The deeper fix is to address the underlying unfelt emotion. See [[feeling-the-unfelt-emotion]]. Joe's pot habit dissolved not through willpower but through running toward the sadness and anger underneath. Once felt, the habit lost its appeal entirely.
