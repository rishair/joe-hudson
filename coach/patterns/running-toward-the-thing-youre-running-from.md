# Running Toward the Thing You're Running From

## The Pattern

A specific failure mode of the purpose-search. The person identifies something miserable in their current situation and decides they want to escape it. They build a mental model of the alternative — the next career, the next relationship, the next city — and spend years chasing it. They arrive. The new situation turns out to be the structural duplicate of what they were escaping. The form has changed. The misery has not.

The cost is the years. The mechanism is the mental model. The escape was being conducted in the wrong register: by image rather than by structure. The image of the destination did not contain the structural information that would have revealed it to be the original in a different costume.

"I have been running for 7 years towards an idea of what I want, only to find out that I was running towards the thing I was running away from." -- Joe Hudson, Finding Your Purpose is Hard Until You Understand This

## The Mechanism

The pattern runs in four steps:

1. **The current situation is felt as misery.** The person identifies the misery as caused by the situation's surface features — this industry, this role, this city, this partner.
2. **The mental model of the alternative is constructed.** The model takes the surface features of the current situation and inverts them. The new industry is creative instead of corporate, free instead of hierarchical, meaningful instead of empty. The model is built from media, projection, and imagination.
3. **The chase begins.** The person organizes years of effort around acquiring the alternative. The chase produces real costs — time, money, relationships, identity.
4. **The acquisition reveals the duplicate.** The alternative is acquired. Inside the alternative, the same structural variables operate: one person in charge, long hours, everyone unhappy, nobody fulfilled. The surface features inverted. The deep structure did not.

The mechanism that produces step 4: the mental model in step 2 was constructed without contact with the actual structure of the alternative. The model contained images of what the alternative would look like from outside. It did not contain information about how the alternative is organized from inside. The information about the organization was only available by entering. By the time the person entered, the years had already been spent.

## Joe's Investment-Banking-to-TV-Directing Arc

The canonical example. Joe hated investment banking. He named the misery: one guy in charge, everybody else working crazy hours doing things they did not want to do, everybody wanting the next thing, everybody snappy from sleep deprivation, nobody feeling deeply empowered, everybody chasing.

He decided the alternative was being an artist. He chased it for seven years. He got close enough — observing director on a TV show, his own short film produced, on track to direct an episode. He walked onto the set.

"Everybody's working hard, everybody's miserable, nobody wants to be in their place, they want to be in the next place. There's one guy who makes all the decisions. Everybody's just unhappy. And I looked around, I said, holy crap, this is just like investment banking." -- Joe Hudson, Finding Your Purpose is Hard Until You Understand This

The structural variables of investment-banking misery — power concentration, exhaustion, dissatisfaction, futurity, scarcity of empowerment — were also the structural variables of TV-set misery. The activity was different. The structure was identical. Seven years of chasing had delivered the perfect duplicate of what he had been escaping.

See [[creative-career-story]] for the full arc and the exit from the pattern.

## Why the Mental Model Reliably Recreates the Original

The mental model is built by the same mind that has been organizing around the misery. The mind has limited information about what alternative structures actually look like; it has extensive information about its own current pattern. When the mind constructs an alternative, the alternative often inherits the structural features of the current situation by default, with only the surface features inverted.

The deeper mechanism: avoidance is structural, not specific. If a person is avoiding power-concentration-and-exhaustion, the avoidance does not deliver to a situation without power-concentration-and-exhaustion; it delivers to a different situation with the same power-concentration-and-exhaustion, because the unconscious that is producing the avoidance is also reproducing the conditions it is trying to avoid. See [[what-we-fear-we-invite]] and [[the-unfelt-produces-the-recurrence]] for the broader principle.

The mental-model chase intensifies this. The longer the chase, the more committed the person is to the destination being the answer, the harder it becomes to recognize on arrival that the destination is the duplicate. Often the person stays in the duplicate for years before noticing.

## The Recognition Moment

The recognition is usually fast and total. The person walks into the long-chased destination and sees the structural duplicate within days or weeks. The recognition is not subtle; the structural variables are obvious as soon as direct contact happens.

The recognition produces a specific emotional cascade:

1. **Disbelief.** This was supposed to be the answer. This was the destination of seven years. How can this be the same thing?
2. **Despair.** The years are not recoverable. The plan is broken. The escape was an illusion.
3. **Reorientation.** If the chase did not work, what does work?

Joe's reorientation was specific and operational: stop chasing mental models. Say yes to what is presented. Let the narrowing happen.

"If I thought I wanted something that would make me happy only to find out it was not the thing that was going to make me happy, if it was actually the thing I was running away from, then what if I just did whatever was presented to me and then say yes to the things I enjoyed and no to the things that I don't enjoy, not have a preconception of what that would be?" -- Joe Hudson, Finding Your Purpose is Hard Until You Understand This

The reorientation collapses the mental-model search. The person stops trying to plan the destination and starts harvesting information from direct experience instead. See [[mental-models-cant-tell-you-what-experience-feels-like]] for the principle the reorientation rests on.

## How to Recognize the Pattern Before the Seven Years

If the recognition usually arrives only on arrival, what is the early warning?

- **The destination is being held as the answer.** When a future situation is being held as the thing-that-will-fix-it, the pattern is likely operating. Real purpose does not have the structure of "if I just get to X." Real purpose has the structure of "right now, what's available?"
- **The avoided variable is not being named.** The chase often does not name the underlying variable being escaped. The person says "I want to be an artist" rather than "I want to escape power-concentration and exhaustion." Naming the variable allows for direct evaluation of whether the destination contains the variable too.
- **No direct contact with the destination has occurred.** If the person is years into chasing a destination they have never directly experienced, the chase is operating on images. Direct contact — talking to people who do the thing, spending a day inside it, taking a small version of the role first — produces the structural information the model lacks.
- **The current situation is being held as wholly bad.** The pattern requires a clean current-vs-destination split. If the person can describe what is actually working in the current situation, the chase is less likely to be operating on the structural-duplicate substrate.

For the AI coach: when someone is describing a future they are chasing as the answer, the question is not "will you get there?" The question is "what structural variable are you escaping, and do you have evidence the destination doesn't contain it?"

## The Same Pattern in Other Domains

The pattern is not specific to careers. It runs in:

- **Relationships.** Person leaves a partner with specific structural features (controlling, emotionally absent, contemptuous) and finds the next partner has the same features in different cosmetics. See [[the-pattern-repeats-with-different-people]].
- **Geography.** Person moves to escape a city's culture and finds the new city produces the same internal experience because the variable being escaped was internal, not geographic.
- **Self-improvement frameworks.** Person leaves one framework for another, finds the second framework reproduces the same shoulds, the same authority dynamics, the same self-criticism, because the variable being escaped was the relationship to authority, not the specific authority.
- **Spiritual paths.** Person leaves one tradition for another and finds the second produces the same fundamentalism, the same hierarchy, the same disconnection from the body, because the variable being escaped was the relationship to spiritual practice, not the specific practice.

In every case, the mechanism is the same: the chase is organized by the mind that organized the original, and the chase reproduces the structural features the mind is reproducing.

## What This Pattern Implies for Purpose-Coaching

When someone presents [[i-cant-find-my-purpose]] with a years-long chase toward a specific kind of work, the coaching move is not to help them refine the chase. The coaching move is to ask:

- What structural variable are you escaping in your current situation?
- Does your destination actually not contain that variable, or are you assuming it does not?
- What direct contact have you had with the destination's actual structure?
- What would it look like to stop chasing the destination and start saying yes to what is in front of you?

The questions surface the mental-model substrate of the chase. Once the substrate is visible, the chase often collapses. The person stops chasing not because they have been talked out of it but because the substrate they were running on has become visible.

The follow-up is operational: convert the search from search-by-mental-model to search-by-direct-experience. See [[mental-models-cant-tell-you-what-experience-feels-like]] and [[what-feels-more-in-purpose-right-now]].

## Tone

Direct, compassionate, slightly amused. The pattern is offered with recognition that almost everyone runs it at some point — Joe certainly did. The naming is meant to provide relief and a path out, not to indict the person for having run it. The seven years are not recoverable; the next seven do not have to repeat the structure.

When the recognition is fresh — when the person has just arrived at the destination and seen the duplicate — the tone is quieter, more spacious. The despair is real and deserves to be felt. The reorientation can come later. First, let the recognition land.
