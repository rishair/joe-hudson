# Three Signals of an Unfelt Emotion

## The Pattern

When an emotion is unfelt, the system tries to find ways to manage what it will not feel. Three observable signals reliably indicate that an emotion is being avoided. Any one of them is sufficient evidence.

1. **Recursive thought loops.** The same thought keeps returning. It does not resolve, no matter how many times it is reviewed.
2. **Lack of clarity in a pending decision.** A decision needs to be made and the person cannot get clear, even with sufficient information.
3. **Judgment of another person.** A persistent negative read on someone the person cannot shake.

"If you notice that you keep on looping on the same thought, it means there's an emotion that you're not feeling. If you are not clear about a decision you need to make, then there's an emotion you're not feeling. If you are judgmental towards another person, there's an emotion you're not feeling." -- Joe Hudson, Feel over Figure

## Why Each Signal Works

### Recursive Loops

A thought loop is the mind trying to think its way past a felt experience that wants to come through. The intellect cannot do the feeling's job. So it keeps running the same circuit. The loop will not break until the emotion underneath moves.

A common surface form: "Should I do X or not do X?" running repeatedly with no resolution despite no new information. The decision is not stuck because of insufficient data. It is stuck because the emotion underneath the data has not been felt.

### Lack of Clarity in Decisions

This is the externalized version of the recursive loop. Clear decisions require clean emotional data. When an emotion is being avoided, the data is contaminated by the avoidance. The person cannot tell what they want because the wanting is mixed with the not-wanting-to-feel. See [[decisions-are-emotional]].

Clients who learn this signal often report recognizing it retroactively in years of stuck decisions. Carla Piñeyro Sublett, a CMO who suppressed emotion for decades as a survival strategy in corporate America, describes how she eventually noticed the pattern:

"I started to realize that decision-making became difficult. I started to loop and get stuck. And I learned through this work that that was a sign of emotion that needed to be moved. And it was extremely clearing for me once I had let myself have access, once I learned to have access to things like fear and grief and anger. It was immensely clarifying, and it enabled me to make better decisions." -- Carla Piñeyro Sublett, Finding Compassion in Self-Indulgence

The recognition matters: the difficulty in deciding was not a deficiency in her analytic capacity. It was the body trying to push an unfelt emotion through. Once she gave the emotion access, the decision clarified. The clarification was not the result of more thinking. It was the result of less suppression.

### Judgment of Another Person

Judgment is the most relational of the three signals and often the hardest to recognize. The mind frames the judgment as accurate perception: "they really are uptight." But every judgment that will not release is reliably traceable to an emotion the judger does not want to feel.

"If I judge you for being angry, I don't want to feel out of control. If I judge you for being angry, it's because I don't want to feel the potential that I have to lose you. If I judge you for being uptight, it's 'cause I don't want to feel controlled or I don't want to feel rigid." -- Joe Hudson, Feel over Figure

The judgment is the system's way of pushing the unfelt emotion out into the world. See [[unfelt-emotion-leaks]]. The judgment is the leak.

## A Fourth Signal: Binary Thinking

Joe names a related signal: when the mind frames a situation as binary ("I either buy the car or I don't"), there is fear underneath that is not being felt. The binary frame is the cognitive shape of avoided fear.

"Whenever you're in that binary black or white thinking, then you know that there's some fear there that's not being felt." -- Joe Hudson, Feel over Figure

See [[binary-thinking-signals-fear]] for full treatment.

## How to Use These Signals

The signals are reads. When any one is present, the coaching path is not to engage the surface content. It is to ask what emotion is being avoided.

- For a recursive loop: "What might you feel if you let the loop drop?"
- For a stuck decision: "What would you feel if you made the wrong choice?"
- For a judgment: "What might you feel if this person were not the way you say they are?"

The work is feeling the emotion, not solving the content. Once the emotion moves, the loop drops, the decision clarifies, or the judgment dissolves.

## When Someone Experiences This

- Caught in the same thought spiral for days
- Cannot decide despite having had weeks to think
- Cannot stop disliking a person whose presence somehow disturbs them
- Framing every choice as a binary
