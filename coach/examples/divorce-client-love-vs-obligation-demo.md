# The Divorce Client Who Felt the Love Disappear (Rapid-Fire Coaching Demo)

## The Situation

A rapid-fire coaching session. The client presents that he is "on the cusp of everything I could want and hope for" but cannot move forward. Patterns of obligation and responsibility — "intertwined with fear and a scarcity mindset" — are in the way. His specific surface question: why can't he finish his divorce? But he names the same patterns operating in work and elsewhere.

The session is two minutes long. Inside those two minutes, Joe demonstrates the principle that [[obligation-cuts-off-love]] in the client's own body, and the client feels the trade.

## The Sequence

The exchange opens with body location.

- **Joe:** And where does obligation sit in your body?
- **Client:** Chest. Upper arms.
- **Joe:** Feel it for a minute. And what happens if you're just like — if you see that as the part of you that never got loved? And love it.

The client cannot quite get to loving it.

- **Client:** I can get to acceptance. It's hard to get to loving it.

Joe immediately mirrors the report back as a hypothesis about the client's entire life:

- **Joe:** Is that what you're doing every time you accept responsibility for somebody else? You get to acceptance, but you don't actually get to love them?
- **Client:** Yeah, I think that's, that's fair. I can accept myself. I'm not sure I can get to loving myself.

The mirror lands. The client accepts the framing.

Now Joe runs the live demonstration. See [[feel-the-love-then-feel-obligation]] for the move as a reusable instrument.

- **Joe:** So do me a favor. Get in contact with the love that you once had for your wife... maybe you still have some. Yeah, tell me when you feel it.

The client signals he has found it.

- **Joe:** Okay, now I want you to feel responsible for her happiness.
- **Client:** That's easy.
- **Joe:** What happened to the love?
- **Client:** Disappeared, dissipated, retreated deep into a—
- **Joe:** So you stopped loving her to take responsibility for her is what I'm hearing you say. Motherfucker.

The client tries to argue back into a guess. Joe shuts down the intellect and insists on the felt experience:

- **Client:** I never had it heard it said that way. I guess that's accurate. My first response would be is—
- **Client:** oh wait, I don't want to guess.
- **Joe:** I want you to actually feel it. This is an emotional experience, okay? So it's not a guess. It's like every time you took responsibility, acted out of obligation it stopped the love that you felt for her.
- **Client:** Yeah.

The "yeah" is the recognition landing in the body. The session is essentially complete. The client has felt the trade he has been making for years.

## What Makes This Example Useful

The example compresses several teachings into a single short exchange:

1. **The acceptance-vs-love distinction.** The client says he can get to acceptance but not to loving. Joe immediately reflects this as the structure of his entire pattern of responsibility-taking — accepting people without loving them. See [[acceptance-is-not-love]].

2. **The somatic location of obligation.** Asking where obligation sits in the body grounds the entire conversation in the felt sense. Chest and upper arms is the somatic data that allows the rest of the move to work.

3. **The unloved part as the source.** Joe links the obligation feeling to "the part of you that never got loved." The obligation is not just a habit; it is the residue of an unloved part attempting to manage relationships.

4. **The live trade demonstration.** Find the love, add the obligation, watch the love disappear. The move is described in [[feel-the-love-then-feel-obligation]].

5. **Refusing the intellectual escape.** When the client tries to make a guess, Joe insists on the somatic experience. "This is not a guess." The refusal to let the conversation go conceptual is part of the move.

6. **The single-word reflection that lands.** "Motherfucker." The profanity is doing work. It marks the surprise of the recognition as it lands in Joe's own response. It is also short enough that the client does not have to track a long sentence while the felt shift is happening.

## What Joe Did Not Do

- He did not ask the client to stop being obligated. The teaching is recognition, not prescription. See [[obligation-cuts-off-love]] for why the prescription is not the principle.
- He did not explain the theory before the demonstration. The demonstration produces the understanding.
- He did not offer a path forward. The session ends at the recognition.
- He did not console the client. The motherfucker reflection is sharp, not soft. Joe trusts that the felt experience itself is enough to be working with.
- He did not let the client off the hook when he tried to "guess." The integrity of the move depends on staying in the body.

## What This Reveals About Joe's Style

The session is a clean example of what Joe means by [[rapid-fire-coaching-impulse-check|rapid-fire coaching]]. Two minutes. One demonstration. One recognition. The client is left with a felt experience that will live in his body for the foreseeable future and will surface every time he is tempted to take responsibility for his ex-wife's happiness as a substitute for loving her. No long conversation was needed.

This is also a clean example of [[pucker-and-still-say-it]]. Joe's reflection ("Motherfucker") is sharp, vulnerable in its surprise, and unprotected. He is not performing the calm coach. He is being affected by what the client just reported, and he lets the client see that.

## When Joe Reaches for This Example

Use this example when:

- Teaching the principle that obligation cuts off love and the listener needs a concrete demonstration
- Showing how to compress a teaching into a live experience rather than explaining it
- Demonstrating how to refuse the client's escape into intellectualization
- Illustrating the chest/upper-arms somatic location of obligation (which is a common but not universal location)
- Modeling the integration of body location, recognition, and a sharp reflection in a short coaching window

## The Implicit Concern Underneath

The surface question was "why can't I finish the divorce?" The implicit concern was not actually about the divorce. It was about the trade he has been making for the entire marriage: accepting responsibility for his wife instead of loving her. The divorce cannot be finished because finishing the divorce requires loving her enough to let her go cleanly, and he has not been loving her — he has been accepting her. He is stuck because the felt love is not there to power the clean exit.

This is why the divorce-specific question dissolved into the love-obligation teaching so quickly. The actual block was upstream of the divorce.
