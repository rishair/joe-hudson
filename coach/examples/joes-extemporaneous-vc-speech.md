# Joe's Extemporaneous VC Speech

## The Story

When Joe was a venture capitalist, he was scheduled to give a presentation at a conference. He arrived. They had lost his presentation. The slides were gone. He was about to speak in front of a couple hundred people for 20 minutes with nothing prepared but the outline in his head and what he knew because he had written it.

It was scary. He remembers that part clearly. He also remembers another part: "I go, shit, I don't have a choice."

He went up, started talking, and worked from what he knew. The talk lasted 20 minutes. When it was over, his relationship to speaking had permanently changed.

"After that experience, I was like, that was that moment I was talking about early in the podcast. I was, I, it was done. I was just, I will never prepare for a speech again. It was so much better than anything I had ever done. Up into that point, it was, it was amazing." -- Joe Hudson, Speaking Authentically with Tristan de Montebello

## What Made It Better

The lost slides forced him out of the chain into the tree. See [[chain-vs-tree-thinking-for-content]]. With no script to memorize and no slides to anchor to, he had to operate from the essence and the outline. The result was a delivery that was alive in a way the prepared version never was.

The mechanism is consistent with the broader principle in [[state-not-framework]] and [[speaking-authentically]]. The chain-based preparation he had been doing was keeping him from being in the state where good speaking happens. The chain was the comfort blanket that, by its very presence, prevented him from being in flow. The moment the blanket was gone, he had no choice but to enter the state. The state delivered.

Tristan, hearing the story, names what would have made it even better: more reps. The state would have arrived even more cleanly with the experience of having done it before. But the state itself was what produced the better-than-anything-prior result, not the technique.

## What Made It Stick

The "I will never prepare for a speech again" decision is the part of the story that matters most for the principle. Joe did not go back to preparing chains. He recognized the difference between the chain-delivered version of himself and the tree-delivered version, and he chose the tree from then on.

This is the same pattern as the two-roads dynamic in [[the-emotional-experience-decides-the-channel]]. After the emotional experience of speaking from the tree, Joe could have done either of two things: avoided it (too scary, won't risk it again) or embraced it (that was better, more of that). He embraced it. The channel opened for the kind of speaking that operates from state.

His entire body of content production since — podcasts, masterclasses, retreats, coaching — operates this way. Joe does almost none of his content extemporaneously prepared. He has the essence, he has the bookmarks if any, and he trusts the rest to arrive. See [[the-butler-metaphor]].

## The Lesson Tristan Names

Tristan's specific reading of the story:

"Most people learn, think about the speaking as something very linear. It goes from A to Z and it's a bunch of chain links together. So you're memorizing word after word or step after step. And if you think about it that way, then any chain link that breaks and you're, and it's gone and there's nothing beyond and you're lost and now you're, it's full panic mode. What you had to do was come back to the very, very essence of what it is you were gonna talk about. Like, what's the one thing I want to convey? And then what were those bookmarks along the way that I knew I need to hit? But then you have total freedom since you didn't know, literally you didn't prepare for it, so you didn't have all the details down to a T." -- Tristan de Montebello

The story is a forced experiment in tree-thinking. The conditions made the chain impossible. The tree became available. The tree turned out to be better than the chain had ever been.

This is also why Tristan asks Joe a follow-up question: "did you access anything in the presentation that you hadn't prepared for? It kind of popped out." Joe's answer is yes, of course — the butler delivered things he had not pre-decided to say. The discovery-while-speaking is one of the gifts of the tree.

## When to Use This Example

This story is useful when:

- A coachee is afraid of speaking without preparation
- A presenter is over-preparing and the performance is dying from over-preparation
- The question is "how do I get into flow when I speak?"
- Someone is convinced their reliance on memorization is the safe path
- The broader teaching is about [[state-not-framework]] or [[chain-vs-tree-thinking-for-content]] and a personal example would land better than abstract principle
- A coach wants to model the embracing-of-the-forced-emotional-experience as the doorway into the channel
