# Freedom to Be Worthless

## The Story

Joe noticed he could not stand being in conversations he found meaningless. Casual small talk was agonizing. He investigated: what is it that I do not want to feel?

He opened himself to the feeling underneath the agitation. For weeks he found himself weeping in seemingly benign conversations. The discovery: his sense of self was so dependent on being valuable that spending time where he was not valuable felt like annihilation. He did not want to feel worthless.

When he let himself feel it fully, the freedom was tremendous. "I'm happy to be worthless. And I'm happy to be of value." Both became acceptable. The grip broke.

The immediate result: his capacity to be with people in any kind of conversation expanded. He discovered that even in "meaningless" conversations, connection was happening in ways that were not verbal or intellectual. Different forms of connection, invisible to the person who was filtering for value.

## What It Illustrates

1. **Inability to empathize points to a personal emotional limit.** Whatever you cannot be with in another person is something you cannot be with in yourself.
2. **The feared emotion is the doorway.** Worthlessness was the feeling Joe was avoiding. Feeling it fully produced the freedom. See [[feeling-the-unfelt-emotion]].
3. **Empathy expands when self-empathy expands.** After working through the need-to-be-valuable pattern, Joe's capacity to be present with all kinds of people and conversations transformed.

## When To Reach For This

When someone describes irritation with certain types of conversations or people. When exploring what limits a person's empathic range. When illustrating that the block is always internal, never in the other person.
