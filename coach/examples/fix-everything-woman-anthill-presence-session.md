# The Fix-Everything Woman

## What the Example Illustrates

The canonical session arc for the [[why-am-i-always-trying-to-fix]] presenting concern. The session demonstrates, end to end, how Joe works a fix-everything pattern: refuse the broken premise, descend through judgment to silence, surface the failure-feeling driver, use the session itself as the demonstration of being-with, refuse the "how do I stop?" question, and close with the mind-deconstructs-itself practice.

The session is approximately 15 minutes. It was recorded during a public Q&A. The woman is articulate, sophisticated, has clearly done self-development work, and presents the fix-everything pattern as the meta-question of her life — fixing herself, fixing others, standing up against injustice, justifying when people don't like how she shows up.

The session is unusually clean for a public-Q&A coaching. Every major move Joe makes on a fix-pattern is present in compressed form, and the session arcs from intellectual opening to embodied closing in a single sitting.

## The Opening: Refuse the Preparation

The woman delivers a polished four-sentence presentation. The energy is contained; the categories are sophisticated; she ends with "I don't know where to go from here. There's this underlying anger and fear and I feel stuck and I'm not sure what to do."

Joe's first move: "Are you reading from a card?" She admits she has been taking notes throughout the call to formulate the question. He asks her to set the notes aside and be fully here. See [[set-aside-the-notes-be-here-with-me]] and the read [[reading-from-notes-or-script]].

He then compresses her question into a single sentence: "I'm always trying to fix shit and I get upset if people don't let me. How do I handle that?" The compression strips the intellectual scaffolding and leaves a direct sentence the work can engage.

She accepts the compression and goes further: "I'm trying to fix myself. That's probably the biggest thing."

## The First Pivot: What's Wrong With You?

Joe's response to "I'm trying to fix myself" is to ask the question that operates on the premise: "What's wrong with you?"

She answers "Nothing's wrong with me."

Joe: "So what's to fix?"

The sequence is three turns. It accomplishes three things: surfaces the broken-premise the fixing is operating on, gets the woman to name (correctly) that nothing is wrong, and lets her register the contradiction between the nothing-is-wrong and the fix-everything pattern. See [[ask-whats-wrong-with-you-into-the-shame]] and [[dont-presume-the-person-is-broken]].

When she tries to escape into "caretaking others," Joe holds the frame: "Hold on, you've said the main thing I'm trying to do is fix myself. So what are you trying to fix? What's wrong with you?"

She delivers the actual material: "My tendency to judge versus to be discerning. ... You're going to judge somebody for not treating others the way I think they should be treated. This sense of fairness."

## The Judgment Descent

Once judgment is named, Joe runs the standard descent.

"Think of that time where that's most prevalent. ... Now imagine there is just no possible way on earth you could judge them. What would you have to feel?"

This is the [[if-you-couldnt-feel-this-whats-underneath]] move applied to judgment.

She answers: "Nothingness. Just silent. It's quiet."

Joe: "Great. So let's go there."

The descent has produced silence as the underneath. The fixing has been operating to avoid silence. The next move is to enter the silence.

## The Anxiety That Arrives at the Threshold

The silence is not entered directly. The body, deprived of its judgment-and-fix activity, generates anxiety as the buffer.

"What if you looked at it through the lens of silence?"

She: "I have anxiety in my arms and hands, and I feel like I have to be doing something."

Joe: "Fantastic. So let's feel the anxiety. ... So basically what we're learning is if you can't go to judgment, then there's a vastness that makes you very anxious."

The structure is now visible: judgment → activity → fixing, all in service of avoiding the vastness/silence. The anxiety is the gateway. Welcoming the anxiety is the work.

## The Effectiveness Reframe and the Purpose Question

Joe adds a small reframe to remove the moral protection of the fixing.

"All those things you've done to right all the wrongs — in the grand scheme of things, the world is still going around the sun. The politics haven't shifted. There's been no decisive shift in the war in Ukraine or the upcoming war with China."

The reframe sits alongside [[purposelessness-is-freedom]]'s anthill view but operates at a smaller domain — what the woman's specific fix-activity has accomplished, not the cosmic meaninglessness of all activity. The function is to disable the effectiveness-justification of the pattern.

She challenges: "What do you mean my actions haven't changed much?"

Joe holds it lightly and pivots to the question that does the descent work: "What is the point then? What's the point of your life if your job isn't to fix the wrongs of the world?"

She: "Yeah, I think that's what I'm struggling with."

Joe: "Yeah, let's struggle. Fix the wrongs of yourself, fix the wrongs of the world. What if you couldn't do that? What would be left to do?"

She: "Nothing. That's how I feel. Nothing."

See [[what-would-be-left-to-do]] for the question. The answer "nothing" is the landing.

## The Sadness That Arrives With the Nothing

Joe names what arrived with the nothing.

"Yeah, but what I notice is there's a sadness that hits you now."

She: "It's very sad because, I mean, where do you go? Like, why are we here?"

She tries to reach for the philosophical detour. Joe declines.

## The Live Demonstration

"Out of the head for a minute. Just be with me. You can do this. Be with me. Just like give me eye contact, I'll give you eye contact, and let's not fix each other. What's to do? What do we do if we're not fixing each other?"

See [[make-the-session-itself-the-demonstration]]. The conceptual conversation drops. The coach and the client are being asked to be with each other in the room as the demonstration.

The first attempt is declined. She reformulates to "support each other," which Joe receives as another version of the fixing.

He delivers the read that explains what just happened: "What I'm noticing is everything you're doing is trying to avoid just actually being with me. You go into your head to stop being with me. You go to fix me. You try to go to fix yourself. And then when you're with me, like right here, there's like some anxiety that occurs."

See [[fixing-as-avoidance-of-presence]].

Her response surfaces the underneath: "I want it to be right, and I don't know how to get there, and so I feel like I'm failing at it over and over again."

## The Failure-Feeling Surfaces

Joe names the driver.

"That's actually the driver of all the fixing — the feeling that everything I touch fails. How about you just be in that feeling with me of everything I touch fails, and I'll just be with you in that, and we'll hang out? And now I feel you with me more now than I have the whole time."

See [[everything-i-touch-fails-drives-fixing]].

Joe reports his own felt experience — the shift in the room. She has arrived. The being-with is now happening.

She names the principle in her own words: "It's easier to fix things that are surface."

Joe: "Yeah. That's why you always have that weird sense of failure. And your mind just went to trying to fix it."

See [[cant-fix-without-being-with]].

## The Aliveness

Joe redirects from the fix-the-moment move back to the moment: "We can keep on talking or we can just be together again. Feel how alive this is. Just to be with each other like this. It's a lot of energy, right? Like, or sensation moving through your body."

She: "Definitely a lot of sensation. Definitely in my— I can feel the end of my skin, like, everywhere that's exposed."

The somatic confirmation has arrived. The thing the fixing has been chasing is here, in the room, in the body, with no fixing required.

She asks how to keep it: "How do I carry it longer? How do I carry it when you're not sitting across?"

Joe: "Exactly not that way, because that puts you back into the fixing, which takes you out of this moment."

The keeping-frame is the fix-pattern reapplied to the experience. The work is the opposite: undo the impulse to keep, return to the moment.

## The Anthill and the Present-Moment Test

Joe deploys the anthill image to give her a generalizable handle.

"I want you to imagine looking at an anthill. And now I want you to imagine trying to fix the anthill."

She: "I cannot. I don't see anything wrong with the anthill."

Joe: "Correct. And if you're in this moment, what's wrong? With me. What's wrong?"

She: "Nothing's wrong."

Joe: "What's wrong in this moment? You can't go to the past. You can't go to the future. Just what's wrong with this moment?"

She: "I feel I'm not fixing it fast enough."

Joe holds it: "Right. Fixing what? What's wrong with this moment?"

She: "Me not being able to sit here in this moment is now what's becoming wrong with my moments."

Joe: "Yeah. And there, there you are in this moment. It just happened."

See [[whats-wrong-with-this-moment]]. The fix-pattern requires time-extraction. The constraint reveals this structurally. The arrival happens in the noticing.

She names what she has been avoiding: "I'm avoiding the silence. I'm avoiding the peace."

Joe confirms: "Yeah. And the pleasure and that aliveness. That's why everybody rejects you trying to fix it."

## The Refusal of the How-Do-I-Stop Question

The woman tries the final fix-move: "And now how do I stop it? How do I stop fixing people?"

A voice in the room says "Just stop."

Joe refuses both. "It's the question that leads to failure. And so it lets you stay in the loop."

See [[how-do-i-stop-is-the-fix-trap]].

He inverts the question: "The question is, how do you be with people? And it's the exact opposite of a doing. It is an undoing, as you're learning right now. So to ask, how do you do it, is counterproductive."

See [[undoing-not-doing]].

She: "Is that the same by asking, how do I undo it?"

Joe: "Let's find out. How do you be with me? The answer: undo it."

She lands: "It does, actually. Yeah, yeah, especially on the intellectual level, because I've done it with the tension where I can feel it."

## The Closing Take-Home

Joe gives her the practice for the mind.

"Intellectually, when you're here, intellectually, the best job for the mind is to deconstruct itself. Every thought. How is that not true? How is that not true? Because it just leaves you here with me or with us."

See [[mind-deconstructs-itself]] and [[apply-how-is-that-not-true-to-every-thought]].

The principle and the practice are paired. The session closes shortly after.

## Why This Session Is the Canonical Example

The session is unusually compressed and complete. Every major piece of the fix-pattern work is present:

- Refusing the preparation/intellectualizing opening.
- Refusing the broken premise with "what's wrong with you?"
- The descent through judgment to silence.
- The anxiety at the threshold of silence.
- The effectiveness reframe.
- The purpose question ("what would be left to do?").
- The sadness underneath the nothing.
- The live demonstration with the coach in the room.
- The naming of fixing-as-avoidance.
- The surfacing of everything-I-touch-fails as the driver.
- The cant-fix-without-being-with principle landing experientially.
- The anthill and present-moment-test.
- The naming of what was being avoided (silence, peace, aliveness).
- The refusal of the how-do-I-stop question.
- The undoing-not-doing reframe.
- The mind-deconstructs-itself closing practice.

A coach working a fix-pattern session can use this transcript as the reference for the full arc. The pieces appear in approximately this order in most fix-pattern sessions, though individual sessions vary in how much time each piece takes.

## When This Example Is Useful

- When a coach is learning to work a fix-everything pattern and needs to see the full arc.
- When a coach is mid-session with a fix-pattern client and is looking for the next move.
