# Eating But Not Digesting the Food

## The Image

A person eats. The food enters the mouth. The food enters the stomach. But the food is not digested. The nutrients do not absorb. The hunger does not abate. The person eats again. The food enters again. The food is not digested. The hunger does not abate. The cycle continues. The person is constantly eating and constantly hungry.

This is what approval-seeking looks like for the person whose compliment-receiving channel is closed. Compliments arrive. The person hears them. The compliments do not land. The hunger for recognition does not abate. The person needs the next compliment. The next compliment also does not land. The hunger persists.

The fix is not more compliments. The fix is digestion. The fix is letting the compliments actually be received by the body, processed, integrated, allowed to nourish.

## Joe's Words

"It was like, I was a person who was eating, but then I was always like not digesting the food. And now I was actually digesting the food and it satisfied my hunger for the very first time." -- Joe Hudson, STOP Chasing Approval

The metaphor arrives at the end of Joe's autobiographical account of learning to receive compliments. He describes the experience after starting to let compliments actually land: "And the more I allowed myself to feel it, the less I needed it." The need diminished because the receiving was finally happening. The hunger was being met by what was already there.

## What the Metaphor Captures

Several specific things the eating-without-digestion image captures that other framings miss:

**The compliments have been arriving all along.** The deflector often reports feeling unappreciated or unrecognized, as if no one has been complimenting them. The eating metaphor reveals that the food has been there. The problem was not the supply. The problem was the absorption.

**The hunger is the diagnostic, not the absence.** Hunger means the body is not getting nourishment. If the food has been arriving but the hunger persists, the issue is digestive, not nutritional. The constant hunger for approval is the signal that the existing approval is not being absorbed.

**Eating more does not help.** The deflector often tries to solve the hunger by chasing more compliments. The metaphor exposes this as futile. Eating ten more meals does not solve the digestion problem. Until the digestion works, no amount of food will satisfy.

**The work is somatic, not cognitive.** Digestion is a body process. Receiving a compliment is also a body process. The image keeps the work in the body rather than in the mind. You do not solve the hunger by thinking about food. You solve it by letting the body do its work with the food.

**The result is paradoxical: needing less.** Once the digestion works, the hunger diminishes. The need for the next compliment fades. This is the structural inversion: the person who can receive compliments needs them less than the person who cannot.

## When Joe Uses This

The metaphor comes up specifically when Joe is teaching about receiving compliments and gratitude. It is the closing image of his autobiographical arc — first the realization that he was calling people liars, then the polite-thank-you-while-disbelieving phase, then the actual receiving, then the discovery that the receiving fed the hunger that the chasing had never fed.

In coaching sessions, the image is useful when a client reports:

- Constantly needing more recognition without ever feeling satisfied
- Receiving compliments but feeling unmoved by them
- Chasing approval, getting some, and immediately needing more
- The sense that no amount of validation ever fills the hole

The metaphor reorients the client toward the receiving capacity rather than toward the supply. The instruction is then to practice the somatic receiving (see [[let-it-move-through-and-clean-the-channel]]).

## The Specific Inversion the Metaphor Names

"The more I allowed myself to feel it, the less I needed it." -- Joe Hudson, STOP Chasing Approval

This is the core counter-intuitive teaching. The approval-seeker's hypothesis is: "if I just get enough approval, I will feel okay." The hypothesis is wrong. The actual mechanism: "if I let the approval I have actually land, I will feel okay, and the seeking will stop."

The chase is the cause of the hunger, not the solution to it. The chase keeps the receiving channel closed (because the chase is the meta-position of monitoring, not the in-body position of receiving). The compliments arrive but cannot be received because the chase is in the way. The receiving is what changes. The receiving fills the hunger. The seeking stops.

The mechanism connects to [[craving-is-the-pushing-away]]: the chase for approval is the pushing-away of approval. The receiving is the dropping of the chase, which makes the receiving possible.

## What This Looks Like in Coaching

When a client describes the constant-hunger pattern with approval, the metaphor lands directly. Most people recognize the eating-without-digesting experience either in themselves or in someone they know. The image bypasses the conceptual frame and goes straight to the bodily intuition.

The follow-up move is the practice: have the client try receiving a single compliment fully. Either a compliment given in the session, or one recalled from the past. Slow down the receiving. Notice the deflection impulse. Do not act on it. Let the compliment land somatically. See what changes.

Often the client reports being surprised by what arrives — a wash of warmth, an unexpected emotional release, a brief contact with the actual nourishment that has been being refused. The surprise is the body discovering it can digest after all. Once it has digested once, it knows it can do it again. The metaphor has been confirmed in the body, which is where the metaphor lives.

## The Broader Implication

The metaphor applies beyond compliments. Anything offered to the deflector — love, care, attention, recognition — runs through the same digestive system. If the system is closed, the offering does not land. The deflector is starving in the presence of abundance.

This connects to:

- [[different-languages-of-love]]: love arrives in shapes the receiver does not recognize and does not let in
- [[letting-it-in-vs-deflecting-the-welcome]]: the same dynamic between partners
- [[receive-the-apology-permeably]]: apologies that do not land for the same reason
- [[always-something-spectacular-happening]]: gratitude is received not searched for

The eating-without-digestion image is the underlying mechanism in all of these. Whenever someone is hungry in the presence of nourishment, the digestion is the place to look.

## Tone

Joe delivers the metaphor with a small wonder, as if the realization had genuinely surprised him. The phrasing "satisfied my hunger for the very first time" carries the felt sense of a need being met that had not been met before. The tone is autobiographical — Joe is recounting his own experience of discovering this, not lecturing about it. The image lands because it is offered as something Joe himself had to find.
