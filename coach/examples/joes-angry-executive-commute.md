# Joe's Angry Executive Commute

## The Story

A woman executive begins working with Joe. She presents as feeling like a victim. The bureaucracy at her company is too big. She does not have control. She is stuck. She has no power.

Joe assigns homework: get angry every night on your commute. Every night, just get pissed at everything that happened that you want to get pissed at.

Within two to three weeks, her empowerment changes significantly. She does not feel as trapped anymore. A low-grade depression that had been present starts shifting into much more empowerment.

"Within like 2 or 3 weeks, her empowerment changed significantly. Her level of empowerment changed significantly. She didn't feel as trapped anymore. And I would say like a kind of a low-level depression started changing for her into much more empowerment." -- Joe Hudson, The Wisdom of Anger

## Joe's Note: He Has Seen This Pattern Many Times

This is not a one-off. Joe explicitly says he has seen the same change dozens of times.

"I've seen that dozens of times." -- Joe Hudson, The Wisdom of Anger

The repeatability is part of why he tells the story. The shift from victim-stance to empowerment via nightly anger release is reliable enough that he assigns it as a homework.

## What It Illustrates

**Unreleased anger is one of the main mechanisms producing low-grade depression.** The executive's flatness was not depression-the-disease. It was depression-the-suppression-symptom. The held anger was producing the disempowered state. Releasing the anger restored the empowerment. See [[anger]] for the link between suppressed anger and disempowerment.

**The change is fast when the right thing is being touched.** Two to three weeks is a short timeline for a meaningful change in someone's baseline empowerment. The speed is diagnostic: when the bottleneck is identified accurately, the system reorganizes quickly. If the change had been slow, the diagnosis might have been wrong. The speed confirms that anger release was the missing piece.

**The car is sufficient.** No retreat. No therapy intensive. No special equipment. A 30-minute commute and the willingness to yell. The accessibility of the practice is part of why it works.

**Empowerment is not produced by external change.** Her bureaucracy did not change. Her boss did not change. The "trap" she described did not change. What changed was her relationship to her own anger about it. The empowerment was internal and showed up in the same external circumstances.

## What Joe Was Reading in Her

The presenting concern matched the concern in [[i-feel-trapped]]: "the system has power over me, there is nothing I can do." But the read underneath the concern was that the absence of anger was the problem, not the bureaucracy. Joe's intervention was not about the bureaucracy at all. It was about the held emotion that was producing the disempowered stance.

The read: this is someone who is not angry enough about her situation. The anger that should be present is being suppressed and the suppression is producing the flatness she calls being trapped.

## When Joe Uses This Story

- When introducing [[get-angry-in-the-car]] as a practice.
- When teaching that anger release produces empowerment.
- When showing how fast change can happen when the right emotion is moved.
- When working with an executive or professional who feels stuck in a bureaucracy.
- When the presenting concern is low-grade depression and the diagnostic is held anger.

## The Companion Example

Joe pairs this story with another in the same conversation: another executive he worked with went to the beach in San Francisco and yelled at the ocean. Same principle, different container. The pairing makes the point that the form is flexible; what matters is private discharge.

"One executive I worked with the other day went to the beach, went right up to the ocean and yelled at the ocean. Nobody could hear, you know, in San Francisco." -- Joe Hudson, The Wisdom of Anger

## Tone

Joe tells this with practical confidence. He has seen it work many times. There is no mystification. The instruction is concrete and the result is reliable.
