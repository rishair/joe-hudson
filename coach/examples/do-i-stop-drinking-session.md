# Do I Stop Drinking? (The Compressed Coaching Session)

## The Setup

A short coaching session recorded in one of Joe's live Q&As. The client raises his hand to ask about sobriety. He has been on and off drinking for two years — at most one month sober in that period, two years sober in the previous five. The drinking is not heavy ("it's not huge drinking we're talking about"), and he is still "deeply attracted to it" even as he senses it is not serving him as he gets older.

The session takes about 60 turns and arrives at a structural shift that the client recognizes somatically. Joe runs through several of his most characteristic moves in compressed form. The session is a useful condensed example of how Joe coaches a habit-change question.

## The Arc (Compressed)

1. **Refuses the decision-frame.** The client presents the question as a binary fork: "Do I just completely stop drinking, or do I just drink a little bit?" Joe does not engage with the fork. He asks what the drinking is giving the client. This is the standard exit from [[treating-it-as-a-decision-when-its-an-emotion]].

2. **Locates the underlying emotion.** Joe asks: "What does the drinking get you?" The client says: "Quick fix relaxation and kind of a change of state." Joe presses: "From what to what? What's the state it gives you?" The client lands in seconds: "I'm feeling into the shame and guilt piece that I'm trying to escape."

3. **Reframes the question.** Joe restates the original question in the language of the underlying emotion: "So what if the question was, should I give up shame and guilt or not?" The client responds: "Juicy. Gets to the heart of it for sure." See [[reframe-the-question-to-the-blocker-emotion]].

4. **Teaches the principle.** Joe gives the Adyashanti compression: "Shame is the locks that hold the chains of bad habits in place." Brief teaching, single sentence, attribution. See [[shame-drives-behavior]].

5. **Names the shame loop.** Joe surfaces the cycle: "How does it feel to know that you've been running away from shame and guilt, and in the running away from it, creating more shame and guilt?" The client confirms: "That's where I am. It's just like a downward spiral." See [[the-shame-avoidance-loop]].

6. **Redirects from head to feeling.** The client starts intellectualizing ("So when I see, um, you know, I have like—"). Joe interrupts: "Out of your head, out of your head for a minute." He redirects to the felt experience of the shame loop.

7. **Surfaces the hidden rebellion.** "How much rebellion is in there? How much of like, you can't fucking tell me what to do, is in there?" The client recognizes: "Until you said it, I couldn't have identified that, but it's there." When asked what the rebellion would say: "I want what I want. Fuck you." See [[hidden-rebellion-under-the-habit]].

8. **Speaks to the rebellious part.** "So I'm gonna just speak to that part of you for a minute. What is it that you do want?" The rebellion-protected want arrives: "I want connection with myself and others. Easy connection with myself and others."

9. **Demonstrates the want is live in the room.** Joe asks: "How hard is your connection with me right now?" The client initially does not register the live connection. Joe points at it: "Right now it's happening." The client acknowledges: "Right now? No, it's happening. Yeah, I'm, I'm, I'm, I'm still feeling so much of the, of the rubbish that I'm struggling to actually be present." Joe reframes: "Yeah, so that means you're connected to the rubbish and me." See [[point-at-the-present-evidence]].

10. **Continues the live A/B until it lands somatically.** Joe asks "How hard is this?" multiple times across the next several turns. The client moves from "It's hard. I'm finding it hard" through "I'm getting confused" to, after Joe asks again at the moment the confusion is happening, "Super easy."

11. **Names the boxed-heart mechanism.** "This is the thing that I see. I see this like fucking amazingly big heart that somehow or another needs like alcohol to give you permission to shine." The client recognizes: "Yeah, that's, that's, that's the nugget." See [[heart-in-a-box-want-to-drink]].

12. **Self-discloses the universality.** Joe shares his own version: "Oh yeah, love was a dangerous motherfucking thing." The disclosure is delivered with no performance and gives the client permission to inhabit the discovery as a survival adaptation rather than as a personal failing.

13. **Compresses the teaching.** "When you, when, when you put your heart in a box, you want to drink. No heart in a box, no want to drink." The teaching arrives as the synthesis of the session. The client: "I love that. Love that."

14. **Closes with embodied recognition.** The client notices his own body: "What I'm noticing right now is my— I'm sitting here with my hands outstretched in, in, um, like connection mode." Joe receives this and confirms the seeing: "Yeah, yeah. When you put your heart in a box, you want to drink. No heart in a box, no want to drink... Yeah, I see you."

The session does not end with an instruction about drinking. The drinking is not addressed directly at any point. The work is on the boxed heart, the shame loop, the hidden rebellion, and the live availability of connection. The drinking is left to dissolve downstream.

## What the Session Illustrates

This session is a compressed example of several principles operating together:

- **[[shame-drives-behavior]]**: The drinking is held in place by the shame the drinking is also producing. The cycle is the engine.
- **[[the-shame-avoidance-loop]]**: The avoidance of the shame creates more shame, which generates more avoidance, which sustains the habit.
- **[[joes-pot-habit]]**: The same structural pattern Joe demonstrates with his own pot habit — the substance was managing an avoided emotion; addressing the emotion dissolves the substance's job.
- **[[rebellion-or-submission-to-should]]**: The long-running "I should stop drinking" has produced both submission (the surface compliant framing of the question) and rebellion (the "fuck you, I want what I want" that is keeping the habit alive against the should).
- **[[what-were-avoiding-is-usually-love]]**: Underneath the avoidance of shame and guilt is the avoidance of the openhearted self that was once dangerous to be. The avoided thing is love.
- **[[heart-in-a-box-want-to-drink]]**: The specific mechanism by which the substance becomes psychologically necessary — it provides access to the boxed-off openhearted self.
- **[[treating-it-as-a-decision-when-its-an-emotion]]**: The presentation as a decision is the cover. The work is on the emotion the decision is avoiding.
- **[[find-the-want-behind-the-should]]**: The want the rebellion was protecting (easy connection with self and others) is the want the should about drinking was suppressing.

## When Joe Reaches for This Session

When teaching about addiction patterns where the substance is providing access to a quality the person is keeping suppressed — warmth, expression, connection. The session is the cleanest demonstration of [[heart-in-a-box-want-to-drink]].

When demonstrating the compressed version of how to coach a habit-change question. The session is short, the moves are sequenced, and the structural shifts are visible turn by turn.

When demonstrating the use of self-disclosure in coaching ("love was a dangerous motherfucking thing"). The disclosure is brief, lived, and gives the client permission to inhabit their own discovery as ordinary rather than as personal pathology.

When showing how a coach surfaces a hidden rebellion in a compliant-seeming client. The "how much rebellion is in there?" move is replicable across many cases.

## Speaker Notes

The session is short partly because the client tracks fast. He recognizes each move as it lands. Not every client tracks at this speed. The structure of the session — refuse the decision, locate the underlying emotion, reframe the question, surface the rebellion, name the boxed quality, demonstrate the access in the room — is durable across different client speeds, but the time taken at each step will vary.

The client's recognition language is somatic. "Juicy." "That's the nugget." "I love that." His hands outstretched at the end. The body is verifying each move as it lands. When the client's body stops verifying, the coach should slow down.

Joe's self-disclosure is unprompted and brief. The client asks "For you?" after Joe says "It fucking felt hard for a while." Joe answers: "Oh yeah, love was a dangerous motherfucking thing." One sentence. He does not extend into his own story. The disclosure serves the client's recognition, then returns to the client.
