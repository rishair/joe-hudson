# The Spaghetti Tower Experiment

## The Story

Groups are given 25 pieces of dried spaghetti, a marshmallow, and masking tape. Task: build the tallest structure. Five kindergartners regularly beat five CEOs.

The kindergartners iterate. They try models, fail, try again, and by the end have tested 3 or 4 designs and found something that works. The CEOs plan toward a perfect design, place the marshmallow at the last minute, and the whole thing collapses.

The twist: add one administrative assistant to the CEO group, and they outperform the kindergartners. One person who connects the group to each other changes the outcome.

## What It Illustrates

Connection (iterating, experimenting, relating to each other and the materials) produces better results than perfection (planning the ideal structure and executing once). This is the core claim of [[connection-over-perfection]].

The administrative assistant functions as a connector, not a builder. The CEOs already have the skills. What they lack is the connective tissue between their individual efforts. One person filling that role unlocks the group's capacity.

## When Joe Uses It

To demonstrate that iteration beats planning in producing outcomes. To show that connection between people (not just individual brilliance) is the missing ingredient. To challenge CEOs and high-performers who believe that getting it right matters more than trying things and learning.
