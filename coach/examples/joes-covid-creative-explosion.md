# Joe's COVID Creative Explosion

## The Example

When COVID hit and the structure of normal life was disrupted, Joe was at home with the same uncertainty everyone else had. He could not predict how long lockdown would last. He could not predict what would happen with the markets, the schools, the economy, his work. The future was wide open in a way it had not been.

In that condition, he asked the how-question. Not "what should I do?" but "how do I want to be in this?" The answers were specific.

"What I want to do is I want to be close with my family. What I want to do is be creative. Like, this is how I want to be in this time. It's when AOA online courses were born. The depth of intimacy I got with my kids during that time was amazing. I had all this time and space to create MasterClass. We started this podcast. All this happened because we knew how we wanted to be in this situation, and it wouldn't have happened without COVID." -- Joe Hudson, Making The Most of Uncertainty

The list of outputs: AOA online courses, MasterClass, the Art of Accomplishment podcast, deep intimacy with his children. None of these would have happened without the disruption. The disruption was the soil. The chosen how was what got planted.

## Why This Example Lands

The example lands because the outputs are concrete and verifiable. AOA online courses exist. MasterClass exists. The podcast exists. Joe's relationship with his kids is what it is. The example is not a hypothetical about what could have come from COVID. It is a list of specific things that did.

The example also lands because the input was specific. Not "I had a great attitude about COVID." The input was two specific hows: "I want to be close with my family" and "I want to be creative." Two answers to one question. Each how then organized the next several months of Joe's life. The close-with-family how produced the intimacy. The creative how produced the courses and the podcast.

The example demonstrates the structure of [[take-the-adventure-stance]] in operation. The COVID disruption could have been met with optimism ("it'll be over by summer"), pessimism ("the economy is finished"), or realism that collapsed the uncertainty ("we'll lose the business"). Joe met it with the adventure stance: this is happening, I am in it, here is how I want to be. The outputs followed from the stance.

## The Pattern the Example Illustrates

The structure of the example is the structure of the principle in operation:

1. A disruption arrives that the person did not choose.
2. The person's normal structure is broken.
3. The person could spend the disruption waiting for normal to return.
4. Or the person could ask: how do I want to be in this?
5. The honest answer reveals wants that have been suppressed by the normal structure.
6. The wants become the organizing principle for the disrupted period.
7. The outputs from the wants are specific and would not have happened without the disruption.

Joe's COVID is a particularly clean instance of this structure because the inputs and outputs are both legible. The wants were "close with family" and "creative." The outputs were the family intimacy, the courses, MasterClass, and the podcast. The line from input to output is short and visible.

## The Counterfactual

The example also implies its opposite. The same COVID period was met by many people with optimism ("it'll be over soon"), pessimism ("we're fucked"), or freeze (just-get-through-the-day mode). None of these orientations produced the specific outputs Joe's did. Some of these orientations produced burnout, depression, or relational damage instead.

The same external situation, met with different orientations, produced different lives. The variable was not the external situation. The variable was the orientation brought to it.

This is the reason the example is worth telling. It is not "Joe is exceptional." It is "the orientation makes the difference, and here is what one orientation produced." The implied invitation: what orientation are you bringing to your current disruption?

## Tone

Joe tells this story with a quiet satisfaction. There is no boasting. The tone is more like someone saying "this is what happened, and it surprised me too." The surprise is part of what makes it a teaching: even the person who lived it did not predict the outputs. The outputs arrived because the wants were honored, not because they were targeted.
