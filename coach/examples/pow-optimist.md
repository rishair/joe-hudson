# The POW Optimist (Stockdale Paradox)

## The Story

An admiral in the Navy was in a POW camp in Vietnam. He was asked who made it and who did not. He said the people who did not make it were easy to identify: the optimists. The interviewer asked what he meant. The admiral said: the optimists were the ones who thought they would get out by Christmas, or by the next season. When that timetable came and went, they became defeated, and they did not make it.

Who made it? The people who maintained the vision of their own freedom, without attaching to when it would arrive.

"There's a great story of—it was an admiral in the Navy who got into a POW camp in Vietnam, and he was asked, 'Who made it? Who didn't make it?' He says, 'Well, who didn't make it was easy. That was the optimist.' And the interviewer is like, 'What do you mean optimist?' And he said, 'Means that they thought they were going to get out by Christmas or by, you know, the next season or whatever it was. They didn't make it because when that came and that timetable came and left, they became defeated in it and they didn't make it. He said, well, who did make it? He said, well, that's clear. It was the people who thought that they would get out, the people who maintained that vision of their own freedom.'" -- Joe Hudson, Empower over Power

## The Point It Illustrates

The distinction between performing success and embodying the certainty of one's truth.

Performance attaches to a specific outcome by a specific date. When the date arrives without the outcome, the performance collapses, and what is underneath collapses with it.

Embodied empowerment holds the vision without gripping the timetable. The form may change. The vision endures.

This is why [[acting-as-if-already-true]] is not the same as positive thinking. The civil rights leader acts as if equality is already real, but does not collapse when this Tuesday's protest fails. The CEO acts as if the company is already successful, but does not collapse when this quarter's numbers miss. The vision is unconditional. The timeline is not.

## When Joe Reaches for This

When someone is "manifesting" or visualizing success in a way that depends on a specific outcome by a specific time. The story shows that this stance is fragile.

When someone is performing confidence and Joe can see that the performance will not survive contact with disappointment.

When someone is gripping a transformation ("I hope this lasts") in a way that signals attachment to form rather than embodiment of substance. See [[quit-hope-lose-cycle]].

When the question arises: how do I tell the difference between empowered vision and wishful thinking? The Stockdale answer: the empowered vision is the one that does not depend on a specific timetable.

## The Related Teaching

"It might be the next business." -- Joe Hudson, Empower over Power

The truly empowered person who believes they will be successful is willing for the business in front of them to fail entirely. They are not the business. The business is one expression of their capacity. If it fails, the next one will be the expression.

This is structurally identical to [[process-over-result]]: commit to the process (here, the embodiment of the empowered stance), release the result (which specific form, on which specific timeline).
