## Near-Duplicate Scan

For each new article from this absorb, grepped for topic keywords across same and adjacent layer types. Findings:

1. `shame-stagnates-emotion` vs `shame-as-disconnection`: Not duplicates. shame-as-disconnection now references shame-stagnates-emotion in its "What People Experience" section but the two articles cover distinct facets (relational disconnection signal vs. emotional stagnation mechanism). The cross-link is clean.

2. `shame-stagnates-emotion` vs `the-shame-avoidance-loop`: Not duplicates. The loop is the social/relational structure (avoiding shame from others creates self-disconnection shame). Stagnation is the felt mechanism. Loop describes how shame perpetuates structurally. Stagnation describes why it stays stuck physiologically.

3. `addiction-to-shame` vs `the-shame-avoidance-loop`: Not duplicates. Loop is about avoiding shame (people-pleasing form). Addiction-to-shame is about appetites that have shame wired into them as part of the appeal (compulsion form). Different mechanisms even though both involve shame perpetuating itself.

4. `addiction-to-shame` vs `negative-self-reinforcement`: Adjacent but distinct. Negative-self-reinforcement is the procrastination/avoidance loop where punishing yourself for not doing something makes you avoid it more. Addiction-to-shame is the appetite/compulsion loop where shame becomes part of why the activity is wanted. Cross-linked.

5. `if-i-were-inherently-good` vs `find-the-want-behind-the-should`: Adjacent but distinct. find-the-want-behind-the-should excavates the want under a should; if-i-were-inherently-good reinterprets a want that is already named but loaded with shame. Both can apply in sequence. Cross-linked.

6. `welcome-the-shame-without-extracting` vs `welcoming-the-others-emotion`: Adjacent. welcoming-the-others-emotion is the broader move applied to any emotion (anger, sadness, fear). welcome-the-shame-without-extracting is the shame-specific version with the special two-part structure ("I don't want you to be in it AND you're welcome to be in it"). Shame's stickiness requires the two-part move; other emotions usually don't. Cross-linked.

7. `dont-shame-kids-they-self-shame` vs `dont-extract-apologies`: Adjacent. dont-extract-apologies is the anti-pattern around forcing apologies (specific behavior). dont-shame-kids-they-self-shame is the broader parenting principle. The former is one application of the latter. Cross-linked.

8. `list-the-shames-you-avoided` vs `joes-26-year-old-list`: Related but distinct. joes-26-year-old-list is Joe's specific example/story (the things in his life that hadn't changed in 10 years had shame attached). list-the-shames-you-avoided is the prescribed practice for individuals. Cross-linked in body.

9. `natural-shame-vs-installed-shame` vs `past-shame-vs-current-shame`: Adjacent but cut differently. Past-vs-current is temporal (was vs. is). Natural-vs-installed is sourced (intrinsic vs. socially-trained). Both can apply: current shame can be either natural or installed; past shame can be either. The distinctions intersect, they do not overlap. Cross-linked.
