# What makes me uniquely me?

## What Someone Says

The presenting frame is identity-as-bullet-points. The person knows their broader purpose. They know what they want to do. What they want is the language — specifically, the business-friendly, brand-friendly, self-description-friendly language for what they specifically and uniquely bring. The search is not for direction. The search is for the right framework to fit themselves into so they can articulate it.

- "How do I know what I uniquely have to offer?"
- "What's my superpower?"
- "What makes me uniquely me?"
- "I'm looking for language around my superpower, more from a business perspective."
- "What's my unique how?"
- "I want to know my zone of genius."
- "I want to fit into a mold of, well, you should know what you're really great at and your genius and your superpower and kind of these fixed buckets."

The structure is recognizable: the person is not lost about direction. They have a sense of purpose (often clear enough to state in a sentence). What they are looking for is something more specific — a labeling, a framework, a category they can hang on themselves so they can communicate what they do. The premise is that this label exists and that finding it would unlock something.

## Detection Cues

**Surface signals:**
- The phrase "superpower," "zone of genius," "unique value proposition," "what makes me me."
- The framing distinguishes between "purpose" (which is already known) and "uniqueness" (which is the question).
- Business-context language: branding, positioning, articulation, niche.
- The fantasy of a clean, articulable, repeatable answer: "I help people [X]" with X being a specific identifiable category.

**Indirect signals:**
- The person has already done significant work on themselves. They are not in years-of-flailing. They have a sense of what they care about.
- Energy is constricted rather than expanded when describing the search. The search itself feels "by the book and not very expansive."
- The person references frameworks from other people (StrengthsFinder, Wealth Dynamics, Zone of Genius, etc.) and is trying to fit themselves into one.
- A small flare of recognition when asked about a specific past moment of having been seen — "yeah, that was a singular moment of feeling seen."

**The most telling tell:** the person is looking for a label that another person could grant them. The framework they want to fit into was designed by someone else. The search is for the right bucket that already exists, not for what is actually present in them. See [[contorting-into-someone-elses-framework]].

## What This Concern Hears Underneath

**The gift is the nature, not the skill.** What the person is calling "a gift" is what Joe would call "his nature." See [[your-gift-is-your-nature-not-your-skill]]. The person is searching for a skill-shaped or technique-shaped answer when the actual answer is a quality-of-being. The world-famous coach who answered "I help people die better" was not naming a skill. He was naming the place he was already standing.

**The how-of-the-search contradicts the purpose.** If the purpose is to bring joy, doing a joyless search for the right brand-language for the purpose is structurally incoherent. See [[the-means-must-match-the-end]]. The universe would be "fucking you hard" to let a joyless search for joy succeed. The how of the search is data about whether the search itself is in alignment.

**The framework is someone else's mold.** Fitting into pre-existing buckets — "you should know your genius, your superpower, your fixed buckets" — is the approval-frame wearing self-discovery language. Someone else built the framework. Fitting into it gets approval from the framework-holder. The contortion required to fit is what makes the search exhausting. See [[purpose-as-approval-seeking-in-disguise]] for the broader pattern.

**The answer is already known.** The person typically already has the data. There has been a moment — sometimes years ago — when someone named them clearly ("she motivates people"), and the person experienced "one of the most singular moments of feeling seen." The data has been there. The wild goose chase happens despite the data being there. See [[the-answer-is-already-known]].

**The body knows before the mind does.** When the person is asked to drop out of the brain and into the heart, the heart often produces the answer in seconds — "it's very warm... it's also chill." These are the gifts. The mind cannot find them because the mind is looking in the wrong place. See [[go-to-your-heart-not-your-brain]].

**The "from a business perspective" framing is the tell.** When the person specifies that the search is for business-language, the search has already been distorted by an imagined audience. The audience is being optimized for in the choice of search. The activity becomes inauthentic because the audience is now driving. See [[do-it-for-yourself-to-find-purpose]].

**Purpose is the how, gifts are the nature.** Purpose lives in how you do anything; gifts are the qualities you bring when you are not contorting. See [[purpose-is-how-not-what]]. Looking for the right what (the unique skill) is the same structural error as looking for the right activity. The how is the unit. The gifts are the felt-qualities that color the how.

## What Joe Looks For (Reads)

Two people saying "what's my superpower?" can go completely different directions based on what is read.

- If the person is doing a joyless search for the language of their joyful purpose: the read is [[the-means-contradicts-the-end]]. The move is to ask "how joyful is that search?" and name the structural incoherence. See [[how-joyful-is-that-search]].
- If the person is trying to fit themselves into a pre-existing framework someone else built: the read is [[contorting-into-someone-elses-framework]]. The move is to surface the framework as foreign and to surface how many times the person has done this contortion before.
- If the person has already had a moment of being clearly named by someone else and still asks the question: the read is [[the-answer-is-already-known]]. The move is "what's with the wild goose chase when you've had the answer?" See [[wild-goose-chase-when-the-answer-is-known]].
- If the person is in their head trying to think their way to the answer: the read is that the mind is the wrong instrument. The move is [[go-to-your-heart-not-your-brain]] — literally redirect attention to the heart and ask what the heart's answer is.
- If the person frames it "from a business perspective": the read is approval-orientation wearing self-discovery language. The audience is driving the search. The move is to point at the structural problem of using approval-grammar to find one's own nature.
- If the person resists naming what is already obvious (warm, loving, chill, motivating people): the read is that the answer is too simple to feel like it should be the answer. The move is to confirm — "yeah, that seems like at least two of your gifts" — and name that they have been on a wild goose chase for the obvious.

## Coaching Path

A typical flow when this concern surfaces.

1. **Distinguish the question from the purpose-question.** "How is that question different than 'what is my purpose?'" Often the person can name a clear purpose and is asking a different question — about the specific qualities or labels. Get clarity that the purpose is known.

2. **Surface the joyfulness of the search itself.** "How joyful is that search?" The answer is almost always: not very. Name the structural problem of using a joyless method to find one's joyful contribution. See [[the-means-must-match-the-end]].

3. **Name the framework as foreign.** Once the person describes wanting to fit into "fixed buckets" or "what you're really great at," name that this is someone else's framework. The contortion required to fit into someone else's mold is the source of the exhaustion.

4. **Trust that the answer is already known.** "The interesting thing on top of that is that I know you know the answer." This is delivered as flat certainty, not as a question. The person will often confirm — and then immediately try to find the answer with the mind that has been failing to find it.

5. **Redirect from brain to heart.** "Do me a favor — go to your heart instead of your brain. Put your attention in your heart. What's the heart's answer?" When the mind tries to answer, redirect: "that was the brain. Get out of the brain. Heart. Take its time. Don't force anything." See [[go-to-your-heart-not-your-brain]].

6. **Confirm what the heart produces.** Whatever surfaces — "warm, loving, chill" — confirm it as the gifts. "Yeah, that seems like at least two of your gifts." No qualification, no elaboration. The naming is the work.

7. **Tell the "die better" story.** When the person needs the principle illustrated, tell the world-famous coach's "I help people die better" story. The gift is not a skill or technique. The gift is the nature, the place the person is already standing. See [[i-help-people-die-better]].

8. **Distinguish gift from nature.** "What you're calling 'the gift,' I would just call his nature." See [[your-gift-is-your-nature-not-your-skill]]. The reframe collapses the search for the right skill into the recognition of where the person is already standing.

9. **Surface the prior moment of being-seen.** Ask if there has been a moment of someone naming them clearly. Often the person has a story — "a friend introduced me and said 'she motivates people' and it was one of the most singular moments of feeling seen." That moment is the data. The data has been there. The search has been ignoring it.

10. **Ask the wild-goose-chase question.** "What's with the wild goose chase when you've had the answer?" The person typically names the answer themselves: "I think it's trying to fit into a framework that somebody else has created for what validates us." See [[wild-goose-chase-when-the-answer-is-known]].

11. **Surface how many times the contortion has been done.** "How many times have you tried to put yourself into some framework that is going to apparently validate you?" The answer is usually "many, and it's always an awkward contortionist experience." Naming the recurrence makes the pattern visible.

12. **Offer the apology to self.** "You want to apologize to yourself for doing that?" See [[apologize-for-contorting-into-frameworks]]. The apology is for the years of forcing the self into shapes that were not the self. The apology completes the recognition.

## What Not to Do

- **Don't help them find the language.** Generating possible superpower-descriptions, suggesting frameworks, asking about their skills — all of this validates the search for the right label. The label-search is the problem.
- **Don't validate the business-framing of the question.** "From a business perspective" is the audience being elevated above the self. Treating the business need as the legitimate frame entrenches the approval-orientation.
- **Don't recommend a framework.** StrengthsFinder, Wealth Dynamics, Human Design, Enneagram — recommending any of these would deliver the person to another framework someone else built. The work is to drop out of the framework-search entirely.
- **Don't accept "I'm having a hard time grasping it" as the problem.** The person is having a hard time grasping it because grasping is the wrong operation. The answer is not grasped; it is received from the heart. Treat the "hard time grasping" as a redirect-cue, not as a research problem to solve.
- **Don't moralize the contortion.** The person has been contorting because it seemed like the available option. The apology Joe offers is to self, with warmth — not "you've been doing it wrong." It is "I'm sorry for forcing you into something that has nothing to do with you."
