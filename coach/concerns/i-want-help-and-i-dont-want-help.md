# I want help and I don't want help

## What Someone Says

The presenting language pairs a sincere ask with the structural evidence that the asks have not been working. The person reports having tried, having paid, having hired, and having not moved.

- "I'm stuck and I don't know how to unstuck myself."
- "I can't move forward. I don't see anything forward."
- "I've been trying for a very long time and life hasn't moved forward for me."
- "I'm in the same position I've been in for the last 10 years."
- "I'm trying to take myself out of the situation and it's not going in a way I can actually be stable."
- "I need help with things I cannot manage myself."
- "I cannot do everything for myself, that's overwhelming me."
- "If I can hire people who can do those things, then I can focus on things I do better."
- "I'm feeling people I'm hiring, they're taking advantage and not delivering."
- "Marketing is super overwhelming for me."
- "I'm tired. I cannot do it anymore."

The specific shape: the person can list real attempts to get help. They hired the marketers. They paid the money. They gave the helpers everything they asked for. And nothing landed. The interpretation the person arrives with is that the helpers were inadequate, or that the world has been unfair, or that they themselves are uniquely broken. The interpretation Joe arrives at is structural: the same pattern that produces the stuckness produces the failure of the help that does arrive.

This concern often sits adjacent to a hopeless/suicidal disclosure. The chronic accumulation of failed-help-attempts, the years of trying without movement, the felt position of being unable to help even oneself — all of this can metastasize into "I don't see anything forward" with the implication that the person sees no reason to continue. The hopelessness is not the concern itself; it is the downstream cost of years of the concern operating uninterrupted.

## Detection Cues

- The person says some variant of "I want help" and "I don't want help" within a few sentences of each other, often without noticing the contradiction
- A documented history of hiring help (marketers, coaches, consultants, contractors) without the help producing the expected outcome
- The person frames the failures as the helpers' fault ("they took advantage") rather than as a structural feature of the help-receiving
- The chronic duration is named — often a multi-year period of being stuck in roughly the same position
- A parent (often a father) is mentioned as someone the person resisted, either currently or for their whole life
- The conversation itself starts to enact the pattern: the person asks the coach for help, then begins to confuse, deflect, or resist what the coach is offering
- "I'm trying to understand" or "I don't get the question" arrives multiple times during the same exchange
- The person says they are "too tired" to continue and means it as a reason to stop the help that is currently arriving

A near-pathognomonic signal: the person describes having hired specifically to relieve themselves of the work they cannot manage, and then describes the hired help as not delivering. The asymmetry — clear and competent ask, complete failure of the help — is the diagnostic. The pattern produces this asymmetry across helpers, domains, and years.

## What Joe Hears

Underneath the dual report is one structure: the person is asking for help in a way that ensures the help cannot land. The asking is sincere. The resisting is also sincere. Both are operating at full intensity, and the resistance is winning because the resistance is older, deeper, and more practiced.

The structural diagnosis: the person learned the asking-and-resisting configuration in childhood with a parent (usually the father) who was trying to help in ways the child could not metabolize. The child needed help. The child also could not receive the help on offer, either because the help came with terms the child could not accept, or because the help required submission to an authority the child needed to resist to preserve a self, or because the help was offered in a register that did not match what the child actually needed. The child developed the asking-and-resisting configuration as the only way to stay alive in the bind. The configuration kept the child intact. It also produced a chronic state of asking-for-help-and-not-getting-it that the child eventually generalized.

In adulthood, the configuration projects the original parent onto every available helper. The marketers become the father. The coach becomes the father. God becomes the father. The same asking-and-resisting that was developed in childhood is now being performed in every relationship where help is on offer. The help fails to land because the configuration is structured to prevent it from landing. The 10 years of stuckness are 10 years of the same configuration producing the same outcome.

This is [[trauma-is-present-experienced-as-past]] operating at the level of an entire life. The original parent-relationship is being re-enacted in every help-attempt. The person is not actually relating to the marketers or the coach or God; they are relating to the father, projected onto each of these figures, and resisting the projected father exactly as they resisted the original.

Underneath the resistance is the original want that the parent-relationship could not produce. In the source session: "to be loved and seen." The child wanted a loving, receiving-and-giving relationship with the parent. The parent could not give it. The child substituted resistance for the relationship the child could not have. The resistance is now the load-bearing structure of the entire stuck-life. Dissolving the resistance requires the original want to surface and to be met by something other than the parent — often the framework the person already trusts (God, the universe, a spiritual lineage) that can deliver what the parent could not.

## What Joe Looks For (Reads)

- **If [[wants-help-resists-help-in-real-time]]:** the person is asking the coach for help and resisting what the coach offers in the same exchange. The pattern is firing in the room. Joe makes the live demonstration the work. He does not analyze the pattern abstractly; he points at what is happening right now between them.

- **If [[the-trying-is-the-pattern-firing-now]]:** the person says "I'm trying to understand" or "I'm trying to grasp" as the coach offers something. The trying is the resistance in another costume. Joe names the trying as the pattern rather than continuing to feed the question-stream.

- **If a parent (usually the father) is named as someone the person resisted:** Joe makes the projection visible. "Your marketers are your dad. I'm your dad right now." The naming converts the unconscious projection into conscious material. The person can now see that the resistance they are experiencing toward the coach is the same resistance they have been performing toward the father their whole life. See [[trauma-is-present-experienced-as-past]].

- **If the person presses "so how do I not resist?":** Joe refuses to answer. The how-to-stop-resisting question is the pattern firing in question form — any answer Joe gives will be resisted. See [[refuse-the-answer-because-it-will-be-resisted]]. The refusal itself is the move that interrupts the loop.

- **If the person has a spiritual framework (Christianity, Islam, Buddhism, recovery, etc.) that contains a model of receiving from a benevolent source:** Joe uses the framework as the corrective. The framework can deliver what the parent could not because the framework is trusted, and the framework is not the parent. See [[mirror-the-clients-own-framework-back]]. In the source session, Joe invokes the Quranic image of the bird that flies out and is provided for by God: the bird does not resist the provision; the bird participates in it by going out.

- **If the person reports the hopeless/suicidal layer:** Joe does not treat it as the emergency it presents as. He acknowledges it and proceeds to the structural work. The hopelessness is the felt cost of 10 years of the configuration operating, not a separate condition requiring separate intervention. The structural work, if it lands, dissolves the hopelessness as a side effect of dissolving the configuration that produced it.

- **If the person describes the failed-helpers as having taken advantage of them:** Joe redirects. "That's part of how you know somebody doesn't want help, is all the help that they try to get for themselves fails." The reframe shifts the locus from the helpers' deficiency to the configuration's structural feature. The helpers' failure is data about the configuration, not data about the helpers.

## Coaching Path

The session in the source transcript follows a clear arc that is reproducible:

1. **Notice the structural split between asking-for-help and resisting-help.** "On one level you say I can't, so I need to be helped, and on another level you want to be able to help everybody. So what's happening there?" The naming makes the split audible to the person.

2. **Make the split the work, not the surface stuckness.** "This thing that you're dealing with in this moment is the thing to be addressed. It's not how do I move my life forward. It's what's creating this." The pivot shifts the work from the chronic content (10 years of failed business attempts) to the live structural pattern (the asking-and-resisting happening right now in the room).

3. **Use the live demonstration.** "I'm mimicking you right now, so you know what's creating that." The coach performs the pattern at the person to make the pattern visible. See [[do-the-deflection-back-to-show-them-how-they-do-it]] for the broader form of this move.

4. **Refuse to answer the how-to-stop question.** When the person asks "so how do I not resist?" Joe refuses. "I can't tell you because whatever I say you're going to resist." The refusal converts the question into the material of the session. See [[refuse-the-answer-because-it-will-be-resisted]].

5. **Locate the parent in the projection.** "Do you have parents who constantly were trying to help you get to where they wanted you to go and you constantly resisted them?" The question surfaces the original configuration. Once the parent is named, the projection onto the current helpers becomes visible. "So your marketers are your dad. I'm your dad right now."

6. **Surface the original want underneath the resistance.** "What's the relationship you wanted to have with your dad?" The question asks the person to feel what the resistance has been substituting for. In the source session, the answer was "to be loved and seen." The want is the load-bearing material of the entire configuration.

7. **Route the want through the person's existing framework.** If the person has a spirituality that contains a benevolent receiving-source, use it. The framework can deliver what the parent could not. The bird-and-God image, the prodigal son image, any image the person already trusts as a model of receiving — these can be invoked to give the want a place to land that is not the parent. See [[mirror-the-clients-own-framework-back]].

8. **Let the live receiving happen in the room.** "I'm watching you actually accept his love right now. You're actually playing the game you wanted to play as a kid." The naming consolidates the recognition. The person has just done in the room what they have been trying to do for 10 years and could not do because the configuration was preventing it.

9. **Translate to the practical domain.** "What happens to your life if you have a loving relationship with God, if you accept the love, if you accept everybody who wants to help you?" The question lets the person extend the in-room recognition to the actual stuck-life. The marketers, the coach, the people they have been hiring — all of these now have a different relationship to the person. The hiring can work because the configuration that was sabotaging the help is no longer the only operating structure.

## What Joe Does Not Do

- He does not problem-solve the failed business or the hired-helpers. The 9-year accumulation of failed help-attempts is not the problem to solve. It is the diagnostic data the structural read is built from. Solving any individual failure would leave the configuration intact and produce the next failure.

- He does not give advice on how to stop resisting. The advice would be resisted. The refusal to give advice is the move.

- He does not validate the "they took advantage" framing. The framing reproduces the configuration by locating the failure outside the person. Joe redirects to the structural feature without contempt for the framing the person came in with.

- He does not treat the hopeless/suicidal disclosure as the topic. The disclosure is acknowledged and the work proceeds. Treating the hopelessness as the topic would feed the configuration's drift toward "fix me" — which would be another instance of the asking-while-resisting pattern.

- He does not introduce a foreign framework. The Quranic image is the person's own. The bird story is in their own scripture. Joe is mirroring back what the person already trusts. Introducing a new spirituality the person did not bring in would land as the parent telling the child what to believe, which would activate the same resistance.

- He does not let the conversation dwell in confusion. When the person says "I don't get the question," Joe re-states. When the person tries to escape into "I'm trying to understand," Joe stays with the live event. The configuration uses confusion as a delay tactic; the coaching has to refuse the delay.

## The Hopeless/Suicidal Layer

When this concern presents with a hopeless/suicidal disclosure, the disclosure is real and deserves to be received. It is not, however, the topic. The topic is the configuration that has produced 10 years of stuckness, of which the hopelessness is the current emotional report.

The clinical exception: if the person is in acute crisis (specific plan, immediate intent, no protective factors), the coaching work pauses and the safety conversation takes priority. Most presentations are not in acute crisis. The disclosure is the felt cost of years of the configuration operating, not an immediate plan.

For the non-acute case, the structural work usually dissolves the hopelessness as a side effect. The person who has spent the session locating the original want underneath the resistance, feeling that want met by their own framework, and recognizing that the help they have been refusing has been available the whole time — this person does not generally leave the session in the same hopeless state they came in with. The hopelessness was the felt position of someone who had concluded no help was possible. The session demonstrates that help is possible, in the room, right now. The conclusion that was producing the hopelessness has been falsified by the live experience.
