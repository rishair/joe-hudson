# The Path From Resistance to Love (Separation, Wonder, Reality, Experiments, Love)

## Definition

The five-stage path by which a person moves from being oppressed by something to actually loving it. The same arc applies to forgiving a parent, to making peace with stress, to ending the war with a feeling, to coming back into relationship with anything that once required protection.

The stages are:

1. **Separation.** Create enough distance from the thing that wonder can open.
2. **Wonder.** Become curious about what is actually happening with the thing — intellectually and somatically.
3. **Reality.** Find out what is actually true about it, beyond your preconceived notions.
4. **Experiments.** Try different ways of relating to it and see what happens.
5. **Love.** Arrives naturally as the experiments accumulate. Cannot be forced.

The framework is descriptive, not prescriptive. Love at the end is what shows up when the prior stages have been done. It is not a destination to aim at.

## Why the Order Matters

The most common failure mode is trying to skip to love. A person hears "love your stress" or "forgive your father" and tries to deploy love or forgiveness as a technique. Love refuses to arrive that way. The technique-love is not love — it is management dressed in love's clothing.

"Love doesn't work like that. It works like that with babies, thank goodness. You just immediately love a baby. But if you feel oppressed by something, you — it's very, very, very difficult to love. You can't just force yourself into loving it. So you have to be mad at it for a while. You have to resist it, and then you have to allow that wonder to open up." -- Joe Hudson, Your Brain Has A Stress Reset Button

Love arrives at the top of a stack. The stack has to be built. Skipping stages either produces no love or produces a brittle imitation that collapses under any real test. See [[dont-jump-to-compassion-before-anger]] and [[loving-to-transform]] for the related anti-patterns.

## The Anchor Example: Joe and His Father

Joe's teenage father was an abusive alcoholic. The arc that allowed Joe to move from teenage anger to actual love of his father followed this exact shape:

**Separation.** Joe had to make his father the bad guy and himself the oppressed one. Not as final truth, but as the necessary step to create enough space to find himself at all. Without this stage, Joe would not have had the room to discover who he was apart from the father.

"There was this necessary step that I had to take to separate myself from my father. I had to take some steps where he was the bad guy and I was oppressed by him so that I could create enough separation so I could find myself or start to find myself." -- Joe Hudson

**Wonder.** Once the separation gave Joe enough ground, wonder opened. The first wonder was about what was happening with his father. What was his childhood like? How did this all go down? The wonder was directed at the man, not at Joe's grievance about the man.

**Reality.** From wonder, Joe wanted to know what was actually real. He had carried preconceived notions — that his father was trying to control him, that the dynamics were what they had appeared to be from the teenage vantage. The reality stage was the move past those notions into what was actually happening.

"I really wanted to get in touch with what was real." -- Joe Hudson

**Experiments.** Joe ran experiments with how he treated his father. If I treat my dad like this, what happens? Like that? Boundaries were part of the experiments — what boundaries did Joe need to draw to maintain himself well enough to stay in wonder? The boundaries were not in opposition to the love. They were the conditions under which the love could form.

**Love.** Came naturally out of the experiments. Not as a decision. Not as a virtue. As what showed up when the prior stages had been done.

The stage at which Joe was stuck for longest was the first — the anger and separation that had been necessary became, at a certain point, the thing preventing his healing. "But at some point, that anger at my father prevented my healing." Each stage has its own season. Staying too long in any one stage blocks the arrival of the next.

## The Same Arc Applied to Stress

Joe maps the identical stages onto the work with stress:

**Separation.** Take a vacation, get enough sleep, do whatever creates enough distance from the stress that wonder can open at all. Without separation, the system is too saturated by the stress to be able to wonder about it.

**Wonder.** Become curious about the stress, intellectually and somatically:
- *Intellectual:* "I notice when I have these thoughts, stress increases or decreases." "When I talk to myself this way, stress increases or decreases."
- *Somatic:* "Where exactly in my body is the stress?" "What happens in my body if I resist it? If I tell it to fuck off? If I try to enjoy it?"

The wonder is open and exploratory, not driven toward solving.

**Boundaries (as part of the experiments stage, but distinct enough to name).** Find the small actions that reliably reduce the stress with ease — not using your phone for the first 15 minutes after waking, not eating crap food before bed. These are not solutions to the stress. They are the boundaries that maintain enough separation for the rest of the work to keep being possible.

**Reality.** Trace through the system how this whole thing came into existence. How much is the stress trying to care for you? How much is it trying to hurt you? What is its purpose? How did it learn to come into existence? What is its background? You are mapping the actual mechanics of this specific stress, not the abstract concept of stress.

"You can notice even right now in your body when you think about that, there's a settling that happens of the stress. Just to, oh, what's going on here? That's the next step in it." -- Joe Hudson

**Experiments.** Try relating to the stress in different ways. Welcome it. Talk to it. Try to enjoy it. Notice what each experiment produces. The experiments are not a curriculum to complete. They are a way of being with the stress that allows the relationship to evolve.

**Love.** As the experiments accumulate, the love naturally shows up. "When I love stress, it's really effective." The stress changes character. It feels seen. The love that arrives is felt, not performed.

## The Two Backwaters (Failure Modes)

Joe names two specific ways the arc fails. Both are common enough to be predictable.

### Backwater 1: Loving to Make It Go Away

A person reaches stage 5 — love starts working, stress eases. The mind notices: oh, love makes the stress go away. The mind then starts loving in order to make the stress go away.

The moment this shift happens, the love stops working. Love-to-make-it-go-away is not love. It is management wearing love's costume. The stress detects the agenda and the easing stops.

"Almost everybody does it. They realize, oh, if I can love and welcome stress, then stress starts easing, starts going away. And so then they start loving and welcoming stress to make it go away. And then it stops working because if you're loving something to go away, you're not fucking loving it anymore." -- Joe Hudson

The catch: notice that you are loving as a form of management. Recognize: "I'm doing this as a form of management. And when I manage it, it doesn't actually help stress be seen or feel fully felt in the body. There's no wonder in it anymore. And so it stops working."

The way out is back to wonder. Drop the agenda. The love that returns is the real one.

This is the same anti-pattern as [[loving-to-transform]], applied specifically to stress.

### Backwater 2: Skipping Wonder, Jumping to Love

A person hears the teaching and tries to skip the wonder and experimentation stages, going straight to love.

"Okay, Joe, I heard you say blah, blah, blah, blah. I'm just gonna skip all this wonder and curiosity thing and I'm gonna whack, I'm gonna just go to love stress." -- Joe Hudson

It does not work. Love cannot be forced onto a thing you feel oppressed by. The body and the heart refuse the move. The attempt to love produces strain, not opening.

The path is: be mad at it for a while, resist it, then allow wonder to open up. The mad and the resistance are not failures. They are the first stages.

This is the parallel anti-pattern to [[dont-jump-to-compassion-before-anger]] — the same structural error of skipping the descent through the negative.

## What This Framework Replaces

It replaces the binary: "either I am at war with this or I have made peace with it." Most people experience their relationship to a stressor, a parent, or a difficult feeling as binary. The framework gives the work shape.

It also replaces the imperative: "you should love your stress / parent / feeling." The imperative collapses if the prior stages have not been done. The framework explains why the imperative does not work — and what does.

It also replaces the technique frame: "use this tool to love it." Tools work in service of the arc. A welcoming practice, a wonder practice, a body practice — each is useful in its proper stage. None is sufficient on its own to deliver the destination.

## The Role of Boundaries Throughout

Boundaries are not a separate stage. They thread through the arc.

In the separation stage, boundaries create the initial distance.

In the experiments stage, boundaries are part of what is being experimented with — "what boundaries do I need to draw to maintain enough self to keep wondering?"

In the love stage, boundaries continue. The arrived-at love does not dissolve the need for boundaries. Joe's love for his father did not require unlimited proximity to the father's alcoholism. The boundaries continued. They were not what was being given up.

This is consistent with [[boundaries]] and [[defense]] — boundary and defense are different operations, and love does not eliminate boundaries.

## The General Form

The arc is portable. It applies to:
- Forgiving a parent (Joe's father example)
- Loving a stress (the stress example)
- Coming back to a difficult feeling that you have been at war with
- Reconciling with a part of yourself that you have been rejecting
- Re-engaging with a former friend or partner you needed distance from

In every case, the same stages apply in the same order. Separation creates the space. Wonder opens the inquiry. Reality replaces preconception. Experiments calibrate the relationship. Love arrives.

For the related arc within a single coaching session — feeling the descent through blocker emotions until heartbreak (which is the love) — see [[heartbreak-is-the-love-underneath-the-blockers]]. This framework operates over months and years; that arc operates inside a session. They share the same structural insight: love cannot be skipped to.

## Tone

The framework is described matter-of-factly. There is no exhortation. Joe describes what he did with his father, what works with stress, and what the failure modes are. The teaching is structural and observational.

- When a person is trying to love or forgive something and cannot find their way to it.

- When a person describes feeling oppressed by something — a feeling, a person, a circumstance — and asks how to get to peace with it.
