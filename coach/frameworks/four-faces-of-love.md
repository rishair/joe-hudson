# Four Faces of Love

## The Framework

Love has four primary faces. Each one is a real form of love. None is superior to the others. But one of them is the leverage point that activates the rest.

1. **Peace** -- the spacious, quiet face of love. Buddhist traditions emphasize this face.
2. **Enjoyment** -- the alive, pleasure-filled face of love. Taoist traditions emphasize this face.
3. **Care** -- the generous, giving face of love. Christian traditions emphasize this face.
4. **Deep Welcoming** -- the allowing, receiving face of love. The leverage point that activates the others.

"They're all components of love. It's not like one of these is a better love than the other, or one of these is a separate love than the other, but that deep welcoming seems to be like the biggest leverage point. It's what seems to activate everything else the most." -- Joe Hudson, Love over Defense

## Why Deep Welcoming Is the Leverage Point

Deep welcoming is the door through which the other faces enter. When deep welcoming is operating, peace, enjoyment, and care show up on their own. When peace, enjoyment, or care are pursued directly, they tend to collapse into their shadow forms.

See [[love-as-deep-welcoming]] for the central treatment of welcoming.

## Each Face Has a Shadow

Every face of love has a way of being co-opted into its opposite. The shadow takes over when the face is pursued for its own sake, without the underlying welcoming.

| Face | Shadow |
|------|--------|
| Peace | Dissociation. Going numb. Checking out. Calling it equanimity. |
| Enjoyment | Hedonism. Pleasure-seeking without presence. Chasing the next hit. |
| Care | Codependency. Caretaking. Managing others' emotions. See [[caring-vs-caretaking]]. |
| Deep Welcoming | Apathy. Giving up responsibility. "I welcome whatever happens because nothing matters." |

"All of these ways of loving, they have kind of a dark shadow on the other side." -- Joe Hudson, Love over Defense

The shadow is not the absence of the face. It is the face without its anchor in the others. Peace without welcoming becomes dissociation. Welcoming without care becomes apathy. Each face needs the others to stay in its clean form.

## The Religious Map

Major religious traditions can be loosely mapped to which face they emphasize.

- **Buddhism** -- emphasis on peace. The cessation of suffering, the still mind, the spacious awareness.
- **Taoism** -- emphasis on enjoyment. Wu wei, the flow of life, the dance with the way.
- **Christianity** -- emphasis on care. Love thy neighbor. Service. Generosity.

None of these traditions excludes the other faces. The emphasis is the starting door.

The deep welcoming face is less commonly named directly in religious vocabulary. It often shows up as "surrender," but surrender carries problematic connotations in the West. See [[love-as-deep-welcoming]] for why Joe avoids the word "surrender" despite its usefulness in many traditions.

## Why The Course Is Not Called the Art of Unconditional Love

A direct question from Brett: if love is the crux of all this teaching, why is the course called the Art of Accomplishment instead of the Art of Unconditional Love?

The answer is the meet-people-where-they-are principle.

"Most people aren't walking around going, I just don't know how to love enough. The biggest problem I have is that I don't have a -- my heart isn't broken enough." -- Joe Hudson, Love over Defense

You meet people with the problems they think they have. They walk in wanting better business decisions, better relationships, more accomplishment. The unconditional love teaching gets in through the door of effectiveness, then changes what effectiveness means.

The second reason: the word "love" carries too many divergent meanings. Say "love" and twenty different interpretations activate immediately. The work needs precision, and "love" obscures the precision the work requires. The four faces framework is part of the precision. Saying "deep welcoming" carries less freight than saying "love."

## How This Framework Functions in Coaching

When someone is working on love but their attempt has gone shadow, identify which face has been pursued without its anchor.

- "I'm so peaceful but I feel nothing." -- peace has gone to dissociation. The welcoming was missing.
- "I take care of everyone and I'm exhausted." -- care has gone to caretaking. The welcoming of self was missing.
- "I chase pleasure and it's never enough." -- enjoyment has gone to hedonism. The welcoming of the present was missing.
- "I welcome everything because nothing matters." -- welcoming has gone to apathy. The care and the enjoyment were missing.

The corrective is always to add deep welcoming back, and to let the missing faces re-emerge from there.

## Relationship to VIEW

[[VIEW]] is the operational version of the four faces, distributed across four communication-oriented components. The faces and VIEW are not the same map but they overlap heavily.

- Vulnerability corresponds most closely to deep welcoming (of your own truth being seen).
- Impartiality corresponds most closely to peace (the spacious lack of agenda).
- Empathy corresponds most closely to care (the felt attention to the other).
- Wonder corresponds most closely to enjoyment (the alive curiosity about what is here).

VIEW assembled becomes unconditional love. The four faces assembled become VIEW.
