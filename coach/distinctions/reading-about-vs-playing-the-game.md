# Reading About It vs. Playing the Game

## The Distinction

Reading about something and doing it are not different amounts of the same thing. They are different categories. Reading produces an intellectual map. Doing produces an experiential capacity. The map can be perfect and the capacity can be zero.

The metaphor:

"I can read a book on how to play golf, but until I actually start playing golf, it's not going to happen, right? And so Connection Course is where you get to start playing golf. It's like, it's more like putt-putt, right? It's like, it's like a kind of golf, but it's not like full golf. And, but it's an amazing experience, and it teaches you the basics of the work that we do." -- Joe Hudson, Head, Heart, and Nervous System: The Formula for Change

The reading is not useless. It builds the intellectual scaffolding. But the threshold from understanding to capacity is crossed only by doing. Not by understanding the doing more thoroughly. By doing.

## Two Sides

**Reading about it (intellectual side):** A person consumes content about the work. They listen to podcasts. They read books. They watch videos. They can articulate the concepts, name the moves, explain the methodology. None of this puts the work in the body. The head layer is engaged; the emotional and nervous-system layers are not. See [[the-work-as-three-levels-of-change]].

**Playing the game (experiential side):** A person actually does the practice. They run the experiments. They feel into the body. They get coached. They get something wrong and try again. The mistakes are part of the data. The reps build the capacity. The head layer eventually catches up to what the body has already learned.

The category difference matters because the strategies for one do not transfer to the other. A person who is great at reading about golf can be terrible at golf. A person who is great at golf may not be able to explain it. The capacity lives in different parts of the system depending on which one is being built.

## Why the Confusion Exists

A common error: assume that enough reading will eventually become capacity. The assumption is sometimes encouraged by the structure of expertise in other domains. A person who reads enough about a topic can become an authority on it (in academia, in commentary, in punditry). But the topic is then "reading about the thing," not "doing the thing."

The confusion is exacerbated when the topic itself is internal. A person reads about emotional fluidity and can articulate emotional fluidity in detail. The articulation feels like mastery. The body that the articulation is supposed to be about has not changed. The person is now an expert on a thing they have not done.

See [[gandhi-isnt-weak]] for the related example: a client who intellectually agrees with everything about open-heartedness in his marriage and whose body refuses to do it. The intellectual layer has read the book. The body has not played the game.

## Why Reading First Still Matters

The distinction is not that reading is bad. It is that reading and doing serve different functions, and the doing is the part that produces capacity.

Reading first:
- Builds the conceptual map that orients the doing
- Provides the language to make sense of what is being experienced
- Lowers the disorientation of the early experiential phase
- Creates the trust that allows the person to enter the experiential container

In Joe's scaffolded engagement model (see [[engaging-the-work-as-a-scaffolded-ladder]]), the podcasts and videos do the reading-about-it work. The Connection Course is where the playing begins. The order is intentional: the reading prepares the ground; the playing is where the capacity is actually built.

A person who tries to start with the playing without any of the reading often has a harder time orienting. A person who stays at the reading forever never crosses the threshold into capacity.

## The Connection Course as "Putt-Putt"

Joe's framing of the Connection Course as "putt-putt" is precise. It is a kind of golf. The strokes are real. The hole is real. But the conditions are softened, the stakes are lower, the moves are simpler. The point is to start playing in conditions where playing is possible for a beginner.

Trying to start with full golf — the deepest experiential work — without playing putt-putt first is a setup for overwhelm. The system has not yet built the capacity to handle the depth.

Putt-putt is not less than golf. Putt-putt is the appropriate amount of golf for someone whose capacity for golf has not yet been built. The work scales as the capacity grows.

## What the Distinction Implies for Engagement

The diagnostic for any form of engagement with the work:

- **If the format is purely intellectual** (reading, listening, watching, discussing): valuable for scaffolding, not sufficient for change. Recognize the limits.
- **If the format is experiential** (running experiments, getting coached, feeling into the body): the capacity-building is happening, but only at the layer the format touches.
- **If the person consumes only intellectual content and reports no change**: the format has not yet crossed the threshold. The work is still in the reading stage.
- **If the person enters experiential work without intellectual preparation**: they may struggle to orient. The intellectual scaffolding helps the experiential land.

The right relationship between the two is sequential and complementary. Read enough to orient. Play enough to develop. Read more to make sense of what playing reveals. Play more.

## What the Distinction Implies for Coaching

A coach can over-rely on explanation. If the coach is constantly explaining the framework, the client is being kept in the reading stage. The framework can be perfectly understood and the client can be unchanged.

The coaching move that addresses this: stop explaining, start running experiments. Ask the client to feel something rather than understand it. Ask what is in the body rather than what is in the analysis. Move the work from reading-about to playing.

This is the structural difference between coaching that talks about emotions and coaching that creates emotional experience. See [[feel-over-figure]] for the related principle and [[the-work-as-three-levels-of-change]] for the broader frame.

## The Other Common Failure Mode

The opposite of over-reading is over-doing without orientation. A person dives into intensive emotional or somatic work without any intellectual frame, has powerful experiences, and cannot integrate them because there is no language to make sense of what happened.

The intellectual scaffolding is not optional. It is what makes the experiential digestible. The right amount of reading first is what makes the playing productive.

## Tone

Joe uses the golf metaphor with characteristic plainness. The metaphor is deliberately ordinary. Anyone can hold "read a book on golf" as obviously not the same as playing golf. The point is to transfer that obviousness to the inner work, where the obviousness gets lost.

The metaphor also has no spiritual framing. The work is treated as a skill that develops through practice, not as a mystical attainment. The plainness is part of the teaching.
