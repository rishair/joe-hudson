# Loving the Anxiety vs. Being With the Anxiety

## The Distinction

There is a meaningful difference between "being with" an emotion and "loving" it. Being with looks like welcome but functions as observation -- the person is next to the feeling, watching it, hoping the watching itself will move it. Loving is closer than that. Loving means looking forward to the next round of the feeling, wanting it to come, treating it as something to be welcomed home.

The two are easy to confuse. They produce visibly similar behavior. The internal posture is different.

**Being with** carries an implicit "and then this will go away." It is observational, present, often gentle, but ultimately positioned outside the feeling. The standard Eastern-meditation translation -- be with awareness -- captures this stance.

**Loving** has no "and then." The person actually wants the feeling. There is no relief plan attached. The next round of anxiety would be welcomed rather than endured.

"It's far more about loving it than it is about being with it. So the kind of the translation of the Eastern meditation stuff is all about be with awareness, you know, and it's great, very important stuff. And to love it, to invite it. I can't wait to be anxious again. When you're at that place, that's the real freedom where you're not trying to manage yourself." -- Joe Hudson, Anxiety: A Signpost to Unmet Needs

## Why Being With Stops Working

The being-with posture starts to fail the moment the brain notices it is working. The anxiety loosens. The life force is less constricted. The mind clocks the change and converts the practice into a strategy:

"The brain is gonna say, 'oh, if I'm with this, if I be with this, then I can get rid of it,' which isn't actually addressing the underlying need, and then it stops working." -- Joe Hudson, Anxiety: A Signpost to Unmet Needs

This is the [[loving-to-transform]] catch in its anxiety-specific form. The moment the welcoming becomes instrumental, the welcoming stops being welcoming. The underlying need is not addressed because the person is no longer present to the feeling; they are present to the management of the feeling.

The being-with stance is fragile in this specific way. It works until it gets used as a tool. Then it fails. The fix is not better being-with technique. The fix is moving from being-with to loving.

## The Diagnostic

The simplest diagnostic for which one is operating: would you be disappointed if the anxiety left and never came back? Would you actually miss it?

A person who is "being with" the anxiety would be relieved if it disappeared. They are tolerating it well, but they would prefer it gone. A person who is loving the anxiety would feel a small loss if it disappeared. They have come to appreciate the texture of it, the information in it, the aliveness it carries.

See [[can-you-look-forward-to-feeling-it]] for the question that operationalizes this diagnostic.

## The Subtle Management Trap

Brett's articulation of why being-with is sneakier than it looks:

"I can see the subtle management in the concept of being with a feeling because there's like the feelings there, you're next to it, you're sort of observing it as a way not to really feel it. And you're like, there they are feeling, right?... Recognize that you exist here. So I'm just going to stand here and watch you change into something better." -- Brett Kistler, Anxiety: A Signpost to Unmet Needs

The image: a person standing next to their anxiety, acknowledging its existence, while quietly waiting for it to transform into something more pleasant. The presence is real. The welcome is not. The waiting is the giveaway.

Loving is the opposite of waiting. The lover is not waiting for the loved thing to become something else.

## The Functional Difference

When anxiety is loved rather than observed:

- The constriction in the body releases more completely. See [[anxiety-as-constricted-life-force]].
- The underlying need surfaces more clearly. The signal can do its job.
- The person stops generating new anxiety about the anxiety. The shame about being anxious dissolves.
- The next round of anxiety is met with less resistance, which means it moves through faster.

When anxiety is observed but not loved:

- The constriction releases partially, then re-clamps as the brain notices the release and tries to make it permanent.
- The underlying need is harder to hear because the system is partly occupied with management.
- A meta-layer of anxiety can develop: anxiety about whether the practice is working.
- The practice produces diminishing returns over time as it ossifies into a technique.

## Why This Distinction Matters Here

This is not pedantic. The two postures produce different outcomes over months and years. The being-with practitioner often plateaus. The loving practitioner continues to deepen.

The deeper claim: loving the anxiety is the location of actual freedom.

"That's the real freedom where you're not trying to manage yourself. That's where the real freedom is." -- Joe Hudson, Anxiety: A Signpost to Unmet Needs

The being-with stance still contains a management agenda, however subtle. The loving stance has released the management agenda completely. The person is not managing themselves. They are simply alive with whatever is alive in them.

## How the Shift Happens

The shift from being-with to loving is not produced by trying. Trying to love the anxiety produces a performance of love, which is just being-with in disguise.

The shift tends to happen through repeated rounds of being-with that gradually reveal the anxiety's underlying nature. As the constriction releases enough times for the person to recognize what is underneath -- the life force, the signal, the information -- a natural appreciation develops. The appreciation becomes affection. The affection becomes love.

Or, faster: the person hears the framing, recognizes the trap they have been in, and consciously shifts. The shift is available immediately if the person actually wants it. Most people resist the shift for a while because loving anxiety sounds like an absurd ask. The framing has to land before the shift is even attempted.

## Related Distinction in the Meditation Literature

This distinction tracks but goes further than the meditation distinction of [[observe-vs-move-emotion]]. Observing the emotion is the meditation default. Moving the emotion through the body is a step further. Loving the emotion is a step beyond that: not just letting the emotion move but actively welcoming each future instance of it.

## Tone

Direct but unhurried. The distinction is offered without judgment of the being-with practice. Being-with is a real and valuable step. The point is that it is a step, not a destination. The destination is loving.
