# Purpose Is How You Do It, Not What You Do

## The Distinction

The standard frame: purpose is a specific activity, career, or calling. The wrong job is the wrong purpose. The right job is the right purpose. Find the right one and you are in purpose; stay in the wrong one and you are not.

The reframe: purpose is the quality of action, not the content of action. The same activity can be in purpose or out of purpose depending on how it is being done. The wrong activity done in alignment can be more in purpose than the right activity done out of alignment. The how is the purpose; the what is the vehicle.

"It's really not about what you're doing either, which is really interesting. It's really about how you're doing it." -- Joe Hudson, The Myth Of Finding Your Purpose

This distinction collapses the years-long search for the right thing. The right thing was never the answer. The question was always about how the person was being in whatever they were doing.

## Two People, Same Activity

Two people can do the same job. One is in their purpose; the other is not. The difference is not the job. The difference is what the person is bringing to the job — the felt orientation, the aliveness, the willingness to be stretched, the willingness to be cracked open, the doing-it-for-themselves rather than for approval.

The sharpest formulation collapses the activity-axis entirely: "No matter what I'm doing, whether I'm picking up trash for a living or whether I'm the President of the United States, how I do it is more of an expression of my purpose than what I'm doing." -- guest, You Won't Find Your Purpose By Searching For It

The trash-collector and the president bracket the whole range of social status, income, prestige, and influence. The claim is that the activity-axis does not capture purpose at any point along it. The president can be out of their purpose. The trash collector can be in theirs. The how-quality is the unit of measurement; the activity is the medium through which the how is expressed.

Two activities, same person. Brett describes BASE jumping, business, traveling, meeting people across cultures — and finds the through-line is exploration. The content varies; the how is constant. The purpose lives in the how-of-exploring, not in any one of those activities.

"I could look at the contents of what I've done in my life or the contents of what I do in my career, and that might tell me less than how it is that I prefer to move about the world and what kinds of ways of relating to my experiences move me the most deeply." -- Brett Kistler, The Myth Of Finding Your Purpose

The contents tell less than the how. The how is the through-line.

## The Through-Line

Looking back at a varied life, the through-line is not the activities. It is the quality the person brought to the activities. Joe describes his own: songs, comic strips, art, then business. The activities changed completely. The thread underneath did not.

"There's a way in which I was constantly interested in who am I, what am I, how do humans work, how do we see ourselves more clearly. It was always there." -- Joe Hudson, The Myth Of Finding Your Purpose

The through-line is visible only in retrospect. The person living it does not see it as a thread; they see it as scattered activities that did not stick. Looking back, the scattered activities all turn out to have been one thing — done in different forms.

## How to Tell the How

The how-of-purpose has identifiable felt-signatures. It is not a vague quality. Specific markers:

- **It is a little scary.** If it is important to you, it is going to be a little scary. It is going to take some oomph.
- **It requires you to go beyond what you think is possible.** It stretches the edge.
- **It cracks you open.** Like real love, real purpose breaks your heart open. See [[purpose-cracks-you-open]].
- **It produces aliveness.** The energy moves. The system reports vivacity.
- **It is done for the doer, not for approval.** See [[do-it-for-yourself-to-find-purpose]].

If none of these are present, the activity is in the right shape but the how is not the how of purpose. The reverse is also true: if all of these are present in an unremarkable-looking activity, the activity is being done in purpose.

## The Diagnostic Reframes Everything

If purpose is in the how, the relevant question is not "what should I do?" The relevant question is "how am I being in what I am already doing?" The search collapses immediately. The years of trying different activities resolve immediately. The person is not in the wrong activity; the person is bringing the wrong how.

This also reframes what it means to "change to be more in purpose." It does not mean leaving the job. It might. But more often it means shifting how the current activity is being done — bringing more aliveness, more honesty, more willingness to be stretched, more doing-it-for-yourself. The shift happens in the how, not in the what.

"There's been no moment that I can find where I don't know if I'm more aligned or less aligned with my purpose. How I'm being, how I'm thinking, how I am with you, how I am with myself, all of that, there's a purpose in that." -- Joe Hudson, The Myth Of Finding Your Purpose

In any moment, the person can read how aligned they are. The question is always live. The answer is always available.

## The "How Do I Explore Purposefully?" Move

Brett's reframe of his own question captures the distinction in one sentence:

"How do I explore purposefully?" -- Brett Kistler, The Myth Of Finding Your Purpose

Not "what should I explore?" but "how should I explore?" The same question can be asked of any activity. How do I write purposefully? How do I cook purposefully? How do I do my job purposefully? Each version produces a different inquiry than the what-question. The what-question sends the person looking outward for an activity; the how-question turns the person inward toward the quality they are bringing.

## The Whole Company Could Collapse

The test of whether purpose lives in the how is: what if the activity disappeared? If the activity disappeared and the person would still be in purpose, the purpose was always in the how. If the activity disappeared and the purpose disappeared with it, the person was attached to the what, not living the how.

"I have no doubt in my mind that this whole company, my whole career could completely collapse and I would still be living my purpose. It's not about what I do. It's about how do I do it?" -- Joe Hudson, The Myth Of Finding Your Purpose

This is the freedom of the how-frame. The person becomes uncollapsable. The purpose cannot be taken away because it is not in any particular form. The forms change; the how stays.

## Why People Default to the What

The what-frame is easier to imagine, talk about, and pursue. It produces a clear external target. The mind likes targets. "I will find the right career" is a coherent project; "I will bring a different quality to whatever I am doing" is harder to operationalize.

The what-frame is also where approval lives. Specific activities are recognizable, namable, achievable. The how-frame is invisible from the outside; nobody can approve you for the quality you are bringing to the moment. See [[purpose-as-approval-seeking-in-disguise]].

The what-frame is also where the search-state lives. The mind can search for an activity. The mind cannot search for a quality of being; quality of being is received, not searched. See [[searching-for-purpose-pushes-it-away]].

For all these reasons, the what-frame is the default and the how-frame requires a deliberate shift.

## The Same Distinction Applied to Gifts

The how-vs-what distinction has a parallel form for gifts. The standard frame: gifts are specific skills, abilities, or capabilities. Find the right skill and that is your gift. The reframe: gifts are the felt-quality of nature, not the content of skill. The same activity, done by two people, comes out differently because of the different qualities each brings — and those qualities are the gifts. See [[your-gift-is-your-nature-not-your-skill]].

The "I help people die better" story illustrates this perfectly. The world-famous coach was not deploying a skill when he gave that answer; he was coming from his space — his nature. The gift was the space, not the answer. The space produced the answer. See [[i-help-people-die-better]].

This is why the search for the right superpower-label fails for the same reason the search for the right purpose-activity fails. Both searches treat the unit-of-purpose as a what (a skill, a label, an activity) when the unit is actually a how (a quality, a nature, a space). The label-search collapses into nature-recognition the same way the activity-search collapses into how-of-being.

## When the Activity Does Matter

The distinction is not "the activity doesn't matter at all." Some activities make it harder to bring the how-of-purpose; some make it easier. A job that requires constant self-betrayal is going to be a harder vehicle for purpose than a job that allows the person to be themselves.

But the activity is the vehicle, not the purpose. A good vehicle does not produce purpose; it only makes the how easier to live. A bad vehicle does not prevent purpose; it only makes the how harder to live. The person is responsible for the how regardless of the vehicle.

The mature practice: choose a vehicle that supports the how, and then bring the how. Not "find the right vehicle and the purpose will follow." The how does not follow from anything external; it has to be brought.

## Tone

Slightly amused, slightly relieved. The distinction is delivered as a structural correction, not a heavy teaching. The person has been working very hard at finding the right thing; the relief is recognizing that the question was wrong. The lightness invites the person to drop the search rather than try harder.
