# Suffering vs. Pain

## Definition

Pain is what happens. Suffering is the resistance to the pain. The distinction comes from Buddhist teaching and Joe invokes it directly.

Pain is the emotion, the loss, the discomfort. Suffering is what happens when the person will not let themselves feel the pain: the avoidance, the stories about the pain, the fight against it.

"Suffering is the resistance to the pain." -- Joe Hudson (referencing the Buddhist distinction), If You Can't Love the Feeling, Love the Resistance

## In Practice

A person loses a parent and feels grief. That is pain. The same person refuses to think about the parent because every memory hurts. That is suffering. The grief itself would move through if allowed. The refusal to feel it is what locks the person into rigidity, denial, and unfelt emotion.

This distinction is the foundation of [[resistance]]. All resistance involves some degree of suffering: the refusal to feel what is present. The path through is not to eliminate the pain but to stop adding resistance to it. See [[feeling-the-unfelt-emotion]].
