# Short-Term vs. Long-Term Partiality

## Definition

Not all partiality operates the same way. Short-term partiality is fear-based: grasping at an immediate outcome to avoid a feeling. Long-term partiality is principles-based: orienting toward a vision of who you want to be and what world you want to live in.

"I think short-term partiality is far more fear-based than long-term partiality. I think long-term partiality is far more principles-based. It's like, how do I want to be, and what's the world that I want to be in, and what's my vision for the world?" -- Joe Hudson, Impartiality -- Connection Course Series #3

## The Two Sides

**Short-term partiality (fear-based):**
- Driven by wanting to avoid a feeling right now.
- Produces urgency, grasping, and rigidity.
- The person is trying to force the world to comply.
- More likely to generate avoidance of difficult conversations, messy problems, and uncomfortable truths.

**Long-term partiality (principles-based):**
- Driven by a vision of how one wants to live.
- The person watches for how the world naturally provides and takes advantage of those moments rather than forcing outcomes.
- Willingness to go through difficulty in service of a larger direction.
- Less effort, more happiness, even when the path is hard.

## The Kitchen Drawer Theory

A CEO Joe worked with called this the "kitchen drawer theory." Every home has a drawer nobody wants to look at because it is a mess. His job as CEO was to find all the kitchen drawers and go look in them. That is long-term, principles-based partiality: the commitment to face messes rather than avoid them, because facing messes produces the life you want.
