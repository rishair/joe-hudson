# Wants vs. Partiality

## Definition

Wants are owned. Partiality is not. That is the core distinction. Having goals, desires, and preferences is not partiality. Hiding them while maneuvering to get them met is.

"The main difference is that wants are owned and partiality is not owned. So to own your wants outright in a conversation is quite vulnerable. It allows you to feel exposed, and you're telling people your actual truth." -- Joe Hudson, Impartiality -- Connection Course Series #3

Partiality is implied, concealed, operating under the surface. Wants stated openly are compatible with [[impartiality]]. In fact, stating wants clearly is an act of [[vulnerability]].

## The Two Sides

**Wants (compatible with impartiality):**
- Stated openly: "What I really want is for us all to be aligned."
- Vulnerable: the person feels exposed in admitting what they care about.
- Visible: because they are named, they can be worked with, negotiated, integrated with others' wants.
- Relieve intensity when owned: "Oh, I want for you to be different than you are, and I apologize for that." Naming the want can dissolve its grip.

**Partiality (the thing to dissolve):**
- Hidden: the person maneuvers toward the outcome without naming it.
- Self-serving: others feel the agenda but cannot address it because it is not stated.
- Sticky: because the want is not seen clearly, it cannot be released or worked with.
- Exhausting: requires managing impressions, controlling conversations, steering without being caught.

## Why Wants Stay Hidden

Often, partiality is not strategic. It is shame-based. The person has a want they are ashamed of having, so they try to get it met without admitting it exists.

"We have a want that we're ashamed of having. And so we're trying to get our wants met without having to admit that we have them." -- Joe Hudson, Impartiality -- Connection Course Series #3

This connects [[shame-drives-behavior]] to the mechanics of partiality. The shame about wanting keeps the want underground. The underground want drives partial behavior. The partial behavior is felt by others and breaks trust.

## Why Owning Wants Dissolves Partiality

When a want is fully owned, the person returns to themselves. They realize what is underneath: wanting safety, wanting love, wanting to matter. The want becomes an emotion to feel rather than an outcome to engineer.

"When you own your want completely, ownership can actually make the want less intense and allow you to see what the world is like." -- Joe Hudson, Impartiality -- Connection Course Series #3

This is why owning partiality mid-conversation is both an act of [[vulnerability]] and a return to [[impartiality]]. Example: "I noticed that I'm giving you advice. I'm trying to fix you. How do you feel about that?"

## Upstream (What Leads Here)

- When someone is being partial in a conversation and needs a path back to [[impartiality]].
- When someone confuses having wants with being controlling.
- When a manager fears that impartiality means abandoning goals.

## Downstream (Where This Leads)

- Owning wants creates space for collaboration: everyone's wants become visible and workable.
- Leads to boundary-setting: if the want is owned, a boundary can be stated.
- Connects to [[vulnerability]]: owning a want is inherently vulnerable.
