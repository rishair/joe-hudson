# Empathy

## Definition

Empathy is being with a person in their emotions. Not avoiding their emotional state. Not trying to change it. Not trying to make them happy or push them toward an epiphany. Not losing yourself in their experience. Being next to them. Holding their hand.

"True empathy is being with somebody, it's being next to them, it's like holding their hand, it's not losing yourself in them." -- Joe Hudson, Introduction to VIEW

## What Empathy Is Not

- **Not fixing.** Trying to make someone feel better is not empathy. It is a response to your own discomfort with their emotion.
- **Not merging.** Feeling whatever the other person feels until you lose your own emotional state is not empathy. It is codependence.
- **Not agreeing with the story.** Saying "you're right, your boss is terrible" is validating a narrative, not being with a person's emotion.
- **Not absorbing shame.** When someone is ashamed, empathy is not joining them in shame ("Oh, you're right, I am the bad guy"). That is losing yourself.

## In Practice

Empathy is the E in [[view]]. It requires maintaining your own center while being fully present to another person's emotional experience. The person feels accompanied, not managed.

Empathy combined with [[impartiality]] creates safety. The person senses that you are with them AND that you have no agenda to change them. This is what allows them to relax, be honest, and move at their own pace.

## When Someone Experiences This

The absence of empathy is instantly felt. When someone shares something emotional and meets a blank wall or a quick redirect, trust erodes. When someone shares and is met with genuine presence without agenda, something relaxes in their system. This is often the moment people become willing to go deeper.

## Upstream (What Leads Here)

- When a person is emotional and the temptation is to fix, redirect, or analyze.
- When someone has shared something and the conversation feels like it wants to speed past the feeling.
- When the coach notices an impulse to make things better rather than be present.

## Downstream (Where This Leads)

- The person feels safe enough to access what is underneath the surface emotion.
- Creates the conditions for [[vulnerability]] in the other person.
- Combined with [[wonder]], opens the space for genuine exploration rather than performance.
