# Measuring Transformation Across Three Brains (Head, Heart, Gut)

## Definition

Transformation can be measured, but only carefully. Each of the three brains has its own marker. Looking at all three together gives a fuller picture of where the person is in the work than any single measure can.

- **Head (prefrontal cortex):** sense of self and sense of time, becoming more universal over development.
- **Heart (mammalian brain):** [[emotional-fluidity]] — the capacity to feel what arises in a non-reactionary way, no matter what it is.
- **Gut (reptilian brain / nervous system):** [[pleasure-capacity]] — the capacity to enjoy pleasure, defined as awareness of sensations in the body without resistance.

"The way I like to measure it is to say we have 3 brains... you want to measure how the progress is in each." -- Joe Hudson, How To Measure Your Progress In Personal Development & Transformation

## The Map Is Not the Territory

Before the measurement itself, the disclaimer. Any measurement tool used to track transformation is a map. It is not the road being driven. The person looking at the map is not actually driving on the road.

"If you're going to use a measurement tool, it's more of like a map and it is not the territory... You're not actually driving on the road. You're looking at a map." -- Joe Hudson, How To Measure Your Progress In Personal Development & Transformation

This matters because the measurement is the thing most likely to be misused. People take measurement tools and immediately weaponize them into self-attack: "I'm not there yet. I haven't done it yet. I'm not high enough yet." See [[dont-use-measurement-to-beat-yourself-up]] for the dedicated anti-pattern.

The right relationship to the measurement: it gives orientation. It is not the work itself. It is not even the most important thing. It is a sometimes-useful overview of a process that is happening whether or not anyone is measuring it.

## The Head Marker: Universality of Sense of Self and Sense of Time

On the head level, the marker is how universal the sense of self and the sense of time have become.

### Sense of Time

Time horizon expands with development. A two-year-old cannot get excited about a birthday in 8 months — the time scale is too large. A 50-year-old can feel excited about retirement 8 months away. Time horizon expands further: from days, to weeks, to two-to-four-year planning, to generational thinking ("I'm doing something today for my grandkids"). Then at some point it implodes into the present.

"At some point, the thing implodes on itself or seemingly implodes on itself and there's just the present." -- Joe Hudson, How To Measure Your Progress In Personal Development & Transformation

The implosion is not loss of time-awareness. It is the recognition that the future-past axis was always a construction the mind was using, and that the actual living happens here. See [[sense-of-time-expansion]] for the dedicated article.

### Sense of Self

The same shape. The self begins undifferentiated (the small child who assumes mom wants what the child wants — "GI Joe with a kung fu grip"). It differentiates (the child can see mom wants a Tesla and dad wants a tie). Then it expands: me, family, community, tribe, nation, humanity, the ecosystem. At each level the sense of who "I" am gets larger. Then, like time, it implodes: everything is you and you are everything and/or nothing.

"Sense of self that is me to family to community to tribe to nation and then it continues to grow into humanity and it continues to grow into like the earth... But at some point, again, it implodes on you, and all of a sudden you see yourself as everything." -- Joe Hudson, How To Measure Your Progress In Personal Development & Transformation

See [[expanding-sense-of-self]] for the development. The two markers — sense of time and sense of self — move on parallel tracks toward the same universality and the same implosion.

## The Heart Marker: Emotional Fluidity

On the heart level, the marker is [[emotional-fluidity]] — the capacity to feel what arises in a non-reactionary way and to express it in a non-reactionary way, no matter what it is. Pain, sadness, fear, joy, excitement — the system can move through all of them.

"Emotional fluidity basically means that you can feel what arises in a non-reactionary way, express what arises in a non-reactionary way, no matter what it is." -- Joe Hudson, How To Measure Your Progress In Personal Development & Transformation

The symptom — the visible downstream effect — is a lot more joy. The repressed anger gets felt and moved, and joy comes in. The repressed sadness gets felt and moved, and joy comes in. See [[joy-as-matriarch]] for the mechanism.

The qualitative shift: at first, allowing emotions feels like pain. The pain is the resistance to the emotion, not the emotion itself. As the resistance drops, emotions stop feeling like discrete categories and start feeling like aspects of one experience.

"It becomes less like there's sadness and there's fear and there's excitement. And it becomes far more like aspects of a diamond with like slightly different tonalities to it." -- Joe Hudson, How To Measure Your Progress In Personal Development & Transformation

This is the heart-level signature of progress: emotions becoming facets rather than categories, with the resistance-pain dropping out.

## The Gut Marker: Pleasure Capacity

On the gut (nervous system) level, the marker is [[pleasure-capacity]] — the capacity to allow pleasure in.

The definition Joe offers, with his refinement: pleasure is "the awareness of energy moving in your body." Joe's edit: pleasure is more precisely "awareness of sensations in your body without resistance."

"One of the greatest definitions I've ever heard for pleasure is the awareness of energy moving in your body. I would say that it's more awareness of sensations in your body without resistance." -- Joe Hudson, How To Measure Your Progress In Personal Development & Transformation

The mechanism: the more pleasure the body can hold, the more the nervous system reads itself as safe. The reverse is also true — when the system is safe, pleasure is the natural state.

"And the more pleasure that we can feel, the more that our nervous system thinks it's safe. If we are safe, then naturally we take pleasure in being alive." -- Joe Hudson, How To Measure Your Progress In Personal Development & Transformation

The somatic signature of safety: looking at the sunset and not waiting for the other shoe to drop. Being in a great conversation and not bracing for an attack. Not anticipating trouble.

"You're not looking for the other foot to fall. You're not looking for the punch in the face, the attack that's coming. You're not thinking you're gonna get in trouble. You're just like in this place of enjoyment and pleasure." -- Joe Hudson, How To Measure Your Progress In Personal Development & Transformation

And the meaning of pleasure here is not the curated pleasure of massage or yoga. It is the immediate, here-and-now pleasure: the pleasure of a breath in. The capacity expands without ceiling. There is no maximum.

"There's not like, oh, now I have maximum pleasure. That's— it's like not how it works." -- Joe Hudson, How To Measure Your Progress In Personal Development & Transformation

## The Three Markers Together

Each marker is incomplete on its own. Together they give a fuller read.

A person can have a head-level shift (the sense of self has expanded into nation or humanity) while still being emotionally constricted and nervous-system-clamped. Another person can have deep emotional fluidity but a head-level identity that has not loosened. A third can have a settled nervous system that allows pleasure but a contracted sense of self and an emotional flatness.

The map is fully informative only when all three are read together. This parallels the structure of the work itself, which operates on all three layers simultaneously. See [[the-work-as-three-levels-of-change]] for why the three-layer structure is foundational rather than incidental.

## How This Relates to the Leading-Indicator Principle

The three brains framework gives lagging indicators — markers of where transformation has actually occurred. There is also a leading indicator: willingness to embrace intensity. See [[embracing-intensity-as-leading-indicator]].

The relationship: the leading indicator predicts what will appear in the lagging indicators. A person with high willingness to embrace intensity will move on all three markers over time. A person with low willingness will not, regardless of how much measurement is happening.

The leading indicator is what to look at to predict change. The three-brain markers are what to look at to confirm change has happened. Both are useful. Confusing them — measuring the lagging when the question is "is this person going to move?" or measuring the leading when the question is "how far has this person come?" — produces noise.

## The Closing Disclaimer

The teaching ends with three statements that recontextualize the entire measurement frame:

1. **There is no rush to get anywhere.** Transformation is not a race.
2. **You are not going to be a better person on the other side.** The improvement frame does not apply to where this leads. See [[improvement-vs-authenticity]] and [[there-is-no-better-person-on-the-other-side]].
3. **On the other side, the idea of better-or-worse goes away, and the idea of a you that's there to be measured starts to fade away as well.**

"There is no rush to get anywhere. You're not going to be a better person on the other side. Actually, on the other side, the idea of better or worse goes away, and the idea of like a you that's there to be measured starts to fade away as well." -- Joe Hudson, How To Measure Your Progress In Personal Development & Transformation

The map is for the journey. The territory is what dissolves the need for the map. The measurement is for the early and middle phases of the work. The deeper phases dissolve the measurer.

## When Someone Experiences This

- A person asking "how do I know if I'm making progress?" can use this as the orientation.
- A coach trying to assess where a client is can read across all three markers.
- A person stuck on one marker can be redirected to whether the other two are moving.
- Someone who has weaponized their personal development into self-attack can be redirected to the map-is-not-the-territory framing.
