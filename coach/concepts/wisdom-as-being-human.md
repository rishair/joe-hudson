# Wisdom Is Being Really Good at Being Human

## Definition

Wisdom is being really good at being human. The two are not analogous; they are the same. The capacities that make a person wise are the capacities that make a person human in the full sense: capable of feeling what is here, deciding from a clean place, seeing the patterns in themselves and others, connecting with others as themselves rather than as roles, walking toward what others are avoiding.

"And I would to some degree call wisdom being really good at being human." -- Joe Hudson, How to Succeed in the Age of AI

The framing collapses a distinction the culture treats as separate. In the standard frame, "wisdom" is a special add-on that some people develop and others don't, and "being human" is just the default condition of breathing. In this frame, the default condition of breathing is unfinished. Being human is something you become more or less of. The thing the culture has been calling "wisdom" is just the more-of version.

## What Being Human Includes

In practice, the capacities Joe groups under "being human" cover most of the AOA methodology:

- Feeling your emotions fully and letting them cycle through ([[emotional-fluidity]], [[emotional-inquiry]])
- Making decisions from a clean state rather than from compulsion ([[decisions-are-emotional]])
- Doing the hard thing and feeling the difficult thing
- Walking into what everyone else is avoiding ([[run-toward-friction]])
- Connecting with other humans in a way that does not require self-abandonment
- Having the conversations that companies and families fail by avoiding
- Recognizing patterns in yourself and others, including the patterns of how people are showing up that the person themselves cannot see ([[reads]]) -- including the real-time reads that determine outcomes in high-stakes interactions; see [[ai-as-equalizer-forces-honest-game]] for the case study of Joe noticing an unconscious intimidation move in a legal counterparty and not reacting in the way the move was designed to produce
- Eliciting good input from others, creating alignment among small groups
- Knowing your own value and the value of others independent of what is produced

These are not separate "soft skills." They are what it takes to be a fully realized human, and they are also what it takes to make wise decisions and to lead in an AI-saturated environment.

## Why Being Human Has Become the Skill

For decades, possibly centuries, the way to survive was to be machine-like. To do the factory job. To do the accounting work. To do the spreadsheet work. To do the email-processing work. Each generation got better at the machine work because the machine work was what produced income.

"We have been training ourselves to become computers... And once we got really, really good at doing that, we finally invented computers who can really be computers in the way that we want them to be... But one of the things that that's doing is, in my view, it seems like it's opening up the capacity to step back and be human the way we wanna be again, in new ways." -- Joe Hudson, How to Succeed in the Age of AI

The trade-off was real. People got good at the machine work and forgot how to be human in the larger senses. Families became more dysfunctional. People felt disconnected from themselves and others. The capacity to be in community got atrophied. The metric that mattered was production, so the parts of being human that did not produce got pruned.

The arrival of actual machines that can do the machine work changes what is needed. The capacities that were pruned are now the capacities that matter. The pruning was always a cost; now it is a competitive disadvantage too.

## Being Human Is Not Restricted to Joe's Methodology

The concept is broader than any specific practice. When Joe references the indigenous people who looked at the West and said "you're not acting human" -- meaning, you go away from your family eight hours a day to support your family, which doesn't make sense -- the point lands without requiring any particular framework. Being human is something most humans can recognize when they see it and recognize when they are not doing it.

"There was something that I keep on thinking about that they did see. They did see, Oh, this basic thing of be with family, be with community, support community, support themselves. That is something that's inherent to us. It's inherent to chimpanzees. It's inherent to, I think, all primates. It is just inherent in us to be collective, to be in connection with ourselves and with others. And it's something that we've lost because that wasn't the way to survive for the last, whatever it was, 3 or 4 decades." -- Joe Hudson, How to Succeed in the Age of AI

The capacity is innate. The training is in not losing it. The work of the AOA methodology is, in this framing, the work of remembering how to be the thing you already are.

## When Someone Experiences This

The person who is being human in this larger sense reports:
- Decisions feel cleaner and require less re-doing
- Conversations land more often without rehearsal
- Family time feels like the point, not the consolation prize
- Work feels chosen rather than endured
- The body settles when the work is done; the work does not bleed into everything
- Other people feel met, not managed
- The need to perform recedes; the cost of pretending gets higher than the cost of just being

The person who has been operating in machine-mode reports the opposite: decisions get re-litigated, conversations require scripts, family time is squeezed in, work is a sacrifice that gets justified by what it pays for, the body never settles, other people feel like obstacles or audiences, the performance is more comfortable than the dropping of it. The second set is what the era is asking to be released. The first set is what the era is making it more possible to live.

## The Risk in This Era

The framing also names what is at stake. If the time that AI frees up does not get redirected into building the capacity to be human, the alternative is societal dysfunction. The historical pattern: when a population has time and no purpose, the time becomes revolution and dysfunction. The era is offering a redirection. The redirection has to be chosen at scale.

"So if we do not find a way to take all the extra time that's potentially created to create a deeper level of purpose, like how to be more human, then the alternative is, oh, we could actually have a society that starts imploding on itself because it's lacking that purpose." -- Joe Hudson, How to Succeed in the Age of AI

The individual move is the same as the societal move at smaller scale: take the freed capacity and use it to develop the human skills. The compendium exists, in part, to make that direction more accessible to the people who want it.

## How Joe Talks About It

The tone is matter-of-fact. The framing of wisdom as being-human is not delivered as a profound spiritual claim; it is delivered as a practical observation. The capacity in question is the same capacity that lets a family work, a team work, a company work, a decision land. Joe is not lecturing about a separate domain of personal growth; he is naming what has always been the differentiator and is now the only one left.
