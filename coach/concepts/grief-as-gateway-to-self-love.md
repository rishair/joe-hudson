# Grief as Gateway to Self-Love

## Definition

A watershed moment in healing a pattern: the person grieves the fact that they have been doing this to themselves for years or decades. They see how much they have missed. This grief is not a setback. It is the precursor to loving the part of themselves that carried the pattern.

## In Practice

"There's a moment where they grieve the fact that they've been doing this to themselves or they've been agreeing to this for the last 20 or 30 years and that they could have -- and that moment is often the precursor to loving themselves or loving that aspect of themselves. That grief." -- Joe Hudson, A Discussion with Dr. Bessel van der Kolk & Joe Hudson

The grief is not about the original wound. It is about the time spent in the pattern after the original wound. The person sees clearly: "I have been recreating this for decades. I did not have to." That recognition, fully felt, opens the door to self-compassion.

This connects to shame: the part of the pattern that is hardest to face is not what was done to the person but what the person did in response. The ways they complied, adapted, repeated. Grieving that, rather than defending it, is the turn.

## Upstream (What Leads Here)

- After [[feeling-the-unfelt-emotion]] when the person sees the pattern clearly for the first time
- After [[trauma-mapping]] patterns have been named and the person realizes how long they have been operating

## Downstream (Where This Leads)

- Toward [[loving-all-parts]], particularly the parts that carried the pattern
- Toward resolution rather than management (see [[managing-vs-healing-trauma]])
