# Expanding Sense of Self

## Definition

The sense of self develops and expands over time. A child begins with no distinction between self and world (the child wants GI Joe, so mom must want GI Joe too). Then the self differentiates. Then it expands: from "I am me" to "I am my family" to "I am my nation" to "I am the ecosystem."

At each stage, the parts of the self belong to the larger whole. The ambition and will feel less personal and more universal. "In some aspects, far more gentle, and in some aspects, far more powerful." -- Joe Hudson, If You Can't Love the Feeling, Love the Resistance

## In Practice

This expansion is not linear or clean. People get stuck at intermediate stages. Overidentification with nation or political party is the sense of self expanding to group level but stopping there. "People like get lost in like, I am the nation, or I am my political party. And if they feel offended, it's not just them that are offended." -- Joe Hudson, If You Can't Love the Feeling, Love the Resistance

At advanced stages, the desire to be different transforms. Early in personal development, the drive is: "I want to be better." Later: "I don't want anything to be different. I just want to be me. I want to be as me as me can be. I want to see what I am." The friction of wanting to improve stops serving the person. Development continues, but the engine shifts from self-improvement to self-discovery.

## Collaboration and Stages

The level of the sense of self affects collaboration capacity. At some stages, people fall into false consensus (everyone's voice is equal, all decisions by committee). Joe references a 1940s/1950s CIA manual on destroying organizations: delegate all decisions to committees of more than 6. This is what dysfunctional organizations do naturally.

Eventually, collaboration becomes natural because [[selfishness-is-the-engine-of-unity]]. Individual perspectives remain distinct but serve the larger system. "Selfishness is the engine of unity." Effective collaboration requires individuals with different perspectives, not consensus that erases difference.

## Upstream (What Leads Here)

- When someone asks "who am I doing this for?"
- When personal ambition starts feeling hollow or forced
- When the question "should I be selfish or generous?" arises (see [[selfishness-is-the-engine-of-unity]])
- When [[resistance]] to the current self-concept intensifies

## Downstream (Where This Leads)

- Toward patience with the developmental process. Do not rush to get to the stage where wanting disappears. The wanting phase has its own wisdom.
- Toward [[impartiality]]: as the sense of self expands, attachment to personal outcomes loosens.
- Toward better collaboration, not through technique but through a wider sense of who "I" includes.
