# Impartiality

## Definition

Impartiality is not trying to achieve an outcome for yourself or others. It is the difference between driving to work on a fixed route at a fixed time and taking a Sunday drive with no destination. Both involve driving. One is controlled by agenda; the other is open to what arises.

Complete impartiality is asymptotic. Every sentence carries some degree of agenda. The practice is to move steadily toward less partiality, not to achieve zero.

"It's far more like wandering than it is like goal orientation." -- Joe Hudson, Introduction to VIEW

## In Practice

Impartiality is the I in [[view]] and the component most people find hardest to grasp, especially in business.

The common objection: without partiality, nothing gets done. The reframe: clear intention and clear goals are compatible with impartiality. The partiality that kills effectiveness is attachment to *how* the goal is reached. A basketball team has a clear goal (put the ball in the hoop) but cannot plan every pass in advance. The player who is open to what emerges makes the right pass. The player locked into a scripted play passes to a covered teammate.

"If I can be impartial in these ways, not give up my goal, but be impartial in how this thing happens and be impartial with people, I actually discover what is. And then I can use that information and use that data to create a better plan." -- Joe Hudson, Introduction to VIEW

In business, impartiality reveals a natural current: where customers want to go, where employees want to go, what the market needs. Reading and following this current takes less effort than fighting it. Going against the flow is like building a canal instead of following the river.

## When Someone Experiences This

People resist impartiality because they equate it with passivity or indifference. It is neither. Impartiality does not mean having no opinions or boundaries. It means holding opinions and boundaries openly while remaining genuinely open to being wrong about them.

When practiced, impartiality produces a notable increase in efficiency. Ten-minute VIEW conversations outperform hour-long agenda-driven meetings because the agenda is not constricting the flow of information.

## Upstream (What Leads Here)

- When someone is trying to control an outcome and creating resistance.
- When a conversation feels like a power struggle.
- When someone notices they are "selling" rather than exploring.

## Downstream (Where This Leads)

- Opens access to information that agenda-driven conversation blocks.
- Allows the best solution to emerge rather than forcing a predetermined one.
- Combined with [[empathy]], creates the safety others need to speak honestly.

## Boundary Conditions

Impartiality does not mean surrender. Stating "this doesn't work for me" is compatible with impartiality. The key distinction: hold boundaries firmly while remaining open to hearing why someone sees it differently. If convinced, update the boundary. If not, maintain it honestly rather than pretending to agree.
