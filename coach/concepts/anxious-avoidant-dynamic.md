# The Anxious-Avoidant Dynamic (Chasing as the Push-Away)

## Definition

The configuration where one partner pursues and the other withdraws. From inside, the pursuer experiences the partner as unavailable and themselves as the wanting one. From outside, the pursuit itself is the structure that produces the distance. The chasing, jealousy, and neediness are not responses to unavailability; they are the inputs that generate it.

This is [[trauma-mapping]] applied to attachment specifically. The person has wired love and chasing together. Their body produces relationships where the chasing form of love is available because it does not know any other form.

## The Pattern Is Chosen, Not Inflicted

The empowering and difficult-to-hear claim: the chaser is making the choice. They are pushing the partner away while experiencing themselves as wanting the partner closer.

"Every one of you -- like, all the ways in which you're wanting the attention are also ways in which you're pushing the person away. This is like -- jealousy is like that. Jealousy is like, I really want that person, but my jealousy in and of itself is pushing them away, or my neediness is pushing them away." -- Joe Hudson, Triggered

This is uncomfortable. It removes the victim frame. It also gives back the agency required to actually change the pattern.

"There's something in me that doesn't want that level of intimacy. There's something in me that equates love with chasing, not love with receiving." -- Joe Hudson, Triggered

This is the same mechanism as [[craving-is-the-pushing-away]] and [[engineering-heartbreak-to-avoid-heartbreak]]. The grasping is the rejecting. The wanting and the pushing away are the same move.

## What the Configuration Is Avoiding

The chasing is keeping the person in cycle around an emotional state they are not letting themselves fully feel. Joe names several common ones:

**The felt sense of receiving love.** A love where you are simply adored, where you do not have to earn it, where someone is across the room wanting you and you do not have to perform for it. For the chaser, this state feels foreign, even threatening.

"I'm scared of the other guy across the room who's like, wants to adore me and wants to be needy of me. I'm like, no, fuck that. I don't even find you attractive." -- Joe Hudson, Triggered

The available person registers as unattractive. The unavailable person registers as desire. This is the configuration's signature.

**The felt sense of being empowered.** "They can earn me. They don't -- I'm not going to chase them." This stance feels arrogant or unsafe to the chaser. It triggers fear of being alone, fear of overestimating one's worth, fear of seeming entitled.

**The abyss.** A felt fall into something open, undifferentiated, larger than the configuration. The receiving stance opens this. The chasing keeps the person above it.

"It's either scary because it feels like an abyss that I'm going to fall into, or it feels I'm going to be arrogant if I'm like, you know, you got to earn me." -- Joe Hudson, Triggered

The configuration is keeping the person away from these states by keeping them in the loop of chase-and-rejection. Until those states are felt and loved, the configuration will persist.

## The Childhood Template

The configuration was usually installed in childhood with a parent whose love had to be earned, fought for, performed for, or chased. The chaser learned that love is something you reach for and frequently do not get. Receiving love freely, being adored without performance, never showed up in the template. So as adults, they generate relationships that match the template they know. See [[childhood-imprinting]].

The partner choice is precise. The chaser does not just find people who happen to be distant. They find the person whose own configuration -- often "do not let love land" -- complements theirs exactly. See [[complementary-trauma-surfaces]]. The configuration is a two-person system. Both are performing their assigned half.

## The Exit

The path out is not "find an available person." That move would, if successful, surface exactly the avoided states (receiving, being adored, the abyss) without preparation. The person would either flee back to chasing or shut down with the available person.

The path out is to feel and fall in love with the avoided states while still in the loop. Two moves Joe names:

**1. Feel the emotional state of being the chaser and the rejected one fully.** Not as suffering, but as material to be loved.

"The way through is to fully fall in love with and accept and look forward to the emotional state that happens when you are rejected or when you are chasing." -- Joe Hudson, Triggered

This is the same mechanism as [[feeling-the-unfelt-emotion]]. The body keeps producing the configuration because the emotional content keeps not being fully felt. When the content is fully felt and welcomed, the body no longer needs to produce the configuration.

**2. When the relationship ends, grieve it fully and grieve the years of the pattern.** Not just the loss of this person, but the loss of "20 years of your life chasing pretty much your mom or dad's love through the face of a boyfriend or a girlfriend."

"You can fully grieve that experience, you know, that can also be a huge part of that healing journey." -- Joe Hudson, Triggered

See [[without-grief-we-recreate-the-pattern]]. The grief is what frees the body from regenerating the configuration in the next relationship.

## The Other Side of the Configuration

This article centers the chaser because that is who usually presents. The avoidant partner is performing the matching half. They have learned that closeness equals threat or absorption, that being chased is the form of love that does not engulf them. Their work has the same shape -- feel and love the avoided states (closeness, being needed, being wanted) -- but the felt content is different.

When both partners are doing this work, the dynamic can transform within the relationship. When only one is, the chasing partner sometimes finds that the work itself produces enough internal change that the configuration with this partner is no longer sustainable. See [[relationships-as-personal-growth-path]].

## Why Therapy Often Does Not Touch This

The configuration is so well-defended that it usually resists the surface frame ("you need to find an available person" / "you need to communicate better"). The actual work is at the level of the avoided emotional content. Without that, the person rotates through partners while telling themselves the next one will be different. The body keeps voting for the same configuration.

## When Someone Experiences This

- Multiple relationships in a row with the same dynamic
- The person across the room who is into you is unattractive; the distant one is magnetic
- The relationship feels alive only when you are uncertain whether they will return your calls
- Jealousy and neediness that the person knows are damaging but cannot stop
- The recognition, often through grief, that the configuration matches a parent
- The sense that "available" partners are boring even when you have wanted availability for years

## Tone

Joe is direct and a little unsparing on this. The empowering reframe ("you are choosing this") is delivered as a fact, not as a confrontation. He acknowledges that most people will reject it at first. The tone is steady, not soft. The pattern has been doing real harm and the person already knows.

## Position Within the Family of Push-Pull Forms

The chasing-of-the-unavailable is one of the most-developed forms of the broader [[push-pull-dynamics-in-dating]] pattern. The structural shape -- consciously reaching for love while subconsciously pushing it away -- is the same across the family. The forms differ in which specific behavior is doing the pushing:

- **Chasing the unavailable** (this article): the chase is the push. The available person is unattractive precisely because availability is the refused state.
- **Jealousy**: the abuse and possession is the push. "I want you, I want you / I'm going to drive you away."
- **Neediness**: the parent-child installation is the push. "Be here for me / I'm putting you in a position where you cannot be my peer."
- **Hyper-independence**: the wall is the push. "I'm yours / I don't need anything from you." See [[self-reliance-pushes-people-away]].
- **Transactionality**: the constant negotiation is the push. "Let's be together / let me contract our way out of intimacy." See [[transactional-relationships-as-defense]].
- **The criteria checklist**: the narrow criteria are the push. See [[the-partner-checklist-doesnt-survive-the-actual-relationship]].
- **The "not enough" frame**: the wait-to-qualify is the push. See [[whatever-you-think-you-need-to-be-lovable-is-self-rejection]].

Most people running this configuration run multiple forms at once. The chasing-of-the-unavailable rarely shows up alone. The diagnostic and the work are the same: see the structure, surface what is being avoided ([[fear-of-losing-yourself-in-love]]), feel the avoided emotional content, and the configuration dissolves.
