# Bad Habits Are the Avoidance of Essential Self

## Definition

The compulsive behaviors people most want to stop — porn, procrastination, scrolling, overeating, drinking — are not the problem they appear to be. They are the last stage of a cascade that runs in this order:

1. **The essential self is too big to stay with.** Whatever you actually are underneath all the stories — love, awareness, "a lot," the thing that has never changed and never been hurt — arrives in the body and registers as overwhelming.
2. **The mind takes over to manage the overwhelm.** Thinking is the first line of defense. The cognitive system grabs the felt sense and converts it into something to figure out.
3. **The thinking turns shame-filled.** Once the head is doing the work, the content of the thinking turns into "I'm bad / I'm not enough / I'm responsible for my own happiness / I don't act the way I want to act." The thinking is no longer trying to figure out what you are; it is now trying to figure out what is wrong with you.
4. **The shame-thinking is itself unbearable, so it gets avoided through behavior.** Porn, doom-scrolling, procrastination, eating, drinking, gaming. The behavior is downstream. The shame is downstream. The thinking is downstream. The thing being avoided at the top of the cascade is the felt presence of essential self.

The implication: trying to stop the behavior at the bottom of the cascade is the wrong move. The behavior is the symptom of the avoidance. As long as the avoidance is happening, the behavior or its equivalent will continue to be needed.

## In Practice

Joe states the cascade explicitly in a coaching session with a man asking how to stop watching porn and procrastinating:

"So that's the best part, is that's your — so your first line of defense against what you are essentially is to think, and then the thinking gets so fucking rattled and shame-filled that you need to avoid the shit thinking. And you do that with procrastination, doom scrolling, pornography, blah, blah, blah, blah. There's probably a dozen others. So what you are essentially feels good, but somehow overwhelming, moves to thinking. Moves to self-critical thinking, moves to avoidance of self-critical thinking." -- Joe Hudson, How Do I Stop Watching Porn And Procrastinating?

The four-stage architecture is the teaching:

- **Stage 1 — Essential self.** What you are. The thing that has never changed, never been hurt, never been touched by any of the stories. In the session, the person eventually names it as love. The naming is less important than the felt contact.
- **Stage 2 — Thinking.** The brain grabs the felt sense and starts working on it. The thinking is the first avoidance. It looks like analysis or processing. It is actually departure.
- **Stage 3 — Shame thinking.** The thinking turns on the self. "I'm bad. I'm not enough. I don't act the way I want to act. I'm so good at making myself unhappy."
- **Stage 4 — Behavior.** The compulsive behavior arrives to escape the shame-thinking. The behavior is the avoidance of the avoidance of the avoidance of what the person essentially is.

## When Someone Experiences This

The cascade reliably fires for people with long-running compulsive behaviors who have tried many times to stop. The signature is the combined presentation:

- A persistent behavior the person genuinely wants to stop ("I've been trying for my whole life")
- Heavy self-shame about the behavior ("I feel so much shame")
- A self-story that the problem is the person's character ("I'm so good at making myself unhappy")
- The pattern of attempting to control the behavior through willpower and failing
- An intellectualized inner life that feels active but does not produce change
- A vague sense that there is something underneath the behavior that the person cannot name

The teaching does not require the person to have the language of "essential self" or "what you are." Most people experience stage 1 as a fleeting moment of feeling whole, then immediately leave it. They notice the leaving but not the cause. The cascade is what is operating between the moment they touched something and the moment they reached for the phone.

## How the Cascade Was Installed

In the source session, the cascade was installed in childhood through the suppression of exuberant love. The boy was loud in a restaurant; the parents quieted him. He got angry at his parents; he was yelled at and put in the car. The exuberant love that was the essential self had nowhere to go. The pattern that got installed: when love arrives in the body, suppress it.

The adult version of that suppression is the four-stage cascade. The parents are no longer the ones doing the shushing. The person does it to themselves. The trying-to-figure-out is the contemporary form of being-put-in-the-car. The shame-thinking is the contemporary form of being-yelled-at. The compulsive behavior is what the system reaches for when the self-administered suppression becomes too painful to bear.

"And now you're shutting it down every time. I ask you the question, what are you essentially? The same way your parents did. They shush you with your voice, try to figure it out, try to control you, try to manage you, put you in the car." -- Joe Hudson, How Do I Stop Watching Porn And Procrastinating?

This means the work on the addiction is not separate from the work on the childhood. The addiction is the present-tense form of a suppression that was installed early. Loving the part that learned to suppress is the work that lets the suppression relax. When the suppression relaxes, the cascade has nowhere to begin, and the bottom of the cascade — the compulsive behavior — loses its function.

## Why Stopping at Stage 4 Doesn't Work

The standard approach to compulsive behaviors works at stage 4: install a habit, restructure the environment, find an accountability partner, white-knuckle through the urges. These approaches sometimes produce short-term abstinence. They almost always fail in the longer term, because the upstream cascade is still firing. As long as stages 1–3 are operating, the system will produce a stage 4 behavior. If porn becomes inaccessible, the system finds scrolling. If scrolling becomes inaccessible, the system finds food. The specific behavior changes; the function does not.

Joe's compression of this:

"You've been focused on how do I stop procrastinating? How do I stop using porn? It — that never works, or very seldom works. If you want it to go away, stay focused on being with what you are, essentially all of it." -- Joe Hudson, How Do I Stop Watching Porn And Procrastinating?

The phrase "all of it" matters. The person has been able to touch the essential self in fragments. The work is to be with all of it, not the fragments the person can manage to keep partial. The fragmentary contact is itself another version of the suppression.

## The Resistance Is Harder Than the Not-Resisting

A specific corollary of the cascade: the cost of running stages 2–4 (thinking, shame, addiction) is much higher than the cost of staying with stage 1 (essential self). The person's intuition is the opposite — they assume that staying with what they are is the hard thing, and that the cascade is the easier path of least resistance. The cascade is the harder path.

Joe surfaces this directly:

- **Joe:** "How is this difficult?"
- **Person:** "Uh, I think, yeah, usually—"
- **Joe:** "What's more difficult? What's more difficult, to be with what you are essentially or to not be?"
- **Person:** "Well, I can't not be. I can only, um, escape from it or distract myself from it."
- **Joe:** "Yeah, yeah, yeah. Which is more difficult? To resist it or not resist it."
- **Person:** "Oh yeah, resistance is, is hard. Yeah. Oh, it hurts. I don't want to do it anymore. Yeah, yeah, I know. Doesn't make sense. Yeah, it doesn't make sense. Oh yeah."
- **Joe:** "So the story is that it's hard, but in reality it's far easier to not resist it."

*(Source: How Do I Stop Watching Porn And Procrastinating?)*

The recognition lands as a felt collapse of the previous framing. The person had been organizing his entire life around the assumption that the resistance was necessary. The recognition that the resistance was the harder path is what makes the next move available — letting the resistance fall away because it was never actually serving him.

## Tone

When Joe names the cascade, the tone is matter-of-fact and slightly playful. He is giving the brain "a story so it can shut the fuck up for a minute and we can get back to the real work." The cascade-naming is not the work; it is a way of giving the head something to chew on so the body can come back to the front.

The naming is also delivered without pathologizing. The cascade is not described as broken or disordered. It is described as what the brain does. "So your first line of defense against what you are essentially is to think." The matter-of-factness keeps the person from collapsing into shame about the cascade itself.

## Anti-Patterns

- Coaching the behavior directly. The behavior is downstream. Working on it produces white-knuckle abstinence and recurrence. See [[i-cant-be-disciplined]].
- Coaching the shame as the problem. The shame is also downstream. Working on the shame without addressing what is being avoided produces shame-about-the-shame and prolongs the cycle. See [[the-shame-avoidance-loop]].
- Letting the person stay in the thinking layer in the coaching room. The thinking is the first stage of the cascade firing in real time. If the coaching becomes another think-through, the cascade has consumed the session.
- Treating "what you essentially are" as a metaphysical question. It is being deployed here as a coaching instrument for an addiction concern. The instrument's job is to surface the contact, not to produce a philosophical answer.

## Distinctions

- [[improvement-vs-authenticity]] — the broader frame. The cascade is the specific addiction-shaped form of the improvement project. The person is trying to fix themselves to escape themselves. The medicine is the same: stop trying to improve, stay with what is actually there.
- [[the-shame-avoidance-loop]] — the closely related but distinct loop. The shame-avoidance loop describes the recursion between shame and the strategy to avoid it. The cascade described here is upstream: the shame itself is the second-to-last stage of an even longer avoidance, and the actual upstream is the avoidance of essential self.
- [[addiction-to-shame]] — the related pattern where the shame becomes part of the appetite for the addictive behavior. The cascade describes the structural origin; the addiction-to-shame describes how the cycle locks once it is established.

## Boundary Conditions

This is not a general theory of all compulsive behaviors. Substance-use disorders with strong physiological components (alcohol dependence, opioid addiction) have neurochemical layers the cascade does not address. The cascade is most clearly operating for behavioral compulsions in people with otherwise functional lives — porn, scrolling, gaming, procrastination, mild over-eating. The principle still applies to substance use (see [[heart-in-a-box-want-to-drink]] for the related teaching) but does not replace the work that physiological dependence requires.

This is also not a teaching that bypasses harm reduction or external support. A person actively destroying their life through a behavior may need environmental redesign, support groups, or professional treatment as part of stabilizing the situation enough for the deeper work to become possible. The cascade tells you what the deeper work is. It does not tell you that the immediate work is unnecessary.
