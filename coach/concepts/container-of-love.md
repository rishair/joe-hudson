# Container of Love

## Definition

The environment in which emotional healing happens. Not a technique but a quality of space, either one-on-one or in a group, where a person can feel the emotions they have been avoiding. The container does not fix the person. It allows the person to do what they could not do alone: feel what was too overwhelming to feel without support.

## In Practice

"If they get to a point where they can look forward to that emotion, that underlying emotion, and actually invite it and feel it and process it in a container, particularly in a container of love, then that's the moment they stop attracting or going towards the same trauma in their life." -- Joe Hudson, A Discussion with Dr. Bessel van der Kolk & Joe Hudson

In group settings, the container functions differently than one-on-one. Joe describes his week-long courses where participants list their inner critic's voice on the wall, making it visible to the group. The healing happens not from the exercise alone but from the experience of being seen and loved despite what is revealed.

"When people excavate all this stuff and then they look around the room and they see 11 other people loving them despite of this, that that's no excuse not to love yourself. It does something for the human." -- Joe Hudson, A Discussion with Dr. Bessel van der Kolk & Joe Hudson
