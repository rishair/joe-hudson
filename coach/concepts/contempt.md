# Contempt

## Definition

Contempt is the felt sense of being better than another person — paired, on the inside, with the fact that at your essence you are not. The combination makes contempt a shadow signal: it shows you a piece of yourself you have not been willing to look at.

"Contempt is when you think you're better than somebody else, and hint, at your essence, you're not. And so what it's doing is it's showing you a blind spot." -- Joe Hudson, 11 Human Emotions Explained

The structural claim has two parts. First, contempt is felt-superiority. The person feels themselves as higher than, more evolved than, more virtuous than the contemned person. Second, the felt-superiority is false — at the level of essence, no one is above anyone, and the contempt is therefore the body's report of a story being held that is not true. The signal that the story is false is precisely the trait you find contemptible: it is in you, and the contempt is the alarm bell that you have not owned it.

## What This Reframes

The default cultural picture of contempt: a feeling that confirms you are right and the other person is wrong. The signal-of-disowned-trait picture: contempt is the body reporting that you have not owned the trait you are condemning, and the work is to look at yourself rather than at the other person.

The reframe takes contempt out of the moral register ("they are bad and I am justified in feeling this way") and puts it into the diagnostic register ("there is something in me I have not been willing to see, and the contempt is pointing at it"). The exit from contempt is not to find more evidence that the other person deserves it. The exit is to find the trait in the self and own it.

## The Shadow Mechanism

Contempt operates through projection. The trait the person cannot own in themselves gets projected outward; the outward person becomes the carrier of the disowned trait; the original person then feels contempt for the carrier. The contempt is the relational pressure that keeps the disowning intact.

"When contempt comes up, your job, which is not gonna be comfortable, is to see how you have those exact traits of the person you have contempt for. They're usually in your shadow, and we usually don't wanna look at it or own it." -- Joe Hudson, 11 Human Emotions Explained

The "usually in your shadow" is the operational specification. The shadow is the set of traits the person has decided not to be — not because the traits are absent, but because owning them would conflict with the identity the person has built. Contempt is one of the most reliable diagnostics of what is in the shadow.

The "we usually don't wanna look at it" is the predicted resistance. The work is uncomfortable precisely because the contempt has been doing the work of keeping the trait disowned. Doing the shadow work undoes the protection that the contempt provided.

## The Action Step

The action step is direct and brief:

See how you have those exact traits of the person you have contempt for.

The instruction is to look, not to fix. The work is to find the same trait — the laziness, the cruelty, the self-absorption, the dishonesty, whatever the contempted trait is — operating in your own life, often in subtler or more socially acceptable forms. The finding is the work. The trait becomes legible; the contempt dissolves; the shadow gets one piece less heavy.

The trait often shows up in a different form in the self. A person who feels contempt for someone's loud arrogance may have a quiet but equally invested arrogance about something else. A person who feels contempt for someone's lateness may be chronically late in a domain they have not connected to the contempt. The pattern is the same; the surface is different.

## Connection to the Essence Claim

The "at your essence you're not" piece carries the metaphysical weight of the teaching. The reason contempt is reliably wrong is that at the level of essence — the level of being itself, prior to personality and behavior — no one is above anyone. The hierarchies the personality builds are not present at that level.

This is why contempt can be read as a signal rather than a verdict. The signal works precisely because the verdict is wrong. If superiority were real at the level of essence, contempt would be an accurate report and there would be nothing to look at. Because the verdict is wrong, the contempt has to be doing something else — and what it is doing is keeping a disowned trait disowned.

See [[essence]] for the broader treatment of the level at which the claim operates.

## The Relational Cost

Contempt is also one of the most reliably destructive emotions in a relationship. Once contempt has set into a marriage, a friendship, a team, the relationship is in serious trouble. Other difficult emotions — anger, fear, sadness — can move and resolve. Contempt does not move on its own because its job is to maintain the disowning, and the disowning is in the contemnor, not the contemned.

## What Contempt Is Not

Contempt is not the same as anger. Anger is energy directed at a boundary or a violation; contempt is felt-superiority. Anger can be clean; contempt is structurally compromised because the superiority itself is the disowning move.

Contempt is not the same as disgust. Disgust is the body's report of forced-toxic-intake; contempt is felt-superiority. They can co-occur (a person can feel both disgusted by and contemptuous of a behavior), but they point at different material. The disgust points outward at what was put inside; the contempt points inward at what was disowned. See [[disgust]].

Contempt is not the same as discernment. Discernment is the clean recognition that a behavior, a path, or a person is not aligned with you. It does not require felt-superiority. A person can discern clearly that a relationship is over without contempt. Contempt is the addition of "I am above this person" to the discernment, and the addition is the part that signals the shadow material.

Contempt is not the same as judgment. Judgment is a cognitive position; contempt is a felt sense of being above. They often overlap, but a person can hold a judgment without contempt (the cognitive position is held without the felt superiority) and a person can feel contempt without articulating the judgment (the felt sense is present even without a clean thought to attach to it).

## The Kinked Forms

Contempt that is not allowed to be read as a shadow signal takes destructive forms:

- **Compounding contempt:** the contempt produces behavior toward the contemned person that produces more reasons to feel contemptuous, and the loop tightens. This is the dynamic in dying marriages and on dying teams.
- **Self-righteousness:** the contempt gets converted into a moral position about the contemnor's superiority, which makes the shadow work even less accessible.
- **Group contempt:** the contempt gets extended to a category of people (a political group, a class, a race, a profession), which both extends the projection and gives it social reinforcement.
- **Inverted contempt:** the contempt gets turned at the self ("I am contemptible"), which combines the felt-superiority with the disowning in a particularly stuck configuration. The person above themselves and the person below themselves are the same person.

All four are the kinked forms. None of them produce the shadow work that resolves the contempt. All four extend the disowning into more entrenched configurations.

See [[kinked-emotions]] for the broader treatment.

## In Coaching

When a person presents contempt for someone, the first move is the diagnostic question: where in you is this same trait? The question is uncomfortable and is often resisted. The resistance itself is the signal that the shadow material is being touched.

The path is the shadow work: finding the trait in the self, often in a different form than it appears in the contemned person. The work is not to like the trait or to defend it. The work is just to see it, to acknowledge it operates in you too. The acknowledgment is what dissolves the contempt.

When contempt has set into a partnership, the work is significantly harder. The contemnor often does not want to do the shadow work because the contempt has been doing protective relational work (keeping the contemnor's identity intact while the relationship dies). The path begins with the contemnor's recognition that the contempt is theirs to address, not the partner's to deserve.

When contempt is paired with disgust (the contempt of an ideology, a behavior, a community), both signals need to be honored separately. The disgust may be accurate (something toxic was taken in). The contempt is still a shadow signal even when the disgust is appropriate. The person can leave the situation cleanly (acting on the disgust) without leaving with contempt (which would carry the disowning forward into the next configuration).

See [[love-the-aspect-through-someone-else]] for the inverse practice: when a person cannot find love for a trait in themselves, they can often find it in someone else first and use the other person as a bridge back to self-love. The same bridge structure operates in reverse for contempt: the contempted person carries the trait the contemnor cannot love in themselves.

## Tone

Joe delivers the contempt definition with a slight cheekiness. The "hint" — "and hint, at your essence, you're not" — is doing comedic work, but also doing real work: it lowers the gravity of the teaching enough for the listener to receive the hard message that the contempt is a signal about themselves rather than a verdict about the other person.

The "not gonna be comfortable" in the action step acknowledges the predicted resistance. The work is named as uncomfortable up front so the discomfort does not become evidence that the work is wrong.
