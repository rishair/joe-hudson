# Reclaiming Abandonment

## Definition

Reclaiming abandonment is the work of going back to the original source of an abandonment wound and addressing it directly, rather than only working with its surrogates and downstream effects. The abandonment wound usually installed in early relationship with a parent or caregiver. Adult life is then full of restagings of that abandonment: the manager who did not promote you, the partner who left, the friend who pulled away. Each can be worked with. But reclamation requires returning to the source.

The work has two components: feeling the original abandonment as it was felt at the time, and (where the source person is alive and accessible) speaking the unspoken truth to them.

## Why the Source Matters

Working only with the downstream restagings can produce real progress. The person can learn to identify the pattern, calm the reactivity, and live with more ease. The pattern continues to recur, however, because the original loop has not closed. The body is still organizing experience around the original wound.

Returning to the source closes the loop. When the unspoken truth has been spoken to the original person, or the original feeling has been fully felt with the original target in mind, the body no longer needs to keep generating restagings. See [[trauma-mapping]] for the mechanism.

"All these things go back to abandonment. At one point, I had to go back to the original source, which was the relationship with my father." -- Stacy Brown-Philpot, Reclaiming Abandonment

The shift is not gradual after the source work. It is often watershed: the body recognizes that the loop is complete and the entire organizational pattern reorganizes.

## How the Surrogates Look

Before the source work, the abandonment wound shows up in patterns like:

- Always trying to prove something to someone. The proving is a defense against being abandoned again.
- Self-reliance to a fault. The inability to depend on others is the strategy installed by the original abandonment.
- Reading abandonment into ambiguous events. The boss who did not respond to an email, the friend who took two days to reply.
- Compartmentalization of emotional life. The parts that were unsafe in the original relationship are still kept out of current relationships.
- A felt sense of being alone, even surrounded by people who love them.

See [[i-have-to-do-it-all-myself]] for the concern this most often presents as.

Stacy's surrogates were the proving and the self-reliance:

"I was always trying to prove something. And that could translate into ambition. And so people saw that ambition and said, wow, she's really ambitious. Like, we're going to work with her because we're going to get there because she wants to get there. But underneath it was this desire to prove and this desire to fight. And what I realized was that the proof and the fighting was -- something was underneath that. Underneath that was this feeling that I needed to prove something because if I didn't, I would be abandoned again and again and again." -- Stacy Brown-Philpot, Reclaiming Abandonment

## What "Going Back to the Source" Means in Practice

It can mean any of:

1. **Tracing the feeling to its first occurrence.** See [[trace-emotion-to-its-first-felt]]. Following the body sensation back to the original event makes the source visible.

2. **Feeling the original abandonment as it was felt at the time.** Inside a [[container-of-love]], with the original event in mind, letting the original grief move. The grief that was too big for the child's body can now be felt and completed in the adult body.

3. **Speaking the unspoken truth to the original person.** When the person is alive and accessible. The conversation that has been waiting for decades. See [[stacy-father-conversation]] for the canonical example and [[prepare-and-release-the-difficult-conversation]] for the structure.

4. **Writing to the original person when not accessible.** When the parent has died, when contact is unsafe, when the relationship cannot bear the conversation. The writing carries much of the same release.

## The Layered Nature of the Work

The source work is not always one conversation or one sit-down. Abandonment often installed in many small moments rather than one big event. Multiple traces, multiple conversations, multiple felt completions may be needed.

"I give him a lot of credit for pushing me to go deeper into understanding myself. And I've learned that there are layers of trauma that I've dealt with and layers of abandonment that I've dealt with. But at its core, that was really it." -- Stacy Brown-Philpot, Reclaiming Abandonment

The layered nature means the work happens over time. The core can be addressed in a single conversation when the conditions are right. The integration after the core conversation continues for months and years.

## What Reclaiming Requires

- A clear identification of what was abandoned. Stacy could name it: the felt sense that she did not matter to her father because he was not around.
- A capacity to feel what was unfelt at the time. This is usually the slow work of months or years before the source conversation becomes possible.
- A willingness to risk the original outcome happening again. The conversation may be met with denial, rejection, or indifference. The reclamation has to be able to land regardless. See [[prepare-and-release-the-difficult-conversation]].
- Usually, a long stretch of work on the surrogates first. The source work is not the first move. It is often the move that becomes possible after substantial work elsewhere has prepared the ground.

Stacy describes the timing:

"My first conversation with Joe was like, I think I have some daddy issues that I need to work on. That was like 2 and a half years ago. It was really like more facetious and true at the same time. But it was just a recognition that I knew I could be a better person." -- Stacy Brown-Philpot, Reclaiming Abandonment

She named the source in the first conversation. The source work itself happened over two years and culminated in the conversation with her father in July 2021. The naming was easy. The reclamation took time.

## What Reclamation Produces

- Release of the proving pattern. The drive to prove that built the career can soften without the career falling apart. What remains is the work without the abandonment-driven engine.
- Capacity to receive. The hyper-independent self-reliance softens. The person can let others be there for them.
- Different leadership. The CEO who has done this work can be vulnerable with their team without losing authority. The team can show up more fully because the leader is no longer compartmentalized.
- A different relationship with the original person. The relationship that was guarded becomes available for actual closeness.

"I want all of them to be successful, but we're not going to be the person who -- the company that provides the capital. And so when we pass on a deal and it's someone that I know, I talk about, I want to stay connected with you. I want you to be successful. And here's why we're not going to invest right now. It's a very different conversation. And almost always, the response that I get is a thank you." -- Stacy Brown-Philpot, Reclaiming Abandonment

The capacity to deliver no with love comes from having reclaimed the no said to her at the source.

## The Parallel With Joe's Own Abandonment Work

Stacy's reclamation echoes Joe's own work with abandonment. Joe describes recreating emotional abandonment until he fell in love with it. The mechanism is similar but the route differs. Joe's work was largely with the feeling itself. Stacy's work culminated in a conversation with the source person. Both routes complete the same loop. See [[joes-abandonment-mapping]].

## When Reclamation Is Premature

- When the client has not yet identified the source. Naming it speculatively does not do the work.
- When the client is in fight mode toward the source person. The work needs to come from a settled place, not from rage.
- When the conditions for the conversation are not safe. Some original sources are not available for the conversation. The writing or the felt completion in a container is the path then.
- When the client is treating the source conversation as a magic bullet. The work is what makes the conversation possible, not the other way around.

## Upstream (What Leads Here)
