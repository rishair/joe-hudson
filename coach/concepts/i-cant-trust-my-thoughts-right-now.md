# I Can't Trust My Thoughts Right Now

## The Recognition

When a pattern fires, the nervous system enters a recognizable state. The state has a distinctive body signature, separate from the content of the upset. Learning to recognize this signature is one of the most useful tools for not acting from inside the pattern.

The recognition is not "I am upset." It is more specific: "the sensation that's carrying this upset is the sensation of being in my pattern." Once recognized, the next move is not to argue with the thoughts. The next move is to refrain from trusting them until the state has passed.

"There was this really wonderful moment where somebody came to me, it was in a business thing, and they were like, hey, what do you, what should we do? And I said, I can't trust anything I think right now. My brain isn't in a place where I can trust my thoughts. So I'm gonna go get my brain in a place and my body in a place where I can trust my thoughts, where I'm out of my trauma so that I can think clearly. Because if I'm acting out of the trauma, I will recreate it over and over and over and over again." -- Joe Hudson, Seeing Through Family Dynamics

## The Body Signature Is the Tell

The diagnostic is not the size of the upset. It is the type of sensation underneath the upset.

"If you think about like the time that you got most angry for no good reason, or most felt most out of control for no good reason, even though you might identify the reason, you realize, 'Oh, this doesn't make logical sense that I'd be this upset.' That's the sensation that you have, not the upsetness, but the sensation that's carrying that upsetness, that's how you know you're in your trauma." -- Joe Hudson, Seeing Through Family Dynamics

The clue is the disproportion. The mind manufactures a reason to justify the size of the response, and the reason sounds plausible. But the body knows the response is bigger than the trigger. That gap — between the size of the felt experience and the size of the actual situation — is the marker.

Each person's signature is their own. For some it is a tightness in the chest. For some a coldness, a buzz, a sense of being slightly removed. The work is to learn your own signature so you can name it the next time it appears.

## What to Do With the Recognition

Two simultaneous things:

**1. Do not act on the thinking.** The thinking inside the pattern is the pattern. Acting on it recreates the situation the pattern is built to create. This is the core insight: thoughts produced while in the pattern serve the pattern, not the present moment.

"If I'm acting out of the trauma, I will recreate it over and over and over and over again." -- Joe Hudson, Seeing Through Family Dynamics

**2. Restore the body.** Get out of the pattern's nervous-system state before deciding anything. This may be a walk, a feeling practice, sleep, water, time. It may take minutes; it may take a day. The duration depends on the depth of the pattern fire.

The implication: postponing the decision is not avoidance. Postponing the decision while in the pattern is the difference between a clear decision and a decision built to recreate the wound.

## The Rational Brain's Right Use

This concept clarifies the relationship between the intellect and the emotional system. The intellect cannot heal the pattern. But the intellect can recognize the state and refuse to weaponize itself on the pattern's behalf.

"That's where the rational brain can be really helpful and say, oh, when I'm in my pattern, I just can't believe my thoughts right now, I can't believe this." -- Joe Hudson, Seeing Through Family Dynamics

This is the intellect doing its real job: knowing the limits of its own current reliability. The rational brain that knows when not to trust itself is the rational brain at its most useful.

## The Trap of Justification

The pattern is sophisticated. It does not present itself as "irrational upset." It presents itself with a fully-formed reason. The reason sounds correct. The reason often is partially correct. But the size, the heat, the urgency of the response is the tell that the reason is being recruited to justify a much older response.

A person who skips the body signature and engages with the reason will lose the diagnostic. The body signature is the only reliable marker, because the content of the trauma will always present itself as a current legitimate grievance.

## The Couple Form: Naming It Out Loud to the Partner

When the trigger is firing inside a relationship, the recognition becomes a piece of relational communication rather than just an internal self-talk. The couple-version of "I can't trust my thoughts right now" is naming it out loud to the partner.

"I've said that dozens of times to Tara. She said it to me, 'Oh, I'm in my trauma right now.' And then the other one can say, 'Oh, right. This isn't you. This isn't actually...' How do I hold you? How do I love you? How do I be with you and your trauma so it doesn't retraumatize and you can actually see what's really going on?" -- Joe Hudson, How To Fight And Win

The naming-to-the-partner version requires the same body-level recognition as the internal version. The difference is the use of the recognition. Internally, the recognition produces "postpone the decision." Relationally, the recognition produces "tell my partner this is trauma firing and ask them to receive me from that frame." Both are the same underlying move applied to different contexts. See [[name-im-in-my-trauma-as-couple-language]] for the full operational form of the relational version.

## When Someone Experiences This

- An upset that is larger than the trigger seems to warrant
- Knowing intellectually that the reaction is disproportionate and still feeling fully justified
- A familiar body sensation accompanying the upset, recognizable from other firings
- A pull to make a decision or send a message now, immediately
- A sense that everyone else is missing something obvious that you can see

## In Coaching

When someone presents inside a trauma fire, the coaching move is rarely to engage with the content. The coaching move is to name what is happening at the body level. "What's the sensation underneath the upset? Have you felt this before?" Locating the signature does two things: it makes the pattern observable, and it gives the person a recognition tool they can carry into the next firing.

This sets up a practice: the person learns to wait when they recognize the signature. The waiting is not suppression. The waiting is the refusal to act from inside the pattern.

## Tone

Plain, almost clinical. Joe describes this without drama. The matter-of-fact tone is part of the medicine: treating "I'm in my trauma right now" as a piece of useful information, not a confession of weakness, models the stance that allows the recognition to be useful.
