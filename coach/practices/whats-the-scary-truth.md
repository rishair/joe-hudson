# What's the Scary Thing That's True?

## The Practice

At any moment, ask: "What's the scary thing that's true for me right now?" This is the operational compass for [[vulnerability]]. It locates the intersection of truth and fear, which is where vulnerability lives.

"The easiest is just to say, oh, what's the scary thing that's true for me right now?" -- Joe Hudson, Vulnerability -- Connection Course Series #5

## How to Do It

This practice works both in conversation and alone.

**In conversation:** Before speaking, notice what you are holding back. What is true but feels risky to say? That is the vulnerable thing.

**Alone:** Close your eyes. Notice what is emotionally uncomfortable in your system right now. Lean in, open to it, embrace it. This is the internal equivalent of speaking your truth in a conversation. The same cliff-jumping feeling applies.

"Right now, in this moment, you can close your eyes and there's something in your system emotionally uncomfortable, and you can lean in and open to it, embrace it, and welcome it. And that's the same feeling of vulnerability." -- Joe Hudson, Vulnerability -- Connection Course Series #5

## What It Produces

The practice has three layers of benefit:

1. **Fear shifts to excitement.** When fear is embraced rather than avoided, it often transmutes. The cliff becomes a thrill rather than a threat.

2. **Self-knowledge deepens.** Speaking truth aligns the world around your actual self rather than your presentation. Over time, this creates clearer self-understanding because you are getting feedback on who you really are, not who you pretend to be.

3. **Judgment loses its power.** Most self-judgment is louder than the world's judgment. Being vulnerable reveals that other people judge you far less than you judge yourself. See [[letting-judgment-in]].

## Three Signals of the Vulnerable State

- **Heart:** An opening, sometimes fear shifting to excitement.
- **Gut:** A "yes" sensation, a sense of alignment and truth, even if the nervous system says no.
- **Body:** The willingness to feel whatever comes, rather than bracing against it.
