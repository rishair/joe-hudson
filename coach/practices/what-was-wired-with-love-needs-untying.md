# Untie What Was Wired With Love

## The Practice

The most important sub-move inside [[from-looking-for-love-to-being-love]] is the untying of what was wired with love in childhood. Whatever the early environment paired with love -- guilt, criticism, withdrawal, abandonment, jealousy, control, fear, shame -- got wired with love. The body now recognizes those things as love. The body now generates relationships in which those things appear, because the body believes those things are how love shows up. The work is to separate love from those wired-in companions, so that the body can recognize love when it arrives without the wired-in companion attached.

"Most importantly, it is untying what was wired with love." -- Joe Hudson, Finding a Romantic Partner

The practice is not cognitive. The wired-in pairings live somatically. They have to be undone at the level they were installed. The work is felt, repeated, slow.

## What Got Wired

Joe gives one specific example in the transcript:

"Having somebody who guilt trips you as your imprint of love. And then you constantly did stuff that you didn't want to do because you felt guilty. As that example means that you're going to be scared of being in a relationship where you're going to be guilted and lose yourself trying to please somebody who's not pleasable, and they constantly guilt you." -- Joe Hudson, Finding a Romantic Partner

The mechanism: a child whose parent loved them and guilted them now has love and guilt wired together. As an adult, when love shows up, guilt is what the body expects to come with it. The adult will either:
- Be drawn to partners who guilt them (the wired love-shape is recognized)
- Treat partners who do not guilt them as not-really-loving (the wired love-shape is absent so love is not registered)
- Guilt themselves on behalf of the absent guilting partner (the body produces the wired pairing internally when it does not arrive externally)
- Avoid intimate relationships altogether to avoid the inevitable guilting (the body anticipates the wired pairing)

The same shape applies to whatever else got wired:

- Love wired with **criticism**: drawn to critical partners, dismisses kind partners as boring, criticizes self
- Love wired with **withdrawal**: drawn to distant partners, panics when partners stay close, withdraws when given consistent presence
- Love wired with **shame**: drawn to partners who produce shame, experiences shame as part of intimacy, shames self
- Love wired with **fear**: drawn to scary partners, only feels love through anxiety, generates fear in calm relationships
- Love wired with **abandonment**: drawn to partners who leave, treats stable partners as suffocating, abandons before being abandoned
- Love wired with **control**: drawn to controlling partners, experiences love through being controlled or controlling, struggles with autonomy in love

The list extends to whatever specific pairing the early environment produced. The pattern is consistent: whatever appeared alongside love in the early environment is now part of what love means to the body.

## Why This Has to Be Done Before Love Can Be Recognized

Until the wiring is partially undone, the body cannot recognize clean love when it shows up. Clean love comes in (a partner who is consistently kind, present, not guilting, not critical) and the body registers it as not-love because it lacks the wired-in companion. The conscious mind says "this seems good." The body says "this isn't it."

The result is the all-too-common report: "I keep being attracted to people who treat me badly and bored by people who treat me well." This is not a moral failure or bad luck. The body is doing its job correctly. The job description was set in childhood, and the job description requires the wired companion for love to be recognized.

The untying work is what changes the job description. As the wired pairing dissolves, the body becomes able to recognize love without the companion. The partners who were previously boring become interesting. The partners who were previously magnetic become recognizably toxic. The selection mechanism updates.

## The Stages of the Untying

The untying is not a single move. It has stages:

**1. Naming the pairing.** What specifically got wired with love in your early environment? The naming is precise: not "I had a hard childhood" but "love in my house came with guilt" or "love in my house came with constant criticism" or "love in my house came with my father's withdrawals." The specificity is part of what makes the work workable.

**2. Recognizing the pairing operating in the present.** Once named, the pairing can be tracked in current life. Where am I doing the guilting to myself? Where am I generating the criticism? Where am I producing the withdrawal? The pairing is usually not just outside; it is also being run from inside. The internalized version is often what keeps the externalized version available.

**3. Stopping the self-version.** This is the immediate work that is in the person's hands. If the wiring is guilt, the move is to stop guilting yourself. If the wiring is criticism, the move is to stop the self-criticism. The body experiences this as the removal of something that felt like love. There is grief in this step. See [[love-misconceptions-inherited-from-childhood]] for the somatic fear that arises -- the body interprets the removal of the wired companion as the removal of love.

**4. Feeling the original material.** The wiring was installed through specific moments in childhood. Underneath the wiring is grief for what was missed -- the love that did not come without the companion, the parent who could not love without the guilt or the criticism or the withdrawal. The grief has to be felt. See [[feeling-the-unfelt-emotion]] and [[without-grief-we-recreate-the-pattern]].

**5. Practicing recognition of clean love.** As the wiring loosens, opportunities to recognize love without the companion appear. The work is to practice noticing them. The kind friend whose kindness used to register as nothing now lands. The partner whose presence used to feel suffocating now feels like presence. The body slowly trains a new recognition.

**6. Taking contrary action.** When the wired-in version reasserts itself, the move is to act against it. See [[take-contrary-action]]. The body needs repeated demonstrations that love does not require the companion to be present.

## The Permission It Requires

The work requires a specific permission the person often has not given themselves. Joe states it:

"Nobody has the right to guilt you. Nobody has the right to shame you. Nobody has -- you have every right to draw your boundary." -- Joe Hudson, Finding a Romantic Partner

The permission is the assertion that the wired-in companion is not a legitimate part of love. The right to be loved without the guilt. The right to be loved without the criticism. The right to be loved without the withdrawal. The wiring has often included an implicit agreement that the companion is the price of being loved. The permission revokes that agreement.

This permission is also the foundation of the boundary work that makes love available without the companion. The boundary against the guilt is what makes clean love possible. The boundary against the criticism is what makes clean love possible. See [[boundaries]] and [[walled-no-vs-open-hearted-limit]].

## The Connection to Partner Selection

As the untying progresses, the partners the body selects for change. The body that no longer needs guilt as part of love stops being attracted to guilters. The body that no longer needs withdrawal as part of love stops being attracted to withdrawn partners. The selection mechanism, which had been organized around the wired pairing, reorganizes around the unwiring.

This is the structural mechanism behind the three-to-four-month observation in [[i-cant-find-love]]. The untying work changes who the body is available to recognize as a partner. The partner who shows up after this work is usually someone the previous body would not have noticed. See [[complementary-trauma-surfaces]] for the developed treatment of how the body's selection works.

## What This Practice Is Not

It is not a one-time event. The wiring is deep and long. The untying is repeated, layered, slow. Each pass undoes some of it. The work continues for years and produces continuous expansion in the capacity to recognize love.

It is not the elimination of the imprint. The imprint will continue to fire in moments. The work is not to make it never fire. The work is to recognize the firing for what it is and to act from a fuller relationship to love rather than from the firing.

It is not done alone. The work is felt and relational. Some of it can happen in solitude, but much of it requires actual encounter with love that does not match the imprint -- in friendships, in coaching relationships, in eventual partnership, in the felt sense of being received without the companion. The relational practice is part of the work.

It is not cognitive correction. The intellectual recognition that "love does not require guilt" does not displace the somatic wiring. The somatic wiring requires somatic work. See [[love-misconceptions-inherited-from-childhood]] for the intellectual-vs-somatic-belief layer.

## When This Practice Applies

- When a person has named the imprint of love from their childhood and is asking what to do about it
- When [[from-looking-for-love-to-being-love]] has been delivered and the fourth sub-move is needed
- When a person reports being drawn to partners who treat them badly and bored by partners who treat them well
- When a person notices they are doing to themselves what was done to them in the name of love
- When a person is in a relationship that has the wired pairing operating and the work is to surface it
