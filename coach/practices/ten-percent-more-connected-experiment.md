# The 10% More Connected Experiment

## The Practice

Set a single intention for the day, the week, or the period: be 10% more connected in everything you do. Nothing in the calendar changes. Nothing about the practical content of the day changes. The only change is the small modulation toward more connection in each interaction.

The 10% is deliberate. Not 100%. Not "be fully present at all times." The small increment is the part that makes the practice doable. A small increase, applied consistently across the texture of an ordinary day, produces effects out of proportion to the effort.

The practice has two operating instructions:

1. In any activity, ask: what would this look like 10% more connected? Then do that version.
2. In any moment, notice where the attention is. If it is not in the body, drop 10% of it into the body. Leave the other 90% where it was.

Both instructions can run together. The first applies the filter to the structure of what is being done. The second applies it to the felt experience of doing it.

## The Reminder Mechanism

The practice requires a reminder system. Most people cannot hold the intention through the day without one. The intention drifts within minutes of being set.

The minimum-viable reminder: an app that produces an auditory or vibratory prompt at random or scheduled intervals throughout the day. Joe specifically names Yap as one such tool -- an app whose entire function is to make a small sound that interrupts whatever was happening and returns attention to the present.

The instruction attached to the reminder is short: "How do I be 10% more connected right now?" The question is asked. The attention adjusts. The activity continues.

The reminder is not the practice. The practice is the asking and the adjusting. The reminder is the scaffolding that makes the practice possible until the asking becomes automatic.

Other reminder mechanisms that work: a sticker on the laptop, a phone wallpaper change, a bracelet that catches the eye, a transition between rooms used as a cue. The mechanism does not matter. The function matters: produce regular interruptions that route attention back to the question.

## What It Produces

Almost immediately the person notices that small adjustments in connection produce disproportionate changes in the quality of an interaction. Ten percent more connected in a meeting changes the meeting more than ten percent. Ten percent more connected with a partner during a single dinner changes the dinner more than ten percent. The non-linearity is one of the reasons the practice works.

Over a few days the person starts to catch themselves before reaching for the unconnected default. The pause that the reminder used to produce starts to happen on its own. The reminder becomes redundant for some categories of activity.

Over a few weeks: the operating frame of the person's day shifts. Tasks that were previously dreaded start to be available as opportunities for the practice. The dread does not disappear; it becomes a doorway. "This is the thing I least want to do" becomes "this is where the practice has the most to offer."

The most reported effect: things in the day stop taking the energy they used to take. The exhaustion that came from being slightly out of connection in dozens of small interactions across a day reduces. The person ends the day with more energy than they used to, doing the same activities, because the underlying connection cost has dropped.

## When to Install

- After a coaching session in which connection has been identified as the underlying want of the person's various career or life questions.
- After a person has experienced the immediate effect of being more connected in a single conversation (often within the coaching session itself).
- When the person is in transition between major chapters and needs an operating principle that is not tied to a specific goal. See [[whats-next-after-i-reached-my-big-goal]].
- When the person is recovering from a long stretch of performance-mode and the body is just starting to come back online.
- When the person is asking how to apply [[connection-as-life-bottom-line]] in daily life.
- When the person is approaching burnout from being slightly out of connection in too many interactions per day.
- For someone who specifically gravitates to "hacks" -- the framing as a small daily filter is more likely to land than a more abstract instruction about presence.

## What Joe Does With the Implementation

If the person installs the practice and reports that it works: celebrate, do not amplify. The practice does not need to be turned into a bigger thing. Ten percent is enough. Eleven percent is not the next move. Sustained ten percent is the next move.

If the person reports they forget to use the reminder: install a better reminder. The forgetting is not a character flaw; it is the system reverting to the default. The reminder needs to be more frequent, more disruptive, or more pleasant. Iterate until it works.

If the person uses the practice as a new should: see [[wants-over-shoulds]]. The 10% experiment turned into "I should be more connected" is not the practice. The practice is genuinely an experiment -- the person is allowed to try it, notice what happens, and adjust. The frame of experiment is doing real work.

If the person reports they cannot tell whether they are 10% more connected: that is also useful data. The early phase of the practice is often about building the felt-sense vocabulary for what "more connected" actually feels like. The discrimination develops with practice.

## The Body-Will-Remember Variant

A specific instruction Joe pairs with this practice: do not rush to write things down. When an insight or a creative idea arrives during a 10%-more-connected moment, let it land in the body first. The body has its own memory. If the insight is allowed to integrate somatically, it will be available later. If it is immediately written down, the writing is often a way to leave the body and the integration does not complete.

Joe's explicit form: "As you move away from the brain and all the intellect stuff and you move into your body, you'll start to be able to trust that your body will catch stuff that you don't need to write on a list. If you're all in the head, then there's a lot of like, oh, I have to capture this. But if you actually allow it to hit your body and resonate in your body, oftentimes that works better than a list because you write it on a list. But do you remember to read the list and whatever happens next?"

This is not a prohibition on writing things down. It is a sequencing instruction. Let the insight integrate in the body first. Then write it down if needed. The writing-after-the-integration is different from the writing-as-the-integration.

See [[let-your-body-remember-this-moment]] for the closely-related coaching move.

## What This Practice Is Not

- Not meditation. There is no posture, no timer, no breath count. The practice runs in the middle of normal activities.
- Not mindfulness training in the standard sense. The target is not awareness for its own sake. The target is connection -- with self, with the activity, with the other person, with the larger system the activity is part of.
- Not a productivity hack. The point is not to do more or do faster. Sometimes the connected version of an activity takes longer. That is acceptable.
- Not a substitute for the deeper work on what is blocking connection. If the person has a chronic blocker -- a body that has been shut down for decades, a chronic anxiety, an unresolved relationship that is taking attention -- the practice will eventually surface the blocker. The blocker still needs the deeper work. The practice does not bypass it; it makes it visible.

## Tone

Practical, light, slightly playful. The practice is presented as an experiment, not as a discipline. The person should feel they are testing something that might not work, not signing up for a regimen. The light tone is part of what makes the practice sustainable.

If the practice is delivered with heaviness, the person will treat it as another should. The whole point of the framing as a 10% experiment is to keep the practice under the threshold at which the system will reject it as obligation.

## Variations

- For the person who wants a more granular target: 1% more connected, applied to a single activity per day, for one week.
- For the person whose work is performance-oriented (presenters, performers, teachers): 10% more connected during one specific recurring performance moment per day.
- For the person who is in a relationship that has gotten flat: 10% more connected with the partner during one specific recurring interaction (the morning coffee, the evening hand-off).
- For the leader: 10% more connected during one specific recurring meeting. The team will notice within two or three iterations.
