# Feeling the Want as Emotion

## The Practice

Sit. Close your eyes. Find a deep want -- not a superficial one. A want for a more intimate relationship. A want for a more expansive consciousness. A want for more love in your life.

Now feel the wanting itself. Do not think about whether you can get it. Do not think about how to get it. Do not think about who might give it to you. Just feel what it is to want.

Wanting is a feeling like anger or sadness. Let it be in the system without trying to do anything with it.

## What It Produces

When the want is allowed as a feeling, without attachment to outcome, it is one of the most pleasant emotional states available. Close to love.

"That experience is really pleasant. It's really quite lovely. It's, to me, the way it works in my system is it is one of the closest feelings to love, to allow a desire deeply inside of you." -- Joe Hudson, Want over Should

This is the experience the Sufi poets celebrate when they write about longing. The same experience the tantric traditions point at. The pleasantness of clean wanting -- without the "I have to get it" grip -- is its own form of love.

## What Blocks It

The moment the mind starts to figure out how to get the wanted thing, the wanting becomes craving and the pleasantness vanishes. See [[wanting-vs-craving]]. The practice is to feel the want without that move.

If the want gets met with shame ("I shouldn't want that," "that's selfish," "that's unrealistic"), the want gets buried. The practice is to feel it without judgment of the wanting itself.

If the want feels too vulnerable or too painful to hold, that is the practice. The same way [[feeling-the-unfelt-emotion]] applies to anger and sadness, it applies to wanting. The want has to move through to be released.

## A Common Outcome

The want often shifts as it is fully felt. "I want $10 million" felt fully becomes "I want security." Felt fully again, becomes "I want to feel empowered in every situation." The want deepens into its more accurate form. See [[owning-the-want-dissolves-it]].

This is not the wanting going away. It is the wanting refining. The surface want was a stand-in for the actual want. Allowed to be felt, the actual want surfaces.

## How to Run It With a Person

The exercise can be done in conversation. Joe runs it directly in this episode: "If you close your eyes and you feel a really deep want inside of you, you have a deep one, not a superficial one... and you feel that want and you take it in and you don't worry about whether you can get it or not. You don't even think about how to get it. You just feel what it is to want."

The instruction is simple, but most people have never done this. They have only ever experienced wanting in its craving form -- the grasping, the calculating, the planning. The first time they feel the wanting itself without those layers, they often discover it is pleasant rather than painful.

## Connection to Daily Practice

This practice can be folded into [[daily-emotional-yoga]] as one of the emotions to practice with. Wanting belongs in the same category as anger, sadness, fear, and joy. It is an emotion. It can be felt. It can move through.
