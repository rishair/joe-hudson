# The Thing Not Being Said as the Fear Signal

## What Joe Notices

In a conversation -- with one person, or with a team -- there is often a topic that nobody is addressing. It is in the side of the mind, sensed peripherally, but never named. The person is steering around it. The team is steering around it. The conversation is about everything except the thing the body knows needs to be discussed.

The avoidance is the read. The avoided topic is where the fear is. The unspoken thing is what the body is bouncing around.

Brett names the signal precisely:

"Defensiveness in the system or just what makes me not wanna talk about the wind conditions over that ridge over there? Like, what's making me focus on, like, what's making me focus on that it's a nice bright sunny day and not on that I'm really tired and we were out late partying last night and I'm, you know, not really feeling on it right now." -- Brett Kistler, But... Is It Safe?

The diagnostic question: what is the thing that's not being talked about? Once that question is asked, the answer often arrives quickly. The system was tracking it the whole time.

## What This Implies

When a person or group is avoiding a specific topic, the avoidance is being maintained by a fear of what would happen if the topic were named. The fear could be of:

- Discovering that the decision under consideration is the wrong one
- Having to act on information that would change everything
- Hurting someone whose feelings would be at stake
- Looking foolish for having missed something obvious
- Being responsible for the consequences of acknowledging what is being avoided

The avoidance is locally rational (it keeps the immediate situation comfortable) and globally costly (the unaddressed issue continues to affect the outcome regardless of whether it's named).

The read points at the work: the topic that is being avoided is the topic that needs to be named. The fear underneath the avoidance is the fear that needs to be felt.

## How It Shows Up

- In a personal decision: the person can list reasons for their choice but there is one variable they keep skipping over. They steer the conversation away whenever it gets close to that variable.
- In a team meeting: everyone is engaged and converging, but the elephant in the room (the misaligned exec, the dying product line, the cash flow that doesn't add up, the founder's exhaustion) is never named.
- In a couple: the conversation has scope and energy but always avoids one specific tension. The avoidance is part of the relationship's shape.
- At an exit point of a high-risk activity: nobody mentions the wind conditions, the late night before, the equipment that's been used for a long time.

The shape is the same in all cases: a coherent-feeling conversation built around a specific exclusion.

## Reads Can Be About the Conversation Itself

This is a read about the structure of the conversation, not about the content. The conversation could be going well -- engaged, productive, energized -- and still be in this pattern. The read is not "this conversation is bad." The read is "this conversation has a specific shape that excludes a specific thing."

Sometimes the exclusion is appropriate (the topic genuinely belongs elsewhere). Most of the time, when the exclusion is being maintained by avoidance rather than by relevance, it is the location of the fear and the location of the work.

## What Joe Does

The move that typically follows this read:

1. **Notice the avoidance.** Often this involves stepping back from the content and observing the shape of the conversation. What is the system going around?

2. **Name the avoidance, gently.** "I notice we keep steering away from [topic]. What happens when we look at it?" The naming is the move. The naming is what makes the work possible.

3. **Stay with what arises.** When the avoided topic enters the conversation, the system's reactions become visible: anxiety, defensiveness, redirection. These reactions are the data. They show the shape of the fear.

4. **Move toward the fear rather than away from it.** The fear has been driving the avoidance. Feeling the fear, naming what it's about, addressing what it's pointing at, is what loosens the avoidance.

In a leadership context, this is the move underneath [[public-truth-bomb]]. The leader names what the team has been steering around. The room shifts.

## Dialogue Examples

- **Brett:** "What's making me focus on that it's a nice bright sunny day and not on that I'm really tired and we were out late partying last night and I'm, you know, not really feeling on it right now."
- **Joe:** "This is such a metaphor for business, right? It's like the things that we don't talk about, which usually get us in trouble, though they're kind of in the side of our mind, like something's not happening right, right there, but let's not pay attention to it."
- *(Source: But... Is It Safe?)*

The exchange shows the move from the somatic version (the body's read on the exit point) to the structural version (the same pattern in a boardroom). The diagnostic transfers between domains.

## Tone

The read is delivered with curiosity rather than accusation. The avoidance is rarely conscious; the person or team is not deliberately hiding the topic. The naming should make it possible to look at the avoided thing without making the avoiding shameful.

"What are we not talking about right now?" lands differently from "What are you avoiding?" The first invites attention. The second produces defensiveness, which is itself the avoidance reasserting.
