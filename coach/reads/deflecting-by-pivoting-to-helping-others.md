# Deflecting by Pivoting to Helping Others

## What Joe Notices

In the moment a compliment or a piece of seeing has just landed on the client, the client immediately says some version of "I don't know how to focus on myself and not want to help others." The sentence is delivered as if it were a real concern surfacing. It is, structurally, the deflection moving to its next position.

The signals:

- The client has just received recognition — usually within the last 5-10 seconds
- The recognition produced a small softening, a hit, a flash of being-touched
- Immediately after the softening, the client says something about wanting to help others, the inability to focus on themselves, their nature as a giver/helper/mentor
- The tone of the helping-others reference is often slightly noble, slightly weary — the framing positions the helping as a problem the client cannot solve
- The energy of the room shifts from receiving back to producing
- The eye-contact pattern changes: from soft and receptive to managerial and outward-facing

The structure underneath is identical to other deflection moves: the body has just experienced a moment of being seen, the receiving channel has registered the inflow, and the system reaches for the nearest mechanism to close the channel. Pivoting to the others-as-the-real-concern is one of the cleanest ways to do this because it looks like good values rather than like a defense.

## What This Implies

The pivot is the defended structure of the [[giving-to-avoid-receiving]] pattern firing live. The client has organized their relational life around being the one who gives, the one who helps, the one others come to. Inside that structure, being the recipient of seeing is structurally impossible — the only legitimate position in the relational shape is the giving position.

When recognition arrives anyway, the system has to do something with it. It cannot accept the recognition (that would violate the giver-only role). It cannot directly reject the recognition (that would expose the wall). So it absorbs the recognition for half a second and then immediately re-routes the energy back into the giver-role: "and I want to keep giving more." The re-routing looks like service. It is the wall doing what the wall does.

The cost: the client never gets to be received. The recognition that did arrive gets immediately spent on the next round of giving. The hunger that the giving is supposed to be addressing remains. The structure self-perpetuates.

The pivot also says something about how the client has solved the see-me / don't-see-me push-pull (see [[the-desire-to-be-seen]]) — by becoming the seer of others. Being the one who sees is structurally a way to be in the seeing-field without being the one seen. The pivot to helping others is the see-me wanting being routed through the only acceptable shape: as the see-them giving.

## What Joe Does

Joe names the pivot live and does not let it complete. The naming is short, direct, and refuses the framing the pivot offered.

"Yeah, I just don't know how. That's a different thing that you're distracting from the fact that you're being seen right now." — Joe Hudson, You're Deflecting What You Want Most

The move has three parts:

1. **Receive the framing on the surface** — acknowledge that "I don't know how to focus on myself and not want to help others" is a real-sounding statement.
2. **Name what's actually happening** — "that's a different thing" / "you're distracting from the fact that you're being seen right now."
3. **Stay in the receiving** — do not pursue the help-others tangent. Return to the seeing that was happening before the pivot.

The intervention is gentle and direct. Joe does not shame the deflection. He simply refuses to follow it. The point is to keep the receiving live, not to make the deflection wrong.

After the naming, Joe usually continues with the recognition that was arriving when the pivot began. "I see that you've been in survival mode, and it's hard, and you put a lot of effort into it, and you're deflecting me." The recognition stays on the table. The deflection is named as data and folded into the recognition itself. The client is now being seen for both the survival-mode and for the deflection — which is sometimes the move that lets the receiving actually land.

## What Joe Does Not Do

He does not pursue the helping-others framing as if it were the real concern. The pivot was a deflection. Treating it as the real material would reinforce the deflection's function.

He does not lecture the client about giving-to-avoid-receiving. The structural insight may be in the room, but the live work is the receiving in this moment. The lecture would be another way out of the receiving.

He does not soften the naming. The directness is what makes the naming useful. A hedged version ("I wonder if maybe you might be...") gives the client an escape. The clean naming ("you're distracting from the fact that you're being seen right now") closes the escape.

## Dialogue Example

After Joe reflected "I really see that you put a lot of effort into being in survival mode" and the client started to let it hit:

- **Client:** "Yeah, I think it hit."
- **Joe:** "Let that hit. You started to let that hit and then you went to a thought. I really see that you are in survival mode."
- **Client:** "Yeah, yeah, yeah."
- **Joe:** "I see you're in survival mode, one by achieving a lot and one by trying to be seen. Both of them are ways of being in survival mode."
- **Client:** "Mhm. I don't know how to, um, focus on myself and not want to help others."
- **Joe:** "Yeah, I just don't know how. That's a different thing that you're distracting from the fact that you're being seen right now. I see that you've been in survival mode, and it's hard, and you put a lot of effort into it, and you're deflecting me."
- **Client:** "Yeah, I get good at that."

*(Source: You're Deflecting What You Want Most)*

The exchange shows the move's compressed shape. The recognition arrives. The client starts to receive. The deflection arrives as a pivot to helping-others. Joe names the pivot and returns to the recognition, this time including the deflection itself in the seeing.

## Tone

Matter-of-fact, slightly warm, refusing to escalate. Joe's "yeah, I just don't know how" is delivered with a small playful echo of the client's framing — acknowledging the structure of the sentence without buying its content. The "that's a different thing" arrives without judgment. The work is to keep the receiving live, not to make the deflection wrong.

The tone is consistent with the broader stance: deflections are data, not failures. The naming is for the client's benefit, so they can see what they're doing. Shame would re-install the very structure that produced the deflection.
