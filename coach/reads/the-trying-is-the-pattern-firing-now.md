# The Trying Is the Pattern Firing Now

## What Joe Notices

The person describes a pattern they want to change — they push emotion away, they dissociate, they hide. They then say "I'm trying" to indicate they are working on it. The trying is the pattern, happening in the room, right now.

The defining signals:

- The person reports being a certain way ("I'm dissociated," "I'm a masker") and then immediately says "I'm trying" to acknowledge they are working on it
- The body is visibly doing the pattern as they describe the pattern
- The "trying" has the same somatic signature as the thing they're trying to stop — the same tension, the same hold, the same outward shape
- The pattern they describe in third person ("I push emotion away") is happening in first person as they describe it
- The "I'm trying" arrives with a posture that is itself the pattern — head pulled back, breath held, eyes deflected

The decisive marker: the trying produces no movement away from the pattern, only more of the pattern. The system has substituted the effort to leave for the effort to stay, and they look identical.

## What This Implies

The person has not yet seen that the trying is the same gear as the doing. They are using effort to undo a pattern that is itself made of effort. The result is that the pattern persists with a new name on top of it.

Specifically with dissociation: the dissociation is held in the system by sustained tension. The "trying" to come back is also sustained tension. The tension is the dissociation. As long as the trying continues, the dissociation continues.

The structural mechanism: the pattern was originally installed as the body's response to a specific kind of pain. The pattern still operates because the pain is still there. When the person tries to stop the pattern, they are pushing on the response without addressing what installed it. The push uses the same musculature as the response. The body, asked to push, produces the response.

This is the live, in-the-room form of [[trying-prevents-the-feeling]]. There the principle is stated structurally. Here it is being witnessed in real time: the person is demonstrating the principle on themselves while reporting that they cannot solve it.

## What Joe Does

Name it. Plainly. "It's happening right now. The trying is trying to stop it from happening right now."

The naming has to be specific to the live moment. Not "trying doesn't work in general" — that is a teaching. The live naming points at the exact instance: "What you are doing right now, in this sentence, with this body, is the pattern. The trying is the doing of the pattern."

Decline the explanation. The person often pivots into a description of why they are the way they are ("I live a lot of my life in my head"). The pivot is more of the pattern. Joe does not follow the pivot. He stays with what just happened in the room.

In the source session: the client said "I'm trying" and started to explain himself as an introvert who lived in his head. Joe interrupted with: "You're isolated right now, bro. You're isolating the sadness that's happening right now, like in this moment. You're isolating your sadness. What just happened?" The interruption refused the descriptive frame and pointed at the live event.

The follow-up question Joe uses after the naming: "What just happened? What the fuck was that?" The question forces the person to attend to the felt experience of the moment they were just told they are inside. The attention itself is what allows the pattern to update — the person sees what they were doing.

## The Linked Move

The naming is the read; the follow-up move is usually [[amplify-the-dissociation-then-opposite-with-no-effort]] or its analog for the specific pattern. The structural logic: once the person has seen the trying as the pattern, the way to release it is not to try less but to do the pattern deliberately at maximum intensity, then release the effort, then go to the opposite end without effort.

The reason the move follows the read: simply being told "the trying is the pattern" does not by itself release the pattern. The body needs to feel the trying as a voluntary action it is doing. The amplification makes the trying conscious. Once conscious, it can be released.

## Dialogue Examples

The full read-and-move sequence from the source session:

- **Joe:** "So my answer to your question is, how do you do it properly? Is to not listen to any of that bullshit."
- **Client:** "I'm trying, I'm trying."
- **Joe:** "Well, it doesn't sound like you need to try. What makes you try?"
- **Client:** "Just trying to—"
- **Joe:** "It's happening right now. The trying is trying to stop it from happening right now. I mean, that's what I'm looking at."
- **Client:** "Yeah, um, I mean, I, I live a lot of my life in my head. Um, you know, I'm a very introverted person and I've kind of been isolated through a lot of different aspects of my life, which, you know, I'm learning."
- **Joe:** "You're isolated right now, bro."
- **Client:** "Yeah."
- **Joe:** "You're isolating the sadness that's happening right now, like in this moment. You're isolating your sadness. What just happened? What the fuck was that?"

A second instance later in the same session:

- **Joe:** "So your body knows who you are, knows where you are, knows what's going on with you."
- **Client:** "So just trying to listen to my body more."
- **Joe:** "Trying?"
- **Client:** "Well, just — I think I've neglected—"

The second instance shows the same read deployed compactly. The client says "trying," Joe echoes it with a question mark to make it audible, then uses [[paradoxical-instruction-to-resist]] to make the trying visible by asking for it deliberately.

## Tone

Direct. Slightly impatient with the descriptive frame, but warm. The "What the fuck was that?" is delivered with curiosity, not aggression — the implication is "something just happened that you didn't notice, let's both look at it." The profanity makes it casual; the question makes it serious.

The naming is short. Joe does not lecture. "You're isolated right now, bro." Six words. The brevity prevents the person from converting the naming into a concept to think about. The teaching is the present-tense fact.
