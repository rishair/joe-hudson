# Pyannote voiceprint speaker labeling for Joe Hudson

## Hypothesis

A voiceprint extracted from a 30-60 second clip of Joe Hudson speaking alone can be used with pyannote speaker embeddings to correctly label Joe Hudson's segments across different episodes with different guests, with at least 90% accuracy.

## Method

1. Identify a segment where Joe Hudson speaks alone (e.g., an episode intro, a solo segment, or a clip where only Joe is talking for 30-60 seconds). Extract it with ffmpeg.
2. Generate a speaker embedding using `pyannote/embedding` model on the extracted clip.
3. Take the diarized output from [[E-005]] (WhisperX prototype). For each speaker segment, compute its embedding and calculate cosine similarity against Joe's voiceprint.
4. Apply a threshold (start at 0.7, tune if needed) to classify segments as "Joe Hudson" vs "Guest."
5. Manually verify 20+ speaker turns per episode against the audio. Count correct and incorrect attributions.
6. Test across 2-3 episodes with different guests to confirm the voiceprint generalizes.

## Prerequisites

- [[E-005]] must produce diarized output first (this experiment uses its segments).
- Need at least one audio file with a known Joe-only section.

## Success Criteria

- Joe Hudson correctly identified in 90%+ of his speaking turns across multiple episodes.
- False positives (guest labeled as Joe) under 5%.
- The voiceprint works across episodes recorded at different times with different guests.
