# Prototype compendium absorption on 5 transcripts

## Hypothesis

Reading 5 diverse transcripts and manually extracting coaching content into compendium articles will validate whether the taxonomy from R-004 works, whether directional navigation links are natural to create, and whether the article format is rich enough to serve a chatbot.

## Method

1. Select 5 transcripts for diversity: 1 solo Joe episode, 1 coaching session (Joe + student), 1 Joe + Brett episode, 1 multi-guest panel, 1 recent episode
2. Create `meta/wiki/compendium/` directory with subdirectories per the taxonomy from R-004
3. Read each transcript fully. For each coaching concept, pattern, technique, or framework Joe discusses:
   a. Check if an article already exists. If yes, enrich it with this new source.
   b. If no, create a new article with YAML frontmatter (type, id, title, related, tags)
   c. Include direct quotes from Joe with episode attribution
   d. Add directional links: "upstream" (what leads here) and "downstream" (where Joe goes from here)
4. After all 5 transcripts, create `_index.md` and `_backlinks.json`
5. Validate: do articles have at least 2 navigation links? Do they cite sources? Is the taxonomy holding up?

## Success Criteria

- At least 10 articles created from 5 transcripts
- Each article has frontmatter, quotes, and directional links
- The taxonomy from R-004 accommodates all content (no orphan concepts)
- Articles cross-reference each other naturally
- A human reading the articles can trace coaching navigation paths

## Blast Radius

Creates new directory `meta/wiki/compendium/` and files within. Does not modify existing transcripts or wiki pages. New content only.

## Result

Absorbed 5 transcripts into 11 compendium articles across 6 of 7 taxonomy categories:

| Category | Count | Articles |
|----------|-------|----------|
| Concepts | 4 | VIEW, Vulnerability, Wonder, Should |
| Distinctions | 2 | Vulnerability vs Weakness, Curiosity vs Wonder |
| Frameworks | 2 | Stages of Emotional Development, Three Ways We Push Away Love |
| Principles | 2 | Joy Requires All Emotions, Resistance Causes Pain |
| Moves | 2 | Reflecting the Pattern Back, Following Wonder |
| Practices | 1 | How and What Questions |
| Applications | 0 | (none extracted yet) |

All articles have YAML frontmatter with type, id, title, related, tags, and sources. All include direct quotes with attribution. All have upstream/downstream navigation links.

Transcripts absorbed:
1. Introduction to VIEW (Connection Course #1)
2. Father Figures and Authority (Coaching Session)
3. Stages of Emotional Development (Emotion Series #1)
4. Want over Should (Master Class #6)
5. You're Pushing Away What You Want Most (2026)

## Analysis

**Taxonomy validation**: The 7-category taxonomy from R-007 holds up. All extracted content fits naturally into one category. No orphan concepts. The "Applications" category was not populated because application-specific content (e.g., VIEW in business) naturally emerged as subsections within concept articles rather than standalone articles. This may change at scale.

**Navigation links**: Upstream/downstream links were natural to create. The flow patterns from R-007 (concept -> distinction, pattern -> origin, concept -> practice) appeared consistently. Articles average 3-4 navigation links each.

**Article quality**: Each article is 20-40 lines with direct quotes, clear definitions, and navigation. The format works for a chatbot: articles are self-contained enough to answer a question but interconnected enough to follow threads.

**Coverage**: 5 transcripts produced 11 articles. At this rate, 480 transcripts would produce ~1000 articles, though many would enrich existing articles rather than create new ones. The core concepts will likely stabilize within 30-50 transcripts.

**Boundary condition**: This prototype was done manually by reading full transcripts. Scaling to 480 will require LLM-assisted extraction. The manual prototype establishes the quality bar.

## Dead Ends

None. The taxonomy and format worked on first attempt.
