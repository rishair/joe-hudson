# Extract Joe Hudson voiceprint embedding

## Hypothesis

A speaker embedding extracted from a 60-second clip of Joe Hudson speaking alone can reliably distinguish him from other speakers via cosine similarity.

## Method

1. Installed speechbrain with ECAPA-TDNN model (`speechbrain/spkrec-ecapa-voxceleb`) in `.venv` (Python 3.13, torch 2.12.0). Pyannote's embedding model was tried first but requires gated HuggingFace access (user must accept license at hf.co/pyannote/embedding). Speechbrain's model is open and ungated.
2. Extracted two 60-second clips of Joe Hudson from episode intros (solo speech):
   - Sample 1: "Introduction to VIEW" (2020-10-26), first 60s
   - Sample 2: "Wonder" (2020-11-04), first 60s
3. Extracted two 60-second clips from a different episode at timestamps where a guest is likely speaking:
   - Guest sample: "Triggered" (2022-09-30), 10-minute mark
   - Mixed sample: "Triggered" (2022-09-30), 20-minute mark
4. Computed 192-dimensional ECAPA-TDNN embeddings for all four clips.
5. Measured cosine similarity between all pairs.

## Result

| Comparison | Cosine Similarity |
|---|---|
| Joe (ep1) vs Joe (ep2) | **0.954** |
| Joe voiceprint vs guest (10min) | **0.314** |
| Joe voiceprint vs mixed (20min) | **0.358** |

The voiceprint file was saved to `meta/raw/joe-hudson-voiceprint.npy` (192-dimensional, L2-normalized average of both Joe samples).

All success criteria are met:
- Joe-vs-Joe similarity (0.954) exceeds the 0.75 threshold.
- Joe-vs-Guest similarity (0.314) is far below Joe-vs-Joe.
- The gap (0.954 - 0.314 = 0.640) far exceeds the 0.15 minimum.

## Analysis

The ECAPA-TDNN voiceprint approach works well for distinguishing Joe Hudson from other speakers. A threshold of 0.6-0.7 would cleanly separate Joe from guests with no overlap observed in these samples.

**Boundary conditions**: Tested on 4 clips from 3 episodes, all recorded in similar podcast studio conditions. The voiceprint may be less reliable on: (a) clips with significant background noise, (b) very short segments (<5s), (c) segments with overlapping speech from two people. These edge cases should be tested in [[E-006]].

The guest sample at the 10-minute mark may contain some Joe speech mixed in. Even so, the similarity (0.314) is far from Joe's (0.954), suggesting the model is robust to moderate contamination.

**Model choice**: ECAPA-TDNN via speechbrain was used instead of pyannote/embedding because pyannote's model is gated (requires HuggingFace license acceptance). ECAPA-TDNN is a standard speaker verification model trained on VoxCeleb, producing 192-dimensional embeddings. It is compatible with pyannote's diarization output from [[E-005]].
