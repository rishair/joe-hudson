# Extract Joe Hudson voiceprint embedding

## Hypothesis

A pyannote speaker embedding extracted from a 30-60 second clip of Joe Hudson speaking alone can reliably distinguish him from other speakers via cosine similarity. This is the independent prerequisite for [[E-006]] (voiceprint-based speaker labeling) and does not require WhisperX diarization output.

## Method

1. Install pyannote.audio and torch.
2. Pick a podcast episode known to start with Joe speaking (e.g., an early Connection Course episode where he introduces the topic solo).
3. Use ffmpeg to extract the first 60 seconds as a WAV clip.
4. Load `pyannote/embedding` model and compute the speaker embedding vector for the clip.
5. Save the embedding to `meta/raw/joe-hudson-voiceprint.npy`.
6. As a sanity check: extract a second 60-second clip from a different episode where Joe speaks, compute its embedding, and measure cosine similarity against the first. Should be >0.8 if the voiceprint is stable.
7. Extract a clip where a guest is clearly speaking. Compute similarity against Joe's voiceprint. Should be <0.5 (or at least significantly lower than the Joe-vs-Joe comparison).

## Success Criteria

- A voiceprint embedding file is saved.
- Joe-vs-Joe cosine similarity across two different episodes is >0.75.
- Joe-vs-Guest cosine similarity is measurably lower (>0.15 gap).
- If the gap is too small, the voiceprint approach may not work and E-006 should be reconsidered.
