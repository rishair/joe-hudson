# yt-dlp audio download from AoA YouTube channel

## Hypothesis

yt-dlp can reliably download audio-only files from the Art of Accomplishment YouTube channel without triggering 403 errors or rate limiting.

## Method

1. yt-dlp 2026.03.17 was already installed (from [[E-001]]). FFmpeg 8.0.1 confirmed present.
2. Tested single video download without cookies: `yt-dlp -x --audio-format mp3 --embed-metadata --output "transcripts/%(upload_date>%Y-%m-%d|)s_%(title)s/%(title)s.%(ext)s" <URL>`. Succeeded immediately.
3. Tested batch of 5 videos from the channel with sleep intervals (3-10s): `yt-dlp -x --audio-format mp3 --embed-metadata --output "..." --download-archive archive.txt --sleep-interval 3 --max-sleep-interval 10 "https://www.youtube.com/@ArtofAccomplishment/videos" --playlist-items 1-5`. All 5 downloaded.
4. Verified output files with `file` command: valid MPEG ADTS layer III audio with ID3 v2.4 metadata.

## Result

**5 of 5 videos downloaded successfully** as MP3 audio files. No cookies were required. No 403 errors or rate limiting occurred.

| File | Size | Date in filename |
|------|------|-----------------|
| You're Pushing Away What You Want Most | 8.5 MB | 2026-05-19 |
| Why You Can't Ask for What You Want | 9.8 MB | 2026-05-14 |
| Anxiety Explained In A Way That Finally Makes Sense | 11.0 MB | 2026-05-12 |
| The Fear of Being Seen and the Strange Way Out | 31.2 MB | 2026-05-08 |
| After 14 Years of Coaching... | 27.4 MB | 2026-05-05 |

Total: ~88 MB for 5 videos. The `--download-archive archive.txt` flag correctly skipped the first video on the batch run (already downloaded).

Output directory format: `transcripts/{YYYY-MM-DD}_{title}/{title}.mp3`. Upload dates are correctly embedded in filenames via the `%(upload_date>%Y-%m-%d|)s` template.

Audio quality: 96 kbps stereo at 48 kHz. This is adequate for speech transcription. Higher bitrates are available but unnecessary for speech-only content.

## Analysis

The experiment succeeded on all criteria. Cookies are not required for this channel. yt-dlp's JS challenge solver (deno-based) handles YouTube's anti-bot measures without authentication.

**Scaling estimate**: 329 videos at ~18 MB average = ~5.8 GB total. With 3-10s sleep intervals, downloads would take approximately 30-60 minutes for the full channel. The `--download-archive` flag enables resumable downloads.

**Boundary conditions**: This test used only 5 videos. Rate limiting may appear at higher volumes (100+ videos in a single session). The sleep intervals (3-10s) should mitigate this. If 403 errors appear during full-channel download, cookies from a logged-in session would be the next step.

## Dead Ends

None. The original method specified cookies as required; they turned out to be unnecessary for this channel.

## Next Steps

- Full-channel download can proceed using the same command without `--playlist-items` limit. Should be run as a background task given the ~30-60 minute estimate.
- The `archive.txt` file prevents re-downloading files already obtained.
- Consider adding `--write-info-json` to save per-video metadata (title, description, upload date, duration) alongside the MP3 files.
