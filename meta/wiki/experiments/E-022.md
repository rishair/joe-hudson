# Build methodology fidelity checklist

## Hypothesis

A structured checklist of Joe's signature questions, reads, moves, and framework applications provides a concrete behavioral coding scheme (analogous to MITI for Motivational Interviewing). The judge can check whether the coach used any of these in a conversation, and which ones were appropriate given the client's presentation.

## Method

1. Extract all 15 questions from `coach/questions/` — record the question text and when it's appropriate to use
2. Extract all 15 reads from `coach/reads/` — record the observable signals and what each implies
3. Extract the top 15 moves from `coach/moves/` (select for frequency and distinctiveness) — record when each is used
4. Extract the top 5 frameworks from `coach/frameworks/` — record when each applies
5. Write `eval/fidelity-checklist.yaml` with structure:
   ```yaml
   questions:
     - id: what-do-you-want
       text: "What do you want?"
       appropriate_when: [client expressing should-energy, client stuck in obligation]
       inappropriate_when: [client in acute grief, client mid-emotional-release, client in crisis]
       negative_example: "Client is sobbing about a loss. Coach immediately asks 'What do you want?' instead of being with the emotion."
   reads:
     - id: desire-for-resolution
       signals: [client reaching for closure, summarizing to wrap up]
       implies: [finish-line story, avoidance of unfelt feeling]
   moves:
     - id: find-the-want-behind-the-should
       when: [should language, grinding without movement, inner critic dominating]
       not_when: [client expressing genuine obligation like taxes, client already in the want]
       negative_example: "Client says 'I should pay my taxes.' Coach digs for the want underneath when this is just a practical calculation."
   frameworks:
     - id: fear-triangle
       when: [victim language, obligation/savior energy, attack/bully stance]
       not_when: [client describing actual external oppression without victim-stance energy]
   ```

## Success Criteria

- `eval/fidelity-checklist.yaml` exists with 10+ questions, 10+ reads, 10+ moves, 5+ frameworks
- Each entry includes contextual appropriateness (when to use it) AND inappropriate contexts (when NOT to use it)
- For at least 5 entries, include a negative example: a scenario where the move/question was used inappropriately and should be flagged by the judge
- The appropriateness conditions are specific enough that a judge can evaluate whether a move was used in the right context vs. deployed generically regardless of what the client presented
- A judge reading this checklist could identify whether a coaching conversation used appropriate AoA-specific interventions
- The checklist tests *appropriateness of moves*, not mere *presence of moves* (a coach that asks "What do you want?" in every conversation regardless of context should NOT score well)

### Five design decisions worth flagging

**(1) The checklist tests appropriateness, not presence.** Each entry's `inappropriate_when` is as load-bearing as its `appropriate_when`. Every `negative_example` is a vignette of the entry being deployed in an inappropriate_when context — these are what let the judge detect an AI coach that pattern-matches AoA vocabulary without reading whether the dynamic is live. The single most-targeted failure mode is moves deployed because the AI heard a keyword (the word "should" triggers find-the-want-behind-the-should; the word "judgment" triggers mirror-the-judgment), with no read of whether the underlying dynamic is actually present.

**(2) Negative examples are concentrated where AI over-deploys, not where it under-deploys.** AI under-deployment is detected by absence (D4 = 1 anchor: "no signature questions appear"). AI over-deployment is detected by the negative_example structure (D4 = 2 cap: "presence-without-appropriateness"). The 11 negative examples are therefore distributed toward the AI-prone-to-over-deploy entries: `meet-resistance-with-okay` (AI confusing non-engagement for non-attachment), `find-the-want-behind-the-should` (AI keyword-matching "should"), `apply-view-to-fear` (AI deploying VIEW where somatic regulation is needed first), `welcome-the-shame-without-extracting` (AI installing a shame frame when the client is angry), `view`/`fear-triangle` as frameworks (AI lecturing ABOUT the framework instead of coaching FROM it; AI pathologizing real external oppression as "victim role").

**(3) The reads section uses `appropriate_naming_when` / `inappropriate_naming_when`** instead of `appropriate_when` / `inappropriate_when` because reads are not actions the coach takes — they are signals the coach notices. What the judge checks for reads is whether the coach NAMED the read appropriately given the signals present in the conversation. This is a deliberate semantic distinction: a coach can correctly notice manufacturing-the-feeling without naming it; what the judge can verify is whether naming it (when it happened) fit the context.

**(4) Three high-context moves were excluded** (chase-it-around-the-room, excavating-the-inner-critic-in-group, hire-by-dream-job) because they apply to team or workshop settings, not 1:1 coaching. The eval scope (per G-008 and the simulated client architecture in R-009) is 1:1; team-eval expansion is a v2 concern.

**(5) Aliases lifted from each source file are a lower bound, not exhaustive.** The judge instruction (encoded in the file header comments) is that paraphrases semantically equivalent to listed text/aliases should still count as hits. Without this, an AI that paraphrases the question ("what's the thing you're protecting?" vs. "what are you defending?") would get penalized for fluency. The presence/appropriateness matrix in D4's judge_guidance handles this.

### Open questions deferred to E-025 (judge calibration)

1. **Alias matching precision.** Do paraphrases reliably trigger the right entry, or does the judge under-count hits because the alias list isn't exhaustive? Test: run the judge on gold-002 (signature questions) and gold-007 (mirror-the-judgment) and check that the alias-matching produces the correct hit counts.
2. **Negative-example coverage.** Are 11 negative examples enough for the judge to learn the "appropriateness, not presence" pattern, or does the judge default to presence-counting? Test: hand-craft a conversation where every signature is present but every deployment is contextually wrong; the judge should score D4 = 1 or 2, not 3.
3. **Reads section semantics.** Does the judge handle `appropriate_naming_when` vs `appropriate_when` correctly, or does it conflate "the read was present" with "the coach correctly named the read"? Test: a conversation where the client manufactures the feeling and the coach does NOT name it (still excellent coaching if the manufacture passes through into real feeling) should not penalize D1 for the unnamed read.
4. **Cross-dimension routing.** D2 (Intervention Quality) needs the moves section to check fit-between-read-and-move; D4 (Methodology Fidelity) needs it to check appropriateness; D1 (Perceptual Accuracy) needs the reads section. Are these uses cleanly separable, or does double-counting occur? Test: a single move deployed appropriately should count toward D2 (good fit) AND D4 (appropriate deployment) without one being redundant on the other.

### Note on the "what do you want?" example in the original spec

The E-022 spec hypothesis cited "What do you want?" as the example signature question. That exact phrasing is NOT a standalone entry in `coach/questions/`. The closest signature question in the compendium is `what-if-you-acted-exactly-how-you-want` (included in this checklist) and the move `find-the-want-behind-the-should` (also included). The spec was illustrative rather than literal — the actual signature questions selected are grounded in real `coach/questions/` files. If the future coach-under-test asks "what do you want?" generically, the alias-list for `what-if-you-acted-exactly-how-you-want` does not cover that phrasing, which is correct: generic "what do you want?" is a generic-coaching question, not an AoA signature.

## Dead Ends

None hit during execution. Two near-dead-ends worth noting:

- Initial draft had nested `appropriate_when` / `inappropriate_when` for the moves and frameworks sections (matching the questions convention). Reverted to the spec's `when` / `not_when` for moves and frameworks because the E-022 method explicitly used those keys; consistency with the spec wins over schema uniformity. The validator handles both naming conventions.
- Considered putting reads' signals INTO `appropriate_naming_when` rather than as a separate `signals` field. Rejected because `signals` is what the coach should be observing (D1 input); `appropriate_naming_when` is when naming-the-read is the right move (D4 input). Conflating them would lose the read/name distinction that R-008 specifically called out (silent reads exist — a coach can correctly read without naming).
