# Managing vs Healing

## The Distinction

Joe draws a sharp line between managing a pattern (keeping symptoms under control, reducing reactivity, coping) and healing it (the pattern resolves so completely that the person can say "it's over").

## How Joe Explains It

Joe asks van der Kolk directly: "How do you define the difference between managing PTSD and healing it?" Van der Kolk's answer aligns with Joe's position: "The right outcome criteria is for people to say, it's over. I used to be an abused and neglected kid, but now I feel loved inside."

Joe's work aims for resolution, not management. The goal is not "I have tools to handle my triggers." The goal is "the trigger no longer triggers me because the underlying emotion has been fully felt." See [[feeling-unfelt-emotions]].

## When Someone Experiences This

When you have been in therapy or coaching for a long time and things are "better" but not resolved. When you have coping strategies that work but the pattern is still there underneath. When you wonder if this is as good as it gets. Joe's position: resolution is possible for most people. Management is a step on the way, not the destination.

## Upstream (What Leads Here)

- When someone has been doing "the work" but still feels stuck
- When someone has good coping tools but the underlying pattern persists
- When a coach or therapist is measuring success by symptom reduction rather than resolution

## Downstream (Where Joe Goes From Here)

- Joe moves toward [[feeling-unfelt-emotions]] as the mechanism for resolution (not just management)
- Joe creates a [[container-of-love]] where resolution can happen
