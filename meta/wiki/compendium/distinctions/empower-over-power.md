# Empower over Power

## The Distinction

Power is control over other people and situations. Empowerment is being yourself despite the consequences. Power is fear-based and addictive: the more you have, the more you need. Empowerment is available in any situation, including prison.

"Power is control over other people, and empowered means that you're not looking for control of others. You're just being you despite the consequences." -- Joe Hudson, Empower over Power

## Power never satisfies

Power works like an addiction. There is a short-term high and then it is over. A billionaire drives around thinking "I don't have enough" just like anyone else. Having power does not resolve the fear that drove the search for it. It just adds the burden of maintaining what you have accumulated.

"As long as you need to control a situation to feel empowered, then you are subjugated." -- Joe Hudson, Empower over Power

Everyone seeking power is scared. When the seeking stops, the fear is still there. See [[fear-invites-what-we-fear]]: the fear of powerlessness drives controlling behavior that produces more situations requiring control.

## Empowerment inside constraints

Empowerment does not require freedom from constraints. It requires showing up authentically within whatever constraints exist. The constraints then have to adjust.

"If you are scared of losing your job and you say, forget it, I'm gonna show up the way that feels right for me, and if I get fired, I get fired, you will change the system. There's no way for it not to change." -- Joe Hudson, Empower over Power

The CEO has more bosses than anyone: employees, customers, shareholders, board. The billionaire cannot control elections. Everyone is interdependent. The question is not "how do I get more control?" but "how do I show up as myself within whatever situation I am in?"

## Empowerment vs power in relationships

In relationships, seeking power looks like: trying to get someone to change, controlling the emotional temperature, managing how others perceive you, hoarding information. Empowerment looks like: [[vulnerability]], [[owning-wants-vs-being-partial]], [[drawing boundaries without asking someone to change]].

## When Someone Experiences This

When you feel trapped by circumstances and believe more control would solve it. When you have achieved a position of power and feel no more secure than before. When you are managing everyone around you and exhausted. When you cannot distinguish between asserting yourself and controlling others.

## Upstream (What Leads Here)

- When [[my-team-isnt-getting-along]]: the leader may be using power rather than empowerment
- When someone reports feeling powerless in a relationship or job
- When someone has accumulated power (money, status, authority) and is still insecure
- When [[caring-vs-caretaking]]: caretaking is a form of power (managing others' emotions for your comfort)

## Downstream (Where the Coaching Path Goes)

- [[vulnerability]]: empowerment requires being yourself despite consequences, which is the definition of vulnerability
- [[what-do-you-want]]: shift from "what do I need to control?" to "what do I actually want?"
- [[inherent-goodness]]: if you are inherently good, you do not need to control the situation to be safe
- [[feel-before-acting]]: feel the fear of losing control, then act from clarity rather than from fear
