# Technique vs State of Mind

## The Distinction

Joe draws a sharp line between communication techniques (learnable scripts, frameworks, strategies) and the state of mind from which communication arises. His position: technique applied over the wrong state of mind fails or causes harm. The right state of mind produces effective communication with minimal technique.

## How Joe Explains It

Joe's evidence is neurological. Microexpressions, body language, mirror neurons, and vocal intonation transmit the speaker's actual internal state regardless of words. These signals cannot be faked.

"Neurologically and physically, when we communicate, there's a lot of things going on besides what we say. There is our body language, there is the empathy that's happening via mirror neurons, there's intonation, there's microexpressions... and they can't be faked." -- Joe Hudson, Introduction to VIEW

Joe cites NVC (Nonviolent Communication) as an example of a good technique that gets "weaponized" when the underlying state is hostile or controlling. See [[dont-use-view-as-technique]].

## When Someone Experiences This

When you have learned a communication technique and it initially worked but then started "going south." When people accuse you of being manipulative even though you are using "the right" words. When you sense you are performing openness rather than being open.

## Upstream (What Leads Here)

- When someone asks "what's the technique?" or wants a script for a conversation
- When someone reports that a communication method stopped working or made things worse
- When NVC or other communication frameworks are being used mechanically

## Downstream (Where Joe Goes From Here)

- Joe redirects from "what do I say?" to "what state of mind am I in?"
- Leads to [[view]] as the practice of cultivating the right state
- Leads to [[vulnerability]]: "are you in your truth?"
