# Enjoy over Manage

## The Distinction

Management is fighting the river to get a specific result. Enjoyment is reading the river and flowing with it while holding a clear intention. Both require determination. The difference is whether you are forcing the path or allowing it.

"Imagine that you're on a boat and you are going down a river. Management is when you're fighting against the river, but when you're in that effortless flow of the river, there's an enjoyment to it. What you have to do is you have to be listening to that river deeply. You have to be listening to that impulse." -- Joe Hudson, Enjoy over Manage

## Management defined

Management is intellectually trying to assure a specific outcome. It shows up as:
- Trying to get a conversation to go a specific way instead of following what is true
- Pushing people toward a result instead of asking what speaks against it
- Controlling the process instead of recognizing when it is right
- Trying to change internal states (managing emotions, forcing motivation)

Every place of management is an inefficiency indicator. The need to manage points to a system that is not working well enough to run on its own. Great employees do not require management. Great products do not require management. Great meditation is not managing the mind; it is enjoying whatever is happening.

"If you learn to manage your life, you end up with a life that you need to manage, and that is not happiness." -- Joe Hudson, Enjoy over Manage

## Enjoyment defined

Enjoyment is not doing things you like. It is allowing pleasure in whatever you are doing. It costs nothing. It requires no change in circumstances. It is always available.

"Right now I can say to everybody, hey, enjoy yourself just a little bit more right now. Just a little. Just allow a little more enjoyment in at this moment." -- Joe Hudson, Enjoy over Manage

See [[enjoy-yourself-ten-percent-more]] for the specific practice.

## Goals are fine. Specific paths are not.

Determination and unacceptance of a result you do not want are critical. But the determination must be general ("I want a successful company") not specific ("I want it done this exact way"). Specific path attachment is management. General intention with river-reading is enjoyment.

"You have to keep all that determination, you have to keep all that fortitude. And there is also a reading the river and letting the river flow and paying attention to that river and following it to get there." -- Joe Hudson, Enjoy over Manage

## When Someone Experiences This

When you feel like you are fighting your way through your day. When your to-do list is a source of dread rather than energy. When you are exhausted from managing people, processes, or yourself. When meditation feels like torture (managing the mind vs being with it). When you have achieved what you set out to achieve and it does not feel the way you expected.
