# Don't try to fix people

## The Anti-Pattern

Joe provides an explicit list of what not to do in a VIEW conversation, all forms of partiality:
- Don't try to fix people
- Don't try to get them to a conclusion
- Don't try to be valuable or have them see you as valuable
- Don't want them to be different
- Don't try to get them to be different
- Don't look to convince
- Don't try to lead them to a solution
- Don't want them to see things your way

## Why It Fails

The subtle message behind fixing is: "I think you're not good enough to figure this out yourself." This reinforces the other person's shame. It produces the opposite of what is intended.

"If you're telling somebody that, you're also making that true for them. Whereas if you are looking at them and in the conversation, you see them as wise, you speak to the wise part of them, the part of them that knows, then that's the part that's going to come out and meet you." -- Joe Hudson, Impartiality (Connection Course #3)

Fixing is also self-serving: the fixer needs to feel valuable, needed, or smarter. When Joe names this in himself mid-conversation, it sounds like: "I noticed that I'm trying to fix you and I'm so sorry."

## What Joe Says Instead

Trust the other person's wisdom. "I think you know what you need better than anybody else could know what you need." See them as wise and the wise part shows up. See them as helpless and the helpless part shows up.

Own your partiality when you notice it: "Oh, I noticed that I'm giving you advice. I'm trying to fix you. How do you feel about that?" Joe notes that most people, most of the time, will say: "Yeah, please don't."

## Joe's Words

"As far as partiality, it's easier to define them as don'ts. So it'd be don't try to fix people. Don't try to get them to a conclusion. Don't try to be valuable or have them see you as valuable. Don't want them to be different. Don't try to get them to be different. Don't look to convince. Don't try to lead them to a solution. Don't want them to see things your way." -- Joe Hudson, Impartiality (Connection Course #3)
