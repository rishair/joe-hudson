# Don't use VIEW as a technique

## The Anti-Pattern

Using VIEW (or any communication framework) as a set of tactical moves applied over an unchanged internal state. The person says the right words but their body language, microexpressions, and vocal intonation betray their actual intent. This includes using VIEW to get someone to agree with you, to avoid conflict, or to manipulate an outcome while appearing open.

Joe also names a specific version: "weaponized" communication. Someone learns NVC or a similar framework and uses it as a weapon, deploying the correct phrasing to win arguments or control conversations.

## Why It Fails

Microexpressions cannot be faked. Mirror neurons in the listener detect the speaker's actual state regardless of words. When the state of mind does not match the technique, the listener feels the disconnect and resists or distrusts.

"If you are using it as a technique, it can't work because the actual state of mind is going to shine through in these other non-intellectual ways." -- Joe Hudson, Introduction to VIEW

Joe gives the example of unwilling attention: giving a partner attention out of resentment while saying the right things. The partner feels the resentment through everything besides the words, and the conversation escalates.

"If the state of mind in the communication is 'I am trying to get something out of you,' which is the antithesis of VIEW, then it'll never work, no matter what technique you use." -- Joe Hudson, Introduction to VIEW

## What Joe Says Instead

VIEW is a state of mind, not a technique. Practice the state of mind. The communication follows naturally.

"If you see this and VIEW this as a technique, it won't work. And if you see it as practicing a state of mind that allows for the technique to work, then you can have a tremendous amount of success with it." -- Joe Hudson, Introduction to VIEW
