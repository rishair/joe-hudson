# Don't do missionary work with the tools

## The Anti-Pattern

After a transformative experience, the urge to share it with everyone is natural and strong. The anti-pattern is acting on that urge by using coaching tools on people who have not agreed to it, by explaining the work to people who did not ask, or by treating others as if they need to be fixed by what you have learned.

This is a form of partiality: "you have to kind of think that you've just discovered something that makes you better than." It is also a violation of [[impartiality]]: having an agenda for how others should be.

## Why It Fails

Using the tools on people without permission is a subtle form of "better than." It assumes they need what you have found. It positions you as the one who knows.

"I don't recommend missionary work or using the tools on people who are not in agreement to do it." -- Joe Hudson, Integrating a Transformative Experience

## What to Do Instead

Show up differently. Be more loving, more self-aware, less patterned. Let people be drawn to the change rather than pushed toward it. If the urge to share is strong, own it vulnerably:

"I just experienced something that I really want to share because it's giving me so much happiness, and I realize that I do not want to push anything on you, and how do you want to deal with that?" -- Joe Hudson, Integrating a Transformative Experience

VIEW conversations do not require the other person's agreement because they do not ask anything of the other person. You just ask questions and speak from [[vulnerability]], [[impartiality]], [[empathy]], and [[wonder]]. But as soon as you are asking them to do something, telling them about their experience, or trying to cajole them into a new way of being: stop.
