# VIEW

## Definition

VIEW is the foundational framework in Joe Hudson's coaching work. It stands for Vulnerability, Impartiality, Empathy, and Wonder. Joe describes it not as a communication technique but as a state of mind from which effective communication naturally emerges. The four components are not steps. They are simultaneous qualities of a single state.

## How Joe Explains It

"Practically, it's a way to have conversations. It's a communication methodology that allows your conversations to be far more effective. And by effective, I mean more connected, more intimate, and more productive... But realistically, it's a state of mind. It's communicating from a state of mind." -- Joe Hudson, Introduction to VIEW

## Origin

Joe discovered VIEW by studying people with an unusual capacity for effective conversations. A consultant named Case could produce breakthroughs and sell with an 80% success rate. When asked how, Case insisted: "It's not the technique. You can't do this if you're trying to learn the technique." After Case died, Joe traveled the world studying people with similar abilities and identified what they all had in common: the VIEW state of mind.

## The Four Components

- **[[vulnerability]]**: Speaking your truth even when it's scary
- **[[impartiality]]**: Not trying to achieve an outcome for yourself or others
- **[[empathy]]**: Being with a person in their emotions (not fixing, not losing yourself)
- **[[wonder]]**: Curiosity without needing the answer; living in the question with awe

Joe describes these as "great ways to sink into [the state of mind] quickly. If you're not there, what are the best words, reminders to get you there quickly?"

## Why state of mind, not technique

Joe is emphatic: VIEW applied as a technique over the wrong state of mind fails. Microexpressions, body language, and mirror neurons transmit the true internal state regardless of words. See [[technique-vs-state-of-mind]] and [[dont-use-view-as-technique]].

"If you are using it as a technique, it can't work because the actual state of mind is going to shine through in these other non-intellectual ways." -- Joe Hudson, Introduction to VIEW

## The technique layer

The only technique on top of the state of mind is asking [[how-what-questions]] (open-ended questions). These naturally emerge from wonder and impartiality.

## When Someone Experiences This

When your conversations feel stuck, adversarial, or shallow. When communication techniques you have learned "go south" or feel manipulative. When you sense that your partner, team, or client can tell you are not genuine even when you are saying the right words. VIEW addresses the root cause: the state of mind behind the words.

## Upstream (What Leads Here)

- When someone wants more effective communication in business or relationships
- When someone has tried communication techniques (NVC, active listening) and they "got weaponized"
- When a team is not getting along and needs deeper connection
- When someone senses inauthenticity in their own communication
