# Trauma Mapping

## Definition

Trauma mapping is Joe's term for the process by which people unconsciously recreate their early traumatic experiences. A person who learned that love equals criticism will attract critical people, hear criticism where none exists, and manipulate situations to confirm that love is criticism. Joe describes a three-part cycle: attracting, manipulating, and proving.

## How Joe Explains It

Joe frames the cycle as the organism seeking homeostasis. During early trauma, certain emotions could not be fully felt. The system keeps returning to the same situation in an attempt to complete the cycle.

"If a person has been taught that love is criticism, they go around manipulating the world, attracting critical people, proving that love is criticism. They're hearing criticism when it doesn't actually happen." -- Joe Hudson, A Discussion with Dr. Bessel van der Kolk & Joe Hudson

"Our organism is looking for homeostasis again. And so there were some emotions that they weren't allowed to feel in those moments of trauma. And if you allow them to feel-- once the body actually allows and looks forward to those emotions that are being held back, then they no longer are attracted to the trauma." -- Joe Hudson, A Discussion with Dr. Bessel van der Kolk & Joe Hudson

Van der Kolk summarizes the core dynamic: "People choose what's familiar over what's safe." Joe agrees.

## When Someone Experiences This

When you find yourself in the same relationship pattern again. When you think "why does this always happen to me?" When different people in your life produce the same feeling in you. When you realize you are hearing criticism, abandonment, or control even when it may not be there. See [[i-keep-ending-up-in-the-same-pattern]].

## Upstream (What Leads Here)

- When someone presents [[i-keep-ending-up-in-the-same-pattern]]
- When Joe notices a person describing the same dynamic with multiple different people
- When someone describes "attracting" a particular kind of person repeatedly

## Downstream (Where Joe Goes From Here)

- Joe connects the present pattern to childhood: what did love look like in your family?
- Joe identifies the unfelt emotion underneath and moves toward [[feeling-unfelt-emotions]]
- Joe creates a [[container-of-love]] for the emotion to surface
- If the person is already deep in their work and regressing, this may be [[the-deepening]]

## Questions Joe Asks

- Connecting present to origin: "What did love look like in your family?"
- Identifying the unfelt emotion: "What couldn't you feel as a child in those moments?"

## Tone

Joe is matter-of-fact when describing this pattern. Not clinical. Not soft. He states it plainly, as if describing how gravity works. The pattern is not the person's fault, and Joe's tone reflects that.
