# What's the feeling I'm resisting?

## The Question

- "What's the feeling I'm resisting?"
- "What's the thing I don't want to feel?"
- "What does [this pattern] become if there's no resistance in it?"

## When to Use It

This is the most commonly missed question. It applies whenever there is resistance, stuckness, avoidance, or a pattern that will not shift despite effort. The resistance is almost always about an emotion that is not being felt.

Specific triggers:
- Procrastination: the resistance is not about the task. It is about an emotion the task is associated with (fear of failure, fear of being seen, fear of imperfection)
- Addiction or bad habits: the substance or behavior is an escape from an emotion. What emotion?
- Stuckness in a conversation: something is not being said or felt

## What It Produces

The question bypasses the surface behavior and goes to the root. The answer is usually an emotion: sadness, helplessness, fear of rejection, anger. Once named, the emotion can be felt. Once felt, the resistance often dissolves without willpower.

An example: procrastination. The question "what does procrastination become if there's no resistance in it?" produced the answer: "the impulse to do something." The procrastination contained the energy to act. The resistance was converting that energy into avoidance.

Another example: a pot habit. The underlying feeling was sadness. Once the sadness was felt and embraced, the habit dissolved without effort over months. No willpower. No trying. Just a lack of interest.

"I didn't try at all to end this thing. I didn't at all try to stop it. And now it holds absolutely no appeal. There's just zero appeal. There's no temptation. There's no willpower. There's just a lack of interest." -- Joe Hudson, If You Can't Love the Feeling, Love the Resistance

## What to Do With the Response

Once the feeling is named: feel it. Not analyze it. Not understand it. Feel it in the body. Welcome it. See [[feeling-unfelt-emotions]] and [[embrace-intensity]].

## Tone

Warm, curious. Not diagnostic. The question invites exploration, not confession.
