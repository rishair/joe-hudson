# What pleasure do you find in that?

## The Question

- "What pleasure do you find in that?"
- "What pleasure do you find in that new identity that's coming online?"

## When Joe Uses It

Joe asks this when someone is describing a difficult transition or a loss they are resisting. In this transcript, van der Kolk describes struggling with aging and losing relevance. Joe asks: "What pleasure do you find in that new identity that's coming online?"

The question reframes a situation the person experiences as purely negative into one that might contain something they want. It is a wonder-based question: Joe is genuinely curious whether there is pleasure hidden inside what looks like pain.

## What It Produces

Van der Kolk responds honestly: "Not sure how much pleasure I find it." He cannot find pleasure in it. Joe does not push. The question landed, the person answered truthfully, and the honesty itself was the value.

This question can produce:
- A pause and genuine reflection (the person has never considered pleasure in this context)
- An honest "no" (which itself is data)
- A surprise discovery of something they do enjoy about the situation
- Resistance (which tells Joe something about what the person is avoiding)

## What Joe Does With the Response

When van der Kolk says he finds no pleasure, Joe accepts it and moves on. He does not force a reframe. The question was an invitation, not a demand.

## Tone

Warm, curious, light. Joe asks this with genuine wonder, not as a therapeutic maneuver. The word "pleasure" itself is disarming because it is unexpected in the context of loss.
