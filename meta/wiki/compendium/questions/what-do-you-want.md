# What do you want?

## The Question

- "What do you want?"
- "What do you actually want here?"
- "Forget what you should do. What do you want?"
- "What's the impulse underneath the should?"

## When to Use It

When someone is describing what they should do, what they need to do, what they have to do. The language of obligation signals that [[shoulds-vs-wants]] is operating. The should is sitting on top of a want, compressing it. This question lifts the should to reveal the want underneath.

Also when someone is stuck, indecisive, or procrastinating. See [[i-keep-procrastinating]]. The stuckness often comes from a should that has killed the natural impulse.

Also when someone cannot name what they want, only what they should want. See [[i-dont-know-what-i-want]].

## What It Produces

The want is "just that impulse that moves through you, that animates your actions." It is somatically expansive, intellectually empowering, and energizing. When someone connects with a clean want (not craving, not attached, not struggle), something loosens. The body opens. Energy becomes available.

Common responses:
- The person gives a should disguised as a want ("I want to be more disciplined"). Push deeper: that is a should wearing a want's clothes
- The person gives an external goal ("I want a million dollars"). Ask what the deeper want is underneath that. Usually empowerment, freedom, safety, or love
- The person says "I don't know." This is data, not a dead end. See [[i-dont-know-what-i-want]]
- The person connects with a genuine want and emotion surfaces. Stay with the emotion

## What to Do With the Response

If the response is a should in disguise: "That sounds like something you think you should want. What do you actually want?"

If the response is a surface want: follow it deeper. "And if you had that, then what?" Keep asking until the want feels expansive rather than grasping.

If the person connects with a genuine want: stay with it. Let them feel it. The want itself, when contacted clearly, often resolves the stuckness without any additional intervention.

Wants deepen when followed. "You follow the chocolate cake because you want to have this sense pleasure. Follow that want home and you find out it has 7 more beautiful siblings." -- Joe Hudson, Want over Should

## Tone

Warm, curious, unhurried. The question is an invitation, not a challenge. The person may have spent years not being asked this question.
