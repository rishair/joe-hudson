# Learning to Love All Parts

## Definition

Joe's summary of his entire methodology in one sentence: all the work is about learning to love different aspects of yourself. Not getting rid of parts. Not fixing them. Not managing them. Loving them.

## How Joe Explains It

"Almost everything that we do in our work is about learning to love the different aspects of ourselves." -- Joe Hudson, A Discussion with Dr. Bessel van der Kolk & Joe Hudson

Joe describes the process as fractal: the same pattern of identifying a part, feeling the emotions around it, and learning to love it happens at every scale. Small patterns resolve in a single session. The deepest patterns, "the ones at the very base of the personality," can take years.

In group settings, Joe creates conditions for this through public vulnerability. In week-long courses, participants list their critical inner voice publicly. The group's continued love in the face of the "worst" parts dissolves the justification for self-hatred. "Not getting rid of it, but learning to love it."

Joe connects to IFS (Internal Family Systems) without naming it. Van der Kolk raises IFS explicitly, describing how "manager" parts resist vulnerability. Joe agrees and adds the fractal observation.

## When Someone Experiences This

When you are trying to "fix" or "get rid of" a part of yourself. When you hate something about yourself and want it gone. When you think healing means becoming someone different. Joe's position: the part you hate is the next thing to love.

## Upstream (What Leads Here)

- When any specific pattern, emotion, or behavior has been identified
- When shame is present: the parts people hide are the ones most in need of love
- When someone says "I need to get rid of this" or "I need to fix this about myself"

## Downstream (Where Joe Goes From Here)

- For specific parts: Joe moves toward [[feeling-unfelt-emotions]] for the emotions the part carries
- For shame-based hiding: Joe moves toward [[container-of-love]] and witnessed vulnerability
- The principle generates direction: whatever you do not love in yourself is the next thing to work on

## Anti-Patterns

Joe says "not getting rid of it, but learning to love it." The distinction matters. This is not a technique for eliminating unwanted behavior. It is a reorientation toward self-compassion. The behavior often changes as a byproduct, but the goal is love, not change.
