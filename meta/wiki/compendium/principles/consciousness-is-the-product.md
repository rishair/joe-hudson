# Your Consciousness Is the Product

## Definition

The state of mind brought to any work affects the product. Anxiety produces anxious products. Enjoyment produces products people want to be around. This is not metaphorical. Consciousness is consumed by the customer, the team, the partner, the audience.

"It doesn't matter if it's Facebook or Ford or Van Gogh, we're feeling the consciousness of those people who made it. We're feeling that experience. And if they were anxious and nervous, then we're perpetuating anxiousness and nervousness." -- Joe Hudson, The Art of Accomplishment

## In Practice

This principle connects [[technique-vs-state-of-mind]] (the state shows through regardless of words) to business and product development. A software product built by a team in conflict will have that conflict embedded in its design. A company run on fear produces fear in its customers. A Subway employee who works with joy attracts opportunity; within 3 weeks someone offered him a better job.

"How we get something done affects what it is. It's as simple as if we rush to sweep the porch or if we enjoy sweeping the porch, we're going to get two different jobs done." -- Joe Hudson, The Art of Accomplishment

## Efficiency measured by enjoyment

Speed is not efficiency. A fast car is not an efficient car. Efficiency in human terms is measured by enjoyment: the least amount of effort and friction needed to produce the result. When there is enjoyment, there is ease. When there is ease, there is mastery. The master butcher's blade "finds the space between the meat and the bone." No sharpening needed. Minimal friction.

## When Someone Experiences This

When the work feels like a grind and the output reflects it. When a team is producing technically correct but lifeless work. When a product or service feels off and no one can articulate why. The consciousness of the creators is embedded in the result. See [[enjoy-over-manage]]: management produces managed results; enjoyment produces enjoyed results.
