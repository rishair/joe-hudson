# If You Can't Love the Thing, Love the Resistance

## Definition

Resistance is not the enemy of transformation. It is a natural step in it. Fighting resistance creates more resistance. Resisting the resistance is just another layer of war. The way through is to love the resistance itself.

"In a war with yourself, you always lose." -- Joe Hudson, If You Can't Love the Feeling, Love the Resistance

## In Practice

Resistance is not being at peace with what is. Intellectually, it shows up as denial, avoidance, defensiveness. Emotionally, it amounts to not wanting to feel something. In the nervous system, there is fear underneath it even when it looks like anger or frustration.

The response is not to overcome resistance but to be with it. Listen to it. Find the wisdom in it. There is always some wisdom, even if 99% is ignorance and 1% is wisdom.

"There's always some wisdom in the resistance. There's always some." -- Joe Hudson, If You Can't Love the Feeling, Love the Resistance

## Meta-resistance

The most common trap: resisting the resistance. "I should stop procrastinating" is resistance to procrastination, which is resistance to the project, which is resistance to an emotion. Each layer compounds the war. "You can't fight your way out of a war."

Judging the resistance, trying to fix the resistance, wishing the resistance away: these are all more resistance. The question is not "how do I get rid of this?" but "how do I hold this as lightly, as gently, as lovingly as possible?"

## When Someone Experiences This

When you are stuck in a pattern and fighting it makes it worse. When you judge yourself for not being further along. When self-improvement efforts create more self-criticism. When you are trying to force a change and the harder you push the more stuck you get.
