# Grief as Gateway to Self-Love

## Definition

Joe identifies a recurring pattern in long-term healing work: the moment a person grieves the years they spent trapped in a pattern is the moment that precedes genuine self-love. The grief is not a setback. It is the door.

## How Joe Explains It

Joe calls this a "watershed moment." It occurs after the person has already done significant work and can see their pattern clearly. The grief is specific: not sadness about what happened to them, but grief about what they did to themselves by perpetuating the pattern.

"That's a watershed moment in healing a certain pattern in a human, is that there's a moment where they grieve the fact that they've been doing this to themselves or they've been agreeing to this for the last 20 or 30 years and that they could have-- and that moment is often the precursor to loving themselves or loving that aspect of themselves is that grief." -- Joe Hudson, A Discussion with Dr. Bessel van der Kolk & Joe Hudson

## When Someone Experiences This

When you can finally see the pattern clearly and realize how long it has been running. When you feel sadness not about what was done to you but about what you did to yourself. When the weight of lost years hits you. This is not a setback. It is the door opening.

## Upstream (What Leads Here)

- After [[feeling-unfelt-emotions]] has occurred and the person can see their pattern clearly
- After [[trauma-mapping]] has been named and the person recognizes how long they have been in the cycle
- When the person has enough self-awareness to see the gap between who they were and who they could have been

## Downstream (Where Joe Goes From Here)

- Grief naturally transitions into self-love and self-compassion
- Joe does not rush this transition. The grief itself is the work
- Connects to [[learning-to-love-all-parts]]: loving the part of yourself that kept you in the pattern

## Tone

Joe is quiet and spacious when describing this moment. He names it plainly but does not push toward it. He recognizes it when it arrives.
