# Enjoy What You're Doing (Not Do What You Enjoy)

## Definition

The guiding principle of transformation: not "do the things you enjoy" but "enjoy the things you are doing." This includes enjoying discomfort, enjoying confusion, enjoying resistance, enjoying not knowing. Enjoyment is not a reward for doing the right thing. It is the approach that makes the right thing happen.

"The question isn't, how do I do the things that I enjoy? The question is, how do I enjoy the things that I'm doing?" -- Joe Hudson, Embarking on the Journey

## Why it works

Enjoyment creates a positive feedback loop. When something is enjoyed, there is more approach and less avoidance, which accelerates learning. Two people on a roller coaster having the exact same physical experience: one releases anti-carcinogenic chemicals (enjoyment), the other releases carcinogenic chemicals (fear). Same experience, opposite effect. The variable is enjoyment.

This is not about forcing a smile. That is less enjoyable than admitting the discomfort. It is about asking: how can I meet this experience most joyfully? If resistance shows up, how can I meet the resistance joyfully? Not how do I make the resistance go away. See [[love-the-resistance]].

## In Practice

- Criticizing yourself? How do you enjoy criticizing yourself? (This sounds absurd, which is the point. The absurdity breaks the cycle.)
- Feeling unclear after a breakthrough? How do you enjoy not knowing?
- In pain? How do you enjoy the pain? Not masochistically. But the embrace of it, the intimacy with it, the honesty of it.
- Driving by the house that triggers a knot in the stomach? Drive by on purpose. Hope the feeling comes back. Be with it. Attend to it. Love it.

## When Someone Experiences This

When transformation feels like work. When personal development is exhausting. When there is dread about "having to do the work." When someone is efforting toward change and burning out. The reframe: this is not work to endure. It is experience to enjoy. Even the hard parts. Especially the hard parts.

## Upstream (What Leads Here)

- When any coaching path has become effortful, punishing, or joyless
- When [[love-the-resistance]] has been established but the person is still not finding ease
- When someone is on the right path but treating it as obligation rather than exploration
