# Conflict as Resource

## Definition

Joe asserts that conflict, when approached from a [[view]] state of mind, consistently produces better solutions than existed before the conflict. This reframes conflict from something to avoid or endure into something to seek out.

## How Joe Explains It

"Conflict is an amazing thing because what happens every time is that conflict gives you better solutions than you had before it. You start looking for conflict, not looking for conflict like, ah, here's a way I can pick a fight, but like, oh, there's a tension, let's go explore it. Because I know if I explore it from this frame of mind, all of a sudden I'm gonna have better solutions, better answers, better paths forward." -- Joe Hudson, Introduction to VIEW (Connection Course #1)

Joe distinguishes between seeking conflict as aggression and seeking tension as information. The shift is from "conflict is a problem to solve" to "tension is a signal that unexplored territory exists."

## When Someone Experiences This

When you avoid difficult conversations because you are afraid of conflict. When tension exists in a team or relationship and everyone manages around it. When you think of conflict as failure rather than information. Joe's reframe: "Oh, there's a tension, let's go explore it."

## Upstream (What Leads Here)

- When someone avoids conflict or dreads difficult conversations
- When a team is performing poorly because tensions are unaddressed. See [[my-team-isnt-getting-along]]
- After [[view]] has been established: conflict only becomes a resource when the state of mind is right

## Downstream (Where Joe Goes From Here)

- [[vulnerability]] is the first step: being willing to name the tension
- Joe uses this to motivate teams to address tensions directly
- Without [[view]], conflict is just a fight. This principle depends on the state of mind

## Anti-Patterns

This principle depends entirely on the VIEW state of mind. Conflict approached from fear, agenda, or judgment does not produce better solutions. Joe is specific: "if I explore it from this frame of mind." See [[dont-use-view-as-technique]].
