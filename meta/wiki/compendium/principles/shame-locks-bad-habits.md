# Shame Locks Bad Habits in Place

## Definition

Joe asserts that the causal direction between shame and unwanted behavior is the opposite of what most people assume. People think they feel shame because of their behavior (not working out, not being liked, etc.) and that fixing the behavior will remove the shame. Joe says the causation runs the other way: shame causes the behavior. Remove the shame and the behavior changes naturally.

## How Joe Explains It

"There's this correlation between thinking that you're not good enough -- let's call that shame -- and that there's actions that prevent you from being happy. You think you're not good enough because you haven't worked out. You think you're not good enough because you still get angry. And you think that if you do all that stuff, then you're going to be happy. But the causation is actually the opposite of what people think. It's far more that shame causes the actions than it is that the actions are what cause the shame." -- Joe Hudson, Impartiality (Connection Course #3)

"Shame is kind of like the locks that keep the chains of bad habits in place." -- Joe Hudson (attributed to Adyashanti), Impartiality (Connection Course #3)

## When Someone Experiences This

When you keep trying to fix your behavior (diet, exercise, productivity) but cannot sustain the change. When you beat yourself up for failing and then fail again. When self-improvement efforts create a cycle of effort, failure, and shame. Joe's frame: the shame is not the result of the failure. The shame is the cause. Address the shame and the behavior follows.

## Upstream (What Leads Here)

- When someone presents "I keep trying to change but can't"
- When [[i-cant-stop-criticizing-myself]] is present: the inner critic is the shame mechanism
- When someone is caught in a cycle of self-improvement and failure

## Downstream (Where Joe Goes From Here)

- Joe does not try to change the behavior. He addresses the shame underneath
- Connects to [[learning-to-love-all-parts]]: loving the part that feels shame
- Connects to [[dont-try-to-fix-people]]: trying to fix someone adds to their shame
