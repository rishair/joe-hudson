# The 3-5 Rule

## Definition

When you change a behavior or draw a new boundary, expect the other person to test it 3 to 5 times, escalating each time. After 3-5 tests, the old pattern breaks and the new one is accepted.

## In Practice

A person who has been accepting yelling decides to leave the room when their partner yells, returning in 30 minutes. The partner will test this 3-5 times. Each test will escalate: they will try guilt, anger, other tactics to pull the person back into the old agreement. After 3-5 consistent responses, the yelling stops because it no longer produces the old result.

"Most likely they're gonna get even more -- they're gonna use a whole bunch of other tricks to keep you in. So they're gonna up the ante 3 to 5 times and then the behavior falls apart." -- Joe Hudson, Integrating a Transformative Experience

## Why this is a gift

This forced repetition is not punishment. It is training. Each pushback forces deeper commitment to the new behavior under increasing pressure. Without the pressure, the new boundary would not solidify.

"I'm really grateful that it's built this way, that humanity seems to tend to act in this way, because it really forces us to learn to keep that boundary. It forces us to really learn what's necessary to change this behavior even under stressful circumstances." -- Joe Hudson, Integrating a Transformative Experience

## When Someone Experiences This

When you have set a new boundary and the other person is escalating. When you changed your behavior and your environment seems to be pushing you back into the old pattern. When you wonder if the transformation is "working" because everything seems harder. This is normal. This is the 3-5 rule at work.

## Upstream (What Leads Here)

- After any transformation or boundary shift, the social environment pushes back
- When [[i-had-a-breakthrough-but-feel-lost]]: part of the "lost" feeling is the environment resisting the new you
- After [[vulnerability]] practice changes how someone shows up in relationships

## Downstream (Where the Coaching Path Goes)

- Hold the boundary through the 3-5 tests. The pattern breaks after that
- Some people (20-30%) may leave. Most stay and adapt
- The new agreements that form on the other side are more authentic
