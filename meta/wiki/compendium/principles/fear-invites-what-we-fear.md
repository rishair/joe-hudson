# Fear Invites What We Fear

## Definition

Joe asserts that the actions people take to avoid what they fear are often the very actions that bring the feared outcome to them. Fear produces controlling, partial behavior, and that behavior creates the conditions for the thing to happen.

## How Joe Explains It

"The thing that we fear is often something that we are unknowingly, subconsciously inviting into our world. Because the things that we do to avoid the thing that we fear are often the things that bring them to us." -- Joe Hudson, Impartiality (Connection Course #3)

Joe gives the base jumping analogy: two people about to jump. One is petrified of screwing up. One has accepted that screwing up is possible. The petrified one has a higher chance of actually screwing up, because fear creates constriction and constriction creates mistakes.

This connects to [[trauma-mapping]]: the person who fears abandonment acts in ways that push people away, producing the abandonment they feared.

## When Someone Experiences This

When you notice that the thing you were most afraid of has happened again. When your attempts to prevent something seem to produce it. When controlling behavior creates the very outcome you were controlling against.

## Upstream (What Leads Here)

- When [[trauma-mapping]] is at work: the person's avoidance behaviors produce the feared outcome
- When fear is driving partiality: the controlling behavior creates resistance and failure
- When someone asks "why does this keep happening to me?"

## Downstream (Where Joe Goes From Here)

- [[visualize-the-feared-outcome]]: feel the fear in advance so the avoidance behaviors stop
- [[impartiality]]: release the need to control the outcome
- [[feeling-unfelt-emotions]]: the fear is rooted in unfelt emotions from the original wound
