# I can't trust myself

## What Someone Says

- "How do I know if it's intuition or fear?"
- "I'm so aware of all these contradictory parts of myself that I can't come to conclusions anymore"
- "I used to just know. Now I second-guess everything"
- "Which voice do I listen to?"
- "I've been hurt before for going after what I wanted, so now I make myself small to feel safe"

Often presents as an intellectual question about discernment ("how do I tell the difference between intuition and fear?") when the actual issue is emotional: the person is running from something.

## What the Coaching Hears

Underneath "I can't trust myself" is usually fear that has not been embraced. The person is not struggling with discernment. They are struggling with attachment to outcomes. If they were focused on how they want to BE rather than what RESULTS they want, the "decision" would not feel like a decision at all. We make thousands of decisions per day without noticing. The ones that feel like decisions are the ones where fear is present.

"If you think you're making a decision, you're in fear." -- Joe Hudson, How Do I Trust Myself?

The deeper layer: the person has been hurt for wanting things or going after things. They have made themselves small to feel safe. A friend says "where's your fire?" They know they have dimmed.

## What to Look For (Reads)

- **Intellectualizing.** The person presents a conceptual question ("how do I distinguish intuition from fear?") instead of a personal one. The body is having one experience while the words are having another. Redirect from head to body.
- **Dissociating in real-time.** Emotion surfaces, then the person jumps to a question or a story. Name it: "What just happened? You were here, and then you left."
- **Parts overwhelm.** The person has become so aware of internal contradictions (through parts work, therapy, or self-study) that they have lost the ability to act. Too many voices, no center. The coaching path: "Where are all the parts when you're here in this moment? What's essentially you?"

## Coaching Path

1. **Redirect from intellectual to personal.** "That's a really intellectual question. What's actually happening for you?" Find the personal version of the presenting question.
2. **Find what's being run from.** "What's the ultimate thing you're running from?" The answer is often helplessness, the abyss, "I won't come out." See [[helplessness]].
3. **Reframe the decision.** "You get to determine all of your results, or you get to determine how you are as a person. Which do you choose?" This reveals the attachment to outcomes driving the fear.
4. **Permission for fear and helplessness.** "It's okay to be scared. It's okay to not know what to do. It's okay to be completely helpless." For many people, no one has ever said this. See [[embrace-intensity]].
5. **Notice the spaciousness.** When the person drops into the emotion, the parts dissolve and spaciousness appears. Name it: "Where are all the parts now?" The answer: gone. This IS the trust they were looking for.
