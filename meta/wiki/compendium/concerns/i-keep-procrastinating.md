# I keep procrastinating

## What Someone Says

- "I keep putting it off even though I know I need to do it"
- "I can't make myself start"
- "I know what to do but I just can't do it"
- "Every day I tell myself I'll do it and every day I don't"
- "I feel lazy but I don't think I actually am"

## What's Underneath

Procrastination is not laziness. It is a negative reinforcement loop. Every time the task comes to mind, self-criticism fires: "why haven't I done this yet?" The task becomes associated with pain. Approaching it feels like approaching punishment. So the organism avoids it, the same way a dog avoids a person who kicks it.

"Every time you think about the problem, you kick yourself in the face. It's like procrastination can be really easily solved if every time you think about the problem, you then envision yourself enjoying the shit out of doing it." -- Joe Hudson, If You Can't Love the Feeling, Love the Resistance

Deeper: the resistance is not to the task. It is to an emotion the task is connected to. Usually fear of failure, fear of imperfection, fear of being seen. The procrastination protects against that emotion. See [[what-feeling-am-i-resisting]].

## What to Look For (Reads)

- Is the person beating themselves up about the procrastination? If so, that is the problem, not the procrastination itself. The self-criticism IS the reinforcement loop
- What emotion is the task associated with? Fear of failure? Fear of being judged? Fear of not knowing what to do?
- Does the person confuse the resistance with laziness? Laziness implies a character flaw. Resistance implies an emotion that needs to be felt

## Coaching Path

1. Break the negative reinforcement loop: stop kicking yourself every time you think about the task. Envision enjoying it instead
2. Ask [[what-feeling-am-i-resisting]]: what emotion is the task connected to?
3. A key question from the transcript: "What does procrastination become if there's no resistance in it?" The answer is usually: the impulse to act. The energy is already there. The resistance is converting it to avoidance
4. Feel the underlying emotion. Once felt, the procrastination often dissolves without willpower. See [[feeling-unfelt-emotions]]
5. See [[love-the-resistance]]: fighting procrastination creates more procrastination
