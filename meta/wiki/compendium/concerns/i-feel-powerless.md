# I feel powerless

## What Someone Says

- "I feel stuck and I can't change anything"
- "I don't have any control over my situation"
- "My boss / partner / parents have all the power"
- "If only I had more money / authority / influence, then I could..."
- "I'm trapped by my circumstances"

## What's Underneath

The feeling of powerlessness is not about the external situation. It is about the belief that control over the situation is necessary to feel safe. See [[empower-over-power]]: power (control) and empowerment (being yourself despite consequences) are different things. Powerlessness is the absence of control, not the absence of empowerment.

"As long as you need to control a situation to feel empowered, then you are subjugated." -- Joe Hudson, Empower over Power

The deeper issue: something about this person's authentic expression feels too dangerous to show. The fear of consequences (rejection, firing, abandonment) has shut down authenticity, which produces the felt sense of powerlessness.

## What to Look For (Reads)

- Is the person in victim mode? Blaming external circumstances for their situation. The coaching path does not start by changing the circumstances. It starts by examining what authentic action the person is avoiding and what emotion they are afraid to feel
- Is the person seeking more power (control) rather than empowerment? More money, more status, more authority are power solutions. They do not resolve the underlying fear
- What would this person do if they could not be fired / rejected / abandoned? That is the empowered action they are avoiding

## Coaching Path

1. Ask: "What would you do if you could not fail?" or "What would you do if the consequences did not matter?" This reveals the authentic action underneath the powerlessness
2. Identify the feared emotion: what will you have to feel if you do that? Usually fear of rejection, abandonment, or loss. See [[what-feeling-am-i-resisting]]
3. [[feel-before-acting]]: feel the feared emotion. Then act from clarity
4. [[vulnerability]]: the empowered action is almost always a vulnerable one
5. [[the-three-to-five-rule]]: expect the system to push back 3-5 times. The system adjusts after that
