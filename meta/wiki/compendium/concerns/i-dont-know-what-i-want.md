# I don't know what I want

## What Someone Says

- "I don't know what I want"
- "I know what I should do, but I don't know what I want"
- "I've been so busy doing what everyone needs that I've lost track of what I want"
- "Nothing sounds appealing"
- "I can list what I don't want but I can't name what I want"
- "I used to know what I wanted but now I have no idea"

## What's Underneath

The connection to desire has been severed by years of [[shoulds-vs-wants]]. The natural impulse (the want) is still there, but it is buried under layers of obligation, shame, and management. The person has been following "should" for so long that the want signal is too faint to hear.

This is not a thinking problem. It is a feeling problem. Wants are somatic: they show up as expansive, energizing sensations in the body. Shoulds show up as constriction, threat, and obligation. The person has been trained to respond to constriction (should) and ignore expansion (want).

## What to Look For (Reads)

- Is the person intellectualizing? If they are trying to figure out what they want, they are in their head, not in their body. Wants are felt, not figured out
- Is there a should underneath the "I don't know"? Sometimes "I don't know what I want" means "I know what I want but I'm afraid to want it" or "I know what I want but I think I shouldn't want it"
- Is the person exhausted? If so, the want may simply be rest. Years of should-driven living produces burnout, and the first want to surface may be the want to stop

## Coaching Path

1. Start with the body: what do you notice in your body right now? Is there expansion anywhere? Constriction?
2. Ask [[what-do-you-want]] without requiring a big answer. "Right now, in this moment, what do you want?" It can be as small as "I want a glass of water"
3. If "I don't know" persists: "What did you want as a child?" or "What would you want if no one would judge you?"
4. If a should surfaces disguised as a want: name it. "That sounds like something you think you should want. I'm asking what you actually want"
5. If exhaustion surfaces: honor it. The want might be rest. That is a valid want. See [[shoulds-vs-wants]]: a child from 0 to 8 follows only wants and develops more than any other period in life
6. Follow the want home when it surfaces: "And if you had that, then what would you want?"
