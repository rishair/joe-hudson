# I never have enough money

## What Someone Says

- "If I just had more money, everything would be fine"
- "I'm always stressed about money"
- "I can't do what I want because I don't have enough money"
- "Money is the thing standing between me and happiness"
- "I need to make more before I can feel secure"

## What's Underneath

Money has become a surrogate. It is standing in for something else: parental love, security, power, community, self-worth. The relationship with money mirrors the relationship with whatever it has replaced, usually a relationship from childhood.

If the parental relationship was one of scarcity (love was hard to get), money becomes hard to get. If the parental relationship was one of criticism, money becomes something to prove worthiness through. If the parental relationship was one of absence, money becomes the thing that is never enough no matter how much is present.

"I had a relationship with my father early on where his love was not something that I could get easily. And so money had become this thing that I couldn't get easily. And the promise of money, like the promise of my father's love, was something that would make me whole again, or at least I thought it would." -- Joe Hudson, Money Can't Save You

The same internal experience exists whether the person has $1,000 or $1 billion. A billionaire drives around thinking "I don't have enough" with the same felt sense as someone with no savings. The amount is irrelevant. The relationship is the issue.

## What to Look For (Reads)

- Is money serving as a surrogate for something else? What did money represent in their family? What does having more money promise to fix?
- Are they in [[shoulds-vs-wants]]? "I should make more money" vs "what do I actually want that I believe money will provide?"
- Is [[fear-invites-what-we-fear]] operating? The fear of not having enough produces behaviors (over-spending, under-earning, risk-avoidance) that perpetuate not having enough
- Is [[inherent-goodness]] missing? If the person believes they need to earn the right to feel safe, money becomes the currency of self-worth

## Coaching Path

1. Separate the money from what it represents. Ask: "If money were not an issue, what would you want?" See [[what-do-you-want]]. This reveals what money is a surrogate for
2. Trace the money relationship to childhood: "What was money like in your family?" or "What did your parents' relationship with money look like?" See [[trauma-mapping]]
3. Identify the unfelt emotion: what feeling does more money promise to eliminate? Usually fear, helplessness, or unworthiness. See [[what-feeling-am-i-resisting]]
4. [[feel-before-acting]]: feel the fear of not having enough. Then make financial decisions from clarity, not from avoidance
5. Resources vs resourcefulness: the belief that money is required is itself a limiting belief. "The idea that you need money to do something is a limiting belief system. What did they need to start their business? Apparently nothing."
