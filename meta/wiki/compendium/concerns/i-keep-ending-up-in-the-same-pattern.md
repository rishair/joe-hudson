# I keep ending up in the same pattern

## What Someone Says

- "I keep ending up with the same kind of person"
- "Why does this always happen to me?"
- "I thought I was past this but here I am again"
- "I keep attracting people who criticize me / abandon me / control me"
- "Every relationship ends the same way"
- "I know what I should do differently but I can't seem to stop"

## What Joe Hears

Joe hears [[trauma-mapping]] at work. The person learned in childhood what love looks like (criticism, abandonment, control) and their system is now "manipulating, attracting, and proving" that reality. They are choosing what is familiar over what is safe. The pattern is not a choice in the usual sense. It is the organism seeking homeostasis by returning to unfelt emotions from the original attachment wound.

Joe does not hear a behavioral problem to be fixed. He hears unfelt emotions from childhood that are driving the cycle. The cycle will not stop through willpower or insight alone. It stops when the underlying emotions are fully felt. See [[feeling-unfelt-emotions]].

## What Joe Looks For (Reads)

- Is the person telling the story of what happened (recounting events) or are they in touch with how it feels? If they are in their story, Joe will need to move them toward feeling.
- Can the person name what the original wound is? Do they have awareness that this pattern connects to childhood, or do they think it is just bad luck?
- Is the person in grief about the pattern, or still fighting it? If grief, they may be close to the shift. See [[grief-as-gateway]].

## Coaching Path

1. Joe names the pattern: the person is "manipulating, attracting, and proving" a reality learned in childhood
2. Joe connects the present pattern to the childhood origin: "If a person has been taught that love is criticism, they go around attracting critical people, proving that love is criticism"
3. Joe identifies the unfelt emotion underneath: what could the child not feel? (aloneness, helplessness, being out of control)
4. Joe moves toward [[feeling-unfelt-emotions]]: the emotion must be "fully felt and loved" in a [[container-of-love]]
5. If the person has already done significant work and is regressing, this may be [[the-deepening]]
