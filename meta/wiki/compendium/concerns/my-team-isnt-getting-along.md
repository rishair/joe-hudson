# My team isn't getting along

## What Someone Says

- "My team can't get along"
- "We keep having the same conflicts and nothing gets resolved"
- "My direct reports aren't being productive together"
- "There's tension on the team and nobody addresses it"
- "We hit our goals individually but can't collaborate"

## What Joe Hears

Joe hears a communication problem rooted in state of mind, not skill. The team members are likely operating from fear, agenda, or knowing rather than [[view]]. They are not feeling heard, which produces resistance, stubbornness, and defensive behavior.

"Tell me a time that you were at your peak productivity and you felt unheard. Nobody can." -- Joe Hudson, Introduction to VIEW (Connection Course #1)

Joe also hears unexplored tension. The team is avoiding conflict rather than engaging with it. In Joe's framework, unaddressed tension is wasted data. See [[conflict-as-resource]].

## What Joe Looks For (Reads)

- Are team members trying to be right rather than be curious? See [[not-hearing-the-person-in-front-of-them]]
- Is there unexplored conflict being managed around rather than engaged with?
- Are people using communication as strategy rather than connection? See [[dont-use-view-as-technique]]

## Coaching Path

1. Joe teaches [[view]] as a state of mind, not a technique
2. Joe creates a [[container-of-love]] where team members can be vulnerable with each other
3. Joe facilitates VIEW conversations: 10-minute meetings using [[how-what-questions]] that produce more than hour-long agenda-driven meetings
4. Joe reframes [[conflict-as-resource]]: "conflict gives you better solutions than you had before it"
5. The team discovers that when they feel deeply connected, they perform at their best

Joe tells the story of a team with an ex-Navy SEAL, an MIT triple graduate, and other high-performers who were not getting along. Half a day into a 2-day VIEW workshop, "the people who hadn't been able to get along were crying with each other." The team hit their performance metrics that quarter after missing them for 3 years.
