# I had a breakthrough but now I feel lost

## What Someone Says

- "I had this big realization but now I feel worse"
- "I was in this amazing state and now I can't access it"
- "Things were clear and now everything is confusing"
- "I feel like I'm going backwards"
- "I don't know who I am anymore"
- "My old habits are coming back even though I saw through them"

## What's Underneath

This is not regression. It is [[integration]]. Transformation is not a straight line. The confusion, the feeling of going backwards, the loss of the "high" are all normal stages of integrating a shift. The old patterns show up not because the work failed but because the next layer needs to be loved.

"As you're moving forward, it isn't a straight line. And so what you think to be moving backwards might just be the way that humans learn. Kids go from walking to crawling." -- Joe Hudson, Integrating a Transformative Experience

The epiphany itself is "dead almost as soon as it arrives." The freedom was real, but attaching to the epiphany turns it into the next rut. The value was the moment of lifting away, not the new belief. See [[epiphany-vs-transformation]].

## What to Look For (Reads)

- Is the person resisting what is arising? The discomfort comes from resistance, not from the transformation itself. "Almost all of what can go wrong is resistance to what is."
- Is the person trying to get back to the "high"? Confusing the elation of the epiphany with the skill that was gained. Like an infant confusing the excitement of first walking with walking itself.
- Is the person scared of the new sensitivity? Transformation often increases sensitivity because repression has lifted. The temptation is to shut that down.
- Has the person's social environment pushed back? See [[the-three-to-five-rule]]: expect 3-5 pushbacks before a new boundary holds.

## Coaching Path

1. Normalize: this is common. This is how transformation works. It passes.
2. Reframe "going backwards" as the next thing showing up to be loved. "The next thing that's been rejected shows up to be loved. And we don't see it as showing up to be loved. We see it as showing up and fucking with us."
3. Encourage the sensitivity rather than blocking it
4. Do not chase the epiphany. "After a good integration, you have less answers, not new answers."
5. The question to hold: do I have more emotional fluidity? Do patterns have less grip? Am I taking things less personally? These are the real markers, not whether the "high" returned.
