# Connecting Present Pattern to Childhood in the Body

## Definition

A coaching move that connects a current pattern to its childhood origin not through analysis but through felt sensation. The coach names the childhood parallel, then asks the person to feel it in their body, then asks how similar that body sensation is to what they feel in their current pattern.

## How It Works

1. The present pattern is identified (e.g., hyper-focusing and losing balance)
2. The coach names a childhood parallel (e.g., diverging from family's religion)
3. The instruction: "Stop talking about it. Just feel that. Feel that place you felt when [childhood moment]."
4. The person locates the sensation in their body
5. The question: "How similar is that feeling to [current pattern]?"
6. The person recognizes: they are the same feeling. The current pattern IS the childhood pattern, replayed somatically

In the coaching session: Brett's pattern of hyper-focusing on new interests was connected to his childhood experience of diverging from his family's religion. The anxious tension in his chest was the same body sensation in both. The current pattern was the childhood trauma, still running.

## What Read Triggers This

- When [[trauma-mapping]] is suspected but the person has not seen the childhood connection
- When the person is intellectualizing the pattern (analyzing it rather than feeling it)
- When a specific childhood parallel is visible to the coach

## What It Produces

Recognition that is felt, not thought. The person does not just understand the connection intellectually; they feel that the body sensation is identical. This bypasses the intellect's defenses and lands in the body where the pattern lives.

## Tone

Direct but warm. The instruction "stop talking about it, just feel it" is blunt. The follow-up "how similar?" is curious.
