# Get 30 Rejections

## Definition

A practice for shifting from a perfection mindset (trying to succeed) to a connection mindset (just engaging). Instead of trying to get accepted or approved, set the goal as getting a specific number of rejections. This inverts the fear and removes the perfectionism from the interaction.

## In Practice

Joe assigns this to artists, salespeople, and anyone stuck trying to get approval. The instructions: go get 30 (or 50) rejections. That is the goal. Not success. Rejection.

Joe gave this to an artist who was struggling to get gallery representation. The artist's job was to get 30 rejections in a year. Within 10 calls, the artist had 3 gallery representations and had sold 12 pieces. The perfection pressure was gone, so the artist could just be himself, which is what the galleries wanted.

"As soon as he could drop the partiality of trying to convince them, then he was successful at being himself, which is what they wanted." -- Joe Hudson, Connection over Perfection

## Why It Works

The goal of rejection eliminates the fear of rejection. Without fear of rejection, the person shows up authentically rather than performing. Authenticity produces connection. Connection produces results. See [[fear-invites-what-we-fear]]: the fear of rejection was creating the very behavior that produced rejection.

## How to Practice

1. Set a specific number of rejections as your goal (30, 50)
2. Set a timeframe (3 months, 6 months, a year)
3. Each interaction is a success if it produces a rejection. Acceptance is a bonus
4. Track rejections, not acceptances
5. Notice how the interactions change when the fear of rejection is removed

## When Someone Experiences This

When [[connection-over-perfection]] is the issue and the person needs a concrete entry point. When sales, dating, job searching, or creative submission is paralyzed by fear of rejection. When the person "knows" they should put themselves out there but cannot.
