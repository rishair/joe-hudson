# How and What Questions

## Definition

The only technique Joe layers on top of the [[view]] state of mind. Ask how and what questions instead of closed or why questions. These are open-ended questions that emerge naturally from wonder and impartiality.

## How Joe Explains It

"The entirety of the technique is to ask how and what questions." -- Joe Hudson, Introduction to VIEW

Joe gives the comparison: "Do you like ice cream?" produces yes or no. "How do you feel about ice cream?" produces stories, memories, preferences, information you could not predict. Open questions give access to more data, which shows that you are in [[wonder]].

## Why not "why" questions

Joe avoids "why" questions for two reasons:

1. Most "why" questions carry judgment. "Why'd you do that?" is not curiosity. It is "you should feel bad and I want you to make an excuse."
2. "Why" questions are the hardest to answer. "Why is the sun?" is unanswerable. "How did the sun get there?" is answerable.

"Why questions are the questions that scientists can't answer." -- Joe Hudson, Introduction to VIEW

## How to Practice

Joe reframes existing questions into how/what form. This forces the brain to approach the topic differently. "How and what questions make you reframe questions in a new way, which triggers your brain."

Even aggressive content can work in VIEW if the state of mind is right. Joe gives the example: "What's making you such a dick right now?" asked with genuine curiosity, warmth, and without agenda, often produces laughter and honest sharing.

## When Someone Experiences This

When your conversations feel like interrogations. When you ask questions and get one-word answers. When you notice you are asking questions to confirm what you already believe rather than to discover.

## Upstream (What Leads Here)

- [[view]] as the technique layer
- [[wonder]] as the state from which these questions naturally arise
- When someone wants a concrete practice to start with

## Downstream (Where Joe Goes From Here)

- The responses to how/what questions often reveal material for deeper coaching
- People feel heard, which reduces defensiveness and enables [[vulnerability]]
