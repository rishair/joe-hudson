# Feel Before Acting

## Definition

A practice for making clear decisions and having productive conversations: feel the avoided emotion first, then take the action. Not after. Before.

"The reason that somebody gets angry at somebody else is because they haven't gotten angry by themselves. Go release your anger and then talk to the person. Go mourn and then talk to your wife about what you need from the relationship. Go get really, really scared and then talk to your boss about the raise that you want. You have the emotional experience and then go take the action." -- Joe Hudson, Feel over Figure

## Why it works

Actions taken while avoiding an emotion are distorted by the avoidance. If you talk to your spouse about your needs while avoiding the grief of potentially losing the relationship, your words will be constrained by that avoidance. You will not say what is true. You will say what keeps the feared emotion at bay.

Once the emotion has been felt, the action comes from a different place: clarity rather than avoidance. What you want becomes visible because you are no longer organized around what you are afraid to feel.

## The marriage example

When couples are stuck, the coaching path is: go mourn the marriage first. Assume it is over. Feel all the grief of being left. Feel 20 years down the tubes. Feel the kids not having two parents. Feel all of it.

After that, speak your truth. 80% of the time, the partner receives it and the relationship deepens. 20% of the time, they leave. Either way, the action comes from clarity, not from fear.

## How to practice

1. Notice indecision, looping thoughts, or avoidance of a conversation (see [[three-signals-of-unfelt-emotion]])
2. Ask: what emotion am I avoiding by not taking this action?
3. Feel that emotion fully, by yourself. Not at anyone. Privately. Let it move
4. After the emotion has moved, take the action
5. The action will be different than what fear would have produced

## When Someone Experiences This

When there is a conversation that keeps getting postponed. When a decision has been circling for weeks. When you know what you need to do but cannot make yourself do it. The block is not about the action. It is about the emotion the action will produce.
