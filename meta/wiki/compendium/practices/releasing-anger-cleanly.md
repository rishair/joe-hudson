# Releasing Anger Cleanly

## The Core Principle

Some part of you must remain outside the process while the anger moves. Both things must be happening: the anger is moving AND there is awareness watching it move. Without the awareness, dysregulation. Without the anger, repression.

"If you're following the mind, it's very hard to allow anger to move cleanly. Whether your story is 'they're absolutely wrong and they deserve this,' or your story is 'no, they deserve compassion,' any kind of thick story around it is going to really make the anger have a hard time coming out in a clean way." -- Joe Hudson, The Wisdom of Anger Part II

## Mental Blocks That Appear

The mind will try to stop the anger before it moves:
- **Premature compassion.** "They have their reasons. I should be understanding." Compassion comes naturally after the anger moves. If it arrives before, it is suppression.
- **Moral judgment.** "Anger is bad. Good people don't get angry." Religious or ethical framing that tabs certain emotions as forbidden.
- **Self-judgment.** "If I've lost control and I'm angry, I'm ignorant."
- **Story of stuckness.** "I can't express this because of the consequences." Stuck means: I don't want the imagined consequences. Name the real fear.

Response to any of these: "Hey, I've listened to you for a decade. I'm just going to listen to anger for 20 minutes."

## What Goes Wrong During Release

### Collapse

Mid-release, shame hits. The back bends, energy drops, the person folds inward. Common for people whose repression is self-abusive or passive-aggressive.

**What to do:** Use will, the same way you would in a workout. Find a phrase that responds to the oppression of the collapse: "You will not stop me. You will not stop me from being angry." Or simply: "I exist." The phrase fights the early childhood message ("No, you can't be angry") with determination.

### Dysregulation

The person buys into the story completely and loses themselves. They leave their body. Time and space blur. They say things they don't mean. This is not clean anger. It is another form of repression.

**What to do:** Use the actor technique. Play the role of yourself getting angry. Give a good performance, but know you are performing. This keeps the observer online. For people prone to dysregulation, the observer position prevents the story from taking over.

Conversely, for people who freeze: get into the story a bit. Believe it enough to access the anger. Then listen to the body. The actor technique works both ways: frozen types need to commit to the role; explosive types need to see themselves as an actor.

### Shame Recreation

The person releases anger in a way that hurts someone or breaks something, then uses that as proof that anger is dangerous. The shame cycle is recreated.

**What to do:** Awareness is sufficient. Just notice: "One of the things I might do is recreate the cycle of shame around this anger." If turned into a should ("I should not break things"), it takes longer. Awareness without shoulding resolves it.

## The Clarity Test

Clean anger release ends in clarity and determination. The test:
- **Visceral relief.** The tension dissolves. No more fight.
- **Clarity is about you.** "I realize I don't want to be around them" = clear. "I'm clear, fuck them" = not clear. Real clarity points toward your own action, not what others should do.
- **No recycling.** If anger keeps coming back every few days without producing clarity, the anger is covering something underneath: fear, hurt, helplessness, grief. Switch to those emotions.

"At the end of an anger movement, you're gonna get clarity and determination. If you don't get there, it means anger isn't the proper emotion or you got completely lost in the story." -- Joe Hudson, The Wisdom of Anger Part II

## Practical Techniques

- **Angry music.** Tool, or whatever resonates. Angry music that also speaks truth.
- **Fake it.** Just pretend. "If you were playing a 5-year-old, what would you say?" The fake anger often triggers real anger.
- **Physical release.** Voice, body, movement. Best results come when voice and body are both involved.
- **Breathwork.** Breathe in a way that builds intensity. "Breathe like the Hulk."
- **Tell the body it is safe.** Literally say: "I'm not going to be abused here. Nobody is going to attack me. I'm not going to destroy anybody." Simple, and it works.
- **Deconstruct the victim.** If the victim story ("I'm stuck, I can't do anything") is holding the anger down, challenge it directly. The victim, questioned, gets angry. That anger is real. See the move: challenge the story that keeps the anger trapped.

## Ask Compassion to Be Compassionate to Anger

When the system defaults to compassion to avoid anger: "If compassion's really being compassionate, it's accepting of everything including the anger." Compassion that excludes anger is selective. Real compassion makes room for all emotions.
