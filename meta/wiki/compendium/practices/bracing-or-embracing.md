# Bracing or Embracing?

## The Practice

Close your eyes. Take a deep breath. Ask yourself: right now, am I bracing or am I embracing?

That is the entire practice. No analysis required. The body knows the answer immediately. Bracing is contraction, tightening, holding against. Embracing is opening, allowing, welcoming what is present.

"I asked myself that question for — there was a time when I asked myself that question like 10 times a day. It was this beautiful question that I just loved asking myself." -- Embracing Intensity

## Why This Works

The question bypasses intellectual understanding and goes straight to the felt sense. It does not ask what emotion you are feeling, what is causing the tension, or what you should do about it. It asks a single binary: is the system opening or closing?

This makes it usable anywhere -- in meetings, in conversations, in moments of procrastination, in moments of joy. The answer itself is the shift. Noticing bracing often softens it.

## When to Use It

- As a daily check-in, multiple times per day
- When a conversation feels stuck or tense
- When procrastination or avoidance appears
- When something good happens and there is an impulse to deflect or minimize it
- When facing a decision and the body is tight

## What It Produces

Increased awareness of the baseline state of the system. Over time, the default shifts from unconscious bracing to conscious embracing. The practice builds the muscle of [[embrace-intensity]] without requiring intense emotional work each time.
