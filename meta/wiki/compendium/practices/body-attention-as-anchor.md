# Body Attention as Anchor

## Definition

Joe's specific practice for maintaining empathy without losing yourself in the other person. Put some of your attention in your own physical body while being with someone. This simple act prevents the dissolution of self that highly empathetic people experience.

## How Joe Explains It

"The easiest way to do it is just put some attention in your own body while you're with somebody. So like right now, when you're listening to me, you could also be paying attention to the bottom of your feet, or you could also be paying attention to how the sound of this podcast feels in your ear, in your inner ear. And then that allows you to be with yourself while listening to me and being with me in my experience." -- Joe Hudson, Empathy (Connection Course #4)

Joe contrasts this with the traditional approach of building defenses against feeling: "The kind of the traditional way people do it is they become defensive. They just hold a level of defense. And that works, but it doesn't allow you to be empathetic. It just prevents you from getting lost in them."

## How to Practice

Joe recommends practicing for one week: put some attention on your own physical body during every conversation. Feel the bottom of your feet. Notice your breath. Feel where you sit in the chair.

"For the rest of the week, put some of your attention into your physical body during every conversation. See what that does to your world. It'll rock your world if you do that for every conversation for a week." -- Joe Hudson, Empathy (Connection Course #4)

## When Someone Experiences This

When you lose yourself in other people's emotions. When you are drained after interactions. When you carry other people's emotional residue for hours or days. When you are an "empath" who avoids high-energy situations because they are overwhelming.

## Upstream (What Leads Here)

- When [[empathy]] is being practiced and the person reports losing themselves
- When someone identifies as an "empath" and sees it as a burden
- After asking [[is-this-mine]] reveals that much of what they feel is not theirs

## Downstream (Where Joe Goes From Here)

- With body attention in place, [[empathy]] becomes sustainable rather than depleting
- The person can stay present in difficult emotional situations (children's tantrums, team conflicts)
- Opens the door to reading emotional reality rather than managing it
