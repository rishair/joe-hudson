# Unfelt Joy

## Definition

Much of what presents as anxiety is actually constricted joy. The life force being squeezed is not just negative emotions trying to emerge. It is exuberance, pleasure, laughter, and love that have been trained out of the system.

Joy is the emotion most suppressed by society. Crying in a cafe is uncomfortable. Getting angry is uncomfortable. But laughing out of pure bliss and joy in a cafe full of strangers is the most uncomfortable of all.

## How Joy Gets Squashed

Children are told to calm down constantly. "Hey Johnny, calm down." Rampant exuberance is treated as a problem. The message: your full joy is too much for the world to handle.

In adulthood, accepting compliments becomes difficult. Orgasms are short. Laughter is cut off mid-release. The internal narrative: "If I let this in, the other shoe will drop. If I feel good, I'll get hurt. If I let the love in, that person can use it against me."

## Pleasure Anxiety

The fear of feeling good. Indicators:

- How well do you accept compliments? Do you deflect, minimize, or explain them away?
- Do you stop laughing before the laughter finishes naturally?
- When something great happens, how quickly does "but what if..." arrive?
- After a breakthrough or release, how quickly does the system pull back to baseline?

"You'll see people oftentimes like laugh because they get it in a session and then they'll stop laughing. And they'll stop the laughing because they have this like, oh shit, I'm not allowed to feel good." -- Joe Hudson, Anxiety: A Signpost to Unmet Needs

## Joy and Authenticity

Suppressing joy produces shame. The more inauthentic someone is (including suppressing their joy, laughter, or exuberance), the more shame accumulates in the system. The system knows when it is not being itself.

"I notice that the more that I'm not authentic, the more shame I have in my life. I don't know why that mechanism happens, but it does seem to be the case." -- Joe Hudson, Anxiety: A Signpost to Unmet Needs

## Connection to the Emotional Development Framework

In [[stages-of-emotional-development]], joy is described as the matriarch who will not enter a house where her children (fear, anger, sadness) are not welcome. Unfelt joy adds the reverse angle: even when the other emotions are being felt, joy itself may be the one being resisted. The constriction of life force often IS the constriction of joy.
