# Container of Love

## Definition

The environment Joe creates for emotional processing, whether in one-on-one coaching, group work, or week-long intensives. The "container of love" is the condition under which [[feeling-unfelt-emotions]] becomes possible. Joe uses this specific phrase to describe what must be present for a person to safely access emotions they suppressed as a child.

## How Joe Explains It

Joe frames healing as requiring both the emotion and the environment. The emotion alone, felt in isolation or hostility, does not heal. The emotion felt in a container of love does.

"If they get to a point where they can look forward to that emotion, that underlying emotion, and actually invite it and feel it and process it in a container, particularly in a container of love, then that's the moment they stop attracting or going towards the same trauma in their life." -- Joe Hudson, A Discussion with Dr. Bessel van der Kolk & Joe Hudson

Joe holds that trauma created in relationship is best healed in relationship. He describes two forms: one-on-one work and group work.

In group settings, the container operates through witnessed vulnerability. Joe describes week-long courses where participants list their critical inner voice publicly: "When people excavate all this stuff and then they look around the room and they see 11 other people loving them despite of this, that that's no excuse not to love yourself. It does something for the human." -- Joe Hudson, A Discussion with Dr. Bessel van der Kolk & Joe Hudson

## When Someone Experiences This

When you have identified your pattern and the unfelt emotion but cannot access it alone. When shame prevents you from feeling the emotion because you need witnesses who still love you after seeing the worst of you. When you know what you need to feel but it feels too dangerous without someone holding the space.

## Upstream (What Leads Here)

- After [[trauma-mapping]] has been named and the unfelt emotion identified
- When shame is blocking access to the emotion (the person needs to be seen and still loved)
- When the person has tried to process alone and cannot get to the feeling

## Downstream (Where Joe Goes From Here)

- Within the container, Joe guides toward [[feeling-unfelt-emotions]]
- Group containers enable [[learning-to-love-all-parts]] through witnessed vulnerability
- The container enables the deep work that can trigger [[the-deepening]] in long-term participants

## Boundary Conditions

Van der Kolk pushes back: body-based work (Rolfing, yoga) can heal without a relational container. Joe acknowledges this, framing it as "very helpful" rather than the only path. The concept applies most strongly to attachment wounds and shame-based patterns.
