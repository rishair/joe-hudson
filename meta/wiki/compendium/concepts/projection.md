# Projection

## Definition

The world you see is not the world as it is. It is the world as you are. There are four layers of projection, each building on the last:

1. **Psychological projection.** The parts of yourself you cannot own (good or bad) get assigned to other people. If someone is "so arrogant," that judgment is itself arrogance: presuming to know their internal reality. If someone is "so brilliant" and you cannot see that quality in yourself, you are projecting your own unacknowledged brilliance. This is why [[reflecting-the-judgment-back]] works: the judgment of the other IS the disowned part of the self.

2. **World projection.** The assumptions about how life works that were learned in the first 8 years (theta brainwaves). If love was associated with criticism, every romantic partner will somehow criticize. If money was associated with scarcity, money will always be scarce. This is [[trauma-mapping]] framed as projection: the childhood learning becomes the lens through which all of reality is interpreted.

3. **Self projection.** How you relate to yourself is how you relate to the world. If crying is weakness for you, it is weakness in others. If self-love is your orientation, atrocities look like failed attempts at love. The inner relationship IS the outer relationship. "The relationship with the self is reflected in the relationship with the people around us." -- Joe Hudson, Allowing Yourself to Change

4. **Identity projection.** The assumption that there is a separate "I" that must be protected. This is the deepest layer and connects to the spiritual dimension of the work.

## In Practice

Projection is not a flaw to fix. It is how perception works. The coaching value is in recognizing projections so they stop being invisible filters.

People who see through their projections appear innovative or creative. They are not smarter. They simply see fewer limitations because they have fewer unexamined assumptions.

"People think somebody who really sees through projections is really smart. It's not so much that they're smart, it's that they don't see the same level of limitation on everything that somebody who fully buys into their projections sees." -- Joe Hudson, Your World is a Projection

## When Someone Experiences This

When you keep encountering the same type of person or situation. When everyone seems to be a certain way. When your judgments of others are persistent and charged. When the world seems hostile, or when the world seems to conspire against you in familiar ways. These are all projections operating.
