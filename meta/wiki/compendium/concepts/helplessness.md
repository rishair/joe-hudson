# Helplessness

## Definition

Helplessness is the deepest form of fear. It is the feeling that absolutely nothing can be done. It arises during traumatic experiences: rape, car accidents, a parent who will not love no matter what the child does. It is the emotion that was most impossible to feel in the moment it occurred.

It is also one of the most freeing emotions to feel. Like all emotions, it produces its opposite when allowed: feeling helplessness leads to feeling potent, viable, empowered.

## The Confusion

People conflate being scared to feel helplessness with helplessness itself. Because the emotion is frightening, they believe the fear of feeling it IS the emotion. This makes helplessness one of the most challenging emotions to actually reach.

"There's this weird little nuance that happens when people feel fear or helplessness, is that they're scared and therefore they conflate being scared to feel the feeling with the feeling itself." -- Joe Hudson, Fear: A Path to Authenticity

## What It Sounds Like

In the body: screaming, tight constriction, or sometimes silence. Not being able to say anything. Feeling frozen and stuck. The sounds of helplessness can range from wailing to complete muteness.

## When Someone Experiences This

When there is a situation that genuinely cannot be controlled and the person is fighting for control anyway. When all the fixing, managing, and problem-solving strategies have been exhausted and the only thing left is to feel what could not be felt as a child. Often the deepest layer in [[the-developmental-arc]]: after the pattern is seen, the childhood origin is found, and the unfelt emotion is approached, what remains at the bottom is often helplessness.
