# The Voice in the Head

## Definition

The repetitive, critical, bossy narrator running in the background of consciousness. It tells you what to do, what you did wrong, what others think of you, and what you should be worried about. It repeats the same messages thousands of times. It is not the same as unique, creative, or inspired thinking. It is the loop.

"When you have this deep critical voice in your head, it's like you're living with a horrible micromanaging boss all the time. And we know what that's like if we're actually sitting next to one of those people and they're constantly barraging us. And yet we just think it's normal when it's coming from ourselves." -- Joe Hudson, Who is the Voice in Your Head?

## How it operates

The voice has two roles (from Gestalt psychology): the **upper dog** (the bully, the should-er, the critic) and the **underdog** (the quiet rebel that resists but never wins). Most people are aware of the upper dog. The underdog takes years to notice.

The voice creates the reality it is trying to avoid. The perfectionist voice makes perfection impossible (too anxious to perform). The "you should lose weight" voice makes weight loss harder (see [[shame-locks-bad-habits]]). The rehearsal voice makes conversations worse (too prepared to be present).

## Forms it takes

Not always obviously critical. Sometimes it shows up as:
- Shoulding: "you should do X, you should have done Y"
- Rehearsing: replaying past conversations or preparing future ones
- Worrying: running scenarios about what might go wrong
- Wondering what others think: incessant speculation about others' opinions
- Perfectionism: demanding everything be done to an impossible standard
- Checking: "did you do this? what about this? have you forgotten something?"

The somatic companion is always present: tension, constriction, anxiety. The voice may be quiet but the body signal is constant.

## How to work with it

The approach is NOT to fight it, silence it, or replace it with positive thoughts. Using the voice to fight the voice is "like asking a thief to be the security of your house."

Instead, experiment. Play with it:
- Notice it without engaging: "oh, there it is again"
- Love it: "I see you care that I do a good job"
- Ask it to use better management techniques
- Laugh at it
- Turn [[wonder]] on it: "what is this thing constantly managing me? What makes it think it's good at its job if it's still having to manage me over the same shit 10 years later?" (from [[i-cant-stop-criticizing-myself]])
- Get in a massive fight with it and see what happens when you are exhausted

Simple awareness, without resistance, reduces the voice over time. The awareness itself is the change agent. Just like writing down everything you do not want to admit about yourself changes most items through awareness alone (see [[shoulds-vs-wants]]).

## When Someone Experiences This

When internal narration is constant and critical. When self-talk would be unacceptable if spoken by another person. When the same thoughts repeat daily, weekly, for years. When rehearsing conversations or worrying about others' opinions consumes significant attention. See [[i-cant-stop-criticizing-myself]].
