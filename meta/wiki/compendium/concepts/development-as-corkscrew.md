# Development as Corkscrew

## Definition

Development moves like a corkscrew, not a straight line. The same core issues (authority, abandonment, control, shame) return at every turn of the spiral. Each return is at a more subtle level. What looks like regression is actually refinement.

"I thought I dealt with all my dad issues" is a common statement. The response: you did deal with them, at that level of sensitivity. As capacity for awareness increases, more subtle versions of the same pattern become visible and workable.

## In Practice

Increased sensitivity is a feature, not a bug. A novice musician does not notice a slightly out-of-tune instrument. An expert cannot ignore it. Similarly, someone early in their development does not notice subtle forms of their patterns. Someone deeper in notices tension that would have been invisible before.

"Development moves like a corkscrew. Every time you're at the bottom of that corkscrew, it's daddy issues, and every time you're at the top, it's mommy issues. But you have these core things going through your life and you're refining them, and they're becoming more and more subtle." -- Joe Hudson, Embarking on the Journey

## Pendulation

Learning goes back and forth. A baby does not crawl, then walk, then never crawl again. Development pendulates between the new capacity and the old pattern. This back-and-forth is necessary. Without it, the learning does not integrate properly.

"What they know is that when that back and forth doesn't happen, like particularly in babies crawling, that their brain develops differently. If you pull a butterfly out of the cocoon, it won't be able to fly." -- Joe Hudson, Embarking on the Journey

## When Someone Experiences This

When you thought you were past something and it showed up again. When old patterns return after a breakthrough. When you feel like you are going backwards. See [[i-had-a-breakthrough-but-feel-lost]]. This is not regression. This is the next turn of the corkscrew, the next level of subtlety becoming visible.

## Upstream (What Leads Here)

- When [[i-had-a-breakthrough-but-feel-lost]]: the person interprets the corkscrew as failure
- When old patterns return and the person feels discouraged
- When increased sensitivity is interpreted as "getting worse" rather than "seeing more"

## Downstream (Where the Coaching Path Goes)

- Normalize the return of familiar patterns at a new level
- [[enjoy-what-youre-doing]]: enjoy the corkscrew rather than fighting it
- [[love-the-resistance]]: meet the returning pattern with love rather than frustration
- Appreciation of where you are prevents clinging to where you were
