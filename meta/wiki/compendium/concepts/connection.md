# Connection

## Definition

Connection is being in touch with what is in the present. It leads to expansion, more energy, and contact with something bigger than the self.

Connection is not a particular emotional state. It is a meta-state that can exist within any state. You can be connected while sad, angry, crying, having sex, or working at a computer. You can be out of connection during all of those too. The distinction is not what you are feeling but whether you are in contact with what you are actually experiencing.

"You being in connection with yourself and others is not dependent on anybody else, because being in connection with what is doesn't require anybody else." -- Joe Hudson, Connection: A State Beyond States

## Connection as the Bottom Line

Connection is the most effective principle for organizing a life. When connection is the bottom line, everything else improves: effectiveness, relationships, health, longevity, joy.

The Harvard Grant Study (the longest-running study in the US) found that quality of relationships is the number one predictor of happiness, health, and long life. Not wealth, status, or achievement. The quality of relationships, which is a result of connection.

"How much would you sell your ability to connect for? Half of it. How much would you charge me?" The question bends the brain because the answer is: no amount. With half the ability to connect and infinite money, life is still worse. Connection is the most priceless asset.

## Connection, Connection, Connection

The principle is stated three times because:
1. **Start** with connection
2. **Stay focused** on connection
3. **End** in connection

When things are not going well with customers, focus on connection. When things are not going well in a marriage, focus on connection. When things are not going well internally, focus on connection. This is the heuristic: "Am I optimizing for being right or for connection? Am I trying to convince someone or connect with them? Am I trying to change myself or be in connection with myself?"

## How to Get Into Connection

VIEW works as a set of hacks: [[vulnerability]] creates connection, [[impartiality]] creates connection, [[empathy]] creates connection, [[wonder]] creates connection. See [[view]].

Beyond techniques, there is also just noticing. Connection is always present. There has never been a moment of genuine disconnection from existence. Every thought, every breath, every experience is already connected to the larger fabric of life.

Two questions that converge: "How would I want to be treated in this situation?" and "What is my deepest truth right now?" Mine both. They arrive at the same place.

## Connection Deepens Through Tension

The cycle: tension arises → embrace the difficulty → work through it → return to love and safety → repeat. Each cycle deepens the connection. This is visible in soldiers who have shared adversity, teams who have overcome crises together, and couples who have navigated conflict.

The same cycle operates internally. Embrace the friction between different parts of the self (fear, anger, greed). Be in the tension. Get back to self-love. The internal connection deepens through the same process as external connection. See [[embrace-intensity]].

The tension does not need to be created. It will arise on its own. A symphony without tension is boring. A team without tension is stagnant. The practice is being with tension in a connected way, not eliminating it.

## What Breaks Connection

Putting connection in the future. "I'll be connected when I quit smoking. When I have money. When someone loves me." This is a carrot on a stick. Connection is available now or not at all.

"You're there right now." -- Joe Hudson, Connection: A State Beyond States

## No One Can Break Your Connection

Others can leave, do hurtful things, cut off contact. They cannot break the connection unless the person agrees to it. The choice to stay in love and connection is internal, not dependent on the other person's response. See [[shame-as-disconnection]] for what it feels like when connection appears to break.

This does not mean staying in harmful situations. Boundaries can be drawn while maintaining love. "I'm not gonna stop loving you. I'm gonna stop doing business with you." See [[boundaries]].
