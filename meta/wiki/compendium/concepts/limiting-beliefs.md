# Limiting Beliefs

## Definition

A limiting belief is an idea that limits the capacity to be yourself, get what you want, change habits, or be happy. Beliefs affect everything. Limiting beliefs, by definition, constrain the range of possible action, vision, and joy.

"It's about being able to integrate new knowledge, and if you can't integrate new knowledge because you think you have the whole story, then you're limited, period." -- Joe Hudson, Limiting Beliefs

## Three Types

### False Thoughts

The mind believes something factually untrue. Joe's father was CEO of a company that was shut down by its parent. He believed he had failed. Years later, a chance encounter revealed the company had been shut down because his work was so successful it threatened the parent company's main product. The parent was sued, and the company was rebooted. The father had spent 15-20 years defining himself by a lie.

Even if the failure were real, the limiting belief would be in the interpretation: "failure" vs "learning experience." The falseness is in taking one frame as the only frame.

### Misinterpretation of Experience

The same experience, interpreted differently, produces opposite effects. On a spinning ride at a boardwalk: frozen with terror, body shaking, all tension. Then hearing someone laugh on the adjacent car. Started laughing. Suddenly the same ride was exhilarating. Same body, same forces, same experience. The interpretation shifted from "this must be resisted" to "this is exciting." The body's chemical response changed from carcinogenic to anti-carcinogenic.

This is the emotional lie, as distinct from the head lie. "Exercise is difficult." "I'll be happy when I find the right partner." "No one wants my anger." All misinterpretations of experience that limit capacity.

### Thinking You Know the Whole Story

A man on a subway watches a father ignore his misbehaving children. Agitation builds. Then the father says: "I'm sorry, they lost their mom just the other day." Instantaneous shift. Same events, completely different understanding. The limitation was in thinking the whole story was known.

Every perspective is like a photograph of a building from one angle. Useful, true from that angle, incomplete. Integration of perspectives produces better solutions. Thinking you have the complete truth stops integration.

## How to Find Them

**Signals in the body:** Physical constriction, tension, stress, being triggered. These are where limiting beliefs live.

**Signals in the mind:** Binary thinking (indicates fear, indicates limited thinking). Feeling trapped (indicates false belief). Life feels like a chore or happiness is located in the future (indicates misinterpretation). Stagnation and "that's just how it's done" (indicates incomplete story).

**The trick:** The limiting belief is usually the thing you absolutely believe to be true. It is the water you swim in. Look for the context you are not questioning.

## How to See Through Them

Three levels of tools, matching the three types:

**Inquiry (head):** Question what you believe. Argue the counter case in writing. List everything you don't know about the situation. For blame-based beliefs, Byron Katie's inquiry process.

**Emotional fluidity (heart):** Allow the emotion underneath the belief to move. The belief often dissolves when the emotion it was protecting against is fully felt. See [[feeling-unfelt-emotions]].

**Allowing pleasure, love, safety (nervous system):** The deepest beliefs live in the nervous system. These shift through experienced safety and love, not through understanding.

## The Coaching Questions

- "What do you get out of this belief? What's the benefit?"
- "What would be scary about letting it go?"
- "Is there something about this situation you don't understand?"
- "What are the things you absolutely believe to be true here?" (These are the culprits)

## The Meta-Trap

"Once I see through all my limiting beliefs, I'll be happy" is itself the most common limiting belief. "If I do X, then I'll be happy. Then I'll be good enough. Then I'll be worthy of love." This is the foundational societal limiting belief, and it causes the most misery. See [[inherent-goodness]].

## Upstream (What Leads Here)

- When someone feels stuck and cognitive understanding has not changed the behavior
- When binary thinking or "I have no options" appears
- When someone describes their identity in fixed terms ("I'm the kind of person who...")
- When [[projection]] is present: what you believe about others reflects beliefs about yourself
