# Three Ways of Knowing

## Definition

Three distinct intelligences operate simultaneously: head, heart, and gut (body). Each captures a different angle of truth, like photographs taken from different positions. None captures the whole truth. All three are needed.

"If I take a picture of you and then I walk 2 feet to the right and take another picture of you, both of those pictures are true. They're both lies because they're going to capture different things. They're not gonna be the entirety of the truth." -- Joe Hudson, How Your Body Speaks

## The Three Intelligences

**Head.** Facts, logic, analysis. Default for most Americans. Produces clarity about structures and patterns but can create analysis paralysis and endless debate. Tends toward binary (right/wrong, agree/disagree). People operating primarily from head may not recognize their emotions are driving them.

**Heart.** Connection, gratitude, care. Less binary than the head. The idea of judging something right or wrong is far less important to the heart. When listening from the heart, there is less of a yes/no quality and more openness. People operating primarily from heart produce warmth and connection.

**Gut (Body).** Instinct, felt sense, physical knowing. How you actually feel right now. Nervous, scared, hungry, excited. People operating primarily from gut can be very successful but may believe their stories uncritically because the gut feeling is so strong. "The body lies just as much as the mind."

## Speaking and Listening From Each

Both speaking and listening shift depending on which intelligence is foregrounded.

**Experiment:** Introduce yourself from your head (facts and data), from your heart (who you are as a person), and from your gut (how you actually feel right now). Three different introductions emerge from the same person.

Listening also changes: listening from the head evaluates (good idea or bad idea?). Listening from the heart receives without evaluating. Listening from the gut notices what the body feels in response.

## Each Intelligence Has Blind Spots

Head-dominant people do not recognize emotions driving their decisions. They debate "objectively" while angry, without seeing the anger. Gut-dominant people believe their stories uncritically. If the gut says "I've been wronged," they build a story around it without questioning it. Heart-dominant people can lose themselves in others' emotions.

The remedy is not switching to a "better" intelligence. It is cultivating all three so they can inform each other. A natural step on the learning path is to discover a new intelligence and treat it as the "real truth." The body becomes God's direct communication. But the body can get confused too. The heart can get confused too. Only all three together provide full clarity.

## Being In Your Body

The simplest definition: put part of your attention into your body. While talking, while listening, while working. This simple act produces:

- Less likelihood of losing yourself in a situation
- More awareness of emotional and nervous system state
- Faster access to flow state (which is why athletes and musicians do body exercises before performing)
- Greater sensitivity to others' states over time
