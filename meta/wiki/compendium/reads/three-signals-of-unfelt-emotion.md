# Three Signals of Unfelt Emotion

## What to Notice

Three observable signals that an emotion is not being felt:

1. **Looping thoughts.** The same thought keeps returning. The mind circles the same idea, argument, or worry repeatedly. This means an emotional reality underneath the thought has not been acknowledged or felt. Once the emotion is felt, the loop breaks.

2. **Unclear decisions.** The inability to decide is not an information problem. It is an emotional problem. Some emotion associated with one or more options is being avoided. Once the avoided emotion is felt, the decision becomes clear.

3. **Judgment of others.** Judging someone else is a way to suppress an emotion. Judging someone for being angry means not wanting to feel out of control or not wanting to feel the potential loss of them. Judging someone for being uptight means not wanting to feel controlled or rigid. Every judgment points to an unfelt emotion.

A fourth signal: **binary thinking.** Whenever the mind collapses to either/or ("I either have to buy the car or not buy the car"), there is unfelt fear. Non-binary options (buy from different sellers, different packages, different timing) become visible once the fear is felt.

## What This Implies

The intellect is attempting to solve an emotional problem using logic. It cannot work. "You can think about this in terms of your own life: how many decisions of your life were made because you didn't want to feel like a failure? Or how many decisions were made so that you could feel loved?" All decisions are emotional. When the emotion is blocked, the decision is blocked.

## What to Do

Ask [[what-feeling-am-i-resisting]]. The answer is the emotion underneath the loop, the indecision, or the judgment. Once named, the coaching path moves to [[feeling-unfelt-emotions]]: feel it, and the clarity appears.

"If you are judgmental towards another person, there's an emotion you're not feeling." -- Joe Hudson, Feel over Figure

## Tone

Matter-of-fact. These signals are presented as diagnostic tools, not as judgments. The tone is: "here is how you can tell."
