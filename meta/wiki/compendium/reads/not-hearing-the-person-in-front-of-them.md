# Not Hearing the Person in Front of Them

## What Joe Notices

The person is reacting to someone from their past, not the person in front of them. They hear criticism, abandonment, or control that is not actually present in the conversation. Their emotional response does not match the actual situation. They may be escalating a minor interaction into a major conflict.

Joe describes this as a trauma response that happens in "minor ways all the time." When someone reminds us of our original attachment wound, "we're not actually hearing them, we're not actually with them, we're with the person or people who helped us get that trauma into our bones."

## What This Implies

The person's system has been activated by something in the current interaction that pattern-matches to their childhood experience. They are not fully present. They are partly in the past. This connects to [[trauma-mapping]]: the person is "hearing criticism when it doesn't actually happen."

Joe also describes a second dynamic: "We'll believe a lie if it rhymes with the truth that we want to believe." People can be duped not because the deception is skillful but because they want the thing being offered (love, safety, validation) badly enough to override their perception.

## What Joe Does

Joe has not yet been observed coaching someone through this read in a live session (teaching episode only). Based on his explanatory framework, he would likely:
- Name what he sees: "I wonder if you're hearing something that isn't being said right now"
- Connect to childhood: "Who does this person remind you of?"
- Move toward [[feeling-unfelt-emotions]]: the emotion from the original wound needs to be felt so the person can be present in current relationships

## Tone

Matter-of-fact. Joe describes this dynamic without judgment, as a natural consequence of how the nervous system works.

## Connected Concerns

- [[i-keep-ending-up-in-the-same-pattern]]: the person keeps having the same conflict because they keep hearing the past
- Future concern: "My partner and I keep having the same fight"
