# Fight or Submit Binary

## What to Notice

The person can only see two options: fight (rebel, resist, push back) or submit (comply, roll over, give in). They oscillate between these two positions. Both feel bad. There is no third option visible.

Signals:
- "I either want to fight or I want to roll over"
- Alternating between resistance and collapse
- Describing the relationship as win-or-lose
- Feeling powerless despite being capable
- Productive in some contexts but frozen around specific people (often authority figures who resemble a parent)

## What This Implies

Both fight and submit are positions of disempowerment. Both give power to the other person. Rebellion against someone is still being controlled by them (you are reacting to what they do). Submission to someone is being controlled by them (you are doing what they want). See [[empower-over-power]].

The third option that is not visible: empowered, undefended, upright love. Being yourself without needing to fight or collapse. Standing in your truth with an open heart. This is what [[vulnerability]] and [[love-over-defense]] point to.

This binary is almost always rooted in childhood: the child could either fight the parent (and be punished) or submit (and be controlled). No other option was modeled or available.

## What to Do

1. Name the binary: "It sounds like you see two options: fight or submit. Both of them put the other person in control."
2. Ask what the third option would feel like in the body: "What is the experience of non-fight, non-rolling-over?"
3. Often the third option arrives as something somatic: balance, uprightness, aliveness, openness
4. Practice the third option through [[role-playing-the-dynamic]]
5. Connect to [[empower-over-power]]: empowerment is being yourself despite consequences

## Tone

Matter-of-fact when naming the binary. Warm and spacious when exploring the third option. The person may resist the third option because it feels more vulnerable than either fight or submit.
