# Discover and catalog all Joe Hudson / Art of Accomplishment content sources

## Outcome

A complete catalog file at `meta/wiki/catalog.md` listing every known Joe Hudson content item with:
- Title
- Publication date
- Platform (YouTube, Apple Podcasts, Spotify, guest appearance, etc.)
- URL or feed entry identifier
- Participants (if known)
- Duration (if known)

The catalog should distinguish between: (a) content on the Art of Accomplishment YouTube channel, (b) Art of Accomplishment podcast episodes, (c) guest appearances on other channels/podcasts.

## Context

We cannot download what we haven't found. This sub-goal must produce a comprehensive list before bulk downloading begins. Joe Hudson's content may span multiple platforms and formats. The YouTube channel and podcast are the primary known sources, but he may appear as a guest elsewhere.

## Decision Log

- 2026-05-19: Goal created. Starting with platform/feed discovery research before attempting any scraping or cataloging.
- 2026-05-20: R-001 complete. Two primary content sources confirmed: YouTube channel and podcast (Simplecast RSS). Key finding: RSS feed is windowed to ~25 episodes, so we cannot rely on it alone for a full catalog. The official website podcast page or a podcast index service is needed for historical episodes. Guest appearances are scattered across 10+ shows with no single index. Next steps: run experiments to extract full listings from YouTube (E-001), the website (E-002), guest appearance indexes (E-003), and check Vimeo (E-004). YouTube and website experiments are highest priority since they cover primary content.
- 2026-05-20: E-001 succeeded. 329 YouTube videos extracted with IDs, titles, durations, and URLs. Upload dates not available in flat-playlist mode but can be obtained via full JSON extraction or YouTube API in a follow-up. 21 playlists found providing categorization. YouTube catalog is essentially complete for listing purposes. Next priority: E-002 (podcast archive) to cover the second major content source.
- 2026-05-20: E-002 succeeded. iTunes Lookup API returned all 167 podcast episodes (Oct 2020 to May 2026) with titles, dates, durations, and direct audio URLs. Saved to `meta/raw/itunes-episode-list.tsv`. The AoA website (Webflow, JS-rendered), Podnews (JS-rendered), and RSS feed (~20 episodes only) all failed or were insufficient. The iTunes API is the best source: public, no auth, complete catalog with downloadable audio URLs. This likely makes E-009 (PodcastIndex approach) unnecessary. Two of three primary catalogs now complete: YouTube (329 videos) and podcast (167 episodes). Remaining: E-003 (guest appearances) and E-004 (Vimeo check).
- 2026-05-20: E-003 succeeded. 14 unique guest appearances cataloged (Jan 2022 to May 2026). Saved to `meta/raw/guest-appearances.tsv`. Listen Notes had false positives (common name); Podchaser and player.fm blocked with 403. Web search was the most reliable method. Appearances accelerating in 2026 (8 of 14). List is likely incomplete but meets the 10+ threshold. Three of four catalog tracks now complete: YouTube (329), podcast (167), guest appearances (14). Remaining: E-004 (Vimeo).
- 2026-05-20: E-004 succeeded. Vimeo account has 73 public videos, almost entirely unique from YouTube. Content is course supplementary material: user manuals, Q&A recordings, coaching clips, transformation stories, testimonials. These are lower priority than podcast/YouTube for transcription but represent a significant unique content trove. All four catalog tracks now complete. Total known content: YouTube (329), podcast (167), guest appearances (14), Vimeo (73) = **583 items**. G-002 is substantially complete. Remaining question: whether to formally merge these into a single catalog file, and whether Vimeo content should be included in G-003 download scope.
