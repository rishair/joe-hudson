# Approaches to large-scale transcript concept extraction

## Question

What are the natural categories of Joe Hudson's coaching content? What taxonomy of article types emerges from the transcripts themselves?

## Why This Matters

The compendium's directory structure needs to match the actual shape of Joe's content. Starting with hypothesized categories but validating against real transcripts before committing to a structure for 480 transcripts.

## Sources

- 5 transcripts absorbed during initial prototyping (Introduction to VIEW, Connection Course episodes, Master Class #1, and others)
- Farzaa's personal Wikipedia skill for structural inspiration
- Review of the full transcript folder listing (480 folders) for content diversity

## Findings

The initial 5-category hypothesis (concepts, patterns, techniques, frameworks, examples) was too narrow. The transcripts revealed 10 natural article types:

| Type | Definition | Example |
|------|-----------|---------|
| concept | Core ideas Joe teaches | VIEW, vulnerability, "should" |
| pattern | Recurring human patterns Joe identifies | people-pleasing, resistance |
| technique | Specific things Joe does in sessions | asking "what do you want?" |
| framework | Multi-part models with named components | VIEW (4 parts), stages of emotional development |
| distinction | Pairs Joe differentiates | care vs caretaking, curiosity vs wonder |
| principle | Rules or truths Joe asserts | "resistance causes pain, not the emotion" |
| move | Specific coaching actions in live sessions | reflecting the pattern back, following wonder |
| practice | Exercises Joe assigns | how/what questions, daily wants practice |
| example | Stories Joe uses repeatedly to illustrate | the car salesman study, unwilling attention |
| application | Domain-specific applications of concepts | VIEW in sales, VIEW in parenting |

Key finding: the distinction between "moves" (what Joe does in-session) and "techniques" (teachable methods) matters. Moves are observed in coaching transcripts. Techniques are explained in teaching transcripts. They overlap but serve different purposes for the chatbot.

Boundary condition: this taxonomy was validated against 5 transcripts. It may need refinement after 50+ absorbs. The `/compendium checkpoint` at every 20 absorbs includes a taxonomy review.

## Implications

- The `/compendium` skill was built with all 10 types and provisional directory structure
- E-018 (prototype absorb) is partially complete: 5 transcripts absorbed, 13 articles created across 7 of the 10 types
- The taxonomy should be treated as provisional. The checkpoint system will catch needed changes.
- R-007 (duplicate research) should be marked complete or merged
