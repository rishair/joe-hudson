# Survey transcripts to identify Joe Hudson's coaching taxonomy

## Question

What kinds of things does Joe Hudson teach across his content? What are the natural categories that emerge from the transcripts themselves? What article types should the compendium use?

## Sources

Surveyed 5 transcripts for diversity:
1. **Introduction to VIEW** (Connection Course #1) - foundational concept teaching
2. **Father Figures and Authority** (Coaching Session) - live coaching
3. **Stages of Emotional Development** (Emotion Series #1) - developmental framework
4. **Want over Should** (Master Class #6) - concept/principle teaching
5. **You're Pushing Away What You Want Most** (2026, solo video) - recent, pattern-focused

## Findings

### Content types observed

**1. Named Concepts (most frequent)**
Joe names specific concepts and defines them precisely. These are the atoms of his methodology.
- VIEW (Vulnerability, Impartiality, Empathy, Wonder) - each is a sub-concept with its own definition
- "Should" as a mechanism of shame
- "Wanting" vs "shoulding"
- Emotional fluidity vs emotional repression vs emotional management
- "Inherent goodness" (referenced as established concept)
- "Chasing love," "avoiding love," "fighting for love" as three patterns

**2. Developmental Stages / Frameworks**
Multi-step models with a specific progression. These are NOT single concepts but ordered sequences.
- Stages of emotional development (7 stages: fluid/non-personal -> repression -> emotions control us -> management -> allowing/feeling -> fluidity again -> emotions wrapped in love)
- Three ways we push away love (chasing, avoiding, fighting)
- VIEW itself is a 4-part framework

**3. Coaching Moves (what Joe does in sessions)**
Observable patterns in how Joe steers a coaching conversation. These are not things Joe names; they are things Joe does.
- Reflecting the pattern back: "How is that not true about you?"
- Following his wonder: "My wonder just went in this other direction"
- Asking "what if the opposite were true?" style questions
- Connecting present behavior to childhood origin
- Naming the thing the person is avoiding
- Using how/what questions (never why)
- Stating his own vulnerability: "That's a scary question for me to ask"

**4. Principles / Aphorisms**
Compressed wisdom Joe repeats across episodes. Often a single sentence.
- "Joy is the matriarch of a family of emotions. She won't come into your house unless her children are welcome."
- "It's not the emotion that hurts, it's the resistance to feeling the emotion that hurts."
- "Shame is the locks that keep the chains of bad habits in place."
- "We'll believe a lie if it rhymes with the truth we want to believe."
- "There's no sense in loving somebody who doesn't love themselves."

**5. Exercises / Practices**
Specific things Joe tells people to do.
- "Enjoy yourself 10% more" (referenced in Emotion Series)
- Practice VIEW in conversations
- Write down everything about yourself you don't want to admit (referenced in Want over Should)
- Track your negative self-talk to discover how love was wired

**6. Distinctions**
Joe frequently draws sharp distinctions between things people conflate.
- Vulnerability vs weakness
- Empathy vs losing yourself in someone
- Curiosity vs wonder
- Repression vs management (of emotions)
- Submission vs surrender
- Rebellion vs empowerment
- Wanting vs shoulding

**7. Domain Applications**
Joe applies his core concepts to specific life domains. The concept is the same, but the context changes.
- Business/leadership (impartiality in management, VIEW in sales)
- Parenting (power struggles with children, how love gets wired)
- Romantic relationships (chasing/avoiding/fighting patterns)
- Self-relationship (internal voice, self-criticism)

### What the hypothesized categories got right and wrong

The original hypotheses were: concepts, patterns, techniques, frameworks, examples.
- "Concepts" is confirmed as the largest category but needs splitting: named concepts, principles/aphorisms, and distinctions are all different.
- "Patterns" maps loosely to what I'm calling "coaching moves" (what Joe does) but also overlaps with developmental stages.
- "Techniques" maps to exercises/practices. Confirmed but small category.
- "Frameworks" is confirmed (developmental stages, multi-part models).
- "Examples" is not a natural category. Joe uses examples within every other category. They are not standalone articles.

### Proposed taxonomy (7 types)

| Type | Directory | Description | Example |
|------|-----------|-------------|---------|
| Concept | `concepts/` | A named idea Joe defines and teaches | VIEW, vulnerability, impartiality, emotional fluidity |
| Distinction | `distinctions/` | Two things people conflate that Joe separates | vulnerability vs weakness, curiosity vs wonder |
| Framework | `frameworks/` | Multi-step model or ordered progression | stages of emotional development, 3 ways we push away love |
| Principle | `principles/` | Compressed wisdom, often a single sentence Joe repeats | "Joy won't come unless her children are welcome" |
| Coaching Move | `moves/` | What Joe does in a session (observable pattern) | reflecting the pattern back, following wonder |
| Practice | `practices/` | Specific exercise or assignment | enjoy yourself 10% more, VIEW conversations |
| Application | `applications/` | A concept applied to a specific domain | VIEW in business, wants in parenting |

### Navigation patterns observed

Joe's content has clear directional flow:
- **Concept -> Distinction**: When teaching a concept, Joe immediately distinguishes it from what people confuse it with
- **Pattern recognition -> Childhood origin**: In coaching, Joe connects present behavior to childhood wiring
- **Concept -> Practice**: Teaching leads to "here's what you can do"
- **Framework stage N -> Framework stage N+1**: Developmental models progress
- **Domain problem -> Core concept**: A business/relationship/parenting problem maps back to a core concept

These navigation patterns are the directional links the compendium needs.

## Implications

1. The compendium should use 7 article types, not 5. The key additions are: distinctions (very frequent in Joe's teaching), coaching moves (critical for chatbot navigation), and principles (compact, quotable wisdom).
2. "Examples" should not be a standalone category. Examples live within other articles as supporting material.
3. Navigation links should encode the patterns above (concept -> distinction, pattern -> origin, etc.)
4. The coaching moves category is the most novel and valuable for a chatbot. It captures what Joe *does*, not just what he *says*.
5. E-018 should test this taxonomy on 5 transcripts to validate.
