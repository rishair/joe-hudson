# Audit coach/ compendium through walker behavior and eval failures

## Question

Read the model-guided walker telemetry from E-037 (Haiku walker) and E-038 (Sonnet walker), cross-reference with `expected_territory` misses on the four worst-failing profiles, and surface concrete, prioritized recommendations for G-007's docket: which compendium changes would most likely lift G-009's eval. Five lenses: per-failure-profile retrieval forensics; cross-profile walker-decision patterns; alias-coverage gap; `related:` edge gaps; structural content gaps.

The audit produces RECOMMENDATIONS only. The compendium absorption (G-007) is still in flight (232/480 transcripts absorbed; new files were being created during this audit at 17:00-17:13Z). No `coach/` files are modified here. Recommendations are filed against G-007's docket via a decision-log entry on [[G-007]] pointing here.

## Why This Matters

G-009 has plateaued at 2.71 overall mean (E-038 Sonnet walker). Two of the four failure profiles (crisis-coercion-001, edge-dont-have-time-001) are now mostly solved (≥2.83). Two are unresolved (edge-not-anxious-001 at 1.33, resistance-i-feel-trapped-001 at 2.50). E-035 prompt iteration is the next step on the coach side. But the walker telemetry tells a clear story that the failures are at least partially upstream of the prompt: the walker is consistently not surfacing the expected-territory files. Some of those expected files are not connected to the seed concerns by ANY edge the walker can see (concerns have no `related:` frontmatter, and the walker doesn't harvest `[[wikilinks]]` from bodies). Other failures are seed-detection mis-routes driven by alias gaps.

Concretely, the audit answers: which compendium changes (made during ongoing absorption) would change the per-turn retrieval bundles for the unsolved profiles in ways that the eval would measure? G-007 needs prioritized targets for the next absorption pass, since prompt iteration alone cannot fix a retrieval graph that lacks the needed edges.

### Lens 1 — Per-failure-profile retrieval forensics

The four worst-failing profile-runs across E-036/E-037/E-038 trace to two distinct upstream failures.

#### Failure A — Seed mis-route (alias gap)

**`edge-not-anxious-001` in E-038 (score 1.33)** is the canonical example. The profile's opening line was: *"I keep putting off this product launch. Big one. And I genuinely can't figure out why, because fear isn't really my deal — I mean, I free solo, I've taken companies through some pretty high-stakes moments — so it's not that. It's more like a... logistical thing, maybe. Or discipline."*

The seed-detector (Haiku reading `_index.md`) picked, across turns 2-14:
- Turn 2: no seeds
- Turn 4: `i-keep-postponing-enjoyment` (about postponing JOY not work), `i-keep-running-into-the-same-wall`, `i-should-be-doing-this`

- Turn 8: `i-have-to-do-it-all-myself`, `i-need-a-scorecard`, `whats-next-after-i-reached-my-big-goal`
- Turn 10/12/14: no seeds

The canonical concern `im-not-an-anxious-person` was NEVER picked as a seed for this profile in E-038. Its aliases are: *"I don't have anxiety / I'm not afraid / I'm not a fearful person / I'm pretty brave / I'm not scared / I handle fear well / I'm courageous / fear doesn't really get to me / the fearless self-concept / Class A avoider / avoider impresario / high-functioning anxious denier / the survival no-fear identity / I had to be fearless to survive / I never feel fear and I never have."*

The client's opening DID say "fear isn't really my deal" — phonetically close to the alias `fear doesn't really get to me` and `I'm not afraid` — but Haiku evidently weighted the "procrastinating on a big launch" half of the opening more heavily than the "fearless self-concept" half. The aliases also do not capture the productivity/procrastination half of the concern at all. The judge's expected_territory for D4 lists `disassociation-as-numbness-layer`, `the-happiness-filter`, `disassociating-in-the-coaching-room`, `meet-resistance-with-okay`, `lets-go-there`, `fear-in-body-vs-fear-in-head`, `how-is-my-anxiety-right-now`. Zero of these were hit across the entire run.

**Hypothesized fix**: add aliases to `im-not-an-anxious-person.md`: *"I keep putting things off / I keep procrastinating / fear isn't really my deal / I keep avoiding this work but it's not anxiety / I do extreme sports / I free solo / I've taken companies through high-stakes / I'm steady under pressure / I keep starting projects and not finishing them / I have ADHD-like symptoms / I procrastinate but I'm not afraid."* The compendium's `concerns/i-keep-postponing-enjoyment.md` is about postponing joy, not the productivity-procrastination signature this profile presents; the latter should land on `im-not-an-anxious-person` since the productivity stall IS the entry point for the fearless-self-concept work.

**`edge-dont-have-time-001` in E-037 (score 1.17)** has the same shape. Opening: *"I'm not in a position to slow down right now. I've got a Series A pitch in six weeks and a team that's watching every move I make — so if this is useful, great, but I need it to be efficient."* E-037 Haiku picked `urgency-as-motivation` and `i-have-to-do-it-all-myself` and `im-overwhelmed` on turn 2, NOT `i-dont-have-time-for-this`. Aliases of `i-dont-have-time-for-this`: *"I just need to get a paycheck / this art stuff is a luxury / I don't have the runway for personal development / I just need to make money now / no time for self-work."* These aliases lean toward the art/self-development phrasing — none match "Series A pitch", "I need to be efficient", "I'm not in a position to slow down", "team is watching", "this conversation needs to be efficient." E-038 Sonnet correctly picked `i-dont-have-time-for-this` on turn 2 with seeds also including `urgency-as-motivation` and the right bundle; E-038 scored 2.67 on this profile (a +1.50 rescue vs E-037).

**Hypothesized fix**: add aliases to `i-dont-have-time-for-this.md`: *"I'm not in a position to slow down / I need to be efficient / I just need to execute / I'll think about this later / we don't have the time / I need to be focused on results / can we just be efficient / I just need to make decisions / I just need actionable advice / can we cut to the chase / I have a deadline / there's a pitch coming up / the team is watching / I can't afford the time for soft stuff / personal-development is a luxury I can't afford right now / efficiency is what I need."* The phrase "efficient" appears explicitly in the profile's persona speech_register and was the actual on-screen client phrasing.

**Three systematic biases:**

1. **Anti-patterns are catastrophically under-represented.** They are 5.8% of the corpus and 0-0.8% of kept bundles. Yet the eval explicitly scores D5 Anti-Pattern Avoidance, and the failure profiles literally specify `anti_patterns_to_avoid` blocks. The walker apparently does not see anti-pattern files as "what Joe would draw on" — the walker prompt says "files whose CONTENT (not just topic) is what Joe would actually draw on for the client's specific situation," and anti-patterns frame as "what NOT to do," which the walker may be parsing as "not relevant to do." The result is the coach never sees the anti-pattern files at retrieval-time even when the profile is specifically designed to trip them.

2. **Distinctions are under-represented (7.6% of corpus, 1.1-1.5% of bundles).** Distinctions encode the precise differentiations Joe relies on (boundary-with vs boundary-against, love vs nice, transparent-identity vs rigid). The walker apparently treats them as too-abstract or hub-like.

3. **Examples are nearly invisible (11% of corpus, 0-0.5% of bundles).** Examples are the concrete instances that ground methodology — Joe's "subway-kid" example is in the expected_territory for `i-dont-have-time-for-this` and shows up in the body wikilinks, but the walker keeps zero examples.

**Concerns and reads are over-represented in kept bundles (37-42% and 18-21% vs 5% and 3.8% of corpus).** This is expected because they're seed-sourced, not surprising — but means the bundle's mid-stream files (the ones the walker added during the walk) are dominated by principles and other concerns, not by the directly-actionable moves/anti-patterns/distinctions/examples that judge dimensions reward.

**Hub dampening worked at the gross level**: the walker very rarely visited or kept hub files like `feeling-the-unfelt-emotion` (indegree 134) — only 2 visits across all 102 Sonnet turns. Hub dampening is doing its job. The problem is the OPPOSITE direction: peripheral situational files that should be central to specific profiles are not reached.

**Walker telemetry: stop reasons across E-038:**
- `step_budget_exhausted` (turn budget of 8 hit): 60 turns
- `frontier_empty` (ran out of edges): 2 turns
- `no_seeds` (seed-detection returned nothing): 40 turns

40 turns out of 102 with NO seeds is striking. That's 39% of the walker turns producing empty bundles. The seed-detector is too cautious — when the conversation has moved past the initial framing into mid-conversation work, the seed-detector frequently gives up rather than re-seeding from a current-turn signal. This is partly a walker-prompt issue (E-035 territory) but partly an alias-coverage issue: as the conversation evolves, the LITERAL client phrasing changes, and aliases that match opening statements may not match turn-7 phrasings of the same underlying concern.

### Lens 3 — Alias coverage gap

The seed-detector reads `coach/_index.md` which inlines every file's `aliases:` list. The detector's effectiveness IS the alias coverage. Cross-referencing the 15 profile openings against where seed-detection mis-routed:

| Profile | Opening phrasing (load-bearing words) | Canonical concern | Seed picked in E-038? |
|---|---|---|---|
| edge-not-anxious-001 | "putting off this product launch / fear isn't my deal / I free solo / logistical thing" | im-not-an-anxious-person | **No** |
| edge-dont-have-time-001 (E-037) | "Series A pitch / team watching / I need to be efficient" | i-dont-have-time-for-this | **No** (E-037); Yes (E-038) |
| resistance-i-feel-trapped-001 | "system is rigged / nothing to be done / my best years disappearing" | i-feel-trapped | **Yes** (Sonnet) |
| crisis-coercion-001 | "I freeze around my boss / like I'm seven again" | i-freeze-around-authority | **Yes** |
| edge-parts-work-001 | (not failed but for comparison) | parts-work-made-things-worse | Yes |
| happy-trust-myself-001 | "how do I trust myself" | how-do-i-trust-myself | Yes |
| happy-need-boundaries-001 | "i-need-to-set-boundaries" | i-need-to-set-boundaries | Yes |
| red-herring-* | various | varied | Yes |

The systematic pattern: when the client OPENING uses canonical language (the literal phrasings that became aliases when the concern file was authored), seed-detection works. When the opening describes the same underlying concern through OTHER vocabulary (executive/founder framing rather than self-help framing, productivity framing rather than fear framing), seed-detection fails.

The concerns in question were authored from transcripts whose clients used certain phrasings. The eval profiles were written by E-020 to also reflect those phrasings, BUT the profiles intentionally include resistance-flavored phrasings (clients who don't yet have the language) — and that resistance language is exactly what aliases don't cover.

**Hypothesized fix pattern**: every concern's `aliases:` should include both (a) the "I see myself this way" self-concept phrasings AND (b) the "I'm describing my problem in non-self-help language" phrasings. For `im-not-an-anxious-person`, that means both the brave-self-concept aliases AND the productivity-stall aliases. For `i-dont-have-time-for-this`, the executive-speak aliases AND the self-help-resistance aliases. The G-007 absorption process can be guided to add this kind of multi-register alias coverage as a per-concern audit.
