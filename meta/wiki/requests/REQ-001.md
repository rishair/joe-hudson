## Notes from session

**Session 1 — 2026-05-25, Rishi, v5b (Sonnet walker), web app.** Real concern brought: "I don't know how to love people, or let people in" / not being able to let birthday wishes land. ~6 turns observed.

**Verdict: close-but-flat.** The coach lands on presence, curiosity, and felt-sense tracking (it correctly reflected "that glimmer, and then the forgetting feeling rushing in over it" and stayed at texture rather than jumping to advice). But it reads as **lacking authority**. It only asks open curious questions and never offers anything of substance back — no read, no frame, no naming of what it notices as a statement. The user reported not trusting it and not feeling given anything to work with.

**Core diagnosis (user's words, paraphrased):** In live sessions Joe's authority is carried by tone, posture, presence, and timing. Text strips all of that. A coach that *only* asks curious questions reads as hollow or evasive in text. To earn trust in text, the coach must **share more** — offer reflections, name patterns, occasionally put a distinction or frame on the table as a statement — not just lob the felt-sense back as another question. The calibration risk is overcorrecting into therapist-interpretation / advice-giving, which the methodology forbids. "I don't want to overdo it though."

**Turn-level:** Every assistant turn in the session ended in a question and offered no declarative substance. T1 "what's it like to sit with it right now?", T2 "what's happening in your body... what made you say it just now?", T3 "what happens in you when a wish comes in?", T4 "what's it like to let that glimmer be real for a second?". All curious, none authoritative.
